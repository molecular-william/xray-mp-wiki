# Crystallization Methods

## Overview

Crystallization is the process of forming ordered arrays of molecules. For membrane proteins, special methods are often required due to the hydrophobic nature of transmembrane domains.

## Methods

### Vapor Diffusion
- **Description**: Droplet equilibrates against reservoir through vapor phase
- **Variants**: Sitting drop, hanging drop
- **Advantages**: Simple, widely used, requires small volumes
- **Disadvantages**: Slow, can have nucleation issues
- **Pages**: [[vapor-diffusion]], [[microbatch]]

### Lipidic Cubic Phase (LCP)
- **Description**: Membrane protein embedded in lipid bilayer mesophase
- **Advantages**: Native-like environment for membrane proteins
- **Disadvantages**: More complex setup, specialized equipment needed
- **Page**: [[lipidic-cubic-phase]]

### Microbatch
- **Description**: Oil-overlay method to prevent evaporation
- **Advantages**: Simple, fast screening
- **Disadvantages**: Limited equilibration control
- **Page**: [[microbatch]]
