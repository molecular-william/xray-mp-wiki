# Expression Systems

## Overview

Expression systems are the host organisms used to produce recombinant proteins. The choice of expression system depends on the protein, required post-translational modifications, and yield requirements.

## Systems

### Baculovirus Expression
- **Host**: Sf9 insect cells
- **Advantages**: High yield, eukaryotic post-translational modifications
- **Disadvantages**: More complex than bacterial systems, viral contamination risk
- **Page**: [[baculovirus-expression]]

### HEK293 Cells
- **Host**: Human embryonic kidney cells
- **Advantages**: Mammalian system, proper folding and modifications
- **Disadvantages**: Lower yield, more expensive, requires biosafety level 2
- **Page**: [[hek293-cells]]

### Pichia pastoris
- **Host**: Methylotrophic yeast
- **Advantages**: High yield, eukaryotic modifications, inexpensive
- **Disadvantages**: Glycosylation patterns differ from mammals
- **Page**: [[pichia-pastoris]]

### Sf9 Cells
- **Host**: Insect cells (Spodoptera frugiperda)
- **Advantages**: High yield, eukaryotic modifications, Baculovirus compatible
- **Disadvantages**: Requires virus production, lower yield than bacterial systems
- **Page**: [[sf9-cells]]
