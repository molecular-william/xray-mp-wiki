# Buffers

## Overview

Buffers maintain pH stability during membrane protein purification and crystallization. Proper buffer selection is critical for protein stability and crystal formation.

## Common Buffers

### HEPES Buffer
- **pH range**: 6.8–8.2
- **Use**: General purpose, good compatibility with most proteins
- **Page**: [[hepes-buffer]]

### Tris Buffer
- **pH range**: 7.0–9.0
- **Use**: General purpose, widely used
- **Page**: [[tris-buffer]]

### MES Buffer
- **pH range**: 5.5–6.7
- **Use**: Acidic pH range
- **Page**: [[mes-buffer]]

### MOPS Buffer
- **pH range**: 6.5–7.9
- **Use**: Near-neutral pH
- **Page**: [[mops-buffer]]

### Sodium Phosphate Buffer
- **pH range**: 5.8–8.0
- **Use**: High buffer capacity, crystallization
- **Page**: [[sodium-phosphate]]

### Bis-Tris Propane Chloride (BTP)
- **pH range**: ~6.5–8.5
- **Use**: High buffering capacity
- **Page**: [[bis-tris-propane]]

### Sodium Acetate
- **pH range**: 3.6–5.6
- **Use**: Acidic conditions
- **Page**: [[sodium-acetate]]

### Sodium Citrate
- **pH range**: 3.0–6.2
- **Use**: Acidic conditions, crystallization
- **Page**: [[sodium-citrate]]
