# Additives

## Overview

Additives are small molecules, salts, and polymers added to crystallization or purification buffers to improve protein stability, solubility, or crystal quality.

## Categories of Additives

### Salts
- **Sodium Chloride (NaCl)** - Ionic strength adjustment
- **Lithium Sulfate (Li2SO4)** - Precipitant, crystallization
- **Ammonium Dihydrogen Phosphate (NH4H2PO4)** - Crystallization precipitant
- **Magnesium Sulfate (MgSO4)** - Crystallization salt
- **Potassium Thiocyanate (KSCN)** - Ion source for crystallization
- **Calcium Chloride** - Divalent cation for crystallization
- **Sodium Sulfate (Na2SO4)** - Salting-out precipitant

### Polyethylene Glycols (PEGs)
- **PEG 2000** - Medium MW precipitant
- **PEG 300** - Low MW, cryoprotectant
- **PEG 400** - Low MW, cryoprotectant
- **PEG 3350** - Medium MW crystallization precipitant
- **PEG 4000** - Medium-high MW precipitant
- **PEG 5000 MME** - Monomethyl ether crystallization precipitant
- **PEG 6000** - High MW precipitant

### Other Additives
- **Glycerol** - Cryoprotectant, stabilizer
- **Imidazole** - Elution agent, metal chelator
- **Jeffamine M600** - Polymer additive
- **Spermine** - Polyamine for DNA/protein crystallization
