# Detergents

## Overview

Detergents are amphipathic molecules used to solubilize membrane proteins by forming micelles around hydrophobic transmembrane domains.

## Detergent Classes

### Nonionic Detergents
- **DDM** (n-Dodecyl-β-D-Maltopyranoside) - Mild, widely used
- **LMNG** (Lauryl Maltose Neopentyl Glycol) - Mild, neopentyl glycol-based
- **MNG** (Maltose Neopentyl Glycol) - Mild, neopentyl glycol-based
- **OG** (n-Octyl β-D-Glucopyranoside) - Shorter chain, used for GPCR crystallization
- **DM** (Decylmaltoside) - Shorter chain than DDM
- **LDAO** (Lauryldimethylamine N-Oxide) - Zwitterionic detergent
- **LAPAO** - Zwitterionic detergent
- **Anzergent 3,12** - Zwitterionic detergent
- **NG** (Nonylglucoside) - Shorter chain
- **HpG** (Heptylglucoside) - Shorter chain
- **OGNG** (Octyl Glucose Neopentyl Glycol) - Hybrid detergent
