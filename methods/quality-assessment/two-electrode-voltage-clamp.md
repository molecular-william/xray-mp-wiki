# Two-Electrode Voltage Clamp (TEVC)

## Overview

Two-electrode voltage clamp (TEVC) is an electrophysiology technique for measuring ion channel currents in Xenopus oocytes. In the study of NMDA receptors, TEVC was used to assess the functional effects of conformational trapping by bifunctional MTS reagents (M2M, M4M, M8M) on engineered cysteine mutants of GluN1b and GluN2B subunits, demonstrating that the open-conformation of the GluN2B ATD represents the active state of the NMDA receptor.

- Xenopus laevis oocytes (defolliculated)
