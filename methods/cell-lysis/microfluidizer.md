# Microfluidizer (M-110EH)

## Overview

The Microfluidizer is a high-pressure homogenizer used for cell lysis and membrane fractionation in membrane protein purification workflows. The M-110EH model from Microfluidics applies extreme pressure to force cell suspensions through a narrow interaction chamber, causing shear-induced cell disruption.

## Equipment Details

- **Model**: M-110EH (Microfluidics)
- **Operating pressure**: 22,000 p.s.i. (typical for yeast/mammalian cell lysis)
- **Passes**: 5 passes recommended for complete disruption
- **Application**: Yeast cell lysis, membrane fractionation, emulsification

## Proteins Using Microfluidizer (from this wiki)

- [[nTMATE2-transporter]] — *Pichia pastoris* SMD1168 cells harvested after methanol induction, resuspended in 300 mM NaCl, 20 mM Tris-HCl pH 8.0, 10% [[glycerol]]; lysed by 5 passes at 22,000 p.s.i. in M-110EH Microfluidizer; membrane fraction separated by centrifugation

## Why Use Microfluidizer?

- **Consistent cell lysis**: Uniform pressure ensures reproducible disruption
- **No beads or enzymes needed**: Mechanical disruption avoids contamination
- **Scalable**: Suitable for large-scale membrane protein production
- **Cold-friendly**: Can operate with cooling jackets for temperature-sensitive proteins

## Related Cell Disruption Methods

- **French press**: Older high-pressure homogenizer technology
- **Bead beating**: Mechanical disruption using glass beads
- **Detergent lysis**: Direct solubilization of membranes
