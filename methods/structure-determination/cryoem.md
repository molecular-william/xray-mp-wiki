# Cryo-EM (Cryogenic Electron Microscopy)

## Overview

Cryo-electron microscopy is a structural biology technique where protein samples are flash-frozen in vitreous ice and imaged under electron microscopy. It has become a major complementary method to X-ray crystallography for membrane protein structure determination, particularly for large complexes and proteins that resist crystallization.

## Cryo-EM in Membrane Protein Studies (from this wiki)

- [psi-lhci-supercomplex](/proteins/psi-lhci-supercomplex/) — CryoEM used to visualize [psi-lhci-supercomplex](/proteins/psi-lhci-supercomplex/) supercomplex:
  - Revealed extra densities on luminal and stromal sides
  - Densities situated near electron transfer pathway
  - Complemented X-ray crystallography data
- [navab-sodium-channel](/proteins/navab-sodium-channel/) — Resting-state NaVAb structure at 4.0 Å; MBP-NaVAb/KAV/G94C/Q150C disulfide-crosslinked complex in [digitonin](/reagents/detergents/digitonin/) detergent; pore module resolved at 3.0–3.9 Å; complete voltage-dependent gating mechanism elucidated

## Relationship to This Wiki

While this wiki focuses on X-ray crystallography reagents, cryo-EM is increasingly used alongside crystallography in the same workflows:

- **Same solubilization reagents**: Detergents (DDM, LMNG, [gdn](/reagents/detergents/gdn/)) used for both techniques
- **GDN (GlycoDiosgenin)**: Specifically mentioned as extremely mild for cryo-EM sample prep
- **[lipidic-cubic-phase](/methods/crystallization/lipidic-cubic-phase/)**: Both cryo-EM and crystallography use lipidic cubic phase
- **Vitrification**: Flash-freezing in liquid nitrogen used in both (cryo-EM) and X-ray crystallography

## When Cryo-EM Is Preferred

- Large complexes (>150 kDa)
- Proteins that resist crystallization
- Conformational heterogeneity studies
- Near-native membrane environment (nanodiscs, amphipols)
