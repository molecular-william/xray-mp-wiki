# IMISX In-Situ Serial Crystallography

## Overview

IMISX (in meso in situ) is a serial X-ray crystallography technique that combines lipidic cubic phase (LCP) crystallization with direct in situ data collection from unharvested crystals. The method uses specialized in-situ crystallization plates with defined spacer thickness and transparent cover films that allow X-ray beam access without crystal harvesting. Crystals are grown in LCP drops dispensed onto the plate, overlaid with precipitant solution, and data are collected directly from multiple crystals within the drop after direct freezing in liquid nitrogen. This eliminates crystal harvesting artifacts and is particularly valuable for microcrystals that are difficult to harvest conventionally.
