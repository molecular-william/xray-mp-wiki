# Microbatch Crystallization

## Overview

Microbatch crystallization is a technique where protein and precipitant are mixed under a layer of inert oil (paraffin or [microbatch](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/microbatch/)) to prevent evaporation. The oil layer creates a sealed environment, and water activity equilibration occurs slowly through the oil phase.

## Types

- **Microbatch under paraffin oil**: Traditional method; lower viscosity, suitable for standard drops
- **Microbatch under [microbatch](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/microbatch/)**: Higher viscosity (e.g., 500 cSt); prevents crystal drying, suitable for flash-freezing without cryoprotectant
- **Capillary microbatch**: Micro-volume drops in capillary tubes (Ismagilov method)

## Proteins Using Microbatch (from this wiki)

- [psi-lhci-supercomplex](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/psi-lhci-supercomplex/) — Microbatch under silicone oil (500 cSt) in Terazaki plates
  - Best diffracting crystals grown under [microbatch](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/microbatch/)
  - Crystals flash-frozen directly into liquid nitrogen (no cryoprotectant needed)

## Advantages

- Minimal sample volume (0.1-1 μL drops)
- No evaporation — stable conditions over long periods
- Compatible with LCP crystallization
- Direct flash-freezing (especially under [microbatch](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/microbatch/))
- Reduced crystal handling
