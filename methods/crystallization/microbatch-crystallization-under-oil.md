# Microbatch Crystallization Under Oil

## Overview

Microbatch crystallization under oil is a crystallization method where protein and precipitant solutions are mixed and placed under a layer of oil (paraffin oil, silicone oil, or a mixture) in microbatch plates. The oil prevents evaporation while allowing slow equilibration, enabling crystal growth without vapor diffusion. This method is particularly useful for screening and optimizing crystallization conditions with low protein consumption.

## Advantages

- Low protein consumption (1-3 µL drops)
- High throughput screening possible (72-well plates)
- No equilibration via vapor phase - controlled by oil composition
- Can use oil mixtures to fine-tune evaporation rate

## Disadvantages

- Oil can interfere with crystal harvesting
- Limited drop size for high-volume crystallization
- Crystal growth may differ from vapor diffusion methods
- Oil may absorb some crystallization components
