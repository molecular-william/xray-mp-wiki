# Liquid Sponge Phase Crystallization

## Overview

Liquid sponge phase ([LSP](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/lipidic-sponge-phase/)) crystallization is a membrane protein crystallization technique that uses a lipidic sponge phase as the matrix for crystal growth. It is a liquid analogue of the lipidic cubic phase (LCP) method, providing a continuous lipidic bilayer that mimics the natural membrane environment and aids membrane protein crystal growth. [LSP](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/lipidic-sponge-phase/) differs from LCP in that it operates at a different lipid-to-water ratio, forming a sponge (L3) phase rather than a bicontinuous cubic phase. Novel approaches for [LSP](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/lipidic-sponge-phase/) crystallization include crystallization in Hamilton gas-tight glass syringes and in plastic pipetting tips (in-tip crystallization), which increase the total crystallization volume while maintaining a sufficient phase boundary surface, enabling production of large numbers of crystals for serial crystallography.
