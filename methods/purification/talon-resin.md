# TALON Cobalt Resin

## Overview

TALON resin is a cobalt-based affinity chromatography medium designed for His-tag protein purification. Cobalt has higher selectivity for polyhistidine tags than nickel, reducing background binding of endogenous proteins.

## Properties

- **Bead matrix**: Agarose
- **Metal ion**: Co2+ (TALON® technology)
- **Manufacturer**: Clontech (now Takara Bio)
- **Selectivity**: Higher than Ni-NTA for His-tagged proteins

## Proteins Using TALON (from this wiki)

- [etb-receptor](/proteins/etb-receptor/) — GFP-His₁₀ tag captured on TALON resin; washed with 20 mM Tris-HCl pH 7.5, 500 mM NaCl, 0.1% LMNG, 0.01% CHS, 15 mM [imidazole](/reagents/additives/imidazole/); eluted with 20 mM Tris-HCl pH 7.5, 500 mM NaCl, 0.01% LMNG, 0.001% CHS, 200 mM imidazole

- [nTMATE2-transporter](/proteins/nTMATE2-transporter/) — His8-tagged [nTMATE2-transporter](/proteins/nTMATE2-transporter/) captured on TALON resin
  - Equilibration: 300 mM NaCl, 50 mM HEPES-Na pH 7.0, 5% [glycerol](/reagents/additives/glycerol/), 0.05% DDM, 0.005% CHS, 20 mM imidazole
  - Wash: 20-40 mM imidazole
  - Elution: 200 mM imidazole
  - Followed by SEC on [superdex-columns](/methods/purification/superdex-columns.html)
