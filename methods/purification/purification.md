# Purification Methods

Membrane protein purification isolates target proteins from cell lysates using a series of chromatography and concentration steps. The goal is to obtain homogeneous, functional protein at sufficient concentration for crystallization or structural studies.

## Overview

Purification of membrane proteins is more complex than soluble proteins due to detergent requirements. Proteins must be maintained in detergent micelles throughout the workflow to prevent aggregation and precipitation. Typical purification involves affinity capture, ion exchange, and size-exclusion chromatography, with detergent concentration carefully optimized at each step.

## Methods

- [affinity-chromatography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/purification/affinity-chromatography/) — Affinity Chromatography. Initial capture step using specific ligand-protein interactions (e.g., His-tag/Ni-NTA, GST/glutathione). High specificity and selectivity.
- [amicon-filters](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/purification/amicon-filters/) — Amicon Ultra Filters. Centrifugal concentration devices with molecular weight cutoff membranes. Used to concentrate dilute protein samples and exchange buffers.
- [centrifugation](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/purification/centrifugation/) — Centrifugation. Separation of cellular components by density and size. Membrane fractions pelleted at high speed before solubilization.
- [dialysis](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/purification/dialysis/) — Dialysis. Buffer exchange and detergent removal using semipermeable membranes. Commonly used for protein sample preparation before crystallization.
- [imac](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/purification/imac/) — IMAC (Immobilized Metal Affinity Chromatography). His-tag purification using Ni²⁺, Co²⁺, or Cu²⁺ chelating resins. Most widely used affinity method for recombinant proteins.
- [size-exclusion-chromatography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/purification/size-exclusion-chromatography/) — Size-Exclusion Chromatography (SEC). Final polishing step that separates proteins by hydrodynamic radius. Removes aggregates and confirms sample homogeneity.
- [superdex-columns](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/purification/superdex-columns/) — Superdex Columns. Prepacked SEC columns (Superdex 75, 200) for protein size analysis and purification. Widely used for membrane protein SEC.
- [talon-resin](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/purification/talon-resin/) — TALON Cobalt Resin. Immobilized metal affinity resin using cobalt instead of nickel. Higher specificity, reduced background binding compared to Ni-NTA.
