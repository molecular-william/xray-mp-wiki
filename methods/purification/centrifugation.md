# Centrifugation in Membrane Protein Workflows

## Overview

Centrifugation is used throughout membrane protein workflows for cell lysis, membrane fractionation, complex isolation, and sample preparation.

## Types Used in This Wiki

### Ultracentrifugation (High-Speed)
- [[nTMATE2-transporter]] — 130,000 g for 30 min to separate solubilized membrane proteins from insoluble debris
- [[psi-lhci-supercomplex]] — high-speed centrifugation of sonicated lysates

### Sucrose Density Gradient Ultracentrifugation
- [[psi-lhci-supercomplex]] — sucrose density gradient used to isolate [[psi-lhci-supercomplex]] supercomplexes
  - Yields PSI-4LHCI, PSI-6LHCI, PSI-8LHCI, PSI-9LHCI, PSI-10LHCI forms
  - Followed by β-DDM → GDN exchange for cryo-EM prep

### Low-Speed Centrifugation
- [[nTMATE2-transporter]] — Microfluidizer at 22,000 p.s.i. for cell lysis (not centrifugation per se, but similar purpose)
