# MB-15 Monobody

## Overview

MB-15 is a synthetic ICMT-binding monobody engineered as a crystallization chaperone for the beetle isoprenylcysteine carboxyl methyltransferase (ICMT). Based on a randomized fibronectin protein domain, MB-15 is an inhibitor of ICMT with an IC50 of approximately 1 uM and exhibits specificity for the beetle orthologue. MB-15 binds adjacent to the ICMT active site, preventing prenylated substrates from reaching the catalytic center and facilitating crystal formation in lipidic cubic phase.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Beetle ICMT (Tribolium castaneum)

MB-15 binds ICMT adjacent to the active site and interacts with portions of helices M5 and M8 and the M6-M7 loop. The FG loop of the monobody, which is diversified in the combinatorial library, dips into the membrane region and presents a tryptophan residue (Trp80) that occupies part of the crevice between M5 and M8. Ser77 of the monobody forms a hydrogen bond with Arg246 of ICMT, which is predicted to coordinate the C-terminal carboxylate of the prenylcysteine substrate. The monobody exhibits an IC50 of approximately 1 uM and specificity for the beetle ICMT orthologue.
