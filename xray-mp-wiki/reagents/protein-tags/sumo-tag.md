# SUMO Tag (Small Ubiquitin-like Modifier)

## Overview

SUMO (Small Ubiquitin-like Modifier) is a 11 kDa protein tag used to enhance solubility and expression of recombinant proteins, particularly difficult-to-express targets such as membrane protein domains. SUMO tags are cleaved by SUMO-specific proteases (e.g., Ulp1) at a specific recognition sequence, leaving a native methionine or glycine residue at the N-terminus of the target protein.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Small Ubiquitin-like Modifier (SUMO)
- **Class**: solubility-enhancement fusion tag

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Enhancing solubility of difficult-to-express proteins

SUMO fusion enhances solubility and expression of proteins that are otherwise insoluble or poorly expressed, particularly membrane protein domains and other challenging targets.


### N-terminal fusion tag for affinity purification

SUMO can be fused to the N-terminus of target proteins along with a His-tag, enabling affinity purification. The SUMO-His tag is cleaved off after purification to release the native target protein.


### Generating correctly folded proteins for crystallization

SUMO fusion can promote correct folding of target domains, facilitating crystallization trials. After cleavage, the untagged protein can be evaluated for crystallization.
