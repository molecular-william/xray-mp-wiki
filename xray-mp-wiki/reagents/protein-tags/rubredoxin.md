# Rubredoxin Fusion Protein

## Overview

Rubredoxin is a small iron-sulfur protein used as a fusion partner to replace the flexible intracellular loop 3 (ICL3) of GPCRs for crystallization. The fusion replaces the disordered ICL3 with a well-folded, stable scaffold that promotes crystal formation. Rubredoxin (residues M1-E54) has been used to crystallize several GPCRs including the [Human P2Y1 Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-p2y1-receptor/), apelin receptor, and [CCR2A](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/ccr2a/) receptor.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: rubredoxin (M1-E54)
- **Class**: fusion partner protein

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### GPCR crystallization construct stabilization

Rubredoxin replaced ICL3 in the P2Y1R construct 1 (residues K247-P253 replaced by M1-E54 of rubredoxin), combined with a D320(7.49)N mutation for improved yield and stability. This construct enabled crystallization of P2Y1R in complex with [MRS2500](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/mrs2500/) at 2.7 A resolution (PDB 4M48).
