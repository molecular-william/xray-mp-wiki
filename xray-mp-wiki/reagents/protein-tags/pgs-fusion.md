# PGS (Pyrococcus abyssi Glycogen Synthase) Fusion

## Overview

PGS (glycogen synthase from the hyperthermophilic archaeon Pyrococcus abyssi) is a fusion partner used to replace the third intracellular loop (ICL3) of GPCRs to facilitate crystallization. PGS is a 196-amino-acid protein that substitutes for flexible or disordered ICL3 regions, providing a stable crystallization interface. The OX2R-PGS fusion construct was used to determine the crystal structures of human OX2R bound to [EMPA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/empa/) (PDB 5WS3, 2.30 A; PDB 5WQC, 1.96 A). PGS was substituted for residues 255-293 in the OX2R ICL3.
