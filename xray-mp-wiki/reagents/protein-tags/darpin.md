# Designed Ankyrin Repeat Protein (DARPin)

## Overview

Designed ankyrin repeat proteins (DARPins) are engineered, non-immunoglobulin binding proteins consisting of multiple ankyrin repeat domains. They are commonly used as crystallization chaperones for membrane proteins, binding to specific epitopes and facilitating formation of well-diffracting crystal lattices. In the context of AcrB, DARPin was co-crystallized with the AcrBZ complex to improve diffraction quality.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Co-crystallization chaperone for AcrB

DARPin was co-crystallized with the AcrBZ complex from E. coli to improve crystal quality. The AcrBZ-DARPin co-crystals diffracted to 3.3 A resolution, better than the AcrBZ complex alone (3.7 A). DARPin engaged with an AcrB protomer in the crystallographic asymmetric unit. The presence of DARPin did not disrupt the AcrZ-AcrB interaction, as confirmed by unbiased difference maps.
