# Monobody S9

## Overview

Monobody S9 is an engineered protein inhibitor selected from a combinatorial library of designed ankyrin repeat proteins to bind specifically to the fluoride channel from Escherichia coli (Ec2). S9 blocks Ec2 at nanomolar concentrations and was used to crystallize the Ec2-S9 complex (PDB 5A43) at 2.6 Angstrom resolution. The Ec2-S9 structure was solved using SAD phasing with selenomethionine-labelled protein to avoid model bias. S9 binding to Ec2 confirmed that the F- binding sites identified in Bpe are conserved across the Fluc family, as two strong difference densities appeared at precisely the same locations as the F1 ions in Bpe.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [Fluoride Channel from E. coli (Ec2)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/fluc-ec2/)

S9 blocks Ec2 at nanomolar concentrations. The interaction surface of S9 wraps around a protrusion formed from the TM1-TM2 and TM3b-TM4 connecting loops of the Ec2 channel, similar to S7 and L2 binding to Bpe. S9 was used in crystallization to form Ec2-S9 complexes that diffracted to 2.6 Angstrom resolution.
