# Polyhistidine Tag (His6)

## Overview

The polyhistidine tag (His6) is a short peptide sequence of six consecutive histidine
residues fused to the N or C terminus of a recombinant protein. It enables rapid
purification via immobilized metal affinity chromatography (IMAC), most commonly using
Ni-NTA or Co-TALON resins. His6 tags are widely used in membrane protein expression
and purification workflows.


## Properties


## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Immobilized metal affinity purification

His6-tagged proteins are purified on Ni-NTA or Co-TALON affinity resins. The
histidine imidazole side chains coordinate with Ni2+ or Co2+ ions on the resin
surface. Bound protein is eluted with imidazole or by lowering pH. His6 tags
are often left in place for membrane proteins when cleavage is not required.
