# Green Fluorescent Protein (GFP)

## Overview

Green Fluorescent Protein (GFP) from the jellyfish Aequorea victoria is widely used as a fusion tag in membrane protein research. In structural biology, GFP fusions enable fluorescence-detection size-exclusion chromatography (FSEC) for rapid screening of expression, solubilization, and monodispersity without purification. The GFP tag can be fused to either the N- or C-terminus of the target protein and is typically removed by protease cleavage before crystallization. GFP-based thermal shift assays are also used to assess membrane protein stability in different detergents.
