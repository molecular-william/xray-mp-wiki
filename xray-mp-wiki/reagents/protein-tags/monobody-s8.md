# Monobody S8

## Overview

Monobody S8 is an engineered fibronectin type III domain (FN3) protein selected from diversified phage-display libraries as a crystallization chaperone for the fluoride channel (Fluc) from Bordetella pertussis (Bpe). S8 is a relatively weak channel blocker with affinity in the ~200 uM range, inducing only short-lived (~20 ms) interruptions of the otherwise always-open channel. S8 binding partially blocks fluoride current to ~10% of open-channel conductance, unlike other monobodies (e.g., L3) that completely block current. S8 was used to solve the asymmetric Bpe-S8 crystal structure (PDB 6BQO), demonstrating that monobody binding does not allosterically alter the channel structure.
