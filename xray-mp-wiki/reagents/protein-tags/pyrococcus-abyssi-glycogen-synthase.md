# Pyrococcus abyssi Glycogen Synthase (PGS)

## Overview

Pyrococcus abyssi glycogen synthase (PGS) is a 196-amino-acid protein from the hyperthermophilic archaeon P. abyssi. It was used as a novel fusion chimera for GPCR crystallography, replacing the third intracellular loop (ICL3) of the human OX2 orexin receptor. The PGS fusion enabled lipid-mediated crystallization and yielded the 2.5 A structure of hOX2R bound to suvorexant.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### hOX2R crystallization

PGS (PDB 2BFW) was substituted for residues 255-293 in the hOX2R ICL3. The fusion protein hOX2R-PGS crystallized in lipidic cubic phase and diffracted to 2.5 A resolution. This was a novel fusion approach distinct from the more common T4L or BRIL fusions used in other GPCR structures.
