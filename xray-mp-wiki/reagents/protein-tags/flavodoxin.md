# Flavodoxin Fusion Tag

## Overview

Flavodoxin is a small electron-transfer protein used as a fusion partner in protein crystallization. In the human Smoothened receptor (SMO) study, flavodoxin was not used as a fusion tag; instead the SMO multi-domain structure was determined using a specially designed tool ligand TC114 with an X-ray free-electron laser. Flavodoxin is more commonly employed as a crystallization chaperone where it can be inserted into flexible loop regions of target proteins to provide additional polar surfaces for crystal contact formation.
