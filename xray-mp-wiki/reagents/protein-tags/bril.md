# BRIL (Thermostabilized Apocytochrome b562RIL)

## Overview

BRIL (b562RIL) is a thermostabilized variant of apocytochrome b562 from
Escherichia coli. It is commonly used as a fusion protein to replace flexible
intracellular loop 3 (ICL3) in GPCRs to facilitate crystallization by providing
a rigid, well-diffracting scaffold while preserving the receptor's pharmacological
properties. In the [DRD4](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/d4-dopamine-receptor/) structure, residues 228-336 of ICL3 were replaced with
BRIL, yielding a construct with binding affinity for 3H-N-methylspiperone similar
to native [DRD4](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/d4-dopamine-receptor/).

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### DRD4 crystallization scaffold

The DRD4-BRIL fusion construct (replacing residues 228-336 of ICL3) enabled
determination of the [DRD4](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/d4-dopamine-receptor/)/nemonapride complex at 1.95 Å resolution — the
highest-resolution [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) receptor structure reported. The BRIL fusion
preserved native-like ligand binding affinity and pharmacological properties.
