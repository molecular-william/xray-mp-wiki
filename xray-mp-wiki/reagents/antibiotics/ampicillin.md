# Ampicillin

## Overview

Ampicillin is a beta-lactam antibiotic that inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis by binding to penicillin-binding proteins (PBPs) and preventing peptidoglycan cross-linking. It is a broad-spectrum antibiotic effective against Gram-positive and some Gram-negative bacteria. In Escherichia coli, ampicillin resistance is mediated by efflux pumps such as [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) and by beta-lactamase enzymes. The crystal structure of the [AcrB multidrug efflux pump](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) in complex with ampicillin revealed binding within the central cavity of the trimer.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) (Acriflavine resistance protein B)

Six ampicillin molecules were observed bound within the central cavity of the [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) trimer. Ampicillin binds to the same location as other substrates including [Nafcillin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibiotics/nafcillin/), [Ethidium - Fluorescent Intercalating Dye](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ethidium/), and [Linezolid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibiotics/linezolid/), near the A385/F386 loops on the wall of the upper portion of the central cavity. The binding site accommodates a broad range of substrate structures, consistent with the multidrug specificity of [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/).

- **Key residues**: A385, F386
