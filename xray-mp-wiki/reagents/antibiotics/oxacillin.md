# Oxacillin

## Overview

Oxacillin (OXA) is a beta-lactam antibiotic of the penicillin class, part of the isoxazolyl penicillin family. It is resistant to penicillinase (beta-lactamase) enzymes and is used primarily to treat infections caused by penicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus. In AcrB efflux pump studies, oxacillin serves as a model carboxylated beta-lactam substrate that binds to the TM1/TM2 groove and is transported via the PN2/PC1 interface pathway (CH4) towards the deep binding pocket.


## Properties

- **Molecular weight**: 401.4
- **Class**: beta-lactam antibiotic (isoxazolyl penicillin)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for AcrB efflux pump transport pathway studies

Oxacillin is used alongside fusidic acid, dicloxacillin, and piperacillin to characterize the TM1/TM2 groove binding site and CH4 transport pathway in AcrB. Mutagenesis studies showed that N298A and L300A variants in the PN2 subdomain reduce AcrB-mediated resistance to oxacillin, while the I27A hyperactive variant increases resistance.
