# Dicloxacillin

## Overview

Dicloxacillin (DCX) is a beta-lactam antibiotic of the isoxazolyl penicillin class, similar to oxacillin but with a dichlorophenyl side chain. It is penicillinase-resistant and used to treat infections caused by Gram-positive bacteria. In AcrB studies, dicloxacillin serves as a model carboxylated substrate that binds to the TM1/TM2 groove and is transported via the PN2/PC1 interface pathway (CH4).


## Properties

- **Molecular weight**: 470.3
- **Class**: beta-lactam antibiotic (isoxazolyl penicillin)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for AcrB efflux pump transport pathway studies

Dicloxacillin is used together with fusidic acid, oxacillin, and piperacillin to map the TM1/TM2 groove drug binding sites (S1, S2, S3) and the CH4 transport pathway in AcrB. Cross-linking protection assays first identified DCX as binding to the TM1/TM2 groove. The I27A variant confers hyper-resistance to DCX (MIC 1024 ug/ml vs 512 ug/ml wild-type).
