# Piperacillin

## Overview

Piperacillin (PIP) is a broad-spectrum beta-lactam antibiotic of the ureidopenicillin class, often used in combination with tazobactam (a beta-lactamase inhibitor). It targets penicillin-binding proteins (PBPs) involved in bacterial cell wall synthesis. In AcrB efflux pump studies, piperacillin serves as a model carboxylated substrate that binds to the TM1/TM2 groove and is transported via the PN2/PC1 interface pathway (CH4).


## Properties

- **Molecular weight**: 517.6
- **Class**: beta-lactam antibiotic (ureidopenicillin)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for AcrB efflux pump transport pathway studies

Piperacillin is used alongside fusidic acid, oxacillin, and dicloxacillin to study the TM1/TM2 groove binding and CH4 transport pathway in AcrB. The N298A variant in the PN2 subdomain was most severely affected for PIP resistance, while L300A showed wild-type levels of PIP resistance. The I27A variant conferred mild hyper-resistance to PIP.
