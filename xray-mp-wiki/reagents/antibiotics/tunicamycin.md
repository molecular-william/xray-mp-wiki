# Tunicamycin

## Overview

Tunicamycin is a naturally occurring antibiotic that inhibits phospho-MurNAc-pentapeptide translocase (MraY), the enzyme catalyzing the first membrane step of bacterial peptidoglycan biosynthesis. The crystal structure of CbMraY (from Clostridium boltrae) in complex with tunicamycin reveals the detailed molecular basis of MraY inhibition by tunicamycin.


## Properties


## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Antibiotic targeting bacterial cell wall biosynthesis

Tunicamycin inhibits MraY, blocking the formation of Lipid I from UDP-MurNAc-pentapeptide and undecaprenyl phosphate (C55-P), thereby preventing peptidoglycan biosynthesis and bacterial cell wall formation.


### Structural probe for MraY active site

The CbMraY-tunicamycin complex structure provides detailed insights into the MraY active site geometry and inhibitor binding, enabling structure-based design of novel MraY-targeting antibiotics.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to MraY (phospho-MurNAc-pentapeptide translocase)

Tunicamycin binds in the MraY active site, with the complex structure revealing detailed inhibitor-protein interactions including contacts with conserved residues across the MraY active site cleft. The binding mode differs from that observed in the AaMraY-muraymycin D2 complex (PDB 5CKR).
