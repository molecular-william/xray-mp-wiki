# Bis-Tris Propane Buffer

## Overview

Bis-Tris propane (1,3-bis[tris(hydroxymethyl)methylamino]propane) is a zwitterionic buffer with two pKa values (6.8 and 9.0), providing buffering capacity across a broad pH range (6.3–9.5). It is commonly used in protein crystallization.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 1,3-bis(tris(hydroxymethyl)methylamino)propane

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### GsuK crystallization buffer

100 mM Bis-Tris propane pH 8.5 was used as the buffer in the crystallization reservoir solution for the [GSUK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/gsuk/) channel, containing 20-23% [PEG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg/) 3350, 120 mM KCl, 80 mM NaNO3, and 1% [Glycerol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glycerol/).
