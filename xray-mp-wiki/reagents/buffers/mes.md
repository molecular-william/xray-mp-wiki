# 2-(N-Morpholino)ethanesulfonic Acid (MES)

## Overview

MES (2-(N-morpholino)ethanesulfonic acid) is a zwitterionic Good's buffer widely used in biochemical and structural biology applications. It has a useful pH range of 5.5-6.7 (pKa 6.15 at 25°C) and is commonly employed in protein crystallization screens and chromatography buffers for membrane protein purification. MES is particularly useful for crystallizing membrane proteins at mildly acidic to neutral pH conditions.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 2-(N-morpholino)ethanesulfonic acid
- **Class**: morpholine-based buffer

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization and purification buffer

In [Isoprenylcysteine Carboxyl Methyltransferase (ICMT) from Methanosarcina acetivorans](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/ma-icmt/) studies, MES buffer at pH 6.5 was used in the crystallization buffer (20 mM MES pH 6.5, 200 mM NaCl, 10% glycerol, 0.024% DDM) for protein concentration and crystallization setup.


### Crystallization buffer for squid rhodopsin

In the sitting-drop vapor diffusion crystallization of C-terminally truncated [squid rhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/squid-rhodopsin/), MES buffer at pH 6.4 (32 mM) was used as the crystallization buffer component along with 3.2 M ammonium sulfate, 38 mM EDTA, and 10 mM beta-mercaptoethanol. Hexagonal P62 crystals grew after >6 months at 277 K.
