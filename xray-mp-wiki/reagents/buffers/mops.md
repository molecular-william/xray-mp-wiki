# MOPS (3-(N-Morpholino)propanesulfonic Acid)

## Overview

MOPS (3-(N-morpholino)propanesulfonic acid) is a zwitterionic Good's buffer widely used in membrane protein crystallization and biochemistry. It has a pKa of 7.20 at 20 deg.C and maintains buffering capacity in the pH range 6.0-7.6. MOPS is commonly used for crystallizing membrane proteins including GPCRs and ion channels, where its zwitterionic nature provides minimal interference with protein-detergent interactions.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 3-(N-morpholino)propanesulfonic acid
- **Class**: zwitterionic Good's buffer

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### GPCR crystallization

Used as a buffer component in crystallization screens for GPCRs. Commonly used at 50-100 mM concentration for screening conditions at near-physiological pH.


### Ion channel crystallization

Buffer component for crystallization of various ion channels, providing pH stability in the near-neutral range.


### SERCA purification buffer

MOPS-KOH pH 6.8 used as primary buffer for SERCA purification from rabbit sarcoplasmic reticulum membranes at 100 mM. Also used in initial membrane wash buffer.
