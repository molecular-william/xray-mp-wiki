# Sodium Acetate

## Overview

Sodium acetate is a common buffer used in membrane protein crystallization, particularly in the pH range 4.0-5.5. It was used at 100 mM concentration in the crystallization of both the Enterococcus hirae V-ATPase K ring (NtpK10 ring) and the Ilyobacter tartaricus F-ATPase c11 ring. In both cases, sodium acetate promoted sodium ion binding, allowing visualization of Na+ bound at the ion-binding sites of these ATP synthase rotor rings.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Sodium acetate
- **Chemical formula**: CH3COONa
- **Molecular weight**: 82.03
- **Class**: Buffer

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization buffer for ATP synthase rotor rings

Sodium acetate at 100 mM was used as the crystallization buffer for both the E. hirae V-ATPase K ring and the I. tartaricus F-ATPase c11 ring. The acetate buffer provided Na+ ions that bind to the ion-binding sites of the rotor rings, allowing structural determination of the Na+-bound locked conformation state.
