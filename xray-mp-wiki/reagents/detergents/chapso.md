# CHAPSO

## Overview

CHAPSO (3-[(3-cholamidopropyl)dimethylammonio]-2-hydroxy-1-propanesulfonate) is a zwitterionic detergent derived from cholic acid. It is structurally similar to CHAPS but with a hydroxyl group on the propanesulfonate chain. CHAPSO is commonly used in bicelle mixtures with DMPC for membrane protein crystallization in a lipid-like environment.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 3-[(3-Cholamidopropyl)dimethylammonio]-2-hydroxy-1-propanesulfonate
- **Molecular weight**: 630.9 g/mol
- **Class**: Zwitterionic bile-acid-derived detergent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Bicelle-based crystallization of rhomboid protease GlpG

CHAPSO was used with DMPC at a 2.6:1 ratio to form bicelles at 20% total concentration. DMPC/CHAPSO bicelles were mixed with GlpG S201T at ~9 mg/ml and crystallized by vapor diffusion. The bicelle method yielded Type I membrane protein crystals diffracting to 1.7 Å (PDB 2XTV) with an ordered lipid annulus around GlpG.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Forms well-ordered bicelles with DMPC (optimal at 2.6:1 DMPC:CHAPSO ratio) that provide a native-like lipid bilayer environment for membrane protein crystallization (doi/10.1016##j.jmb.2011.01.029)
- Bicelle method using DMPC/CHAPSO yielded GlpG crystals diffracting to 1.7 Å (PDB 2XTV) with an ordered lipid annulus around the protein, demonstrating preservation of protein-lipid interactions (doi/10.1016##j.jmb.2011.01.029)
- Type I membrane crystal packing in DMPC/CHAPSO bicelles allows ordered lipid molecules to form a near-complete bilayer annulus, providing structural insights into protein-lipid interactions

### Disadvantages

- High cost relative to conventional detergents like DDM or OG
- Requires specialized bicelle preparation protocols and specific lipid-to-detergent ratios (typically 2.6:1 DMPC:CHAPSO), adding complexity to crystallization workflows
- Not suitable for all membrane protein targets; bicelle compatibility depends on the hydrophobic thickness and surface properties of the target protein
