# Octyl Glucose Neopentyl Glycol (OGNG)

## Overview

Octyl [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) neopentyl glycol (OGNG) is a non-ionic neopentyl glycol class detergent with two octyl (C8) hydrophobic chains linked via a neopentyl glycol spacer, each with a glucose-based headgroup. As a member of the neopentyl glycol (NG) detergent family, OGNG provides improved stability for membrane protein crystallization compared to conventional single-chain glucoside detergents like OG.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 2,2-Dioctylpropane-1,3-diyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside
- **Class**: Non-ionic neopentyl glycol detergent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein crystallization

OGNG is used as a solubilization and crystallization detergent for membrane proteins, typically at concentrations around 0.06-0.2% (w/v). Its neopentyl glycol core links two [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) headgroups and two octyl chains, providing enhanced stability compared to conventional single-chain octyl glucoside (OG). OGNG is particularly useful for crystallizing ion channels and transporters that require mild detergent conditions for structural studies.
