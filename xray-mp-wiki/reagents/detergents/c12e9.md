# C12E9 (Dodecyl Nonaglycol)

## Overview

C12E9 (dodecyl nonaethylene glycol ether, also known as Thesit or polidocanol) is a nonionic detergent with 9 ethylene oxide units and a C12 alkyl chain. It is a mild detergent suitable for solubilization and purification of membrane proteins. It has a critical micelle concentration (CMC) of approximately 0.08-0.1 mM. The long polar headgroup provides steric stabilization, making it useful for maintaining protein stability during purification. C12E9 is related to the better-known C12E8 but has one additional ethylene oxide unit, giving it slightly different micelle properties.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Dodecyl nonaethylene glycol ether
- **Molecular weight**: ~583 g/mol
- **Class**: detergent-nonionic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein solubilization and stabilization

> C12E9 (Thesit/polidocanol) is a nonionic detergent with 9 ethylene oxide units that provides steric stabilization for solubilized membrane proteins. Its long polar headgroup makes it useful for maintaining protein stability during purification. The low CMC (~0.08-0.1 mM) allows use at low detergent concentrations for crystallization experiments.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Long polar head group (9 EO units) provides excellent steric stabilization, reducing protein aggregation during purification
- Low CMC (~0.08-0.1 mM) allows effective solubilization at low concentrations, beneficial for crystallization
- Mild nonionic character minimizes protein denaturation, making it suitable for sensitive membrane proteins

### Disadvantages

- Higher cost compared to simpler nonionic detergents
- Forms relatively large micelles which may interfere with crystallization of small membrane proteins
- Cloud point effect at elevated temperatures can complicate purification workflows
