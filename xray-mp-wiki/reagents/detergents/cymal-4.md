# Cymal-4 (4-Cyclohexyl-1-butyl-beta-D-maltoside)

## Overview

Cymal-4 (4-cyclohexyl-1-butyl-beta-D-maltoside) is a short-chain nonionic detergent in the Cymal family, characterized by a cyclohexyl-modified butyl tail and a maltoside head group. It is used for solubilizing membrane proteins and is particularly effective for crystallization of membrane protein complexes. In the crystal structure of the Spirulina platensis ATP synthase c15 ring, Cymal-4 molecules bind non-specifically to the hydrophobic surfaces of the complex, with 15 molecules per ring mimicking the outer membrane leaflet.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 4-Cyclohexyl-1-butyl-beta-D-maltoside
- **Molecular weight**: 468.5 g/mol
- **Class**: detergent-nonionic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein crystallization of rotary ATPases

Cymal-4 was used as the primary detergent for crystallization of the Spirulina platensis c15 ring from proton-dependent F1Fo ATP synthase. The protein sample was prepared at 1.8 mg/mL in 2.5% (w/v) Cymal-4 solution in water, and crystals grew by vapor diffusion against a reservoir containing PEG 600, lithium sulfate, and sodium acetate buffer at pH 4.3. Cymal-4 molecules bind to hydrophobic surfaces of the c15 ring, with 15 molecules per ring mimicking the outer membrane leaflet and forming a belt around the outer perimeter.


### Detergent belt formation around membrane protein complexes

Cymal-4 forms ordered detergent belts around crystallized membrane protein complexes. On the outer hydrophobic face of the c15 ring, Cymal-4 molecules form pear-shaped densities with a minimal distance of 5.5 Å to the protein surface, covering the surface at the level of the proton-binding site at Glu62. The cyclohexyl groups of Cymal-4 interact closely with the hydrophobic surface of the c-ring, replacing the native thylakoid membrane lipids.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Short alkyl chain (cyclohexyl-butyl) provides a good balance between solubilization power and mildness for membrane protein complexes
- Forms ordered detergent belts around crystallized membrane proteins that mimic the native lipid environment, aiding crystal packing and structure solution
- Effective for crystallization of large oligomeric membrane protein assemblies such as the 15-mer c-ring of ATP synthase
- Cymal-4 molecules provide direct structural information about protein-detergent interactions in crystal structures

### Disadvantages

- High CMC (~7.6 mM) means higher concentrations are required for solubilization compared to longer-chain detergents
- Short hydrophobic tail provides less stabilization for sensitive membrane proteins compared to longer-chain alternatives like Cymal-6 or DDM
- Primarily used for crystallization; may not be ideal for initial membrane extraction from native membranes
