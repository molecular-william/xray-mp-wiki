# Dodecylphosphocholine (DPC)

## Overview

Dodecylphosphocholine ([DPC](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/dpc/)) is a zwitterionic detergent commonly used
for membrane protein solubilization and NMR studies. It forms micelles
that provide a membrane-mimetic environment suitable for solution NMR
and analytical ultracentrifugation of membrane proteins.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: n-Dodecylphosphocholine
- **Molecular weight**: 351.46

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Solubilization of Rocker for biophysical characterization

[ROCKER](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/miscellaneous/rocker/) (de novo Zn²⁺ transporter) was solubilized in [DPC](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/dpc/) micelles
for analytical ultracentrifugation (AUC) and solution NMR studies.
AUC showed [ROCKER](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/miscellaneous/rocker/) forms tetramers in a Zn²⁺-dependent manner in [DPC](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/dpc/)
micelles. Solution NMR titration with Zn²⁺ in [DPC](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/dpc/) confirmed two
Zn²⁺ binding sites per tetramer with negative cooperativity.
