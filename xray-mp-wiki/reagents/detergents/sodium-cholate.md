# Sodium Cholate

## Overview

Sodium cholate is an anionic bile salt detergent commonly used as a co-detergent in membrane protein solubilization. It is often combined with milder nonionic detergents like [DDM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/ddm/) to improve solubilization efficiency for difficult membrane proteins, particularly GPCRs. Sodium cholate helps disrupt lipid-protein and protein-protein interactions while the co-detergent maintains protein stability. After solubilization, the sodium cholate concentration is typically reduced or exchanged to milder detergents for downstream purification and crystallization.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Sodium cholate
- **Molecular weight**: 430.6 g/mol
- **Class**: detergent-ionic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### GPCR co-solubilization

Sodium cholate is used as a co-detergent with DDM for solubilizing GPCRs from insect cell membranes. In the CB1 cannabinoid receptor study, membranes were solubilized with 1% DDM, 0.2% sodium cholate, and 0.2% CHS, enabling efficient extraction while maintaining receptor integrity.


### Membrane protein purification co-detergent

Sodium cholate is included at low concentrations (0.01-0.02%) in wash and purification buffers alongside the primary detergent to help maintain protein solubility and prevent aggregation during chromatography steps.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Anionic charge provides different solubilization properties compared to nonionic detergents, aiding disruption of protein-protein and lipid-protein interactions
- Effective co-detergent that improves extraction efficiency when combined with mild detergents like DDM or LMNG
- Bile salt structure forms small micelles, useful when larger micelles from nonionic detergents interfere

### Disadvantages

- Can be denaturing at high concentrations for sensitive membrane proteins
- Relatively high CMC (~10 mM) means it can be difficult to remove completely by dialysis
- Anionic nature may interfere with certain chromatography resins or crystallization conditions
- Must be used in combination with milder detergents for most structural biology applications; not suitable as a sole detergent for crystallization
