# Sucrose Monododecanoate (SM)

## Overview

[Sucrose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/sucrose/) monododecanoate (SM) is a non-ionic detergent with a [Sucrose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/sucrose/) headgroup and a dodecyl (C12) hydrocarbon tail. It was used as the primary solubilization detergent for [ECRSEP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/ecrsep/) membrane extraction from E. coli membranes prior to purification. After solubilization, [ECRSEP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/ecrsep/) was exchanged into [DDM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/ddm/)/CHS-containing buffers for subsequent purification steps.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Sucrose monododecanoate
- **Class**: Non-ionic [Sucrose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/sucrose/) ester detergent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein solubilization for EcRseP

[ECRSEP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/ecrsep/) was solubilized from E. coli membrane fractions using 1% [Sucrose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/sucrose/) monododecanoate (SM) in 40 mM [Tris-HCl Buffer](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/tris/) pH 8.5, 150 mM NaCl. After solubilization, the detergent was exchanged to 0.03% [DDM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/ddm/) with 0.006% [CHS](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/cholesterol-hydrogen-succinate/) for [Affinity Chromatography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/purification/affinity-chromatography/) and SEC.
