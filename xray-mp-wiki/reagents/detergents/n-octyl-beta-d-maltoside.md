# n-Octyl-beta-D-maltoside (OM)

## Overview

n-Octyl-beta-D-maltoside (OM) is a nonionic detergent with an octyl (C8) alkyl chain and a maltose head group. It is structurally related to n-octyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside (OG) but with a disaccharide maltose head group instead of a monosaccharide glucose. OM is used for solubilizing membrane proteins and is particularly noted for its role in crystallizing the urea transporter from Desulfovibrio vulgaris (dvUT).


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: n-octyl beta-D-maltopyranoside
- **Chemical formula**: C20H38O12
- **Molecular weight**: 446.5
- **Class**: nonionic maltoside detergent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Solubilizing membrane proteins for crystallization

OM is used at 40 mM for solubilizing the urea transporter from Desulfovibrio vulgaris (dvUT) during gel-filtration purification. It was noted that high-purity OM yielded crystals diffracting to 4.5-5 A, while low-purity (Sol-Grade) OM enabled higher resolution crystals at 2.3 A.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Maltose head group may provide additional stability compared to glucoside detergents
- Low critical micelle concentration
- Mild solubilization properties suitable for crystallization

### Disadvantages

- Purity may significantly affect crystallization outcomes
- Lower critical micelle concentration than OG may make it harder to remove
