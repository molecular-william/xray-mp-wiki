# n-Dodecyl-N,N-dimethylamine-N-oxide (LDAO)

## Overview

Lauryldimethylamine N-oxide (LDAO) is a zwitterionic detergent with a 12-carbon hydrocarbon chain commonly used for solubilizing and crystallizing membrane proteins. In the [Rps. viridis Photosynthetic Reaction Centre](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/rps-viridis-reaction-centre/) crystal structure, LDAO was found to be the only detergent molecule well-ordered in the crystal lattice. LDAO is also widely used for functional assays and proteoliposome reconstitution studies.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: N,N-dimethyldodecylamine N-oxide
- **Chemical formula**: C14H31NO
- **Molecular weight**: 229.4 g/mol
- **Class**: detergent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Final dialysis buffer for KcsA potassium channel

[KCSA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kcsa/) from Streptomyces lividans was exchanged into 5 mM [LDAO](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/ldao/) by dialysis
following cobalt affinity and gel filtration purification. Crystals were grown
from this LDAO-containing protein solution.


### Membrane protein solubilization

Used in combination with [Nonyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/nonylglucoside/) for purification of
E. coli ecPgpB phosphatidylglycerophosphate phosphatase B for X-ray crystallography.


### KcsA potassium channel crystallization

> LDAO at 5 mM was used as the final dialysis buffer detergent for KcsA from Streptomyces lividans. Following cobalt affinity and gel filtration purification, the protein was exchanged into 5 mM LDAO by dialysis. Crystals were grown from this LDAO-containing protein solution, leading to the landmark structure of a potassium channel at 3.2 Å resolution.


### Crystallization medium for photosynthetic reaction centres

> LDAO was found to be the only well-ordered detergent molecule in the crystal lattice of the Rps. viridis photosynthetic reaction centre structure, highlighting its unique ability to form defined crystal contacts. This property makes LDAO particularly valuable for crystallizing membrane proteins that require ordered detergent belts for lattice formation.


### Membrane protein solubilization for functional assays

> LDAO is used for solubilizing membrane proteins for functional reconstitution into proteoliposomes. Its zwitterionic nature maintains protein activity while providing effective solubilization of bacterial membranes.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Zwitterionic nature provides both hydrophilic and hydrophobic interactions, enabling effective solubilization of diverse membrane proteins
- Tends to form well-ordered detergent belts in crystal lattices, aiding in structure determination and refinement
- Moderate CMC (1-2 mM) balances ease of removal with stability of protein-detergent complexes
- Compatible with a wide range of crystallization conditions and additives
- Non-denaturing for many membrane proteins, preserving functional activity during purification

### Disadvantages

- May be too harsh for some sensitive GPCRs and multi-subunit complexes compared to nonionic detergents like DDM
- Zwitterionic head group can interfere with certain biochemical assays and mass spectrometry analyses
- Higher propensity for phase separation in crystallization screens compared to nonionic detergents
- Not as extensively characterized for LCP crystallization as maltoside detergents
