# Digitonin

## Overview

Digitonin is a mild nonionic triterpene glycoside detergent extracted from the roots of plants in the genus Digitalis. It is widely used for solubilizing membrane proteins and maintaining their native structure and activity. Digitonin is particularly valued for cryo-EM sample preparation of membrane protein complexes due to its ability to produce well-dispersed, homogeneous particles.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: digitogenin pentaglycoside
- **Chemical formula**: C56H90O26
- **Molecular weight**: 1142.3 g/mol
- **Class**: nonionic triterpene glycoside

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein solubilization

Digitonin is used for gentle solubilization of membrane proteins, particularly for preserving protein-protein interactions and complex integrity. Its large hydrophobic triterpene aglycone and hydrophilic pentasaccharide head group provide mild detergency suitable for fragile membrane protein complexes.


### Cryo-EM sample preparation

Digitonin is the preferred detergent for cryo-EM studies of many membrane protein complexes. It produces well-dispersed particles with good contrast in electron micrographs and yields homogeneous populations suitable for high-resolution 3D reconstruction.


### Ion channel studies

Digitonin is commonly used for functional studies of ion channels, as it can permeabilize cell membranes without disrupting channel protein complexes, allowing intracellular access while maintaining membrane integrity.
