# Dodecanoylmorpholine (DM)

## Overview

Dodecanoylmorpholine (DM) is a nonionic surfactant commonly used as a mild detergent for solubilizing and purifying membrane proteins. It is particularly valued for preserving the native oligomeric state of membrane proteins during purification and is widely used in ion channel studies.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: dodecanoylmorpholine
- **Molecular weight**: 285.47 g/mol
- **Class**: detergent-nonionic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein solubilization and purification

DM was used to solubilize and purify the NaK nonselective cation channel from Bacillus cereus. All channel proteins used in flux assays were purified in DM detergent. The protein was reconstituted into lipid vesicles composed of [POPE](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/pope/) and [POPG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/popg/) using 10 mM DM to solubilize the lipid, with detergent removed by dialysis.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Preserves ion channel function during purification and reconstitution — NaK channels purified in DM retain activity in 86Rb flux assays
- Suitable for reconstitution into lipid vesicles — DM can be removed by dialysis after lipid solubilization at 10 mM, enabling functional studies in POPE/POPG liposomes
- Mild nonionic detergent that maintains native oligomeric state of membrane proteins

### Disadvantages

- Relatively high CMC (~1.5 mM) compared to longer-chain detergents like DDM, requiring higher concentrations for solubilization
- Limited availability and higher cost relative to standard detergents like DDM or OG
