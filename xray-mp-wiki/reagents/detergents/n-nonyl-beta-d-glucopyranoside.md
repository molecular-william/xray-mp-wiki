# n-Nonyl-beta-D-Glucopyranoside (Nonyl Glucoside)

## Overview

n-Nonyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside (nonyl glucoside, NG) is a nonionic detergent with a nonyl (C9) hydrophobic tail and a glucose head group. It was used as the primary detergent for purification and crystallization of the GlpG rhomboid protease from E. coli.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: n-Nonyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside
- **Molecular weight**: 306.4
- **Class**: Nonionic alkyl glucoside detergent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### GlpG rhomboid protease purification and crystallization

Nonyl glucoside was used as the primary detergent for purification and crystallization of GlpG, an E. coli rhomboid intramembrane protease. The protein was maintained at approximately 8 mg/ml in nonyl glucoside for crystallization trials. In the trigonal R32 crystal form (PDB 2XTU, 1.85 A resolution), 17 nonyl glucoside molecules were modelled occupying positions analogous to annular lipids.


### Mimicking substrate binding in rhomboid active site

In the GlpG S201T structure, nonyl glucoside (modelled as BNG514) was observed protruding into the active site through the gap between TM2 and TM5, partially displacing loop L5. This detergent molecule is thought to mimic the lateral diffusion and bending of a rhomboid substrate TM helix toward the active site.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- C9 alkyl chain effectively substitutes for native lipids around the rhomboid protease, preserving the global architecture of GlpG in delipidated crystals
- Well-ordered in crystal structures with 17 molecules modelled at 1.85 A resolution, occupying positions analogous to annular lipids
- Short chain length allows crystallization with bicelle methods and Type I crystal packing

### Disadvantages

- C9 chain is shorter than average E. coli inner membrane lipids (C16-C18), resulting in incomplete hydrophobic matching and higher mobility compared to lipids
- Higher CMC (~6.5 mM) relative to longer-chain detergents such as DDM (~0.17 mM), requiring higher concentrations for solubilization
- Limited hydrophobic thickness coverage may not be suitable for proteins with wider transmembrane domains
