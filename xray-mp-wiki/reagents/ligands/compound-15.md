# Cmpd-15 (Compound-15)

## Overview

Cmpd-15 is a negative allosteric modulator of the beta2-adrenergic receptor (beta2 AR), isolated from a DNA-encoded small-molecule library using purified beta2 AR protein unoccupied by any ligand. Unlike conventional beta-blockers that target the orthosteric site, Cmpd-15 binds to an intracellular allosteric pocket and represents the first allosteric beta-blocker. Cmpd-15 exhibits negative cooperativity with agonists and positive cooperativity with inverse agonists. It crosses the plasma membrane and binds to the intracellular surface of the beta2 AR, stabilizing the inactive conformation and inhibiting coupling to both G proteins and beta-arrestins.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Beta2-Adrenergic Receptor (beta2 AR)

Cmpd-15 binds to a pocket formed primarily by the cytoplasmic ends of transmembrane segments 1, 2, 6 and 7, as well as intracellular loop 1 and helix 8. The binding site is distinct from the orthosteric pocket and overlaps with the G-protein-binding interface. Cmpd-15 stabilizes TM6 in the inactive conformation, preventing the outward movement characteristic of receptor activation. The bromo-benzyl group of Cmpd-15 interacts with Phe61^1.60 and Leu64^ICL1 in ICL1. Mutations of key binding pocket residues (Thr274^6.36, Asn69^2.40, Ile72^2.43) diminish inhibitory activity of Cmpd-15 on both maximal beta-arrestin recruitment and EC50 shift for isoproterenol.

- **Key residues**: Val54, Ile58, Leu64, Ile72, Asn69, Thr274, Ala271, Leu275, Tyr326, Phe332, Phe61
