# CGS21680

## Overview

CGS21680 (2-[p-(2-carboxyethyl)phenylethylamino]-5'-N-ethylcarboxamidoadenosine) is a high-affinity selective agonist of the human adenosine A2A receptor (A2AR). It was the first highly selective ligand allowing discrimination of A2AR from A2B and A1 receptors in rat brain. The crystal structure of CGS21680-bound A2AR-GL31 at 2.6 A resolution (PDB 4UG2, 4UHR) revealed that the adenosine moiety binds identically to NECA and adenosine, while the (2-carboxyethyl)phenylethylamino extension protrudes into an extended vestibule formed by TM2, TM7, EL2, and EL3.

- **Class**: adenosine analog agonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Selective A2AR agonist for structural biology

CGS21680 was co-crystallized with the thermostabilized A2AR-GL31 construct (containing mutations L48A, A54L, T65A, Q89A) using the lipidic cubic phase method. Two crystal forms were obtained: monoclinic (P21) and orthorhombic (P212121), yielding structures at 2.6 A resolution. The orthorhombic form (PDB 4UG2) showed clear density for the extended (2-carboxyethyl)phenylethylamino group, while the monoclinic form (PDB 4UHR) showed weaker density for this region, suggesting conformational flexibility of the extracellular loops.


### Functional characterization of A2AR extracellular region

Mutagenesis of residues interacting with the CGS21680 extension (S67A, E169A, H264A, L267A) revealed that L267A caused a 24-fold reduction in CGS21680 potency, and H264A caused a 6-fold reduction. Mutations S67A, E169A, and H264A impaired basal activity of A2AR, indicating the extracellular region modulates constitutive activity.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to A2AR orthosteric binding pocket with extension into extracellular vestibule

Adenosine moiety binds identically to other A2AR agonists. The (2-carboxyethyl)phenylethylamino group makes van der Waals contacts with Glu169(EL2), His264(EL3), Leu267(7.32), and Ile274(7.39). An amine group forms a hydrogen bond with Ser67(2.65). The binding pocket narrows at the extracellular surface due to an inward tilt of TM2.

- **Key residues**: Ser67, Glu169, His264, Leu267, Ile274
