# Ivermectin

## Overview

Ivermectin is a macrocyclic anthelmintic drug from the avermectin family that acts as a positive allosteric modulator of several Cys-loop receptors including glutamate-gated chloride channels (GluCl), gamma-aminobutyric acid receptors (GABA_A Rs), glycine receptors (GlyRs), and neuronal alpha-7 nicotinic receptors (alpha-7 nAChRs). It activates invertebrate GluCl at nanomolar concentrations and vertebrate Cys-loop receptors at micromolar concentrations. Crystal structures of GlyRalpha3 in complex with ivermectin revealed its binding site in the transmembrane domain.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Glycine Receptor Alpha 3 (GlyRalpha3)

Ivermectin binds in the transmembrane domain at the interface between adjacent subunits, specifically at the plus subunit interface formed by M2, M3, and M4 helices. Key interacting residues include Ser267 on M2 and Ala288 on M3. The binding pocket is primarily hydrophobic with hydrogen bond interactions. C10 of ivermectin is 4.7 A from the C-alpha of Ala288 in GlyRalpha3. The compound is buried about 1 A shallower in GlyRalpha3 compared to GluCl, where it reaches deeper into the cleft. Binding of ivermectin stabilizes the desensitized state of the channel and expands the extracellular portion of the pore by wedging apart M3(+) and M1(-) by about 3 A and dragging M2 away from the pore axis.

- **Key residues**: Ser267 (M2), Ala288 (M3)

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Potent activator of invertebrate glutamate-gated chloride channels (nanomolar EC50)
- Structural insights into allosteric modulation of Cys-loop receptors
- Potential basis for designing CNS-penetrant analgesics targeting GlyRalpha3

### Disadvantages

- Lower potency on vertebrate Cys-loop receptors compared to invertebrate GluCl
- Narrow therapeutic window in some applications
- Limited ability to directly activate alpha7 nAChRs, 5HT3Rs, or GABA_A Rs
