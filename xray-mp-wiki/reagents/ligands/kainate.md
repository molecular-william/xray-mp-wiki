# Kainate (KA)

## Overview

Kainate (KA) is a natural marine product originally isolated from the red alga Digenea simplex. It is a potent agonist of ionotropic glutamate receptors (iGluRs), with selectivity for AMPA and kainate receptor subtypes. In the context of AMPA receptor structural biology, kainate acts as a partial agonist, inducing intermediate clamshell closure of the ligand-binding domain (LBD) compared to full agonists like glutamate. It has been used extensively in crystallographic studies of the GluA2 AMPA receptor to trap functionally relevant conformations, including pre-open and desensitized states.
