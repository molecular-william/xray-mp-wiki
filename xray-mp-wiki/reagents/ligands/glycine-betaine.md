# Glycine Betaine

## Overview

Glycine betaine (betaine) is a quaternary ammonium compound derived from the amino acid glycine. It serves as a primary compatible solute (osmolyte) in many bacteria, plants, and some animals, where it protects cells against osmotic stress by balancing internal and external osmolarity without interfering with cellular metabolism. Glycine betaine is transported into cells by dedicated secondary active transporters, notably the BCCT (Betaine/Carnitine/Choline Transporter) family of Na+-coupled symporters such as BetP from Corynebacterium glutamicum.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: glycine betaine
- **Molecular weight**: 117.15 g/mol
- **Class**: quaternary ammonium osmolyte

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Compatible solute in osmoregulation

Glycine betaine is accumulated by many microorganisms under hyperosmotic stress to maintain turgor pressure and protect cellular components. It is one of the most effective compatible solutes due to its high solubility and lack of interference with enzyme function at high concentrations.


### Substrate for BCCT family transporters

Glycine betaine is the primary substrate for Na+-coupled symporters in the BCCT family, including BetP, OpuD, and ButA. Transport is driven by the sodium electrochemical gradient across the membrane.
