# Dilazep

## Overview

Dilazep is a non-nucleoside adenosine reuptake inhibitor (AdoRI) clinically used as a vasodilator and antithrombotic drug for the treatment of cardiopathy, renal disorders, and hypertension. It comprises two trimethoxybenzoic acid groups linked to a central diazepane ring and binds to hENT1, adopting a crescent conformation.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 3-[4-[3-(3,4,5-trimethoxybenzoyl)oxypropyl]-1,4-diazepan-1-yl]propyl 3,4,5-trimethoxybenzoate

## Binding Mode

### Binding to hENT1 (orthosteric site + opportunistic site 1)

One trimethoxyphenyl ring occupies the orthosteric site, interacting with Trp29 (TM1) and Gln158 (TM4). The central diazepane ring contacts Met33 (TM1). The other trimethoxyphenyl ring occupies opportunistic site 1 at the extracellular thin gate, forming pi-pi stacking with Phe307 (TM7) and Phe334 (TM8) and H-bonding with Asn338 (TM8).
