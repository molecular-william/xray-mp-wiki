# NNC0640

## Overview

NNC0640 is a small-molecule negative allosteric modulator (NAM) that targets both the
glucagon receptor (GCGR) and the glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor (GLP-1R). It was
originally developed by Novo Nordisk and co-crystallized with GCGR (PDB 4L6R), although
the ligand was not resolved in that initial structure. The NAM binding site was later
confirmed with MK-0893. NNC0640 was subsequently co-crystallized with the GLP-1R TMD
(PDB: 5VEX, 3.0 A, Song et al., Nature 2017), revealing the same extra-helical binding
pocket between helices V-VII near the intracellular half of the receptor.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Glucagon receptor (GCGR) / GLP-1R

NNC0640 is a dual NAM targeting both GCGR and GLP-1R. At GCGR, it binds to the extra-helical allosteric site between TM6 and TM7 (PDB 4L6R), competing with MK-0893 for the same binding pocket. At GLP-1R (PDB 5VEX, 3.0 A), it binds to the common extra-helical NAM pocket between helices V, VI, and VII, involving a hydrophilic subpocket (R348, K351, N408) and hydrophobic contacts with helix V residues (I328, V332).

- **Key residues**: GCGR: Arg346, Lys349, Ser350, Asn404; GLP-1R: I328, V332, S352, T355, R348, K351, N408
