# beta-FOA (beta-Fuoxymorphamine)

## Overview

beta-Fuoxymorphamine (beta-FOA) is a morphinan agonist that differs from the antagonist [beta-FNA (beta-Funaltrexamine)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/beta-fna/) solely in having a methyl substituent at the morphinan tertiary amine. When docked into the inactive muOR structure (replacing [beta-FNA (beta-Funaltrexamine)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/beta-fna/)), molecular dynamics simulations show that beta-FOA shifts toward the active-state pose, accompanied by rearrangement of TM3 residues D147^3.32 and N150^3.35 toward their active-state positions. The W293^6.48 side chain also shifts toward its active-state rotamer within 455 ns of simulation.
