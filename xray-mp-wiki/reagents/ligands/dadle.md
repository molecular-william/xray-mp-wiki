# DADLE (D-Ala2-MePhe4-Glyol5-Enkephalin)

## Overview

DADLE (D-Ala2-MePhe4-Glyol5-enkephalin) is a selective delta-opioid receptor (δ-OR) peptide agonist derived from the endogenous enkephalin sequence. It is one of the most widely used pharmacological tools for studying δ-OR function and is characterized by strong sodium sensitivity in binding, making it an excellent probe for allosteric sodium modulation studies.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Tyr-D-Ala-Phe(Me)-Phe-Gly-OH (with Gly modified to Glyol)
- **Class**: pentapeptide opioid agonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Selective delta-opioid receptor agonist

DADLE is a selective δ-OR agonist used extensively in functional assays. It shows strong sodium sensitivity with a 10-fold decrease in affinity in the presence of 140 mM NaCl (Ki 280 nM without NaCl vs 2600 nM with NaCl at HEK293 WT δ-OR). This makes it an excellent probe for studying allosteric sodium modulation of opioid receptor function.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- High selectivity for delta-opioid receptor
- Strong sodium sensitivity makes it an excellent probe for allosteric modulation
- Well-characterized pharmacology

### Disadvantages

- Peptide nature limits cell permeability and CNS penetration
- Diminished potency at beta-arrestin recruitment in some mutant backgrounds
