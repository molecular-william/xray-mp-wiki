# Tetramethylammonium (TMA)

## Overview

Tetramethylammonium (TMA) is the smallest quaternary ammonium compound used as an open channel blocker of cation-selective ion channels. TMA has four methyl groups, making it smaller than [TEA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tetraethylammonium/) ([TEA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tetraethylammonium/)) and [TBA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tetrabutylammonium/) ([TBA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tetrabutylammonium/)). In [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/), TMA binds closer to the intracellular pore entry compared to larger homologs, demonstrating a size-dependent shift in binding position within the pore.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: tetramethylammonium
- **Molecular weight**: 74.14 g/mol
- **Class**: quaternary ammonium compound (ion channel blocker)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Open channel blocker for pore architecture studies

TMA was used in [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) crystallography at 30 mM concentration for soaking of native crystals. The smaller size of TMA compared to [TEA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tetraethylammonium/) and [TBA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tetrabutylammonium/) resulted in a binding position shifted toward the intracellular side of the pore, demonstrating the funnel-shaped architecture of the channel pore.
