# 13-cis-Retinal

## Overview

13-cis-retinal is a stereoisomer of retinal, the chromophore found in microbial rhodopsins. Unlike the 11-cis isomer found in vertebrate visual rhodopsins, 13-cis-retinal is the predominant dark-adapted form in many microbial rhodopsins including bacteriorhodopsin (BR) and sensory rhodopsin II (SRII). It is covalently bound to a conserved lysine residue on transmembrane helix G via a protonated Schiff base. In sensory rhodopsin II from Natronomonas pharaonis (NpSRII), the retinal chromophore undergoes photoisomerization from all-trans to 13-cis during the photocycle, driving proton pumping and signal transduction. The referenced paper describes the active-state structure of NpSRII.
