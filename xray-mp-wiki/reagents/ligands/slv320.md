# SLV320

## Overview

SLV320 is a selective adenosine A1 receptor antagonist with a unique pyrrolopyrimidine core and an aminocyclohexanol group. Unlike other selective A1AR antagonists that explore the hydrophobic pocket near T270(7.35), SLV320 engages the other side of the wide binding site of the A1AR, with its aminocyclohexanol group oriented toward a space occupied by the linker and irreversible moiety of DU172.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Docking study into A1AR binding pocket

SLV320 was docked into the A1AR crystal structure. The pyrrolopyrimidine core assumes a topologically similar orientation to the xanthine cores of other compounds. The aminocyclohexanol group is positioned within van der Waals distance from V87(3.32), H278(7.43), and V62(2.57), and H-bonding distance from the carbonyl of V62(2.57), instead of exploring the hydrophobic pocket near T270.
