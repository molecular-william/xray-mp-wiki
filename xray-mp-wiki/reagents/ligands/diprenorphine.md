# Diprenorphine

## Overview

Diprenorphine is a broad-spectrum opioid receptor antagonist with affinity for mu-, kappa-, and delta-opioid receptors. It is used in radioligand binding assays to characterize opioid receptor binding properties in the presence and absence of sodium ions.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 17-(cyclobutylmethyl)-4,5-alpha-epoxy-3,6-dihydroxy-6,14-ethano morphinan
- **Class**: morphinan opioid antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Opioid receptor binding study

Diprenorphine is used in radioligand displacement binding assays to study opioid receptor binding properties. At human δ-OR expressed in HEK293 cells, diprenorphine shows Ki values of 13 nM (0 NaCl) and 24 nM (140 mM NaCl), showing moderate sodium sensitivity.
