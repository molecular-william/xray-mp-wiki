# Carotenoid

## Overview

Carotenoids are lipid-soluble pigments found in photosynthetic complexes and other membrane proteins. They serve as light-harvesting pigments and photoprotective agents. In the reaction center from Rhodobacter sphaeroides 2.4.1, the carotenoid spheroidene is located near the BB bacteriochlorophyll monomer between the B and C helices of the M subunit, in a hydrophobic environment of mostly aromatic side chains. The constrained conformation contributes to a red shift in the carotenoid absorption spectrum.
