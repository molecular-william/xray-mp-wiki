# Rifampicin

## Overview

Rifampicin is a high-molecular-mass antibiotic (Mr ~823) that inhibits bacterial RNA polymerase. In studies of the [AcrB multidrug efflux pump](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/), rifampicin was shown to bind to the proximal (access) pocket of the [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) access monomer, separated from the distal pocket by the Phe-617 (F617) loop. The crystal structure of [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) bound to rifampicin revealed that rifampicin binds to the access monomer at the proximal binding pocket, with no electron density observed at the corresponding positions in the binding or extrusion monomers.

## Properties

- **Molecular weight**: 822.94
- **Class**: ansamycin antibiotic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Mechanism of action

Rifampicin inhibits bacterial DNA-dependent RNA polymerase by binding to the beta-subunit, blocking RNA chain elongation.


### AcrB binding studies

Co-crystallization with E. coli [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) revealed rifampicin bound in the proximal (access) pocket of the access monomer at the junction of three entrance channels. The naphthofuranone ring system of rifampicin is inclined approximately 60 degrees from parallel with respect to the macrolide ring of [Erythromycin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/erythromycin/).

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) proximal pocket (access monomer)

Rifampicin binds to the proximal binding pocket of the [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) access monomer. Hydrogen bonds with interacting amino acid side chains stabilize binding. The 4-methyl and 12-methyl groups of the naphthofuranone moiety overlap with the 13-ethyl and 4-methyl groups of [Erythromycin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/erythromycin/).
