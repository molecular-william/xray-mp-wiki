# Taranabant

## Overview

Taranabant is a potent, selective CB1 receptor inverse agonist used for structural studies of the human cannabinoid receptor 1. It is a pyrazole-based compound that binds to the orthosteric pocket of CB1 with high affinity. Taranabant was used to crystallize CB1 at 2.6 A resolution using lipidic cubic phase method, revealing the inactive conformation of the receptor. Taranabant has been studied in clinical trials for obesity treatment but was not approved due to adverse CNS side effects.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Cannabinoid Receptor 1 (CB1/CNR1)

Taranabant makes multiple contacts with both TM1 and TM7 of CB1. The orthosteric binding pocket is highly hydrophobic. Three polar side chains contact taranabant: Asp104 (acidic side chain points towards extracellular space), Ser123 (near the access channel, forms polar contact with trifluoromethyl group), and Ser383 (implicated in agonist binding). A large number of hydrophobic residues including six Phe, three Met, two Trp, three Leu, and three Ile side chains line the orthosteric pocket. The major divergence between CB1 and CB2 within the subset of binding residues lies in the membrane-proximal N-terminal region, where Phe102, Met103, Asp104, Ile105, and Phe108 make van der Waals contacts with taranabant.

- **Pocket volume**: 1,109 A^2 buried surface area
- **Key residues**: Asp104, Ser123, Ser383, Phe102, Met103, Ile105, Phe108, Phe170, Phe174, Leu193, Trp279, Trp356, Phe379, Leu387
