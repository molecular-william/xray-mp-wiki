# L745870

## Overview

L745870 (1,4-disubstituted aromatic piperidine/piperazine, 1,4-DAP compound) is a high-affinity DRD4-selective antagonist with Ki approximately 0.43 nM. It exhibits more than 1000-fold higher affinity towards D4 receptors compared to other dopaminergic receptors. The compound has been extensively analyzed for its pharmacology in behavioral tests and clinical trials for treating schizophrenia. The crystal structure of mouse DRD4 complexed with L745870 reveals a 4-chlorophenyl group inserted into a DRD4-specific crevice between transmembrane helices 2 and 3.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: L-745,870
- **Molecular weight**: ~380 g/mol
- **Class**: DRD4-selective antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### DRD4 subtype-selective antagonist

L745870 is used as a pharmacological tool to selectively block DRD4
over DRD2 and DRD3. The compound binds to the orthosteric pocket with
its protonated piperazine forming a hydrogen bond to D112(3.32),
while a 4-chlorophenyl group extends into a DRD4-specific secondary
binding pocket between TM2 and TM3. This extended binding mode underlies
its subtype selectivity within the D2-like dopamine receptor family.


### Crystallographic chaperone for DRD4 structure determination

L745870 was used to stabilize the receptor for LCP crystallization of
mouse DRD4 at 3.5-angstrom resolution (PDB 6IQL). The antagonist was
present throughout purification at 30 uM concentration and during
crystallization to maintain receptor stability.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to DRD4 (Dopamine Receptor D4)

Protonated piperazine hydrogen bonds D112(3.32); 4-chlorophenyl group fits into TM2-TM3 crevice; heterocyclic ring occupies orthosteric pocket surrounded by V113, F330, F331, C116, W327
