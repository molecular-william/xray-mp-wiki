# Ubiquinone-2

## Overview

Ubiquinone-2 (Q-2, Coenzyme Q-2) is a short-chain ubiquinone homolog with
two isoprenoid units in its side chain. It is widely used as a QB-site
electron acceptor ligand in studies of the bacterial photosynthetic
reaction center from Rhodobacter sphaeroides. Ubiquinone-2 is more soluble
than longer-chain ubiquinones and is used to replenish the QB binding site
lost during RC purification. In crystallographic studies, RC crystals are
soaked in 1 mM ubiquinone-2 to increase QB occupancy before data
collection and light-activation experiments.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Higher solubility than longer-chain ubiquinones (ubiquinone-10)
- Readily occupies the QB binding site in bacterial RCs
- Restores electron transfer activity to QB-depleted RC preparations
