# Rostafuroxin

## Overview

Rostafuroxin (ROS) is a novel cardiotonic steroid (CTS) developed as a potential antihypertensive agent that antagonizes the long-term pressor effect of ouabain in adducin-dependent hypertension (PST2238). Unlike classical cardiotonic steroids, rostafuroxin has a simple steroid core lacking a lactone ring at C17 — instead, it features a carbonyl group at C6 and a hydroxyl at C14. Crystal structures of the Na+,K+-ATPase E2P state with bound rostafuroxin at ~3.5 A resolution reveal that ROS binds more superficially than other CTSs. ROS shows a notably higher affinity under turnover conditions than in E2P^Pi·Mg2+, in marked contrast to ouabain.

## Properties


## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Na+,K+-ATPase inhibition

Rostafuroxin inhibits Na+,K+-ATPase by binding to the E2P state. It shows a unique profile: higher affinity under turnover conditions than in E2P^Pi·Mg2+, opposite to ouabain. The K+ antagonism is almost absent for ROS at 100 mM K+, unlike ouabain (13x) and digoxin (10x). The carbonyl group at C6 forms a hydrogen bond with Gln119 on M2, and the C14 hydroxyl bonds with Thr797 on M6, tethering the steroid core to the immobile part of the ATPase rather than to M5/M6.


### Antihypertensive agent

Rostafuroxin (previously known as PST2238) was developed as an orally active antihypertensive compound that antagonizes the long-term pressor effect of ouabain. It shows potential for treating adducin-dependent hypertension by correcting renal and vascular Na+,K+-ATPase alterations.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Na+,K+-ATPase alpha subunit (E2P state)

Rostafuroxin binds in the preformed extracellular cavity of the E2P state of Na+,K+-ATPase. Unlike classical CTSs, ROS lacks a lactone ring at C17. The carbonyl group at C6 forms a hydrogen bond with Gln119 (M2). The hydroxyl at C14 hydrogen bonds with Thr797 (M6), tethering the steroid core to the immobile M5/M6 segment. The simple steroid core without bulky sugar moieties allows ROS to bind more superficially. Due to its interaction with Gln119 on M2, dissociation of ROS may be accelerated in the transition to E2-Pi·2K+ since M1 and M2 move while M5 and M6 do not.

- **Key residues**: Gln119 (M2), Thr797 (M6), Phe783 (M5), Phe786 (M5)
