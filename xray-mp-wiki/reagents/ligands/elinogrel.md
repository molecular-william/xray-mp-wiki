# Elinogrel

## Overview

Elinogrel is a reversible antagonist of the P2Y12 receptor that was studied in functional assays alongside selatogrel and ticagrelor. While it demonstrated inverse agonist efficacy at the P2Y12 receptor in BRET assays, it also showed significant off-target effects by affecting multiple G-protein subtypes (Gi, Go, Gq, and Gs) in HEK293T cells. These broad effects limited its therapeutic window, and bleeding requiring medical attention was more common in patients receiving elinogrel in clinical trials. Elinogrel was subsequently excluded from further profiling.


## Properties

- **Class**: Reversible P2Y12 receptor antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Inverse agonist comparison in P2Y12 functional assays

Elinogrel was tested in BRET2 assays and showed inverse agonist efficacy at the P2Y12 receptor by increasing the BRET signal in cells co-expressing P2Y12 and G alpha-RLuc8/G beta gamma-GFP2 probes. However, it also affected Gi, Go, Gq, and Gs-protein signaling in HEK293T cells, suggesting off-target activity. Due to the broad effect on multiple G-protein subtypes, the identity of involved GPCRs could not be established. Clinical profiling reported that bleeding requiring medical attention was more common with elinogrel, and these off-target activities likely limited its therapeutic window.
