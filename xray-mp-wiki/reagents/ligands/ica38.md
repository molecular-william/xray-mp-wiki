# ICA38

## Overview

ICA38 is an indolcarboxamide compound that targets MmpL3, an essential mycobacterial membrane transporter. Indolcarboxamides have entered clinical trials or preclinical studies as anti-tuberculosis agents. ICA38 binds in the MmpL3 transmembrane binding pocket with a binding mode similar to SQ109 and AU1235, disrupting the conserved Asp-Tyr pairs involved in proton translocation.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to MmpL3 (Mycobacterium smegmatis)

ICA38 binds in the transmembrane region with similar positioning to SQ109 and AU1235. The bulky indole group fits into the hydrophobic subsite on top of the channel. The amide group hydrogen bonds to Asp645, and the carbonyl oxygen accepts a non-canonical hydrogen bond from Tyr646. The carbocyclic spiro group inserts into the bulky hydrophobic subsite at the bottom of the tunnel, producing more extensive hydrophobic interactions compared with the adamantine group in SQ109 and AU1235.

- **Pocket volume**: not reported
- **Key residues**: Ile249, Ile253, Ser293, Ile297, Leu642, Gly641, Ile319, Ala637, Val638, Leu686, Asp645, Phe260, Phe649, Tyr257, Tyr646
