# Verapamil

## Overview

Verapamil is a phenylalkylamine calcium channel blocker and one of the earliest and most well-known substrates of the multidrug efflux transporter P-glycoprotein (P-gp). It stimulates the ATPase activity of P-gp, serving as a functional readout for P-gp drug transport activity. In the C. elegans P-gp study, verapamil was identified as one of the best substrates of P-gp based on its ability to stimulate P-gp ATPase activity in detergent-solubilized protein. Verapamil is also a potent inhibitor of L-type voltage-gated calcium channels, binding to a pore-blocking site on the intracellular side of the selectivity filter in a state-dependent manner.
