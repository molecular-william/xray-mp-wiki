# Doxepin

## Overview

Doxepin is a first-generation H1 receptor antagonist (antihistamine) belonging to the dibenzoxepin class of tricyclic compounds. It was co-crystallized with the human histamine H1 receptor ([H1R](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-histamine-h1-receptor/)) at 3.1 A resolution (PDB 3RZE). Doxepin binds deep in the [H1R](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-histamine-h1-receptor/) ligand-binding pocket, approximately 5 A deeper than ligands in other non-rhodopsin GPCR structures, making extensive hydrophobic interactions with Trp428^6.48 of the conserved CWxP motif. The tricyclic dibenzo[b,e]oxepin ring of the E isomer sits in a hydrophobic pocket defined by helices III, V and VI, while the amine moiety forms an anchor salt bridge with Asp107^3.32.
