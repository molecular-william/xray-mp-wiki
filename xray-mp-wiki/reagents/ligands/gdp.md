# Guanosine Diphosphate (GDP)

## Overview

Guanosine diphosphate (GDP) is a nucleotide that binds to the G alpha subunit of heterotrimeric G proteins. In the context of GPCR structural biology, GDP-bound G proteins are used to form stable receptor-G protein complexes for X-ray crystallography. The presence of GDP in the mini-Gs structure reflects the properties of the engineered G protein, which after complex formation is insensitive to GTP-gamma-mediated dissociation.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Guanosine-5'-(dihydrogen diphosphate)
- **Chemical formula**: C10H15N5O11P2
- **Molecular weight**: 438.20 g/mol
- **Class**: nucleotide

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### G protein complex stabilization for GPCR crystallography

GDP is co-purified with mini-Gs (an engineered G alpha s variant) to form stable complexes with activated GPCRs. In the A2AR-mini-Gs complex (Nature 2016), GDP is bound to mini-Gs and contributes to the overall structure determination at 3.4 A resolution. The GDP-bound state represents the active conformation of the G protein, consistent with its ability to couple to the activated receptor.
