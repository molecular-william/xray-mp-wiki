# Lysergamide (LSA)

## Overview

Lysergamide (LSA, ergine) is the unsubstituted ergoline amide, lacking the diethylamide substituent of LSD. In the crystal structure study of LSD-bound 5-HT2B receptor, LSA was used as a functional control alongside SSAz and RRAz to investigate the role of the diethylamide moiety in LSD pharmacology. LSA showed much reduced potency for β-arrestin2 recruitment at both 5-HT2B and 5-HT2A receptors compared to LSD and SSAz, with functional differences attributed to the absence of the amide substituent that engages in hydrophobic contacts with the receptor binding pocket. LSA is also a naturally occurring ergot alkaloid with psychoactive properties, though considerably less potent than LSD.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: (8β)-6-methyl-9,10-didehydroergoline-8-carboxamide
- **Class**: ergot alkaloid; ergoline derivative

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Molecular tool compound

LSA was used as a negative control to demonstrate the functional importance of the diethylamide conformation of LSD at serotonin 5-HT2 receptors.
