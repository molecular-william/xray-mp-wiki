# Selenocyanobarbital

## Overview

Selenocyanobarbital is a synthetic barbiturate derivative containing a selenium atom that provides a strong anomalous scattering signal for X-ray crystallography. It was chemically synthesized for this study in a five-step sequence starting from dimethyl 2-ethylmalonate, including alkylation, [UREA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/substrates/urea/) condensation, hydrolysis, iodination, and reaction with potassium selenocyanate. Co-crystallization with the locally-closed [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) mutant (PDB 5L47, 2.99 A resolution) revealed the barbiturate bound in the central ion channel pore. Selenocyanobarbital forms three hydrogen bonds with 6' serines and is found in a symmetric, disordered binding pose.
