# Adenosine Diphosphate (ADP)

## Overview

Adenosine diphosphate (ADP) is a nucleotide that serves as the endogenous agonist for P2Y12 receptors, a family of purinergic G-protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) expressed on platelets. ADP binding to P2Y12R mediates platelet activation and aggregation, making it a critical target for antithrombotic drug development. ADP is also commonly used as a substrate analog for ATP in crystallization studies of ATP-binding proteins and ATPases.


## Properties

- **Class**: Purinergic receptor agonist; endogenous ligand for P2Y12R

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### P2Y12 receptor agonist in binding assays

ADP is the endogenous agonist of P2Y12R with Ki = 332 nM in wild-type receptors. In the P2Y12R-[AZD1283](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/azd1283/) crystal structure, ADP docking studies suggest that one nucleotide may bind in pocket 1 (formed by helices III-VII) while the second half of a dinucleotide molecule wraps around helix III to reach pocket 2, with the polyphosphate moiety occupying a highly cationic region of the binding site.
