# SR142948

## Overview

SR142948 is a non-peptide neurotensin receptor antagonist. In the structural
study of directed evolution-selected NTR1 variants, it was used in ligand-binding
assays to characterize antagonist affinity. The TM86V-ΔIC3A construct showed
an IC50 of 30 ± 2.4 nM for SR142948 (compared to 8.4 ± 0.9 nM for wild-type
NTR1), with the moderate increase attributed to the F358V mutation at a residue
specifically involved in antagonist (but not agonist) binding.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### NTR1 antagonist binding characterization

SR142948 was used in competition binding assays on whole E. coli cells expressing evolved NTR1 variants to measure antagonist affinity. Unlike the agonist neurotensin, SR142948 had never been used as a selection ligand during directed evolution, making it an independent validation of receptor functionality.
