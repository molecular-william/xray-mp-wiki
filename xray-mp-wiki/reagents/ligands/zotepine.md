# Zotepine

## Overview

Zotepine is a second-generation antipsychotic drug with a dibenzothiepine scaffold. It targets both the [Serotonin (5-Hydroxytryptamine, 5-HT)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/serotonin/) 5-HT2A receptor and [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) D2 receptor (D2R), and is used for the treatment of schizophrenia and bipolar disorder. The crystal structure of human 5-HT2A receptor in complex with zotepine (PDB 6A94, 2.9 A resolution) reveals that zotepine stabilizes the inactive receptor conformation by forming direct contacts with residues in the PIF motif at the bottom of the ligand-binding pocket.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human 5-HT2A Receptor

Zotepine binds in the orthosteric pocket of 5-HT2A with its tricyclic dibenzothiepine core. The benzene ring of zotepine extends into the bottom hydrophobic cleft, contacting PIF motif residues Ile163^3.40, Phe332^6.44, and Trp336^6.48. The protonated amine forms a salt bridge with Asp155^3.32. The side chain of Leu229^45.52 on ECL2 interacts with zotepine.

- **Key residues**: Asp155^3.32, Ile163^3.40, Phe332^6.44, Trp336^6.48, Leu229^45.52
