# 2-Methylthio-ATP (2MeSATP)

## Overview

2-Methylthio-adenosine 5'-triphosphate (2MeSATP) is a stable analog of adenosine triphosphate (ATP) that acts as a potential partial agonist at P2Y12 receptors. It has a thiomethyl group at the 2-position of the adenine ring and a triphosphate moiety, in contrast to the diphosphate of 2MeSADP. The co-crystal structure of P2Y12R with 2MeSATP at 3.1 A resolution shows a similar binding mode to 2MeSADP.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 2-methylthio-adenosine 5'-triphosphate
- **Class**: P2Y12 receptor partial agonist; ATP analog

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### P2Y12 receptor crystallization

2MeSATP was co-crystallized with the P2Y12R-BRIL fusion protein at 3.1 A resolution. The structure reveals a similar conformation to the 2MeSADP-bound complex, suggesting ATP derivatives may qualify for similar signaling properties at P2Y12R as ADP derivatives. Partial occupancy for the gamma-phosphate group was noted, possibly due to hydrolysis of 2MeSATP to 2MeSADP.
