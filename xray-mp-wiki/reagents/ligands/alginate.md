# Alginate

## Overview

Alginate is an acidic polysaccharide produced by brown seaweeds and certain bacteria, composed of two types of uronate residues: beta-D-mannuronate (M) and alpha-L-guluronate (G). It serves as the natural substrate for the [AlgM1M2SS Alginate ABC Transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/algm1m2ss/) ABC transporter from Sphingomonas sp. A1, which imports alginate across the cytoplasmic membrane for degradation and metabolic utilization. The transporter specifically recognizes oligoalginate chains of 3-7 sugar residues, with preference for M-rich blocks.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: (1->4)-beta-D-mannuronate-(1->4)-alpha-L-guluronate copolymer
- **Class**: acidic polysaccharide (uronate polymer)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for ABC transporter AlgM1M2SS

Alginate is the natural substrate of the [AlgM1M2SS Alginate ABC Transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/algm1m2ss/) ABC importer from Sphingomonas sp. A1. The transporter specifically recognizes and transports oligoalginate chains of 3-7 sugar residues (trisaccharides to heptasaccharides). Poly- and oligoalginate enhance the ATPase activity of the reconstituted transporter. Unsaturated alginate trisaccharides (Delta-MMM and Delta-GGG) and saturated variants (MMM and GGG) were used in functional assays. The periplasmic binding protein AlgQ2 binds alginate with recognition of at least four subsites at the non-reducing end.


### Crystallization ligand for AlgM1M2SS/AlgQ2 complex

Unsaturated alginate trisaccharide (Delta-MMM) was added to the crystallization drop at 1 mM concentration. A tetrasaccharide density (Delta-MMMM) was observed in the AlgQ2 binding cleft, likely formed from trisaccharide extension during crystallization. The binding tunnel in the AlgQ2/AlgM1M2SS complex was simulated with hexadecamannuronate substrate.
