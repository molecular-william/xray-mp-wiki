# Carboxymycobactin (Fe-cMBT)

## Overview

Carboxymycobactin (cMBT) is a water-soluble siderophore produced by mycobacteria
for iron acquisition. Unlike mycobactin (MBT), carboxymycobactin lacks the long
alkyl chain that anchors MBT to the membrane, making it freely diffusible in
aqueous environments. Fe-cMBT is imported across the inner mycobacterial membrane
by the ABC transporter IrtAB and can also be imported via alternative
transporters. Upon import, Fe-cMBT can diffuse into the cytoplasm where it is
reduced by ferric reductases.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Carboxymycobactin import by IrtAB

Fe(III)-carboxymycobactin is imported across the inner mycobacterial membrane
by IrtAB in an ATP-dependent manner. IrtAB acts as a high-affinity importer
for Fe-cMBT, with maximal ATPase stimulation at ~5 µM Fe-cMBT. An alternative
import pathway for Fe-cMBT also exists in mycobacteria.
