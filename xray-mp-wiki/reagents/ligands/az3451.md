# AZ3451 (PAR2 Antagonist)

## Overview

AZ3451 is a potent small-molecule antagonist of the human protease-activated receptor 2 (PAR2). The chemotype was identified by screening DNA-encoded libraries on purified PAR2-StaR and further optimized. AZ3451 binds to a remote allosteric site outside the helical bundle of PAR2, distinct from the AZ8838 binding pocket. It shows much higher potency than AZ8838 in functional assays, with sub-nanomolar IC50 values in IP-One and FLIPR assays. The structure of the PAR2-AZ3451 complex was solved at 3.59 A resolution.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human PAR2

AZ3451 binds to a remote allosteric site outside the transmembrane helical bundle of PAR2. The exact residue-level interactions are not detailed in the crystal structure at 3.59 A resolution, but the binding site is distinct from the AZ8838 pocket and the orthosteric tethered ligand binding site.

- **Pocket volume**: remote allosteric site outside helical bundle
