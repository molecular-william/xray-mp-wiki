# ABI-PP (tert-butyl thiazolyl aminocarboxyl pyridopyrimidine)

## Overview

ABI-PP is a pyridopyrimidine derivative that acts as a specific inhibitor of the RND-family multidrug efflux pumps [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) (Escherichia coli) and [MEXB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mexb/) (Pseudomonas aeruginosa). It does not inhibit [MexY (Pseudomonas aeruginosa multidrug exporter)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mexY/), another RND efflux pump in P. aeruginosa. ABI-PP binds tightly to a hydrophobic trap that branches off from the substrate-translocation channel in the distal drug-binding pocket of [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) and [MEXB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mexb/). By occupying this trap, ABI-PP sterically hinders the functional rotation mechanism of the transporters, thereby potentiating the activity of all antibiotics exported by [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/)/MexB. Unlike the PA-beta-N class of inhibitors, ABI-PP does not cause membrane dysfunction and has almost no antibiotic activity on its own.


## Properties

- **Class**: pyridopyrimidine derivative

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### RND efflux pump inhibition

ABI-PP binds to the hydrophobic trap in the distal drug-binding pocket of [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) and [MEXB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mexb/), sterically hindering the functional rotation mechanism. The TAP moiety (tert-butyl thiazolyl aminocarboxyl pyridopyrimidine) inserts into the narrow hydrophobic trap surrounded by phenylalanine residues (F136, F178, F573, F610, F615, F628 in [MEXB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mexb/)). The hydrophilic tetrazole ring interacts with D274, R620, and K151. ABI-PP is not exported by [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/)/MexB and thus potentiates the activities of all antibiotics exported by these pumps, including both distal-binding and proximal-binding drugs.
