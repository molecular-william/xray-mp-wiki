# Diacetylchitobiose (GlcNAc2)

## Overview

Diacetylchitobiose, also known as (GlcNAc)2 or chitobiose, is a disaccharide consisting of two N-acetylglucosamine (GlcNAc) units linked by a beta-1,4 glycosidic bond. It is the core structure of N-linked glycans and serves as the natural substrate for the ChbC diacetylchitobiose transporter.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: N,N'-diacetylchitobiose
- **Chemical formula**: C14H25NO11
- **Molecular weight**: 423.35 g/mol
- **Class**: disaccharide (chitin oligosaccharide)

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [bcChbC (Bacillus cereus Chitobiose Transporter)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/bc-chbc/)

The non-reducing GlcNAc unit interacts with conserved residues His250 (TM5) and Glu334 (TM9) through hydrogen bonds with the C6-OH group. Additional hydrogen bonds and hydrophobic interactions stabilize both sugar units in the binding pocket. The C6-OH of the non-reducing sugar is oriented toward the cytoplasmic side, consistent with the proposed transport direction.

- **Pocket volume**: The sugar-binding cavity lies near the cytoplasmic surface of the protein, within the C-terminal substrate-binding domain (TM6-TM10 and helical protrusions HP1 and HP2).
