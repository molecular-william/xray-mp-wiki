# Copper(I) Ion

## Overview

Copper(I) (Cu+) is a monovalent copper ion that serves as a substrate for the CusABC efflux system in Escherichia coli. Copper is required in trace amounts for bacterial growth but is highly toxic even at low concentrations. The [CusB Membrane Fusion Protein](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/cusB/) membrane fusion protein contains multiple Cu(I) binding sites, each coordinated by one methionine residue. The CusB-Cu(I) crystal structure (PDB 2L9Q) revealed four Cu+ binding sites in the asymmetric unit, representing the first structure of any membrane fusion protein solved in complex with its ligand.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [CusB Membrane Fusion Protein](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/cusB/) (Membrane Fusion Protein)

Four Cu+ binding sites identified in the asymmetric unit: C1 and C2 in molecule A, C1' and C2' in molecule B. Site C1 (Domain 1, N/C-termini region) coordinated by M324, F358, R368. Site C2 (Domain 4, three-helix bundle) coordinated by M190, W158, Q162. Each binding site contains one methionine residue. This binding mode is similar to peptidylglycine monooxygenase and [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) beta-monooxygenase.

- **Key residues**: M324, F358, R368 (site C1); M190, W158, Q162 (site C2)
