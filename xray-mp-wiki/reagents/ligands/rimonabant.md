# Rimonabant

## Overview

Rimonabant is a cannabinoid CB1 receptor antagonist that was approved by the European Medical Agency for treating obesity. It was discovered as an unexpected MmpL3 inhibitor through virtual screening. Rimonabant binds to the same pocket as SQ109, AU1235, and ICA38 but in a distinct binding mode. It inhibits M. tuberculosis growth with an MIC of 54 uM. The compound targets the mycobacterial MmpL3 transporter in a manner completely different from its interactions with the human CB1 receptor, suggesting potential for developing mycobacteria-specific CB1 analogs.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to MmpL3 (Mycobacterium smegmatis)

Rimonabant binds at the heart of the MmpL3 transmembrane domain in a manner distinct from SQ109, AU1235, and ICA38. The pyrazole ring core (including 4-methyl group) inserts at the center of the TMH bundle, perpendicular to the membrane, interacting with Gly641, Leu642, and Ile253. The 2,4-dichlorophenyl ring (Arm 1) occupies a hydrophobic pocket. The 4-chlorophenyl ring (Arm 2) protrudes into a neighboring hydrophobic subsite formed by Val245, Ile319, Val638, and Leu708. The bulky pi-peridinyl-1-carbamoyl group (Arm 3) thrusts into the heart of the two Asp-Tyr pairs, breaking their hydrogen bonds through polar interactions with Asp645. Phe260 remains undisturbed while Phe649 rotates downward to form pi-pi interactions with Phe260.

- **Pocket volume**: 305 A^3
- **Key residues**: Gly641, Leu642, Ile253, Val245, Ile319, Val638, Leu708, Asp645, Phe260, Phe649
