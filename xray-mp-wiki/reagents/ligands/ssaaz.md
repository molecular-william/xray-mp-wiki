# SSAz ((S,S)-Azetidide)

## Overview

SSAz ((S,S)-Azetidide) is a sterically constrained LSD analogue in which the diethylamide moiety of LSD is replaced with a rigid azetidine ring in the (S,S) configuration. The azetidine ring constrains the amide substituent to a conformation more closely resembling the diethylamide conformation observed in the LSD-bound 5-HT2B receptor crystal structure (PDB 5TVN). Functional studies demonstrated that SSAz and LSD have nearly identical efficacies and potencies at both 5-HT2B and 5-HT2A receptors, including comparable potencies for β-arrestin2 recruitment and Gq-mediated calcium flux. The compound was used as a molecular tool to establish that the specific diethylamide conformation observed in the crystal structure is critical for LSD's functional properties.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: (8β)-N-[(2S)-azetidin-2-ylmethyl]-6-methyl-9,10-didehydroergoline-8-carboxamide
- **Class**: LSD analogue; ergoline derivative

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Molecular tool for studying LSD pharmacology

SSAz was used to investigate the functional significance of LSD's diethylamide conformation at 5-HT2 receptors. Its near-identical functional profile to LSD confirmed that the receptor-bound conformation is critical for activity.
