# Rimantadine

## Overview

Rimantadine is a racemic adamantyl-amine antiviral drug that inhibits the M2 proton channel of influenza A virus. Like [Amantadine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/amantadine/), it features an adamantane cage and a primary ammonium group. The R and S enantiomers have equal potency in vitro and are bound as an equimolar mixture in the crystal structure. Rimantadine binds to both Inward_closed and Inward_open conformational states of M2, enabling pH-independent channel inhibition.


## Properties

- **Class**: adamantyl-amine

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Influenza A antiviral therapy

Rimantadine is an adamantyl-amine class drug structurally similar to [Amantadine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/amantadine/). It targets the M2 proton channel of influenza A virus, where the hydrophobic adamantane cage binds in a hydrophobic pocket and the ammonium group engages water networks in the channel pore. The drug binds to both Inward_closed and Inward_open states of M2, providing pH-independent inhibition of proton conductance.
