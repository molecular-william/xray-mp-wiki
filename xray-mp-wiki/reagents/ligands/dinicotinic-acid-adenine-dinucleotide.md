# Dinicotinic Acid Adenine Dinucleotide (DaAD)

## Overview

Dinicotinic acid adenine dinucleotide (DaAD) is a reaction intermediate in the nickel-pincer nucleotide (NPN) cofactor biosynthesis pathway. DaAD is formed by C5 carboxylation of [Nicotinic Acid Adenine Dinucleotide (NaAD)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/nicotinic-acid-adenine-dinucleotide/) by the [LarB (Lactiplantibacillus plantarum)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/larB/) enzyme. It exhibits thermal instability and was identified by mass spectrometry as a transient intermediate that diminishes at later time points as it is converted to the final product [P2CMN](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/pyridinium-3-5-biscarboxylic-acid-mononucleotide/).

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### LarB Reaction Intermediate

DaAD is the C5-carboxylated intermediate in the LarB-catalyzed reaction. It was
identified by LC-ESI-MS analysis comparing wild-type and S127A [LarB (Lactiplantibacillus plantarum)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/larB/) variants. The
S127A variant, which exhibits reduced phosphoanhydride hydrolysis activity, allowed
accumulation of DaAD to detectable levels (~20-fold greater than WT). DaAD was not
detected with heat quenching, confirming its thermal instability.
