# Sucrose

## Overview

Sucrose is a non-reducing disaccharide composed of glucose and fructose units linked by an alpha-1,2-glycosidic bond. It serves as a substrate for sugar transporters, particularly SemiSWEET family members, and can also be used as a crystallization additive or ligand.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for sugar transporters

Sucrose is the natural substrate for the SemiSWEET family of bacterial sugar transporters, particularly BjSemiSWEET1 from Bradyrhizobium japonicum. In crystallization experiments, sucrose was used at 20 mM concentration to capture the transporter in a ligand-bound state, with electron density observed in the central pocket of TySemiSWEET consistent with a disaccharide molecule.


### Crystallization ligand

Sucrose can be included in crystallization conditions to promote ligand-bound conformations of sugar transporters. In the case of TySemiSWEET, 20 mM sucrose in the crystallization conditions led to visible electron density in the central pocket, allowing tentative building of a sucrose molecule.


### Cryoprotectant for squid rhodopsin crystals

Sucrose at 20% (w/v) was used as a cryoprotectant for hexagonal P62 crystals of C-terminally truncated [squid rhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/squid-rhodopsin/). Crystals were soaked for 10 min in cryoprotectant solution and flash-frozen in liquid propane at 100 K for X-ray diffraction data collection.
