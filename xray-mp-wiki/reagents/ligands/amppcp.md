# Adenylyl Methylenediphosphonate (AMP-PCP / AMPPCP)

## Overview

Adenylyl methylenediphosphonate (AMP-PCP or AMPPCP) is a non-hydrolyzable ATP analog in which the oxygen atom bridging the β- and γ-phosphates is replaced by a methylene (−CH₂−) group. It is used to trap ATPases in the ATP-bound state without catalysis. In SERCA2b and SERCA2a structures, AMPPCP was used to stabilize the E1-2Ca²⁺ state.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Stabilization of SERCA2b and SERCA2a in E1-2Ca²⁺ state

AMPPCP was used to occupy the ATP binding site in SERCA2b and SERCA2a crystals, locking the proteins in the E1-2Ca²⁺ conformation. The non-hydrolyzable nature of AMPPCP prevents phosphoryl transfer, allowing structural characterization of the pre-phosphorylation state.
