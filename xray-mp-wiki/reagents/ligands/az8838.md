# AZ8838 (PAR2 Antagonist)

## Overview

AZ8838 is a selective small-molecule antagonist of the human protease-activated receptor 2 (PAR2). It binds in a fully occluded pocket near the extracellular surface of PAR2, lined by residues from TM1-3, TM7 and ECL2. AZ8838 exhibits slow binding kinetics with a low on-rate (1,021 M-1 s-1) and slow off-rate (residence time 1.4 h at 30 C), making it an attractive PAR2 antagonist for competing against the tethered ligand. The R-isomer is completely inactive, indicating chiral selectivity. AZ8838 is selective for PAR2 over PAR1 due to His135 2.64 in PAR2 (replaced by Tyr162 in PAR1, which causes steric clash).

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human PAR2

AZ8838 is completely buried in a small binding pocket near the extracellular surface. The imidazole makes two hydrogen bonds to D228ECL2 and Y82 1.39. The hydroxyl group forms a hydrogen bond to His135 2.64. The fluorophenyl ring is near TM3 and makes pi-stacking interactions with H227ECL2 and the charged lysine of K131 2.60. The propyl tail sits under the C148 3.25-C226ECL2 disulfide bond in a hydrophobic pocket lined by K131 2.60, Y134 2.63, W141ECL1 and L151 3.28.

- **Pocket volume**: fully occluded pocket
