# Compound 1

## Overview

Compound 1 (Cpd 1) is a quinazolinone-biphenyltetrazole derivative that acts as a highly selective antagonist of the angiotensin II type 2 receptor (AT2R). It exhibits approximately 530-fold selectivity for AT2R over AT1R, with Ki values of 0.34 nM for AT2R and 180 nM for AT1R. The compound was used in co-crystallography studies of AT2R to reveal the structural basis for ligand selectivity between AT1R and AT2R.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Angiotensin II Type 2 Receptor (AT2R)

Binds in the orthosteric ligand-binding pocket of AT2R. The tetrazole moiety forms hydrogen bonds with Arg182^ECL2, Lys215^5.42, Thr125^3.33, and Thr178^4.60. The thiophene ring interacts hydrophobically with Trp100^2.60. The biphenyl scaffold is rotated ~45 degrees compared to AT1R-bound ligands. Weak binding to AT1R (~530-fold lower affinity) due to distinct shape of AT1R ligand-binding pocket.
