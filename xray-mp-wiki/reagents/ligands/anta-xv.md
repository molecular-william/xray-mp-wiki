# Anta XV

## Overview

Anta XV is a small-molecule antagonist of the human Smoothened (SMO) receptor. It shares a phthalazine ring core with LY2940680 but has distinct substitutions that result in a different binding mode. Anta XV binds in the SMO transmembrane pocket and forms direct interactions with D473(6.54f), a key residue in the chemoresistance mutation cluster. Unlike LY2940680, Anta XV does not interact with ECL3 residues.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: phthalazine-ring SMO antagonist (substituents not specified in crystallography paper)
- **Class**: phthalazine-based SMO antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### SMO antagonism in structural studies

Anta XV was used to crystallize human SMO (PDB: 4QIM, 2.6 A, P212121). The ligand binds near the extracellular entrance of the binding pocket. Unlike LY2940680, Anta XV does not contact ECL3 residues. It forms a direct hydrogen bond with D473(6.54f) at 3.3 A distance and a hydrogen bond with R400(5.43f) to the phthalazine core, inducing an inward shift of helix VI. The D473(6.54f)A mutation causes a >100-fold affinity drop for Anta XV.
