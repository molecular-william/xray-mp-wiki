# PSB36

## Overview

PSB36 is a selective adenosine A1 receptor antagonist with a 3-hydroxy-propyl group and a 3-nor-adamantyl group. It has subnanomolar affinity for A1AR (Ki = 0.7 nM) and submicromolar affinity for A2AR. Crystal structures of PSB36 bound to both A1AR (3.3 A) and A2AR (2.8 A) reveal the molecular basis of subtype selectivity.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### A1AR structure determination

PSB36 was used to crystallize the thermostabilized A1R-StaR-Delta3-b562RIL construct in lipidic cubic phase, yielding a 3.3 A resolution structure.


### A2AR binding comparison

PSB36 was soaked into A2AR-theophylline crystals to obtain a 2.8 A structure, revealing that PSB36 binds at submicromolar affinity in A2AR and sits higher in the binding pocket compared to A1AR.
