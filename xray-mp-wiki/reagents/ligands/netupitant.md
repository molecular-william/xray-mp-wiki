# Netupitant

## Overview

Netupitant is an FDA-approved small-molecule antagonist of the human neurokinin 1 receptor (NK1R), used clinically for the treatment of chemotherapy-induced nausea and vomiting (CINV) in combination with palonosetron (as NEPA, Akynzeo). Netupitant has a distinct chemical scaffold from aprepitant, featuring a substituted pyridine core that does not share the morpholine scaffold of earlier chiral antagonists. It was co-crystallized with NK1R at 2.20 A resolution (PDB 6HLP), the highest resolution NK1R structure determined.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Clinical antiemetic for CINV

Netupitant is approved as part of the fixed-dose combination NEPA (netupitant + palonosetron) for chemotherapy-induced nausea and vomiting. Like aprepitant, it acts as an insurmountable antagonist,
 inducing the "histidine lock" interhelical hydrogen bond network
between H197(5.39), Y272(6.59), T201(5.43) and H265(6.52) that cross-links helices V and VI.


### Highest resolution NK1R antagonist structure

Netupitant was co-crystallized with NK1R-PGS to 2.20 A resolution, revealing a distinct binding pose. Due to its elongated arm 1 linker, the aromatic core is pushed upwards towards helices V and VI, engaging in an edge-to-face pi-pi interaction with H197(5.39). Arm 3 targets a hydrophobic groove between F268(6.55), P271(6.58) and Y272(6.59). A water molecule mediates coordination of the amide oxygen in the arm 1 linker with Q165(4.60).
