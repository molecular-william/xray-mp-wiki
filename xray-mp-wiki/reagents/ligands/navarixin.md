# Navarixin (SCH-527123 / MK-7123)

## Overview

Navarixin (also known as SCH-527123 or MK-7123) is a small molecule antagonist
of CXCR1 and CXCR2 that was in phase II clinical trials for its anti-metastatic
effect on colorectal and other aggressive cancers. Navarixin contains a
cyclobutene-dione core motif that is geometrically almost identical to the
thiadiazole-dioxide core of Cmp2105. In a 3D shape similarity screen, Navarixin
was identified as a CCR7 modulator that binds the intracellular allosteric
pocket at the TM7-H8 turn. Navarixin suppresses arrestin recruitment in
response to CCL19 (IC50 33.9 uM) and stabilizes CCR7 (EC50 13.38 uM, maximal
stabilizing effect 7.5 C). This cross-reactivity suggests that part of
Navarixin's anticancer effects may involve silencing CCR7 in addition to
CXCR1/CXCR2.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### CXCR1/CXCR2 antagonist (clinical)

Navarixin is a potent and bioavailable antagonist for CXCR1 and CXCR2, tested in phase II clinical trials for anti-metastatic effects on colorectal cancer.


### CCR7 cross-reactive modulator

Identified by 3D shape similarity screening using Cmp2105 as a seed, Navarixin was experimentally shown to bind and stabilize CCR7 via the intracellular allosteric pocket at the TM7-H8 turn.
