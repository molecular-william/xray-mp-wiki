# UK-432097

## Overview

UK-432097 (2-(3-[1-(pyridin-2-yl)piperidin-4-yl]ureido)ethyl-6-N-(2,2-diphenylethyl)-5'-N-ethylcarboxamidoadenosine-2-carboxamide) is a potent and selective full agonist of the human adenosine A2A receptor (A2AAR). Developed as a drug candidate for COPD, it is a substituted derivative of adenosine. At 778 daltons, it is more than twice as large as the antagonist ZM241385 and occupies most of the A2AAR ligand-binding cavity, forming an extensive interaction network including 11 hydrogen bonds, one aromatic stacking interaction, and numerous van der Waals contacts. It has also been used in co-crystal structures of A2AR variants, including the D52N and S91A sodium-site mutants, providing key insights into the structural connection between the allosteric sodium site and activation microswitch.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### GPCR structural biology — agonist-bound A2AAR crystallization

UK-432097 was co-crystallized with the engineered human A2AAR (A2AAR–T4L-ΔC fusion) to obtain a 2.7 Å crystal structure of the agonist-bound receptor in an active-like state. The agonist's extensive ligand-receptor interaction network (11 hydrogen bonds, aromatic stacking, and van der Waals interactions) provided sufficient stabilization to obtain high-resolution diffraction without requiring additional stabilization by a G protein or nanobody. This was the first demonstration that an agonist-bound GPCR could be crystallized in an active-like state without a stabilizing binding partner.


### A2AR agonist co-crystal structure with mutant variants

UK-432097 was used as the full agonist to co-crystallize with A2AR-D52N (PDB 5WF5, 2.6 A) and A2AR-S91A (PDB 5WF6, 2.9 A) variants. The ribose moiety of UK-432097 makes unique contacts with S277(7.42) and H278(7.43) in helix VII, which is a characteristic feature of all A2AR full agonists. These structures revealed how agonist binding leads to conformational changes that propagate to the intracellular surface.


### Investigating GPCR activation mechanism

Comparison of the UK-432097-bound A2AAR structure with ZM241385-bound (antagonist) A2AAR allowed detailed mapping of agonist-induced conformational changes from the extracellular binding pocket to intracellular G protein-coupling regions. The agonist-specific interactions with the ribose moiety (including His250, Trp246) trigger helix VI rotation and tilt, while the bulky C2-substituted moiety imposes conformational changes on helices I, II, VII and ECL3.
