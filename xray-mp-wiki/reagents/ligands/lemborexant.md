# Lemborexant

## Overview

Lemborexant (Dayvigo) is a dual orexin receptor antagonist (DORA) approved by the US FDA in 2019 for the treatment of insomnia. It is a pyrimidine-based compound with a fluorophenyl group and a carboxamide moiety. Lemborexant binds to both OX1R and OX2R with high affinity. The crystal structure of lemborexant-bound OX2R (PDB 7XRR, 2.89 A) revealed a unique binding mode involving hydrogen bonding with Gln134^3.32, a halogen bond between the carboxamide and fluorine atoms, and pi-stacking between pyrimidine and pyridine rings sandwiched by His350^7.39 and Pro131^3.29. MD simulations showed the aromatic rings of lemborexant remain stacked in solution, indicating pre-organized bound-state conformation favorable for entropy.
