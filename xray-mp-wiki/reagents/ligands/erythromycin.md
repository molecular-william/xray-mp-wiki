# Erythromycin

## Overview

Erythromycin is a macrolide antibiotic (Mr ~734) that inhibits bacterial protein synthesis by binding to the 50S ribosomal subunit. In structural studies of the AcrB multidrug efflux pump from E. coli, erythromycin binds to the access monomer in the proximal binding pocket. The macrolide ring of erythromycin is inclined approximately 60 degrees relative to the naphthofuranone ring of rifampicin at the AcrB binding site.


## Properties

- **Molecular weight**: 733.93
- **Class**: macrolide antibiotic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Binding studies in AcrB multidrug efflux pump

Erythromycin binds the proximal pocket of AcrB in the access/loose monomer. The contact with gate loop residue Phe617 accounts for ~10% of erythromycin surface area. Removal of the gate loop (Phe617) does not abolish binding, though it severely impairs erythromycin export. Erythromycin-soaked AcrB structures show ~0.89 occupancy in the proximal pocket with no distal pocket binding observed.
