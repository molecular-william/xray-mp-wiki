# Salinixanthin

## Overview

Salinixanthin is a C40-[Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) (keto-[Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/)) that functions as a light-harvesting antenna in [Xanthorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/xanthorhodopsin/) from the eubacterium Salinibacter ruber. It is noncovalently bound to the protein and transfers energy to the [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) chromophore with ~45% efficiency. The keto-ring of salinixanthin occupies the space normally held by Trp-138 in [Bacteriorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/bacteriorhodopsin/), with the [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) β-ionone ring forming part of the [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) binding site. The center-to-center distance between the salinixanthin and [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) polyenes is 11.7 Å, with the ring moieties within 5 Å of each other, and the angle between chromophore axes is 46°, representing a compromise between optimal light capture and energy transfer efficiency.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Light-harvesting antenna for xanthorhodopsin

Salinixanthin absorbs light and transfers energy to the [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) chromophore in [Xanthorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/xanthorhodopsin/) with ~45% efficiency. Singlet-singlet energy transfer occurs from the short-lived S2 [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) level to [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/).


### Chromophore geometry in xanthorhodopsin

The salinixanthin keto-ring is within 5 Å of the [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) β-ionone ring, with the two polyenes at a 46° angle. The [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) ring forms part of the [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) binding site, explaining the dependence of the [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) spectrum on the presence of [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/).
