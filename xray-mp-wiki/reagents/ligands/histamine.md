# Histamine

## Overview

Histamine is a biogenic amine that functions as a neurotransmitter and immune signaling molecule. In the context of the GABA_A receptor β3 homopentamer (PDB 4COF), histamine acts as an agonist capable of inducing desensitization. The EC50 for histamine activation of GABA_A R-β3_cryst was 61 ± 12 µM by electrophysiology, and the EC50 for thermostabilization in detergent micelles was 400 ± 150 µM. Histamine is noted to act as a potentiator of heteromeric GABA_A receptors rather than as a direct agonist, distinguishing its effect at β3 homopentamers.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 2-(1H-imidazol-4-yl)ethan-1-amine
- **Chemical formula**: C5H9N3
- **Molecular weight**: 111.15 g/mol
- **Class**: biogenic amine; neurotransmitter
