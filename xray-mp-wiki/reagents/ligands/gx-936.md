# GX-936

## Overview

GX-936 is an aryl sulfonamide small-molecule antagonist that selectively inhibits the human voltage-gated sodium channel Nav1.7 by binding to the activated state of voltage-sensor domain IV (VSD4). It was co-crystallized with a Nav1.7 VSD4-[NAVAB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navab/) chimeric channel for X-ray structure determination, revealing the molecular basis of isoform-selective Nav1.7 inhibition through a voltage-sensor trapping mechanism.


## Properties

- **Class**: Aryl sulfonamide (small-molecule antagonist)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### X-ray crystallography probe for Nav1.7 VSD4

GX-936 was co-crystallized with the Nav1.7 VSD4-[NAVAB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navab/) chimeric channel for structure determination at 3.53 A resolution. The compound's anionic aryl sulfonamide warhead engages the R4 gating charge (R1608) through a bidentate salt bridge. The structure revealed three regions of the binding site: an anion-binding pocket, a selectivity pocket, and a lipid-exposed pocket.


### Selective Nav1.7 inhibition for pain research

GX-936 belongs to the aryl sulfonamide class of Nav1.7-selective gating modifiers that show extreme state dependence and isoform selectivity. Related compound GX-674 shows >100,000-fold selectivity over Nav1.5 (cardiac isoform), making this class promising for developing pain therapeutics without cardiac side effects.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Nav1.7 VSD4 (voltage-sensor domain IV)

Bidentate salt bridge between aryl sulfonamide warhead and R4 gating charge (R1608); pi-stacking with Y1537; van der Waals contacts with V1541, M1582, W1538; polar interaction with N1540 and R2

- **Pocket volume**: Not specified
- **Key residues**: R1608 (R4), R1605 (R3), R1602 (R2), Y1537, W1538, V1541, M1582, N1540, G1581, A1585
