# U18666A

## Overview

U18666A is a [Cholesterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/cholesterol/) biosynthesis inhibitor that targets Emopamil-Binding Protein ([EBP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/human-ebp/), 3-beta-hydroxysteroid-Delta8,Delta7-isomerase) and also inhibits the Niemann-Pick C1 ([NPC1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/human-niemann-pick-c1/)) protein involved in lysosomal [Cholesterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/cholesterol/) export. It is a cationic amphiphilic compound with a positively-charged amine group that mimics the carbocationic sterol intermediate in [Cholesterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/cholesterol/) biosynthesis. U18666A was co-crystallized with human [EBP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/human-ebp/) at 3.2 A resolution.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### EBP structural studies

U18666A was used to obtain the crystal structure of human [EBP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/human-ebp/) in the ligand-bound state. The compound was present at 10 uM in all purification steps and crystallization. The structure revealed that U18666A binds in the membrane cavity of [EBP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/human-ebp/), with its positively-charged amine group recognized by Asn193 and Glu122, and stabilized by pi-cation interaction with Trp196.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [EBP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/human-ebp/) (Emopamil-Binding Protein)

Positively-charged amine group interacts with Asn193, Glu122; pi-cation stabilization by Trp196; hydrophobic interactions with cavity residues
