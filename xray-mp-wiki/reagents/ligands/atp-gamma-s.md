# ATPγS (Adenosine 5'-O-(3-thiotriphosphate))

## Overview

ATPγS (adenosine 5'-O-(3-thiotriphosphate)) is a non-hydrolyzable ATP analog in which the gamma phosphate is replaced by a sulfur atom. It is commonly used in structural studies to trap ATP-bound protein conformations, as the sulfur substitution prevents ATPase-catalyzed hydrolysis while maintaining binding affinity.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Adenosine 5'-O-(3-thiotriphosphate)
- **Chemical formula**: C10H17N5O15P3S
- **Molecular weight**: 547.2 g/mol
- **Class**: ATP analog

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural trapping of ATP-bound protein conformations

ATPγS was used to crystallize the isolated [TRKA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/trka/) tetramer from Klebsiella pneumoniae at 3.05 A resolution. The non-hydrolyzable nature of ATPγS allowed visualization of eight ATPγS binding sites across the tetramer, revealing two binding sites per protomer. Likelihood-weighted Fo-Fc maps calculated without ATPγS confirmed the binding sites at 3 sigma contour level.
