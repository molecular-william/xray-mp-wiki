# C-CPE (Clostridium perfringens Enterotoxin)

## Overview

C-CPE (C-terminal fragment of *Clostridium perfringens* enterotoxin) is a ~25 kDa toxin fragment that binds with high affinity to several claudin family tight junction proteins, including claudin-3, claudin-4, claudin-6, claudin-7, claudin-8, and claudin-19. C-CPE binding disrupts tight junction strands by blocking the hydrophobic interactions that mediate claudin–claudin association, leading to loss of epithelial barrier function. The crystal structure of C-CPE bound to mouse claudin-19 reveals a hydrophobic cavity formed by three conserved tyrosine residues (Y306, Y310, Y312) that accommodates the ECS2 toxin-binding motif of claudins.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Tight junction disassembly tool

C-CPE is widely used as a research tool to study claudin biology and tight junction function. By disrupting claudin–claudin interactions, C-CPE increases paracellular permeability and is used to investigate the role of individual claudins in barrier function.


### Drug delivery enhancer

Because C-CPE reversibly opens tight junctions by targeting claudins, it has been explored as an absorption enhancer for drug delivery across epithelial barriers.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Claudin-19, claudin-3, and other CPE-sensitive claudins

ECS1 loop (residues 35–42, motif (A/N/S)-I-(I/L/V)-(T/V)) and ECS2 loop (residues 150–154, motif NP(V/L)(V/L)(P/A)) form the binding interface. Three conserved C-CPE tyrosine residues (Y306, Y310, Y312) create a hydrophobic cavity that accommodates the ECS2 motif.

- **Key residues**: ['C-CPE: Y306, Y310, Y312, P311, R227', 'Claudin-19 ECS1: A39, I40, I41', 'Claudin-19 ECS2: N150, P151, S152, T153, P154']
