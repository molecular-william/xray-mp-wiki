# MT7 (Muscarinic Toxin 7)

## Overview

MT7 is a 65-amino acid three-finger toxin (3FT) from the venom of the green mamba snake (Dendroaspis angusticeps). It is a highly subtype-selective negative allosteric modulator (NAM) of the [M1 Muscarinic Acetylcholine Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/m1-muscarinic-acetylcholine-receptor/), with subnanomolar affinity and >100,000-fold selectivity over other muscarinic receptor subtypes. MT7 binds to the extracellular vestibule of M1AChR, acting as both a NAM for agonist activation and a positive allosteric modulator (PAM) for orthosteric antagonist binding. It stabilizes an outward movement of TM6, ECL3, and TM7 through insertion of finger loop 2 into the extracellular vestibule, representing a distinct structural mechanism of GPCR allosteric modulation by a protein toxin.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [M1 Muscarinic Acetylcholine Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/m1-muscarinic-acetylcholine-receptor/)

Finger loop 2 inserts into extracellular vestibule
- R34 interacts with E401^7.36 and Y82^2.61
- Y51 and R52 (finger loop 3) interact with E397^7.31
- P33 interacts with W400^7.35 and E401^7.36 backbone
- W400^7.35 outward displacement stabilizes TM6 via interaction with M384^6.54
