# MIPS2712

## Overview

MIPS2712 is a selective A1 adenosine receptor antagonist. It was synthesized as a derivative of DU172 by hydrolysis of the ethyl ester to reveal the free sulfonic acid. The chemical name is 4-((3-(8-cyclohexyl-2,6-dioxo-1-propyl-1,2,6,7-tetrahydro-3H-purin-3-yl)propyl)carbamoyl)benzenesulfonic acid. In the A1 adenosine receptor study, MIPS2712 was docked into the A1-AR crystal structure along with other selective antagonists (DPCPX, PSB36, rolofylline, SLV320, and MIPS2719) to understand the molecular basis for A1 versus A2A selectivity. The docking used the A1-AR crystal structure (PDB 5UEN) with the binding pocket defined around DU172.
