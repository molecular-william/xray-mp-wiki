# beta-CFT (CFT)

## Overview

beta-CFT (2-beta-carbomethoxy-3-beta-(4-fluorophenyl)tropane) is a tropane alkaloid derivative that acts as a high-affinity competitive inhibitor of the dopamine transporter (DAT). It is a cocaine analogue with enhanced affinity for DAT.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Drosophila melanogaster dopamine transporter (dDAT)

Tropane-based competitive inhibitor with 4-fluorophenyl group. Binds to central S1 site overlapping cocaine and nortriptyline binding sites. D121G and S426M subsite B mutations in dDAT_mfc enhanced binding affinity. Carboxylate of Asp121 does not form favorable interactions with fluorophenyl group, explaining reduced affinity of dDAT compared to human DAT.

- **Pocket volume**: 439.2 A^2 total surface area, 403.1 A^2 buried (91.8%)
- **Key residues**: Asp46, Phe319, Phe325, Phe43, Ala44, Ala48, Ser421, Asp121
