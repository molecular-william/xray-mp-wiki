# Dansyl-galactopyranoside (D2G)

## Overview

Dansyl-galactopyranoside (D2G), formally 2'-(N-dansyl)aminoalkyl-1-thio-beta-D-galactopyranoside, is a fluorescent galactopyranoside analog used as a probe for studying sugar binding to melibiose permease (MelB). D2G binds specifically to MelB and is used in fluorescence resonance energy transfer (FRET) assays and steady-state melibiose accumulation assays to measure cation-stimulated sugar binding. Melibiose can displace D2G from MelB, allowing determination of inhibition constants (IC50).
