# Globomycin

## Overview

Globomycin is a cyclic depsipeptide antibiotic produced by select strains of Streptomyces that inhibits bacterial lipoprotein signal peptidase II (LspA). It is a 19-member cyclic depsipeptide containing an alpha-methyl-beta-hydroxy fatty acid, N-methyl-L-Leu, L-allo-Ile, L-Ser, L-allo-Thr, and Gly. Globomycin inhibits LspA by acting as a non-cleavable tetrahedral intermediate analog, with the beta-hydroxyl of its serine residue positioning between the catalytic aspartate dyad. It is effective against Gram-negative bacteria, and synthetic analogs with lengthened fatty acyl chains have shown up to 10-fold improved potency.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Inhibition of LspA for structural studies

Globomycin was used to obtain the crystal structure of LspA from Pseudomonas aeruginosa complexed with the antibiotic, revealing the molecular basis of LspA inhibition. The structure was solved at 2.8 A resolution using the in meso crystallization method and Se-Met SAD phasing. Globomycin was modeled into a donut-shaped region of electron density between transmembrane helices MH2, MH3, and MH4 on the periplasmic side of the membrane.


### Convergent evolution of LspA inhibitors

Globomycin and myxovirescin, despite completely different molecular structures and biosynthetic origins, both inhibit LspA by the same mechanism. They share a common 19-atom spine motif that recapitulates part of the natural lipoprotein substrate, demonstrating convergent evolution.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to LspA (lipoprotein signal peptidase II)

The beta-hydroxyl of g-Ser forms a hydrogen bond with the carboxyl of Asp143 (catalytic dyad). Backbone carbonyls of g-Leu, g-Ile, and g-Ser hydrogen bond with strictly conserved Asn112 and Arg116. The g-Leu side chain and the fatty acyl chain have extensive hydrophobic interactions with apolar residues in the binding pocket.

- **Key residues**: Asp124, Asp143, Asn112, Arg116, Phe59, Phe61
