# Epinephrine

## Overview

Epinephrine (also known as adrenaline) is an endogenous catecholamine hormone primarily released from the adrenal medulla into the systemic circulation. It binds beta1 AR and beta2 AR with similar affinity, unlike norepinephrine which is selective for beta1 AR. Epinephrine contains an additional methyl group on the primary amine compared to norepinephrine, which modifies the electron distribution and makes its protonated nitrogen less positively charged, rendering it less sensitive to the electrostatic differences between beta1 AR and beta2 AR extracellular vestibules.
