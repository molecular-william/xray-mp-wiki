# MK-591

## Overview

MK-591 is a small-molecule inhibitor of 5-lipoxygenase-activating protein
(FLAP) that blocks leukotriene biosynthesis. It binds to surface grooves
at the interfaces between monomers of the FLAP homotrimer, intercalating
between adjacent subunits. The crystal structure of human FLAP was solved
in complex with both MK-591 and an iodinated analog of MK-591.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### FLAP Inhibition in Leukotriene Biosynthesis

MK-591 binds to FLAP at three equivalent binding sites located in
~750 Å³ grooves between adjacent monomers on the lipid-exposed surface
of the trimer. The inhibitor forms van der Waals interactions with
residues V20, V21, G24, F25, A27 (helix α1); Y112, I113 (cytosolic
loop C2); A63 (helix α2); and I119, L120, F123 (helix α4 of the
adjacent monomer). Weak polar contacts are formed with N23 (α1), D62,
T66 (α2), and K116 (α4 of the neighboring subunit). Mutagenesis
confirmed these binding determinants.


### Structural Tool for FLAP Mechanism Studies

Co-crystallization of FLAP with MK-591 and its iodinated analog
enabled identification of the inhibitor binding site via anomalous
difference maps. The iodinated analog was also used in radiolabeled
binding assays to validate the binding site through mutagenesis.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Potent and selective FLAP inhibitor
- Enables structural characterization of FLAP-ligand complex

### Disadvantages

- Does not distinguish between AA and inhibitor binding sites (partial overlap per mutagenesis studies)
