# Mazindol

## Overview

Mazindol is a stimulant drug that acts as a [Norepinephrine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/norepinephrine/) and [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) reuptake inhibitor. It is used clinically for the treatment of obesity and attention-deficit hyperactivity disorder. Mazindol binds to the primary binding site of biogenic amine transporters, forming characteristic salt bridges and hydrogen bonds with key residues.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Delta6/Delta13 [LEUBAT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/leubat/) ([LEUT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/leut/) engineered mutant)

Amine nitrogen forms a salt bridge with the carboxyl group of Asp24. The hydroxyl group hydrogen bonds with the carboxyl group of Asp24 and the phenol group of Tyr108. The chlorophenyl ring inserts into a hydrophobic pocket formed by Tyr21, Val104, Tyr108 and Phe259. Surrounded by a hydrophobic pocket.
