# SANT1

## Overview

SANT1 (N-[(1E)-(3,5-dimethyl-1-phenyl-1H-pyrazol-4-yl)methylidene]-4-(phenylmethyl)-1-piperazinamine) is a small-molecule antagonist of the human Smoothened (SMO) receptor. It was the first potent and selective SMO antagonist discovered and binds at a deep site within the transmembrane domain binding pocket. SANT1 inhibits hedgehog signalling by preventing cilia translocation of SMO and suppresses GLI transcription factor activation.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: N-[(1E)-(3,5-dimethyl-1-phenyl-1H-pyrazol-4-yl)methylidene]-4-(phenylmethyl)-1-piperazinamine
- **Class**: pyrazole-based SMO antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### SMO antagonism in structural studies

SANT1 was used to crystallize human SMO (PDB: 4N4W, 2.8 A, C2221). The ligand binds very deep in the pocket of the seven-transmembrane helix bundle, deeper than other SMO ligands. It forms extensive contacts with residues in the binding pocket and does not interact with D473(6.54f), the key residue in the chemoresistance mutation cluster.
