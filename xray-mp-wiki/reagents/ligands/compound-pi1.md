# Compound PI1 (5-(4-bromophenyl)-2-(4-bromophenethyl)-1H-isoindole-1,3(2H)-dione)

## Overview

PI1 is a brominated isoindole-dione compound that acts as a potent sodium channel blocker. It blocks [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navms/) with IC50 = 178 nM and hNav1.1 with IC50 = 373 nM, making it over 1000-fold more potent than [Lamotrigine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/lamotrigine/). Its bromine atoms enable anomalous scattering detection in X-ray crystallography, and co-crystal structures with [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navms/) revealed the drug binding site at the intramembrane fenestrations.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Research tool for sodium channel blocker binding

PI1 is used as a high-affinity probe to map the sodium channel blocker
binding site. Its two bromine atoms enable anomalous diffraction mapping
in crystal structures. Co-crystal structures with [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navms/) revealed drug binding
at four sites adjacent to intramembrane fenestrations.
