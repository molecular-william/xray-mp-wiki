# Bongkrekic Acid

## Overview

Bongkrekic acid (BKA) is a polyunsaturated methoxy tricarboxylic acid polyketide produced by Burkholderia gladioli pathological variant cocovenenans. It is a specific, conformation-specific competitive inhibitor of the mitochondrial ADP/ATP carrier (AAC), binding to the matrix-open state (m-state) and locking the transporter in a fixed conformation. BKA mimics the biophysical properties of ATP — its dicarboxylate end mimics the phosphate groups, and its polyunsaturated backbone partially mimics the adenine ring — but its larger molecular volume (443 A3 vs 354 A3 for ATP) prevents the conformational changes required for transport, creating an abortive state.
