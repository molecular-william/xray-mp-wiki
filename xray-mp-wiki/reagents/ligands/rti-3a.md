# RTI-3a (Partial NTSR1 Agonist)

## Overview

RTI-3a is a nonpeptide partial agonist of the rat [Neurotensin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/neurotensin/) receptor 1 ([NTSR1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/neurotensin-receptor-1/)), derived from the inverse agonist [SR48692](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/sr48692/) by replacing the adamantyl moiety with an L-Leu side chain. Based on a pyrazole core scaffold, RTI-3a exhibits reduced efficacy compared to full agonists due to a different binding mode that results in less contraction of the extracellular receptor portion. EC50 at wild-type rNTSR1 is 1815 nM for Gq signaling.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: RTI-3a

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural characterization of NTSR1 partial agonism

RTI-3a was co-crystallized with NTSR1-H4x ([DARPIN](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/protein-tags/darpin/) D12 fusion construct). The pyrazole core of RTI-3a occupies a substantially different space than Ile12 in the NTS8-13 complex, interacting with fewer aromatic residues (F128(2.65), F331(6.58), Y347(7.31), Y351(7.35)). The L-Leu moiety (replacing the adamantyl group of [SR48692](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/sr48692/)) engages the hydrophobic subpocket at F331(6.58). Compared to full agonists, the RTI-3a complex exhibits a slightly less contracted binding site, particularly at TM7, explaining its reduced efficacy.
Crystallization was performed in LCP, structure determined at 2.5-3.2 A resolution.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Rat [NTSR1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/neurotensin-receptor-1/) (NTSR1-H4x construct)

L-Leu moiety engages hydrophobic subpocket at F331(6.58); carboxylate anchors to R327(6.54); pyrazole core makes fewer contacts than full agonists

- **Key residues**: F128(2.65), F331(6.58), Y347(7.31), Y351(7.35), R327(6.54)
