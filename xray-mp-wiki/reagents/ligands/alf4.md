# AlF4 (Aluminum Fluoride)

## Overview

Aluminum fluoride (AlF4-) is a phosphate analog used in structural biology to trap phosphorylated intermediate states of ATP-dependent enzymes. It mimics the geometry and charge distribution of inorganic phosphate (Pi) or the aspartyl-phosphate intermediate of P-type ATPases in the E2.Pi conformation. When combined with Mg2+, AlF4- binds to the phosphorylation site and forms a tetrahedral complex that structurally resembles the transition state of phosphate transfer.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Forms a stable tetrahedral complex with Mg2+ that closely mimics inorganic phosphate
- Useful for trapping the E2.Pi state of P-type ATPases for structural studies
- Can be used to study the forward reaction (dephosphorylation) step of the transport cycle

### Disadvantages

- Aluminum compounds require careful handling due to potential toxicity
- AlF4- complexes are labile and must be prepared immediately before use
- May not perfectly reproduce the geometry of the native aspartyl-phosphate bond
