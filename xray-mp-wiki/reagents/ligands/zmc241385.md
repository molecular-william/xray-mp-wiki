# ZM241385

## Overview

ZM241385 is a high-affinity antagonist of the A2A adenosine receptor (A2A AR). It was co-crystallized with the human A2A AR in the landmark structure determination of the receptor (PDB 3EML). ZM241385 binds in the orthosteric ligand-binding pocket located in the extracellular half of the transmembrane domain. The antagonist binding is characterized by extensive hydrophobic and hydrogen bonding interactions with key residues including Asn2536.55, Glu1695.30, and His2506.52. ZM241385 is a triazolotriazine derivative that has been extensively used as a pharmacological tool to study adenosine receptor pharmacology and as a reference compound in radioligand binding assays ([3H]-ZM241385). The drug prevents agonist binding by stabilizing the inactive state of the receptor, including stabilization of the ionic lock between Arg1023.50 (D/ERY motif) and Glu2286.30.
