# ORG27569

## Overview

ORG27569 is a negative allosteric modulator (NAM) of the cannabinoid receptor CB1 (CNR1). It is an indole-containing small molecule with an unusual pharmacological profile: it increases the apparent binding of the full agonist CP55940 while simultaneously reducing Gi/o coupling and signaling ('insurmountable inhibition'). The crystal structure of CB1 bound to CP55940 (agonist) and ORG27569 (NAM) was determined at 3.1 Å resolution. ORG27569 binds to an extrahelical site in the inner leaflet of the membrane at the TM2/TM3/TM4 interface, partially overlapping with a conserved cholesterol-binding site. The chloro-indole group packs against Trp241⁴·⁵⁰, and the amide-linked piperidinylphenyl chain extends across the receptor exterior against Thr242⁴·⁵¹ and Ile245⁴·⁵⁴. The ternary complex captures an intermediate receptor state where the orthosteric pocket contracts around the agonist but the dual aromatic toggle switch (Phe200³·³⁶/Trp356⁶·⁴⁸) remains in an inactive 'off' conformation.


## Properties

- **Class**: negative allosteric modulator; indole derivative

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### CB1 negative allosteric modulator

ORG27569 binds to an extrahelical site on CB1 at the membrane inner leaflet, stabilizing an intermediate receptor state with the orthosteric pocket contracted but the G-protein interface in an inactive conformation.
