# Bromoform (Tribromomethane)

## Overview

Bromoform (tribromomethane, CHBr3) is a trihalomethane general anesthetic that inhibits pentameric ligand-gated ion channels (pLGICs) including [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) at sub-millimolar concentrations. It is a small, lipophilic molecule widely used as a research tool for studying anesthetic binding sites and mechanisms of action on ion channels.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Tribromomethane
- **Chemical formula**: CHBr3
- **Molecular weight**: 252.73
- **Class**: Trihalomethane anesthetic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Anesthetic binding site mapping in pLGICs

Bromoform is used to map anesthetic binding sites in pentameric ligand-gated ion channels. Its lipophilicity allows it to access binding sites from the lipid bilayer, making it an ideal probe for studying membrane-accessible anesthetic pockets. Crystallographic studies with bromoform have revealed multiple binding sites in [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) including intra-subunit, inter-subunit, and pore sites.


### Crystallization additive for anesthetic-bound structures

Bromoform (2% w/v) is included in crystallization drops to co-crystallize anesthetic-bound protein conformations. This has been used to capture [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) in both open and locally closed states with bromoform bound to multiple sites.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) (Gloeobacter violaceus Ion Channel)

Bromoform binds to multiple interconnected sites in [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) including the pore site (PS) in the transmembrane domain, the intra-subunit DP-exp cavity (with DP and DP' subsites), the DP-deep site, and the inter-subunit DP-tunnel site. The pore site is specifically occupied in the locally closed and resting (inactive) conformations but not in the open (active) state. Binding is predominantly from the lipid bilayer. Y197 side chain orientation modulates access to the DP-exp site.

- **Pocket volume**: Multiple pockets identified; DP-exp approximately 248 A3
- **Key residues**: Y197, T244, W213, L209, S212, Ile 240 (I9'), Ser 236 (S6')
