# Etorphine

## Overview

Etorphine is a potent broad-spectrum opioid receptor agonist with high affinity for mu-, kappa-, and delta-opioid receptors. It is characterized by strong sodium sensitivity in binding, consistent with its classification as a peptide-like agonist.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 4,5-alpha-epoxy-3-hydroxy-17-(2-(1-methyl-2-pyrrolidinyl)ethyl)morphinan-6-one
- **Class**: morphinan opioid agonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Opioid receptor binding study

Etorphine is used in radioligand displacement binding assays to study opioid receptor binding properties. At human δ-OR expressed in HEK293 cells, etorphine shows Ki values of 88 nM (0 NaCl) and 613 nM (140 mM NaCl), demonstrating strong sodium sensitivity (7-fold decrease).
