# Valacyclovir

## Overview

Valacyclovir (trade names Valtrex, Zelitrex) is a prodrug derivative of the antiviral agent
acyclovir, used in the treatment of herpes virus infections (including herpes zoster, HSV-1
and HSV-2) as well as in prophylaxis and suppression of latent disease. Valacyclovir is an
L-valine ester prodrug of acyclovir that was designed to target the intestinal peptide
transporter PepT1 (SLC15A1) for improved oral bioavailability. The oral bioavailability of
valacyclovir exceeds 50%, compared with 15% for the parent drug acyclovir. A crystal
structure of the bacterial POT family transporter PepT_Sh in complex with valacyclovir
revealed the structural basis for prodrug recognition, showing that the L-valine scaffold
interacts with conserved residues in the peptide binding site.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Improved oral bioavailability (>50%) compared to acyclovir (15%)
- Well-established prodrug strategy for targeting intestinal peptide transporters

### Disadvantages

- Requires enzymatic cleavage to release active acyclovir
- Higher cost compared to parent drug acyclovir
