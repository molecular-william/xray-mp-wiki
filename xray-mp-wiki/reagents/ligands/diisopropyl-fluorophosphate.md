# Diisopropyl Fluorophosphate (DFP)

## Overview

Diisopropyl fluorophosphate (DFP) is an organophosphate mechanism-based inhibitor of serine proteases. It covalently reacts with the catalytic serine residue to form a stable phosphorylated adduct. In rhomboid protease [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/glpg/), DFP binds in the active site and induces conformational changes in the membrane protease, including shifts in TM helices S2, S1, L1, S3, and S5, and rotation of the L5 cap. The conformational changes induced by DFP are similar in trend to those caused by larger inhibitors (CAPF, [Isocoumarin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibiotics/isocoumarin/)) but smaller in amplitude, suggesting that the degree of conformational change correlates with the size of the inhibitor occupying the active site.
