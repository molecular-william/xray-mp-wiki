# GDC-0449 (Vismodegib)

## Overview

GDC-0449 (vismodegib) is a small-molecule antagonist of the human Smoothened (SMO) receptor. It was approved by the US Food and Drug Administration in 2012 for treating basal cell carcinoma. GDC-0449 acts as a potent antagonist of the canonical hedgehog signalling pathway by binding to the SMO 7-transmembrane domain. The D473(6.54f)H mutation confers chemoresistance to GDC-0449, making it unable to inhibit the receptor. Derivatives of GDC-0449 have been developed to overcome this drug resistance.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Clinically approved SMO antagonist

GDC-0449 (vismodegib) was approved by the FDA in 2012 for treating basal cell carcinoma. The D473(6.54f)H mutation in human SMO abolishes the antitumour effects of GDC-0449. Chemical modifications to GDC-0449 derivatives have been shown to overcome drug-resistant mutations. The D473(6.54f)A mutation causes a >100-fold affinity drop for GDC-0449.
