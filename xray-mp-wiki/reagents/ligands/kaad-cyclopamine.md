# KAAD-cyclopamine

## Overview

KAAD-cyclopamine is a BODIPY-tagged derivative of cyclopamine with a long-chain flexible linker. It was developed to enable attachment of bulky reporter moieties (such as fluorescent BODIPY) to cyclopamine for structural and biochemical studies. The long-chain flexible linker allows the bulky BODIPY group to extend from the SMO binding pocket through the extracellular entrance, demonstrating that the extracellular entrance can accommodate large reporter groups.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: cyclopamine-BODIPY conjugate with long-chain flexible linker
- **Class**: tagged SMO antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural studies with bulky reporter groups

KAAD-cyclopamine was used to demonstrate that the extracellular entrance of the SMO ligand-binding cavity can accommodate bulky reporter moieties through a long-chain flexible linker. Molecular docking of KAAD-cyclopamine was performed using an all-atom energy-based docking procedure with side-chain flexibility in the binding pocket, revealing the binding pose of the cyclopamine moiety within the SMO transmembrane domain.
