# Norepinephrine

## Overview

Norepinephrine (also known as noradrenaline) is an endogenous catecholamine neurotransmitter released from sympathetic nerves. It is the major circulating catecholamine and plays a central role in regulating cardiovascular function via beta-adrenergic receptors. Norepinephrine is approximately tenfold selective for the beta1 adrenergic receptor over beta2 adrenergic receptor, a selectivity that arises from differences in the extracellular vestibule shape and electrostatic properties rather than the orthosteric binding pocket.
