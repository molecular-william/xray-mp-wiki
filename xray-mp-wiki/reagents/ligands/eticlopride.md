# Eticlopride

## Overview

Eticlopride is a D2/D3 [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) receptor-selective antagonist used to study [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) receptor pharmacology. It features an ethyl-pyrrolidine ring connected to a substituted aromatic ring via an amide bond, which maintains an almost planar conformation stabilized by intramolecular hydrogen bonds. The crystal structure of the human [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) D3 receptor ([D3R](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-dopamine-d3-receptor/)) in complex with eticlopride revealed the structural basis for D2/D3 selectivity.
