# All-trans Retinal

## Overview

All-trans retinal is the chromophore of bacteriorhodopsin, attached via a Schiff base linkage to Lys216. Photoisomerization of all-trans retinal to 13-cis drives the proton pumping cycle. In the 1.55 Å structure of bacteriorhodopsin (PDB 1C3W), all-trans retinal was modeled with 20 atoms in the active site.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Bacteriorhodopsin

- Schiff base linkage to Lys216
- Pi-stacking with Trp86 and Trp182 flank the polyene chain
- Van der Waals contacts within 3.6 Å of Trp86, Thr89, Thr90, Met118, Trp138, Ser141, Thr142, Met145, Trp182, Tyr185, Trp189, and Asp212

- **Key residues**: ['Lys216 (Schiff base attachment)', 'Asp85 (proton acceptor)', 'Asp212 (counterion)']
