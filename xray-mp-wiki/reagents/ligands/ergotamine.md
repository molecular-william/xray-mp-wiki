# Ergotamine (ERG)

## Overview

Ergotamine is an ergoline alkaloid and GPCR ligand that acts as a biased agonist at serotonin 5-HT2B receptors, preferentially activating beta-arrestin signaling over Gq signaling. It was the ligand used in the 5-HT2B crystal structure determination that revealed structural mechanisms of functional selectivity at serotonin receptors.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Ergotamine D-tartrate
- **Class**: Ergoline alkaloid

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### GPCR Functional Selectivity Probe

Ergotamine serves as a key pharmacological tool for studying biased agonism at serotonin receptors. At 5-HT2B receptors, ERG shows strong beta-arrestin bias (log(tau/KA) 7.05 for arrestin vs 5.95 for Gq), while at 5-HT1B receptors it shows weaker bias. This differential profile provided the basis for structural studies of functional selectivity.


### GPCR Structural Biology

Used as a stabilizing ligand for the 5-HT2B receptor during LCP crystallization. Present at 100 uM throughout purification (membrane incubation, solubilization, and chromatography steps) to maintain receptor-ligand complex integrity. Final structure at 2.7 Å resolution captured ERG in the orthosteric binding pocket with the tripeptide moiety extending into an extended binding site.


### SFX Structure Determination

Ergotamine was used as the agonist ligand for the first GPCR structure determined by [Serial Femtosecond Crystallography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/serial-femtosecond-crystallography) (SFX). The 5-HT2B-ergotamine complex was crystallized in [LCP](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/lipidic-cubic-phase), delivered via an LCP injector at the LCLS XFEL, and a room-temperature structure was obtained at 2.8 Å resolution using only 300 ug of protein.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to 5-HT2B

Ergoline core in orthosteric pocket; phenyl ring makes hydrophobic contacts to helices V, VI, VII

- **Key residues**: S2225.43, N3446.45
