# GABA (γ-Aminobutyric Acid)

## Overview

GABA (γ-aminobutyric acid) is the principal inhibitory neurotransmitter in the adult mammalian central nervous system. It binds to GABA_A receptors—pentameric ligand-gated chloride channels of the Cys-loop superfamily—at orthosteric binding pockets located between extracellular domains of adjacent subunits. In the crystal structure of the human GABA_A receptor β3 homopentamer (PDB 4COF), the neurotransmitter-binding pocket is formed by loops A-C (principal face: β4 strand, β7-β8 loop, β9-β10 loop) and loops D-E (complementary face: β2 strand, β6 strand). GABA acts as an agonist, opening the chloride channel to depress neuronal excitability. The EC50 for thermostabilization of GABA_A R-β3_cryst by GABA was 2.3 ± 0.2 mM.
