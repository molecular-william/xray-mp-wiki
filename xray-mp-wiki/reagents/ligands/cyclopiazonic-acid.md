# Cyclopiazonic Acid (CPA)

## Overview

Cyclopiazonic acid (CPA) is a specific inhibitor of the sarco/endoplasmic reticulum Ca2+-ATPase (SERCA). It binds in the transmembrane region of SERCA near the cytoplasmic surface, in a site partially overlapping with that of 2,5-di-tert-butyl-1,4-dihydroxybenzene (BHQ) but separate from that of thapsigargin. CPA binding induces significant conformational changes in the transmembrane helices that propagate to the cytoplasmic domains.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### SERCA inhibition for E2 state stabilization

CPA is used to stabilize the calcium-free E2 state of SERCA1a for crystallization studies. At 0.1 mM concentration, CPA binds to the transmembrane domain near the cytoplasmic surface, altering the positions of M1' helix and thereby M2, M4, and M5 helices. CPA binding stabilizes the E2 conformation with a more upright cytoplasmic headpiece compared to thapsigargin-stabilized E2.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [SERCA1a](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/serca1a/)

CPA binds in a pocket surrounded by transmembrane helices M1-M4 near the cytoplasmic surface. Key interactions include: N1 of CPA forms hydrogen bond with Gln-56 at C-terminal end of M1'; hydroxyl group forms hydrogen bond with Leu-253 carbonyl (M3); carbonyl oxygen O1 within hydrogen bond distance from Asp-59 carboxyl (M1). At least 10 van der Waals contacts within 4.0 A, including main chain atoms of Leu-93 (M2), Asn-101 (M2), Gly-257 (M3), Glu-309 (M4), and Pro-312 (M4).

- **Pocket volume**: Partially overlaps with BHQ binding site; separate from thapsigargin binding site
- **Key residues**: ["Gln-56 (M1')", 'Asp-59 (M1)', 'Leu-93 (M2)', 'Asn-101 (M2)', 'Leu-253 (M3)', 'Gly-257 (M3)', 'Glu-309 (M4)', 'Pro-312 (M4)']
