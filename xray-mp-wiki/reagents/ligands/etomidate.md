# Etomidate

## Overview

Etomidate is an intravenous general anaesthetic that potentiates and activates GABA_A receptors. In the crystal structure of the human GABA_A receptor β3 homopentamer (GABA_A R-β3_cryst, PDB 4COF), the putative etomidate-binding pocket was identified at transmembrane inter-subunit interfaces. Binding of etomidate displaces the fluorescent ligand 1-aminoanthracene (1-AMA) from the anaesthetic pocket with 50% maximal displacement at 7.1 ± 1.1 µM, as measured by fluorescence displacement assays. Etomidate binding was also confirmed to activate GABA_A R-β3_cryst currents in HEK293T cells. The etomidate-binding pocket overlaps with a distinct propofol-binding site described in the same paper.


## Properties

- **Class**: general anaesthetic; imidazole derivative

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Intravenous general anaesthetic

Etomidate acts as a positive allosteric modulator and direct activator of GABA_A receptors, inducing anaesthesia by potentiating GABAergic inhibitory neurotransmission.
