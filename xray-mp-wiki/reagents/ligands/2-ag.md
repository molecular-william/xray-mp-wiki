# 2-AG (2-Arachidonoylglycerol)

## Overview

2-Arachidonoylglycerol (2-AG) is an endogenous cannabinoid (endocannabinoid) and the most abundant full agonist at cannabinoid receptors CB1 and CB2. It consists of arachidonic acid esterified to glycerol at the 2-position. 2-AG is synthesized from diacylglycerol (DAG) by diacylglycerol lipase (DGL) and is degraded by monoacylglycerol lipase (MAGL). Phosphorylation of 2-AG at the 1-position produces 2-arachidonoyl-LPA (2-ALPA), which was predicted and confirmed to activate LPA1, linking the endocannabinoid and lysophospholipid signaling systems.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 2-arachidonoylglycerol

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Endogenous cannabinoid receptor agonist

2-AG is one of the two primary endogenous cannabinoids (along with AEA) and activates CB1 and CB2 receptors. In this paper, 2-AG is mentioned as a known cannabinoid agonist whose phosphorylated form (2-ALPA) was predicted to activate LPA1. 2-AG was commercially obtained from Cayman Chemical for use in assays.
