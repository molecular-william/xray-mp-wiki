# Lamotrigine

## Overview

Lamotrigine is a clinically important antiepileptic drug that blocks voltage-gated sodium channels. It blocks both the prokaryotic [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navms/) sodium channel from M. marinus and the human Nav1.1 channel with similar potencies (~100-300 uM), supporting the use of [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navms/) as a model system for studying sodium channel antagonism. Lamotrigine block is reversible in both channel types.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 6-(2,3-dichlorophenyl)-1,2,4-triazine-3,5-diamine
- **Molecular weight**: 256.09 g/mol
- **Class**: ligand

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Antiepileptic drug

Used clinically as a sodium channel blocker for epilepsy treatment.
Binds to voltage-gated sodium channels and inhibits sodium currents.


### NavMs blocker

Lamotrigine blocks [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navms/) with IC50 = 273 uM and hNav1.1 with IC50 = 196 uM
in whole-cell patch-clamp electrophysiology. Block follows single exponential
time course (tau = 450 ms) and is fully reversible.
