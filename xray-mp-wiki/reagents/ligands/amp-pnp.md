# AMP-PNP

## Overview

AMP-PNP (adenosine-5'-(beta,gamma-imido)triphosphate) is a non-hydrolyzable analogue of ATP in which the oxygen atom linking the beta and gamma phosphates is replaced by an imido (NH) group. It is used to trap ABC transporters and other ATPases in the ATP-bound state for structural studies, as the imido bond prevents hydrolysis by ATPases.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Adenosine-5'-(beta,gamma-imido)triphosphate
- **Chemical formula**: C10H17N6O12P3
- **Molecular weight**: 506.2
