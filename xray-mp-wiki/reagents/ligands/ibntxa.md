# IBNtxA (Iodobenzamide Naltrexamine)

## Overview

IBNtxA is a morphinan opioid receptor agonist with a distinct pharmacological profile. It features an iodo-benzamide moiety instead of the iodophenyl group found in the related compound MP1104, and a saturated C-ring (chair configuration) vs. the unsaturated C-ring of MP1104. In the κ-opioid receptor (KOP), IBNtxA acts as a balanced full agonist in Gi signaling and arrestin recruitment. In the μ-opioid receptor (MOP), IBNtxA displays G-protein-biased signaling with much reduced arrestin recruitment (Emax = 23%), yielding a bias factor of 12 toward G protein. Molecular docking of IBNtxA into the active-state KOP structure suggests the iodo-benzamide moiety interacts with Tyr3127.35 via a hydrogen bond, while the morphinan scaffold makes conserved interactions with Asp1383.32 and His2916.52.


## Properties

- **Class**: morphinan opioid; opioid receptor agonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Biased opioid receptor agonist

IBNtxA is a G-protein-biased agonist at MOP and a balanced agonist at KOP, making it a useful tool for studying biased opioid signaling.
