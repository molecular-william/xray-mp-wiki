# BI-167107

## Overview

BI-167107 is a potent, selective full agonist ligand for the human beta2-adrenergic receptor (beta2 AR) developed by Boehringer Ingelheim. It has an extremely high binding affinity with a Kd of 84 pM and a very slow dissociation rate (t1/2 ~150 h). It was used to occupy the beta2 AR during preparation of the active-state ternary complex with the Gs heterotrimer, enabling the first high-resolution crystal structure of an agonist-bound GPCR coupled to its G protein. The high-affinity binding of BI-167107 allowed stable complex formation and subsequent crystallization. BI-167107 induces a larger change in the fluorescence intensity and lambda_max of bimane bound to Cys265 than isoproterenol. In the presence of Nb80 nanobody, the affinity of BI-167107 for beta2 AR increases by 95-fold (Ki from 107 nM to 1.13 nM).
