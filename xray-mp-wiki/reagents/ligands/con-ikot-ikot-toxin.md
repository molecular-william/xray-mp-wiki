# Con-ikot-ikot Toxin

## Overview

Con-ikot-ikot is a homodimeric cone snail toxin from Conus striatus that acts as a positive allosteric modulator of AMPA-type ionotropic glutamate receptors. The toxin spans the tetrameric LBD gating ring, participating in extensive interactions with all four GluA2 subunits. It simultaneously fortifies intradimer interfaces and spans the interdimer cleft, acting like a straightjacket that locks the LBD layer in an expanded, activated conformation. Toxin binding requires agonist-induced clamshell closure and stabilizes the D1-D1 interface in a nondesensitized conformation. It only potentiates AMPA-subtype iGluRs, not kainate or NMDA receptors.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to GluA2 AMPA receptor LBD tetramer (A/C and B/D subunit pairs)

Extensive electrostatic interactions via negative toxin surface patches complementary to positively charged receptor residues. Key contacts: A86 (toxin C-terminal carboxyl) with K752 (B/D D1 lobe); E48 (toxin) with R660 (A/C D2 lobe); Q37 (toxin) with R453 (A/C D1 lobe). Shape complementarity between convex toxin face and concave receptor binding site

- **Key residues**: R453, R660, K752 on GluA2; A86, E48, Q37 on con-ikot-ikot
