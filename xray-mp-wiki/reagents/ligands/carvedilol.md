# Carvedilol

## Overview

Carvedilol is a beta blocker that acts as a biased agonist of beta-adrenoceptors. Unlike most beta blockers, carvedilol is an inverse agonist of the G protein-signaling pathway but stimulates arrestin-mediated signaling pathways independently of G protein activation. The structure of the beta1-adrenoceptor bound to carvedilol at 2.3 A resolution reveals a polar contact with extracellular loop 2 mediated by a bridging water molecule.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Beta1-adrenoceptor biased agonist

Carvedilol was co-crystallized with the turkey beta1-adrenoceptor thermostabilized mutant (beta1AR-m23) at 2.3 A resolution. The structure reveals that carvedilol makes a polar contact with extracellular loop 2 (EL2) mediated by a bridging water molecule. Carvedilol is a more efficient inverse agonist of the human beta1-AR Arg389 isoform than metoprolol and bisoprolol, and may confer greater cardioprotection through beta-arrestin-mediated signaling.
