# S1P (Sphingosine-1-Phosphate)

## Overview

Sphingosine-1-phosphate (S1P) is a bioactive lysophospholipid signaling molecule and the endogenous agonist for sphingosine-1-phosphate receptors (S1PR1-5). It consists of a sphingosine backbone with a phosphate group at the 1-position. S1P is produced from sphingosine by sphingosine kinases (SphK1, SphK2) and plays critical roles in lymphocyte egress, vascular development, and angiogenesis. S1P is the ligand for S1P1, whose crystal structure (PDB: 3V2Y) was used as a structural comparison with LPA1 in this paper.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: sphingosine-1-phosphate
- **Class**: lysophospholipid; sphingolipid derivative

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Endogenous S1P receptor agonist

S1P is the endogenous agonist for S1P1 (S1PR1/EDG1). The crystal structure of S1P1 bound to S1P (PDB: 3V2Y) was described in Hanson et al. 2012 and used as a structural comparison with LPA1 in this paper. The S1P1 binding pocket is linear with membrane-embedded ligand access, contrasting with the spherical pocket and extracellular access of LPA1.
