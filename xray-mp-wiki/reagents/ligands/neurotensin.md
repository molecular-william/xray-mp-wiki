# Neurotensin

## Overview

Neurotensin is an endogenous tridecapeptide (YLSRNKEDPKYYP) that acts as the primary endogenous ligand for the neurotensin receptors NTSR1 and NTSR2. It is produced in the hypothalamus and gastrointestinal tract and is involved in neurotransmission, pain modulation, thermoregulation, and regulation of gastrointestinal function. The C-terminal hexapeptide fragment NTS(8-13) (KEDPKY) retains high affinity for NTSR1 and was the ligand used in the crystal structure determination of NTSR1. Amidation of the C-terminus of neurotensin results in a 500-fold reduction in receptor affinity compared to the non-amidated NTS(8-13) peptide.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Neurotensin Receptor 1 (NTSR1)

The full-length neurotensin peptide binds deep within the transmembrane bundle of NTSR1. The NTS(8-13) fragment interacts through multiple key contacts: Arg8 forms van der Waals contacts with Phe344 (TM7); Arg9 interacts with Phe331 (TM6); Pro10 contacts Trp339 (ECL3); Tyr11 hydrogen bonds to Leu55 (N-terminal region) and Thr226 (ECL2); Ile12 contacts Tyr347 (TM7); Leu13 forms a salt bridge with Arg327 (TM6) and a hydrogen bond with Tyr146 (TM3). The C-terminal carboxylate group is critical for high-affinity binding.

- **Key residues**: Phe344, Phe331, Trp339, Leu55, Thr226, Tyr347, Arg327, Tyr146
