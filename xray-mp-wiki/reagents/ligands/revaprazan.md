# Revaprazan

## Overview

Revaprazan is a potassium-competitive acid blocker (P-CAB) with a pyrimidine-based chemical structure that is considerably different from SCH-type P-CABs. Approved for clinical use in South Korea (2007), it shows a novel binding mode in which its tetrahydroisoquinoline moiety binds deep in the cation transport conduit, similar to vonoprazan. Revaprazan has low water solubility (up to 5.3 uM) and hydrophobic nature (clog P = 5.53).

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Gastric H+,K+-ATPase (E2P state)

Tetrahydroisoquinoline moiety binds deep in the cation transport conduit; fewer close hydrophobic contacts than other P-CABs; relatively loose binding

- **Key residues**: Tyr799, Glu795, Glu820
