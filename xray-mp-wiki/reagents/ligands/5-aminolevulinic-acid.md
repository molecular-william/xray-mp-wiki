# 5-Aminolevulinic Acid

## Overview

5-Aminolevulinic acid (5-ALA) is an endogenous nonprotein amino acid that forms the first
part of the porphyrin synthesis pathway in mammals. It is used clinically for photodynamic
detection and treatment of cancer, including bladder cancer and esophageal carcinoma.
5-ALA is a substrate of the proton-coupled oligopeptide transporters PepT1 (SLC15A1) and
PepT2 (SLC15A2), with the intestinal transporter PepT1 being responsible for its oral
absorption. Crystal structures of the bacterial homolog PepT_Sh in complex with 5-ALA at
2.5 and 2.8 Å resolution revealed that 5-ALA adopts a vertical binding orientation in the
peptide binding site, with its C-terminal carboxyl group facing the extracellular gate and
making fewer interactions than physiological peptides.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Endogenous compound with low toxicity
- Selective accumulation in tumor cells

### Disadvantages

- Low oral bioavailability without transporter-mediated uptake
- Requires light activation for therapeutic effect
