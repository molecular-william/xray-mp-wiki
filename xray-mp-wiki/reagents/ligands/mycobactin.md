# Mycobactin (Fe-MBT)

## Overview

Mycobactin (MBT) is a lipid-bound siderophore produced by mycobacteria for iron
acquisition. It consists of a hydroxamate/catechol-based iron-binding core attached
to a long alkyl chain, anchoring it to the mycobacterial membrane. Mycobactin is
imported across the inner membrane by the ABC transporter IrtAB. After import,
Fe-MBT remains confined to the membrane and is reduced by the siderophore interaction
domain (SID) of IrtA to release Fe(II).

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Mycobactin import and reduction by IrtAB

Fe(III)-mycobactin is imported across the inner mycobacterial membrane by
IrtAB. After import, the membrane-anchored Fe-MBT is reduced by the SID domain
of IrtA using NADPH as an electron donor, releasing Fe(II) for bacterial
metabolism. The SID is essential for iron utilization via Fe-MBT but
dispensable for Fe-cMBT.
