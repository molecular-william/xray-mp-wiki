# IT1t

## Overview

IT1t is a small-molecule antagonist of the chemokine receptor [Human CXCR4 Chemokine Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/cxcr4/) used to obtain the highest-resolution crystal structure of [Human CXCR4 Chemokine Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/cxcr4/) at 2.5 Angstrom resolution. The ligand contains a symmetrical isothiourea group with a net positive resonance charge and an imidazothiazole ring system. IT1t occupies a binding pocket defined by side chains from helices I, II, III, and VII, making no contact with helices IV, V, and VI, in contrast to ligands in previous GPCR structures. Key interactions include a salt bridge between a protonated isothiourea nitrogen and Asp97(2.63), and a salt bridge between the protonated imidazothiazole N1 and Glu288(7.39).
