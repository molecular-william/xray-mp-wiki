# Spiperone

## Overview

Spiperone is a typical antipsychotic drug of the butyrophenone class with high affinity for the D2 dopamine receptor (DRD2) and the serotonin 5-HT2A receptor. It is used as a research tool for radioligand binding assays and was the ligand used to solve the spiperone-bound inactive-state structure of DRD2 (PDB: 7DFP) by serial femtosecond crystallography at an X-ray free-electron laser. The structure revealed a dynamic extracellular loop 2 (ECL2) conformation and an extended binding pocket (EBP) that accommodates spiperone's phenyl ring.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to D2 Dopamine Receptor (DRD2)

Spiperone binds in the orthosteric pocket of DRD2. The fluorophenyl ring penetrates deeply into a bottom hydrophobic cleft, forming CH-pi interaction with Cys118^3.36, hydrophobic interactions with Thr119^3.37, Ile122^3.40, Ser197^5.46, Phe198^5.47, Phe382^6.44, and edge-to-face pi interactions with Trp386^6.48 and Phe390^6.52. The extended binding pocket (EBP) formed by a flipped Phe110^3.28 side chain stacks with Trp90^2.60 and accommodates the phenyl ring.

- **Pocket volume**: Extended binding pocket (EBP) unique to the spiperone-bound structure, formed by flipped Phe110^3.28 stacking with Trp90^2.60, extending from subsite B to subsite C. Bottom hydrophobic cleft stabilized by steric contact between TM5 and ECL2.

- **Key residues**: Cys118^3.36, Thr119^3.37, Ile122^3.40, Ser197^5.46, Phe198^5.47, Phe382^6.44, Trp386^6.48, Phe390^6.52, Phe110^3.28, Trp90^2.60
