# Haloperidol

## Overview

Haloperidol is a typical antipsychotic drug belonging to the butyrophenone class that acts as a potent antagonist of the D2 [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) receptor ([DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/)). It is one of the most commonly used antipsychotic medications for treating schizophrenia, acute psychosis, and Tourette syndrome. The crystal structure of [DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/) bound to haloperidol (2020) revealed a second extended binding pocket (SEBP) that distinguishes [DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/) from other D2-like receptor subtypes.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural probe for DRD2 second extended binding pocket (SEBP)

The DRD2-haloperidol complex structure revealed an unexpected second extended
binding pocket (SEBP) formed by TM2, TM3, EL1, and EL2. Haloperidol's
chlorobenzene moiety extends into the SEBP, where it directly contacts
Phe110^3.28. Mutation of Phe110^3.28 to Ala dramatically enhanced haloperidol
binding (15.33-fold), while mutation to Trp or Tyr greatly reduced binding
(28-41-fold), demonstrating that the size of residue 3.28 plays a key role in
binding affinity and [DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/) subtype selectivity.


### DRD2 agonist development template

The haloperidol-bound [DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/) structure was used to guide structure-based drug
design of selective [DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/) agonists. Analysis of the SEBP and orthosteric binding
pocket (OBP) led to the discovery of highly subtype-selective [DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/) agonists
O4SE6 and O8LE6, which showed no detectable activity at DRD3 or [DRD4](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/d4-dopamine-receptor/).

## Binding Mode

### Binding to D2 [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) Receptor ([DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/))

Haloperidol forms a salt bridge with Asp114^3.32 via its tertiary amine. The chlorobenzene moiety extends into the SEBP and contacts Phe110^3.28. The hydroxyl group on the piperidine ring forms hydrogen bonds with surrounding residues.

- **Key residues**: Asp114^3.32, Phe110^3.28, Trp100^EL1
