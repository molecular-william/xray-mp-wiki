# Dequalinium - Quaternary Ammonium Cation

## Overview

Dequalinium (Dq) is a quaternary ammonium cation with two quinolinium moieties connected by a decamethylene linker. It is a cationic disinfectant and antimicrobial agent. In membrane protein research, dequalinium serves as a model substrate for studying multidrug efflux pump binding mechanisms with more localized charge distribution compared to other cationic dyes.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to AcrB Multidrug Efflux Transporter

Dequalinium binds to the upper part of the central cavity of AcrB. The top quinolinium moiety interacts with two acidic residues Asp99 and Asp101 (highly conserved), as well as Ile102, suggesting electrostatic neutralization of the formal positive charge. The bottom quinolinium moiety is not within 6 A of cavity wall residues but is ~6.3 A from Phe386 and Phe386' from neighboring subunits. The three bound Dq molecules form a tight cluster of quinolinium moieties at the top. The contacts are dominated by hydrophobic interactions with strong electrostatic contribution at the top quinolinium.
