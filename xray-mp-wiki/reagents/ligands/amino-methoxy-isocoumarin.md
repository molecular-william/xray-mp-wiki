# Amino-Methoxy-Isocoumarin (JLK-6)

## Overview

Amino-methoxy-[Isocoumarin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibiotics/isocoumarin/) (7-amino-4-chloro-3-methoxy [Isocoumarin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibiotics/isocoumarin/), also known as JLK-6) is a stable mechanism-based inhibitor of serine proteases, including the rhomboid intramembrane protease [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/glpg/). It is a derivative of [Isocoumarin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibiotics/isocoumarin/) with increased stability (half-life ~13 h in phosphate buffer) compared to diisocoumarin (DCI), making it suitable for crystallographic studies. It inhibits [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/glpg/) with an IC50 of approximately 6 uM and forms a doubly covalent-bonded alkylated acyl enzyme complex with the catalytic serine (S201) and histidine (H254) of [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/glpg/).
