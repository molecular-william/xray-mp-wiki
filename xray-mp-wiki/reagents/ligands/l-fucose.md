# L-Fucose

## Overview

L-Fucose (6-deoxy-L-galactose) is a monosaccharide that is a major constituent of N-linked glycans on cell surfaces of microbes, plants, and animals. It can serve as the sole carbon source for some bacteria. In Escherichia coli, L-fucose uptake is mediated by the MFS proton symporter FucP (fucose permease). The binding affinity between L-fucose and FucP is approximately 0.47 mM as measured by isothermal titration calorimetry (ITC).


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: L-Fucose
- **Chemical formula**: C6H12O5
- **Molecular weight**: 164.16 g/mol
- **Class**: Monosaccharide (deoxy sugar)
