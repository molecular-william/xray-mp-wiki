# LJ-4517 (Compound 2)

## Overview

LJ-4517 (compound 2) is a novel truncated nucleoside antagonist of the adenosine A2A receptor (A2AAR). It consists of a truncated 4'-oxoadenosine scaffold with a 2-hexynyl group at the C2 position and an 8-thien-2-yl (thiophene) group at the C8 position of the adenine moiety. This compound represents the first nucleoside-derived A2AAR antagonist, converting an agonist scaffold into an antagonist through C8-thiophene substitution. It has a Ki of 18.3 nM at the wild-type A2AAR.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 2-hexynyl-8-(thiophen-2-yl)-4'-oxoadenosine derivative (truncated 4'-hydroxymethyl adenine with 2-hexynyl at C2 and 8-thien-2-yl)

- **Molecular weight**: not specified
- **Class**: truncated nucleoside antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Nucleoside antagonist for A2AAR structural studies

LJ-4517 was co-crystallized with A2A-StaR2-bRIL (PDB 8CU7, 2.05 A) and A2A-StaR2-S277-bRIL (PDB 8CU6, 2.80 A) constructs. It demonstrated that a single thiophene modification at the C8 position converts an agonist scaffold into an antagonist, enabling the design of nucleoside functional switches for GPCR ligands.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to A2AAR orthosteric binding pocket

The adenine core forms pi-pi stacking with Phe168(5.29). The truncated ribose-like moiety establishes transient contacts with His278(7.43) (direct: ~5% of time, water-mediated: ~41% of time). The C8-thiophene group occupies a hydrophobic subpocket in the bottom of the binding site. The n-hexynyl group extends into an A2AAR exosite.

- **Key residues**: Phe168, His278, Phe260
