# Choline

## Overview

Choline is a quaternary ammonium compound that serves as an essential nutrient in many organisms. It is structurally similar to glycine betaine and serves as a substrate for the BCCT (Betaine/Carnitine/Choline Transporter) family of Na+-coupled symporters. While glycine betaine is the primary natural substrate for wild-type BetP from Corynebacterium glutamicum, a G153D mutant of BetP gains additional specificity for choline with sixfold increased affinity for sodium.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: choline
- **Chemical formula**: C5H15NO+
- **Molecular weight**: 104.19 g/mol
- **Class**: quaternary ammonium compound

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for BCCT family transporters

Choline is transported by members of the BCCT family, including wild-type BetP at lower affinity and the G153D mutant with significantly increased affinity. The BCCT family (Betaine/Carnitine/Choline Transporter) is named after these three related quaternary ammonium substrates.


### Nutrient and osmolyte

Choline is an essential nutrient required for cell membrane synthesis, neurotransmitter production (acetylcholine), and methyl group metabolism. It also functions as a compatible solute in some microorganisms under osmotic stress conditions.
