# Bufalin

## Overview

Bufalin is a bufadienolide cardiotonic steroid, characterized by a six-membered unsaturated lactone ring at C17 and lacking glycosylation (nonglycosylated aglycone). It is a potent inhibitor of the Na+,K+-ATPase and is a pharmacological agent found in toad venom (Chan Su). The crystal structure of the pig kidney Na+,K+-ATPase E2P form in complex with bufalin was determined at 3.4 A resolution. Unlike the digoxin and ouabain complexes, the E2P-bufalin complex was crystallized in the presence of K+, revealing both cation-binding sites I and II occupied by K+. Bufalin binding is unique among cardiotonic steroids in that its six-membered lactone ring extends ~1.5 A deeper into the binding site and directly coordinates with K+ at cation site II, explaining why bufalin shows improved affinity and complete inhibition in the presence of K+.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Na+,K+-ATPase inhibition

Bufalin inhibits Na+,K+-ATPase by binding to the E2P phosphoenzyme state. Unlike ouabain and digoxin, bufalin does not require Mg2+ for binding and its affinity is improved rather than antagonized by K+ (Kd ~9 nM with K+ vs 14 nM without). The six-membered lactone ring directly coordinates with K+ at cation site II, retaining the E2P conformation without unwinding alphaM4. This K+-independent behavior is unique among cardiotonic steroids.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Na+,K+-ATPase alpha subunit (E2P state)

Bufalin binds in the same cavity as ouabain and digoxin, formed by alphaM1-M6. It contains only two conserved hydroxyl groups (OH14beta and OH3beta) compared to ouabain's four. OH14beta hydrogen bonds with Thr797 (alphaM6), stabilized by Asp121 (alphaM2). The six-membered lactone ring extends ~1.5 A deeper into the hydrophobic funnel than the five-membered lactones of cardenolides, and its carbonyl group directly coordinates with K+ at cation site II. The bulky six-membered lactone prevents alphaM4 unwinding that would normally occur upon K+ binding at site II. The lack of hydroxyl substituents and glycosylation at the steroid core facilitates deeper sliding into the binding site.

- **Key residues**: Thr797, Asp121 (alphaM6/M2); Leu125 (alphaM2); Ala323 (alphaM4); Ile800 (alphaM6); cation site II K+ ion
