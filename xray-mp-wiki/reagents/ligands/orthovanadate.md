# Orthovanadate (VO₃⁻)

## Overview

Orthovanadate (VO₃⁻) is the monomeric form of vanadate and a potent inhibitor of P-type ATPases including the sarcoplasmic reticulum Ca²⁺-ATPase (SERCA). It binds at the catalytic site in a trigonal bipyramidal geometry analogous to the pentavalent transition state of phosphoryl transfer, mimicking the dephosphorylation intermediate of the SERCA reaction cycle. Orthovanadate binding occurs preferentially in the E2 state of SERCA.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural probe for dephosphorylation transition state of P-type ATPases

Vanadate binds at the catalytic site of SERCA1a in the E2 state as planar VO₃⁻, adopting a trigonal bipyramidal conformation that mimics the pentavalent intermediate of dephosphorylation. This was confirmed by anomalous difference Fourier maps using long-wavelength X-ray data (0.98 and 2.21 Å), providing definitive structural evidence for the associative transfer mechanism of phosphate ester hydrolysis in P-type ATPases.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to SERCA1a

Coordinated between Asp351 and an attacking water molecule, flanked by a Mg²⁺ ion and three water molecules, forming a trigonal bipyramidal complex mimicking the phosphate transition state
