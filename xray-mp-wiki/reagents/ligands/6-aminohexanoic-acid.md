# 6-Aminohexanoic Acid (6-AHA)

## Overview

6-Aminohexanoic acid (6-AHA) is a seven-carbon amino acid (epsilon-aminocaproic acid)
used as a model substrate analog for dipeptide transport assays. It mimics the
dipeptide substrate recognized by proton-dependent oligopeptide transporters (POTs)
and is frequently used in radiolabeled uptake assays to measure transporter activity.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Functional assay for POT transporter activity

Radiolabeled 14C-6-AHA uptake assays are used to verify transport activity of
POT family members. Cells expressing the transporter take up 6-AHA in a
proton-coupled manner. Uptake is measured by scintillation counting after
filtration, providing a quantitative readout of transporter function.
