# ZM241385

## Overview

ZM241385 is a bicyclic xanthine-based antagonist of the adenosine A2A receptor (A2AAR). It is one of the most widely studied A2AAR antagonists and has been used extensively for structural studies of A2AAR in complex with various constructs. ZM241385 has been crystallized with A2AAR in 17 different crystal structures. It serves as the key comparison ligand for understanding subtype selectivity between A1AR and A2AAR. ZM241385 was used in the A2AAR reference structure (PDB 4EIY) for structural comparison with DU172-bound A1AR.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 4-(2-[7-amino-2-[2-furyl][1,2,4]triazolo[2,3-a][1,3,5]triazin-5-ylamino]ethyl)phenol
- **Chemical formula**: C17H14N8O
- **Molecular weight**: 362.34 g/mol
- **Class**: bicyclic xanthine

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### A2AAR structural biology tool

ZM241385 has been co-crystallized with A2AAR constructs 17 times. It served as a reference ligand for comparing binding modes of Preladenant derivatives. The bicyclic scaffold differs from the tricyclic Preladenant scaffold but shares similar orientation in the A2AAR binding pocket.


### Reference structure for A1AR-A2AAR comparison

ZM241385 was bound to the A2AAR reference structure (PDB 4EIY) and compared with DU172 bound to the A1AR structure. Despite different orientations of the xanthine ring relative to the triazolo-triazine substructure, key interactions anchoring the ligand in the binding site are preserved between the two receptors.


### Binding affinity comparison between A1AR and A2AAR

ZM241385 was tested in competition binding experiments at both A1AR and A2AAR. The M270T substitution in A2AAR led to a significant loss of ZM241385 affinity, since M270 is involved in coordinating ZM241385 in the A2AAR binding pocket.
