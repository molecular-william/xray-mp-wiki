# Reboxetine

## Overview

Reboxetine is a selective norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor (NRI) and antidepressant used for the treatment of panic disorder and ADHD. It is an enantiomerically pure compound (R,R)-reboxetine that exhibits high-affinity binding to human NET.

## Properties


## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural studies of biogenic amine transporters

Co-crystallized with dDAT_mfc to reveal how bulky substituents on aromatic rings of SNRIs are accommodated in the substrate-binding pocket. The dichlorophenyl group inserts into the hydrophobic cleft at subsite B.


### Pharmacological studies

Used as a NET-specific probe in neuroscience research and as a therapeutic agent for affective disorders.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- High selectivity for NET over DAT and SERT
- Enantiomerically pure (R,R) form available
- Useful for structure-activity relationship studies

### Disadvantages

- Limited clinical use in some regions
- Structural similarity to other tricyclic antidepressants may cause off-target effects
