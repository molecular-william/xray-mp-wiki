# cis-22a (PCHPD TRPV6 Inhibitor)

## Overview

cis-22a is a (4-phenylcyclohexyl)piperazine derivative (PCHPD) that acts as a potent and selective nanomolar-affinity inhibitor of the epithelial calcium channel TRPV6. It inhibits TRPV6 by plugging the open pore at the intracellular entrance and by binding to the modulatory lipid-binding site 2 (LBS-2), mimicking the physiological Ca2+-calmodulin inactivation mechanism. cis-22a is the prototypical member of the PCHPD class of TRPV6 inhibitors.


## Properties


## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### TRPV6 inhibition by pore block

cis-22a binds at the intracellular entrance of the TRPV6 ion channel pore, physically obstructing ion conductance. The compound mimics the tryptophan cage mechanism of calmodulin-dependent inactivation. Four W583 residues from each TRPV6 subunit form a cubic cage around the inhibitor. Key pore-lining residues involved in binding include I575, D580, and W583.


### TRPV6 inhibition via LBS-2 binding

cis-22a also binds at lipid-binding site 2 (LBS-2), located at the interface between S3, S4, and S4-S5 linker of one subunit and S5 and S6 of the neighboring subunit. This site is normally occupied by cholesterol/cholesteryl hemisuccinate. Key residues include R470, Q483, C463, N464, and F504.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to TRPV6

- **Key residues**: Pore: I575, D580, W583. LBS-2: R470, Q483, C463, N464, F504

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Nanomolar potency (IC50 ~83 nM for hTRPV6)
- High selectivity for TRPV6 over other TRP channels
- Inactivation-mimicking mechanism that is compatible with natural cellular processes
- Biomimetic design strategy reduces potential for adverse side effects

### Disadvantages

- Currently a research tool; not yet evaluated in vivo
- Limited selectivity data against broader ion channel panel
