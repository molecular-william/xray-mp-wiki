# CVX15

## Overview

CVX15 is a 16-residue cyclic peptide antagonist of the chemokine receptor [Human CXCR4 Chemokine Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/cxcr4/) used to obtain crystal structures of [Human CXCR4 Chemokine Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/cxcr4/) at 2.9 Angstrom resolution. The peptide forms a disulfide-stabilized (Cys4-Cys13) beta-hairpin with D-Pro8-Pro9 at the tip of the turn exposed to the extracellular milieu. Key interactions include Arg1 and Arg2 at the peptide N-terminus forming polar interactions with Asp187, Thr117(3.33), and Asp171(4.60), and Arg14 forming a salt bridge with Asp262(6.58). The bulky naphthalene ring of NaI3 anchors in a hydrophobic region bordered by helix V. CVX15 binding induces a conformational change in [Human CXCR4 Chemokine Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/cxcr4/), including a tilt in the extracellular part of helix V.
