# Maraviroc

## Overview

Maraviroc (UK-427,857) is an antiretroviral drug used for the treatment of HIV-1 infection. It is a selective allosteric inverse agonist of the [CCR5 Chemokine Receptor (C-C Chemokine Receptor Type 5)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/ccr5/) chemokine receptor, blocking HIV-1 entry by binding to [CCR5 Chemokine Receptor (C-C Chemokine Receptor Type 5)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/ccr5/) and stabilizing it in an inactive conformation. Maraviroc was the first [CCR5 Chemokine Receptor (C-C Chemokine Receptor Type 5)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/ccr5/) co-receptor antagonist approved for clinical use. The crystal structure of the CCR5-maraviroc complex revealed that maraviroc binds in a deep pocket within the transmembrane domain of [CCR5 Chemokine Receptor (C-C Chemokine Receptor Type 5)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/ccr5/), formed by residues from helices I, II, III, V, VI, and VII. The drug contains a tropane group (salt bridge with Glu283(7.39)), a carboxamide nitrogen (hydrogen bond with Tyr251(6.51)), a triazole group (hydrogen bonds with Tyr37(1.39) and a water molecule), a cyclohexane ring with fluorine substituents (hydrogen bonds with Thr195(5.39) and Thr259(6.59)), and a phenyl group (hydrophobic interactions with five aromatic residues).

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### HIV-1 treatment — CCR5 co-receptor antagonist

Maraviroc blocks HIV-1 entry into host cells by binding to the [CCR5 Chemokine Receptor (C-C Chemokine Receptor Type 5)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/ccr5/) co-receptor, preventing interaction with the viral envelope glycoprotein gp120. It is used in combination antiretroviral therapy for treatment-experienced patients with CCR5-tropic (R5-tropic) HIV-1.


### GPCR structural biology — stabilized inactive state for crystallization

Maraviroc was co-crystallized with [CCR5 Chemokine Receptor (C-C Chemokine Receptor Type 5)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/ccr5/) to obtain the crystal structure of the CCR5-maraviroc complex. The ligand's extensive interactions with the receptor binding pocket (salt bridge, multiple hydrogen bonds, and hydrophobic contacts) stabilized [CCR5 Chemokine Receptor (C-C Chemokine Receptor Type 5)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/ccr5/) in an inactive conformation suitable for crystallization and structure determination.
