# SBL-MC-31

## Overview

SBL-MC-31 is a newly synthesized seven-residue macrocyclic peptide antagonist of the human melanocortin 4 receptor ([MC4-R](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/mc4-r/)). It is an expanded analog of [SHU9119](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/shu9119/), replacing His6 with Pro6 and inserting Arg10 between Trp9 and Lys10, increasing the macrocycle size from six to seven residues. SBL-MC-31 was co-crystallized with [MC4-R](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/mc4-r/) at 3.3 A resolution (PDB 8WKZ). The Arg10 side chain forms hydrogen bonds with N285^7.36, revealing a second binding sub-pocket at the TM7 domain. SBL-MC-31-derived peptides achieved up to 132-fold [MC4-R](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/mc4-r/) over MC1-R selectivity, significantly better than [SHU9119](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/shu9119/). SBL-MC-31 itself induces modest Kir7.1 channel closure.
