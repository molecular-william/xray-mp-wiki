# Ethidium - Fluorescent Intercalating Dye

## Overview

Ethidium (Et) is a fluorescent intercalating dye that is widely used as a substrate probe for multidrug efflux pumps. It is a phenanthridinium derivative that intercalates into DNA and is commonly used in molecular biology. In membrane protein research, ethidium serves as a model substrate for studying multidrug transport mechanisms.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to AcrB Multidrug Efflux Transporter

Ethidium binds in the central cavity of AcrB at a site distinct from but partially overlapping with the R6G binding site, approximately 6 A above the R6G binding pocket. The binding involves mostly hydrophobic interactions with Phe386, Ala385 from one subunit and Phe386' from the neighboring subunit. The phenyl moiety of ethidium is bound above Phe386' at ~4 A distance. The carbonyl oxygen of Phe386 is close to one amino group of Et (3.6 A), potentially contributing electrostatic stabilization. Three bound Et molecules are ~3.2 A apart from one another, indicating strong drug-drug interactions within the central cavity.
