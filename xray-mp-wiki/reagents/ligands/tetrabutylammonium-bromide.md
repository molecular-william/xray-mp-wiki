# Tetrabutylammonium Bromide (TBSb)

## Overview

[TBA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tetrabutylammonium/) bromide (TBSb) is a quaternary ammonium salt consisting of the [TBA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tetrabutylammonium/) cation and a bromide anion. It is used as an open channel blocker of cation-selective ion channels. The bromide counterion provides a bromine atom that generates a strong anomalous signal in X-ray crystallography, making TBSb particularly useful for identifying the binding site of quaternary ammonium compounds in channel structures.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: tetrabutylammonium bromide
- **Molecular weight**: 323.38 g/mol
- **Class**: quaternary ammonium salt (ion channel blocker)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallographic probe for quaternary ammonium binding

TBSb at 5 mM was used for soaking of [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) crystals. The bromine atom in the bromide counterion provides a strong anomalous signal, allowing precise identification of the [TBA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tetrabutylammonium/) binding site in the [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) pore. The anomalous difference density revealed the position of the bound [TBA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tetrabutylammonium/) molecule in the center of the membrane.
