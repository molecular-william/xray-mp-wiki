# Vonoprazan

## Overview

Vonoprazan is a clinically approved K+-competitive acid blocker (P-CAB) used to treat gastric acid-related diseases. It was co-crystallized with pig gastric H+,K+-ATPase in the luminal-open E2P state at 2.8 Å resolution (PDB 5YLU). Vonoprazan binds in the middle of a conduit running from the gastric lumen to the cation-binding site, partially overlapping with the binding site of SCH28080 but with a distinct binding mode. Vonoprazan blocks access of K+ to the transport site by occupying the luminal cavity near Tyr799. Key binding residues include Tyr799 along with other residues in the transmembrane cavity. Unlike proton pump inhibitors (e.g., omeprazole) that require acid activation, vonoprazan binds directly to the active E2P state of the ATPase.


## Properties

- **Class**: K+-competitive acid blocker (P-CAB); pyrrole derivative

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Clinical acid suppressant

Vonoprazan is used as a potassium-competitive acid blocker for treating gastroesophageal reflux disease, peptic ulcers, and Helicobacter pylori eradication.
