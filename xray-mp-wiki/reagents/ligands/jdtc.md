# JDTic

## Overview

JDTic is a potent and selective [Kappa Opioid Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/kappa-opioid-receptor/) (KOR) antagonist/inverse agonist. It is a member of the trans-3,4-dimethyl-4-(3-hydroxyphenyl)piperidine class of KOR antagonists. The crystal structure of KOR bound to JDTic was first solved in 2012 (PDB 4DJH), and the KOR-JDTic-Nb6 complex structure (2020) revealed a unique nanobody-stabilized inactive state with a distinct ECL2 conformation.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural probe for KOR inactive state

JDTic acts as a potent inverse agonist that stabilizes KOR in a very low-affinity
state for agonists. In the KOR-JDTic-Nb6 complex (2020), the structure revealed
that ECL2 moves ~6 A to form an apparent lid on top of JDTic. The binding
affinity of JDTic is nearly identical with or without Nb6, confirming that
JDTic's inverse agonist activity alone stabilizes the receptor in a low-affinity
state for agonists.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [Kappa Opioid Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/kappa-opioid-receptor/) (KOR)

JDTic binds in the orthosteric pocket of KOR. In the Nb6-stabilized structure, ECL2 forms a lid on top of JDTic, a feature also observed in other GPCR structures including LSD-bound 5-HT2B and risperidone-bound D2 [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) receptors.
