# Dihydrotestosterone (DHT)

## Overview

Dihydrotestosterone (DHT) is the most potent endogenous androgen steroid hormone
and a potent agonist of the androgen receptor (AR). DHT is biosynthesized from
dehydroepiandrosterone ([DHEA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dhea/)) via a multi-step pathway involving 3beta-hydroxysteroid
dehydrogenase (3HSD), 17beta-hydroxysteroid dehydrogenase type 3 (17HSD3), and
5alpha-reductases. [DHEA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dhea/) itself is produced by [Steroid Sulfatase (hSTS)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/steroid-sulfatase/) from
[DHEA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dhea/) sulfate (DHEAS). DHT plays a critical role in prostate cancer progression
and is a therapeutic target in advanced prostate cancer.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 5alpha-androstan-17beta-ol-3-one
- **Chemical formula**: C19H30O2
- **Molecular weight**: 290.44 g/mol
- **Class**: steroid hormone (androgen)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Potent androgen receptor agonist in prostate cancer

DHT is the most potent agonist of the androgen receptor (AR). It is produced from [DHEA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dhea/) (itself generated by hSTS from DHEAS) through the actions of 3beta-HSD, 17beta-HSD3, and 5alpha-reductases types 1/2. In LNCaP prostate cancer cells, hSTS-catalyzed production of [DHEA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dhea/) feeds into DHT biosynthesis, driving AR signaling and cancer progression.
