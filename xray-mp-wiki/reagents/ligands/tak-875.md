# TAK-875 (Fasiglifam)

## Overview

TAK-875 (fasiglifam) is an orally available, potent and selective partial agonist of the human GPR40 (FFAR1) receptor. It acts as an ago-allosteric modulator, binding to a non-canonical allosteric pocket between TM3-5 and ECL2. TAK-875 reached phase III clinical trials for the potential treatment of type-2 diabetes mellitus. It stimulates glucose-dependent insulin secretion with a unique mode of action distinct from full agonists.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### hGPR40 structure determination

TAK-875 was used as the ligand for crystallization of the hGPR40-TAK-875 co-complex in lipidic cubic phase, yielding a 2.3 A resolution structure. Present throughout purification at 1 uM concentration.


### Type-2 diabetes treatment

TAK-875 advanced to phase III clinical trials as a novel treatment for type-2 diabetes mellitus, demonstrating improved glycaemic control and low hypoglycaemic risk.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human GPR40 (FFAR1) receptor

Carboxylate moiety participates in extensive charge network with Arg183(5.39), Arg258(7.35), Tyr91(3.37), Tyr240(6.51), Glu172(ECL2), Ser187(5.43), Asn241(6.53), Asn244(6.55)

- **Pocket volume**: Non-canonical site between TM3-5 and ECL2, roughly perpendicular to canonical orthosteric site
- **Key residues**: ['Arg183(5.39)', 'Arg258(7.35)', 'Tyr91(3.37)', 'Tyr240(6.51)', 'Asn244(6.55)']
