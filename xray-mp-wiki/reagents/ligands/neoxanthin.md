# Neoxanthin

## Overview

Neoxanthin is a [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) pigment found in photosynthetic light-harvesting complexes. In [LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/), it is the 9-cis isomer and occupies a peripheral binding site where it uniquely protrudes into the lipid bilayer. It is present in a stoichiometric ratio of one per two [LUTEIN](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/lutein/) molecules and may help protect exposed chlorophylls against photodamage through interactions with other pigment-protein complexes in the membrane.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: (3R,3'S,5R,6R)-3,6-diepoxy-3',5'-dihydroxy-beta,c-carotene
- **Chemical formula**: C40H56O4
- **Molecular weight**: 600.87 g/mol
- **Class**: xanthophyll [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Peripheral carotenoid binding in LHC-II

One neoxanthin molecule is bound per [LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/) monomer at a peripheral site. It is the 9-cis isomer, the only isomer found in chloroplast thylakoids. Unlike other pigments in [LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/), neoxanthin protrudes into the lipid bilayer, potentially interacting with other pigment-protein complexes in the membrane and helping to protect exposed chlorophylls against photodamage.
