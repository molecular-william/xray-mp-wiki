# AEA (Anandamide / N-Arachidonoylethanolamine)

## Overview

Anandamide (AEA, N-arachidonoylethanolamine) is an endogenous cannabinoid (endocannabinoid) and a primary agonist at cannabinoid receptors CB1 and CB2. It consists of arachidonic acid amide-linked to ethanolamine. AEA is synthesized from N-acylphosphatidylethanolamine (NAPE) by NAPE-specific phospholipase D and is degraded by fatty acid amide hydrolase (FAAH). Phosphorylation of AEA produces pAEA (phosphorylated anandamide), which was predicted and confirmed to activate LPA1, linking the endocannabinoid and lysophospholipid signaling systems.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: N-arachidonoylethanolamine
- **Class**: endocannabinoid; ethanolamine amide

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Endogenous cannabinoid receptor agonist

AEA is one of the two primary endogenous cannabinoids (along with 2-AG) and activates CB1 and CB2 receptors. In this paper, AEA is mentioned as a known cannabinoid agonist whose phosphorylated form (pAEA) was predicted to activate LPA1. AEA was commercially obtained from Cayman Chemical for use in assays.
