# CP-99,994

## Overview

CP-99,994 is a high-affinity, selective non-peptide antagonist of the human neurokinin 1 receptor (NK1R). It was developed from the first-disclosed non-peptide NK1R antagonist CP-96,345 through medicinal chemistry optimization. CP-99,994 features a central six-membered piperidine ring scaffold and displays high affinity for NK1R as well as efficacy in animal models, providing a valuable pharmacological tool for investigating Substance P signaling through NK1R. It was co-crystallized with NK1R at 3.27 A resolution (PDB 6HLL).

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### NK1R antagonist for structural biology

CP-99,994 was used in co-crystallization with the human NK1R-PGS fusion construct, yielding the first structure of NK1R with a small-molecule antagonist at 3.27 A resolution. The structure revealed the binding mode of the prototypical antagonist scaffold, with its piperidine core situated between F268(6.55) and Q165(4.60), arm 1 targeting a hydrophobic pocket formed by N109(3.29), P112(3.32) and I113(3.33), and arm 2 contacting H197(5.39), V200(5.42) and I204(5.46).
