# Protoporphyrin IX (PpIX)

## Overview

Protoporphyrin IX (PpIX) is an endogenous porphyrin compound that serves as a natural ligand of the translocator protein (TSPO). In [RSTSPO](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/rstspo/), PpIX copurifies with the protein and is observed bound in a cavity between TM-I and TM-II underneath loop LP1. Binding of PpIX to [RSTSPO](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/rstspo/) is consistent with the proposed role of TSPO in porphyrin transport and regulation of the switch between photosynthesis and respiration in *Rhodobacter sphaeroides*. The A139T mutation reduces PpIX binding affinity from a Kd of 0.3 µM (wild-type) to 1.9 µM.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Endogenous TSPO ligand

PpIX is a proposed natural ligand of TSPO, binding with high nanomolar affinity. In R. sphaeroides, TSPO is involved in porphyrin transport and regulates the switch from photosynthesis to respiration in response to increased oxygen.


### Structural probe

Copurifies with [RSTSPO](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/rstspo/) and is observed in the crystal structure of the A139T mutant, confirming the porphyrin binding site between TM-I and TM-II.
