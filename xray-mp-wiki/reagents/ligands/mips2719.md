# MIPS2719

## Overview

MIPS2719 is a selective A1 adenosine receptor antagonist. It was synthesized as a derivative of DU172 by reaction with dimethylamine to introduce a N,N-dimethylsulfamoyl group. The chemical name is N-(3-(8-cyclohexyl-2,6-dioxo-1-propyl-1,2,6,7-tetrahydro-3H-purin-3-yl)propyl)-4-(N,N-dimethylsulfamoyl)benzamide. In the A1 adenosine receptor study, MIPS2719 was docked into the A1-AR crystal structure (PDB 5UEN) along with other selective antagonists (DPCPX, PSB36, rolofylline, SLV320, and MIPS2712) to understand the molecular basis for A1 versus A2A selectivity.
