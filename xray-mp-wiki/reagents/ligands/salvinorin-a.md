# Salvinorin A

## Overview

Salvinorin A (SalA) is a selective kappa opioid receptor (KOP) agonist derived from the plant Salvia divinorum. It is a neoclerodane diterpene and one of the few known non-nitrogenous psychoactive compounds. SalA acts as a full agonist at KOP and was used in this study for nanobody screening and as a reference full agonist in functional assays.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Kappa opioid receptor (KOP)

- Carbonyl oxygen at 1-position forms hydrogen bond with Y312^7.35
- Acetyl group at 2-position forms hydrophobic interactions with Y312^7.35 and may hydrogen bond with Y313^7.36
- Methyl ester at 4-position sits in a small enclosed pocket, stabilized by H-bond with C210^ECL2 backbone NH
- Furan ring engages in edge-face aromatic interactions with Y139^3.33


## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Selective for KOP over other opioid receptors
- Non-nitrogenous structure distinguishes it from classical opioids
- Useful pharmacological tool for studying KOP signaling

### Disadvantages

- Hallucinogenic and dysphoric effects limit clinical utility
- Poor oral bioavailability due to ester hydrolysis
