# PK11195

## Overview

PK11195 is an isoquinoline carboxamide ligand that binds to the translocator protein (TSPO) with high affinity. It is extensively used in positron emission tomography (PET) as a diagnostic imaging agent for detecting areas of inflammation in the brain, particularly in neurological diseases such as Alzheimer's and Parkinson's. PK11195 binding to [RSTSPO](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/rstspo/) is affected by the A139T mutation (mimicking the human A147T polymorphism), with Kd increasing from 10 µM for wild-type to 42 µM for the mutant.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### PET imaging agent

PK11195 is used as a PET ligand for visualizing neuroinflammation by binding to TSPO, which is upregulated in activated microglia in various neurological disorders.


### TSPO binding studies

Added to crystallization medium to improve crystal quality of [RSTSPO](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/rstspo/). Binding affinity is sensitive to the A139T mutation, making it a useful probe for studying the effects of the human A147T polymorphism.
