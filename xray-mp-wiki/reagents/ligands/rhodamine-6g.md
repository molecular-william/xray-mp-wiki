# Rhodamine 6G - Fluorescent Xanthene Dye

## Overview

Rhodamine 6G is a fluorescent xanthene dye widely used as a substrate probe for multidrug efflux pumps. It is a positively charged organic cation that serves as a model substrate for MATE family transporters and other drug efflux systems. Rhodamine 6G binds to NorM-VC with a dissociation constant (Kd) of 2.09 +/- 0.0115 uM as determined by fluorescence polarization. It is also used in functional transport assays to measure efflux pump activity in whole cells.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to NorM-VC

Rhodamine 6G binds to the internal cavity of NorM-VC with Kd = 2.09 +/- 0.0115 uM. Excitation wavelength: 485 nm, Emission wavelength: 535 nm. The binding was measured by fluorescence polarization using native NorM-VC protein.
