# Agmatine

## Overview

Agmatine (4-aminobutylamine) is a polyamine formed by the arginine decarboxylation reaction catalyzed by the enzyme AdiA in Escherichia coli. It serves as the exchanged substrate in the AdiC arginine/agmatine antiporter, which functions as a virtual proton pump in the extreme acid resistance system. AdiC imports L-arginine from the extracellular environment and exports agmatine, with the decarboxylation of arginine consuming a proton and thereby raising the intracellular pH. The rebuilt AdiC structure reveals a distal substrate binding site between Trp293 and the distal gate (Tyr93, Glu208, Tyr365) that preferentially favors agmatine binding over arginine.
