# Daridorexant

## Overview

Daridorexant (ACT-541468, formerly nemorexant) is a dual orexin receptor antagonist (DORA) approved by the FDA for the treatment of insomnia. It features a 4-methyl-5-chloro-benzimidazole portion connected via a (2S)-methylpyrrolidine ring to a 5-methoxy-2-triazolephenyl moiety. The crystal structure of daridorexant bound to OX1R at 3.03 A resolution revealed the benzimidazole portion sits in a similar position to the substituted benzoxazole of [Suvorexant](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/suvorexant/), making an intramolecular pi-stacking arrangement to the triazolephenyl moiety. The central pyrrolidine ring connects the two aromatic pi-stacking substituents and is located under the salt bridges that stabilize ECL2 placement.
