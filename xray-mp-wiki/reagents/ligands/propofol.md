# Propofol

## Overview

Propofol (2,6-diisopropylphenol) is a widely used intravenous general anesthetic. It acts as a positive allosteric modulator of GABA-A receptors and an inhibitor of excitatory pentameric ligand-gated ion channels (pLGICs) such as nicotinic acetylcholine receptors. X-ray crystallography revealed propofol binds to an intra-subunit cavity in the upper transmembrane domain of the bacterial pLGIC GLIC.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to GLIC (pentameric ligand-gated ion channel)

Van der Waals interactions with residues from M1, M2, M3, M4 helices and beta6-beta7 loop; B-factor 135 A^2 indicating significant mobility within the cavity

- **Key residues**: I201, I202, M205, L206, V242, Y254, T255, I258, I259, F303, N307, Y119
