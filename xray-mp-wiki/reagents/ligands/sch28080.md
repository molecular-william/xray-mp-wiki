# SCH28080

## Overview

SCH28080 is a K+-competitive acid blocker (P-CAB) and prototype antagonist of the gastric H+,K+-ATPase (the gastric proton pump). It was co-crystallized with pig gastric H+,K+-ATPase in the luminal-open E2P state at 2.8 Å resolution (PDB 5YLV). SCH28080 binds in the middle of a conduit running from the gastric lumen to the cation-binding site, blocking access of K+ to the transport site. The binding site partially overlaps with vonoprazan but is clearly distinct; SCH28080 also permitted Rb+ binding at the cation-binding site in the crystal. SCH28080 was found to be hepatotoxic and has never been developed for clinical use, but serves as an important in vitro tool compound. Key binding residues include Tyr799 and other residues in the luminal cavity.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 3-(cyanomethyl)-2-methyl-8-(phenylmethoxy)imidazo[1,2-a]pyridine
- **Class**: K+-competitive acid blocker (P-CAB); imidazopyridine derivative

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### H+,K+-ATPase antagonist

SCH28080 is a specific antagonist of gastric H+,K+-ATPase that blocks acid secretion by competing with K+ for the luminal cation-binding site.
