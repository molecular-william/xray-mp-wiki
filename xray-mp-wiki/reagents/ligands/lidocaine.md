# Lidocaine

## Overview

Lidocaine is a widely used local anesthetic (LA) and class IB antiarrhythmic drug (AAD)
that blocks voltage-gated sodium channels. It is used clinically for local anesthesia and
for treatment of ventricular arrhythmias. Lidocaine binds to a receptor site in the central
cavity of sodium channels, on the intracellular side of the selectivity filter. A crystal
structure of the bacterial sodium channel NavAb in complex with lidocaine revealed the drug
bound in the central cavity, with its positively charged tertiary amino group projecting
toward the selectivity filter. The fenestrations connecting the lipid phase to the central
cavity control lidocaine's access to its binding site during resting-state block, with the
size of the fenestrations modulating drug potency.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Fast onset of action for local anesthesia
- Rapid dissociation from sodium channels (class IB kinetics)
- Well-characterized pharmacology and safety profile

### Disadvantages

- Cardiotoxic at high doses
- CNS side effects including seizures at toxic levels
