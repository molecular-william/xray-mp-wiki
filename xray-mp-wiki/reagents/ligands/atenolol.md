# Atenolol

## Overview

Atenolol is a selective beta-1 adrenergic receptor antagonist (beta-blocker) used to stabilize the beta2-adrenergic receptor during solubilization and purification for structural studies. It is a competitive antagonist that binds to the orthosteric site of adrenergic receptors, locking the receptor in its inactive conformation. Atenolol is widely used in pharmaceutical applications for the treatment of hypertension and cardiovascular disorders.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Beta2-Adrenergic Receptor (beta2 AR)

Atenolol binds to the orthosteric site of the beta2-adrenergic receptor, forming hydrogen bonds with the conserved serine residues in transmembrane segment 5 and 6 (Ser193, Ser194, Ser204). The secondary amine forms a salt bridge with Asp113 in TM3. The chlorophenyl ring makes hydrophobic contacts with residues in the transmembrane bundle. During purification of the beta2 AR-T4L fusion protein, 30 uM atenolol was added to the solubilization buffer to stabilize the receptor.

- **Key residues**: Asp113, Ser193, Ser194, Ser204
