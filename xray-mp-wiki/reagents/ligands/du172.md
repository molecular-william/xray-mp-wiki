# DU172

## Overview

DU172 (4-((3-(8-cyclohexyl-2,6-dioxo-1-propyl-1,2,6,7-tetrahydro-3H-purin-3-yl)propyl)carbamoyl)benzenesulfonic acid) is a selective, covalent adenosine A1 receptor antagonist. It forms a benzene-sulfonate linkage with Y271(7.36) in the A1AR binding pocket. DU172 was used as the crystallization ligand for the first high-resolution A1AR structure (PDB 5UEN, 3.2 A). It promotes substantial thermal stabilization (~16 C increase in Tm) of the A1AR construct.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 4-((3-(8-cyclohexyl-2,6-dioxo-1-propyl-1,2,6,7-tetrahydro-3H-purin-3-yl)propyl)carbamoyl)benzenesulfonic acid
- **Chemical formula**: C25H34N5O6S
- **Molecular weight**: 531.64 g/mol
- **Class**: covalent xanthine antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization ligand for A1AR structure determination

DU172 was used as the covalent antagonist for crystallization of the human A1AR. The protein was purified and crystallized in the presence of 0.2 uM DU172. The covalent benzene-sulfonate linkage with Y271(7.36) anchors the ligand in the binding site. DU172 was preferred over the related covalent antagonist FSCPX due to higher chemical stability.


### Thermal stability assay for ligand screening

DU172 promoted the largest increase in thermal stability among tested ligands, raising the Tm of the A1AR construct by approximately 16 C. It was one of only two covalent compounds (along with FSCPX) that substantially stabilized the A1AR in thermal stability assays.
