# AU1235

## Overview

AU1235 is an adamantyl urea compound that shows good bactericidal activity against both Mycobacterium tuberculosis (MIC = 0.6 uM) and Mycobacterium smegmatis (MIC = 9.8 uM). It targets MmpL3, an essential mycobacterial membrane transporter involved in mycolic acid biosynthesis. The compound adopts a similar binding mode to SQ109 in the MmpL3 transmembrane binding pocket.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to MmpL3 (Mycobacterium smegmatis)

AU1235 binds in the transmembrane region of MmpL3 with a similar position to SQ109. The bulky indole group fits snugly into the hydrophobic subsite on top of the channel, producing a larger hydrophobic interaction than the geranyl tail of SQ109. Binding disrupts both Asp-Tyr pairs participating in proton translocation. The amide group hydrogen bonds to Asp645, and the carbonyl oxygen accepts a hydrogen bond from Tyr646. The carbocyclic spiro group inserts into the bulky hydrophobic subsite at the bottom of the tunnel.
