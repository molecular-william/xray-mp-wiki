# Suvorexant

## Overview

Suvorexant is a dual orexin receptor antagonist (DORA) with subnanomolar affinities for both OX1R and OX2R. It was the first orexin receptor antagonist approved by the US FDA (2014) for the treatment of insomnia. Suvorexant binds with high affinity to both orexin receptor subtypes, with dissociation kinetics that are considerably slower than subtype-selective antagonists like [EMPA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/empa/) (k_off = 0.012 min^-1 for OX2R, 0.005 min^-1). The crystal structures of suvorexant-bound OX1R and OX2R revealed an intramolecular pi-stacked horseshoe conformation of the ligand in the orthosteric binding pocket. MM-PB/SA binding free energies are comparable for both receptors (-9.3 kcal/mol for OX1R, -9.1 kcal/mol for OX2R). Compared to [Lemborexant](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/lemborexant/), suvorexant has a larger contact surface area (641.3 A^2 vs 607.8 A^2) but faster dissociation. The 1,4-diazepane linker in suvorexant does not optimize for ring stacking, unlike the pyrimidine-pyridine stack in [Lemborexant](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/lemborexant/). Suvorexant forms a hydrogen bond with Asn324^6.55 but not with Gln134^3.32.
