# BIIL260

## Overview

BIIL260 is a selective antagonist for the leukotriene B4 receptor [Leukotriene B4 Receptor 1 (BLT1) — Guinea Pig and Human](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/blt1/), bearing a benzamidine moiety that enables it to bind both the orthosteric ligand-binding site and the conserved sodium ion-binding site of class A GPCRs. The benzamidine group occupies the sodium ion position and mimics the entire sodium ion-centered water cluster, locking [Leukotriene B4 Receptor 1 (BLT1) — Guinea Pig and Human](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/blt1/) in the inactive conformation. This dual-site binding mechanism makes BIIL260 an inverse agonist and negative allosteric modulator for [Leukotriene B4 Receptor 1 (BLT1) — Guinea Pig and Human](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/blt1/). The first crystal structure of [Leukotriene B4 Receptor 1 (BLT1) — Guinea Pig and Human](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/blt1/) was determined in complex with BIIL260 (PDB 5X33).

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### BLT1 inverse agonist and crystallization ligand

BIIL260 binds to both the orthosteric binding site and the sodium ion-binding site of [Leukotriene B4 Receptor 1 (BLT1) — Guinea Pig and Human](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/blt1/). Three of its four benzene rings occupy the orthosteric pocket, while the benzamidine moiety extends to interact with D66 (2.50), V69 (2.53), S106 (3.39), W236 (6.48), and S276 (7.45) in the conserved sodium ion-binding site. This dual binding stabilizes [Leukotriene B4 Receptor 1 (BLT1) — Guinea Pig and Human](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/blt1/) in the inactive state and prevents the transmembrane helices from adopting active-state conformations. BIIL260 was used as the crystallization ligand for the first [Leukotriene B4 Receptor 1 (BLT1) — Guinea Pig and Human](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/blt1/) structure (PDB 5X33, 3.7 A).
