# Ketamine

## Overview

Ketamine is a dissociative anesthetic and NMDA receptor antagonist that also inhibits pentameric ligand-gated ion channels (pLGICs) including nicotinic [Acetylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/acetylcholine/) receptors (nAChR) and the prokaryotic ion channel [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/). At low pH, ketamine carries a positive charge on its aminium group, which facilitates electrostatic interactions with acidic residues in anesthetic binding sites.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) (Gloeobacter violaceus Ion Channel)

Ketamine binds to a pre-existing intersubunit cavity in the extracellular domain of [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/). The positively charged aminium group forms electrostatic interactions with N152, D153, and D154 of the beta8-beta9 loop (loop F). The chloro group points toward K183 of beta10, and the phenyl ring faces F174 and L176 of loop C. The carbonyl group can form a hydrogen bond with Y23 of beta1. The amide carbonyl oxygen of N152 is 3 A from the aminium of ketamine. The pocket volume (248 A3) is compatible with ketamine's volume (219 A3).

- **Pocket volume**: 248 A3
- **Key residues**: N152, D153, D154, K183, F174, L176, Y23
