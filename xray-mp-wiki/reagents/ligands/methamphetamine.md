# Methamphetamine

## Overview

Methamphetamine is a potent psychostimulant and phenethylamine derivative that acts as a substrate of the dopamine transporter (DAT). It enhances synaptic dopamine levels by competing with dopamine transport and inducing dopamine release from synaptic vesicles.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Drosophila melanogaster dopamine transporter (dDAT)

Acts as a substrate that competes with dopamine transport. Amine and aromatic functional groups at opposing ends interact with both subsite A (Asp46) and subsite B (TMs 3, 6, 8). Phe325 rotates inward to maintain edge-to-face aromatic interactions. Adopted pose in central binding pocket allows interactions between amino and aromatic groups with transporter subsites.

- **Pocket volume**: 325.7 A^2 total surface area, 301 A^2 buried (92.4%)
- **Key residues**: Asp46, Phe325, Phe319, Tyr124
