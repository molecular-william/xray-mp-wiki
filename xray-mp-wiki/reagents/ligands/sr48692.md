# SR48692

## Overview

SR48692 is a potent, selective, non-peptide antagonist of the neurotensin receptor (NTSR1). It was the first non-peptide neurotensin antagonist radioligand developed for binding studies. SR48692 competes with radiolabeled neurotensin for binding to NTSR1 and has been widely used in pharmacological characterization of neurotensin receptor subtypes. The compound shows approximately 1000-fold higher affinity for NTSR1 compared to other neurotensin receptor subtypes.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Neurotensin Receptor 1 (NTSR1)

SR48692 binds to the orthosteric ligand-binding pocket of NTSR1 within the transmembrane bundle. Binding experiments show a Kd of approximately 9.3 nM for wild-type NTSR1. The thermostabilized NTSR1-GW5 construct shows a 130-200 fold higher IC50 for SR48692 (1247-1806 nM), suggesting either reduced affinity or kinetic effects due to slow off-rates of the agonist [3H]NTS. The reduced antagonist affinity is consistent with the GW5 mutations stabilizing a high-affinity agonist conformation.
