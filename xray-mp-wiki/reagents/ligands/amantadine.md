# Amantadine

## Overview

Amantadine (1-aminoadamantane) is a small adamantyl-amine antiviral drug that inhibits the M2 proton channel of influenza A virus. With a molecular weight of 151 g/mol, it achieves potent inhibition despite its small size through water-mediated interactions in the channel pore. The hydrophobic adamantane cage binds in a hydrophobic pocket while the ammonium group engages hydrogen-bonded water networks that facilitate proton conduction.


## Properties

- **Class**: adamantyl-amine

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Influenza A antiviral therapy

Amantadine is an adamantyl-amine class drug that targets the M2 proton channel of influenza A virus. The hydrophobic adamantane group displaces waters from the hydrophobic region near Val27, while the positively charged ammonium group locks into water networks that stabilize transient hydronium ions during proton transport. The adamantane group has no rotatable bonds, resulting in minimal conformational entropy loss upon binding. Its LogP of 3.98 is similar to n-hexane despite containing ten carbons, due to its compact, cross-linked cage structure.
