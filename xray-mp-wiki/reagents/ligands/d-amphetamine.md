# D-Amphetamine

## Overview

D-amphetamine is a psychostimulant and phenethylamine derivative that acts as a substrate of the dopamine transporter (DAT). It is the active enantiomer of amphetamine and is used clinically to treat ADHD and narcolepsy.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Drosophila melanogaster dopamine transporter (dDAT)

Primary amine interacts with Asp46 at 2.9 A distance. Phenyl group engaged by Phe325 via edge-to-face aromatic interactions. Asymmetric carbon center distinguishes from L-amphetamine. Main chain carbonyl of Phe319 positioned nearby at 3.3 A. Amphetamine occupies central pocket without displacing toward TMs 3 and 8, unlike dopamine. Phe319 retains edge-to-face aromatic interactions with the phenyl group via contraction of TM6a-6b linker.

- **Pocket volume**: 308.3 A^2 total surface area, 284.5 A^2 buried (92.3%)
- **Key residues**: Asp46, Phe325, Phe319, Phe43
