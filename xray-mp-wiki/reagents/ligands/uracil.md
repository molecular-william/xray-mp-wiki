# Uracil

## Overview

Uracil is a pyrimidine base and the natural substrate of the uracil:proton symporter [Uracil:Proton Symporter UraA from Escherichia coli](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/uraA/) from Escherichia coli. It is a planar heterocyclic molecule that is one of the four nucleobases in RNA. [Uracil:Proton Symporter UraA from Escherichia coli](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/uraA/) mediates the coupled transport of uracil with protons across the bacterial inner membrane, utilizing the proton motive force to accumulate uracil against its concentration gradient.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: uracil
- **Chemical formula**: C4H4N2O2
- **Molecular weight**: 112.09 g/mol
- **Class**: pyrimidine nucleobase

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for UraA transporter

Uracil is the natural substrate of [Uracil:Proton Symporter UraA from Escherichia coli](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/uraA/), a uracil:proton symporter from Escherichia coli. The substrate binds in the core domain of [Uracil:Proton Symporter UraA from Escherichia coli](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/uraA/) in an occluded conformation, coordinated primarily by the core domain residues Glu241, Glu290, and backbone amide groups of Phe73 and Gly289. Multiple water-mediated hydrogen bonds contribute to substrate coordination.
