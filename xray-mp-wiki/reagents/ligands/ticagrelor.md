# Ticagrelor

## Overview

Ticagrelor is a reversible, market-approved antagonist of the [Human P2Y12 Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/p2y12-receptor/). It is used clinically for the prevention of atherothrombotic events in patients with acute coronary syndrome. Ticagrelor has been demonstrated to be an inverse agonist at the P2Y12 receptor, counteracting basal receptor signaling. It is approximately 100-fold less potent than [Selatogrel](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/selatogrel/), with an IC50 of 400 nM for platelet aggregation inhibition compared to 14 nM for [Selatogrel](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/selatogrel/). The paper also mentions ticagrelor as having a characteristic adverse effect profile, suggesting an unfulfilled medical need for improved P2Y12R inhibitors.


## Properties

- **Class**: Reversible P2Y12 receptor antagonist/inverse agonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Inverse agonist comparison in P2Y12 functional assays

Ticagrelor was used in BRET2 assays to demonstrate inverse agonist efficacy at the P2Y12 receptor. It increased the BRET signal in cells co-expressing P2Y12 and G alpha-RLuc8/G beta gamma-GFP2 probes, confirming that ticagrelor can stabilize the inactive G protein-coupled state. In dose-response experiments, ticagrelor showed comparable maximal efficacy to [Selatogrel](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/selatogrel/) but was approximately 100-fold less potent (pEC50 ~5.4 vs ~7.0 for [Selatogrel](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/selatogrel/)). Ticagrelor also abolished constitutive cAMP inhibition in CAMYEL-expressing cells.


### Antithrombotic drug comparison in P2Y12 structural studies

Ticagrelor is mentioned in the P2Y12R-[AZD1283](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/azd1283/) crystal structure paper as one of the approved antithrombotic drugs targeting P2Y12R. Its characteristic adverse effect profile and comparison with other P2Y12R antagonists (clopidogrel, prasugrel) motivated the development of next-generation inhibitors like [AZD1283](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/azd1283/).
