# Daltroban

## Overview

Daltroban is a nonprostanoid antagonist of the thromboxane A2 receptor (TP) that significantly attenuates TXA2-induced vasoconstriction in vascular smooth muscle. It belongs to the same chemical series as ramatroban, characterized by a carboxylic acid function, a benzenesulfonamide moiety, and a spacer between these functional groups. Unlike ramatroban which has a bulky tetrahydrocarbazole ring as the spacer, daltroban has a phenylethyl group. The crystal structure of the human TP-daltroban complex was determined at 3.0 A resolution (PDB 6IIV). Daltroban was discontinued in phase III clinical trials.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Thromboxane A2 Receptor (TP)

Daltroban shares a similar binding mode with ramatroban. The carboxylic acid and benzenesulfonamide groups occupy equivalent positions, with the acid group forming salt bridges with H89(2.65) and R295(7.40) and a hydrogen bond with S181. The benzenesulfonamide group makes hydrogen bonds with T81(2.57) and Q301(7.46). Instead of a bulky tetrahydrocarbazole ring, daltroban has a phenylethyl group as the spacer, which introduces an interaction with W182 in ECL2 but forms fewer contacts overall with A31(1.39), F34(1.42), M112(3.32) and T298(7.43) compared to ramatroban.

- **Key residues**: H89(2.65), S181, W182, R295(7.40), T81(2.57), Q301(7.46)
