# Tungstate (WO4 2-)

## Overview

Tungstate (WO4 2-) is a structural analog of inorganic phosphate (Pi) and inorganic pyrophosphate (PPi) used in crystallographic studies of phosphate- binding enzymes. The tetrahedral geometry of tungstate closely mimics that of phosphate, allowing it to bind at the active site of H+-pumping pyrophosphatases (M-PPases) and trap product-bound-like conformations. In [TmPPase (Thermotoga maritima H+-Pumping Pyrophosphatase)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/tmppase/), tungstate forms a Mg:WO4 complex in the hydrolytic center, visible in Fo-Fc electron density maps. Tungstate binding provides structural insights into the phosphate-binding mode and the catalytic mechanism of M-PPases.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [TmPPase (Thermotoga maritima H+-Pumping Pyrophosphatase)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/tmppase/)

Tungstate forms a Mg:WO4 complex in the hydrolytic center of [TmPPase (Thermotoga maritima H+-Pumping Pyrophosphatase)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/tmppase/). The WO4 2- ion (light blue and red) coordinates with Mg2+ (green) through oxygen atoms, mimicking the geometry of inorganic phosphate or pyrophosphate. The Mg:WO4 complex is visible in unbiased Fo-Fc difference density at 3 sigma, calculated from the model before tungstate was added. 2Fo-Fc density at 1.5 sigma is shown at a 1.6 A radius for neighboring residues.
