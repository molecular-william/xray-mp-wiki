# UDP-Glucose (UDP-Glc)

## Overview

UDP-[Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) (UDP-Glc) is a nucleotide sugar that serves as the activated [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) donor substrate for glycosyltransferases, including cellulose synthases (CESAs). In cellulose synthesis, UDP-Glc provides the [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) moiety that is transferred to the non-reducing end of a growing glucan chain, with inversion of configuration at the anomeric carbon from alpha to beta, forming beta-1,4-glycosidic linkages characteristic of cellulose. UDP is released as a byproduct of the reaction.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: uridine 5'-diphospho-alpha-D-glucose
- **Class**: nucleotide sugar

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for cellulose synthase

UDP-[Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) is the essential donor substrate for all cellulose synthases (CESAs). The enzyme catalyzes the transfer of [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) from UDP-Glc to the non-reducing end of a growing cellulose chain, inverting the anomeric configuration from alpha to beta. In the BcsA-[BCSB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/bcsb/) crystal structure, weak electron density for a UDP molecule was observed at the active site, consistent with the structure representing a translocation intermediate captured after glycosyl transfer and before replacement of UDP with UDP-Glc.
