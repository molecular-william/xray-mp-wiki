# L-Arginine

## Overview

L-arginine is a basic, semi-essential amino acid that serves as the primary substrate for the arginine/agmatine antiporter AdiC. In the extreme acid resistance system of Escherichia coli, extracellular L-arginine is imported by AdiC and then decarboxylated by the enzyme AdiA to agmatine, which is exported by AdiC. This arginine/agmatine exchange coupled to arginine decarboxylation functions as a virtual proton pump, allowing E. coli to survive gastric conditions at pH 1.5-4. AdiC specifically imports the deprotonated-carboxylate form of arginine (Arg+), which represents a minor fraction of extracellular arginine below pH 2.
