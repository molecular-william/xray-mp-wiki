# Ethyl Mercury Thiosalicylate EMTS Heavy Metal Derivative

## Overview

Ethyl [Mercury (HgCl2) - Aquaporin Inhibitor](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/mercury/) thiosalicylate (EMTS) is a mercury-containing heavy metal derivative used for experimental phasing by single-wavelength anomalous diffraction (SAD) in X-ray crystallography. The [Mercury (HgCl2) - Aquaporin Inhibitor](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/mercury/) atom provides strong anomalous scattering signals at typical synchrotron wavelengths. In the [TcdB2-TccC3 Toxin Complex B-C Fusion from Photorhabdus luminescens](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/toxins/tcdB2-tccC3/) crystallization, crystals were soaked in 1 mM EMTS for 4 hours prior to data collection. EMTS was used alongside K2Pt(NO2)4 for phase determination.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### SAD phasing of TcdB2-TccC3

Crystals of [TcdB2-TccC3 Toxin Complex B-C Fusion from Photorhabdus luminescens](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/toxins/tcdB2-tccC3/) were soaked in reservoir solution containing 1 mM EMTS for 4 hours before X-ray data collection at the PXII-X10SA beamline at the Swiss Light Source. The [Mercury (HgCl2) - Aquaporin Inhibitor](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/mercury/) heavy metal derivative provided anomalous scattering signals used for experimental phasing of the 2.35 A crystal structure, complementing the K2Pt(NO2)4 derivative data.
