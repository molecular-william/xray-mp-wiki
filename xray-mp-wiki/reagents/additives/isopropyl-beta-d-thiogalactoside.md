# Isopropyl beta-D-1-Thiogalactopyranoside (IPTG)

## Overview

Isopropyl beta-D-1-thiogalactopyranoside (IPTG) is a non-hydrolyzable analog of allolactose used to induce protein expression from lac operon-based expression systems (e.g., T7/lac, lacZ). Unlike lactose, IPTG is not metabolized by cells, providing stable induction.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: isopropyl beta-D-1-thiogalactopyranoside
- **Chemical formula**: C9H18OS
- **Molecular weight**: 208.27 g/mol
- **Class**: inducer

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Inducer for IPCT/DIPPS expression in E. coli

IPTG at 0.5 mM was used to induce expression of IPCT/DIPPS from Archaeoglobus fulgidus in E. coli C43 DE3 cells. Induction occurred when OD600 reached 1.0, followed by temperature reduction to 30°C and harvest 6 hours post-induction.


### Inducer for XylE expression in E. coli

IPTG at 0.5 mM was used to induce expression of truncated E. coli XylE (residues 6-480) from pET-15b vector in E. coli BL21 C43(DE3) cells. Expression was induced at OD600 0.5, culture shifted to 18°C, and harvested overnight.
