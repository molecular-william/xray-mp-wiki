# Cellobiose

## Overview

Cellobiose is a disaccharide composed of two [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) units linked by a beta-1,4-glycosidic bond. It is the repeating unit of cellulose and serves as a substrate analog for cellulose synthases. In membrane protein purification, cellobiose was used as an additive in solubilization and purification buffers for the BcsA-[BCSB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/bcsb/) cellulose synthase complex, likely to stabilize the enzyme-ligand interaction.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: beta-D-glucopyranosyl-(1-4)-D-glucopyranose
- **Class**: disaccharide

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Stabilizing additive for cellulose synthase purification

Cellobiose at 5 mM was included in the solubilization buffer (RB2 buffer: 20 mM [Sodium Phosphate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/sodium-phosphate/) pH 7.2, 0.3 M NaCl, 5 mM cellobiose, 5 mM MgCl2, 40 mM [Imidazole](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/imidazole/), 10% [Glycerol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glycerol/)) and purification buffer for the BcsA-[BCSB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/bcsb/) complex from Rhodobacter sphaeroides. Cellobiose is the structural repeating unit of cellulose and likely helps maintain the active conformation of the cellulose synthase during purification.
