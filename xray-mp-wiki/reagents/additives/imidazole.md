# Imidazole

## Overview

Imidazole is a heterocyclic organic compound commonly used in protein
purification, particularly in immobilized metal affinity chromatography
(IMAC). It competes with histidine-tagged proteins for binding to nickel
or cobalt ions on the affinity resin, enabling selective elution of
target proteins. Imidazole is typically used at concentrations of
20-500 mM for washing and elution steps.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Elution agent in nickel affinity chromatography

Imidazole at 100 mM was used as a wash buffer component during
nickel affinity purification of AtTIP2;1 from Pichia pastoris,
removing non-specifically bound proteins while retaining the
deca-His-tagged target.
