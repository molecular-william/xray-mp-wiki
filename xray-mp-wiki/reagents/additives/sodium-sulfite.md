# Sodium Sulfite (Na2SO3)

## Overview

Sodium sulfite (Na2SO3) is an inorganic salt that provides sulfite ions (SO3 2-) in solution. In the context of membrane protein research, it has been used as a screening agent for [Protein Thermostabilization](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/thermostabilization/), where it was tested for its ability to shift the melting temperature of the [HSC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/hsc/) hydrosulfide channel.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: sodium sulfite
- **Chemical formula**: Na2SO3
- **Class**: inorganic salt

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Protein thermostabilization screening

Sodium sulfite was used in [Protein Thermostabilization](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/thermostabilization/) screening of the [HSC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/hsc/) hydrosulfide channel from Clostridium difficile. Apparent melting temperature (Tm) was determined by fitting peak heights from [Size-Exclusion Chromatography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/purification/size-exclusion-chromatography/) to a Boltzmann sigmoidal curve. Na2SO3 increased the Tm of [HSC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/hsc/) to 53.8 C (control: 53.3 C), providing a modest [Protein Thermostabilization](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/thermostabilization/) effect of +0.5 C.
