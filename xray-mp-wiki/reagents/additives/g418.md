# G418 (Geneticin)

## Overview

G418 (geneticin) is an aminoglycoside antibiotic used as a selection agent in eukaryotic cell culture, particularly for yeast and mammalian cells. It functions by binding to the 40S ribosomal subunit and disrupting protein synthesis. The neo gene encoding aminoglycoside 3'-phosphotransferase confers resistance to G418 and is commonly used as a selectable marker in expression vectors for Pichia pastoris and other eukaryotic expression systems.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 4-O-(2-amino-2-deoxy-D-glucopyranosyl)-2-deoxy-L-streptamine
- **Class**: aminoglycoside antibiotic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Yeast selection

G418 is used to select for Pichia pastoris transformants expressing the neo resistance gene. Typical selection concentrations range from 1-5 ug/mL for initial selection and up to 200 ug/mL for multi-copy integration selection.
