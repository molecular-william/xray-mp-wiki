# Glutathione (GSH)

## Overview

Glutathione (gamma-L-glutamyl-L-cysteinylglycine, GSH) is a tripeptide thiol that serves as a cofactor for mPGES-1 and related MAPEG family enzymes. In mPGES-1, GSH is bound in a bent conformation at the active site and activates the thiolate anion for the isomerization of PGH2 to PGE2.


## Properties


## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Cofactor for mPGES-1 catalysis

In mPGES-1, GSH is coordinated by Arg73, Asn74, Glu77, His113,
Tyr117, Arg126, and Ser127 at the active site. Ser127 activates the
GSH thiol to form a thiolate anion that attacks the endoperoxide
oxygen at C-9 of PGH2, initiating the isomerization reaction.


### Substrate for Atm1-family ABC exporters

Glutathione and its derivatives (GSSG, Ag-GSH, Hg-GSH) serve as
transported substrates for Atm1-family ABC exporters. In the
bacterial NaAtm1 transporter, GSH derivatives stimulate ATPase
activity 3- to 7-fold over basal rate, and direct transport assays
show ATP-driven uptake of GSSG into membrane vesicles. In the
yeast mitochondrial Atm1, GSH binds in the substrate cavity with
Kd = 23 µM and is likely part of the sulfur-containing moiety
exported for cytosolic Fe/S protein biogenesis.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to mPGES-1

Hydrogen bonds with Arg73, Asn74, Glu77, His113, Tyr117, Arg126, Ser127; pi-stacking with Tyr130

- **Pocket volume**: ~15 A deep cavity at monomer interface
