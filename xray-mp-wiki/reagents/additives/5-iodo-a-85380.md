# 5-Iodo-A-85380

## Overview

5-Iodo-A-85380 is a potent agonist ligand selective for the alpha4beta2 subtype of nicotinic [Acetylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/acetylcholine/) receptors. It is structurally related to A-85380 and contains an iodine atom that provides an anomalous signal for X-ray crystallographic phasing. Like [Acetylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/acetylcholine/) and [Nicotine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/nicotine/), 5-Iodo-A-85380 binds only at alpha-beta subunit interfaces, making it useful for confirming subunit identity in heteromeric receptors.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Co-crystallization for subunit identity assignment

The [Human Alpha4Beta2 Nicotinic Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/human-alpha4beta2-nicotinic-receptor/) was co-crystallized with 5-Iodo-A-85380 to confirm subunit identities at the neurotransmitter-binding interfaces. From a low-resolution isomorphous data set (30.00-6.50 A), iodine anomalous signal was observed in only the two assigned alpha-beta interfaces, confirming that [Nicotine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/nicotine/) binding sites are exclusively at alpha-beta interfaces in this heteromeric receptor. No detectable anomalous signal was present in other interfacial pockets (beta-beta or beta-alpha interfaces).
