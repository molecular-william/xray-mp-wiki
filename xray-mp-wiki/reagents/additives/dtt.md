# Dithiothreitol (DTT)

## Overview

Dithiothreitol (DTT, also known as Cleland's reagent) is a strong reducing agent widely used in biochemistry to maintain cysteine residues in their reduced thiol state. DTT contains two sulfhydryl groups that can form a disulfide bond, making it effective at breaking disulfide bonds in proteins and preventing oxidation of free thiols. It is commonly used in protein purification, transport assays, and crosslinking experiments.
