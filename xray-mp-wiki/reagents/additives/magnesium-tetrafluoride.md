# Magnesium Tetrafluoride (MgF₄²⁻)

## Overview

Magnesium tetrafluoride (MgF₄²⁻) is a phosphate analogue used to trap P-type ATPases in the E2·Pi state for crystallography. It forms in the presence of Mg²⁺ and fluoride ions (KF), mimicking the pentavalent transition state of phosphoryl transfer at the catalytic aspartate residue. In the Na⁺,K⁺-ATPase structure at 2.4 Å resolution (Shinoda et al., 2009), MgF₄²⁻ was bound at the phosphorylation site of the α-subunit, fixing the ATPase in a state analogous to E2·2K⁺·Pi.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### E2·Pi state trapping for Na⁺,K⁺-ATPase crystallization

MgF₄²⁻ was used to fix Na⁺,K⁺-ATPase in the E2·2K⁺·Pi state. The crystallization buffer contained 4 mM MgCl₂ and 4-8 mM KF, which formed MgF₄²⁻ in solution. MgF₄²⁻ binds at the phosphorylation site, coordinating between the catalytic Asp and surrounding residues, mimicking the pentavalent transition state of dephosphorylation.
