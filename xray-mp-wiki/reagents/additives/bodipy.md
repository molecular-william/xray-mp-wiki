# BODIPY Fluorophore

## Overview

BODIPY (boron-dipyromethene) is a fluorescent dye used for labeling molecules for imaging and detection. In A2AAR structural studies, BODIPY was conjugated to Preladenant via a PEG linker to create PSB-2115, a fluorescent probe for specific A2AAR imaging. This enabled the first X-ray structure of a GPCR co-crystallized with a fluorophore-labeled antagonist.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: boron-dipyromethene
- **Class**: fluorescent dye
- **Typical concentration**: not applicable (used as label)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Fluorophore for GPCR imaging

BODIPY was conjugated to Preladenant via a PEG linker to create PSB-2115. Analytical SEC confirmed the fluorophore remained bound to A2A-PSB1-bRIL at 495 nm absorption maximum. The BODIPY fluorophore was disordered in the crystal structure with no electron density, extending out of the binding pocket.
