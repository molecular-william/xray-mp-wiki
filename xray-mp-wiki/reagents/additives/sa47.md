# SA47

## Overview

SA47 is a potent exofacial inhibitor of human [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) transporters [SLC2A1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/glut1/) and [GLUT3](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/human-glut3/). It is a 1H-pyrazolo[3,4-d]pyrimidine derivative comprising four moieties: an aryl head, a piperazine moiety, a pyrazolopyrimidine core, and a pyrazoloaryl tail. SA47 inhibits [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) transport by [SLC2A1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/glut1/) and [GLUT3](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/human-glut3/) with IC50 values of 331 nM and 823 nM, respectively, and binds with nanomolar affinity (KD ~310 nM). The crystal structure of [GLUT3](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/human-glut3/) in complex with SA47 reveals its exofacial binding mode, providing a framework for developing GLUT inhibitors for anticancer therapy and glucose-controlled insulin delivery.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Exofacial GLUT inhibitor

Binds to the extracellular side of [SLC2A1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/glut1/) and [GLUT3](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/human-glut3/), partially overlapping with the [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) binding site. Its aryl tail can be conjugated with insulin for glucose-controlled insulin delivery systems.


### Structure-based drug discovery

The GLUT3-SA47 crystal structure (PDB 7SPS) provides a structural template for rational design of exofacial GLUT inhibitors with improved potency and selectivity.
