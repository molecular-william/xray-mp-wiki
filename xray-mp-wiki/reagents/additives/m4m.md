# M4M (Bis-(3-Sulfanylpropionyl)disulfide, 4-Carbon Linker MTS Reagent)

## Overview

M4M is a bifunctional maleimide reagent (MTS: monobromobimane thiosulfonate) with a 4-carbon linker used for site-directed disulfide cross-linking of engineered cysteine residues. In the study of NMDA receptor activation, M4M was used to trap the active conformation of the GluN1b-GluN2B ATD by cross-linking engineered cysteines at the subunit interface. Application of M4M to GluN1b Ala175Cys/GluN2B Gln180Cys and GluN1b Lys178Cys/GluN2B Asn184Cys mutant receptors potentiated NMDA receptor currents by 3-4 fold, confirming that the open-conformation of the GluN2B ATD represents the active state.
