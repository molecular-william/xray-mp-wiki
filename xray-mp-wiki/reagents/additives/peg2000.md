# PEG 2000

## Overview

Polyethylene glycol with an average molecular weight of 2,000 Da ([PEG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg/) 2000) is a crystallographic precipitant used in membrane protein crystallization. It was the primary precipitant for both crystal forms of the *Burkholderia multivorans* [HPNN](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/hpnn/) hopanoid transporter.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization of HpnN Hopanoid Transporter

[PEG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg/) 2000 was used as the precipitant in sitting-drop vapor diffusion crystallization of *B. multivorans* [HPNN](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/hpnn/). Form I crystals used 16% [PEG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg/) 2000 with 0.1 M sodium [Citrate Buffer (Sodium Citrate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/citrate/) pH 3.5 and 0.2 M Li2SO4. Form II crystals used 15% [PEG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg/) 2000 with 0.1 M sodium [Citrate Buffer (Sodium Citrate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/citrate/) pH 4.0 and 0.2 M (NH4)2SO4. Crystals grew to ~0.2 mm within one month at 25 °C.
