# Polyethylene Glycol 200 (PEG200)

## Overview

Polyethylene glycol 200 (PEG200) is a low-molecular-weight [PEG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg/) used as a crystallization precipitant. PEGs exclude volume from protein solutions, promoting protein-protein interactions and crystal nucleation. PEG200 is particularly useful for [HiLiDe Crystallization](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/hilide-crystallization/) of membrane proteins.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: polyethylene glycol 200
- **Chemical formula**: (C2H4O)n, n≈4.5
- **Molecular weight**: ~200 Da
- **Class**: precipitant

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization precipitant for IPCT/DIPPS in HiLiDe method

PEG200 was used as the crystallization precipitant in [HiLiDe Crystallization](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/hilide-crystallization/) of IPCT/DIPPS from Archaeoglobus fulgidus. The protein sample in detergent was mixed with PEG200 and crystallized using the hydrophobic interaction-driven (HiLiDe) method.
