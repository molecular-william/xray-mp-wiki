# Platinum(II) Chloride

## Overview

Platinum(II) chloride (PtCl2) is an inorganic salt that provides platinum ions (Pt2+) used as a heavy atom derivative for anomalous diffraction phasing in X-ray crystallography. Platinum is a heavy metal with a high atomic number (Z=78), making it suitable for single-wavelength anomalous diffraction (SAD) experiments. Platinum compounds have been widely used in structural biology for heavy atom derivatization due to their strong anomalous scattering signal near their absorption edge.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: platinum(II) chloride
- **Molecular weight**: 225.08 g/mol
- **Class**: inorganic salt (heavy metal salt)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Heavy atom derivative for SAD phasing

Platinum(II) chloride was used as a heavy atom derivative for anomalous phasing in the crystal structure determination of the [Photosystem II core dimer from Thermosynechococcus elongatus](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/photosystem-ii-core-dimer/). A platinum derivative dataset was collected at a wavelength of 0.844 A near the platinum absorption edge. The platinum atoms provided anomalous scattering signals that aided in phasing the structure.
