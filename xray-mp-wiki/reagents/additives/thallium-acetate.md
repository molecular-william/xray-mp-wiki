# Thallium acetate

## Overview

Thallium acetate (TlCH₃COO) is a Tl⁺ salt used as a K⁺ congener for crystallographic substitution studies. Tl⁺ has a similar ionic radius to K⁺ and produces strong anomalous electron density at its absorption peak, making it ideal for visualizing sequential ion substitution by X-ray crystallography.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### K⁺ congener for crystallographic substitution

100 mM thallium acetate used to soak Na+,K+-ATPase crystals in the E2·MgF₄²⁻·2K⁺
state. Tl⁺ substitutes bound K⁺ sequentially (site II before site I) with time
constants of ~41 s (site II) and ~105 s (site I), visualized by anomalous
electron density maps at the Tl⁺ absorption peak.
