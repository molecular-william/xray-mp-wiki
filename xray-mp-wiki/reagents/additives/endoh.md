# Endoglycosidase H (EndoH)

## Overview

Endoglycosidase H (EndoH) is a glycosidase enzyme that cleaves high-mannose and hybrid N-linked glycans from glycoproteins. It does not cleave complex N-linked glycans. EndoH is widely used in membrane protein structural biology to reduce glycan heterogeneity, improve protein homogeneity, and facilitate crystallization. Treatment with EndoH removes most N-linked glycans, leaving only a single N-acetylglucosamine residue at each glycosylation site.


## Properties

- **Class**: glycosidase enzyme

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Glycan reduction for crystallization

EndoH treatment of glycosylated membrane proteins reduces glycan heterogeneity, which can impede crystal formation. By removing most N-linked glycans while leaving a single N-acetylglucosamine at each site, EndoH produces a more homogeneous protein sample suitable for crystallization.


### Glycosylation site analysis

LC-MS analysis after EndoH treatment confirms the number of remaining glycosylation sites. A single N-acetylglucosamine per site indicates complete EndoH digestion, confirming the glycosylation status of the protein.
