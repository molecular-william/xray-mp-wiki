# MTSET ((2-trimethylammonium)ethyl methanethiosulfonate)

## Overview

MTSET (2-trimethylammonium)ethyl methanethiosulfonate) is a positively charged, membrane-impermeant thiol-reactive reagent used for covalent modification of cysteine residues in proteins. It is commonly used in structure-function studies to probe the accessibility and functional role of specific cysteine residues, particularly in membrane proteins.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: (2-trimethylammonium)ethyl methanethiosulfonate
- **Class**: Thiol-reactive reagent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Cysteine modification in TrkH potassium channel

MTSET (1 mM) was used to modify the I220C mutant of [TRKH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/trkh/) from Klebsiella pneumoniae. In single-channel recordings, MTSET treatment reduced channel activity, decreasing the Level 1 current from 9.7 to 5.6 pA for [TRKH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/trkh/) I220C alone and from 9.7 to 4.6 pA for the [TRKH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/trkh/) I220C-[TRKA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/trka/) complex. The modification was reversible upon addition of 20 mM [DTT](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/dtt/), confirming disulfide-linked covalent modification. This demonstrated that residue I220 is accessible to the intracellular solution and its modification affects channel gating.
