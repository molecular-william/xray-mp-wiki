# Polyethylene Glycol 8000 (PEG 8000)

## Overview

Polyethylene Glycol 8000 (PEG 8000) is a high-molecular-weight polymer commonly used as a precipitant in protein crystallization. PEG 8000 has an average molecular weight of ~8000 Da and functions by creating an excluded volume effect that promotes protein-protein contacts and crystal formation. It is particularly useful for crystallizing large protein complexes and membrane proteins due to its mild precipitating action compared to salts.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Precipitant in microbatch and vapor diffusion crystallization

PEG 8000 is used at concentrations of 5-25% (w/v) as a primary precipitant for protein crystallization. At 15-22%, it was used to crystallize the bovine F1-ATPase-stator complex (3.2 Å resolution).
