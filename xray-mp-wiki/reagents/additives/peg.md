# PEG (Polyethylene Glycol)

## Overview

Polyethylene glycol (PEG) is a water-soluble polymer used as a linker in conjugating reporter molecules (fluorophores, PEGylated ligands) to drug scaffolds. In A2AAR structural studies, PEG linkers of varying lengths connect Preladenant derivatives to reporter groups while maintaining receptor binding. PEG 400 was also used as a crystallization precipitant in the bcMalT study.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: poly(ethylene glycol)
- **Class**: polymer
- **Typical concentration**: not applicable (used as linker)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Ligand conjugation linker

PEG linkers of different lengths were used to conjugate reporter molecules to Preladenant derivatives. PSB-2113 is a PEGylated Preladenant derivative used for co-crystallization with A2A-PSB1-bRIL. The PEG linker extends out of the binding pocket and was disordered in the crystal structure with no electron density observed.


### Crystallization precipitant

PEG of various molecular weights (PEG 400, 300, 600, 3350, 4000) used as precipitant in crystallization conditions for membrane proteins. PEG 400 at 8-10% was used in vapor diffusion crystallization of AcrB-DARPin complexes, yielding structures at 2.54-3.34 A resolution.
