# Ammonium Sulfate

## Overview

Ammonium sulfate is a common precipitant used in protein crystallization and purification. It is widely used in salting-out procedures for protein concentration and purification before crystallization trials. In rhodopsin crystallization at 2.2 A resolution, ammonium sulfate at 2.5-3.0 M was used as the precipitant in Mes buffer (pH 5.9-6.1), with the concentration substantially lower than in previous crystallization conditions. In archaerhodopsin-2 crystallization by membrane fusion method, ~1 M ammonium sulfate was used.
