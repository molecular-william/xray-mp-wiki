# Jeffamine ED-2001 Polyalkylene Glycol

## Overview

Jeffamine ED-2001 is a branched polyalkylene glycol used as a crystallization precipitant in hanging-drop vapor diffusion experiments. It is a low-molecular-weight polyether with high water solubility and is effective for crystallizing large protein complexes. In the [TcdA1 Toxin Complex A Subunit from Photorhabdus luminescens](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/toxins/tcdA1/) crystallization, 30% Jeffamine ED-2001 in 0.1 M HEPES pH 7.0 with 1.1 M [Sodium Malonate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/sodium-malonate/) yielded crystals suitable for X-ray diffraction at 4.0 A resolution.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### TcdA1 crystallization

Used at 30% concentration in 0.1 M HEPES pH 7.0 with 1.1 M [Sodium Malonate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/sodium-malonate/) as precipitant for crystallization of the [TcdA1 Toxin Complex A Subunit from Photorhabdus luminescens](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/toxins/tcdA1/) TcA subunit from Photorhabdus luminescens. Crystals grew after 5-7 days in hanging drops and reached approximately 300 x 200 x 200 micrometers. The crystals were cryoprotected with 5-25% [Glycerol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glycerol/) and flash-frozen in liquid nitrogen for X-ray data collection at the PXII-X10SA beamline at the Swiss Light Source.
