# Retosiban

## Overview

Retosiban is a potent, nonpeptidic antagonist of the human oxytocin receptor (OTR) with >1400-fold selectivity over the closely related vasopressin receptors (V1aR, V1bR, V2R). It was clinically developed as a tocolytic agent to prevent preterm labor by blocking oxytocin-mediated contraction of uterine smooth muscle. Retosiban features a 2,5-diketopiperazine (DKP) core with three substituents (butan-2-yl, indanyl, and 2'-methyl-1',3'-oxazol-4'-yl morpholine amide) that provide optimal potency and selectivity. It was co-crystallized with OTR for the first crystal structure of this receptor.
