# PEG 3000

## Overview

Polyethylene glycol 3000 (PEG 3000) is a precipitating agent used in protein crystallization. With an average molecular weight of ~3000 Da, it is used at concentrations of 10-25% (w/v) for membrane protein crystallization, particularly in vapor diffusion methods.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization of NarGHI

PEG 3000 at 20% (w/v) was used as the primary precipitant in the crystallization of E. coli nitrate reductase A (NarGHI). The reservoir solution contained 100 mM HEPES pH 7.0, 20% PEG 3000, 200 mM sodium acetate, 200 mM KCl, and 5 mM EDTA. Crystals of both native and selenomethionine-substituted NarGHI were obtained by sitting-drop vapor diffusion. For cryoprotection, crystals were transferred to a solution with 35% PEG 3000.
