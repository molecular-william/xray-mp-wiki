# Iohexol (Histodenz)

## Overview

Iohexol (trade name Histodenz, also known as Nycodenz) is a non-ionic, iodinated X-ray contrast medium with the IUPAC name 5-[N-(2,3-dihydroxypropyl)acetamido]-2,4,6-triiodo-N,N'-bis(2,3-dihydroxypropyl)isophthalamide. It can be dissolved in water to >80% (w/v) and increases solvent electron density by 0.10 e⁻ per Å³ at full concentration. In macromolecular crystallography, iohexol is used as a contrast medium for X-ray solvent contrast modulation to visualize lipid bilayers in membrane protein crystals by modulating the electron density of the solvent phase.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 5-[N-(2,3-dihydroxypropyl)acetamido]-2,4,6-triiodo-N,N'-bis(2,3-dihydroxypropyl)isophthalamide
- **Molecular weight**: 821.14 g/mol
- **Class**: Iodinated contrast medium

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### X-ray Solvent Contrast Modulation for Lipid Bilayer Visualization

In the landmark study of Ca²⁺-ATPase (SERCA1a) protein-phospholipid interplay (doi/10.1038##nature22357), iohexol was used as a contrast medium at concentrations ranging from 0-80% (w/v). Crystals were soaked in buffers containing iohexol to systematically vary the solvent electron density. The iodine atoms in iohexol (f'' of 3.3 at 1 Å wavelength, or 6.5 at 1.5 Å) provide useful anomalous signal. This technique allowed the visualization of the entire first layer of phospholipids surrounding the transmembrane helices of SERCA in four different states, resolving an aspect of membrane protein structure that had largely eluded conventional crystallography.
- Contrast medium concentration: 0-80% (w/v) in crystallization mother liquor
- Solvent electron density increase: up to 0.10 e⁻ per Å³
- Dialysis time for >40% iohexol: at least 48 hours
