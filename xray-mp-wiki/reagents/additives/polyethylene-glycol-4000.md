# Polyethylene Glycol 4000 (PEG 4000)

## Overview

Polyethylene glycol 4000 (PEG 4000) is a common polymeric precipitant used in protein crystallography, with an average molecular weight of approximately 4000 Da. It is widely employed in vapor diffusion crystallization of membrane proteins. PEG 4000 was used as the primary precipitant in the crystallization of AcrB (14% w/v) for the 2.8 Å structure determination of the multidrug efflux transporter (Murakami et al. 2006, Nature 443:173-179).

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Poly(oxy-1,2-ethanediyl), alpha-hydro-omega-hydroxy-
- **Molecular weight**: ~4000 Da (average)
- **Class**: Polymeric precipitant

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### AcrB crystallization

14% (w/v) PEG 4000 in 100 mM sodium phosphate (pH 6.2), 50 mM NaCl was used as the reservoir solution for vapor diffusion crystallization of the AcrB multidrug efflux transporter from E. coli, yielding crystals of the native, minocycline-bound, and doxorubicin-bound forms at 2.8 Å resolution.
