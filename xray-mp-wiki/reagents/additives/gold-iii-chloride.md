# Gold(III) Chloride

## Overview

Gold(III) chloride (AuCl3) is an inorganic salt that provides gold ions (Au3+) used as a heavy atom derivative for anomalous diffraction phasing in X-ray crystallography. Gold is a heavy metal with a high atomic number (Z=79), making it suitable for single-wavelength anomalous diffraction (SAD) experiments. Gold compounds are widely used in structural biology for heavy atom derivatization due to their strong anomalous scattering signal near their absorption edge.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: gold(III) chloride
- **Molecular weight**: 303.32 g/mol
- **Class**: inorganic salt (heavy metal salt)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Heavy atom derivative for SAD phasing

Gold(III) chloride was used as a heavy atom derivative for anomalous phasing in the crystal structure determination of the [Photosystem II core dimer from Thermosynechococcus elongatus](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/photosystem-ii-core-dimer/). A gold derivative dataset was collected at a wavelength of 0.907 A near the gold absorption edge. The gold atoms provided anomalous scattering signals that aided in phasing the structure.
