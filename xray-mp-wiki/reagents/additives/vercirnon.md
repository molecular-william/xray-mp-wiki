# Vercirnon (GSK1605786, CCX282-B)

## Overview

Vercirnon (GSK1605786, CCX282-B) is a selective small-molecule antagonist of CC chemokine receptor 9 (CCR9) that progressed to Phase 3 clinical trials for Crohn's disease. It was the first small molecule crystallographically observed binding to the intracellular allosteric pocket of a GPCR. Chemically, it is 4-tert-butyl-N-[4-chloro-2-[(1-oxidopyridin-4-yl)carbonyl]phenyl]benzenesulfonamide.


## Properties


## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### CCR9 antagonist in inflammatory bowel disease

Vercirnon is a selective CCR9 antagonist that blocks CCL25-mediated signaling. It progressed to Phase 3 clinical trials for Crohn's disease, though efficacy was limited, requiring very high doses to block receptor activation.


### Structural probe for intracellular allosteric antagonism of GPCRs

The CCR9-vercirnon crystal structure (PDB 5LWE, 2.8 A) provides the first detailed view of a small molecule bound on the intracellular surface of a GPCR in a pocket within the helical bundle but open to the cytoplasm. This enables structure-based drug design targeting the intracellular allosteric site of chemokine receptors.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to CCR9 (CC Chemokine Receptor 9)

The sulfone group hydrogen bonds with backbone amino groups of Glu322, Arg323 and Phe324, acting as a helix cap for helix 8 N-terminus. The pyridine-N-oxide is surrounded by polar residues including Thr83, Asp84, Arg144, Arg323. The tert-butylphenyl group interacts with Val69, Val72, Tyr73 in TM1 and Leu87 in TM2. Phe324 and Tyr317 make edge-to-face pi-pi stacking with the aromatic core. The chlorophenyl moiety sits in a narrow apolar cavity between Leu87, Ile140, and Val259.

- **Pocket volume**: Accessible from the cytoplasm, approximately 33 Angstroms from the orthosteric site
- **Key residues**: ['Tyr317 (conserved NPxxY(x)5,6F motif)', 'Gly321 (provides conformational flexibility at TM7-helix 8 junction)', 'Glu322', 'Arg323', 'Phe324', 'Leu87']
