# Zinc Chloride

## Overview

Zinc chloride (ZnCl2) is an inorganic salt that provides zinc ions (Zn2+) used as a crystallization additive and functional probe. Zn2+ is a divalent transition metal ion with a relatively high atomic number, making it useful for anomalous diffraction experiments. In ion channel studies, Zn2+ can bind to specific sites in the pore region and act as a partial blocker of ion conduction.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: zinc chloride
- **Molecular weight**: 136.30 g/mol
- **Class**: inorganic salt (transition metal salt)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization additive and structural probe

ZnCl2 at 100 mM was used in [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) crystallization studies to trap Zn2+ ions in the intracellular pore entry. The Zn2+ anomalous signal was used to identify the binding site at the intracellular pore entry, involving conserved glutamate residues.
