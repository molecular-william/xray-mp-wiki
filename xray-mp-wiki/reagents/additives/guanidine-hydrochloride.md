# Guanidine Hydrochloride

## Overview

Guanidine hydrochloride is a strong chaotropic agent used to chemically denature proteins. It disrupts hydrogen bonding and hydrophobic interactions, causing proteins to unfold. In membrane protein biochemistry, guanidine hydrochloride is used for thermal and chemical denaturation studies to measure protein stability and melting temperatures by monitoring unfolding transitions via [Circular Dichroism Spectroscopy](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/quality-assessment/circular-dichroism-spectroscopy/).


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: guanidinium chloride
- **Chemical formula**: CH6ClN3
- **Molecular weight**: 95.53 g/mol
- **Class**: chaotropic salt

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Chemical denaturation of SNARE complexes

Unfolding of neuronal SNARE complexes monitored by CD spectroscopy at 222 nm at increasing concentrations of guanidine hydrochloride. The complex including linkers and transmembrane regions was significantly more resistant to chemical denaturation than the core complex alone, demonstrating that linkers and TMRs add stability to the [SNARE Complex](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/snare-complex/).
