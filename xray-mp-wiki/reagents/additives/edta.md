# Ethylenediaminetetraacetic Acid (EDTA)

## Overview

Ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid (EDTA) is a hexadentate chelating agent that binds divalent and trivalent metal ions with high affinity. Used in protein purification and crystallization to sequester metal ions that could catalyze oxidation or interfere with enzyme activity.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: ethylenediaminetetraacetic acid
- **Chemical formula**: C10H16N2O8
- **Molecular weight**: 294.18 g/mol
- **Class**: metal chelator

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Metal chelator in lysis buffer for IPCT/DIPPS purification

EDTA at 1 mM was included in the lysis buffer (50 mM sodium [Citrate Buffer (Sodium Citrate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/citrate/), 50 mM [Sodium Phosphate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/sodium-phosphate/), 200 mM NaCl, 5% [Glycerol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glycerol/), 100 mM [Sucrose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/sucrose/), 1 mM EDTA, 0.5 mM PMSF, 2 mM [beta-Mercaptoethanol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/beta-mercaptoethanol/); pH 7.5) for solubilization of IPCT/DIPPS from Archaeoglobus fulgidus membranes. Chelates metal ions that could interfere with downstream purification or crystallization.
