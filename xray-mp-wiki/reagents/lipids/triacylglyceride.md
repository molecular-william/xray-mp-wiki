# Triacylglyceride (TAG)

## Overview

Triacylglycerides (TAGs) are neutral lipids composed of three fatty acid chains esterified to a glycerol backbone. In mycobacteria, TAGs are transported to the outer membrane by the MFS transporter Rv1410 (P55) working in concert with the lipoprotein LprG. The crystal structure of the Rv1410 homologue MHAS2168 from M. hassiacum revealed an outward-facing conformation with a hydrophobic central cavity suitable for accommodating TAGs. MD simulations and mutational analysis showed that TAGs are extracted from the inner membrane into the central cavity via lateral clefts, with the periplasmic helix extensions of TM11 and TM12 channeling TAGs into the lipid binding pocket of LprG.
