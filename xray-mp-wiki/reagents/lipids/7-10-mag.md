# 7.10 MAG

## Overview

7.10 MAG (7.10 monoacylglycerol) is a novel monoacylglycerol lipid developed for use in in meso (lipid cubic phase) crystallization of membrane proteins. It differs from monoolein (9.9 MAG) in having a shorter acyl chain (17 carbons vs 18) and a cis olefinic bond two carbons closer to the glycerol headgroup (delta-7 vs delta-9). These structural differences alter the phase behavior, notably lacking the inverted hexagonal (HII) phase. 7.10 MAG forms cubic-Ia3d and cubic-Pn3m phases over a wide range of temperatures (-5 to 95 C) and hydrations. The lipid has been successfully used to crystallize three distinct membrane proteins: AlgE (beta-barrel), Lnt (alpha-helical with extramembranal domain), and A2aR (GPCR), yielding high-resolution structures (PDB 8RQP, 8RQR, 8RQQ).
