# NBD-Phosphatidylserine (NBD-PS)

## Overview

NBD-[Phosphatidylserine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylserine/) (NBD-PS) is a fluorescent lipid probe consisting of a [Phosphatidylserine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylserine/) (PS) lipid backbone with a nitrobenzoxadiazole (NBD) fluorophore attached to the serine head group. The NBD group has an excitation maximum of 470 nm and emission maximum of 530 nm. NBD-PS is used as a fluorescent marker to monitor phospholipid translocation (scrambling) between the inner and outer leaflets of lipid bilayers, similar to [NBD-PE](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/nbd-pe/). In the [nhTMEM16 (TMEM16 Lipid Scramblase from Nectria haematococca)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/miscellaneous/nhtmem16/) study, NBD-PS scrambling was used to confirm that [nhTMEM16 (TMEM16 Lipid Scramblase from Nectria haematococca)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/miscellaneous/nhtmem16/) catalyzes the movement of both PE and PS between membrane leaflets in a Ca2+-dependent manner.


## Properties

- **Class**: fluorescent lipid analog

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Lipid scrambling assay

NBD-PS is used alongside [NBD-PE](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/nbd-pe/) in lipid scrambling assays to test the substrate specificity of lipid scramblases. In the [nhTMEM16 (TMEM16 Lipid Scramblase from Nectria haematococca)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/miscellaneous/nhtmem16/) study, NBD-PS scrambling was measured to demonstrate that [nhTMEM16 (TMEM16 Lipid Scramblase from Nectria haematococca)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/miscellaneous/nhtmem16/) catalyzes the Ca2+-dependent movement of anionic phospholipids (PS) across the bilayer, consistent with the proposed role of TMEM16 scramblases in exposing [Phosphatidylserine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylserine/) on the outer leaflet of the plasma membrane during processes like blood coagulation and apoptosis.
