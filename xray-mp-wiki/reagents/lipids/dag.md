# Diacylglycerol (DAG)

## Overview

Diacylglycerol (DAG) is a lipid signaling molecule composed of two fatty acyl chains esterified to a glycerol backbone. It serves as a second messenger in cellular signaling pathways, activates protein kinase C, and is an intermediate in lipid metabolism. In the context of Insig proteins, DAG binds in a V-shaped cavity within each Insig protomer, providing structural insights into how these sterol sensors recognize lipid molecules.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 1,2-Diacyl-sn-glycerol
- **Class**: Signaling lipid / second messenger

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural probe for Insig homolog

A bromine-derived DAG (with the end methyl group of the Sn-1 fatty acyl chain substituted by a bromine atom) was co-crystallized with MvINS, a mycobacterial Insig homolog. The structure at 2.1 Å resolution revealed DAG bound in a V-shaped cavity within each MvINS protomer, coordinated by residues from TM1/2/3/6 and periplasmic β-strands. This provided the first structural evidence of how Insig family proteins accommodate lipid molecules in their central cavity.
