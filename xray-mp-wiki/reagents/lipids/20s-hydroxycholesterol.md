# 20(S)-Hydroxycholesterol (20S-OHC)

## Overview

20(S)-hydroxycholesterol (20S-OHC) is a cholesterol-derived oxysterol that acts as a CRD (cysteine-rich domain) agonist of the human smoothened (SMO) receptor. It binds to the extracellular CRD of SMO and activates the hedgehog signalling pathway by releasing CRD-mediated auto-inhibition of the receptor. It is one of the key endogenous activators of SMO.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### CRD agonist for SMO activation

20S-OHC binds to the hydrophobic groove of the SMO extracellular cysteine-rich domain (CRD), acting as an agonist that activates the hedgehog signalling pathway. Binding induces conformational changes in the CRD, mainly involving displacement of key residues (Trp136, Pro137, Phe139, Leu140 in Xenopus SMO numbering; Trp163, Pro164, Phe166, Leu167 in human SMO numbering), transitioning the CRD from an open to a closed conformation.


### Structural comparison with other CRD-bound states

The 20S-OHC-bound CRD structure (PDB: 5KZV from Xenopus laevis SMO) has been compared with apo, cyclopamine-bound, and multi-domain SMO structures. The key residues in the 20S-OHC-bound state adopt a conformation consistent with the 'closed' state, suggesting that the CRD is restricted in its conformational changes when in the context of the full multi-domain SMO receptor.
