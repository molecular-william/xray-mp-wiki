# Diacylglycerol

## Overview

Diacylglycerol (DAG) is a glycerolipid consisting of two fatty acid chains esterified to a glycerol backbone. In the LSP-grown crystal structure of the Blastochloris viridis photosynthetic reaction center (PDB 2WJM, 1.86 Å), a diacylglycerol molecule was observed covalently attached to the N-terminal cysteine of the tetraheme cytochrome c subunit through a thioether bond. This prokaryotic posttranslational modification facilitates the association of the cytochrome c subunit to the membrane and assists quaternary structure organization. One fatty acid tail interacts with the M-branch accessory bacteriochlorophyll by van der Waals forces, and the thioether bond is sensitive to X-ray radiation damage.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Posttranslational membrane anchor in RC_vir

A diacylglycerol molecule is covalently bound via a thioether bond to the N-terminal cysteine of the tetraheme cytochrome c subunit of the Blastochloris viridis photosynthetic reaction center. This modification was detected by mass spectrometry in the detergent-solubilized protein but was not visible in detergent-based crystal structures, possibly due to crystal contact interference. The thioether bond is sensitive to X-ray radiation damage, as shown by the high-dose data set (PDB 2WJN) where electron density for the sulfur atom and lipid moiety is reduced.
