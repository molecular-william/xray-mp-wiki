# Monogalactosyl Diacylglycerol (MGDG)

## Overview

Monogalactosyl diacylglycerol (MGDG) is the most abundant lipid in thylakoid membranes, comprising approximately 50% of total thylakoid lipids. It is a diacylglycerol with a single galactose headgroup attached via a beta-1,1 glycosidic bond. MGDG has non-bilayer propensity and plays a critical role in maintaining the curvature and structural integrity of thylakoid membranes. It is one of the four lipid species integrally bound to [Photosystem II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/photosystem-ii/), with six MGDG molecules per PSII monomer.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 1,2-diacyl-3-O-beta-D-galactopyranosyl-sn-glycerol
- **Class**: galactolipid

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Integral lipid of photosystem II

Six MGDG molecules are integrally bound to the PSII core complex from Thermosynechococcus elongatus. Hydrophilic headgroups are positioned near the membrane surface while hydrophobic fatty acid chains extend into the membrane interior. MGDG contributes to the structural flexibility and assembly of the PSII complex.


### Thylakoid membrane structural component

MGDG is the predominant lipid in thylakoid membranes, where its conical molecular shape promotes negative membrane curvature essential for thylakoid grana stacking. It provides a native-like lipid environment for the assembly and function of membrane protein complexes in photosynthesis.
