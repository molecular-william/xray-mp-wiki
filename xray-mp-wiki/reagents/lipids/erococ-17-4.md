# EROCOC17+4 (Isoprenoid-Chained Lipid)

## Overview

EROCOC17+4 is an isoprenoid-chained lipid (IPCL) consisting of an erythritol head group ester-linked to a 5,9,13,17-tetramethyloctadecanoyl (C17+4) chain. It is used as a crystallization matrix for in meso (lipidic cubic phase, LCP) membrane protein crystallization and as a crystal delivery medium in [Serial Femtosecond Crystallography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/serial-femtosecond-crystallography/) (LCP-SFX). EROCOC17+4 is noted for its resistance to forming the crystalline Lc phase even at cryogenic temperatures, unlike [Monoolein](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/monoolein/) (MO) which transitions to Lc below ~17 C. When mixed with 10% (w/w) [Cholesterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/cholesterol/), it supports the growth of diffraction-quality crystals of the adenosine A2A receptor ([A2AR](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-adenosine-a2a-receptor-a2ar/)).


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 1-O-(5,9,13,17-tetramethyloctadecanoyl)-rac-erythritol (92% 1-O-, 8% 2-O- isomers)
- **Class**: Isoprenoid-chained lipid (IPCL)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### In meso crystallization of A2A receptor

EROCOC17+4 mixed with 10% (w/w) [Cholesterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/cholesterol/) (referred to as EROCOC17+4-matrix) was used to crystallize the adenosine A2A receptor ([A2AR](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-adenosine-a2a-receptor-a2ar/)) with [BRIL](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/protein-tags/bril/) fusion. Crystals were grown at 20 C using 35-40% [PEG400](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg-400/) or [PEG300](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg300/) as precipitant, with 50 mM NaSCN, 2% 2,5-hexanediol, and 100 mM sodium [Citrate Buffer (Sodium Citrate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/citrate/) pH 5.5-5.75. Crystals up to ~25 um were obtained, suitable for SS-ROX (serial synchrotron rotation crystallography) at cryogenic temperature and LCP-SFX at 4 C and 20 C.


### Crystal delivery medium for LCP-SFX

The EROCOC17+4-matrix served as a direct crystal delivery medium for LCP-SFX at SACLA. Data were collected at 4 C and 20 C sample temperatures, yielding structures at 1.8 A resolution. The LCP stream remained stable without Lc phase formation even under XFEL beam and helium gas flow, enabling reliable low- temperature data collection.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Resists Lc phase formation at least down to -20 C (and possibly -180 C), unlike MO which transitions below ~17 C
- Supports LCP-SFX data collection at 4 C with improved crystal hit rate (46.0% vs 20.2% at 20 C)
- Compatible with standard in meso experimental procedures
- Enables low-temperature crystallization of thermally unstable membrane proteins
- Maintains disordered chain state even at cryogenic temperatures
