# Dioctanoyl Glycerol Pyrophosphatidic Acid (PPA)

## Overview

Dioctanoyl glycerol pyrophosphatidic acid (PPA) is a small anionic lipid with a head group consisting only of phosphoric acid, lacking the inositol ring found in PIP2. In the study of PIP2 activation of Kir2.2 channels, PPA was used as a negative control lipid. PPA binds to the conserved non-specific phospholipid-binding region in the transmembrane domain (TMD) of Kir2.2, but does not interact with the cytoplasmic domain (CTD) because it lacks the 4',5'-phosphate-substituted inositol head group. As a result, PPA binding fails to engage the CTD or open the channel, holding the inner helix gate in a tightly closed conformation (4.9 A at Ile177).

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Kir2.2 TMD non-specific lipid-binding region

Binds to the same TMD site as PIP2, contacting the RWR (R78-R80) motif. Does not interact with the CTD phosphatidylinositol-binding region.

- **Key residues**: R78, W79, R80 (TMD binding site)
