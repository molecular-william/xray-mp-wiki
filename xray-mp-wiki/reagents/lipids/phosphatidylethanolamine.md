# Phosphatidylethanolamine

## Overview

Phosphatidylethanolamine (PE) is a glycerophospholipid consisting of a diglyceride backbone with an ethanolamine head group. PE is the most abundant phospholipid in bacterial membranes and is a major component of both the inner and outer membranes of Gram-negative bacteria. PE is one of the primary lipid species bound by MCE (mammalian cell entry) family proteins including [E. coli MlaD MCE Protein](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/mlaD/), [E. coli PqiB Syringe-like MCE Protein](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/pqiB/), and [E. coli YebT Tube-like MCE Protein](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/yebT/), and was identified as a ligand in the NESG crystal structure of an [E. coli MlaC Periplasmic Lipid-Binding Protein](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/mlaC/) homolog (PDB 2QGU). Mass spectrometry confirmed PE co-purification with [E. coli MlaD MCE Protein](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/mlaD/), [E. coli PqiB Syringe-like MCE Protein](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/pqiB/), and [E. coli YebT Tube-like MCE Protein](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/yebT/). PE is also enriched in the transmembrane pockets of the mechanosensitive channel [MSCS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/mscs/), where it intercalates between transmembrane helices and is proposed to play a role in mechanosensation. IM-MS analysis of AmtB showed that PE confers only moderate stabilization.
