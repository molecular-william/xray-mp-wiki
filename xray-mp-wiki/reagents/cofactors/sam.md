# S-Adenosyl-L-Methionine (SAM)

## Overview

S-adenosyl-[L-Methionine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/l-methionine/) (SAM) is the universal methyl donor used by methyltransferases. It consists of a methionine amino acid linked to an adenosine moiety via a sulfonium ion. SAM donates its reactive methyl group to various substrates, becoming [S-Adenosyl-L-Homocysteine (AdoHcy)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/s-adenosyl-l-homocysteine/) (SAH) in the process.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: S-adenosyl-L-methionine
- **Class**: sulfonium cofactor

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### ICMT methyltransferase cofactor

SAM is the methyl donor cofactor for [Isoprenylcysteine Carboxyl Methyltransferase (ICMT) from Methanosarcina acetivorans](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/ma-icmt/). The crystal structure was determined in complex with SAH (the reaction product), which binds in a fully enclosed cofactor-binding pocket within the C-terminal catalytic subdomain of [Isoprenylcysteine Carboxyl Methyltransferase (ICMT) from Methanosarcina acetivorans](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/ma-icmt/). The cofactor adopts a closed conformation with the adenine ring making nonpolar contacts and the amino group interacting with conserved motifs M1 and M2.
