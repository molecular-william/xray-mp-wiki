# S-Adenosyl-L-Homocysteine (AdoHcy)

## Overview

S-adenosyl-L-homocysteine (AdoHcy) is the product of methyl transfer reactions catalyzed by S-adenosyl-[L-Methionine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/l-methionine/) ([SAM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/sam/))-dependent methyltransferases. It is structurally analogous to [SAM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/sam/) but lacks the sulfonium methyl group. AdoHcy serves as a crystallization cofactor for ICMT, binding in the enclosed cofactor-binding pocket of the enzyme and providing a stable structural snapshot of the cofactor-bound state. In the beetle ICMT structure, AdoHcy was included during crystallization to trap the enzyme in a cofactor-bound conformation.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: S-adenosyl-L-homocysteine
- **Chemical formula**: C14H20N6O5S
- **Molecular weight**: 384.41 g/mol
- **Class**: Adenosylhomocysteine, methyltransferase product

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization cofactor for methyltransferases

AdoHcy is used as a crystallization cofactor for SAM-dependent methyltransferases. It binds in the cofactor-binding pocket of the enzyme and provides structural information about the cofactor interaction site. In the beetle ICMT structure (2.3 A resolution), AdoHcy was included at 25 uM during purification and crystallization. The AdoHcy-bound structure reveals the cofactor-binding pocket and serves as a template for modeling the SAM-bound active site by replacing the homocysteine moiety with [SAM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/sam/).


### Product inhibition studies

AdoHcy acts as a competitive product inhibitor of SAM-dependent methyltransferases. Its binding affinity and structural characteristics provide insight into the catalytic mechanism and substrate specificity of methyltransferase enzymes.
