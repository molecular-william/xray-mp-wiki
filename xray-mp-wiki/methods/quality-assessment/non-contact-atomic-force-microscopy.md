# Non-Contact Atomic Force Microscopy (nc-AFM) with CO-Functionalized Tips

## Overview

Non-contact atomic force microscopy (nc-AFM) is a scanning probe technique that maps surface topography and chemical properties by measuring the interaction forces between a sharp tip and a sample surface. When the tip apex is functionalized with a CO molecule, nc-AFM achieves submolecular resolution and can distinguish different chemical species on surfaces, including the protonation state of individual hydroxyl groups.

## Advantages

- Provides sub-atomic spatial resolution capable of distinguishing individual surface hydroxyl groups
- CO-functionalized tip gives chemically sensitive contrast that can discriminate between protonated and deprotonated states
- Enables direct measurement of pKa at individual surface sites, which was previously impossible with ensemble-averaging techniques

## Disadvantages

- Requires ultra-high vacuum and low-temperature conditions, limiting relevance to ambient-pressure catalytic conditions
- CO tip functionalization requires careful preparation and limited tip lifetime
- Force spectroscopy data analysis requires extensive DFT calculations for interpretation
- Currently limited to relatively flat, conductive or semiconducting surfaces
