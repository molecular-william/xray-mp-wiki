# Biolayer Interferometry (BLI)

## Overview

Biolayer interferometry (BLI) is a label-free optical technique for measuring real-time biomolecular interactions by detecting changes in the interference pattern of white light reflected from a biosensor tip. In the context of the E. coli Mla lipid transport system, BLI was used to study protein-protein interactions between components of the Mla pathway. Biotinylated MlaC (periplasmic lipid shuttle) was diluted to 50 µg/mL in 1× kinetics buffer (PBS pH 7.4, 0.01% BSA, 0.002% Tween 20) and loaded onto streptavidin-coated biosensors (ForteBio). Binding was measured at 25°C on an Octet RED384 instrument by incubating the loaded sensors with varying concentrations of MlaFEDB complex, MlaD, or MlaA-OmpF. Empty sensors without MlaC served as negative controls for non-specific interactions.
