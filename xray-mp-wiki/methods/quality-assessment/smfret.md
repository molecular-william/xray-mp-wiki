# Single-Molecule Fluorescence Resonance Energy Transfer (smFRET)

## Overview

Single-molecule fluorescence resonance energy transfer (smFRET) is a biophysical technique that measures distances between two fluorophores (donor and acceptor) at the single-molecule level. It was applied to the human platelet-activating factor receptor (PAFR) to detect ligand-dependent conformational changes at the intracellular surface. PAFR-flavodoxin and PAFR-mT4L fusion proteins were labeled with Cy3 (donor) and Cy5 (acceptor) at the intracellular tips of helices II and IV, and single-molecule traces were collected at 50 ms/frame in buffer containing 25 mM HEPES pH 7.5, 150 mM NaCl, 10% glycerol, 0.05% DDM, and 0.01% CHS with an oxygen-scavenging system. Ligands were added at 25 µM and smFRET efficiency histograms were generated to measure distances between helices II and IV in complex with the antagonist SR 27417, inverse agonist ABT-491, or agonist PAF. The assay confirmed that SR 27417 induces an outward movement of helices II and IV relative to the ABT-491-bound state.
