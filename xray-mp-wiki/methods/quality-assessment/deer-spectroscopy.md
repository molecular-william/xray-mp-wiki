# Double Electron Electron Resonance (DEER) Spectroscopy

## Overview

Double electron electron resonance (DEER), also known as pulsed electron double resonance (PELDOR), is an electron paramagnetic resonance (EPR) technique used to measure nanometer-scale distances between spin labels attached to specific sites in proteins. DEER provides distance restraints in the 1.5-8 nm range, making it ideal for studying protein conformational changes, protein-protein interactions, and validating crystal structures of membrane protein complexes. The method involves introducing nitroxide spin labels (typically R1 side chains) at cysteine positions via site-directed mutagenesis, and measuring the dipolar coupling between unpaired electrons to determine inter-spin distances.
