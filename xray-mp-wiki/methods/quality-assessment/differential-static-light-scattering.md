# Differential Static Light Scattering (DSLS)

## Overview

Differential static light scattering (DSLS) is a thermal stability assay that measures the temperature-dependent aggregation of proteins by detecting scattered light, making it suitable for integral membrane proteins in detergent solution. Protein samples are diluted to 0.2 mg/mL and analyzed in a 384-well plate (35 µL per well) using a Stargazer-384 instrument (Harbinger Biotech) with a temperature gradient from 25°C to 85°C at 1.0°C/min. DSLS was used as a high-throughput biophysical screen for human mPGES-1/inhibitor complexes to identify those most likely to crystallize. A strong correlation was observed between crystallizability and ligand-induced increases in the aggregation temperature: compounds that shifted the aggregation temperature by at least 2°C yielded crystal structures, while those that did not stabilize the protein failed to crystallize. Over 100 high-resolution mPGES-1/inhibitor structures were produced using this prioritization strategy over a five-year period.
