# Bac-to-Bac Baculovirus Expression System

## Overview

The Bac-to-Bac baculovirus expression system (Invitrogen) uses site-specific transposition in E. coli to generate recombinant bacmids for protein expression in insect cells. The target gene is subcloned into a pFastBac donor vector, which is transformed into DH10Bac competent cells containing a baculovirus shuttle vector (bacmid) and a helper plasmid. Transposition between the mini-Tn7 element on the pFastBac vector and the mini-attTn7 target site on the bacmid disrupts the lacZ gene, enabling blue-white selection of recombinant bacmids. Purified bacmid DNA is transfected into Sf9 or Hi5 insect cells to produce recombinant baculovirus, and the amplified virus is used to infect cells for protein expression. In the context of membrane protein structural biology, this system was used to express the bovine urea transporter UT-B with a C-terminal TEV-octohistidine tag from a modified pFastBac Dual vector, with infected Sf9 cells harvested 48-60 h post-infection.
