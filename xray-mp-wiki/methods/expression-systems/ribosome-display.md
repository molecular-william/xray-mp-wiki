# Ribosome Display

## Overview

Ribosome display is an in vitro selection technique for the isolation of
high-affinity protein binders from large combinatorial libraries. It
maintains the genotype-phenotype linkage through the formation of stable
mRNA-ribosome-protein complexes, without the need for transformation,
enabling selection from libraries exceeding 10^12 variants. Ribosome
display was used to select DARPin binders against the multidrug efflux
pump AcrB, yielding inhibitory DARPins with dissociation constants in
the low nanomolar range.

## Advantages

- Larger library sizes than phage display (>10^12 variants)
- No transformation step required
- Can be performed entirely in vitro
- Compatible with detergent-solubilized membrane proteins

## Disadvantages

- Requires careful maintenance of mRNA integrity
- Selection buffer must keep target protein stable and soluble
- May require special considerations for membrane protein targets
