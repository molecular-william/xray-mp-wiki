# Hi5 Insect Cell Expression System

## Overview

Hi5 (High Five) cells are a clonal isolate derived from Trichoplusia ni (cabbage looper) ovarian cells (BTI-TN-5B1-4). They are widely used as an alternative to Sf9 cells for baculovirus-mediated protein expression. Hi5 cells typically produce higher yields of secreted proteins and are preferred for expression of glycoproteins, although their N-glycosylation pattern differs from Sf9 cells (more processed, paucimannose-type glycans).

Hi5 cells are typically cultured in suspension at 27°C in serum-free media (e.g., Express Five SFM, Insect-XPRESS). They are infected with recombinant baculovirus at MOI 1-5 and harvested 48-72 hours post-infection. Common uses in the wiki include membrane protein expression for structural studies.
