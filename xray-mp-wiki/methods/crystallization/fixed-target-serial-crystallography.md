# Fixed-Target Serial Crystallography (On-Chip Crystallization)

## Overview

Fixed-target serial crystallography using on-chip crystallization delivers protein microcrystals on micro-patterned silicon chips (Roadrunner chips) for serial X-ray diffraction experiments. Roadrunner chips are manufactured from single-crystalline silicon with a 10-20 µm thick membrane area featuring a periodic pattern of micro-pores that hold the crystals. Two chip designs are available: Roadrunner I (1.5 × 1.5 mm membrane, 2-3 µl sample volume) for cryogenic and room-temperature work, and Roadrunner II (larger membrane area, ~100 µl sample) for high-throughput serial data collection. Crystals are grown directly on the chips by sitting-drop vapor diffusion using custom 3D-printed crystallization chambers, allowing established crystallization conditions to be transferred directly. After crystal growth, excess mother liquor is blotted away, leaving crystals exposed on the chip for in-situ diffraction measurements with very low background scattering. The method was demonstrated with proteinase K, thermolysin, human aquaporin 2 (at LCLS, 3.7 Å, PDB 6qf5), and DRAK2, and is compatible with both synchrotron and XFEL beamlines.
