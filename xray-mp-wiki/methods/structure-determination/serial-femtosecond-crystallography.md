# Serial Femtosecond Crystallography

## Overview

Serial femtosecond crystallography (SFX) is an X-ray crystallography technique that uses femtosecond X-ray pulses from an X-ray free-electron laser (XFEL) to collect diffraction data from microcrystals. The ultrashort pulse duration enables data collection before radiation damage affects the crystal, making it ideal for membrane protein microcrystals that are too small for synchrotron studies. When combined with lipidic cubic phase (LCP) as both growth and delivery matrix (LCP-SFX), it enables structure determination of challenging membrane targets at room temperature. SFX has also been applied to photosystem II microcrystals using a gas focusing liquid injector for sample delivery, enabling time-resolved studies of the Kok cycle.
