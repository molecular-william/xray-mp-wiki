# Molecular Dynamics Simulation

## Overview

Molecular dynamics (MD) simulation is a computational technique for studying atomic-level motions of biomolecules over time by numerically integrating Newton's equations of motion. MD simulations were used to investigate ligand entry pathways in the sigma-1 receptor (σ1R), a small integral membrane receptor in the ER. Simulations of the human σ1R revealed two drastic conformational changes involving the disruption of hydrogen bonds between multiple β-sheets of the cupin-fold domain, exposing the ligand-binding site (PATH1). Alongside crystal structures of Xenopus laevis σ1R and mutagenesis data, the simulations helped evaluate alternative ligand access routes, supporting the PATH2 model in which ligands enter through the V-shaped α4/α5 helix bundle without requiring large-scale β-barrel disruption.
