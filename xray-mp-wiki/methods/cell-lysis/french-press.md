# French Press Cell Lysis

## Overview

French press cell lysis is a high-pressure homogenization technique used to disrupt bacterial cell walls and release intracellular contents. The method forces a cell suspension through a narrow orifice at high pressure (typically 10,000-30,000 psi), causing shear forces that rupture the cells. It is widely used for large-scale preparation of membrane protein samples from E. coli cultures.

## Principle

A cell suspension is pressurized in a closed chamber and then forced through a narrow sapphire or diamond orifice. The rapid pressure drop and shear forces at the orifice cause cell wall rupture, releasing cellular contents including membrane proteins into the homogenate. The process is performed at low temperature to minimize protein degradation.

## Advantages

- Effective for large-scale cell disruption
- Gentle compared to sonication (minimal heat generation)
- Scalable to large culture volumes
- Suitable for membrane protein preparation

## Disadvantages

- Requires specialized equipment (French press)
- Limited to relatively small sample volumes per run
- May not fully disrupt all cell types
- Membrane fraction still requires detergent solubilization for protein extraction
