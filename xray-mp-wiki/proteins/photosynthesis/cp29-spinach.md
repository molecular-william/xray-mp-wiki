# CP29 Light-Harvesting Complex from Spinach (Spinacia oleracea)


## Overview

CP29 is a minor light-harvesting chlorophyll a/b-binding protein from photosystem II of spinach (Spinacia oleracea). It functions in light harvesting and energy regulation, participating in non-photochemical quenching. The structure was determined to 2.8 Å resolution via X-ray crystallography, revealing 13 chlorophyll molecules (8 Chl a, 5 Chl b), 2 [Lutein](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/lutein/) molecules, and 1 [Violaxanthin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/violaxanthin/) molecule. CP29 plays important roles in energy transfer between the major light-harvesting complex II (LHC-II) and the photosystem II core.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Chlorophyll and carotenoid organization in CP29

The CP29 structure reveals 13 chlorophyll binding sites: 8 Chl a (a602, a603, a604, a609, a610, a611, a612, a613) and 5 Chl b (b606, b607, b608, b614, b615). Site a610 is a mixed Chl a/Chl b site. Two [Lutein](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/lutein/) molecules and one [Violaxanthin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/violaxanthin/) [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) are bound, with the [Violaxanthin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/violaxanthin/) interacting with multiple Chl sites (a602, a603, a604, b606, b607). The Chl pair a611-a615 with center-to-center distance of 7.01 Å and their common ligand G3P ([Glycerol 3 Phosphate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glycerol-3-phosphate/)) form an energetically coupled dimer important for energy transfer.

### Sequence and structural comparison with LHCII

CP29 shares significant sequence homology with LHCII, with conserved secondary structure elements including the three transmembrane helices (B, C, E) and the amphipathic helix D. The chlorophyll binding sites are largely conserved between CP29 and LHCII, though CP29 lacks some of the peripheral chlorophyll sites found in LHCII.
