# Spinach Light-Harvesting Complex II (LHC-II)


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/P12333">UniProt: P12333</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Spinacia oleracea</span>

## Overview

The spinach light-harvesting complex II ([LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/)) from Spinacia oleracea is the major light-harvesting pigment-protein complex of [Photosystem II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/photosystem-ii/) in green plants. It serves as the principal solar energy collector in photosynthesis and functions in photoprotection under high-light conditions. The first X-ray crystal structure of spinach [LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/) at 2.72 A resolution was determined using icosahedral proteoliposome assembly. Each monomer comprises 232 amino acid residues, 14 chlorophylls (8 Chl a and 6 Chl b), 4 carotenoids (2 [LUTEIN](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/lutein/), 1 [Neoxanthin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/neoxanthin/), and 1 xanthophyll-cycle [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/)), and 2 lipids.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Chlorophyll arrangement and assignment

The structure unambiguously assigns 14 chlorophylls per monomer as eight Chl a and six Chl b molecules (Chl a/b molar ratio of 1.33). The transition dipole moments of all 14 chlorophylls were positioned, with Qx and Qy moments assigned by proper head group positioning. Chlorophylls are vertically distributed into two layers: eight near the stromal surface (five Chl a, three Chl b) forming an elliptical ring around helices A and B, and six near the luminal surface (three Chl a, three Chl b) forming two clusters. The average centre-to-centre distance between neighbouring chlorophylls is 11.26 A (range 9.74-12.79 A).

### Carotenoid binding sites and energy transfer

Four carotenoid-binding sites per monomer: two [LUTEIN](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/lutein/) molecules provide structural linkage between helices A and B; one 9'-cis [Neoxanthin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/neoxanthin/) is located in the Chl b-rich region around helix C; and one xanthophyll-cycle [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) ([Violaxanthin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/violaxanthin/)/antheraxanthin) is at the monomer-monomer interface. The [LUTEIN](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/lutein/) polyene chains are fixed in hydrophobic cavities providing rigid linkage. Efficient singlet energy transfer from [LUTEIN](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/lutein/) to Chl a and from [Neoxanthin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/neoxanthin/) to Chl b was demonstrated.

### Structure-based non-photochemical quenching model

A structure-based NPQ model is proposed. DGDG-mediated aggregation of [LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/) trimers establishes energy-transfer pathways between trimers. The xanthophyll-cycle [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) at the monomer-monomer interface serves as a trapping site for non-radiative energy dissipation. Chl a 613-Chl a 614 pair (distance < 10 A) may also function as an excitation energy quencher. Protonation of lumen-exposed acidic residues (Glu 94, Asp 111, Glu 207, Asp 211) under low pH triggers conformational changes in helix D and the BC loop, promoting quenching.

### Phosphatidylglycerol role in trimer stabilization

[Phosphatidylglycerol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylglycerol/) (PG) is critical for [LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/) trimer stabilization. Two PG molecules per trimer bridge adjacent monomers through hydrogen bonds and ionic interactions with Tyr 44 and Lys 182. The Chl a 611 is coordinated to the PG phosphodiester group, a second known case of this coordination mode. Proteolytic removal of the first 49-51 amino acid residues leads to PG loss and complete trimer dissociation into monomers.

### Icosahedral proteoliposome assembly

[LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/) trimers self-assemble into T=1 icosahedral particles with 20 trimers per particle in a closed 532 point group symmetry. The spherical shell has an outer diameter of 261 A and inner diameter of 160 A. Inter-trimer contacts are mediated by DGDG molecules and chlorophyll pairs. This Type III membrane-protein crystal differs from traditional Type I and Type II assemblies, with proteoliposomes contacting each other through polar stromal surface interactions.
