## Overview

The photosynthetic reaction center from Blastochloris viridis (RCvir, formerly Rhodopseudomonas viridis) is an integral membrane protein complex that converts light energy into chemical free energy. The RC consists of four protein subunits (L, M, H, and a c-type cytochrome) and binds multiple cofactors including [Bacteriochlorophyll](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/bacteriochlorophyll/), [Bacteriopheophytin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/bacteriopheophytin/), quinones, and a non-heme [Iron](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/iron/) atom. This study used serial femtosecond crystallography (SFX) at an X-ray free-electron laser (XFEL) to determine the RCvir structure at 2.4 A resolution. The crystals were optimized through a multi-round microseeding strategy starting from known macrocrystal conditions. The room-temperature SFX structure revealed several advantages over conventional cryo-crystallography: the flexible Glu45-Pro54 loop of the H subunit could be modeled (disordered in all previous structures), lipid molecules were observed with improved electron density, and two new lipid sites were identified.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Flexible H-subunit loop resolved by SFX

The Glu45-Pro54 loop of the H subunit was resolved in the 2.4 A SFX structure, whereas it is disordered in all 21 previously deposited RCvir structures solved using conventional cryo-crystallography. Continuous positive Fo-Fc electron density at 3 sigma was observed for this missing region. The high multiplicity of SFX data (1,529-fold) and room temperature data collection likely contribute to improved electron density for this flexible region.

### Lipid molecule identification

Two new lipid sites not observed in other RCvir structures were identified in the SFX structure, likely made observable by the much higher multiplicity of SFX data. Lipid molecules pack around RCvir in a manner closely resembling other structures crystallized in the same space group (P4_3 2_1 2). Several well-ordered lipid molecules are seen in multiple structures, but a putative lipid running along the M chain did not display sufficient electron density to support modeling.

### Multi-round microseeding improves SFX resolution

Each cycle of microseeding improved the resolution: crushed macrocrystals (3.3 A), one round of seeding (2.8 A), and two rounds of seeding (2.4 A). Microcrystals showed greater homogeneity and larger size (~20 micrometers) after two rounds. The seeding approach uses significantly more seed stock (5.4% v/v undiluted) than conventional seeding, providing an enormous number of nucleation sites crucial for producing large quantities of rapidly growing microcrystals. Approximately 4% of all images indexed, two orders of magnitude better than microcrystals grown in a lipidic sponge phase.

### First membrane protein SFX structure at near-atomic resolution

This 2013 study is one of the first membrane protein structures determined by serial femtosecond crystallography (SFX) at an X-ray free-electron laser (XFEL). RCvir microcrystals grown in lipidic sponge phase (LSP) were injected across the focused XFEL beam at the LCLS. From 2 million images, 1,175 were indexed and merged to recover 3.5 A resolution (PDB 4CAS). Molecular replacement used the 2.8 A cryo-structure (PDB 2WJN) as search model. These results demonstrated that SFX can yield side-chain-resolvable structures from membrane protein microcrystals.

### Radiation damage resistance in SFX data

Each RCvir microcrystal received a 42-fs X-ray pulse depositing 33 MGy, ~100x the conventional room-temperature dose limit. Despite this extreme dose, no radiation damage was visible. The covalent thioether bond linking the N-terminal cysteine of the cytochrome subunit to a diacylglycerol molecule remained intact (cleaved at high cryogenic doses). Metal centres (Mg in [Bacteriochlorophyll](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/bacteriochlorophyll/), Fe in [Heme](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/)) showed strong positive residual density in omit maps. These findings established SFX as a damage-free method for radiation-sensitive membrane protein structures.

### LCP microcrystallization of RCvir via unconventional microseeding

A novel LCP microcrystallization protocol was developed for Blastochloris viridis photosynthetic reaction centre (RCvir) using an unconventional seeding approach. Vapour-diffusion microcrystals grown in detergent solution ([LDAO](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/ldao/)) were crushed and used as seed stock for LCP crystallization. This was important because conventional LCP crystallization of RCvir produced needle-like crystals with poor diffraction or hexagonal crystals with unindexable unit cells. Seeding in LCP with a 5.4% v/v seed stock (much higher than typical seeding ratios) provided nucleation sites and yielded well-diffracting rhomboid microcrystals in space group P2_12_12. The resulting LCP microcrystals diffracted to 2.4 A (SACLA) and 2.25 A (SwissFEL), comparable to the best vapour-diffusion crystals.

### High-occupancy QB pocket in LCP-grown RCvir by ubiquinone doping

A key advantage of LCP crystallization is the ability to add hydrophobic cofactors directly to the [Monoolein](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/monoolein/) matrix. UQ1 and UQ2 ([Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) 1 and 2) were doped into the [Monoolein](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/monoolein/) before crystallization, replacing the native [Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) 9 (UQ9) that is lost during RCvir purification. This strategy yielded high occupancy in the QB pocket, with electron density quality comparable to other cofactors. QB was modelled at 100% occupancy with B factor 60.9 A2 (vs 39.0 A2 for QA). The [Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) occupies the proximal position near the non-haem [Iron](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/iron/), forming hydrogen bonds to His190_L, Ile224_L, and Gly225_L. The [Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) tail is flexible, with only the first isoprene unit modelled with certainty.

### Second ubiquinone-binding site observed in RCvir

A second ubiquinone-binding site was observed on the same side of the protein as the QB pocket but closer to the C cytochrome subunit. The [Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) binds in a hydrophobic pocket sandwiched between Phe89_M and Trp263_L. This site has been observed in several other RCvir structures (PDB 2i5n, 3g7f, 3t6d, 3t6e, 3d38, 7pil). Although the occupancy is lower than QB (B factor 91 A2 at 100% occupancy), both Trp263_L and Phe89_M are conserved residues between species, suggesting potential functional importance. The site may represent part of a dynamic [Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) pathway through the membrane.

### Crystal packing differences in the LCP microcrystal form

The LCP microcrystal form has a distinct unit cell (a=84.9, b=125.3, c=182.7 A) compared to earlier LSP structures (a=85.7, b=143.5, c=178.0 A for PDB 5x5u; a=84.5, b=138.5, c=177.8 A for PDB 2wjm). The b axis is 20 A and 13 A shorter, respectively, indicating tighter packing. Hierarchical tree analysis of internal C-alpha distance matrices showed the LCP structures form their own cluster with a mean difference of 0.35 A relative to all other RCvir structures. Despite the same space group (P2_12_12), the shorter b axis of the LCP form appears to give slightly tighter crystal packing, suggesting that differences in crystal packing have a larger effect on the refined structure than the chosen data-collection strategy.
