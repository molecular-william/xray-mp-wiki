# KR2 — Krokinobacter eikastus Rhodopsin 2 (Sodium Pump)

## Overview

[KR2 (Krokinobacter eikastus Rhodopsin 2) — Light-Driven Sodium Pump](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/kr2/) (Krokinobacter eikastus rhodopsin 2) is a light-driven sodium-pumping microbial rhodopsin belonging to the NDQ-rhodopsin family (Asn112, Asp116, Gln123 signature motif). It actively transports sodium ions out of the cell upon light activation, functioning as a type A sodium pump. [KR2 (Krokinobacter eikastus Rhodopsin 2) — Light-Driven Sodium Pump](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/kr2/) has been crystallized in multiple crystal forms: type A (blue, monomeric), type B (red, pentameric), and type C (red), with absorption maxima ranging from 522-566 nm. The structure reveals a characteristic seven-transmembrane helix retinal-binding fold typical of [Microbial Rhodopsins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/rhodopsin-mechanisms/microbial-rhodopsins/).

## Biological / Functional Insights

### NDQ-rhodopsin sodium pump mechanism

[KR2 (Krokinobacter eikastus Rhodopsin 2) — Light-Driven Sodium Pump](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/kr2/) is an NDQ-rhodopsin, named for the replacement of key [Bacteriorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/bacteriorhodopsin/) residues (Asp85, Thr89, Asp96) with Asn112, Asp116, and Gln123. This motif defines sodium-pumping rhodopsins. The ion-translocation pathway involves a network of residues including Asn112, Asp116, Gln123, Asp251, Arg109, and a series of ordered water molecules (w1, w2, w3).

### Structural plasticity in different crystal forms

[KR2 (Krokinobacter eikastus Rhodopsin 2) — Light-Driven Sodium Pump](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/kr2/) shows substantial structural variation between crystal forms. In type A (monomeric blue form), residues 111-114 are in an alpha-helical conformation. In type B (pentameric red form), the helix C region is twisted into a 3(10)-helix-like conformation. The B-C loop and N-terminal helix also vary between crystal forms.
