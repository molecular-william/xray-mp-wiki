# Human Claudin-1 (hCLDN-1)


## Overview

Human claudin-1 (hCLDN-1) is a tetraspanning tight junction membrane protein of the
claudin family. Its CpE receptor capacity has been disputed in the literature. The
hCLDN-4/cCpE structural study resolved this by measuring a cCpE binding affinity of
Kd = 936 nM (381-fold lower than hCLDN-4), due to its divergent DPMTP154 cCpE-binding
motif. Despite this low affinity, CpE treatment of hCLDN-1-expressing Sf9 cells does
cause morphological damage and cell death comparable to other claudins, indicating
that even low-affinity binding can support CpE-induced cytotoxicity. This suggests
that CpE can recognize claudins broadly through conserved cCpE-binding motif residues,
but only high-affinity complexes (Kd < 10 nM) with slow dissociation rates function
as physiologically relevant receptors.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Low-affinity CpE binding sufficient for cytotoxicity

hCLDN-1 has a DPMTP154 cCpE-binding motif (divergent from the NPLVA consensus),
resulting in cCpE affinity of 936 nM. Despite this 381-fold lower affinity than
hCLDN-4, CpE treatment of hCLDN-1-expressing Sf9 cells causes cell death
comparable to high-affinity claudins. This shows that CpE-induced cytotoxicity
requires only transient binding, but physiologically relevant receptor function
(at the low CpE concentrations found in vivo) requires high-affinity complexes
with Kd < 10 nM.
