# Acetylcholine-Binding Protein (AChBP)


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/P58154">UniProt: P58154</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Lymnaea STAGNALIS</span>

## Overview

[Acetylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/acetylcholine/)-binding protein (AChBP) is a soluble protein from the land snail Biomphalaria glabrata that serves as a structural and functional homologue of the extracellular ligand-binding domain of Cys-loop nicotinic [Acetylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/acetylcholine/) receptors (nAChRs). AChBP has been widely used as a surrogate structural model for the extracellular domains of Cys-loop receptors because it retains the characteristic ligand-binding properties and can be crystallized in both ligand-bound and apo states. The AChBP structure has been instrumental in understanding the orthosteric agonist-binding site architecture of Cys-loop receptors.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Carbamylcholine binding site in AChBP as a model for Cys-loop receptors

The AChBP structure with bound [Carbamylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carbamylcholine/) was used as a structural reference for understanding the agonist-binding site in Cys-loop receptors. The superposition of AChBP (grey) with [GLIC (Gloeobacter violaceus Ion Channel)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) (green) in the ligand-binding region revealed similarities in the binding pocket architecture. [Carbamylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carbamylcholine/) bound to AChBP forms ionic interactions with residues in the binding pocket, including contributions from aromatic residues that participate in cation-pi interactions with the positively charged ammonium group of [Carbamylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carbamylcholine/). This structural comparison helped establish the location of the orthosteric binding site in [GLIC (Gloeobacter violaceus Ion Channel)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) and other pLGICs.
