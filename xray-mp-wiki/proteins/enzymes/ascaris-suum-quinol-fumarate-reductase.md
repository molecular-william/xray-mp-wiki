## Overview

The X-ray crystal structure of mitochondrial quinol–fumarate reductase (QFR) from the parasitic nematode Ascaris suum was determined at 2.85 A resolution. This is the first structure of an anaerobic ([QFR](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/enzyme-mechanisms/quinol-fumarate-reductase/)-type) mitochondrial complex II. The enzyme couples the reduction of fumarate to the oxidation of rhodoquinol (RQH2), the reverse reaction catalyzed by the aerobic [Succinate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/succinate/)–[Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) reductase ([Sulfide Quinone Oxidoreductase](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/sulfide-quinone-oxidoreductase/)). The structure comprises four subunits (Fp flavoprotein, Ip iron–sulphur subunit, CybL large cytochrome b subunit, and CybS small cytochrome b subunit) with five cofactors: one FAD, three iron–sulphur clusters ([2Fe–2S], [4Fe–4S], [3Fe–4S]), one haem b, and one rhodoquinone (RQ). The structure is similar to aerobic complex II except for an additional 27-residue N-terminal peptide in CybS that forms unique hydrogen bonds with CybL, Ip, and Fp, likely contributing to structural stabilization. Fumarate binds in a twisted nonplanar conformation near the FAD isoalloxazine ring, inducing partial charge separation (C2 delta+ and C3 delta-) that facilitates hydride transfer from reduced [FAD](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/flavin-adenine-dinucleotide/) N5 to C2. The rhodoquinol binding site is formed by Ip, CybL, and CybS, with [3Fe–4S] as the nearest [Iron](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/iron/)–sulphur centre to RQ (7.9–9.2 A), indicating electrons are first accepted by [3Fe–4S] upon RQH2 oxidation.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### First structure of a mitochondrial QFR-type complex II

This is the first structure of an anaerobic (QFR-type) mitochondrial complex II. It reveals the structural basis for the reverse reaction direction compared to aerobic succinate dehydrogenase, and the critical role of the low redox potential of rhodoquinol (RQH2, Em' = -63 mV) in driving fumarate reduction, compared to ubiquinol (Em' = +110 mV) which favors succinate oxidation.

### Twisted fumarate conformation enabling reduction

Fumarate binds in a nonplanar conformation near the FAD isoalloxazine ring. The C1 carboxyl group is twisted around the C1-C2 bond (dihedral angle 83.7 degrees), stabilized by hydrogen bonds with Gly A85, Thr A288, Glu A289, and Arg A320. This twisting breaks the uniform pi-electron distribution and induces partial charge separation (C2 delta+, C3 delta-). The C2 delta+ contacts FAD N5 (4.05 A), consistent with hydride transfer from reduced FAD to C2 for fumarate reduction. Arg A320 likely supplies a proton to C3 delta- to complete the reduction.

### Additional N-terminal peptide in CybS

A unique 27-residue N-terminal polypeptide in the CybS anchor subunit extends to form hydrogen bonds with CybL, Ip, and Fp. This region is not found in SQR-type complex IIs and likely contributes to stabilization of the A. suum QFR structure, possibly favouring the binding of rhodoquinol and fumarate.

### Rhodoquinol binding site

The RQ binding site is formed by Ip, CybL, and CybS, with 3Fe-4S as the nearest iron-sulphur centre (9.2 A to RQ O1, 7.9 A to RQ O2). Electrons from RQH2 oxidation are first accepted by 3Fe-4S, then transferred via 4Fe-4S and 2Fe-2S to FAD. RQ is surrounded by conserved residues (Ser C72, Arg C76, Asp D106, Tyr D107) involved in hydrogen bond networks. The amino group of RQ (replaced by methoxy in ubiquinone) participates in one of these hydrogen bond networks.
