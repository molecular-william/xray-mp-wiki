# Methanosarcina barkeri Membrane-Bound Hydrogenase (HmMBH)


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/P0AAM1">UniProt: P0AAM1</a> <a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/P0ACD8">UniProt: P0ACD8</a> <a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/P69739">UniProt: P69739</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Escherichia coli</span>

## Overview

HmMBH is a membrane-bound hydrogenase from Methanosarcina barkeri that forms (SL)2 dimers. The enzyme shares conserved residues at the interface between H2ase molecules, particularly from the S subunit. Like EcHyd-1 and [Ralstonia eutropha Membrane-Bound Hydrogenase (ReMBH)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rembh), HmMBH is an O2-tolerant hydrogenase that couples H2 oxidation to electron transport.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Dimeric structure conservation

HmMBH forms (SL)2 dimers, similar to EcHyd-1. The great majority of residues
involved in contacts between the H2ase molecules are conserved in HmMBH,
especially those from the S subunit. This conservation suggests the dimeric
structure may be essential for inter-dimer electron transfer.
