# Signal Peptide Peptidase A from Bacillus subtilis

<div class="expr-badges"><span class="expr-badge expr-e-coli">E. coli</span></div>


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/O34525">UniProt: O34525</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Bacillus subtilis subsp. subtilis</span>

## Overview

Signal peptide peptidase A (SppA) is a membrane-bound self-compartmentalized serine protease that cleaves remnant signal peptides left behind after protein secretion. Here we report the first crystal structure of a Gram-positive bacterial SppA from Bacillus subtilis (SppA_BS). The 2.4 A-resolution structure reveals an octameric dome-shaped complex with eight separate active sites, each formed at the interface between neighboring protomers. The catalytic Ser147-Lys199 dyad is split between adjacent protomers, requiring octameric assembly for proteolytic activity.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Self-processing of C-termini within the octameric chamber

SppA_BS processes its own C-termini in cis, within the enzyme's octameric chamber. The C-terminal peptide (residues 326-335, sequence SPRMMLYAK) is bound in all eight active sites of the octamer, forming a continuous circle of peptides in the substrate-binding grooves. Electrospray ionization mass spectrometry confirmed the identity of the bound peptide (m/z 3355.67, theoretical mass 3355.9 Da). The K199A catalytic mutant slows but does not prevent [Self-processing of Membrane Proteins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/self-processing), and incubating wild-type and mutant enzymes together does not result in trans processing, confirming that processing occurs in cis. C-terminal [Protein Truncation for Crystallization](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/methods-techniques/truncation) mutants (delta 329-335, delta 307-335, delta 295-335) showed the same SEC elution profile as the full-length construct, indicating the C-terminus is not essential for oligomeric assembly.

### C-terminal peptide binds in antiparallel beta-sheet fashion

The C-terminal peptides bind within the substrate-binding grooves in an antiparallel beta-sheet fashion with respect to the residues that line the substrate-binding groove. The P1 position corresponds to Tyr331, P3 to Met329, and P2' to Tyr333. O-gamma of nucleophile Ser147 is within 2.8 A of the carbonyl carbon of Tyr331, and within 3.0 A of the main chain NH groups of the P1' residue (Leu332). The carbonyl oxygen of Tyr331 points into the oxyanion hole formed by Gly114 and Gly148. There are 11 direct hydrogen bonding interactions between the C-terminal peptide and the substrate binding site. The peptides occlude 6886 A^2 of surface area within the substrate-binding grooves (~861 A^2 per groove).

### Substrate specificity pockets S1, S3, and S2' identified

The S1 pocket is deep and hydrophobic, accommodating the P1 residue Tyr331 which is conserved among most Bacillus species. The S3 pocket is located at the interface between two protomers and accommodates the P3 residue Met329. The S2' pocket is a shallow hydrophobic depression created by Ile201, Met202, Val250, Leu166, Met144, Ala146, and Ser169, accommodating one side of the aromatic side chain of Tyr333 at the P2' position. Three protomers come together to form each active site.

### C-terminus may function as a competitive inhibitor for regulation

A synthetic peptide corresponding to the C-terminal sequence of SppA_BS (NH3-SPRMMYLYAK-COOH) functions as a competitive inhibitor of SppA_BS activity, with a Ki of 2.1 +/- 0.4 uM. This suggests that the C-terminus of SppA may function to regulate the activity of this compartmentalized membrane-bound protease until it is properly assembled within the bacterial membrane. The kinetic constants for SppA_BS were determined using dodecanoyl-NGEVAKA-MCA substrate (Km = 17.9 +/- 3.2 uM, kcat = 7.8 x 10^-2 s^-1).

### Octameric dome-shaped assembly with eight active sites

SppA_BS assembles into an octameric dome-shaped complex with eight separate active sites, twice the number seen within Escherichia coli SppA (SppA_EC) which forms a tetrameric complex with four active sites. The octameric state is supported by analytical size-exclusion chromatography and multi-angle light scattering analysis showing a molar mass of 225,000 Da, approximately equivalent to eight SppA_BS protomers.

### Intermolecular catalytic Ser/Lys dyad

The catalytic dyad is formed by nucleophilic Ser147 from one protomer and general base Lys199 from an adjacent protomer. The serine and lysine are more than 29 A apart within a single protomer but come within hydrogen bonding distance only upon octamer assembly. Three neighboring protomers are required for one complete functional unit: the nucleophile (Ser147), oxyanion hole (Gly114, Gly148), general base coordinating residue (Ser169), and S1 pocket residues from the central protomer; the lysine general base from one neighbor; and the S3 pocket partially from another neighbor.

### Deep, narrow S1 substrate specificity pocket

The S1 pocket is narrow and deep with hydrophobic walls (Phe227, Tyr151) and a polar bottom (Glu164 from neighboring protomer). The S3 pocket is shallower, wider, and more polar than S1, constructed from residues of two neighboring proteins. These pockets differ significantly from those in SppA_EC in size and shape, reflected in the different repertoire of peptides the enzymes cleave. SppA_BS can cleave LRR and LLVY peptides (arginine and tyrosine as P1 residues) where SppA_EC shows no detectable activity.

### SppA can digest folded proteins

Both SppA_BS and SppA_EC soluble domains are capable of digesting fully folded proteins. For example, both enzymes digest the E. coli lipoprotein BamD (a periplasmic protein involved in outer membrane protein assembly) to near completion after 17 h incubation. This is consistent with SppA possibly having a membrane protein quality assurance role in addition to signal peptide degradation.

### Gram-positive versus Gram-negative SppA structural comparison

The SppA_BS protomer is half the size of SppA_EC protomer and superimposes onto both the N-terminal domain (r.m.s.d. 2.7 A) and C-terminal domain (r.m.s.d. 1.1 A) of SppA_EC. Sequence identity is 16% to SppA_EC N-terminal domain and 26% to the C-terminal domain. The S1 pocket size difference between SppA_BS and SppA_EC results mostly from three residue substitutions: Tyr151 to Trp413, Ala120 to Glu383, and Gly226 to Arg487.
