# Helicobacter pylori Urea Channel (HpUrel)


## Overview

HpUrel is a [UREA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/substrates/urea/) channel from Helicobacter pylori J99, belonging to the AmiS/Urel superfamily. Urel is essential for H. pylori survival in the acidic gastric environment, facilitating rapid [UREA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/substrates/urea/) permeation to the cytoplasmic urease, which hydrolyzes [UREA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/substrates/urea/) to ammonia and carbon dioxide to neutralize periplasmic pH. HpUrel forms a hexameric channel with a central lipid bilayer plug. Each protomer contains five transmembrane helices (TMH1-TMH5). The structure reveals a constriction region around Trp153 that occludes the channel, and a pH sensor in periplasmic loop 2 (PL2) that regulates channel opening in response to acidic pH.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Hexameric architecture with central lipid bilayer plug

HpUrel forms a hexameric channel (C6 symmetry) generated from three
protomers in the asymmetric unit via a crystallographic two-fold axis.
The center of the hexamer contains a lipid bilayer plug with electron
density for six lipid tails in the periplasmic leaflet and 18 tails
in the cytoplasmic leaflet. The periplasmic side is narrower (17 A
diameter) where six copies of TMH2 converge, while the cytoplasmic
side is wider (28 A diameter) lined by six copies of TMH2 and TMH3.

### Trp153 constriction gate

Trp153 occludes the channel at a constriction region. The sharpened
2Fo-Fc electron density map reveals the position of Trp153 blocking
the channel, suggesting that conformational changes are required for
[UREA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/substrates/urea/) permeation. This gate likely opens in response to the pH signal
detected by the PL2 pH sensor.

### pH sensor in periplasmic loop 2 (PL2)

Periplasmic loop 2 (PL2) shows significant negative electrostatic
potential at pH 5.3 (the crystallization pH), consistent with its
proposed role as a pH sensor. The electrostatic potential is likely
to pre-orient the polar [UREA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/substrates/urea/) molecule as it enters the channel from
the periplasmic side, with NH2 moieties pointing toward PL2 and the
carbonyl oxygen pointing toward TMH2 near the center of the hexamer.
