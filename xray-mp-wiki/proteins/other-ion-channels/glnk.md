## Overview

GlnK is a PII Prostanoid Receptor Family signal transduction Rh Protein from Nitrosomonas europaea from Nitrosomonas europaea from E. coli Signal Peptide Peptidase (SppA, Protease IV) (SppA, Protease IV) coli that regulates the ammonia Ec2 AmtB in response to Intracellular Allosteric Antagonism nitrogen status. GlnK forms a homotrimer that binds to the [GIRK1 Cytoplasmic Pore (G Protein-Gated Inward Rectifier K+ Channel)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/girk1-cytoplasmic-pore/) (G Protein-Gated Inward Rectifier K+ Channel) (G Protein-Gated Inward Rectifier K+ Channel) face of the AmtB trimer, with each T-loop inserting into the [GIRK1 Cytoplasmic Pore (G Protein-Gated Inward Rectifier K+ Channel)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/girk1-cytoplasmic-pore/) (G Protein-Gated Inward Rectifier K+ Channel) (G Protein-Gated Inward Rectifier K+ Channel) pore exit of an adjacent AmtB PH-E Subunit E of F-[ATP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/atp/) Synthase Peripheral Stalk to block ammonia Side-Entry Ion Conduction Pathway. The [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) of GlnK in complex with AmtB reveals the physiologically relevant GPCR Inactive Conformation of the T-loop, which adopts a unique two-stranded [Antiparallel Dimerization in GPCR Crystallization](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/signaling-receptors/antiparallel-dimerization/) in GPCR Crystallization in GPCR Crystallization beta-sheet not seen in uncomplexed PII Rh Protein from Nitrosomonas europaea from Nitrosomonas europaea structures.
