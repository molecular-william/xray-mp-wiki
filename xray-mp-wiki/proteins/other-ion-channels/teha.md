# TehA (E. coli)


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/P44741">UniProt: P44741</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Haemophilus influenzae</span>

## Overview

TehA from Escherichia coli is a 314-residue membrane protein and a bacterial
homolog of the plant nitrate/chloride channel [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) (Deng et al., 2021). It
contains 1 cysteine and 10 methionines. The structure was determined by
native-SAD phasing at 5 keV using a helium path, with data assembled from 18
microcrystals. Anomalous scattering also identified bound Cl- and Na+ ions
that were not observed in previous higher-energy X-ray structures, suggesting
a possible functional role for these ions. The calculated Bijvoet difference
ratio is 1.8% at 5 keV.
