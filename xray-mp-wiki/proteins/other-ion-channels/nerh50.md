# NeRh50 (Nitrosomonas europaea Rh50 Ammonia Channel)

<div class="expr-badges"><span class="expr-badge expr-e-coli">E. coli</span></div>


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/Q82X47">UniProt: Q82X47</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Nitrosomonas europaea</span>

## Overview

NeRh50 is a bacterial homologue of the human Rhesus (Rh) 50 glycoprotein from Nitrosomonas europaea. It is a trimeric integral membrane protein belonging to the Amt/Rh protein superfamily that functions as an ammonia channel. The 1.3 A crystal structure of NeRh50 provides the first high-resolution view of an Rh family protein and reveals key structural differences from Amt proteins, including the absence of a defined ammonium ion binding site in the extracellular vestibule and a more open phenylalanine gate at the extracellular pore entry. The structure supports the model that Rh proteins are homotrimers and suggests they have lower ammonium affinities than Amt proteins and may function bidirectionally.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### NeRh50 is a homotrimer - Rh proteins are unlikely to form heterooligomers

The NeRh50 crystal structure reveals a tightly packed trimer generated by crystallographic threefold axis. Analysis of the trimer interface, involving 43 residues with contact surface >40 A^2, shows that human Rh50 proteins RhAG and RhCE/D share only 46% interface sequence identity (compared to 36% overall), which is too low for heterooligomer formation. Previous proposals of RhAG-RhCE/D heterooligomers are questioned by this structural evidence.

### Absence of ammonium ion binding site distinguishes Rh from Amt proteins

The extracellular ammonium ion binding site found in Amt proteins (shaped by Phe-107, Trp-148, and Ser-219 in EcAmtB) is not conserved in NeRh50. The architecture of the solvent-accessible extracellular vestibule differs significantly between Rh and Amt proteins. NeRh50 has Ile-198 instead of Ser-219, and Trp-148 has no structurally equivalent residue. Five water molecules occupy the corresponding space. This explains the lower ammonium affinity of Rh proteins.

### More open phenylalanine gate in Rh proteins

The phenylalanine gate at the extracellular pore entry is much less constricting in NeRh50 than in Amt structures. This widening results from both different side chain conformations and family-characteristic substitutions, notably Gly for Thr at position 251 of NeRh50, and a larger separation between transmembrane helices M6 and M8 in this region. Both phenylalanines are highly conserved in Amt and Rh50 proteins.

### Bidirectional ammonia conduction possibility

The vestibules at either end of the pore are more similar in NeRh50 than in Amt structures, both in shape and accessibility to water (or NH4+/NH3). There is a well-defined path by which a substrate molecule could pass the Phe gate that separates the extracellular vestibule from the central pore lumen. The main chain carbonyl group of Ala-140 (EcAmtB Ala-162) likely provides hydrogen bond acceptor capacity for translocating substrate molecules.
