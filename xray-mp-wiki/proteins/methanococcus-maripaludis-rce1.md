# Methanococcus maripaludis Rce1 (MmRce1)

<div class="expr-badges"><span class="expr-badge expr-e-coli">E. coli</span></div>


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/Q6LZY8">UniProt: Q6LZY8</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Methanococcus MARIPALUDIS</span>

## Overview

Rce1 (Ras and α-factor converting enzyme 1) is an intramembrane protease that processes CAAX proteins by cleaving the AAX tripeptide from prenylated C-terminal CAAX motifs. The 2.5 Å crystal structure of MmRce1 from Methanococcus maripaludis reveals a novel fold comprising eight transmembrane α-helices with a conical cavity that accommodates the farnesylated CAAX substrate. The catalytic site contains a glutamate-activated nucleophilic water mechanism, establishing Rce1 as the founding member of a novel glutamate intramembrane protease (IMP) family distinct from rhomboids, S2P metalloproteinases, and presenilin-like aspartyl proteases.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Glutamate-activated nucleophilic water mechanism

MmRce1 uses a novel catalytic mechanism for an intramembrane protease: E140 and H173 coordinate a bridging water molecule and act as general base catalysts to deprotonate it for nucleophilic attack on the scissile bond. H227 and N231 form an oxyanion hole to stabilize the transition state. This mechanism classifies Rce1 as the founding member of glutamate intramembrane proteases.

### Farnesyl lipid-binding site

The farnesyl lipid binds at an opening between TM2 and TM4. Nonpolar residues L45 (TM2) and L132 (TM4) flank this site. Disruption by bulky tryptophan substitutions (L45W, L132W) substantially reduces activity without affecting protein structure.

### Conical cavity architecture

The catalytic cavity is conical in shape, ~10 Å deep into the membrane. It is open to the cytoplasm and the membrane interior (via a gap between TM2 and TM4) but sealed from the periplasm/ER lumen by conserved residues R145, T210, and E141. The cavity accommodates the farnesylated CAAX peptide substrate.
