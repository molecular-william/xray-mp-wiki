# Kir7.1 Inwardly Rectifying Potassium Channel

<div class="expr-badges"><span class="expr-badge expr-hek293">HEK293</span></div>


## Overview

Kir7.1 (encoded by KCNJ13) is an inwardly rectifying potassium channel expressed in several tissues within and outside the central nervous system, including the paraventricular hypothalamus (PVH). Kir7.1 is functionally coupled to the melanocortin 4 receptor ([MC4-R](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/mc4-r/)) through a G protein-independent mechanism. [MC4-R](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/mc4-r/) activation by alpha-MSH induces Kir7.1 channel closure (decreased K+ conductance, depolarization), while AgRP induces channel opening (increased K+ conductance, hyperpolarization). This coupling modulates PVH MC4R neuronal firing frequency and feeding behavior. Kir7.1 R125M (M125R) mutant is used for Ba2+-sensitive current recordings as it regains sensitivity to Ba2+ block.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### G protein-independent MC4-R:Kir7.1 coupling

[MC4-R](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/mc4-r/) couples to Kir7.1 through a G protein-independent mechanism. Alpha-MSH (1 uM) induces channel closure, reducing whole-cell K+ currents. AgRP (0.2 uM) induces channel opening, increasing K+ conductance. [SHU9119](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/shu9119/) mimics AgRP effect albeit with lower potency (10 uM). Some [MC4-R](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/mc4-r/) antagonists ([PG-934](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/pg-934/), [SBL-MC-31](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/sbl-mc-31/)) induce modest channel closure. AgRP has an EC50 of 85 pM for [MC4-R](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/mc4-r/):Kir7.1 coupling. This duality of channel opening vs. closing by different ligands demonstrates independent conformational states for G protein recruitment versus channel coupling.

### Functional selectivity and feeding behavior

Peptides that promote Kir7.1 channel closing (e.g., SBL-MC-69) reduce food intake in mice when administered intracerebroventricularly (500 pmol/0.5 uL). Peptides that induce channel opening (e.g., SBL-MC-93) increase food intake. This demonstrates a direct link between [MC4-R](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/mc4-r/):Kir7.1 coupling state and feeding behavior. The channel modulation occurs independently of G protein signaling, opening new therapeutic avenues for treating eating disorders.
