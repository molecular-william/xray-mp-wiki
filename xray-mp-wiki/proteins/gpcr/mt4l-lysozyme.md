# mT4L (Minimal T4 Lysozyme)

<div class="expr-badges"><span class="expr-badge expr-sf9">Sf9</span></div>


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/P00720">UniProt: P00720</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Enterobacteria phage T4</span>

## Overview

mT4L is a minimal variant of T4 lysozyme engineered for [GPCR](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/signaling-receptors/gpcr) crystallography by deleting the smaller flexible N-terminal lobe (amino acids 13-60) and inserting a short GGSGG linker to connect helices A and C. This compact construct eliminates the lobe flexibility inherent in wild-type T4L that can contribute to poor crystal quality. When fused to the M3 muscarinic receptor, mT4L produced untwinned crystals in space group C2 that diffracted to 2.8 A resolution (PDB 4U15), the highest resolution structure among the three M3-T4L variants. The smaller interface of mT4L is mediated by helices 1 and 2, while a second antiparallel interface forms between helices 4 and 5.

**Expression:**

PCR-based mutagenesis using Pfu Turbo Hotstart polymerase with primers T4Ldel_F (GACGAAGGCGGCGCAGTGGCGGCGACGAGGCGGAGAAACTCTTC) and T4Ldel_R (CCTCGTCGCCGCACCTGCCGCCGCCTTCGTCGATGCGCAG). QC program employed 24 cycles with 30 s annealing at 49 C and 6 min extension at 72 C. Iodoacetamide (1 mg/ml) added at lysis stage to alkylate free cysteines.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Minimal T4L provides highest resolution GPCR structure

Among the three T4L variants tested (wtT4L, dsT4L, mT4L), mT4L produced the highest resolution M3 receptor structure at 2.8 A. The deletion of the flexible N-terminal lobe eliminates the conformational heterogeneity that contributes to crystal disorder. The GGSGG linker maintains sufficient connectivity between helices A and C while removing the mobile domain. The mT4L construct also enabled crystallization with both [Tiotropium](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tiotropium) and [NMS](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/n-methylscopolamine) under identical conditions.

### Novel receptor oligomerization interface in mT4L crystals

In M3-mT4L crystals, the receptor forms two distinct oligomerization interfaces, positioning receptors in a linear arrangement. The smaller interface is mediated by helices 1 and 2 of the receptor. A second interface forms through antiparallel interaction between helices 4 and 5 on each receptor molecule. The dimeric packing of mT4L itself is mediated by the C-terminal surface of T4L that becomes exposed upon removal of the N-terminal domain.
