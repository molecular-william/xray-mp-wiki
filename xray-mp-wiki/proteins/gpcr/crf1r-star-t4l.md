# Human Corticotropin-Releasing Factor 1 Receptor StaR-T4L (CRF1R)


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/P29476">UniProt: P29476</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Rattus norvegicus</span>

## Overview

The human corticotropin-releasing factor 1 receptor (CRF1R) is a class A G protein-coupled receptor that mediates the physiological responses to cortisol and stress. CRF1R is a key regulator of the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis and is involved in anxiety, depression, and stress-related disorders. The crystal structure was determined using a thermostabilized construct (StaR) with T4 lysozyme (T4L) fusion replacing the third intracellular loop (ICL3), crystallized in lipidic cubic phase (LCP) with the inverse agonist ligand [Cp 376395](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/cp-376395/). The structure reveals the inactive state of a class A GPCR and provides insights into CRF1R ligand binding and activation mechanisms.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### CRF1R inactive conformation

The crystal structure of CRF1R in complex with the inverse agonist [Cp 376395](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/cp-376395/) reveals
the inactive state of a class A GPCR. The thermostabilized StaR construct, combined
with T4 lysozyme fusion, enabled crystallographic analysis. The structure provides
a framework for understanding CRF1R ligand binding and activation mechanisms.

### Thermostabilization of CRF1R

The CRF1R StaR construct showed significantly improved thermal stability compared to
wild-type CRF1R (Tm 44.7 C vs 18.4 C). Multiple constructs were tested: CRF1R wild
type (Tm 18.4 C), CRF1R StaR (Tm 44.7 C), CRF1R StaR T4 (Tm 37.5 C), and CRF1R 105
truncated construct (Tm 35.7 C). The StaR variant retained high affinity for [Cp 376395](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/cp-376395/)
(Kd 1.5 [NM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/nm/) vs 7.5 [NM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/nm/) for wild type).

### Ligand binding pocket characterization

Alanine mutagenesis of CRF1R residues in the transmembrane domain revealed key
residues involved in [Cp 376395](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/cp-376395/) binding. Residues F203A (3.44), M206A (3.47),
L280A (5.47), N283A (5.50), F284A (5.51), L287A (5.54), I290A (5.57), Y327A (6.53),
and Q355A (7.49) showed significant reductions in binding when mutated, indicating
their role in ligand recognition.
