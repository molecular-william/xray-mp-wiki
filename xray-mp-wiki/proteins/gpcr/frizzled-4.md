## Biological / Functional Insights

### Unusual transmembrane architecture with short helix VI

FZD4 features an unusual Tm fold where Helix Shift Mechanism for Carboxylate Drug Transport VI is short and tightly packed, distinct from all other [GPCR](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/signaling-receptors/gpcr/) Active State High-Affinity Agonist Binding structures. This unique arrangement results In a flat Intracellular Allosteric Antagonism Side with no cavity among helices III, VI, and VII, unlike class-A GPCRs where such cavities serve as Allosteric Mechanism of Aspartate Carbamoyltransferase Turkey Beta1-Adrenergic Receptor Ligand-Free Basal State-AChBP sites.

### Extremely narrow and highly hydrophilic pocket

The FZD4 [De Novo Design of Transmembrane Pores](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/de-novo-design-transmembrane-pores/) [DRD2-EBP](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/drd2-extended-binding-pocket/) (882 Å³) is larger than [SMO](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/smoothened/)'s (763 Å³) but has [TEVC](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/quality-assessment/two-electrode-voltage-clamp/) extremely narrow constrictions (4.8 Å² and 8.0 Å²) caused by bulky Side chains. The [DRD2-EBP](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/drd2-extended-binding-pocket/) is the most hydrophilic of all known [GPCR](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/signaling-receptors/gpcr/) Active State High-Affinity Agonist Binding structures, containing a [Manganese-Calcium Cluster (Mn4Ca Cluster)](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/miscellaneous/manganese-calcium-cluster/) (Mn4Ca Cluster) (Mn4Ca Cluster) of E. coli Polar Lipids residues that are [Conserved Core Triad in GPCR Activation](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/signaling-receptors/conserved-core-triad/) in [GPCR](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/signaling-receptors/gpcr/) Activation in [GPCR](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/signaling-receptors/gpcr/) Activation across all ten Human FZD homologues. This explains the difficulty In developing small-molecule ligands targeting these [Desensitization in Cys-Loop Receptors](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/signaling-receptors/desensitization-in-cys-loop-receptors/) in Cys-Loop Receptors in Cys-Loop Receptors.
