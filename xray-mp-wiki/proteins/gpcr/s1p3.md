# Human S1PR3 (Sphingosine-1-Phosphate Receptor 3)

<div class="expr-badges"><span class="expr-badge expr-sf9">Sf9</span></div>


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/Q99500">UniProt: Q99500</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Homo sapiens</span>

## Overview

S1PR3 (Sphingosine-1-Phosphate Receptor 3, also known as EDG3) is a class A G protein-coupled receptor that binds the bioactive lysophospholipid sphingosine-1-phosphate ([S1P (Sphingosine-1-Phosphate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p/)). S1PR3 is unique among [S1P (Sphingosine-1-Phosphate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p/) receptors in its ability to couple to multiple G protein subtypes — Gq/11, Gi/o, and G12/13 — upon activation. It regulates immune responses including P-selectin-dependent leukocyte rolling via Gq/11-mediated signaling, cellular chemotaxis stimulation via Gi/o-mediated signaling, and cellular chemotaxis inhibition via G12/13-mediated signaling. The crystal structure of active human S1PR3 was determined in complex with its natural agonist d18:1 [S1P (Sphingosine-1-Phosphate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p/) and a Fab antibody fragment (AS55) at 3.2 Å resolution (PDB 7C4S). The structure reveals a long tunnel that penetrates obliquely through the receptor, accommodating the unbent [S1P (Sphingosine-1-Phosphate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p/) molecule. Four residues (Leu122, Trp256, Phe204, Phe260) — termed the "quartet core" — undergo orchestrating rotamer changes to accommodate the alkyl tail of [S1P (Sphingosine-1-Phosphate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p/), inducing the active conformation and determining G protein selectivity.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Endogenous agonist binding mode

The d18:1 [S1P (Sphingosine-1-Phosphate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p/) molecule exhibits an unbent conformation in a long tunnel that penetrates obliquely through S1PR3, crossing from near the extracellular end of TM1-TM2-TM7 through the gap between TM4 and TM5. The phosphate moiety forms hydrogen bonds with Tyr22(N-term) and Thr103(ECL1) and a salt bridge with Arg114(3.28). The amine moiety forms hydrogen bonds with Glu115(3.29) and Asn95(2.60). These polar interactions are more important for binding than the hydrophobic interactions with the alkyl tail.

### Quartet core mechanism for activation

Four residues (Leu122(3.36), Trp256(6.48), Phe204(5.47), and Phe260(6.52)) — collectively termed the "quartet core" — undergo orchestrating rotamer changes upon [S1P (Sphingosine-1-Phosphate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p/) binding. Leu122(3.36) and Trp256(6.48) flip their side chains, Phe204(5.47) forms a π-π interaction with Phe260(6.52) opening the gap between TM4 and TM5, and TM6 is displaced outward by approximately 11 Å. This explains how [S1P (Sphingosine-1-Phosphate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p/) analogs of varying lengths can activate S1PRs.

### G protein-subtype bias determinants

Leu122(3.36) specifically plays an important role in Gq/11 signaling. Short lipidic agonists (FTY720-P, d16:1 [S1P (Sphingosine-1-Phosphate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p/)) lose the ability to interact with Leu122(3.36), resulting in G12/13- and Gi/o-biased activation rather than Gq/11. Trp256(6.48) and Phe260(6.52) are responsible for forming an active conformation capable of coupling with all G protein subtypes, while Phe204(5.47) shows little effect on signaling efficacy.
