# Mouse Claudin-19 (mCldn19)


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/P01558">UniProt: P01558</a> <a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/Q9ET38">UniProt: Q9ET38</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Clostridium perfringens</span>

## Overview

Mouse claudin-19 (mCldn19) is a tight junction membrane protein belonging to the claudin family of tetraspanin-fold proteins. Claudins form the backbone of tight junction strands that regulate paracellular permeability. The crystal structure of a crystallization-optimized construct (mCldn19_cryst) was determined in complex with *Clostridium perfringens* enterotoxin ([C Cpe](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/c-cpe/)) at 3.7 Å resolution using molecular replacement. The structure reveals a four-transmembrane-helix left-handed bundle with two extracellular β-sheet segments (ECS1 and ECS2) that together form the [C Cpe](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/c-cpe/) binding interface. This structural work provides insight into how [C Cpe](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/c-cpe/) binding disrupts tight junction strands, leading to disassembly and loss of epithelial barrier function.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Both ECS1 and ECS2 mediate C-CPE binding

Previous biochemical studies suggested only ECS2 is involved in CPE binding. The mCldn19–C-CPE structure reveals that both extracellular segments ECS1 and ECS2 contact [C Cpe](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/c-cpe/). The conserved ECS1 motif (A/N/S)-I-(I/L/V)-(T/V) (residues 39–42 in mCldn19) interacts with the [C Cpe](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/c-cpe/) surface, while the ECS2 motif NP(V/L)(V/L)(P/A) (N150–P154) fits into a hydrophobic cavity formed by [C Cpe](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/c-cpe/) residues Y306, Y310, and Y312. Mutagenesis confirmed that substitutions in either motif reduce binding affinity for [C Cpe](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/c-cpe/).

### Tight junction disassembly mechanism

[C Cpe](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/c-cpe/) binding disrupts the linear arrangement of claudin protomers in tight junction strands. The enterotoxin creates steric clashes with neighboring claudin protomers and blocks the hydrophobic interactions that normally mediate claudin–claudin association. This structural interference prevents both lateral interactions between claudins in the same cell membrane and trans interactions with claudins from adjoining cells, leading to disassembly of the tight junction strand.

### Claudin tetraspanin fold

mCldn19 adopts the characteristic claudin/tetraspanin fold with four transmembrane segments forming a left-handed bundle. The extracellular domains form two β-sheet segments (ECS1 and ECS2). Compared to the CPE-insensitive claudin-15 structure, the β5 strand in the TM3–TM4 loop is longer in mCldn19, enabling more hydrogen bonds with the adjacent β1 strand. The loop between β1 and β2 (residues 35–42), which is disordered in mCldn15, is clearly resolved in the mCldn19–C-CPE complex and participates in [C Cpe](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/c-cpe/) binding.
