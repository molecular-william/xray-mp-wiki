# Human Claudin-3 (hCLDN-3)


## Overview

Human claudin-3 (hCLDN-3) is a tetraspanning tight junction membrane protein of the
claudin family. It is expressed in various epithelial tissues including the large
intestine, where its overabundance was previously thought to indicate CpE receptor
capacity. However, biophysical measurements from the hCLDN-4/cCpE structure study
demonstrate that hCLDN-3 binds CpE with 100-fold lower affinity (Kd = 252 nM) than
hCLDN-4 (Kd = 2.5 nM). This is due to the NPVVP152 motif in hCLDN-3, where valine
at position 3 provides a shorter hydrophobic side chain than the leucine found in
hCLDN-4 (NPLVA153), resulting in reduced surface groove penetration on cCpE and a
faster dissociation rate. Unlike in mice, where mCLDN-3 is a high-affinity primary
receptor (Kd = 9.5 nM, with NPLVP motif containing leucine at position 3),
hCLDN-3 is not a primary CpE receptor in humans.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Position 3 of NPLVA motif defines CpE affinity

hCLDN-3 has NPVVP152 at the cCpE-binding motif on ECS2. Valine at position 3
(instead of leucine in hCLDN-4) reduces side-chain length and hydrophobic
surface penetration into the cCpE groove, resulting in 100-fold lower CpE
binding affinity (Kd = 252 nM vs 2.5 nM). The dissociation rate (koff) is
30-fold faster for hCLDN-3 than hCLDN-4. Mutation of hCLDN-3 V150L/P152A
(swapping motif residues to match hCLDN-4) increases cCpE affinity 12-fold.

### Human vs mouse divergence in CpE receptor identity

Mouse CLDN-3 (mCLDN-3) has NPLVP152 with leucine at position 3, giving
high-affinity CpE binding (Kd = 9.5 nM) comparable to hCLDN-4. The mouse vs
human sequence difference at position 3 (Leu vs Val) explains why CLDN-3 is
a primary CpE receptor in mice but not humans. This divergence has important
implications for translating CpE-based therapies from mouse models to humans.
