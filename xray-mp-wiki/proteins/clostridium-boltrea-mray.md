# Clostridium boltrea MraY Phospho-MurNAc-pentapeptide Translocase with Tunicamycin


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/R0BTE9">UniProt: R0BTE9</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Clostridium bolteae 90A9</span>

## Overview

MraY (phospho-MurNAc-pentapeptide translocase) is an essential integral membrane enzyme that catalyzes the first membrane step of peptidoglycan biosynthesis: transfer of phospho-MurNAc-pentapeptide from UDP-MurNAc-pentapeptide to undecaprenyl phosphate, forming Lipid I. This paper reports the crystal structure of CbMraY from Clostridium boltrea in complex with the antibiotic [Tunicamycin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibiotics/tunicamycin/) at 2.6 Angstrom resolution. The structure reveals the details of [Tunicamycin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibiotics/tunicamycin/) binding and its mode of inhibition, showing how this nucleoside antibiotic targets the MraY active site. Comparison with the Aquifex aeolicus MraY apo structure (PDB 4J72) and muraymycin D2-bound structure (PDB 5CKR) reveals conformational differences in the active site region, including the lack of helix 9c and shifts in conserved catalytic residues.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Tunicamycin binding mode in MraY active site

The structure of CbMraY with [Tunicamycin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibiotics/tunicamycin/) reveals that the antibiotic binds in a cleft at the active site. [Tunicamycin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibiotics/tunicamycin/) interacts with key conserved residues including D175, N221, F228, D231, H290, and A287. The wide active site cleft contains a small inner pocket between G176 and F228 that accommodates portions of the [Tunicamycin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibiotics/tunicamycin/) molecule.

### Conformational differences from AaMraY

Comparison with the AaMraY apo structure shows that CbMraY has a wider active site cleft. The absence of helix 9c and shifts in loops 5-6 and 7-8 in the AaMraY apo structure result in flipping of several strictly conserved MraY residues including P51, D178, N221, F228 and E300. The loop-helix-loop segment between helix 9 and 10 in CbMraY replaces a peptide coil seen in AaMraY.
