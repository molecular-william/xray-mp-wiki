# Mouse Claudin-15 (mCldn15)

<div class="expr-badges"><span class="expr-badge expr-sf9">Sf9</span></div>


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/Q9Z0S5">UniProt: Q9Z0S5</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Mus musculus</span>

## Overview

Mouse claudin-15 (mCldn15) is a tight junction membrane protein belonging to the claudin family of tetraspanin-fold proteins. Claudins form the backbone of tight junction strands that regulate paracellular permeability in epithelial cell sheets. The crystal structure of a crystallization-optimized construct (mCldn15, with 33 C-terminal residues truncated and membrane-proximal cysteines substituted with alanines) was determined at 2.4 Å resolution using [Selenomethionine (SeMet)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/selenomethionine/) SAD phasing. The structure reveals a characteristic β-sheet fold comprising two extracellular segments (ECS1 and ECS2) anchored to a four-helix transmembrane bundle by a conserved W-GLW-C-C consensus motif. This work provides the first high-resolution view of a claudin family member and insights into the molecular architecture of tight junctions.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### First claudin family crystal structure

The mCldn15 structure provides the first atomic-resolution view of a claudin family member, revealing the characteristic tetraspanin fold with a four-helix transmembrane bundle and a β-sheet extracellular domain formed by five β strands (β1-β5) from two extracellular segments (ECS1 and ECS2).

### Conserved W-GLW-C-C motif as hydrophobic anchor

The conserved W-GLW-C-C motif (Trp29, Leu48, Trp49, Cys52, Cys62) anchors the β-sheet domain to the membrane surface. Trp29 extends from β1 into the helix bundle, while Leu48 and Trp49 protrude from the β2-β3 loop and appear exposed to the lipid environment. Cys52 and Cys62 form an intramolecular disulfide bond that stabilizes the β-sheet structure. Arg79 from TM2 further stabilizes the β-sheet domain through hydrogen bonds with the main-chain carbonyl groups of Leu48 and Phe65.

### Linear polymer assembly suggests tight junction strand architecture

In the crystal lattice, mCldn15 protomers form a linear polymer through tandem intermolecular interactions. A conserved hydrophobic residue (Met68) from the extracellular helix (ECH) of one protomer fits into a hydrophobic pocket formed by conserved residues in TM3 and ECS2 (Phe146, Phe147, Leu158) of the adjacent protomer. Freeze-fracture EM confirmed that mutations of these key residues abolish TJ-like strand formation, supporting the linear polymer model as representative of claudin strand assembly in tight junctions.

### Paracellular ion pathway

The extracellular surface of mCldn15 shows a negatively charged palm region formed by Asp55 and Asp64 on one edge of the β-sheet domain. The groove between the β3-β4 strands and the ECH likely lines the paracellular ion pathway, with charge distribution determining ion selectivity. Homology modeling suggests cation-selective claudin-2 has a similarly negatively charged palm, while anion-selective claudin-10a has a positively charged surface.

### Variable regions in extracellular loops

Two variable regions (V1 in ECS1 and V2 in ECS2) with poorly conserved sequences are defined. V1 corresponds to the disordered loop between β1 and β2 (residues 34-41), while V2 is the unstructured turn in ECS2 following Pro149. These variable regions likely mediate subtype-specific head-to-head interactions between claudins in adjacent cell membranes.
