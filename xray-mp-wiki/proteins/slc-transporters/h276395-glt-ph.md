# H276,395-GltPh (Humanized GltPh Mutant, R276S/M395R)

<div class="expr-badges"><span class="expr-badge expr-e-coli">E. coli</span></div>


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/O59010">UniProt: O59010</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Pyrococcus horikoshii</span>

## Overview

H276,395-[GLTPH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/glt-ph/) is a humanized mutant of the glutamate transporter homologue [GLTPH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/glt-ph/) from Pyrococcus horikoshii, bearing the R276S/M395R mutations that swap a conserved arginine residue from helical hairpin 1 (HP1) to transmembrane segment 8 (TM8). This mutant exhibits a 1,000-fold decrease in substrate affinity and a fourfold faster uptake rate compared to wild-type [GLTPH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/glt-ph/). The crystal structure reveals an unusual unlocked conformation in two of three protomers, where the transport domain swings away from the trimerization scaffold by about 12 degrees, creating a large hydrophobic crevice. The mutant lacks the quiescent locked states observed in wild-type and shows continuous elevator-like transport domain dynamics even under saturating substrate conditions.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### 1,000-fold decreased substrate affinity with fourfold faster uptake rate

The H276,395-[GLTPH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/glt-ph/) mutant exhibits an approximately 1,000-fold increase in apparent substrate dissociation constant (Kd) compared to wild-type [GLTPH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/glt-ph/), while showing a fourfold faster substrate uptake rate. The mutations affect both transport domain dynamics and substrate affinity, even though neither mutated residue (R276S/M395R) coordinates aspartate directly in existing crystal structures. This supports the hypothesis that substrate binding and transport domain dynamics are physically coupled.

### Unimodal dynamic behavior without quiescent locked states

In contrast to wild-type [GLTPH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/glt-ph/), which exhibits quiescent and dynamic periods, the H276,395 mutant shows no evidence of quiescent periods. Rapid transport domain dynamics persist even in the presence of saturating substrate concentrations. The low- and higher-FRET state lifetimes are unimodal, suggesting that the isomerization steps leading to locked configurations of the wild-type protein are strikingly altered or inaccessible under the examined conditions.

### Restricted FRET state sampling

The H276,395 mutant only exhibits transitions between low-FRET (~0.4) and a single higher-FRET (~0.65) state, never reaching the high-FRET (~0.9) state observed in wild-type. This indicates the mutant samples only outward-facing and a single partially inward-facing configuration, never achieving the fully inward-facing conformation. The FRET transition frequency decreased by less than twofold when transport-supporting chemical gradients were established.

### Crystal structure reveals unlocked conformation

The crystal structure of H276,395-[GLTPH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/glt-ph/) bound to Na+ and aspartate at ~4.5 A resolution revealed two distinct conformational states across the trimer. Protomer A occupies an inward-facing locked conformation similar to wild-type. Protomers B and C display an unusual unlocked conformation where the transport domain swings away from the trimerization scaffold by about 12 degrees, opening a large crevice between HP2 and the scaffold. The interface between transport and scaffold domains is reduced from ~1,300 A^2 to ~900 A^2, allowing water, detergent, or lipid access. This unlocked conformation appears determined by the properties of the protein itself rather than crystal contacts.

### Arg395 side chain penetrates membrane hydrophobic core

Molecular dynamics simulations revealed that Arg 395 in the H276,395 mutant faces the hydrophobic core of the bilayer, unlike Arg 276 in wild-type which forms hydrogen bonds with Asp 394 and bulk water inside the protein. The Arg 395 side chain engages in hydrogen bonding with penetrating lipid phosphate groups and accompanying water molecules. This membrane remodeling, driven by hydrophobic matching force, destabilizes the inward-facing locked conformation and increases water accessibility to the substrate-binding site and the domain interface.

### Lipid or detergent molecules stabilize the unlocked conformation

Molecular dynamics simulations showed that the hydrophobic crevice between transport and scaffold domains closes rapidly when solvated only by water. However, the open interface remains stable with lipids positioned in this space, suggesting that solvation by lipid or detergent molecules is necessary to stabilize the unlocked conformation. In simulations with docked lipids, the transport domain positions were maintained for the entire simulation duration.
