# Mj0480 Archaeal DUF106 Protein

<div class="expr-badges"><span class="expr-badge expr-e-coli">E. coli</span></div>


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/Q57904">UniProt: Q57904</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Methanocaldococcus jannaschii (strain ATCC 43067 / DSM 2661 / JAL-1 / JCM 10045 / NBRC 100440)</span>

## Overview

Mj0480 is an archaeal DUF106 protein from Methanocaldococcus jannaschii that functions as a YidC-like insertase in the archaeal plasma membrane. It is the first archaeal member of the YidC/Oxa1/Alb3 family to be structurally characterized, providing direct experimental evidence for the universality of this insertase family across all three domains of life.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### YidC-like fold with conserved hydrophilic surface

Mj0480 adopts a YidC-like fold comprising three transmembrane helices (TM1-TM3) and
two amphipathic helices (EH1, EH2) that lie nearly parallel to the extracellular face
of the membrane. A striking feature is a conserved, lipid- and cytosol-exposed
hydrophilic surface presented by TM1-TM3, which is not accessible from the extracellular
side. This surface is lined with polar and charged residues (N42, T45, W107, Y180,
S184, R192) and is strongly conserved across archaeal DUF106 proteins.

### Selective binding to ribosome-nascent chain complexes

Mj0480 binds selectively to E. coli ribosomes displaying a stalled YidC substrate
(F0c subunit of F0F1 ATPase with SecM stall sequence) but not to total 70S ribosomes.
Photocrosslinking demonstrates that the buried hydrophilic surface of Mj0480 directly
contacts the nascent chain, similar to what has been shown for E. coli and B. halodurans
YidC. All four bilayer positions lining the hydrophilic groove (I41, W107, F114, F185)
crosslinked to the stalled F0c substrate.

### Structural conservation with bacterial YidC despite low sequence identity

Despite only ~14% overall sequence identity, Mj0480 superimposes on Bacillus halodurans
YidC2 (BhYidC) with an [RMSD](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/methods-techniques/rmsd/) of 3.9 Angstroms over 105 core residues. Both share the
core TM1-cytosolic coiled-coil-TM2-X-TM3 motif and a lipid-exposed hydrophilic groove
lined with polar and charged residues. The cytosolic-facing vestibule and coiled coil
(disordered in crystal) are enriched in methionine residues, analogous to bacterial YidC.

### Universality of YidC/Oxa1/Alb3 family across three domains of life

These data provide direct experimental evidence that archaeal DUF106 proteins are
YidC/Oxa1/Alb3-like insertases, establishing the universality of this family across
all three domains of life. The domain-swapped dimer observed in the crystal structure
likely arises from crystallization conditions (high protein concentration and detergent
destabilization) rather than physiological relevance. The archaeal DUF106 proteins
are predicted to have 3-4 TMs compared to the 5 TMs of bacterial YidC, making the
hydrophilic groove smaller and more exposed to the lipid bilayer.
