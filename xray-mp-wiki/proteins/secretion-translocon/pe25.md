# PE25


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/Q79FE1">UniProt: Q79FE1</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Mycobacterium tuberculosis</span>

## Overview

PE25 is the conserved N-terminal domain of the PE (Pro-Glu) protein family from Mycobacterium tuberculosis. PE25 forms a heterodimer with its partner [PPE41](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/ppe41/) through a four-helix bundle interface, creating an elongated all-helical structure characteristic of ESX-secreted substrates. PE25 is a key virulence factor secreted by the ESX-1 type VII secretion system and is essential for mycobacterial pathogenesis. The PE25-[PPE41](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/ppe41/) heterodimer structure was the first solved structure of a PE-PPE complex and has been widely used as a search model for [Molecular Replacement](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/molecular-replacement/) in mycobacterial protein crystallography.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### PE25-PPE41 heterodimer forms a four-helix bundle

The PE25-[PPE41](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/ppe41/) heterodimer interacts via a hydrophobic interface forming a four-helix bundle with two alpha-helices contributed by each partner. This structure represents the conserved N-terminal domain architecture of PE-PPE proteins and was the first solved structure of a PE-PPE complex (Strong et al., 2006). The elongated all-helical fold is shared by other ESX-secreted substrates including [EspB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/espB/) and [WxG100 Family Proteins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/wxg100-family-proteins/).

### PE25-PPE41-EspG5 complex reveals chaperone specificity

The crystal structure of the PE25-PPE41-EspG5 complex revealed that the EspG chaperone interacts with a relatively conserved motif on the [PPE41](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/ppe41/) protein (Korotkova et al., 2014; Ekiert and Cox, 2014). EspG chaperones are essential for PE-PPE heterodimer folding and stability, and protect the hydrophobic regions from self-polymerization. The YxxxD/E secretion motif of PE25 is helical in the complex structure but partially disordered in the isolated heterodimer (PDB 2G38, 4W4K).

### YxxxD/E motif conformation varies between structures

The YxxxD/E secretion motif in PE25 adopts different conformations depending on the structural context. It is helical in the PE25-PPE41-EspG5 complex structures (PDB 4KXR, 4W4L) but partially disordered in the isolated PE25-[PPE41](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/ppe41/) heterodimer structures (PDB 2G38, 4W4K). This conformational flexibility may be relevant to secretion mechanism recognition.
