# MycP1


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/P02787">UniProt: P02787</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Homo sapiens</span>

## Overview

MycP1 (Mycobacterial Protease 1) is a membrane-anchored serine protease of the mycosin family that processes [ESPB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/espB) during secretion by the ESX-1 type VII secretion system of Mycobacterium tuberculosis. MycP1 cleaves full-length [ESPB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/espB) (60 kDa) within its unstructured C-terminal domain to generate the mature 50 kDa secreted isoform. Specific cleavage sites have been identified at residues A358 and A386. MycP1 belongs to a family of mycosin paralogs present in all ESX clusters and is essential for ESX-1, ESX-3, and ESX-5 secretory processes.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### MycP1 processes EspB during secretion

MycP1 cleaves full-length [ESPB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/espB) (60 kDa) within its unstructured C-terminal domain to generate the mature 50 kDa secreted isoform. In vitro analysis identified residues A358 and A386 of [ESPB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/espB) as specific cleavage sites. The cleavage occurs during translocation, and the mature form is predominantly present in M. tuberculosis culture filtrate. MycP1 is essential for ESX-1 secretion and virulence.

### MycP paralogs are present in all ESX clusters

MycP paralogs are present in all ESX clusters and are essential for ESX-1, ESX-3, and ESX-5 secretory processes. This raises the possibility that other MycP proteases target C-terminal domains of PE-PPE proteins in other ESX systems, since all ESX clusters except ESX-4 encode pe and ppe genes.

### MycP1 is a membrane-anchored serine protease

MycP1 belongs to the mycosin family of membrane-anchored serine proteases. The crystal structure reveals the protease domain architecture and provides insight into the mechanism of [ESPB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/espB) processing. MycP1 plays a dual role in regulation of ESX-1 secretion and virulence.
