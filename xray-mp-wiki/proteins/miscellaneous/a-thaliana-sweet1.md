# A. thaliana SWEET1

<div class="expr-badges"><span class="expr-badge expr-s-cerevisiae">S. cerevisiae</span></div>


## Overview

A. thaliana SWEET1 is a glucose transporter from the SWEET family found in Arabidopsis thaliana. It consists of two [SemiSWEET Transporter Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/semisweet)-like units fused via an inversion linker transmembrane helix, forming a seven-transmembrane-helix protein. SWEET1 functions as a glucose transporter and was functionally characterized in a hexose-uptake-deficient yeast strain. Conserved tryptophan and asparagine residues in both transmembrane helix bundle repeats are essential for transport activity.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Conserved tryptophan and asparagine are essential for transport

In the first THB repeat of A. thaliana SWEET1, W56 and N73 correspond to W48 and N64 in L. biflexa [SemiSWEET Transporter Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/semisweet). In the second THB repeat, W176 and N192 are the equivalents. Alanine substitutions of all four residues (W56A, N73A, W176A, N192A) failed to complement the growth defect of a hexose-uptake-deficient yeast strain (EBY4000) in synthetic medium supplemented with 2% glucose as the sole carbon source. This demonstrates that tryptophan and asparagine play critical roles in sugar transport in plant SWEETs, consistent with their role in bacterial SemiSWEETs.

### Mutations do not affect plasma membrane localization

The W56A, N73A, W176A, and N192A mutations had no significant effect on the plasma membrane localization of SWEET1 in yeast, as confirmed by GFP imaging. The wild-type and all mutant SWEET1-GFP fusions showed membrane localization, while the empty vector control showed cytoplasmic GFP localization. This indicates that the transport-deficient phenotype is due to loss of transport activity rather than mislocalization.
