# afTMEM16 (TMEM16 Lipid Scramblase from Aspergillus fumigatus)

<div class="expr-badges"><span class="expr-badge expr-s-cerevisiae">S. cerevisiae</span></div>

## Overview

afTMEM16 is a calcium-activated lipid scramblase from the fungus Aspergillus fumigatus. It is a member of the TMEM16 (anoctamin) protein family, which includes both Ca2+-activated chloride channels and lipid scramblases. afTMEM16 was the first TMEM16 protein shown to function as a lipid scramblase after purification and reconstitution into liposomes. It shares high sequence similarity with [nhTMEM16 (TMEM16 Lipid Scramblase from Nectria haematococca)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/miscellaneous/nhtmem16/) from Nectria haematococca and exhibits similar homodimeric architecture with ten transmembrane helices per subunit. Both proteins provide structural and functional insight into the TMEM16 lipid scramblase subfamily.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### First TMEM16 lipid scramblase identified

afTMEM16 was the first TMEM16 protein demonstrated to function as a lipid scramblase after purification and reconstitution into liposomes. It catalyzes the movement of lipids between the inner and outer leaflets of the bilayer in an [ATP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/atp/)-independent, Ca2+-activated manner. The protein was shown to scramble a broad range of lipids.

### Homodimeric architecture with 10 transmembrane helices

afTMEM16 forms a homodimer with each subunit containing ten transmembrane helices, consistent with the topology revealed by the [nhTMEM16 (TMEM16 Lipid Scramblase from Nectria haematococca)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/miscellaneous/nhtmem16/) crystal structure. The two proteins share high sequence similarity and similar functional properties.
