# Adiponectin


## Overview

Adiponectin is a hormone secreted mainly from adipocytes that stimulates glucose utilization and fatty-acid oxidation. Its plasma levels are reduced in obese humans and associated with insulin resistance and type 2 diabetes. Adiponectin exerts its effects through two integral membrane receptors, ADIPOR1 and ADIPOR2, which belong to the PAQR family. Adiponectin enhances the intrinsic ceramidase activity of both ADIPOR1 and ADIPOR2, linking it to [Ceramide](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/ceramide) signaling pathways and energy homeostasis regulation.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Enhancement of ADIPOR2 ceramidase activity

Adiponectin increases the basal ceramidase activity of ADIPOR2 approximately 20-fold. This enhancement suggests that adiponectin binding allosterically activates the enzymatic function of ADIPOR2, linking adiponectin signaling to [Ceramide](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/ceramide) metabolism and energy homeostasis.

### Enhancement of ADIPOR1 ceramidase activity

Adiponectin also enhances the intrinsic ceramidase activity of [Adiponectin Receptor 1 (ADIPOR1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/adipor1), with approximately 25-fold increase over basal activity. Both ADIPOR1 and ADIPOR2 are activated by adiponectin, suggesting a conserved mechanism of receptor activation across the two receptor subtypes.
