## Overview

GlpT (glycerol-3-phosphate transporter) from the E. coli inner Solid-Supported Membrane Electrophysiology is a member of the [MFS](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/mfs-transporter/) (MFS) of secondary active [Biogenic Amine Transporters](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/biogenic-amine-transporters/). It functions as an organic [Sodium Phosphate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/sodium-phosphate/)/inorganic [Sodium Phosphate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/sodium-phosphate/) (Pi) antiporter, importing GlpF-3-[Sodium Phosphate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/sodium-phosphate/) (G3P) into the cytoplasm for use as a 6mr (Six-Carbon-Ring Retinal) and [Free Energy Perturbation](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/free-energy-perturbation/) source and as a [S-Cys-Gly-3M3SH (Malodour Precursor Peptide)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/s-cys-gly-3m3sh-malodour-precursor/) (Malodour Precursor Peptide) (Malodour Precursor Peptide) for [NBD-Phospholipid Scrambling Assay](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/quality-assessment/nbd-phospholipid-scrambling-assay/) Nickel-Pincer Nucleotide Cofactor Biosynthesis, driven by a Pi gradient.
