# Rv1410 (P55) MFS Transporter from Mycobacterium tuberculosis


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/K5B8L6">UniProt: K5B8L6</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Mycolicibacterium hassiacum DSM 44199</span>

## Overview

Rv1410 (P55) is a proton-driven Major Facilitator Superfamily (MFS) transporter from [Mycobacterium tuberculosis](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/miscellaneous/mycobacterium-tuberculosis/) that exports [triacylglycerides](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/triacylglyceride/) (TAGs) to the mycomembrane. It functions together with its operon partner, the [lipoprotein LprG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/lprg/) (Rv1411), to transport triacylated lipids that seal the mycobacterial cell envelope. Rv1410 belongs to the DHA2 subclass of MFS and is classified as a TAG:H+ antiporter. The structure of its close homologue MHAS2168 from Mycobacterium hassiacum was determined at 2.7 A resolution by [LCP](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/lipidic-cubic-phase/) crystallization in complex with a nanobody, revealing an outward-facing conformation with unusual TM11 and TM12 periplasmic helix extensions.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### TAG extraction from inner membrane by Rv1410

Rv1410 extracts triacylglycerides (TAGs) from the hydrophobic core of the mycobacterial inner membrane via lateral clefts between TM5-TM8 and TM2-TM11 in the inward-facing conformation. TAGs segregate to the membrane core rather than the leaflets. The transporter transitions to outward-facing where TM11-TM12 periplasmic extensions channel the TAG into the hydrophobic pocket of LprG.

### E157-R426 ion lock for proton coupling

A conserved salt bridge between E157 (TM5) and R426 (TM11) constricts the bottom of the central cavity and is proposed to function as an ion lock for proton translocation. Mutation of either residue (E147Q, R417A in Rv1410) inactivates the transporter.
