# Spr0694-0695 ABC Transporter from Streptococcus pneumoniae

<div class="expr-badges"><span class="expr-badge expr-e-coli">E. coli</span></div>


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/Q8DQF7">UniProt: Q8DQF7</a> <a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/Q8DQF8">UniProt: Q8DQF8</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Streptococcus pneumoniae (strain ATCC BAA-255 / R6)</span>

## Overview

The Spr0694-0695 complex from the Gram-positive pathogen Streptococcus pneumoniae
R6 forms a MacAB-like ATP-binding cassette (ABC) efflux pump involved in resistance
to antibiotics and antimicrobial peptides. Spr0694 serves as the nucleotide-binding
domain (NBD) and Spr0695 as the transmembrane domain (TMD), adopting a non-canonical
fold with only eight tightly packed transmembrane helices and an extracellular domain
(ECD) inserted between the first and second helices. The complex assembles with the
membrane fusion protein [Spr0693 Membrane Fusion Protein from Streptococcus pneumoniae](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/spr0693/) to form an intact tripartite efflux pump that
transports substrates through a nanotube channel from the inner membrane to the
peptidoglycan layer.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Non-canonical 8-TM ABC transporter fold

Spr0694-0695 belongs to the prokaryotic ABC3 family of ABC transporters with
only 8 transmembrane helices, unlike canonical 12-TM exporters. The tightly
packed TMD dimer provides no central cavity, suggesting a lateral substrate
access mechanism rather than alternating access. The ECD insertion between TM1
and TM2 is a defining feature of this family.

The structure represents a resting state where the two NBDs adopt a somewhat
closed conformation accessible for ATP, but the substrate entrance is blocked
by the guard helix alpha6.

### Guard helix controls substrate entry

Helix alpha6 at the interface of the ECD and TMD acts as a guard helix that
docks to an acidic pocket on the TM helices. In Spr0695, this guard helix has
a relatively lower B-factor compared to [MACB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/macb/), indicating it is stably positioned
to block substrate entry in the resting state.

Mutagenesis of three positively charged residues (K207A/R208A/K210A) on the
guard helix impaired ATPase activity and reduced the MIC of LL-37 antimicrobial
peptide from 32 to 16 ug/mL. Crosslinking the guard helix to TM helices
(D49C-R208C, K52C-E205C, E374C-K207C) abolished resistance entirely (MIC 8 ug/mL),
confirming that guard helix flexibility is essential for substrate entry.

### ATPase activity requires NBD conformational changes

Spr0694-0695 exhibits ATPase activity in proteoliposomes. The E170Q mutation
in the catalytic Walker B glutamate of Spr0694 drastically reduced activity.
Crosslinking the two NBD subunits via G173C under oxidative conditions, which
covalently fixes the NBD dimer in a closed state, completely abolished ATPase
activity. This confirms that NBD dimer opening and closing are essential for
the ATP hydrolysis cycle in this family of 8-TM ABC transporters.
