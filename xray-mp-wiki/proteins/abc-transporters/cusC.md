# CusC Outer Membrane Channel


<a class="badge badge-uniprot" href="https://www.uniprot.org/uniprot/P77239">UniProt: P77239</a>

<span class="expr-badge">Escherichia coli</span>

## Overview

CusC is the outer-membrane channel component of the CusABC tripartite copper/silver efflux system in Escherichia coli. It functions as part of the RND (Resistance-Nodulation-Cell Division) efflux machinery, working together with the inner-membrane transporter CusA and the periplasmic membrane fusion protein CusB to extrude [Cu(i)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/cu(i)) and Ag(I) ions from the bacterial cell. CusC is functionally analogous to TolC in the AcrAB-TolC system and OprM in the P. aeruginosa multidrug efflux system. While the crystal structure of CusC was not solved in this study, its role as the outer-membrane channel completing the tripartite efflux complex is established. The distinct structural features of CusB (three-helix bundle Domain 4) may imply that CusC also possesses unique secondary structural features distinguishing it from TolC and OprM homologs.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### CusABC tripartite efflux system architecture

The CusABC efflux system consists of three components: CusA (inner-membrane RND transporter), CusB (periplasmic membrane fusion protein), and CusC (outer-membrane channel). This tripartite complex spans both the inner and outer membranes of the Gram-negative E. coli cell, exporting toxic Cu(I) and [Ag(i)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ag(i)) ions directly out of the cell. Heavy-metal efflux by CusABC is driven by proton import, catalyzed through the inner-membrane transporter CusA. The system is functionally analogous to the AcrAB-TolC system but is specifically dedicated to heavy-metal resistance.

### Distinct structural features of CusC

The crystal structure of [Cusb](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cusB) revealed unique features not found in other MFPs, particularly the three-helix bundle Domain 4. This distinct secondary structure of CusB may imply that its tripartite partners, including CusC, also possess unique secondary structural features distinguishing them from existing structures of their homologous proteins (TolC, OprM). The paper notes that exactly how these individual heavy-metal efflux components assemble into a functional complex must await the elucidation of the CusA and CusC structures.

### Predicted CusC trimeric model and full complex

A three-dimensional structure for the trimeric CusC outer membrane channel was predicted based on homology with known outer membrane channels. Based on the CusBA crystal complex structure and the predicted CusC model, a complete CusC3-CusB6-[Cusa](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cusA)3 tripartite efflux complex model was constructed. The model represents a 750-kDa complex that spans both the inner and outer membranes of E. coli and extrudes Cu(I) and Ag(I) ions. The hexameric CusB channel connects the trimeric CusA pump to the trimeric CusC channel, forming a continuous pathway for metal ion extrusion across the bacterial cell envelope.
