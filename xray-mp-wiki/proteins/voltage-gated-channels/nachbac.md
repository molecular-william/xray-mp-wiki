# NaChBac Bacterial Voltage-Gated Sodium Channel


## Overview

[NaChBac](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/nachbac/) is a bacterial voltage-gated sodium channel from Enterococcus hirae, recognized as the founding and functionally best-characterized member of the voltage-gated sodium channel family. [NaChBac](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/nachbac/) is a homotetramer where each subunit contains a voltage-sensing module (S0-S4) and a pore module (S5-P-S6), followed by a C-terminal domain (CTD) with a flexible linker and a four-helix coiled-coil bundle. [NaChBac](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/nachbac/) exhibits a considerably slower rate of inactivation compared to [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/navms), with an inactivation time constant of 203 ms versus 10 ms for [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/navms), which correlates with its shorter C-terminal linker region.
