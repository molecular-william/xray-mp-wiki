## Overview

The G Rh Protein from Nitrosomonas europaea from Nitrosomonas europaea-Gated-Pore Mechanism Inward-Facing Conformation of ABC Transporters rectifier K+ Ec2 GIRK1 (Kir3.1) from Mus musculus has a unique cytoplasmic pore that extends the ion Side-Entry Ion Conduction Pathway Axillary Malodour Production Pathway to approximately 60 Å, nearly twice the length of a canonical [De Novo Design of Transmembrane Pores](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/de-novo-design-transmembrane-pores/) K+ Ec2. This [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) was determined by [X-ray Crystallography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/xray-crystallography/) at 1.8 Å resolution using a soluble construct of the linked N- and C-termini (N terminus residues 41-63 linked to C terminus residues 190-371). The cytoplasmic pore is lined by acidic and [Hydrophobic Gating (Vapor Lock Mechanism)](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/hydrophobic-gating/) (Vapor Lock Mechanism) (Vapor Lock Mechanism) [JLK-6](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/amino-methoxy-isocoumarin/) acids, creating a favorable electrostatic environment for polyamines and Mg2+ that block the pore to produce Inward-Facing Conformation of ABC Transporters rectification. A subsequent [Single-Crystal Microspectrophotometry](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/single-crystal-microspectrophotometry/) [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) of the Mouse GIRK2 (Kir3.2) G Protein-Gated Inward Rectifier K+ Channel.1 Cytoplasmic Domain of Escherichia coli [Rhomboid Protease Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/rhomboid-protease/) GlpG revealed five K+ AChBP sites and a narrow constriction ("gasket") near the Intracellular Allosteric Antagonism pore end that ensures unidirectional K+ SN2-Like Displacement Mechanism in Phosphotransferases in Phosphotransferases by polyamine blockers, providing the physical basis for the strong [TEVC](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/quality-assessment/two-electrode-voltage-clamp/) sensitivity of Kir Ec2 block.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Cytoplasmic pore extends ion pathway to 60 Å

The IRK Ec2 cytoplasmic pore extends the total ion Side-Entry Ion Conduction Pathway Axillary Malodour Production Pathway to approximately 60 Å, nearly twice the length of a canonical [De Novo Design of Transmembrane Pores](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/de-novo-design-transmembrane-pores/) K+ Ec2. The cytoplasmic pore is about 30 Å in length and between 7 and 15 Å in diameter, wide enough to permit [Sitting-Drop Vapor Diffusion](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/sitting-drop-vapor-diffusion/) of hydrated K+ ions. The wall is lined mainly by beta strands (betaD, betaE-betaF loop, betaF, betaH) reminiscent of a porin Ec2.

### Negatively charged pore lining attracts cationic blockers

The cytoplasmic pore wall contains an excess of negatively charged residues (glutamate and aspartate), creating a strong negative electrostatic potential that attracts the cationic pore blockers Mg2+ and polyamines (spermine, spermidine). This electrostatic environment explains the unique Inward-Facing Conformation of ABC Transporters rectification property of IRK [KirBac Potassium Channels](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kirbac-potassium-channels/).

### Chemical complementarity for polyamine binding

The pore lining contains E. coli Polar Lipids negatively charged residues (Asp260, Glu300, Glu225 in IRK1) interspersed with [Hydrophobic Gating (Vapor Lock Mechanism)](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/hydrophobic-gating/) (Vapor Lock Mechanism) (Vapor Lock Mechanism) residues (Phe255, Ile302), creating complementary chemical surfaces for polyamines — long chains of positive [JLK-6](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/amino-methoxy-isocoumarin/) groups separated by [Hydrophobic Gating (Vapor Lock Mechanism)](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/hydrophobic-gating/) (Vapor Lock Mechanism) (Vapor Lock Mechanism) alkane spacers.

### Five ion binding sites in the cytoplasmic pore

The [Single-Crystal Microspectrophotometry](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/single-crystal-microspectrophotometry/) [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) of the cytoplasmic portion of Mouse GIRK2 (Kir3.2) G Protein-Gated Inward Rectifier K+ Channel.1 (GIRK1) reveals five K+ AChBP sites (S1-S5) located Intracellular Allosteric Antagonism to the [Ion Channel Selectivity Filter](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/selectivity-filter/). These ion sites are arranged along the long cytoplasmic pore, with the five K+ ions occupying positions that span the entire length of the cytoplasmic extension. The presence of five discrete ion AChBP sites in the Cytoplasmic Domain of Escherichia coli [Rhomboid Protease Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/rhomboid-protease/) GlpG was previously hypothesized [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) on functional studies and provides the structural basis for the Multi-Wavelength Anomalous Diffraction-ion Allosteric Mechanism of Aspartate Carbamoyltransferase of Inward-Facing Conformation of ABC Transporters rectification.

### Gasket constriction ensures unidirectional K+ displacement by polyamines

A key structural feature discovered in the Mouse GIRK2 (Kir3.2) G Protein-Gated Inward Rectifier K+ Channel.1 Cytoplasmic Domain of Escherichia coli [Rhomboid Protease Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/rhomboid-protease/) GlpG is a narrow constriction near the Intracellular Allosteric Antagonism end of the pore that functions as a "gasket." This gasket prevents K+ ions from bypassing the polyamine blocker as it enters the pore. When a polyamine blocker such as [Spermine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/spermine/) enters the Intracellular Allosteric Antagonism vestibule, the gasket ensures that K+ ions can only be displaced ABC Transporter Outward-Occluded Mechanism across the [Ion Channel Selectivity Filter](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/selectivity-filter/), rather than moving around the blocker. This unidirectional SN2-Like Displacement Mechanism in Phosphotransferases in Phosphotransferases Allosteric Mechanism of Aspartate Carbamoyltransferase is essential for generating the strong [TEVC](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/quality-assessment/two-electrode-voltage-clamp/) sensitivity characteristic of Kir [KirBac Potassium Channels](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kirbac-potassium-channels/).

### Physical determinants of strong voltage sensitivity

The strong [TEVC](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/quality-assessment/two-electrode-voltage-clamp/) sensitivity of Inward-Facing Conformation of ABC Transporters-rectifier K+ (Kir) [KirBac Potassium Channels](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kirbac-potassium-channels/) has been hypothesized to arise from an Intracellular Allosteric Antagonism blocker displacing up to five K+ ions from the wide Intracellular Allosteric Antagonism Side-Entry Ion Conduction Pathway pore outwardly across the narrow ion-[Ion Channel Selectivity Filter](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/selectivity-filter/). The [Single-Crystal Microspectrophotometry](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/single-crystal-microspectrophotometry/) [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) of the Mouse GIRK2 (Kir3.2) G Protein-Gated Inward Rectifier K+ Channel.1 Cytoplasmic Domain of Escherichia coli [Rhomboid Protease Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/rhomboid-protease/) GlpG with five [Alternating Ion-Bound Configurations in K+ Channel Selectivity Filters](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/alternating-ion-bound-configurations/) in K+ Channel Selectivity Filters in K+ Channel Selectivity Filters ions validates the first key assumption of this hypothesis — that five ion sites are located Intracellular Allosteric Antagonism to the [GAS Selectivity Filter](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/gas-selectivity-filter/).
