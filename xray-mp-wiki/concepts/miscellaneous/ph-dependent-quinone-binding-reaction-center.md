# pH-Dependent Quinone Binding in Photosynthetic Reaction Centers

## Overview
The secondary quinone (QB) in bacterial photosynthetic reaction centers occupies two distinct positions (distal and proximal) within its binding pocket, and the relative population of these positions depends on pH. High-resolution crystallography at 1.87 A combined with continuum electrostatics calculations revealed that pH modulates the equilibrium between QB positions. At higher pH, the proximal population decreases, correlated with the deprotonation of key residues (Glu L212 in the dark-adapted state; Asp L213 and the semiquinone in the illuminated state). This pH-dependent positioning of QB is coupled to proton uptake upon charge separation and represents a mechanism for regulating cofactor activity through pH.


## Mechanism/Details
In the dark-adapted state, the deprotonation of Glu L212 above pH 9 correlates with decreased proximal QB population. In the illuminated (charge-separated) state, deprotonation of Asp L213 and the semiquinone anion both correlate with decreased proximal occupancy. The QB movement between distal and proximal positions occurs via a gliding mechanism along backbone atoms (Ile L224 -> Gly L225 -> Thr L226), gradually increasing hydrogen bonds from one to three. This mechanism does not require the propeller twist (180-degree ring flip) proposed earlier.
