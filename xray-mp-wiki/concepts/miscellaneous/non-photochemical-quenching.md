# Non-photochemical Quenching (NPQ) in LHC-II

## Overview
Non-photochemical quenching (NPQ) is a photoprotective mechanism in plants that dissipates excess excitation energy as heat, preventing damage from high-light conditions. The major light-harvesting complex II ([LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/)) plays a central role in NPQ by providing structural pathways for energy dissipation. A structure-based NPQ model was proposed from the 2.72 A X-ray crystal structure of spinach [LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/), revealing how DGDG-mediated trimer aggregation, xanthophyll-cycle carotenoids, and specific chlorophyll pairs work together to quench excess excitation energy. The photosystem II protein [PsbS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/psbs-spinach/) is essential for qE-type NPQ, acting as a pH-sensitive trigger that senses lumenal acidification and activates quenching.


## Mechanism/Details
The structure-based NPQ model for [LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/) involves multiple components. Upon aggregation of [LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/) trimers mediated by [Digalactosyl Diacylglycerol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/digalactosyl-diacylglycerol/) (DGDG), efficient non-photochemical energy-transfer pathways are established so that excitation energy can escape from one trimer to adjacent trimers. The energy migrates to trapping sites where non-radiative dissipation occurs. Key trapping sites include: (1) the xanthophyll-cycle [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) at the monomer-monomer interface, which accepts singlet energy from at least three nearby chlorophylls; (2) the Chl a 613-Chl a 614 pair at the trimer-trimer interface, which has a centre-to-centre distance below 10 A and mutual orientation resembling an irregular distribution, consistent with a statistical pair energy trap; and (3) conformational changes triggered by protonation of lumen-exposed acidic residues (Glu 94, Asp 111, Glu 207, Asp 211) under low lumenal pH conditions, which move and reorient linker chlorophylls (Chl a 614 and Chl b 605) at the trimer-trimer interface to promote quenching. The xanthophyll cycle converts [Violaxanthin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/violaxanthin/) to zeaxanthin through antheraxanthin, with zeaxanthin acting as a direct quencher of excess excitation by accepting singlet energy from chlorophyll.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Spinach [LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/) (2.72 A structure): DGDG-mediated trimer aggregation establishes energy-transfer pathways; xanthophyll-cycle [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) and Chl a 613-Chl a 614 pair serve as quenching sites.
- Pea [LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/) (2.5 A structure): [Violaxanthin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/violaxanthin/) in its binding site can be replaced by zeaxanthin via the xanthophyll cycle; zeaxanthin accepts energy from nearby chlorophylls and dissipates it as heat.
- [LHC-II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/pea-light-harvesting-complex-ii/) in liposomes containing DGDG: NPQ observed upon incorporation, consistent with DGDG-mediated trimer aggregation model.
- [PsbS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/psbs-spinach/) (2.35 A structure): PsbS acts as a pH-sensitive trigger of qE-type NPQ. The low-pH crystal structure reveals a noncanonical LHC fold with four transmembrane helices. PsbS senses lumenal acidification via Glu69/Glu173 and activates NPQ through conformational changes while maintaining its dimeric state.
