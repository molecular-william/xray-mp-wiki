# QseE/F/G Three-Component System

## Overview
The QseE/F/G three-component system is a bacterial signal transduction system that regulates virulence gene expression in Enterobacteriaceae, including enterohemorrhagic Escherichia coli (EHEC), Citrobacter rodentium, Salmonella enterica, and Yersinia pseudotuberculosis. It consists of QseE (a histidine kinase sensor), QseF (a response regulator), and QseG (an outer membrane lipoprotein). Unlike classical two-component systems (TCS) composed of a histidine kinase and a response regulator, QseE/F/G incorporates the outer membrane lipoprotein QseG, which interacts with QseE in the periplasmic space and is required for full QseE/F activity. QseE senses environmental signals such as sulfate, phosphate, and host stress hormones (epinephrine, norepinephrine), leading to autophosphorylation and phosphotransfer to QseF. Phosphorylated QseF regulates expression of virulence genes including the locus of enterocyte effacement (LEE) pathogenicity island and effector proteins such as EspFu.

## Mechanism/Details
Signal transduction proceeds through three steps: (1) QseE, anchored in the inner membrane, senses periplasmic signals (sulfate, phosphate, host hormones) via its N-terminal periplasmic sensor domain (composed of a four-α-helix bundle with conserved arginine residues at the putative dimer interface). (2) Upon signal recognition, QseE autophosphorylates at a conserved histidine residue in its cytoplasmic kinase domain using ATP. (3) The phosphoryl group is transferred to an aspartate residue on QseF, which then regulates transcription of target genes including the sRNAs GlmY and GlmZ, controlling LEE expression and EspFu production. QseG, which is partially encoded by the same operon as QseE/F, localizes to the outer membrane via N-terminal lipidation and interacts with the periplasmic sensor domain of QseE through its negatively charged C-terminal coiled-coil region (α7c). This interaction is essential for full QseE activity, though the precise mechanism of QseG-mediated regulation remains to be determined.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- QseE (E. coli) — Histidine kinase sensor; periplasmic domain structure solved at 1.33 Å (P2₁2₁2₁). Four-α-helix bundle with conserved arginines at putative ligand-binding interface.
- QseG (E. coli) — Outer membrane lipoprotein; structure solved at 2.3-2.6 Å from three crystal forms. N-terminal globular domain + long C-terminal α7 coiled-coil involved in QseE interaction.
- QseE/F/G (Citrobacter rodentium) — ΔqseG mutants show attenuated disease in mice; qseG essential for full virulence.
- QseE/F/G (Salmonella enterica) — qseEF operon contributes to virulence regulation.
