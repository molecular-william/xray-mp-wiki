# Claudin-Mediated Paracellular Ion Selectivity

## Overview
Claudins are the major structural components of tight junctions (TJs) that control paracellular ion permeability in epithelial sheets. Different claudin isoforms create channels or barriers with distinct ion selectivity. A single conserved residue at position 65 (in beta-4 of ECS1) appears to be a key determinant: Asp65 in hCLDN-2 creates an anion barrier (cation-selective pore), while Lys65 in hCLDN-4 and hCLDN-9 creates a cation barrier (anion-selective pore). Residues at positions 31, 48, 53, and 65 align on the same plane of ECS1 to create a single-sided half-pore.


## Mechanism/Details
The extracellular domain of claudins forms a hand-like structure with a palm created by the beta-sheet. The residue at position 65 lies at the penultimate position of beta-4, below a conserved Cys54-Cys64 disulfide bond. Together with residues at positions 31, 48, and 53 on beta-1 to beta-4, these residues face the claudin palm and create a single-sided half-pore. The charge at position 65 (Asp vs Lys) is the primary determinant of whether a claudin functions as a cation barrier (anion permeable) or anion barrier (cation permeable).


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Human Claudin-9 (hCLDN-9) — hCLDN-9 has Lys65 and functions as a cation barrier (reduces Na+ and K+ permeability) and anion pore. It is essential for hearing in the inner ear. Mutations in claudin-9 cause deafness due to elevated K+ levels in the endolymphatic system.

- Human Claudin-4 (hCLDN-4) — hCLDN-4 has Lys65 and functions as a cation barrier (tightens TJs against Na+ permeation). Crystal structure confirms Lys65 position analogous to hCLDN-9.

- Human Claudin-2 (hCLDN-2) — hCLDN-2 has Asp65 and functions as an anion barrier (forms cation-selective pores). The negative charge at position 65 attracts cations and repels anions.

- Mouse Claudin-15 (mCldn15) — The mCldn15 structure (PDB 4P79) was the first claudin crystal structure, revealing the negatively charged palm region formed by Asp55 and Asp64 on the beta-sheet surface. Homology modeling based on this structure showed that cation-selective claudin-2 has a similarly negatively charged palm, while anion-selective claudin-10a has a positively charged surface, confirming the structural basis for charge selectivity.
