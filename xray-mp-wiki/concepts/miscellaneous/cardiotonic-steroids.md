# Cardiotonic Steroids

## Overview
Cardiotonic steroids (CTS), also known as cardiac glycosides, are a class of inhibitors that bind to the Na+,K+-ATPase and inhibit its functional activity. They are named for their positive effect on cardiac performance. [Digoxin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/digoxin/), extracted from the foxglove plant, has been used for treating heart failure since 1785. CTS bind with a stoichiometry of one molecule per one alpha subunit of the Na+,K+-ATPase. In addition to their pump inhibitory action, CTS initiate intracellular signaling when the pump serves as a receptor for endogenous CTS found in human blood at very low concentrations. Crystal structures of the E2·BeF3- complex (E2P ground state) free of CTS, together with eight structures of seven CTSs (ouabain, bufalin, digitoxin, digoxin, rostafuroxin, istaroxime, and anthroylouabain) in the E2P^Pi·Mg2+ state, demonstrate that CTSs bind to a preformed cavity in NKA without inducing large conformational changes — a conformational selection mechanism. The cavity in E2P^Pi·Mg2+ is wide open to the extracellular side for Na+ release and K+ access during the catalytic cycle. CTSs plug into this access channel, preventing K+ binding and closure of the ion pathway.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Na+,K+-ATPase (Pig Kidney) — [Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/), a cardiotonic steroid, binds to the E2P phosphoenzyme state of Na+,K+-ATPase with high affinity; structure determined at 4.6 A resolution (PDB 3N23); the E2P:[Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/) complex reveals a new conformational state of the pump
- Na+,K+-ATPase (Shark) — Low affinity [Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/) complex with [K2]E2:MgF4 2- state determined at higher resolution; comparison with E2P:[Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/) structure reveals conformational basis for high affinity binding
- Shark Na+,K+-ATPase — [Cryo-Electron Microscopy](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/cryo-em/) structures reveal that [Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/) (OBN) and [Istaroxime](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/istaroxime/) (IST) bind to E2P states via conformational selection; IST binds upside down relative to classical CTS with aminoalkyloxime group extending into cation site II
- Shark Na+,K+-ATPase — The new-generation CTS [Istaroxime](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/istaroxime/) has a flat steroid ring and an aminoalkyloxime group at C3; [Cryo-Electron Microscopy](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/cryo-em/) shows it penetrates deeper than [Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/) in E2P^ATP state
- [Na+,K+-ATPase (Pig Kidney)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/na-k-atpase-pig-kidney/) — Crystal structures of seven CTSs (ouabain, bufalin, digitoxin, digoxin, rostafuroxin, istaroxime, anthroylouabain) in complex with NKA in E2P^Pi·Mg2+ reveal conformational selection mechanism; CTSs bind to a preformed cavity (Kanai et al., PNAS 2020)
