# Periplasm (Periplasmic Space)

## Overview
The periplasm is the gel-like compartment between the inner and outer membranes of Gram-negative bacteria. It contains the peptidoglycan layer and various proteins involved in transport, folding, and signaling. In membrane protein crystallography, the periplasmic domains of inner membrane proteins and the soluble domains of outer membrane proteins are often the most well-ordered regions in crystal structures.
