# Surface Hydroxyl Acidity

## Overview
The acidity of individual surface hydroxyl groups determines the deprotonation/protonation state of solid surfaces, which has far-ranging implications for acid-base catalysis, electrocatalytic and photocatalytic water splitting, mineral behavior, and biochemistry. For solid surfaces, acidity is described by the proton affinity and acid dissociation constant (pKa) of individual hydroxyl sites, but experimental measurement of these values at individual surface sites has been a long-standing challenge.


## Mechanism/Details
Acidity of surface hydroxyl groups is fundamentally different from molecular
acidity because the local environment including neighboring surface atoms,
hydrogen bonding networks, and the underlying crystal structure all
contribute to the energetics of proton transfer.

For metal oxide surfaces such as In2O3(111), different types of hydroxyl
groups arise from different coordination environments:
- Terminal hydroxyls (OH-terminated) bound to a single metal center
- Bridging hydroxyls coordinated between two metal centers
- Three-coordinate hydroxyls at defect or step-edge sites
- Hydroxyls on different crystal facets

Traditional methods for estimating surface acidity include:
- Semi-empirical bond-order valence sums
- First-principles molecular dynamics simulations
- Point of zero charge measurements (which integrate over the entire surface)

Non-contact atomic force microscopy (nc-AFM) with CO-functionalized tips
has enabled the direct assessment of individual surface hydroxyl acidity.
By measuring the tip-sample interaction forces as a function of tip-sample
distance (force-distance spectroscopy), the protonation state of individual
hydroxyl groups can be determined. The technique can discriminate between
different types of surface hydroxyls on the same crystal facet and directly
measure their relative pKa values.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Silica surface hydroxyl groups — In2O3(111) serves as a model oxide with four different types of surface hydroxyl groups. Using nc-AFM with CO-functionalized tips, the pKa of each individual hydroxyl type was directly measured, providing the first experimental assessment of site-specific surface acidity at the single- site level.
