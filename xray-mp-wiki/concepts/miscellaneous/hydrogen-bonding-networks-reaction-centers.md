# Hydrogen-Bonding Networks in Photosynthetic Reaction Centers

## Overview
Hydrogen-bonding networks near the primary electron donor ([Bacteriochlorophyll](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/bacteriochlorophyll/) dimer P) in photosynthetic reaction centers play important roles in fine-tuning the redox potential of P and the thermal stability of the complex. In the Rhodobacter sphaeroides reaction center, extensive hydrogen-bonding networks exist at the interface of the L and M subunits, near the P_A and P_B bacteriochlorophylls. These networks involve conserved residues (Asn, Tyr, Asp, Ser) and structural water molecules, with patterns that differ between the two symmetry-related clefts on the P_A and P_B sides. The F(M197)H mutation introduces a new link between BChl P_B and this network through His M197, connecting the [Bacteriochlorophyll](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/bacteriochlorophyll/) to water C, which in turn hydrogen-bonds to Asn M195 and Asp L155. This is distinct from the P_A side where His L168 interacts directly with Asn L166 rather than through a water molecule.


## Mechanism/Details
On the P_B side (M197 cleft), the network includes: Asp L155 - Tyr M198, Asp L155 - water C - Asn M195, and water C - Ser L158 (via water E). With the F(M197)H mutation, His M197 donates a hydrogen bond to the P_B acetyl carbonyl (3.5 kcal/mol) and accepts a hydrogen bond from water C (3.1 A), integrating P_B into the network. On the P_A side (L168 cleft), the network is less extensive: His L168 donates a hydrogen bond to the P_A acetyl carbonyl (4.9 kcal/mol) and interacts with Asn L166. There is no water C equivalent within bonding distance to His L168 - the symmetry-related water D is 5.6 A away. These differences in the hydrogen-bonding networks contribute to the differential effects of mutations at these positions on the P/P+ redox potential.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Rba. sphaeroides Reaction Center — The F(M197)H mutation integrates BChl P_B into an extensive hydrogen-bonding network involving water C, Asn M195, Asp L155, Ser L158, and Tyr M198. The 2.10 A structure (PDB 7od5) at 100 K and 2.04 A multi-crystal structure at RT (PDB 7p2c) reveal these details.
- Thermochromatium tepidum Reaction Center — The high-resolution structure (PDB 5y5s) shows a different pattern of the hydrogen-bonding network near P_A, where conserved residues His, Tyr, and Asp interact directly without intervening water D.
