# Sodium-Motive Force (SMF)

## Overview
The sodium-motive force (smf) is an electrochemical gradient of Na+ ions
across a biological membrane, analogous to the proton-motive force (pmf).
It provides the free energy to drive membrane transport processes, including
ATP synthesis by Na+-dependent F-ATP synthases and Na+ translocation by
V-type ATPases. The smf consists of a chemical potential component (Delta pH
or Delta pNa) and an electrical potential component (Delta psi). In
anaerobic bacteria such as Ilyobacter tartaricus that inhabit Na+-rich
environments, the smf is the primary bioenergetic currency, with the ATP
synthase c-ring containing specific Na+ binding sites that couple Na+
translocation to ATP synthesis.
