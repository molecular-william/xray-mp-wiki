# Mycobacterium tuberculosis

## Overview
Mycobacterium tuberculosis is the pathogenic bacterium that causes
tuberculosis (TB), one of the leading infectious causes of death worldwide.
It is an acid-fast, aerobic bacillus with a characteristic cell wall rich in
mycolic acids, arabinogalactan, and peptidoglycan. M. tuberculosis is an
obligate human pathogen that primarily infects alveolar macrophages in the
lungs, where it can survive and replicate within the hostile intracellular
environment.


## Mechanism/Details
Membrane proteins from M. tuberculosis that have been structurally
characterized include the MFS transporter Rv1410 (P55), which exports
tetracycline and aminoglycosides and contributes to intrinsic drug
resistance. The respiratory complex components, including several
cytochrome oxidases and ATP synthase, are drug targets. The ATP synthase
inhibitor bedaquiline is a frontline drug for multidrug-resistant TB.

Mycobacterial membrane protein large (MmpL) family transporters are
involved in lipid transport and are essential for cell wall assembly.
MmpL3 transports trehalose monomycolate and is the target of several
anti-TB drug candidates. The mycobacterial cell division protein FtsZ,
ribosome, and RNA polymerase have also been structurally characterized.
