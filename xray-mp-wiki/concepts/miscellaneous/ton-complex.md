# Ton Complex

## Overview
The Ton complex is an energy-transducing protein assembly located in the inner membrane of Gram-negative bacteria. It consists of three integral polytopic membrane proteins — ExbB, ExbD, and TonB — and harnesses the proton motive force (PMF) at the inner membrane to transduce energy to TonB-dependent transporters (TBDTs) at the outer membrane. This energy transduction system enables the uptake of essential nutrients including metals, carbohydrates, iron-siderophore complexes, cobalamin, and bacteriocins across the outer membrane barrier.


## Mechanism/Details
The Ton complex operates through a coordinated mechanism linking the inner and outer membranes. ExbB forms a pentamer with a transmembrane pore and a large cytoplasmic domain featuring a strongly electropositive basic belt and an electronegative cap. ExbD assembles as a dimer, with one copy inserting its single transmembrane helix into the ExbB pentamer pore and the second copy located outside the ExbB pentamer. TonB associates with the ExbB-ExbD subcomplex, and its interaction with ExbD triggers conformational changes that lead to ligand uptake by TBDTs. The complex relies on the PMF for its function and has been proposed to act as a proton-conducting channel that shuttles protons from the periplasm to the cytoplasm. The Ton subcomplex forms pH-sensitive cation-selective channels with a conductance of 120 +/- 30 pS at neutral pH and sevenfold greater permeability for K+ than Cl-. At low pH (below 4.5), the channel closes, likely through movement of the ExbD transmembrane helix within the ExbB pore. The Ton complex is evolutionarily related to the Tol complex (TolQ, TolR, TolA) and the Mot complex (bacterial flagellar motor), suggesting a common energy transduction mechanism across different biological systems.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- E. coli ExbB — ExbB forms the pentameric core of the Ton complex. First crystal structure solved at 2.6 A resolution (PDB 5FQ7) and 3.5 A at pH 4.5 (PDB 5FQ6), revealing the transmembrane pore, cytoplasmic cavity with basic belt and electronegative cap, and ion channel properties.
- E. coli ExbD — ExbD assembles as a dimer within the Ton complex. One copy inserts its TMH into the ExbB pentamer pore; the second copy is located outside. pH-dependent movement of the ExbD TMH modulates channel conductance.
- E. coli TonB — TonB associates with the ExbB-ExbD subcomplex. Its periplasmic domain interacts with TBDTs at the outer membrane to deliver energy for ligand transport.
- TolQ-TolR-TolA complex — Evolutionarily related Tol complex required for cell envelope integrity and cell division. Shares functional analogy with the Ton complex — both use the PMF to transduce energy across membranes.
- Mot complex — Evolutionarily related to the Ton complex; drives bacterial flagellar rotation using the PMF. Suggests a conserved mechanism for PMF-driven energy transduction in bacteria.
