# Quartet Core Mechanism in S1PR3

## Overview
The quartet core is a set of four residues in the sphingosine-1-phosphate receptor 3 (S1PR3) — Leu122(3.36), Trp256(6.48), Phe204(5.47), and Phe260(6.52) — that undergo orchestrating rotamer changes upon agonist binding to induce the active conformation of the receptor. These four residues are located in the lower part of the ligand-binding pocket, in proximity to the alkyl tail of sphingosine-1-phosphate (S1P). The quartet core mechanism explains how S1P analogs of varying lengths can activate S1PRs and how ligand length determines G protein-subtype bias. Short lipidic agonists (e.g., FTY720-P, d16:1 S1P) lose the ability to interact with Leu122(3.36), resulting in G12/13- and Gi/o-biased activation rather than Gq/11.

## Mechanism/Details
The quartet core was identified by comparing the active d18:1 S1P-bound S1PR3 structure (PDB 7C4S) with the inactive antagonist (ML056)-bound S1PR1 structure (PDB 3V2Y). The four residues are:
- **Leu122(3.36)**: Flips its side chain in the active state. Alanine substitution specifically reduces Gq/11 signaling while maintaining G12/13 signaling efficacy, making it a key determinant of G protein-subtype bias.
- **Trp256(6.48)**: Flipped side chain in the active conformation. Part of the conserved P-I-F motif in class A GPCRs. Mutations reduce both Gq/11 and G12/13 signaling.
- **Phe204(5.47)**: In the inactive state, this residue plugs the gap between TM4 and TM5. In the active state, it forms a π-π interaction with Phe260(6.52), opening a gap for the alkyl tail of S1P.
- **Phe260(6.52)**: Forms π-π interaction with Phe204(5.47) in the active state. Mutations reduce both Gq/11 and G12/13 signaling.
During activation, TM6 is displaced outward by approximately 11 Å and TM7 shifts inward by approximately 2.8 Å. The rearrangement of the quartet core is associated with conserved activation motifs (P-I-F, sodium-binding site, D/E-R-Y, NPxxY) common to class A GPCRs.
