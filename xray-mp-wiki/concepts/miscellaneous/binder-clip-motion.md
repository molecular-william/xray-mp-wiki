# Binder Clip Motion

## Overview
Binder clip-like motion is a conformational mechanism in [SemiSWEET Transporter Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/semisweet/) and related SWEET/PQ-loop transporters where the PQ-loop motif serves as a flexible hinge, enabling intramolecular conformational changes that alternate substrate access between the intracellular and extracellular sides of the membrane.


## Mechanism/Details
The binder clip-like motion is a conformational mechanism by which [SemiSWEET Transporter Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/semisweet/) and related SWEET/PQ-loop family transporters alternate between inward-open and outward-open states. Each protomer undergoes intramolecular conformational changes (not rigid-body movement of the entire protomer). The conserved PQ-loop motif (Pro21, Gln22) on TM1 serves as a flexible molecular hinge, with the proline residue enabling a sharp bend in TM1. Structural units consisting of TM1b, TM2, TM3, and TM1a' from the adjacent protomer rotate relative to each other, much like the two arms of a binder clip opening and closing. This motion is coupled with gating: the extracellular gate (Tyr53, Arg57, Asp59) and intracellular gate (Phe19, Met39, Tyr40, Phe43) open and close in alternation to prevent simultaneous exposure of the substrate-binding pocket to either side of the membrane. The mechanism allows transport of small amphipathic molecules while preventing ion leakage. Mutagenesis of the proline residue (P21A) abolishes transport activity, confirming its essential role as the hinge.
