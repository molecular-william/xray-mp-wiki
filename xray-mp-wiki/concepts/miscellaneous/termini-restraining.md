# Termini Restraining

## Overview
Termini restraining is a protein engineering strategy for stabilizing small membrane proteins to enable their structural determination. The approach involves restraining the flexible amino and carboxyl termini of a membrane protein by fusing them to two associable protein entities, exemplified by the two halves of a superfolder GFP (sfGFP). When the two halves of sfGFP are brought together by the restrained termini, they reassemble into a functional fluorophore, providing a versatile scaffold for crystallization, phase determination, and density modification. The termini-restrained proteins remain functional and show improved stability during overexpression and purification. This strategy enabled the determination of 14 new structures of five vertebrate proteins from distinct functional families (VKOR, VKORL, SPCS1, JAGN1, CD53) and a high-resolution structure of bacterial DsbB, bringing substantial expansion to the structural database of small membrane proteins.

## Mechanism/Details
The termini-restraining approach addresses fundamental challenges in membrane protein structural biology:
1. **Stabilization during overexpression**: The reassembled sfGFP prevents aggregation by stabilizing the folded state of the membrane protein in cells. The DDM-extractable fraction of restrained proteins is substantially higher than unrestrained ones.
2. **Improved stability during purification**: Restrained proteins show higher melting temperatures (Tm increases of 2.9-40°C observed). For example, DsbB Tm increased from 50°C to over 90°C.
3. **Crystallization scaffold**: The large polar surface of sfGFP provides a highly adaptable crystal packing surface. Crystals form alternating layers of sfGFP and small membrane protein molecules.
4. **Phase determination**: The sfGFP model (PDB 2B3P) readily generates molecular replacement solutions. For low-resolution data, cross-crystal averaging with sfGFP alone can improve electron density maps.
5. **Termini shortening**: Shortening the flexible linkers between the membrane protein and sfGFP can improve crystal diffraction.
The approach was applied to seven model proteins (10-40 kDa, 2-6 TMs): SPCS1, VKOR, VKORL, DsbB, JAGN1, CD53, Cldn4, and Mfrn1.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [SPCS1 (Chicken)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/spcs1/) — First crystal structure of a 2-TM membrane protein; PDB 6WVE
- [Human VKOR](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/hsvkor/) — Multiple structures in complex with warfarin and other anticoagulants; PDBs 6WV3, 6WV6, 6WVH, 6WV4, 6WV5, 6WV7
- [TrVKORL](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/trvkorl/) — Pufferfish VKORL structures in ligand-free and warfarin-bound states; PDBs 6WVI, 6WVB, 6WV8, 6WV9, 6WVA
- [DsbB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/dsbB/) — High-resolution structure (2.9 Å) revealing catalytic triad mechanism; PDB 6WVF
- [JAGN1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/jagn1/) — Human JAGN1 structure revealing fold for vesicle transport; PDB 6WVD
- [Human CD53](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/cd53/) — First structure of tetraspanin partner-interacting state; PDB 6WVG
