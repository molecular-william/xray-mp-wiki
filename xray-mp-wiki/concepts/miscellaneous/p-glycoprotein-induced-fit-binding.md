# P-glycoprotein Induced-Fit Binding

## Overview
P-glycoprotein (P-gp) employs an induced-fit mechanism for substrate
recognition, where ligand binding in the transmembrane domains (TMDs)
can trigger conformational changes in transmembrane helix 4 (TM4),
coupling drug binding to ATP hydrolysis at the nucleotide-binding
domains (NBDs). This concept explains how P-gp achieves both high
polyspecificity and signal transduction.


## Mechanism/Details
**Induced-fit kinking of TM4:** Cocrystal structures of mouse P-gp with a series of homotrimeric cyclopeptide ligands revealed that binding of smaller, more stimulatory ligands (QZ-Ala, QZ-Val — "subset A") induces a large conformational change in TM4, kinking it by up to 11 A at C-alpha positions compared to the straight-helical conformation in apo P-gp. The kink begins at Pro219 and returns to wild-type topology at Tyr243 near the ball-and-socket region close to NBD2.

**Coupling to ATP hydrolysis:** The TM4 kink brings residues 221-228 closer to bound ligands in the lower binding sites, fostering interaction with Trp228 — a residue implicated in steroid binding. This movement is proposed to transmit a signal from the TMDs to the NBDs, accelerating ATP hydrolysis. Subset A ligands that induce TM4 kinking are potent ATPase stimulators (QZ-Ala: ~16-fold), while subset B ligands (QZ-Leu, QZ-Phe) that do not induce kinking are weak stimulators or inhibitors.

**Ligand entry via the elbow helix:** A new ligand-binding site on the surface of P-gp facing the inner leaflet (the EH2 elbow helix, residues 689-694) provides insights into how hydrophobic drugs and lipids enter P-gp. This portal allows substrates from the inner membrane leaflet to access the main drug-binding cavity.

**Multiple binding subsites:** The P-gp binding cavity contains at least four overlapping binding sites. The four cyclopeptide ligands (QZ-Ala, Val, Leu, Phe) group into two subsets based on binding mode:
- Subset A (smaller ligands): upper and lower binding sites; induce TM4 kinking
- Subset B (larger ligands): different upper site; QZ-Phe also has a unique lower site; no TM4 kinking

This structural plasticity explains how P-gp can transport thousands of structurally diverse compounds.
