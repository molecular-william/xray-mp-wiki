# GPCR Crystallization

## Overview
GPCR crystallization encompasses the methods and strategies developed to
obtain diffraction-quality crystals of G protein-coupled receptors for
X-ray crystallography. GPCRs are inherently challenging crystallization
targets due to their conformational flexibility, hydrophobic nature, and
relatively small hydrophilic surface areas. Breakthroughs in GPCR structural
biology were achieved through protein engineering strategies including
receptor stabilization by alanine scanning (StaR technology), fusion protein
insertion (T4 lysozyme, BRIL), C-terminally truncated constructs, and the
use of lipidic cubic phase (LCP) crystallization media.


## Mechanism/Details
The first GPCR structure was bovine rhodopsin (PDB 1F88, 2.8 Å, 2000). The
first ligand-activated GPCR structure was the beta2-adrenergic receptor
(PDB 2RH1, 2.4 Å, 2007) using T4L fusion and LCP crystallization. Since
then, over 100 unique GPCR structures have been determined, covering
classes A, B, C, D, E, and F. More recently, cryo-EM has increasingly
complemented X-ray crystallography for GPCR-G protein complex structures,
which were refractory to crystallization.
