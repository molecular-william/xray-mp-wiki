# Flippase

## Overview
Flippases are membrane transporters that catalyze the translocation (flipping)
of lipid molecules and other amphipathic substrates across the lipid bilayer.
In the context of membrane structural biology, flippases of the Major
Facilitator Superfamily (MFS) include LtaA from S. aureus, an MFS transporter
that catalyzes proton-coupled flipping of the lipid-linked disaccharide
anchor-LLD across the membrane, essential for lipoteichoic acid biogenesis.
Flippases overcome the high energy barrier for polar headgroup translocation
through the hydrophobic membrane interior by providing a hydrophilic pathway.


## Mechanism/Details
Flippases are classified into several families: MFS flippases (LtaA),
ATP-binding cassette (ABC) flippases (MsbA for lipid A), P4-ATPase flippases
(phospholipid translocators), and DedA family flippases. The structural
diversity of flippase folds reflects the diversity of lipid substrates they
transport.

LtaA remains the only MFS flippase with a solved X-ray structure. Its
characterization has provided a paradigm for understanding how MFS
transporters can be adapted for lipid flipping, expanding the functional
scope of the superfamily beyond small molecule transport.
