# Sodium Channel Ion Permeability and Selectivity

## Overview
Voltage-gated sodium channels (Nav) achieve high selectivity for Na+ over other monovalent cations through a conserved selectivity filter (SF) motif (LESWSM or similar). Crystal structures of the [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navms/) channel reveal three sodium ions bound in the SF, coordinated by backbone carbonyls of the conserved residues. The ion binding sites are characterized by different electrostatic properties: the high-field-strength site (HFS) at the extracellular end involves the conserved glutamate side chain, while the low-field-strength sites (LFS) deeper in the SF are formed by backbone carbonyls. Removal of the HFS site through E178D mutation blocks ion conductance. Multi-ion conduction occurs through a 'knock-on' mechanism where electrostatic repulsion between adjacent bound cations facilitates ion passage. The [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navms/) SF closely resembles the calcium channel SF of CavAb, suggesting evolutionary conservation of the ion selectivity mechanism.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navms/) voltage-gated sodium channel — Three Na+ ions bind in the SF of wildtype [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navms/). The E178D mutant removes the top binding site (Na1) and blocks conductance, demonstrating the critical role of the high-field-strength site.
- CavAb voltage-gated calcium channel — The [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navms/) SF superposes closely with the CavAb SF, with 3 Na+ in [NAVMS](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navms/) at positions analogous to 3 Ca2+ in CavAb, revealing conserved principles of cation selectivity in voltage-gated channels.
