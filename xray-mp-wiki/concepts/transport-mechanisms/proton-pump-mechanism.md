# Proton Pump Mechanism in Bacteriorhodopsin

## Overview
The proton pump mechanism of bacteriorhodopsin is a light-driven ion translocation process energized by photoisomerization of all-trans retinal to 13-cis. The 1.55 Å structure revealed a continuous hydrogen-bonded chain on the extracellular side and a discontinuous chain on the cytoplasmic side, providing structural basis for vectorial proton transport. Subsequent work combining X-ray crystallography (PDB 4FPD, 2.65 Å) with extensive molecular dynamics simulations demonstrated that deprotonation of D96 serves a dual role as internal proton donor and as a conformational latch controlling opening of the cytoplasmic proton uptake pathway.

## Mechanism/Details
Light absorption triggers isomerization of all-trans retinal to 13-cis, which drives proton transfer from the Schiff base to Asp85. This initiates a cascade through an extensive hydrogen-bonded network of residues and water molecules to the extracellular surface. Reprotonation of the Schiff base occurs from Asp96 on the cytoplasmic side, with the pi-bulge of helix G and associated water molecules mediating proton conduction. A barrier to proton backflow in the unphotolyzed state is maintained by the discontinuous hydrogen-bonded network on the cytoplasmic side.

A key mechanistic insight from later studies is that D96 plays a dual role: it serves as the internal proton donor to the Schiff base, and its deprotonation subsequently unlatches the cytoplasmic half-channel. When D96 is protonated, the channel remains closed; when deprotonated, a continuous water channel forms connecting the cytoplasm to the Schiff base. This protonation-switch mechanism functions as a latch that opens hydration channels in all photointermediate states, controlling hydration-dependent reprotonation of D96 from the bulk.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Bacteriorhodopsin — Prototypical light-driven proton pump, the best understood ion pump, structural prototype for GPCRs. X-ray structure 4FPD of the D96G/F171C/F219L triple mutant combined with 60 MD simulations revealed the D96 protonation-state latch mechanism.
