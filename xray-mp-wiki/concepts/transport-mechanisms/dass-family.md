# Divalent Anion/Na+ Symporter (DASS) Family

## Overview
The Divalent Anion/Na+ Symporter (DASS) family (TC 2.A.47) is a large family
of secondary active transporters that couple the inward sodium gradient to
the uptake of divalent and trivalent anions. The family includes the
mammalian SLC13 transporters (NaCT/SLC13A5, NaDC1/SLC13A2, NaDC3/SLC13A3,
NaS1/SLC13A1, NaS2/SLC13A4), the Drosophila INDY (I'm Not Dead Yet)
protein, and numerous bacterial homologues. DASS transporters are involved
in citrate, succinate, dicarboxylate, and sulfate transport across plasma
membranes and play critical roles in cellular metabolism, energy
homeostasis, and fat storage regulation.


## Mechanism/Details
DASS family transporters operate through a Na+-coupled symport mechanism.
The structure of VcINDY from Vibrio cholerae (3.2 A resolution) revealed
that each protomer contains 11 transmembrane helices arranged as an
inverted two-fold repeat, with the N- and C-terminal halves forming
hand-shaped domains. Substrate binding involves conserved Ser-Asn-Thr
(SNT) motifs that coordinate the carboxylate groups of the transported
anion. Sodium ions bind to hairpin tip-capping loop motifs in each half
of the protein, and the close proximity of the Na+ and substrate sites
enables ion coupling through an induced-fit mechanism: Na+ binding creates
an optimal substrate-binding site, followed by substrate binding and
subsequent conformational changes that release substrates to the cytosol.

The DASS family belongs to the Ion Transporter (IT) superfamily, which
comprises 16 transporter families and over 32,000 members. Bacterial DASS
proteins typically co-transport two Na+ ions per substrate molecule, while
mammalian SLC13 proteins transport three or four Na+ ions per cycle.
Substrate specificity is primarily determined by the interaction of the
1,5-carboxyl groups of the substrate with the two SNT motifs, explaining
why citrate (tricarboxylate) is transported by NaCT while succinate and
malate (dicarboxylates) are preferred by NaDC1 and NaDC3.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [VcINDY](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/vcindy/) — The bacterial INDY homologue from Vibrio cholerae, whose 3.2 A crystal structure revealed the inverted two-fold symmetry architecture, SNT substrate-binding motifs, and Na+-coupling mechanism of the DASS family.
- [Drosophila INDY](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/drosophila-orai/) — The fly homologue of SLC13 transporters. Mutations in INDY (I'm Not Dead Yet) result in reduced fat storage through calorie restriction, linking DASS transporter function to metabolic regulation and lifespan.
