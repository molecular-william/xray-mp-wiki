# Type II ABC Transporter Family

## Overview
Type II ABC transporters are a distinct subclass of bacterial ABC importers that transport substrates such as haem and vitamin B12. They consist of a TMD dimer and NBD dimer without an associated outer membrane protein, distinguishing them from type I importers.

## Mechanism/Details
Type II ABC transporters are a distinct subclass of bacterial ATP-binding cassette (ABC) importers that transport substrates such as haem and vitamin B12 across the inner membrane. Unlike type I importers (e.g., the maltose transporter MalFGK2), type II importers consist of a transmembrane domain (TMD) dimer and a nucleotide-binding domain (NBD) dimer without an associated outer membrane protein. Key members include the haem importers HmuUV from Yersinia pestis and BhuUV from Burkholderia cenocepacia, as well as the vitamin B12 transporter BtuCD-F from Escherichia coli. The BhuUV-T structure revealed an inward-facing conformation in the nucleotide-free form, providing the missing piece in the transport cycle of type II ABC importers.
