# Conformational Asymmetry in ABC Transporters

## Overview
Conformational asymmetry in ABC transporters refers to the observation that the two transmembrane domain (TMD) subunits of an ABC transporter can adopt different conformations within a single complex, rather than moving synchronously. This phenomenon was first visualized at high resolution in the crystal structure of the Escherichia coli BtuCD-BtuF complex.


## Mechanism/Details
In the canonical ABC transport cycle, the two TMD subunits alternate between outward-facing and inward-facing conformations in concert with ATP binding and hydrolysis at the nucleotide-binding domains (NBDs). However, the BtuCD-BtuF complex structure revealed an intermediate state where one BtuC subunit adopts an outward-facing-like conformation (similar to the apo BtuCD structure) while the other adopts an inward-facing-like conformation (similar to the HI1471 structure). The asymmetry is most pronounced in helices TM3, TM4, TM5, and 5a — a helical subset that controls which side of the membrane the translocation pathway is exposed to.

The functional consequence of this asymmetry is an occluded conformation where the central cavity is accessible to neither side of the membrane and is too small to harbor a B12 molecule. This suggests that during the conversion between inward- and outward-facing conformations, the two TMD subunits may not alter their conformations simultaneously. Instead, they may move sequentially, with one subunit leading and the other following, producing transient asymmetric states.

CW EPR spectroscopy confirmed that this asymmetry is not a crystallographic artifact. Spin labels introduced at equivalent positions in the two BtuC subunits showed distinct spectral components — one mobile and one immobile — consistent with one label at the periphery and the other at the center of the complex in an asymmetric arrangement.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [BtuCD-BtuF Complex](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/btucd-btuf-complex/) — First high-resolution visualization of asymmetric TMD conformations in an ABC transporter. The BtuCD-F complex captured an occluded intermediate with one BtuC subunit in an outward-facing-like and one in an inward-facing-like conformation.
- [Sav1866](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/sav1866/) — Homodimeric ABC exporter whose ATP-bound structure shows symmetric outward-facing conformation, in contrast to the asymmetric BtuCD-F state.
- [HI1470/1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/hi1470-1/) — Putative metal-chelate ABC transporter whose inward-facing structure provided the comparison for the asymmetric BtuCD-F complex.
