# POT Family (Proton-Dependent Oligopeptide Transporters)

## Overview
The POT (proton-dependent oligopeptide transporter) family is a subgroup of the Major Facilitator Superfamily. Members transport di- and tripeptides using a proton gradient. They typically have 12 transmembrane helices and use a scissor-switch gating mechanism. The binding pockets are rich in aromatic residues enabling multispecific recognition. Adversarial ligands such as HEPES can bind in the POT substrate cavity, mimicking peptide binding. The first substrate-bound POT structure was solved for PepT_So2 with alafosfalin, revealing conserved N- and C-terminus coordination by Arg25, Glu402, Asn151, Asn329, and Tyr29. Proton coupling involves distinct mechanisms across family members, with ordered water networks facilitating proton movement via Grotthuss shuttling.

## Mechanism/Details
The POT (proton-dependent oligopeptide transporter) family is a subgroup of the Major Facilitator Superfamily. Members transport di- and tripeptides using a proton gradient. They typically have 12 transmembrane helices and use a scissor-switch gating mechanism. The binding pockets are rich in aromatic residues enabling multispecific recognition. Adversarial ligands such as HEPES can bind in the POT substrate cavity, mimicking peptide binding. The first substrate-bound POT structure was solved for PepT_So2 with alafosfalin, revealing conserved N- and C-terminus coordination by Arg25, Glu402, Asn151, Asn329, and Tyr29. Proton coupling involves distinct mechanisms: non-histidine-containing members (PepT_St, GkPOT) use an Arg-Glu extracellular gate salt bridge, while histidine-containing members (PepT_So, PepT_Xc, mammalian PepT1/2) use a His-Asp-Asn triad. A 2.1 A crystal structure of PepT_Xc revealed ordered water networks connecting Asp322 and Glu425. QM/MM simulations demonstrated proton transport through these waters via Grotthuss shuttling (free energy barrier 6.8 kcal/mol, rate 177 microseconds^-1). Protonation of the TM2 histidine (His67 in PepT_Xc) facilitates reorientation from inward-open to outward-facing state. Different POT members use different numbers of protons per peptide (PepT_St: 5-6 for dipeptides, 3 for tripeptides; PepT_So: same number regardless of peptide size).
