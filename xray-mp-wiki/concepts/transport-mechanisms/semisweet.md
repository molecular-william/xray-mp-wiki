# SemiSWEET Transporter Family

## Overview
SemiSWEETs are bacterial homologues of the eukaryotic SWEET (Sugars Will Eventually be Exported Transporter) family. They are among the smallest membrane transporters, consisting of only three transmembrane helices per monomer that fold into a single three-helix bundle. SemiSWEETs function as dimers and transport mono- or disaccharides across membranes.


## Mechanism/Details
SemiSWEETs comprise a single three-helix bundle (TM1, TM2, TM3), in contrast to the seven transmembrane helices of eukaryotic SWEETs, which consist of two parallel three-helix bundles connected by one central TM helix. The bacterial SemiSWEET monomer is reminiscent of just one of the two three-helix bundles found in eukaryotic SWEETs. SemiSWEETs operate as symmetric dimers, with the dimeric interface involving hydrogen bonds between the two protomers. The central pocket between the two protomers serves as the substrate binding site, and its size varies between SemiSWEET variants depending on a single residue (e.g., Met47 in sucrose-transporting TySemiSWEET versus Phe41 in glucose-transporting LbSemiSWEET). SemiSWEETs undergo conformational transitions between outward-open, occluded, and inward-open states following the alternating-access mechanism. The structural organization of the 3-helix bundle in SemiSWEETs differs from that of the major facilitator superfamily (MFS) transporters, with TM3 positioned on the opposite side relative to the MFS 3-helix bundle.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- TySemiSWEET from Thermotoga yellowstonii: Crystal structure solved at 2.4 A (PDB 4RNG).
  The elongated central pocket accommodates a sucrose molecule, consistent with its
  function as a sucrose transporter.
- LbSemiSWEET from Leptospira biflexa: Crystal structure solved at 1.8 A. The smaller
  central pocket is consistent with its function as a glucose transporter.
- BjSemiSWEET1 from Bradyrhizobium japonicum: Functional characterization demonstrated
  sucrose transport activity. Shares 44.2% sequence identity with TySemiSWEET.
