# Virtual Proton Pump

## Overview
A virtual proton pump is a transport mechanism in which the free energy of substrate decarboxylation is coupled to proton extrusion from the cell, without a direct proton symport or antiport step. In the Escherichia coli extreme acid resistance system, the arginine-dependent virtual proton pump consists of two proteins: AdiC, an arginine/agmatine antiporter in the inner membrane, and AdiA, a cytoplasmic arginine decarboxylase. AdiC imports L-arginine from the extracellular environment and exports agmatine. AdiA decarboxylates arginine to agmatine, consuming a cytoplasmic proton in the process. The net result is extrusion of a proton equivalent per arginine molecule transported, allowing the cell to maintain cytoplasmic pH above 5 during exposure to gastric conditions at pH 1.5-4. AdiC specifically imports the deprotonated-carboxylate form of arginine (Arg+), which represents only a minor fraction below pH 2. This ensures that transport of the predominant Arg2+ form would produce a futile cycle.
