# Glycine-Gating Hinge

## Overview
The glycine-gating hinge is a conserved [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) residue located within the S6 inner helix of potassium channels that serves as a structural pivot point for pore opening and closing. Bending of the inner helix at this [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) residue allows the activation gate to open like the aperture of a camera, enabling ion conduction through the channel. This mechanism was first identified through comparison of the closed [KCSA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kcsa/) structure with the opened [MTHK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/mthk/) structure and subsequently observed in the [KVAP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kvap/) voltage-dependent potassium channel.


## Mechanism/Details
The glycine-gating hinge is located at a specific position in the S6 inner helix of potassium channels, downstream (intracellular side) of the selectivity filter. [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) residues are uniquely suited to serve as hinge points because their small side chain (a single hydrogen atom) imposes minimal steric constraints on backbone phi/psi angles, allowing the helix to bend without disrupting the overall helical structure.
Key features:
- Conservation: The [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) is conserved at a specific location in the inner helix of
  many K+ channels, including [KVAP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kvap/), [KCSA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kcsa/), and [MTHK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/mthk/), indicating its functional importance.

- Gate opening: Bending at the [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) hinge position allows the S6 inner helices from
  each subunit to splay outward, opening the activation gate at the intracellular
  entrance of the pore. The movement is likened to the aperture of a camera iris.

- Coupled movement: The S6 inner helix and the adjacent S5 outer helix maintain an
  approximately antiparallel relationship and appear to move together as a single unit.
  This suggests that forces applied to S5 (e.g., from voltage-sensor movement in Kv
  channels) are transmitted directly to S6 through this coupled arrangement.

- Gating states: The glycine-gating hinge enables transitions between closed and open
  conformations. In [KCSA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kcsa/) (closed state), the hinge is not bent. In [MTHK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/mthk/) (open state),
  significant bending occurs at the [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/). In [KVAP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kvap/), the hinge is bent, suggesting an
  open pore conformation.
