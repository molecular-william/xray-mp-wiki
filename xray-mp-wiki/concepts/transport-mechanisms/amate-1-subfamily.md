# aMATE-1 Subfamily — Archaeal MATE Transporters

## Overview
The aMATE-1 (archaeal MATE-1) subfamily is a newly identified clade of the multidrug and toxic compound extrusion (MATE) transporter family. Discovered through comprehensive phylogenetic analysis of 386 MATE proteins, aMATE-1 represents one of six major MATE clades alongside eMATE-1, eMATE-2, NorM, DinF, and aMATE-2. The aMATE-1 subfamily comprises transporters from epsilon-Proteobacteria and Archaea. Its prototype is [Aq_128 (Aquifex aeolicus MATE Family Multidrug Resistance Transporter) — aMATE-1 Subfamily Prototype](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/aq_128/) from the hyperthermophilic bacterium Aquifex aeolicus.


## Mechanism/Details
aMATE-1 transporters are distinguished from other MATE subfamilies by several key features:

- **Absence of conserved acidic residues**: Unlike NorM and DinF subfamily transporters, aMATE-1 members lack several strictly conserved and functionally essential acidic residues. For example, the conserved aspartate D41 in [PFMATE](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/pfmate/) (DinF subfamily) that is critical for H⁺ coupling is replaced by a serine (S40) in [Aq_128 (Aquifex aeolicus MATE Family Multidrug Resistance Transporter) — aMATE-1 Subfamily Prototype](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/aq_128/). Similarly, N180 and D184 of [PFMATE](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/pfmate/) are absent.

- **Na⁺-dependent transport**: Functional characterization of [Aq_128 (Aquifex aeolicus MATE Family Multidrug Resistance Transporter) — aMATE-1 Subfamily Prototype](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/aq_128/) demonstrated Na⁺-dependent rather than H⁺-dependent drug efflux. The Na⁺ binding site is proposed to be located in the N bundle, involving a cation-π interaction with residue Y154.

- **Distinct hydrogen-bonding network**: [Aq_128 (Aquifex aeolicus MATE Family Multidrug Resistance Transporter) — aMATE-1 Subfamily Prototype](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/aq_128/) contains a pronounced hydrogen-bonding network in the N bundle formed by E36, Y136, H177, and bridging water molecules — distinct from the network in DinF transporters.

- **pH-independent TM1 conformation**: TM1 in [Aq_128 (Aquifex aeolicus MATE Family Multidrug Resistance Transporter) — aMATE-1 Subfamily Prototype](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/aq_128/) remains straight across pH 5–8, in contrast to the pH-dependent bent-to-straight transition proposed for DinF transporters. This suggests a fundamentally different transport mechanism.

- **N bundle substrate binding**: Substrate binding occurs in the N bundle cavity, spatially similar to DinF subfamily transporters but with different residue composition. Key residues include W63 (gating residue), Y33, Y66, Y154, E207, E249, and R250.

The aMATE-1 subfamily, together with the related aMATE-2 clade, expands the known diversity of MATE transporters and provides new insights into the functional evolution of this important transporter family.
