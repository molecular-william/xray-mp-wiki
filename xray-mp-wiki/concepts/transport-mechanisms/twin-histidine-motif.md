# Twin-Histidine Motif in Ammonium Channels

## Overview
The twin-histidine motif is a pair of conserved histidine residues that form a hydrogen-bonded pair within the channel pore of Amt/Mep/Rh ammonium transporters. The twin-His motif is essential for optimum substrate conductance in these channels, serving as a proton relay system during NH3/H+ symport or facilitating NH3 passage. In fungal Mep2 proteins, one of the twin-His residues (His342/348) also participates in channel gating by forming a hydrogen bond with a conserved tyrosine (Tyr49/53) from TM1 to close the channel in the non-phosphorylated state.

## Mechanism/Details
The twin-His motif consists of two absolutely conserved histidine residues that
line the hydrophobic channel of Amt/Mep/Rh ammonium transporters. In E. coli AmtB,
His-168 and His-318 are located near the center of the ~20 A hydrophobic channel.
Mutagenesis to 14 different polar and non-polar residues demonstrated both histidines
are absolutely required for optimum methylammonium transport activity. In fungal
Mep2 proteins, the twin-His residues are His342 and His349 (CaMep2 numbering).
The epsilon-2 nitrogen of His342 forms a hydrogen bond with the hydroxyl group of
a conserved Tyr49 from TM1 in the closed, non-phosphorylated state. This Tyr-His
hydrogen bond is disrupted upon phosphorylation, contributing to channel opening.
In the archaeal Amt-1, the equivalent His pairs are present but the Tyr side chain
is rotated away, leaving the channel open. Some fungal Amt proteins have a natural
Glu substitution for one His (Glu-His pair), which retains partial activity.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Ammonium Transporter AmtB (E. coli)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/amt-b/) — Twin-His (H168, H318) extensively characterized; essential for NH3 conductance. Crystal structures of H168A, H168E, H168F, H318A, H318F, H168A/H318A variants show no structural perturbation but loss of activity.
- [Mep2 Ammonium Transceptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/mep2-ammonium-transceptor/) — Twin-His (H342, H349 in CaMep2) with additional gating role: His342 forms hydrogen bond with Tyr49 from TM1 to close the channel in the non-phosphorylated state. Tyr-His hydrogen bond is disrupted by phosphorylation.
- [Human Rhesus C Glycoprotein (RhCG)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/rhcg/) — Twin-His motif present and essential for NH3 transport. Structure shows the His pair positioned in the channel pore.
