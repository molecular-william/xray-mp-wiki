# Functionally Rotating Mechanism in Multidrug Transporters

## Overview
The functionally rotating mechanism is a drug export mechanism proposed for the AcrB multidrug efflux transporter from Escherichia coli, based on crystal structures revealing three distinct protomer conformations (access, binding, extrusion) within the homotrimer. Unlike the mechanical rotation of F1-ATPase, AcrB employs an ordered binding change mechanism where the three protomers sequentially cycle through access, binding, and extrusion states without physical rotation of a central subunit. The mechanism is analogous to the cooperative catalysis of the (alpha-beta)3 F1-ATPase, where each protomer alternately occupies a different functional state.

## Mechanism/Details
The AcrB trimer cycles through three functional states in each protomer: (1) Access state — the vestibule is open to the periplasm, allowing potential substrates to enter, but the binding site is shrunken; (2) Binding state — the vestibule remains open and the binding pocket expands via movement of subdomains PN2 and PC1 away from PN1 and PC2, accommodating diverse substrates in a voluminous aromatic pocket; (3) Extrusion state — the vestibule closes, the exit opens via inclination of the central alpha-helix, and the binding pocket shrinks to squeeze the drug into the top funnel leading to TolC. This three-step mechanism is coordinated across the three protomers: while one protomer is in the binding state, a neighbouring protomer is in the extrusion state, and the third is in the access state.

The central alpha-helices serve as a valve: in the binding protomer, an inclined helix from the extrusion protomer blocks the exit from the binding pocket. In the extrusion protomer, the helix is inclined away, opening a path to the top funnel while the binding pocket shrinks. The inclination is driven by Gln112-mediated hydrogen bonding between neighbouring protomers.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- AcrB — Prototypical RND multidrug efflux transporter from E. coli. Crystal structures at 2.8 Å (PDB 2DHH, 2DRD, 2DR6) revealed three distinct protomer conformations corresponding to access, binding, and extrusion states of the functionally rotating mechanism.
