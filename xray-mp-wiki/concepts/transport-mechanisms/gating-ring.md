# Gating Ring (RCK Domain Assembly)

## Overview
A gating ring is a large tetrameric or octameric assembly of RCK (Regulator of Conductance of K+) domains that forms the intracellular ligand-binding domain of certain K+ channels, including BK (Slo1) and MthK channels. The gating ring transduces chemical signals (such as Ca2+ binding) into mechanical force that opens or closes the channel pore.

## Mechanism/Details
In BK channels, each subunit contributes two tandem RCK domains (RCK1 and RCK2). Four subunits assemble into a gating ring with four-fold symmetry. RCK domains from adjacent subunits interact in a head-to-tail manner via hydrophobic contacts between alphaD and alphaE helices. Ca2+ binding at specific sites (Ca2+ bowl, Asp367, Glu374/Glu399) triggers conformational changes at the assembly interfaces, altering gating ring dimensions and applying force through the linker to the channel pore domain. The BK gating ring has a diagonal distance of ~82 A and a 20 A central hole, allowing free ion passage in the open state.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [BK channel (human Slo1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/human-bk-channel-slo1/) — Four subunits each with RCK1+RCK2; Ca2+-free closed state at 3.1 A (PDB 3NAF). Gating ring expanded compared to MthK.
- MthK channel — Prokaryotic Ca2+-gated K+ channel; first gating ring structure solved. Eight RCK domains (octamer) from four subunits.
