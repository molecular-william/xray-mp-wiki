# APC Superfamily (Amino Acid-Polyamine-Organocation Transporter Family)

## Overview
The APC (amino acid-polyamine-organocation) superfamily comprises membrane transporters that move amino acids, polyamines, and organic cations across biological membranes. Members include virtual proton pumps like AdiC (arginine/agmatine antiporter), PotE (ornithine/putrescine antiporter), and CadB (lysine/cadaverine antiporter) from Escherichia coli. These transporters share a common architecture of 10 transmembrane helices arranged in two inverted repeat domains (TM1-TM5 and TM6-TM10). The first crystal structure of an APC transporter, AdiC, revealed structural similarity to the NSS family (LeuT fold), suggesting convergent evolution between apparently unrelated transporter families. APC transporters function as secondary active transporters, using ion gradients to drive substrate uptake or exchange.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [AdiC Arginine/Agmatine Antiporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/adic/) — AdiC is the prototypical APC superfamily member whose crystal structure revealed the conserved 10-TM architecture with inverted pseudo-two-fold symmetry.

- [GadC Glutamate/GABA Antiporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/gadc/) — GadC is an APC superfamily member with 12 transmembrane segments (unlike the typical 10-TM architecture), featuring a unique C-terminal plug gating mechanism.
- AgcS (Alanine/Glycine:Cation Symporter from Methanococcus maripaludis) — AgcS (TC# 2.A.25) is an APC superfamily member that transports L-alanine, D-alanine, and glycine with Na+ coupling. Crystal structures revealed substrate selectivity is determined by the small size of the binding pocket, which only accommodates small amino acids. Mutagenesis of Ile-165 and Phe-273 expands selectivity to valine and leucine.
