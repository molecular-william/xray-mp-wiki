# Extended Selectivity Filter

## Overview
The extended selectivity filter is a five-position motif in the pore of
ammonia-permeable aquaporins that determines substrate specificity. In
contrast to the classical four-position aromatic/arginine (ar/R) selectivity
filter of water-specific aquaporins, the extended filter includes an
additional residue at position LCP in loop C that directly participates in
substrate interactions. This concept was defined by the structure of
AtTIP2;1 from Arabidopsis thaliana.


## Mechanism/Details
The extended selectivity filter comprises five positions: H2P (helix 2),
LCP (loop C), H5P (helix 5), LEP (loop E), and HEP (helix E). In
AtTIP2;1, these positions are occupied by His 63, His 131, Ile 185,
Gly 194, and Arg 200, respectively.

A key feature is the conserved arginine at position HEP (Arg 200), which
adopts a unique orientation stabilized by hydrogen bonding interactions
with His 63 at H2P and His 131 at LCP. This differs from water-specific
aquaporins where the arginine is positioned differently.

The histidine at LCP (His 131 in AtTIP2;1) extends the classical
four-position filter and directly forms hydrogen bonds with permeating
ammonia molecules. The residue at H5P (Ile 185) widens the pore compared
to the histidine found at this position in water-specific aquaporins,
allowing passage of the larger ammonia molecule.

The LCP residue also influences the conformation of the backbone carbonyl
at position LEP: when LCP is an asparagine (as in water-specific AQPs),
the LEP carbonyl is oriented toward the pore center via a hydrogen bond
with the asparagine side chain. When LCP is a histidine or phenylalanine
(as in TIPs), the LEP carbonyl adopts a different orientation.

Gain-of-function experiments demonstrated that transplanting the four
TIP2-specific residues (H2P-His, LCP-His, H5P-Ile, LEP-Gly) into
water-specific human AQP1 was sufficient to confer AtTIP2;1-like ammonia
permeability. The single H180I mutation at H5P was sufficient to convert
HsAQP1 from a water channel to an ammonia-preferring channel, with the
ammonia/water selectivity ratio increasing from near zero to 15 x 10^-2.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [AtTIP2;1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/attip2-1/) — H2P-His 63, LCP-His 131, H5P-Ile 185, LEP-Gly 194, HEP-Arg 200.
Confers both water and ammonia permeability.

- [Human Aquaporin 1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/human-aquaporin-1/) — H2P-Phe 56, LCP-Asn 127, H5P-His 180, LEP-Cys 189, HEP-Arg 195.
The classical ar/R filter with four positions; water-specific.
