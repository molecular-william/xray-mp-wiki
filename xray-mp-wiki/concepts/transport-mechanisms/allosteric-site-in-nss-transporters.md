# Allosteric Site in NSS Transporters

## Overview


## Mechanism/Details
The [Neurotransmitter/Sodium Symporter (NSS) Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/nss-family/) of transporters, which includes the [Serotonin (5-Hydroxytryptamine, 5-HT)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/serotonin/) transporter ([SERT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/ssert/)), [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) transporter (DAT), and [Norepinephrine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/norepinephrine/) transporter (NET), possesses an allosteric binding site distinct from the primary substrate/inhibitor binding site (S1). The allosteric site is located at the periphery of the extracellular vestibule, interposed between extracellular loops 4 and 6 and transmembrane helices 1, 6, 10 and 11. Occupancy of the allosteric site sterically hinders ligand unbinding from the central site, thereby slowing the dissociation rate of inhibitors. In the SERT-(S)-citalopram structure, a second (S)-citalopram molecule was observed bound to the allosteric site, approximately 13 A from the central site. The malleability of the allosteric site — as evidenced by different ligands (SERT-bound (S)-citalopram, [Maltose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/maltose/) detergent head group in [Paroxetine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/paroxetine/) structure) inducing distinct conformational changes — suggests that occupancy of this site might not necessarily abrogate transport activity, opening the possibility of a spectrum of small molecules ranging from inhibiting to enhancing transport. EL6, which displays one of the highest regions of diversity among human [SERT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/ssert/), DAT, and NET sequences, is a key determinant of the unique allosteric site architecture in [SERT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/ssert/), consistent with the observation that allosteric ligands of [SERT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/ssert/) do not modulate DAT or NET.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- /xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/ssert/ — (S)-citalopram binds the allosteric site at the extracellular vestibule (between EL4 and EL6, TM1b, TM6a, TM10, TM11), ~13 A from central site; 100 µM (S)-citalopram decreased 3H-citalopram dissociation rate by ~10-fold
- /xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/d-dopamine-transporter/ — Allosteric modulation of ligand unbinding known to occur in DAT; [SERT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/ssert/) structure reveals how EL6 diversity creates distinct allosteric site architecture compared to dDAT
- /xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/ddat-gat/ — The GAT1 inhibitor [SKF89976a](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/skf89976a/) binds the allosteric site in the extracellular vestibule of dDAT_GAT (engineered dDAT with GAT1 binding site), providing a structural rationale for its mixed competitive/noncompetitive inhibition of GAT1. The allosteric site involves EL4 residue E384S (Ser359 in GAT1) and residues in TM10.
