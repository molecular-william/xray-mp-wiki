# Gap Junction Voltage-Dependent Gating (Vj Gating)

## Overview
Gap junction channels exhibit voltage-dependent gating in response to the transjunctional voltage (Vj) across the channel. The crystal structure of the human connexin 26 (Cx26) gap junction channel at 3.5 Å resolution (Maeda et al., 2009) revealed a gating mechanism in which the N-terminal helices (NTHs) function as both the voltage sensor within the conductive pore and the physical plug that closes the pore. This mechanism is distinct from other voltage-sensitive ion channels that use an S4 helix as a voltage sensor.

## Mechanism/Details
The Vj-gating mechanism involves the six N-terminal helices (NTHs) of the connexin hexamer. In the open state, each NTH is anchored to the pore wall through a hydrophobic interaction between Trp3 (NTH) and Met34 (TM1) of the adjacent protomer, forming a funnel with a 14 Å inner diameter. An inside positive Vj causes inward movement of Asp2 (the N-terminal residue), disrupting: (1) the circular hydrogen bond network between Asp2 and the main chain of Thr5, and (2) the Trp3-Met34 interaction. The released NTHs then assemble into a plug that physically blocks the pore. The release of any one of the six NTHs would break down or destabilize the circular hydrogen bond network through the Asp2-Thr5 girdle, resulting in subconductance states. This accounts for the observation that conformational change of a single subunit is sufficient to initiate Vj gating. Cx26 channels close upon inside positive potential, while Cx32 (with Asn at position 2) closes upon inside negative potential, consistent with Asp2 at the voltage-sensing N terminus.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Human connexin 26 (Cx26) — NTH gating mechanism revealed by 3.5 Å crystal structure. Asp2 and Trp3 form the voltage sensor. Met34Thr deafness mutation decreases current by disrupting NTH anchoring.
- Connexin 32 (Cx32) — Has Asn at position 2 instead of Asp, resulting in opposite polarity sensitivity (closes upon inside negative Vj).
