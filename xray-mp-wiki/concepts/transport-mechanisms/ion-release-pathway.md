# Ion-Release Pathway in Copper-Transporting P-type ATPases

## Overview
The ion-release pathway in copper-transporting P-type ATPases (P_IB ATPases) is a unique transmembrane route through which copper ions exit the transporter after being pumped from the cytoplasm. Unlike other P-type ATPases that use different exit routes, P_IB ATPases utilize a pathway involving conserved proline residues (Pro94 and Pro710) that act as hinges for a proline-dependent opening mechanism. The pathway includes an internal water network that facilitates ion movement from the transmembrane copper binding site to the extracellular side, with Glu189 playing a critical role in coordinating ion release.


## Mechanism/Details
The ion-release pathway operates through the following mechanism: During the E2.Pi state of the transport cycle, the cytoplasmic domains undergo conformational changes that trigger opening of the transmembrane ion-release pathway. Two conserved proline residues, Pro94 and Pro710, act as structural hinges that enable a proline-dependent opening mechanism. These proline kinks introduce flexibility into the transmembrane helices, allowing them to tilt and rotate during the conformational transition from occluded to outward-facing states. An internal water network within the release pathway shows prolonged residency times near Glu189 during the E2.Pi state, suggesting that water molecules play an active role in guiding copper ions through the pathway. The pore radius of the release pathway changes dynamically during the transport cycle, with the pathway becoming more open in the E2.Pi state compared to the E2P state. Mutagenesis studies demonstrate that replacing Pro94 or Pro710 with alanine (P94A or P710A) alters the hydration patterns and pore radius of the release pathway, reducing but not eliminating copper transport activity. This indicates that the proline-dependent opening mechanism is essential for efficient ion release but can be partially bypassed by alternative conformations. The copper-binding site in the transmembrane domain involves conserved methionine residues (Met717, Met711, Ala714) that provide soft ligand coordination for [Copper(I) Ion](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/cu(i)/), and these residues are positioned near the exit of the release pathway.
