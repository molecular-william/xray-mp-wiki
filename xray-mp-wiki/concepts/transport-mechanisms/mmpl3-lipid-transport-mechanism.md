# MmpL3 Lipid Transport Mechanism

## Overview
[MmpL3 from Mycobacterium smegmatis](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mmpL3/) (Mycobacterial membrane protein Large 3) is an essential RND-family transporter required for transport of [Trehalose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/trehalose/) monomycolates (TMMs), the precursors of mycolic acid-containing cell wall components in mycobacteria. The 2.59 A crystal structure of M. smegmatis [MmpL3 from Mycobacterium smegmatis](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mmpL3/) revealed a monomeric molecule with a channel-like cavity originating from the outer leaflet of the inner membrane to the periplasmic domain. [MmpL3 from Mycobacterium smegmatis](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mmpL3/) is a promiscuous lipid-binding protein that specifically binds TMM, PE, PG, PI, [DAG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/dag/), and CDL but not [TDM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/tridecylmaltoside/). Transport is driven by proton motive force via a proton-relay network involving conserved residues D256, Y646, Y257, D645, S293, E647, and K591.


## Mechanism/Details
[MmpL3 from Mycobacterium smegmatis](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mmpL3/) functions as a proton-motive-force-dependent antiporter. Protons flow into the cytoplasm through a conserved proton-relay network (D256-Y646, Y257-D645, S293-D645, E647-K591) to energize substrate translocation. Substrates (e.g., TMM) bind in the channel-like cavity formed by TMs 7-10 and the periplasmic domain, which comprises two subdomains (PD1 and PD2) contributed by loops 1 and 2. The N-terminal and C-terminal halves of the transporter are assembled in a twofold pseudosymmetrical fashion. Unlike other RND transporters that function as trimers, [MmpL3 from Mycobacterium smegmatis](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mmpL3/) is monomeric. PE was discovered as a previously unknown ligand bound inside the transporter, suggesting [MmpL3 from Mycobacterium smegmatis](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mmpL3/) may shuttle a variety of lipids to strengthen the mycobacterial cell wall.
