# AbgT Family of Transporters

## Overview
The AbgT family is a large family of approximately 13,000 putative membrane transporters identified across diverse bacterial species. Members of this family were initially characterized for their role in folate biosynthesis, with E. coli AbgT catalyzing the uptake of p-aminobenzoyl-glutamate for de novo folate synthesis. However, the family also includes antimicrobial resistance proteins such as N. gonorrhoeae MtrF, which confers high-level resistance to hydrophobic antimicrobials. The crystal structure of A. borkumensis [YDAH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/ydah/), the first member of this family to be structurally characterized, revealed a novel dimeric bowl-shaped architecture with nine transmembrane helices and two hairpins per subunit, distinct from other transporter families. [YDAH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/ydah/) was further shown to function as a PMF-dependent efflux pump for sulfonamide antimetabolites, suggesting functional diversity within the AbgT family that includes both import and export functions.
