# Antibiotic Efflux Pumps

## Overview
Antibiotic efflux pumps are membrane transporters that export toxic compounds, including antibiotics and antimetabolites, out of bacterial cells. They are a major mechanism of drug resistance in bacteria. The AbgT family member [YDAH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/ydah/) from A. borkumensis functions as an antibiotic efflux pump that exports sulfonamide antimetabolites and the folate precursor PABA. [YDAH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/ydah/) is proton-motive-force (PMF) dependent and its activity is enhanced by Na+. Mutations in conserved channel residues (D180, N390, W400, P418, R426, D429, N433) abolish efflux function and render cells hypersensitive to sulfonamides. This efflux mechanism represents a novel form of [Sulfonamide Resistance](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/miscellaneous/sulfonamide-resistance/) distinct from the target-based resistance caused by mutations in dihydropteroate synthase.
