# SLC29 Family of Nucleoside Transporters

## Overview
The SLC29 family (Equilibrative Nucleoside Transporters, ENTs) comprises
integral membrane proteins that mediate the energy-independent facilitative
diffusion of nucleosides and nucleobases across cellular membranes. Four
human isoforms exist (hENT1-hENT4) with diverse tissue distributions,
substrate specificities, and pharmacological properties. Unlike the
SLC28 concentrative nucleoside transporter family (CNTs), the SLC29 family
does not have prokaryotic orthologs and is evolutionarily distinct.


## Mechanism/Details
SLC29 transporters function via an [Alternating Access Mechanism](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/alternating-access-mechanism/) using a
rocker-switch-like reorientation of N- and C-terminal domains. The
transporter architecture consists of 11 transmembrane helices with a
pseudo-symmetric 6+5 topology, distinguishing it from the canonical 12-TM
Major Facilitator Superfamily ([MFS](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/mfs-transporter/)) fold. An extracellular thin gate and
intracellular thick gate control substrate access to the central cavity.

Substrate transport involves:
1. Outward-facing state: central cavity accessible from extracellular side
2. Substrate binding in the orthosteric site within the central cavity
3. Extracellular thin gate closure upon substrate binding
4. Domain reorientation to the inward-facing state
5. Substrate release to the cytoplasm

Adenosine reuptake inhibitors exploit distinct mechanisms:
- [Dilazep](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dilazep/): sterically blocks extracellular thin gate closure by occupying
  opportunistic site 1 at the gate region
- [NBMPR](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/nbmpr/): occupies orthosteric site and opportunistic site 2 (hydrophobic
  pocket in N-domain), allowing thin gate closure but preventing domain
  reorientation

Nucleoside recognition involves conserved charged residues (Arg345, Asp341)
for ribose coordination, polar residues (Gln158) for nucleobase recognition,
and hydrophobic contacts surrounding the purine moiety.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Human Equilibrative Nucleoside Transporter 1 (hENT1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/hent1/) (SLC29A1) — Adenosine-preferring transporter, target of clinically used adenosine reuptake inhibitors
- hENT2 (SLC29A2) — Broader substrate specificity, lower sensitivity to [NBMPR](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/nbmpr/) inhibition
- hENT3 (SLC29A3) — Intracellular lysosomal nucleoside transporter
- hENT4 (SLC29A4) — Also known as PMAT (plasma membrane monoamine transporter)
