# Voltage-Sensor Paddle

## Overview
The voltage-sensor paddle is a helix-turn-helix structural motif found in voltage-dependent potassium channels (Kv channels). It is composed of the S3b helix and the N-terminal half of the S4 helix, forming a compact structural unit located at the outer perimeter of the channel at the protein-lipid interface. The paddle carries conserved positively charged arginine residues that function as gating charges, moving across the membrane electric field in response to voltage changes to trigger pore opening.

## Mechanism/Details
The voltage-sensor paddle is a conserved structural unit in voltage-dependent K+ channels. It consists of two alpha helices (S3b and the N-terminal half of S4) connected by a short loop, forming a helix-turn-helix arrangement. The paddle is located at the protein-lipid interface on the outer perimeter of the channel, flexibly attached to the main body through an S3 loop connecting S2 to S3a.
Key structural features: - The paddle surface facing the lipid bilayer is mainly hydrophobic, with tightly packed residues between S3b and S4 forming a hydrophobic interface. - The S4 helix contains conserved positively charged arginine and lysine residues that function as gating charges. - Negatively charged amino acids form two clusters: an external negative cluster and an internal negative cluster that stabilize the gating charges on either side of the membrane. - A 'phenylalanine gap' - a hydrophobic zone of roughly 10 A between the negative clusters - creates an energy barrier for arginine side chains, preventing proton transfer and favoring a switch-like transition. - The S4 helix can adopt a 3_10-helical hydrogen-bonding pattern, stretching the helix and allowing a 'concertina effect' during gating. - The paddle is transferable among voltage sensors of different origins, functioning as an independent modular domain.
The high-resolution structure of the paddle-chimaera channel (Kv1.2 with Kv2.1 paddle) at 2.4 A in a lipid membrane-like environment revealed that in the open state, the paddle is tilted away from the central axis of the voltage sensor, towards the lipid membrane, with one entire face exposed to lipid. Gating charges R3 and R4 form ionized hydrogen bonds with the external negative cluster, while K5 and R6 form ionized hydrogen bonds with the internal negative cluster.
