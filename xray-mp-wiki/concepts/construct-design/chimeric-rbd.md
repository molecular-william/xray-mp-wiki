# Chimeric Receptor-Binding Domain (RBD)

## Overview
Chimeric receptor-binding domain (RBD) constructs are engineered protein domains that combine elements from viral RBDs (most commonly from SARS-CoV-2 spike protein) with stabilizing fusion proteins or scaffold domains to facilitate structural biology, immunogenicity studies, and vaccine development. By fusing the RBD to a well-folded carrier protein, researchers can improve expression, stability, and crystallization while preserving the antigenic properties of the native RBD.


## Mechanism/Details
Chimeric RBD constructs typically involve fusing the receptor-binding domain of a viral spike glycoprotein to a heterologous scaffold or fusion partner. For SARS-CoV-2, common designs include: (1) fusion of the RBD to the human IgG1 Fc domain (RBD-Fc) to facilitate dimerization and purification via Protein A; (2) fusion to ferritin or other multimerization domains to generate nanoparticle immunogens; and (3) direct structural studies of the isolated RBD or RBD-ACE2 complex using E. coli- or mammalian cell-expressed constructs. The RBD itself can be further modified with stabilizing mutations (e.g., proline substitutions for improved thermostability) or glycan modifications to enhance structural homogeneity. These chimeric constructs have been critical for understanding the structural basis of antibody neutralization and for vaccine design campaigns.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- SARS-CoV-2 Spike RBD-Fc — Chimeric RBD fused to human IgG1 Fc domain for dimeric display; used for structural studies of antibody-RBD interactions and as a serological reagent
- SARS-CoV-2 RBD-Ferritin Nanoparticle — Chimeric RBD fused to ferritin (SpyTag/SpyCatcher conjugation) for multivalent antigen display in vaccine formulations
