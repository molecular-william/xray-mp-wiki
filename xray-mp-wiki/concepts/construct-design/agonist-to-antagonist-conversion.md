## Mechanism/Details


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- A2A-StaR2-[BRIL](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/protein-tags/bril/) in complex with [LJ-4517 (Compound 2)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/lj-4517/) (compound 2, PDB 8CU7)
 — C8-thiophene substitution converted the nucleoside agonist ([Compound 1](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/compound-1/)) into the antagonist [LJ-4517 (Compound 2)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/lj-4517/). The thiophene group at C8 restricts receptor conformational rearrangements required for activation by sterically hindering engagement of both Ser277(7.42) and His278(7.43) with the ribose-like moiety.

- A2A-StaR2-S277-[BRIL](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/protein-tags/bril/) in complex with [LJ-4517 (Compound 2)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/lj-4517/) (compound 2, PDB 8CU6)
 — The S277A mutation combined with C8-thiophene modification further confirmed that transient His278(7.43) contacts alone are insufficient for activation. Direct H-bonding to His278 occurs in only ~5% of MD simulation time points, with water-mediated bonding in ~41% of snapshots.

- [Human Adenosine A2A Receptor A2A-PSB1-bRIL Complex](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/a2a-psb1-bril/) with PSB-1 derivatives
 — The S91K mutation in [Human Adenosine A2A Receptor A2A-PSB1-bRIL Complex](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/a2a-psb1-bril/) locks the receptor in the inactive state by occupying the allosteric sodium binding site, preventing agonist accommodation. While not a direct agonist-to-antagonist conversion, it demonstrates that single-point mutations can selectively abolish agonist binding while preserving antagonist affinity.
