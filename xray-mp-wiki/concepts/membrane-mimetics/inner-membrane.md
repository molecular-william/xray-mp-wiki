# Inner Membrane

## Overview
The inner (cytoplasmic) membrane of Gram-negative bacteria is the primary permeability barrier and site of many critical cellular processes including energy transduction, solute transport, and signal transduction. It hosts diverse alpha-helical membrane proteins including transporters, channels, receptors, and enzymes. In structural biology, the term distinguishes bacterial inner membrane from outer membrane.
