# Outer Membrane

## Overview
The outer membrane is the outermost lipid bilayer of Gram-negative bacteria, composed of phospholipids in the inner leaflet and lipopolysaccharide in the outer leaflet. It serves as a selective permeability barrier. Outer membrane proteins include beta-barrel porins, lipoproteins, and TonB-dependent transporters. The outer membrane is also found in mitochondria and chloroplasts.
