# Lipidic Sponge Phase (LSP)

## Overview
The lipidic sponge phase (LSP) is a liquid-like isotropic phase formed by a mixture of [Monoolein](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/monoolein/), [Cholesterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/cholesterol/), and buffer containing high concentrations of [PEG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg/) and salt. Unlike the lipidic cubic phase (LCP), which has a highly viscous gel-like consistency, the LSP has lower viscosity, facilitating handling and microcrystal harvesting. The LSP supports nucleation and growth of thousands of uniformly sized crystals per microliter of protein solution, making it ideal for [Serial Femtosecond Crystallography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/serial-femtosecond-crystallography/) (SFX). The LSP-SFX approach was demonstrated for rapid determination of multiple GPCR co-crystal structures from submilligram quantities of protein (Ishchenko et al., IUCrJ 2019). Novel syringe-based and in-tip LSP crystallization methods were developed for the [Rhodobacter sphaeroides Photosynthetic Reaction Center](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/rhodobacter-sphaeroides-reaction-center/), enabling production of larger numbers of crystals for serial crystallography. One caveat of LSP crystallization is that [Monoolein](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/monoolein/), the primary matrix lipid, can displace native hydrophobic cofactors (e.g., carotenoids, ubiquinones) from their binding pockets, requiring co-crystallization with the missing cofactors to restore native ligand occupancy.
