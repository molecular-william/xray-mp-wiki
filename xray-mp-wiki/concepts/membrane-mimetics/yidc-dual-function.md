# YidC Dual Insertase/Chaperone Function

## Overview
The YidC protein from Gram-negative bacteria is a unique dual-function membrane protein that can adopt either an insertase conformation (for independent membrane protein insertion) or a chaperone conformation (as part of the holo-translocon complex with SecYEG). The dual function is enabled by the re-positioning of the N-terminal amphipathic helix (N-AH), the P1 domain, the EH1 helix, and the C1 region.

## Mechanism/Details
In the insertase conformation, N-AH lies on the periplasmic membrane surface acting as a recognition helix, EH1 is parallel to the membrane surface, and the C1 region samples highly flexible conformations near the cytoplasmic bilayer surface. In the chaperone conformation within the holo-translocon, N-AH inserts between TMD elements contacting SecY TM7, EH1 inserts into the membrane tilted and closer to SecY, and the C1 region contacts SecY TM1 and TM10. The hydrophilic groove within the TMD (TM2-TM6) serves as the catalytic center for substrate insertion.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- TmYidC (Thermotoga maritima YidC) — The 3.4 Å structure (PDB 6Y86) shows an active insertase conformation with the N-AH resolved for the first time
- EcYidC (Escherichia coli YidC) — Both insertase (PDB 6AL2) and chaperone (PDB 5MG3) conformations have been structurally characterized
