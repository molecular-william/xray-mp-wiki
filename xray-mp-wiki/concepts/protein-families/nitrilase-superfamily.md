# Nitrilase Superfamily

## Overview


## Mechanism/Details
The nitrilase superfamily comprises enzymes that catalyse hydrolysis or condensation of carbon-nitrogen bonds. It includes 13 branches, with [LNT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/lnt/) representing the ninth branch. Members share a characteristic alpha-beta-alpha folding topology and a conserved Glu-Lys-Cys catalytic triad. The superfamily includes enzymes with diverse functions, from nitrile metabolism to lipid modification.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- E. coli [LNT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/lnt/) — [LNT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/lnt/) belongs to the 9th branch of the nitrilase superfamily, catalysing N-acyl transfer in lipoprotein biogenesis
- [Pseudomonas aeruginosa Lnt](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/pseudomonas-aeruginosa-lnt/) — LntPae is a nitrilase superfamily member with the same Glu-Lys-Cys catalytic triad and alpha-beta-betaalpha sandwich fold; structures of both E. coli and P. aeruginosa Lnt were solved at multiple states of the ping-pong reaction (Smithers et al., Sci. Adv. 2023)
