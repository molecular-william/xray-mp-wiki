# AAA ATPase Superfamily

## Overview
The AAA (ATPases Associated with diverse cellular Activities) superfamily is a large and evolutionarily conserved family of P-loop NTPases that couple ATP hydrolysis to mechanical work, protein unfolding, and macromolecular remodeling. AAA proteins share a conserved AAA domain of ~200-250 residues that contains Walker A, Walker B, and second region of homology (SRH) motifs essential for nucleotide binding and hydrolysis. Most AAA proteins assemble into ring-shaped hexamers (C6 or C2 symmetry) with a central pore through which substrate polypeptides or nucleic acids are translocated. Key membrane-associated AAA proteins include FtsH (a membrane-anchored AAA protease that degrades misfolded membrane proteins), p97/VCP/Cdc48 (involved in endoplasmic reticulum-associated degradation, ERAD), and katanin and spastin (microtubule-severing enzymes). The AAA+ clade, a broader grouping, includes the clamp loader subunits of DNA polymerases and helicase loaders. AAA proteins execute their diverse functions through nucleotide-driven conformational changes that are transmitted through a conserved arginine finger (the SRH motif) to neighboring subunits, enabling concerted ring movements.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- FtsH (Membrane-Anchored AAA Protease) — FtsH is a membrane-bound AAA protease that degrades misfolded membrane and cytoplasmic proteins. Its AAA ring unfolds substrates and translocates them into a proteolytic chamber.
- p97/VCP (ERAD AAA ATPase) — p97 extracts ubiquitinated proteins from the ER membrane for degradation by the proteasome, using ATP hydrolysis in its two AAA domains (D1 and D2) to drive substrate translocation.
