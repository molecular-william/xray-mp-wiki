# DEDDh Exonuclease Family

## Overview
The DEDDh exonuclease family is named after four catalytic residues (Asp, Glu, Asp, Asp) and a general base histidine (His) that mediate nuclease activity through a two-metal ion mechanism. The family includes thousands of members across all domains of life, from bacteria to humans. Some members possess both DNase and RNase activities. Key family members include eukaryotic [Mouse TREX1 (Three Prime Repair Exonuclease 1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/trex1/)/TREX2, bacterial RNase T (the E. coli homolog of [Mouse TREX1 (Three Prime Repair Exonuclease 1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/trex1/)), and other 3'-to-5' exonucleases. Structural and mechanistic studies of [Mouse TREX1 (Three Prime Repair Exonuclease 1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/trex1/) have revealed that the 2'-OH-mediated intra-chain hydrogen bonding in RNA reduces substrate flexibility and thereby reduces the binding affinity and catalytic efficiency for RNA substrates compared to DNA substrates, a mechanism that may be general to other DEDDh exonucleases such as RNase T.
