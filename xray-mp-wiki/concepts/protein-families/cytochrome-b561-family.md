# Cytochrome b561 (Cyt b561) Protein Family

## Overview
The cytochrome b561 (Cyt b561) family is a group of highly conserved
transmembrane ascorbate-dependent oxidoreductases found exclusively
in eukaryotes. Members of this family play essential roles in
ascorbate recycling and [IRON](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/iron/) absorption by transferring electrons
across biological membranes. Key family members include mammalian
chromaffin granule Cyt b561 (CGCyt b561), mammalian duodenal Cyt b561
(DCyt b561), and plant Cyt b561 homologs such as Cyt b561-B from
Arabidopsis thaliana.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Cytochrome b561 from Arabidopsis thaliana — First high-resolution crystal structures of the family, revealing homodimeric architecture with six transmembrane helices and two [HEME](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/) groups per protomer. Structures determined in substrate-free, ascorbate-bound, and monodehydroascorbate-bound states.
- CGCyt b561 (mammalian chromaffin granule) — Resides in chromaffin vesicle membrane; transfers electrons from cytoplasmic ascorbate to intravesicular monodehydroascorbate radical for ascorbate regeneration, supporting [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) beta-hydroxylase in [Norepinephrine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/norepinephrine/) synthesis.
- DCyt b561 (mammalian duodenal) — Present in duodenal mucosa cell membrane; relays electrons from cytoplasmic ascorbate to ferric-chelate in the intestinal lumen for [IRON](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/iron/) absorption. Expression levels associated with [IRON](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/iron/) metabolism disorders.
- ZmCyt b561 (Zea mays) — Plant homolog extensively studied by site-directed mutagenesis; conserved Lys83 (corresponding to Lys81 in A. thaliana) critical for ascorbate-specific transmembrane electron transfer.
