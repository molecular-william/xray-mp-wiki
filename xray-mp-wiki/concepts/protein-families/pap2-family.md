# Type II Phosphatidic Acid Phosphatase (PAP2) Family

## Overview
Type II [Phosphatidic Acid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidic-acid/) phosphatases (PAP2s) are a large family of Mg2+-independent phosphatases found in all life kingdoms. They catalyze dephosphorylation of phosphoric monoester bonds in various phosphorylated carbohydrates, peptides, and lipids. The soluble branch is called class A nonspecific acid phosphatases (NSAPs), while the transmembrane (TM) branch is also known as the lipid phosphatase/phosphotransferase family. Members include E. coli PgpB and human glucose-6-phosphatase (G6Pase), a key enzyme in blood [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) homeostasis.


## Mechanism/Details
PAP2 enzymes share a conserved catalytic core of six transmembrane helices
(TMs 4-6 in TM members) containing three signature sequence motifs (C1-C3).
The active site contains two conserved histidine residues that mediate a
two-step dephosphorylation mechanism:

- **Catalytic mechanism:** His207(3.08) (ecPgpB numbering) acts as a
  nucleophile, forming a phospho-enzyme intermediate with the substrate
  phosphate group. His163(2.04) then recruits a water molecule to complete
  the dephosphorylation, releasing the product and regenerating the enzyme.

- **Lipid substrate entry:** In transmembrane PAP2 enzymes, a V-shaped TM
  helix pair (TMs 2-3) creates a lateral opening at the membrane-solvent
  interface, allowing lipid substrates to access the active site directly
  from the lipid bilayer without entering the aqueous phase.

- **Induced-fit mechanism:** The apo-form structure shows TM3 loosely packed,
  with Lys97(1.01) displaced ~9 A from its active position. Substrate binding
  drives TM3 movement toward TM2, completing the active site and ensuring
  specificity for properly bound lipid substrates.

- **Conserved fold:** Despite low sequence identity (~10-15%), all TM PAP2
  proteins are predicted to share the same six-TM topology with similarly
  located C1-C3 motifs. Human G6Pase has an additional four TM helices
  C-terminal to the canonical six-helix core.

PAP2 enzymes are involved in vesicular trafficking, secretion, endocytosis,
protein glycosylation, energy storage (triacylglycerol biosynthesis), and
stress response.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- E. coli PgpB — Prototypical TM PAP2 enzyme; 3.2 A crystal structure; catalyzes dephosphorylation of undecaprenyl pyrophosphate
- Human glucose-6-phosphatase (G6Pase) — Key enzyme in blood [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) homeostasis; TM PAP2 member with 10 TM helices
