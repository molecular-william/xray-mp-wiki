# Resistance-Nodulation-Cell Division (RND) Superfamily

## Overview
The RND superfamily comprises large tripartite efflux pumps that transport diverse substrates across the Gram-negative cell envelope. Prototypical members include AcrAB-TolC from E. coli and MexAB-OprM from P. aeruginosa, which mediate multidrug resistance. Each RND transporter consists of a periplasmic adaptor protein, an inner membrane transporter, and an outer membrane channel. The transport mechanism involves a rotating elevator or peristaltic pump of the transmembrane domains.
