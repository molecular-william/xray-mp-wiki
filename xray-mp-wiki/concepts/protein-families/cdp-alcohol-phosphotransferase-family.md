# CDP-Alcohol Phosphotransferase Family

## Overview
The CDP-alcohol phosphotransferase (CDP-AP) family comprises integral membrane enzymes that catalyse the transfer of a substituted phosphate group from a CDP-linked donor to an alcohol acceptor, an essential reaction for phospholipid biosynthesis across all kingdoms of life. CDP-APs facilitate the conjugation of polar headgroups (choline, inositol, ethanolamine) to diacylglycerol lipid tails to form phosphatidylcholine (PC), phosphatidylinositol (PI), and phosphatidylethanolamine (PE). Members exhibit broad substrate specificity, with the diacylglycerol tail or polar headgroup able to participate as either the CDP-linked donor or the acceptor alcohol. All CDP-APs share a universally conserved signature motif of eight amino acids (D₁xxD₂G₁xxAR...G₂xxxD₃xxxD₄) that plays a fundamental role in catalysing phosphotransfer. The first crystal structure of a CDP-AP (AF2299 from Archaeoglobus fulgidus, PDB 4O6M/4O6N/4Q7C) revealed a homodimer with each protomer consisting of six transmembrane helices and an N-terminal cytosolic domain, with a polar cavity accommodating the active site.

## Mechanism/Details
CDP-APs catalyse phosphotransfer via a sequential, base-catalysed mechanism. The conserved signature motif (D₁xxD₂G₁xxAR...G₂xxxD₃xxxD₄) lines the active site within a polar cavity at the cytosolic membrane surface. D₁ and D₂ coordinate the primary divalent cation (Mg²⁺), which primes the CDP pyrophosphate for catalysis. G₁ and G₂ provide backbone flexibility for conformational changes upon substrate binding. AR interacts with the CDP pyrophosphate through electrostatic and hydrogen bonding. D₃ is positioned near the scissile phosphate. D₄ acts as the catalytic base, abstracting a proton from the terminal hydroxyl of the acceptor alcohol, activating it for nucleophilic attack on the β-phosphorus of the CDP-linked donor, yielding CMP and the phosphodiester product. The clustering of four conserved aspartates may elevate the pKa of D₄ to function efficiently as a base.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- AF2299 (Archaeoglobus fulgidus) — First structurally characterized CDP-AP. Crystal structure at 2.0 Å (PDB 4O6M/4O6N/4Q7C). Homodimer with 6 TM helices per protomer. Likely catalyses condensation of CDP-glycerol with L-myo-inositol-1-phosphate.
- IPCT/DIPPS from Archaeoglobus fulgidus — Bifunctional enzyme with DIPPS domain belonging to CDP-alcohol phosphatidyltransferase family; 6 transmembrane helices; dimerizes through DIPPS domains
- PIP Synthase (RsPIPS) from Renibacterium salmoninarum — First structure of a CDP-AP that processes a lipid substrate (CDP-DAG). Homodimeric 6-TM architecture. Reveals hydrophobic groove for lipid substrate entry, inositol phosphate acceptor pocket with conserved R155/R195 arginine pair, D93 as catalytic base. Sequential ordered bi-bi mechanism: CDP-DAG binds first, then inositol phosphate.
