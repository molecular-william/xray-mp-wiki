# Cation Diffusion Facilitator (CDF) Family

## Overview
The Cation Diffusion Facilitator (CDF) family is a ubiquitous family of
metal ion transporters that maintain cellular metal homeostasis by exporting
Zn²⁺, Cd²⁺, Co²⁺, Fe²⁺, and other transition metals from the cytoplasm to
the extracellular space or intracellular organelles. In mammals, CDF members
are known as ZnT (Zinc Transporter) proteins. CDF transporters typically
function as Zn²⁺/H⁺ antiporters or metal ion efflux pumps and play critical
roles in zinc homeostasis, neurotransmission, insulin secretion, and
protection against metal toxicity.


## Mechanism/Details
CDF family members share a conserved architecture:

- **Homodimeric assembly:** Most CDF proteins function as homodimers. The
  dimer interface is formed by the cytoplasmic CTDs, often stabilized by
  structural metal ions, while the TMDs remain relatively mobile.

- **Six-helix bundle TMD:** Each protomer contains six transmembrane helices
  (TMs I-VI) that form a compact bundle. TM5 is often short (3-4 turns),
  creating extracellular and intracellular cavities on opposite sides of
  the membrane. These cavities face each other but are not directly connected.

- **Tetrahedral metal binding site:** The primary transport site is formed by
  four conserved residues (typically Asp and His) arranged in tetrahedral
  geometry. In YiiP, this site (Z1) is formed by D45, D49, H153, and D157.
  The tetrahedral coordination provides selectivity for Zn²⁺ and Cd²⁺ over
  ions preferring other geometries.

- **Metallochaperone-like CTD:** The C-terminal domain adopts an open αβ-fold
  that resembles copper metallochaperones (e.g., Hah1), with two α-helices
  and a three-stranded β-sheet. This domain may function as a Zn²⁺-receiving
  or sensing domain.

- **Proton-coupled antiport:** CDF members catalyze metal ion efflux coupled
  to proton import (Zn²⁺/H⁺ antiport). The proton gradient across the
  membrane drives metal export against concentration gradients.

- **Y-shaped conformation:** The TMDs and CTDs are connected by flexible
  linkers, allowing the TMDs to swing outward from the dimer interface,
  producing a characteristic Y-shaped structure that may facilitate metal
  transport across the membrane.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [E. coli YiiP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/yiiP/) — Zn²⁺/H⁺ antiporter; first CDF family member with X-ray crystal structure at 3.8 Å
- E. coli ZitB — Zn²⁺/Cd²⁺ efflux transporter; functionally characterized homolog of YiiP
- Ralstonia metallidurans CzcD — Metal ion efflux transporter; loss-of-function mutations mapped to conserved residues
- Human ZnT-3 (SLC30A3) — Sequesters cytoplasmic Zn²⁺ into synaptic vesicles in glutamatergic neurons
- Human ZnT-8 (SLC30A8) — Stimulates zinc accumulation in insulin secretory vesicles; linked to type 2 diabetes
