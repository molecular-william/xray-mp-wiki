# Rh/Amt/MEP Protein Family

## Overview


## Mechanism/Details
The Rh/Amt/MEP protein family is a family of ubiquitous integral membrane proteins
that mediate movement of ammonium across cell membranes. Almost all Amt/Rh proteins
contain two conserved histidine residues that form a hydrogen-bonded pair within the
channel pore. In E. coli AmtB, the twin-His motif (His-168 and His-318) is essential
for optimum substrate conductance, as demonstrated by comprehensive mutagenesis
to 14 different polar and non-polar residues. In a subgroup of fungal Amt proteins,
one histidine is replaced by glutamate (Glu-His pair), which retains partial activity.
Rh30 proteins (RhD, RhCE in humans) lack the twin-His motif entirely, with the
equivalent residues replaced by Tyr and Phe, suggesting these proteins have acquired
a new function during evolution and do not function as ammonium channels.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Rh Protein from Nitrosomonas europaea](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/rh-protein-nitrosomonas-europaeae/) — First determined structure of an Rh family member; trimeric 11-TM channel with C-terminal helix, twin-His site, and CO2 binding site. Revealed key differences from Amt proteins including absence of pi-cation binding site and presence of 6 internal prolines in TM helices.
- [Ammonium Transporter AmtB (E. coli)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/amt-b/) — Best-characterized Amt family member; trimeric ammonia channel with 11 TM helices per subunit. Shows pi-cation binding site for NH4+ recruitment and two Phe residues blocking the channel. The twin-His motif (H168/H318) is essential for optimum substrate conductance as demonstrated by comprehensive mutagenesis and structural analysis.
- [Human Rhesus C Glycoprotein (RhCG)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/rhcg/) — First human Rh glycoprotein structure at 2.1 A resolution; trimeric 12-TM channel with unique shunt pocket, twin-His NH3 permeation mechanism, and insights into renal pH regulation. Reveals structural basis for Phe gate in open conformation and the molecular differences between Rh and Amt subfamilies.
- [RhAG/RhBG/RhCG (human glycosylated Rh proteins)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/rhcg/) — Human glycosylated Rh proteins contain both conserved His residues and are capable of ammonium transport, in contrast to non-glycosylated Rh30 proteins (RhD, RhCE) that lack the twin-His motif and likely do not function as ammonium channels.
- [Mep2 Ammonium Transceptor (S. cerevisiae and C. albicans)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/mep2-ammonium-transceptor/) — Fungal ammonium transceptor that functions both as transporter and sensor for filamentous growth. Structures show closed, non-phosphorylated conformations with a two-tier cytoplasmic channel block regulated by phosphorylation of the C-terminal region by Npr1 kinase. Reveals the first structural insights into eukaryotic ammonium transporter regulation.
