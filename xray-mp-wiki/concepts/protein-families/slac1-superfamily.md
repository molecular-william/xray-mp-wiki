# SLAC1 Superfamily

## Overview
The [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) superfamily comprises a widespread group of anion channel proteins found in plants, bacteria, archaea, and fungi. The founding member, [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1) from Arabidopsis thaliana, is a plant anion channel essential for stomatal closure in response to environmental stimuli. The superfamily is characterized by a common ten- transmembrane-helix architecture organized from five helical hairpin pairs, forming a central five-helix pore gated by a conserved phenylalanine residue. The superfamily includes plant [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) and SLAH proteins (subfamily SF1A), bacterial [TEHA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/teha/) homologues (subfamilies SF1B and SF1C), and fungal homologues such as Ssu1 and Mae1 (family SF3). Known prokaryotic homologues contain only the predicted transmembrane domain of [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/), while plant homologues have substantial amino- and carboxy-terminal extensions.


## Mechanism/Details
The [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) superfamily was identified through bioinformatic analysis of the [TEHA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/teha/) protein from Escherichia coli, which was found to be a close relative of plant [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) anion channels. The superfamily encompasses over 814 members across multiple domains of life.
The superfamily is organized into several families and subfamilies: - Family SF1: Contains the plant SLAC/SLAH proteins (SF1A), closest bacterial
  homologues (SF1B), and [TEHA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/teha/) homologues (SF1C)
- Family SF2: Contains additional bacterial homologues (SF2A, SF2B) - Family SF3: Contains fungal Mae1 and Ssu1 proteins and their archaeal or bacterial
  homologues

The transmembrane domain of AtSLAC1 shares 19% sequence identity with [HITEHA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/hiteha/) (E-value 3 x 10^-22), 76% with rice [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/), 41% with Arabidopsis SLAH1, 25% with an SL1B homologue from Halorhodospira halophila, 11% with S. cerevisiae Ssu1, and 9% with S. pombe Mae1.
Key conserved features include: - Ten transmembrane helices per protomer - Five helical hairpin pairs with quasi-five-fold symmetry - Conserved phenylalanine residue (Phe 450 in AtSLAC1, Phe 262 in [HITEHA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/hiteha/)) at the
  pore constriction
- Centrally located proline residues in inner helices (TM3, TM5, TM7, TM9) generating
  kinks that maintain pore diameter
- Short extracellular loops (2-5 residues) and longer intracellular connections
The SF1A plant subfamily includes [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) and four homologues (SLAH1-SLAH4) in Arabidopsis. [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) expression is confined to guard cells, while SLAH1 is absent from leaves but can complement [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) mutations. Plant [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) proteins have substantial cytoplasmic N- and C-terminal extensions (179 and 51 residues, respectively) that are absent in prokaryotic homologues and are sites of OST1 kinase phosphorylation.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- **Arabidopsis thaliana [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/)**: Founding member; slow anion channel in guard cells
  controlling stomatal aperture; essential for drought response
- **Arabidopsis thaliana SLAH1**: [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) homologue that complements slac1-1 mutation;
  lacks cytoplasmic terminal extensions of [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/)
- **Haemophilus influenzae [TEHA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/teha/) ([HITEHA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/hiteha/))**: Bacterial homologue; crystal structure at
  1.20 A resolution provides template for [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) homology modeling
- **Escherichia coli [TEHA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/teha/) (EcTehA)**: Central seed sequence for NYCOMPS-targeted
  family expansion
- **Saccharomyces cerevisiae Ssu1**: Fungal homologue (SF3A) with 11% sequence
  identity to [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) transmembrane domain
- **Schizosaccharomyces pombe Mae1**: Fungal homologue (SF3B) with 9% sequence
  identity to [SLAC1 (SLOW ANION CHANNEL 1 from Arabidopsis thaliana)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/slac1/) transmembrane domain
