# PNPT Superfamily (Polypropenyl-phosphate N-acetyl hexosamine 1-phosphate Transferase)

## Overview
The polypropenyl-phosphate N-acetyl hexosamine 1-phosphate transferase (PNPT)
superfamily comprises a group of membrane enzymes that catalyze the transfer of
N-acetyl hexosamine 1-phosphate from UDP-linked sugar donors to polyprenyl
phosphate lipid carriers. The superfamily includes the MraY, WecA, TagO, WbcO,
WbpL, and RgpG enzyme families in bacteria, which are responsible for the
synthesis of cell envelope polymers such as peptidoglycan, O-antigen, and
teichoic acid. In eukaryotes, the GPT (UDP-GlcNAc:dolichol-P
GlcNAc-1-P transferase) family is the eukaryotic counterpart responsible for
the committed step of N-linked glycosylation.


## Mechanism/Details
PNPT family enzymes are integral membrane proteins that use a conserved
mechanism involving the transfer of phospho-N-acetyl hexosamine from a
UDP-linked donor to a polyprenyl phosphate acceptor. The MraY enzyme, the
first PNPT member to be structurally characterized, contains 10 transmembrane
helices with a conserved active site cleft formed by TM3, TM4, TM8, and TM9b.
Three invariant aspartate residues and a histidine residue are critical for
catalysis, with Asp265 coordinating an essential Mg2+ (or Mn2+) ion. The
active site residues are conserved throughout the entire PNPT superfamily,
suggesting a common catalytic mechanism. The enzyme is Mg2+-dependent and
requires a polyprenyl phosphate lipid carrier as the phosphate-accepting
substrate.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Aquifex aeolicus MraY](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/aquifex-aeolicus-mray/) — First structurally characterized member of the PNPT superfamily; catalyzes the formation of Lipid I in peptidoglycan biosynthesis
- WecA — Bacterial enzyme involved in O-antigen and enterobacterial common antigen synthesis
- TagO — Bacterial enzyme involved in teichoic acid biosynthesis
- GPT (UDP-GlcNAc:dolichol-P GlcNAc-1-P transferase) — Eukaryotic family member responsible for the committed step of N-linked glycosylation
