# DHHC Palmitoyltransferase Family

## Overview
The DHHC (Asp-His-His-Cys) palmitoyltransferase family comprises eukaryotic
integral membrane enzymes that catalyze protein S-acylation (palmitoylation) —
the covalent attachment of a fatty acyl chain to cysteine residues via a
thioester linkage. In humans, 23 DHHC family members have been identified.
These enzymes contain a signature DHHC motif within a cysteine-rich domain
in an intracellular loop, situated between the second and third transmembrane
helices. DHHC enzymes catalyze a two-step reaction: autoacylation of the
active site cysteine using acyl-CoA, followed by transfer of the acyl chain
to a substrate protein. Crystal structures of human DHHC20 and zebrafish
DHHC15 revealed that the four transmembrane helices form a tepee-like
organization with the active site at the membrane-cytosol interface.


## Mechanism/Details
DHHC enzymes utilize a two-step ping-pong reaction mechanism. In the first
step (autoacylation), the catalytic cysteine (the Cys of the DHHC motif)
attacks the thioester bond of acyl-CoA, forming an acyl-enzyme intermediate
and releasing CoA. The aspartic acid and the first histidine of the DHHC
motif form a catalytic triad that activates the nucleophilic cysteine by
accepting a proton during catalysis.

In the second step (transacylation), the acyl chain is transferred from the
acylated cysteine to a substrate protein cysteine, forming a thioester linkage
on the substrate. The structures show that the active site is positioned at
the membrane-aqueous interface, with the catalytic cysteine protruding toward
the bilayer. The acyl chain inserts into a hydrophobic cavity formed by the
four TM helices; this cavity is lined by residues that determine acyl
chain-length selectivity. Different DHHC members show distinct selectivity
profiles, ranging from preference for C16 (palmitoyl) to broader chain-length
tolerance.

The C-terminal domain contains conserved motifs critical for activity,
including the TTXE motif that interacts with the active site, the PaCCT
(palmitoyltransferase conserved C-terminus) motif with highly conserved
Phe/Tyr and Asn residues, and an amphipathic helix that contacts TM3 and TM4.
Two Zn2+ ions are bound by the cysteine-rich domain in a CCHC (Cys-Cys-His-Cys)
configuration, providing structural stability and positioning the catalytic
cysteine optimally.
