# Phosphoenzyme E2P State

## Overview
The E2P state is a phosphorylated intermediate of P-type ATPases in which the enzyme carries a covalently bound aspartyl phosphoanhydride at a conserved aspartate residue in the P domain. In the Na+,K+-ATPase, the E2P state has high affinity for cardiotonic steroids (CTS) such as [Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/), making it a key conformational state for understanding CTS-mediated inhibition and signaling. Two distinct E2P states exist in NKA: E2P^ATP (formed by ATP and Mg2+ in the forward reaction) and E2P^Pi·Mg2+ (formed by inorganic phosphate and Mg2+ in the backward reaction). These differ primarily by the presence of a Mg2+ ion at transmembrane site M in E2P^Pi·Mg2+, which confers K+ insensitivity in dephosphorylation. The crystal structure of the NKA E2·BeF3- complex (E2P ground state analog) free of CTS — together with eight CTS-bound structures — reveals that the E2P state contains a preformed extracellular cavity that serves as the CTS binding site. CTSs bind via conformational selection without inducing large structural changes. The cavity is negatively charged (six Asp/Glu residues) and wide open in E2P^Pi·Mg2+ but closed in E2·MgF4^2-·2K+. The E2P state is structurally related to the E2:BeF3- state of [SERCA1a (Sarcoplasmic Reticulum Ca2+-ATPase)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/serca1a/) at the cytoplasmic domains. Phosphorylation can be induced by ATP (type I phosphoenzyme) or by inorganic phosphate (type II phosphoenzyme). The E2·MgF₄²⁻ structure (2.3 Å, PDB 1WPG) from SERCA1a provides an atomic model for the E2·Pi state immediately after aspartylphosphate hydrolysis, showing the TGES motif of the A-domain coordinating the phosphate analogue and positioning a water molecule for in-line attack.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Na+,K+-ATPase (Pig Kidney) — E2P state formed by Pi-induced phosphorylation in the presence of Mg2+; structure determined at 4.6 A resolution (PDB 3N23); [Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/) binds with high affinity; A domain rotates, alphaM1-2 move towards inhibitor
- [SERCA1a (Sarcoplasmic Reticulum Ca2+-ATPase)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/serca1a/) — E2P state mimicked by E2:BeF3- complex (PDB 3B9B); structural comparison reveals similar cytoplasmic domain arrangement; phosphate analog BeF3- binds to the aspartate side chain at the phosphorylation site
- Shark Na+,K+-ATPase — [Cryo-Electron Microscopy](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/cryo-em/) structures of E2P^ATP (3.4 A) and E2P^Pi·Mg2+ (3.9 A) reveal distinct Mg2+ occupancy at transmembrane site M; E2P^ATP has ATP bridging A and N domains; E2P^Pi·Mg2+ has Mg2+ at site M conferring K+ insensitivity
- [Na+,K+-ATPase (Pig Kidney)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/na-k-atpase-pig-kidney/) — Crystal structure of E2·BeF3- (E2P ground state analog) free of CTS reveals a preformed cavity for CTS binding; eight CTS-bound structures show conformational selection mechanism (Kanai et al., PNAS 2020)
