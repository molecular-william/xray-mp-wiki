# CAAX Protein Processing

## Overview
CAAX protein processing is a series of post-translational modifications of the C-terminal CAAX motif (C = cysteine, A = aliphatic amino acid, X = any amino acid) that localizes ~120 mammalian CAAX proteins — including the Ras superfamily of small GTPases, nuclear lamins, the γ-subunit of heterotrimeric GTPases, and several protein kinases and phosphatases — to cellular membranes. The three-step pathway involves: (1) prenylation of the cysteine residue by farnesyltransferase or geranylgeranyltransferase, (2) proteolytic cleavage of the AAX tripeptide by Rce1 or ZMPSTE24, and (3) methylation of the α-carboxyl group of the prenylated cysteine by isoprenylcysteine carboxyl methyltransferase (ICMT).

## Mechanism/Details
The CAAX motif undergoes sequential processing: First, the cysteine thiol is prenylated by farnesyltransferase (C15 farnesyl) or geranylgeranyltransferase (C20 geranylgeranyl). Second, the AAX tripeptide is cleaved by an endoprotease — Rce1 (the major CAAX protease) or ZMPSTE24 (specific for prelamin A). The crystal structure of MmRce1 reveals how the farnesyl lipid binds at an opening between TM2 and TM4 of the transmembrane protease, positioning the scissile bond adjacent to a glutamate-activated nucleophilic water molecule. Third, the newly exposed prenylated cysteine is methylated by ICMT. These modifications increase the hydrophobicity of the C-terminus, promoting membrane association.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- MmRce1 (Methanococcus maripaludis Rce1) — Crystal structure at 2.5 Å resolution reveals the catalytic mechanism of CAAX processing: a conical cavity accommodates the farnesylated peptide with E140 and H173 activating a nucleophilic water for AAX cleavage
- Human RCE1 — Homology model predicts a longer TM4 helix providing more extensive hydrophobic surface for geranylgeranylated CAAX substrates
