# Rotary ATPase Mechanism

## Overview
Rotary ATPases (F-type, V-type, A-type) use a rotary catalytic mechanism to synthesize or hydrolyze ATP. The rotation is driven by proton or ion gradients across membranes. The mechanism involves conformational changes in the catalytic subunits that drive ATP synthesis or hydrolysis. ## Mechanism/Details
Rotary ATPases (F-type, V-type, A-type) use a rotary catalytic mechanism to synthesize or hydrolyze ATP. The rotation is driven by proton or ion gradients across membranes. The mechanism involves conformational changes in the catalytic subunits that drive ATP synthesis or hydrolysis. The 1994 crystal structure of bovine F1-ATPase at 2.8 A resolution provided the first direct structural evidence for this mechanism, showing the three catalytic beta-subunits in different conformations and proposing rotation of the alpha3beta3 subassembly around the central gamma-subunit helix. The 1996 structure of F1-ATPase with efrapeptin showed that inhibitor binding to the beta-E subunit blocks the rotary cycle by preventing beta-E from adopting a nucleotide-binding conformation. The 1999 structure of the yeast F1c10 subcomplex at 3.9 A (Stock, Leslie, Walker) revealed that the c-ring rotor contains 10 subunits, establishing the symmetry mismatch between the 3-fold F1 head and 10-fold c-ring that is essential for smooth rotation. Subsequent structures of c-ring rotors from various organisms, including the spinach chloroplast c14 ring at 3.8 A (2009), revealed that the distance between conserved carboxylates involved in ion transport is 10.6-10.8 A regardless of c-ring stoichiometry, suggesting a conserved gear distance in all rotary ATPases.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Bovine F1-ATPase-Efrapeptin Complex](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/bovine-f1-atpase-efrapeptin-complex/) — Efrapeptin traps the empty beta-E subunit in the central cavity, preventing the conformational changes required for gamma-subunit rotation and supporting the rotary mechanism
- [Spinach Chloroplast c14 Rotor Ring](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/spinach-chloroplast-c14-rotor-ring/) — Crystal structure of the c14 ring from proton-driven ATP synthase reveals 14-fold symmetry and conserved 10.6-10.8 A spacing of proton-binding carboxylates, supporting the universal gear mechanism in rotary ATPases
