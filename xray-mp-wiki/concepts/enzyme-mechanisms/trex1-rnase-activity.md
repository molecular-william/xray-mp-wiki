# TREX1 RNase Activity and DNA/RNA Hybrid Processing

## Overview
[Mouse TREX1 (Three Prime Repair Exonuclease 1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/trex1/) (Three prime repair exonuclease 1) functions not only as a DNase but also as an RNase, capable of digesting single-stranded RNA (ssRNA) and both strands of DNA/RNA hybrids, though with lower activity than its DNase activity. The molecular basis for this differential activity lies not in steric hindrance from the 2'-OH group, but in the 2'-OH-mediated intra-chain hydrogen bonding within RNA that increases the conformational rigidity of the RNA polymer chain, reducing the strength of TREX1-RNA interactions. [Mouse TREX1 (Three Prime Repair Exonuclease 1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/trex1/) does not process double-stranded RNA (dsRNA) even at high enzyme concentrations, distinguishing its substrate profile from related DEDDh exonucleases like RNase T.
