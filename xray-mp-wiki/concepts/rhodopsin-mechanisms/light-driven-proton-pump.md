# Light-Driven Proton Pump

## Overview
Light-driven proton pumps are microbial rhodopsins that use light energy to
translocate protons across the cell membrane, generating a proton motive
force used for ATP synthesis and other cellular work. Bacteriorhodopsin
from Halobacterium salinarum is the archetypal member. The mechanism
involves vectorial proton transfer through a network of internal water
molecules, key aspartate/glutamate residues, and a proton release channel
on the extracellular side.


## Mechanism/Details
Light-driven proton pumps have been structurally characterized from diverse
organisms. Bacteriorhodopsin (BR), archaerhodopsin-2 (aR2), cruxrhodopsin-3
(cR3), deltarhodopsin-3 (dR3), and schizorhodopsins (SzR) represent
different branches. Schizorhodopsins are unusual inward proton pumps that
transport protons from the extracellular to cytoplasmic side.

Proteorhodopsins are abundant light-driven proton pumps in marine bacteria,
where they contribute significantly to solar energy capture in the ocean
photic zone. The green-absorbing and blue-absorbing proteorhodopsin variants
are tuned by residue substitutions at position 105.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Bacteriorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/bacteriorhodopsin/) — Archetypal outward light-driven proton pump from H. salinarum; dozens of structures available in multiple photocycle states.
- [Archaerhodopsin-2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/archaerhodopsin-2/) — Proton pump from Halorubrum sp. aus-2; H32 crystal form at 2.3 Å.
- [Cruxrhodopsin-3](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/cruxrhodopsin-3/) — Proton pump from Halobacterium sp. aus-1 (Xenorhabdus); conserved paired glutamate proton release complex.
- [Schizorhodopsin (SzR4)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/schizorhodopsin/) — Inward light-driven H+ pump from Asgard archaea; 2.1 Å structure with distinct proton release mechanism.
