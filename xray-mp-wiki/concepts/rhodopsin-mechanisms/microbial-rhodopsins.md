# Microbial Rhodopsins

## Overview
Microbial rhodopsins are a family of seven-transmembrane helix proteins
found in haloarchaea and other microorganisms that bind a retinal
chromophore via a protonated Schiff base. They carry out two distinctly
different functions: light-driven ion transport (bacteriorhodopsin BR,
halorhodopsin HR) and photosensory signaling (sensory rhodopsins SRI,
SRII). Despite sharing a common photochemical reaction (light-induced
retinal isomerization) and a similar seven-helical bundle architecture,
these functional differences arise from variations in the intramolecular
channel and protein-protein interaction surfaces.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Sensory Rhodopsin II (NpSRII)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/sensory-rhodopsin-ii/) — Phototaxis receptor from Natronobacterium pharaonis; 2.4 A crystal structure revealed color tuning and transducer interaction mechanisms
- [Bacteriorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/bacteriorhodopsin/) — Light-driven proton pump from Halobacterium salinarum; archetypal microbial rhodopsin
- [Schizorhodopsin (SzR4)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/schizorhodopsin/) — Light-driven inward H+ pump from Asgard archaea; 2.1 A crystal structure revealed distinct untrapped H+ release mechanism
- [Channelrhodopsin C1C2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/channelrhodopsin-c1c2/) — Algal light-gated ion channel; chimera of channelrhodopsins 1 and 2 from Chlamydomonas reinhardtii
- [Pharaonis Halorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/pharaonis-halorhodopsin/) — Light-driven chloride pump from Natronobacterium pharaonis
