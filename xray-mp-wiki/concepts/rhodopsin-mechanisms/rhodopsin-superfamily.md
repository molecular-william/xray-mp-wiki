# Rhodopsin Superfamily

## Overview
The rhodopsin superfamily encompasses two major families of seven-transmembrane
(7-TM) retinal-binding proteins: microbial (type I) rhodopsins found in
archaea, bacteria, algae, and fungi, and animal (type II) rhodopsins found in
metazoan eyes. Despite sharing the same 7-TM architecture and using retinal
as a chromophore via a Schiff base linkage, these families have distinct
sequences, signaling mechanisms, and evolutionary origins. Type I rhodopsins
function as light-driven ion pumps, channels, and phototaxis sensors, while
type II rhodopsins are G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) involved in
visual phototransduction.


## Mechanism/Details
The rhodopsin superfamily has been a paradigm for understanding membrane protein
structure and function. Bovine rhodopsin (PDB 1F88) was the first GPCR
structure determined by X-ray crystallography. Bacteriorhodopsin (PDB 1BRD)
was among the first integral membrane protein structures solved. The
structural biology of rhodopsins has driven advances in time-resolved
crystallography, serial femtosecond crystallography at XFELs, and
spectroscopic techniques.
