# PII Signal Transduction Protein Family

## Overview
The PII signal transduction protein family is a widespread family of small trimeric regulatory proteins found in bacteria, archaea, and plants. PII proteins sense cellular carbon, nitrogen, and energy status through binding of adenyl nucleotides (ATP, ADP, AMP) and 2-oxoglutarate. They regulate a diverse range of targets including transporters, enzymes, and transcription factors. The hallmark feature of PII proteins is a flexible T-loop that mediates interactions with target proteins. The conformation of the T-loop is modulated by the type of bound nucleotide, providing a molecular switch that translates metabolic signals into regulatory output. Well-characterized examples include [GLNK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/glnk/) (regulation of AmtB ammonia channel in E. coli) and [SBTB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/sbtb/) (regulation of [SBTA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/sbta/) bicarbonate transporter in cyanobacteria).


## Mechanism/Details


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [GLNK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/glnk/) (E. coli) — [GLNK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/glnk/) regulates the AmtB ammonium channel in response to nitrogen status. Under nitrogen-sufficient conditions, [GLNK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/glnk/) binds ADP and inserts its T-loop into the AmtB pore, blocking ammonia conduction. Under nitrogen limitation, [GLNK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/glnk/) is uridylylated at Tyr51, which prevents T-loop insertion and relieves inhibition. This is the paradigmatic example of PII-mediated transporter regulation.
- [SBTB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/sbtb/) (Synechocystis sp. PCC 6803) — [SBTB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/sbtb/) regulates the [SBTA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/sbta/) bicarbonate transporter in the cyanobacterial CO2-concentrating mechanism. AMP binding stabilizes the T-loop in a conformation that inserts into the cytoplasmic cavity of [SBTA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/sbta/), locking it in an inward-facing state and inhibiting bicarbonate transport. cAMP binding distorts the T-loop and relieves inhibition. This couples bicarbonate uptake to cellular energy and carbon status.
- GlnB/PII (E. coli) — The archetypal PII protein GlnB regulates nitrogen assimilation by controlling the activity of adenylyltransferase (ATase), which modulates glutamine synthetase activity, and NtrB/NtrC two-component system, which controls transcription of nitrogen-regulated genes.
