# Dual Orexin Receptor Antagonist (DORA)

## Overview
Dual orexin receptor antagonists (DORAs) are therapeutic compounds that inhibit both orexin receptor subtypes, OX1R and OX2R, in the central nervous system. They are used to promote sleep by blocking orexin neuropeptide signalling, which regulates sleep-wake cycles. Suvorexant (Belsomra) is the first FDA-approved DORA for insomnia treatment.

## Mechanism/Details
DORAs bind to the orthosteric binding pocket of orexin receptors, stabilizing an inactive receptor conformation. They sterically inhibit inward motions of transmembrane domains required for activation and stabilize a network of extracellular salt bridges. Suvorexant adopts a pi-stacked horseshoe-like conformation deep in the orthosteric site, contacting all TM alpha-helices except TM1.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Human OX2 Orexin Receptor — Suvorexant is a DORA that binds both OX1R and OX2R with sub-nanomolar affinity. Its binding mode was revealed at 2.5 A resolution in the hOX2R-suvorexant co-crystal structure.
