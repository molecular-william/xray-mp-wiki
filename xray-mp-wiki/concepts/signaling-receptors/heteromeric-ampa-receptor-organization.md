# Heteromeric AMPA Receptor Organization

## Overview
Heteromeric AMPA-type glutamate receptors (AMPARs), composed of GluA1-4 subunits with GluA2 as the essential partner, adopt a distinct compact 'O-shape' architecture that differs substantially from GluA2 homomers. The N-terminal domain (NTD) layer forms a compact alternating arrangement around a central axis, the extracellular region undergoes vertical compression, and the NTD and [LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/) layers come into close contact. This architecture is reminiscent of NMDA receptors and may enable allosteric communication between layers. The O-shape NTD arrangement can transition to the classical N-shape, and both conformations are compatible with receptor function.


## Mechanism/Details
The NTD heterodimers associate to form a tetramer with alternating subunits (e.g., GluA2-GluA3-GluA2-GluA3). This compact O-arrangement is stabilized by three major contact regions between the upper lobes (ULs) of the NTDs. The compact NTD layer causes the [LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/) dimers to splay apart and fill the inter-layer cavity characteristic of GluA2 homomers, producing a vertically compressed receptor. Coarse-grained simulations revealed that low-energy normal modes can interconvert between N- and O-shapes, suggesting the receptor can dynamically sample both conformations. The compression brings NTD helices into contact with the [LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/), creating interfaces that could permit allosteric coupling.
