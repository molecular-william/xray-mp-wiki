# GPCR Dimerization

## Overview
GPCR dimerization refers to the formation of homo- and hetero-oligomeric complexes by G protein-coupled receptors, which can modulate receptor signaling, ligand binding cooperativity, and allosteric regulation. Dimerization is [NOW](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s-5-nitrowillardine/) recognized as a common feature of GPCR biology with important functional consequences.


## Mechanism/Details
GPCR dimerization occurs through specific transmembrane helix interfaces that vary between receptor types. The crystal structures of [Human CXCR4 Chemokine Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/cxcr4/) reveal a consistent homodimer interface involving helices V and VI, with mainly hydrophobic interactions. Key residues include Leu194(5.33), Val197(5.36), Val198(5.37), Phe201(5.40), Met205(5.44), and Leu210(5.49). A Trp195(5.34)-Leu267(6.63) contact involves side-chain stacking and a hydrogen bond. Polar interactions include a hydrogen-bonding network between Asn192 and Glu268 side chains. This dimer interface is distinct from previous models suggesting contacts through helix I or helices IV and V. Binding of bulky ligands such as [CVX15](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/cvx15/) can induce conformational changes in helix V that bring intracellular regions of opposing receptors into close contact, providing a structural basis for cooperative binding and allosteric modulation observed in chemokine receptor heterodimers.
