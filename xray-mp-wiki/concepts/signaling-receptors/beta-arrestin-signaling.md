## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Human Delta-Opioid Receptor (OPRD1) — Asn131 (3.35) mutations (Ala, Val) in the δ-OR allosteric sodium site augment constitutive beta-arrestin-mediated signaling, exceeding wild-type maximal activation by [DADLE](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dadle/). Asp95 (2.50), Asn310 (7.45), and Asn314 (7.49) mutations transform classical δ-OR antagonists ([Naltrindole](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/naltrindole/), [Naltriben](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/naltriben/), [BNTX](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/7-benzylidenenaltrexone/)) into potent beta-arrestin-biased agonists. This demonstrates that sodium-coordinating residues act as efficacy switches controlling beta-arrestin vs G-protein signaling bias.

- [Angiotensin II Type 1 Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/at1r/) — Beta-arrestin mediated signaling by [Angiotensin II Type 1 Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/at1r/) confers cardio-protective benefits. C-terminal tail residues Thr336Pro and Pro341His affect GPCR kinase-dependent phosphorylation required for beta-arrestin recruitment.
