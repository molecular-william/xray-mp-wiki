# Competitive Antagonism in Cys-Loop Receptors

## Overview
Competitive antagonism in Cys-loop receptors occurs when a ligand binds to the orthosteric (agonist-binding) site but fails to trigger channel opening, instead blocking access by agonists. The ELIC-ACh structure provides a structural paradigm for understanding competitive antagonism, where [Acetylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/acetylcholine/) (ACh) binds to the same aromatic cage as agonists but with weaker cation-pi and electrostatic interactions, resulting in insufficient conformational change to open the channel pore.


## Mechanism/Details
In the ELIC-ACh structure, ACh binds at the subunit interface in an aromatic cage formed by residues from loops A, B, C (principal side) and loops D, E, G (complementary side). ACh binding induces contraction of loop C (inward movement, unlike antagonist-induced outward movement in AChBPs), but the channel remains closed. The primary reason for ACh's inability to activate [ELIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/elic/) is weak cation-pi and electrostatic interactions in the binding pocket. The quaternary ammonium of ACh (with three methyl groups) has a more diffuse positive charge compared to primary ammonium, resulting in weaker electrostatic interactions with negatively charged residues (E77, E131) and aromatic residues. A simple substitution from quaternary ammonium (-N+(CH3)3) to tertiary ammonium (-NH+(CH3)2) converts ACh from a competitive antagonist to an agonist.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [ELIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/elic/) (Erwinia chrysanthemi Pentameric Ligand-Gated Ion Channel) — [Acetylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/acetylcholine/) binds to the [ELIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/elic/) orthosteric site as a competitive antagonist (apparent Kd ~2 mM). ACh binding induces loop C contraction and EC domain 'twist' motion similar to agonist binding, but the pore remains closed. The 2-dimethylaminoethylacetate (tertiary ammonium derivative of ACh) activates [ELIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/elic/) currents, demonstrating that the quaternary-to-tertiary substitution converts the ligand from antagonist to agonist.
