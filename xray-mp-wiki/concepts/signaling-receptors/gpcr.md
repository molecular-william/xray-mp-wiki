# G Protein-Coupled Receptor (GPCR) Family

## Overview
GPCRs are the largest family of cell surface receptors, characterized by seven transmembrane helices. They mediate cellular responses to diverse stimuli including hormones, neurotransmitters, and light. GPCRs activate heterotrimeric G proteins upon agonist binding, initiating downstream signaling cascades. They are major drug targets, with ~35% of FDA-approved drugs targeting this family. The first high-resolution GPCR structure was bovine rhodopsin, followed by the beta-2 adrenergic receptor.
