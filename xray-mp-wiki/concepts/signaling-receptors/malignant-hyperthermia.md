# Malignant Hyperthermia and Ryanodine Receptor Mutations

## Overview
Malignant hyperthermia is a genetic disorder associated with mutations in the [Ryanodine Receptor Type 1 (RyR1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/ry1/). The Repeat12 domain of RyR1 harbors five malignant hyperthermia mutations. These mutations affect various structural elements of the domain, including the U-lid and repeat helices. The R1043C mutation (R1044C in rabbit) affects a residue involved in multiple hydrogen bonds including main chain atoms of the U-lid, leading to destabilization. The G1049S mutation (G1050S in rabbit) is located in a tight loop where the main chain conformation is only allowed for [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) residues, causing misfolding during expression.
