# SPRY Domain in Ryanodine Receptors

## Overview
The SPRY domain is a protein domain named after 'SPIA kinase and Ryanodine Receptor' where it was first discovered. Ryanodine receptors contain three different SPRY domains (SPRY1, SPRY2, and SPRY3) located within residues 633-1600. In other proteins, SPRY domains have been implicated in protein-protein interactions, but in RyRs their function was not well defined at the time of the referenced study. High-resolution [Cryo-Electron Microscopy](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/cryo-em/) structures placed all three SPRY domains in the clamp region of the RyR cytosolic cap, but de novo tracing was not possible at those resolutions. Crystal structures of SPRY1 (1.2 A) and SPRY2 (previously reported) revealed beta-trefoil structures with finger substructures. The SPRY1 domain was shown to be a crucial contributor to [FKBP12 (FK506 Binding Protein 12)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/fkbp12/) binding, with the 675-loop containing a hydrophobic cluster critical for interaction.
