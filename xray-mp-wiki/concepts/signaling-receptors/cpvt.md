# Catecholaminergic Polymorphic Ventricular Tachycardia (CPVT) and Ryanodine Receptor

## Overview
Catecholaminergic polymorphic ventricular tachycardia (CPVT) is a genetic cardiac arrhythmia disorder associated with mutations in the [Ryanodine Receptor Type 2 (RyR2)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/ry2/). The Repeat12 domain of RyR2 harbors two CPVT mutations. CPVT is characterized by bidirectional ventricular tachycardia and polymorphic ventricular tachycardia triggered by adrenergic stress. The structural basis of CPVT mutations in RyR2 provides insight into how channel gating defects lead to arrhythmia susceptibility.
