# Stepwise Activation of Cholecystokinin Receptors

## Overview
The stepwise activation of cholecystokinin receptors (CCKₐR and CCKᴅR) describes the sequential conformational changes that occur as these class A GPCRs transition from an inactive antagonist-bound state through an agonist-bound intermediate to a fully active G protein-coupled state. By comparing the crystal structures of CCKₐR bound to antagonists (devazepide, lintitript), agonist (NN9056), and cryo-EM structures of CCKᴅR-gastrin-17-G protein complexes, a systematic model of receptor activation was proposed.

## Mechanism/Details
The stepwise activation process involves three main conformational transitions:

Step 1 — Inactive (antagonist-bound): CCKₐR bound to antagonists devazepide or lintitript represents the fully inactive state. The antagonists occupy the orthosteric binding pocket with an indole group positioned 1.1 Å from ECL3 compared to peptide agonists. The receptor lacks significant outward displacement of TM6, and the intracellular pocket for G protein binding is inaccessible.

Step 2 — Agonist-bound intermediate (partially active): Upon binding the peptide agonist NN9056, an approximately 1 Å inward shift of helix VI occurs in the ligand-binding region. The agonist's indole group (mimicking Trp4 of CCK-8) pushes deeper into the binding pocket compared to antagonists. Conformational changes are primarily observed at the extracellular half of the receptor, while the intracellular G protein coupling interface remains largely unchanged. This intermediate state is not sufficient to fully stabilize the active conformation — the CCKₐR-NN9056 structure is superimposable with antagonist-bound CCKₐR at the intracellular side, confirming that the full active state requires G protein coupling.

Step 3 — Fully active (G protein-coupled): In the CCKᴅR-gastrin-17-G protein complexes, the receptor adopts the fully active conformation with an approximately 6 Å outward movement of TM6 (measured at the intracellular tip). The ECL3 bends down by 2.9 Å (measured at Cα of G349), leading to a counterclockwise twisting of the extracellular regions of TM6 and TM7. This ECL3 movement allows deeper penetration of the agonist, pushing F330 toward W326 and inducing rearrangement of the PIF (Phe-Ile-Phe) motif and the FxxCWxP motif, ultimately opening the intracellular G protein binding cavity.

Key toggle residues: F330(W6.52), W326(W6.48), F218(W5.47), F322(W6.44), and F323(W6.45) form a conserved activation network. Antagonist indole groups are positioned 1.1 Å away from ECL3 compared to peptide agonists, suggesting that the ECL3 conformational change is the initial trigger. The small molecule agonist SR146131 also contains an indole group, suggesting this ECL3-mediated activation mechanism may apply broadly to CCKₐR agonists.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Human Cholecystokinin A Receptor (CCKₐR) — Crystal structures in antagonist-bound (devazepide/lintitript), agonist-bound (NN9056), and simulated G protein-bound states reveal stepwise activation. The agonist alone is insufficient for full activation — G protein coupling is required.
- Human Cholecystokinin B Receptor (CCKᴅR) — Cryo-EM structures with Gᵢ₂ and G₉ in complex with gastrin-17 represent the fully active state. Conformational changes include 6 Å TM6 outward movement, ECL3 bending, and rearrangement of PIF/FxxCWxP motifs.
