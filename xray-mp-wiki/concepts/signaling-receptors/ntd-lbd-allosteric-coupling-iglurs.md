# NTD-LBD Allosteric Coupling in Ionotropic Glutamate Receptors

## Overview
In heteromeric AMPA receptors, the compact O-shape architecture brings the N-terminal domain (NTD) and ligand-binding domain ([LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/)) into close contact, creating inter-layer interfaces that may permit allosteric signal transmission between these domains. This mechanism is well-established in NMDA receptors, where NTD ligands modulate receptor function through NTD-[LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/) coupling. The compressed architecture of GluA2/3 heteromers reveals NTD-[LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/) contact points that are more extensive in the pore-distal (BD/GluA3) chains, suggesting asymmetric allosteric potential.
