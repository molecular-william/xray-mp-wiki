# Membrane Recognition Helix

## Overview
A membrane recognition helix is an amphipathic alpha-helix that lies on the membrane surface with its hydrophobic face embedded in the lipid bilayer, acting as a membrane-targeting signal. The N-terminal amphipathic helix (N-AH) of YidC functions as such a recognition helix, with its N-terminus embedded in the membrane and the helix axis forming an angle of ~15° with the membrane surface.

## Mechanism/Details
Amphipathic helices have segregated polar and hydrophobic residues. The hydrophobic face interacts with the lipid bilayer interior while the polar face contacts the aqueous environment or membrane surface. The tilt angle (~15° for YidC N-AH) allows optimal hydrophobic matching. The recognition helix ensures proper localization and orientation of the protein at the membrane.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- TmYidC (Thermotoga maritima YidC) — N-AH (residues 1-19) lies on periplasmic membrane surface at ~15° angle, N-terminus embedded in bilayer
- EcYidC (Escherichia coli YidC) — N-terminal helix functions as uncleaved signal sequence and recognition helix for Sec translocon interaction
