# Claudin Tight Junction Architecture

## Overview
Claudins are the major structural components of tight junctions (TJs), forming continuous intramembrane strands that seal the intercellular space between epithelial cells. The crystal structure of mouse claudin-15 (mCldn15) at 2.4 Å resolution revealed the characteristic tetraspanin fold: a four-helix transmembrane bundle with two extracellular β-sheet segments (ECS1 and ECS2) forming a β-sheet domain. The structure shows that claudin protomers assemble into linear polymers through conserved hydrophobic interactions, providing the molecular basis for tight junction strand formation.


## Mechanism/Details
Each claudin protomer adopts a four-transmembrane-helix left-handed bundle (TM1-TM4). The extracellular domain comprises two segments: ECS1 (β1-β4 strands) and ECS2 (β5 strand), which together form a five-stranded antiparallel β-sheet. The conserved W-GLW-C-C motif anchors the β-sheet to the membrane surface, with Trp29, Leu48, and Trp49 forming a hydrophobic anchor, and Cys52- Cys62 forming an intramolecular disulfide bond. In the crystal lattice, protomers form linear polymers via a key hydrophobic interaction: Met68 (from ECH) of one protomer inserts into a pocket formed by Phe146, Phe147, and Leu158 of the adjacent protomer. Freeze-fracture EM confirmed that this interface is essential for TJ strand formation in cells. The extracellular surface features a charged palm region that likely forms the paracellular ion pathway, with charge distribution determining ion selectivity.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Mouse Claudin-15 (mCldn15) — First claudin family structure solved at 2.4 Å. The mCldn15 construct was truncated (33 residues removed from C-terminus) and had membrane-proximal cysteines substituted with alanines. Crystallized in LCP, solved by SeMet SAD phasing. PDB ID: 4P79.

- Mouse Claudin-19 (mCldn19) — Related claudin structure solved in complex with C-CPE enterotoxin at 3.7 Å. Both structures reveal the conserved tetraspanin fold but mCldn19 has a longer β5 strand enabling more hydrogen bonds with the adjacent β1 strand. The loop between β1 and β2, disordered in mCldn15, is resolved in the mCldn19-C-CPE complex.
