# RCK Domain Activation Mechanism

## Overview
The RCK (Regulator of K+ conductance) domain activation mechanism describes how ligand binding to cytoplasmic RCK domains triggers conformational changes that control the gating of potassium channels and transporters. RCK domains form octameric gating rings that undergo ligand-dependent structural transitions from inactive to activated states, coupling cytoplasmic ligand binding to transmembrane pore opening.


## Mechanism/Details
The RCK domain activation mechanism follows a multi-step pathway. In potassium channels such as [MTHK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/mthk/) and the human BK channel, two tandem RCK domains per subunit assemble into a tetrameric gating ring at the intracellular membrane surface. In the BK channel, the Ca2+ bowl sequence within RCK2 creates Ca2+ binding sites at the assembly interface between RCK domains, on the outer perimeter of the gating ring. Ca2+ binding induces conformational changes that are transmitted through linker regions to the transmembrane pore domain, opening the ion conduction pathway. The flexible interface between tandem RCK domains (alphaF-alphaG/alphaS-alphaT) mediates the conformational change, analogous to the [MTHK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/mthk/) channel where RCK dimers undergo Ca2+-induced reorientation. A notable evolutionary difference exists: in [MTHK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/mthk/), Ca2+ binds at the flexible interface, while in BK, Ca2+ binds at the assembly interface, reflecting the adaptation of tandem nonidentical RCK domains in eukaryotic channels.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [MTHK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/mthk/) channel — Prokaryotic RCK gating ring with Ca2+ binding at the flexible interface
- Human BK (Slo1) channel — Eukaryotic tandem RCK domains with Ca2+ binding at the assembly interface via the Ca2+ bowl
