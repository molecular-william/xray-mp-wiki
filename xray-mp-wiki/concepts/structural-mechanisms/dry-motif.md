# DRY Motif (Asp-Arg-Tyr)

## Overview
The DRY motif (Asp-Arg-Tyr) is a highly conserved sequence motif located at the cytoplasmic end of transmembrane helix 3 (TM3) in class A G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs). This motif serves as a critical structural microswitch that couples agonist binding to G protein activation. The arginine residue (R3.50) in the DRY motif forms an "ionic lock" with a glutamate residue on TM6 (E6.30) in the inactive state, stabilizing the receptor in its off conformation.


## Mechanism/Details
In the inactive state, the Arg (R3.50) of the DRY motif forms a salt bridge (ionic lock) with Glu (E6.30) on TM6 and additional interactions with Asp (D3.49) and nearby residues on TM3 and TM5. Upon agonist binding, TM6 undergoes an outward movement that breaks the ionic lock, allowing the DRY motif to rearrange and form interactions with the G protein at the intracellular face. The Tyr (Y3.51) residue participates in a rotamer toggle switch that helps propagate the activation signal. This DRY-mediated conformational change exposes binding sites for G proteins and arrestins, making it essential for GPCR signaling.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Beta2-Adrenergic Receptor — The DRY motif (D130-R131-Y132 in human beta2AR) forms the canonical ionic lock with E268 on TM6, demonstrated by the inactive-state crystal structure (PDB 2RH1)
- Rhodopsin — The DRY motif (E134-R135-Y136 in bovine rhodopsin) is a key component of the activation switch, with R135 interacting with E247 on TM6
