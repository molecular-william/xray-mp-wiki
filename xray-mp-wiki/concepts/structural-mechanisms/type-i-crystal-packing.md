# Type I Crystal Packing

## Overview
Type I crystal packing is a crystallographic arrangement characteristic of membrane protein crystals grown from lipidic cubic phase. In this packing, 3D crystals grow as stacked 2D crystals (multi-layered planar lattices), with molecules arranged in stacked anti-parallel planes. The solvent region is divided into lipidic and aqueous regions, where the lipidic region occupies the larger volume.

## Mechanism/Details
In type I packing, membrane proteins diffuse within the curved bilayers of the lipidic cubic phase and incorporate into planar lattices that associate to form highly ordered 3D crystals. The membrane bilayer lies parallel to the ab plane of the unit cell. Adjacent 2D bilayers are orientated anti-parallel to each other. This packing contrasts with type II packing typical of detergent-grown crystals, where molecules are surrounded by detergent micelles. A key advantage of type I packing is that the location of the membrane bilayer within crystals can be assigned with confidence.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Rhodobacter sphaeroides reaction centre (PDB 1ogv) — First type I crystal packing reported for a purple bacterial reaction centre. Crystals grown from monoolein cubic phase, space group P4(2)2(1)2. Strongest crystal contacts mediated by cytoplasmic domains of the H-subunit and by a cardiolipin molecule at membrane-membrane interfaces.
- Bacteriorhodopsin (PDB 1C3W) — Type I packing in space group P63. All molecules orientated vectorially along the c direction. Can lead to hemihedral twinning due to weak contacts along this direction.
- Sensory rhodopsin II (pSRII) — Type I packing in space group C2221. Molecules exhibit alternate directionality within the plane, preventing hemihedral twinning.
