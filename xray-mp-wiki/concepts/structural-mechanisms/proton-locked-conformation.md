# Proton-Locked Conformation

## Overview
The proton-locked conformation is a stable structural state of the conserved glutamate residue (Glu62 in Spirulina platensis c-ring) in proton-dependent F-type ATP synthase c-rings. In this conformation, the glutamate carboxyl group is stabilized by an intricate hydrogen bond network that prevents it from being exposed to the hydrophobic lipid environment, ensuring that protons are only exchanged at the a-c subunit interface.


## Mechanism/Details
The proton-locked conformation is characterized by a precise hydrogen bond network around the conserved glutamate (Glu62). The Oepsilon2 oxygen acts as both a hydrogen bond acceptor (from Gln29 Ne2 on the same subunit) and a donor (to the backbone carbonyl of Phe60 on the neighboring subunit), breaking the alpha-helical hydrogen bonding pattern. The Oepsilon1 oxygen accepts a hydrogen bond from Tyr67 hydroxyl on the adjacent subunit. This network locks the protonated glutamate in an orientation directed toward the groove between two adjacent outer alpha-helices, preventing exposure to hydrophobic lipid alkyl chains.
