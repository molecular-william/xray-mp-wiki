# CpE-Induced Tight Junction Disassembly

## Overview
Clostridium perfringens enterotoxin (CpE) causes tight junction (TJ) disassembly by binding to specific claudin receptors through its C-terminal receptor-binding domain (cCpE). The enterotoxin uses its N-terminal cytotoxic domain to form a pore complex in the plasma membrane, while the cCpE domain binds to claudin extracellular segments, disrupting both cis (side-by-side) and trans (head-to-head) interactions between claudin protomers that are essential for TJ strand formation and maintenance.


## Mechanism/Details
CpE binding to claudins involves two key mechanisms: (1) Trans disruption: CpE binds with high affinity (Kd ~3-5 nM) to the NPLVA motif on claudin ECS2, physically blocking trans interactions between claudins from opposing cell membranes. (2) Cis disruption: CpE binding to both ECS1 and ECS2 of the claudin causes conformational changes that destabilize the cis interaction epitope. Crystal structures of hCLDN-9 with cCpE reveal two conformers: a closed form where cCpE sits deep in the claudin palm (breaking 80% of cis interatomic bonds), and an open form where cCpE rocks outward as a lever (abolishing all cis interactions). This lever-like motion provides a mechanism for physically breaking established TJs. Both mechanisms operate without steric clash between adjacent cCpE molecules.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Human Claudin-9 (hCLDN-9) — Crystal structures at 3.2/3.3 A reveal cCpE binding to both ECS1 and ECS2. CpE binds with Kd = 5.1 nM. The NPLVA153 motif on ECS2 is critical for binding. CpE is cytotoxic to hCLDN-9-expressing cells (59.2% viability decrease).

- Mouse Claudin-19 (mCldn19) — Structure at 3.7 A with [C-CPE (Clostridium perfringens Enterotoxin)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/c-cpe/) reveals both ECS1 and ECS2 contact the toxin. The conserved ECS1 motif (A/N/S)-I-(I/L/V)-(T/V) and ECS2 motif NP(V/L)(V/L)(P/A) are both involved in binding. Mutagenesis confirms both motifs are important.
