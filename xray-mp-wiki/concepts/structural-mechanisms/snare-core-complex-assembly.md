# SNARE Core Complex Assembly and Layer Architecture

## Overview
SNARE (SNAP Receptor) core complexes are four-helix bundles that mediate membrane fusion. The autophagic [SNARE Complex](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/snare-complex/) formed by [STX17](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/syntaxin17/) (Qa), [SNAP29 (Synaptosomal-Associated Protein 29)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/snap29/) (Qb+Qc), and [VAMP8 (Vesicle-Associated Membrane Protein 8)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/vamp8/) (R-SNARE) reveals the canonical SNARE architecture: four parallel alpha-helices with an interior formed by 16 layers of interacting side chains. Layer 0 is the conserved hydrophilic layer (three glutamines and one arginine), while the other 15 layers are mostly hydrophobic. The N-terminal and central layers are critical for complex assembly. The complex has a melting temperature of ~85 C and exhibits characteristic unfolding-refolding hysteresis.


## Mechanism/Details
[SNARE Complex](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/snare-complex/) assembly proceeds from the N-terminal membrane-distal end toward the C-terminal membrane-proximal end (zippering). The layer architecture: - Layer -7 to -1: N-terminal layers, mostly hydrophobic - Layer 0: Hydrophilic layer (Qa-Qb-Qc-R) - Layer +1 to +8: C-terminal layers, increasingly polar Mutagenesis of autophagic [SNARE Complex](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/snare-complex/) layers showed that disrupting the -7th layer (W172Q of [STX17](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/syntaxin17/)), -5th layer (L179Q of [STX17](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/syntaxin17/) or L213Q of [SNAP29 (Synaptosomal-Associated Protein 29)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/snap29/)), or 0-layer (Q196L of [STX17](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/syntaxin17/) or Q230L of [SNAP29 (Synaptosomal-Associated Protein 29)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/snap29/)) essentially abolishes complex formation, while C-terminal layer mutations (A210Q layer +4, L221Q layer +7) only partially weaken assembly. Two highly negatively charged surface patches are implicated in binding the NSF-mediated SNARE disassembly machinery.
