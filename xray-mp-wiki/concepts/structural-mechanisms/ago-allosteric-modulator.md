# Ago-Allosteric Modulator

## Overview
An ago-allosteric modulator is a ligand that both activates a receptor (agonist activity) and binds to a site distinct from the orthosteric (endogenous ligand) binding site (allosteric modulation). TAK-875 (fasiglifam) is characterized as an ago-allosteric modulator of the human GPR40 receptor, binding to a non-canonical pocket between TM3-5 and ECL2 that is distinct from the canonical orthosteric site.

## Mechanism/Details
Ago-allosteric modulators exert their effects by binding to an allosteric site and inducing conformational changes that lead to receptor activation. In the case of TAK-875 binding to hGPR40, the ligand binds to a site roughly perpendicular to the canonical orthosteric site observed between TM3 and TM5-7 in other GPCRs. The compound acts as a partial agonist, stimulating glucose-dependent insulin secretion without the full efficacy of natural ligands like long-chain fatty acids.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Human GPR40 (FFAR1) — TAK-875 binds to a non-canonical allosteric binding pocket between TM3-5 and ECL2 of hGPR40, functioning as an ago-allosteric partial agonist that enhances glucose-dependent insulin secretion.
