# Allosteric Mechanism of Aspartate Carbamoyltransferase

## Overview
Aspartate carbamoyltransferase (ATCase) from Escherichia coli is the archetypal allosteric enzyme, catalyzing the committed step in pyrimidine biosynthesis. It exhibits positive cooperativity toward both substrates (carbamoyl phosphate and [L-Aspartate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/substrates/l-aspartate/)), feedback inhibition by CTP, and activation by ATP. High-resolution crystal structures of the R-state enzyme ligated with phosphonoacetamide, malonate, and either ATP or CTP were determined at 2.8-Å resolution, revealing the structural basis of heterotropic allosteric regulation by nucleotide effectors. ATCase is a soluble dodecameric enzyme (not a membrane protein), but serves as a classic model system for understanding allosteric regulation through X-ray crystallography.


## Mechanism/Details
ATCase is composed of two catalytic trimers and three regulatory dimers arranged with D3 symmetry. The enzyme exists in two quaternary states: a tense (T) state with low substrate affinity, and a relaxed (R) state with high substrate affinity. The T-to-R transition involves a 12-Å separation of the catalytic trimers along the 3-fold axis and a 15° rotation of each regulatory dimer about the 2-fold axes. CTP inhibits by stabilizing the T state, reducing the separation between catalytic trimers by ~0.5 Å and shifting the quaternary structure toward the T conformation. ATP activates by stabilizing the R state. The allosteric nucleotide binding site is located on the regulatory dimers, involving residues from the allosteric domain. Key interactions include: the pyrimidine ring of CTP hydrogen bonds to main-chain carbonyls of Ile12 and Tyr89, the amino group of Lys60, and the NH group of Ile12; the purine ring of ATP makes analogous interactions with the same residues. The triphosphate groups of both nucleotides interact with Lys94 and His20. The binding of CTP causes the base subsite to contract, while ATP causes it to expand, altering the N-terminal interactions between regulatory chains and propagating conformational changes to the active site ~60 Å away.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Aspartate Carbamoyltransferase (E. coli) — Crystal structures of the R-state enzyme with phosphonoacetamide, malonate, and either CTP or ATP were determined at 2.8-Å resolution (space group P321). CTP binding reduces catalytic trimer separation by ~0.5 Å, shifting the quaternary structure toward the T state. ATP binding induces no significant quaternary change. The allosteric binding site accommodates both pyrimidine (CTP) and purine (ATP) bases through a relatively flexible binding pocket with fewer direct hydrogen bonds compared to the active site, enabling recognition of ligands of different sizes.
