# DRD2 Extended Binding Pocket (DRD2-EBP)

## Overview
The [DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/) extended binding pocket (DRD2-[EBP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/human-ebp/)) refers to a set of binding pocket
extensions in the D2 [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) receptor that distinguish it from other D2-like
receptor subtypes (DRD3 and [DRD4](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/d4-dopamine-receptor/)). Two types of extended binding pockets have
been identified: the original DRD2-[EBP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/human-ebp/) defined by the risperidone-bound structure
(PDB 6C38), and a second extended binding pocket (SEBP) revealed by the
haloperidol-bound structure (2020). The SEBP is formed by TM2, TM3, EL1, and
EL2, and directly interacts with [Haloperidol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/haloperidol/)'s chlorobenzene moiety. Phe110^3.28
is the key residue controlling subtype selectivity — its size determines binding
affinity of DRD2-preferring compounds. The SEBP plays a critical role in [DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/)
activation, and structure-based drug design exploiting the SEBP led to highly
selective [DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/) agonists.


## Mechanism/Details
The DRD2-[EBP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/human-ebp/) was originally identified in the risperidone-bound structure
(PDB 6C38) and is defined by residues Val/Phe^2.61, Trp100^EL1, Phe/Leu^3.28,
and Tyr/Val^7.35. This pocket extends toward the extracellular part of TM VII.

The second extended binding pocket (SEBP) was discovered in the
DRD2-[Haloperidol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/haloperidol/) structure. It is formed by TM2, TM3, EL1, and EL2 and
partially overlaps with the DRD3 [EBP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/human-ebp/). The outward movement of EL2 creates
additional space for the SEBP at [DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/) not present in DRD3. Mutagenesis
studies showed that Phe110^3.28 is the key selectivity determinant: F110A
enhances [Haloperidol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/haloperidol/) binding 15.33-fold and L-741626 binding 144.18-fold,
while F110W/F110Y mutations reduce binding 28-57-fold. The alanine/leucine
substitution makes additional space for the chlorobenzene moiety.

The SEBP is critical for both antagonist binding and agonist activation.
The O4LE6-8 compounds lose agonist activity at the F110A mutant, confirming
that contact between these ligands and Phe110^3.28 in the SEBP facilitates
[DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/) activation. Based on this mechanism, highly subtype-selective [DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/)
agonists (O4SE6 and O8LE6) were designed that show no activity at DRD3 or
[DRD4](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/d4-dopamine-receptor/).
