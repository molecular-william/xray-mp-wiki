# Cholesterol Binding in Tetraspanins

## Overview
Cholesterol binding in tetraspanins is a structural and functional interaction between cholesterol and tetraspanin transmembrane proteins that modulates tetraspanin lateral interactions, membrane organization, and signaling function. Tetraspanins are a family of four-transmembrane-domain proteins that organize membrane microdomains (tetraspanin-enriched microdomains, TEMs) and have been shown to contain specific cholesterol-binding sites that influence their membrane-organization properties.


## Mechanism/Details
Structural studies of CD81 and other tetraspanins have revealed cholesterol hemisuccinate (CHS)-binding sites between transmembrane helices. In the CD81 structure, CHS binds in a pocket formed by TM1 and TM3, with the sterol ring system packed against hydrophobic side chains and the succinate moiety interacting with polar residues near the membrane interface. The cholesterol-binding site is conserved among tetraspanins and is distinct from the cholesterol-binding motifs (CRAC, CARC) found in other membrane proteins. Cholesterol binding stabilizes tetraspanin-tetraspanin interactions and promotes the assembly of TEMs, which serve as signaling platforms for integrins, growth factor receptors, and immune receptors.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- CD81 — Crystal structure of full-length CD81 (PDB 5M9H) reveals cholesterol hemisuccinate bound between TM1 and TM3; demonstrates the structural basis for cholesterol-tetraspanin interaction
- Claudin-15 — Related four-TM protein where cholesterol binding modulates tight junction assembly, though the binding mode differs from tetraspanins
