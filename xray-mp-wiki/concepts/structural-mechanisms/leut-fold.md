# LeuT Fold

## Overview
The LeuT fold is a conserved structural architecture shared by the
neurotransmitter/sodium symporter (NSS) family and several other secondary
active transporter families. It was first defined by the crystal structure of
the leucine transporter LeuT from Aquifex aeolicus (PDB 2A65, 1.65 Å). The
fold consists of 10 transmembrane helices (TM1-10) arranged in a pseudosymmetrical
inverted repeat of two 5-helix bundles (TM1-5 and TM6-10), a topology that
creates a central substrate-binding site at the interface between the two
bundles.


## Mechanism/Details
The LeuT fold has been identified in numerous transporter families:
NSS (neurotransmitter/sodium symporters), SLC6 family, amino acid
transporters, betaine transporters (BetP), and some multidrug transporters.
The fold has also been found in eukaryotic transporters such as the
Drosophila dopamine transporter (dDAT) and human serotonin transporter
(SERT). All members share the hallmark inverted 5+5 TM topology and
unwound TM1/TM6 regions forming the substrate-binding pocket.
