# Cross-Protomer Interactions in Proteorhodopsin Oligomers

## Overview
Proteorhodopsins (PRs) form oligomeric assemblies (hexamers or pentamers) in which protomers interact through a conserved cross-protomer hydrogen bond between His75 of one protomer and Trp34 of a neighboring protomer. This inter-protomer interaction is unique among [Microbial Rhodopsins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/rhodopsin-mechanisms/microbial-rhodopsins/) and extends the counterion environment of the primary proton acceptor Asp97. The Trp34 side chain stabilizes His75 in a conformation that favors a deprotonated Asp97 in the dark state, facilitating proton transfer during the photocycle. Functional studies show that disruption of this interaction (W34D mutation) reverses proton transport direction, confirming a physiological role for oligomeric assembly.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Med12BPR (hexamer) — C6 hexameric ring with His75-Trp34 hydrogen bonds between all neighboring protomers in the ring. His75 accepts a hydrogen bond from Trp34 (NE2-NE1 distance 2.98-3.15 A) and donates to Asp97 (2.90 A).
- HOT75BPR D97N (pentamer) — C5 pentameric ring with His75-Trp34 hydrogen bond between all neighboring protomer pairs (chains A/B, B/C, C/D, D/E, E/A). Mutation of Asp97 to Asn eliminates His75-Asp97 interaction.
