# Pore-Forming Toxins

## Overview
Pore-forming toxins (PFTs) are a large class of bacterial virulence factors that disrupt the plasma membrane of host cells by forming transmembrane pores. More than 25% of all cytotoxic bacterial proteins are PFTs, found in virtually all important classes of pathogens including Gram-negative bacteria (*Escherichia coli*, *Aeromonas hydrophila*, *Serratia marcescens*, *Vibrio cholerae*) and Gram-positive pathogens (*Staphylococcus aureus*, *Streptococcus pneumoniae*, *Listeria monocytogenes*). PFTs are classified as alpha-PFTs or beta-PFTs based on the secondary structure of the membrane-spanning region. alpha-PFTs use a ring of amphipathic helices to construct the pore, while beta-PFTs use a beta-barrel. PFTs oligomerize on the membrane surface into ring-shaped structures that insert into the lipid bilayer, resulting in ion flux changes and cell lysis.


## Mechanism/Details
All PFTs are produced as a soluble, generally monomeric form, which recognises the target cell by binding to specific receptors, concentrating the proteins at the membrane surface, before exposure of transmembrane hydrophobic regions, oligomerization, and membrane insertion. Among alpha-PFTs, the ClyA family shows a conserved structural mechanism. Single-component ClyA from E. coli undergoes a dramatic conformational change involving 80% of residues as the soluble subunit rearranges to expose membrane-spanning hydrophobic residues. The tripartite AhlABC system from A. hydrophila employs a more complex assembly: AhlC inserts into a single leaflet of the target bilayer, AhlB undergoes a bi-fold hinge mechanism to transition from soluble to pore form, and AhlA provides the hydrophilic pore lining. The SmhABC system from Serratia marcescens follows a similar mechanism with SmhC inserting into the membrane, SmhB undergoing beta-tongue to extended helix refolding, and SmhA providing the pore lining. PFTs can be classified by the number of components: single (ClyA), bipartite (YaxAB, XaxAB), and tripartite (AhlABC, NheABC, HblABC, SmhABC).
