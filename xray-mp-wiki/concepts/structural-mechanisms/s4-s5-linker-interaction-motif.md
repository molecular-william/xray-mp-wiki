# S4-S5 Linker Interaction Motif

## Overview
The S4-S5 linker interaction motif is a three-way structural interaction in voltage-gated sodium channels that couples voltage-sensor activation to pore opening and gate regulation. It involves the conserved tryptophan residue (W77 in NavMs) in the S3 helix, specific residues in the S4-S5 interdomain linker (R118, Q122), and the C-terminal EEE motif (E229, E230, E231) at the end of the extended S6 helix. This motif was identified in the first complete open-state structure of a sodium channel and provides a physical mechanism for stabilizing the open conformation and regulating opening/closing transitions.


## Mechanism/Details
The interaction motif operates through two principal interaction pairs. First, R118 and Q122 in the S4-S5 linker interact with W77 in the S3 helix — R118 via aliphatic stacking and Q122 via a hydrogen bond. W77 is fully conserved among prokaryotic and human sodium channel isoforms and domains, but not in other ion channel families such as potassium or TPC channels. Second, E229 in the EEE motif forms a salt bridge with R119 in the S4-S5 linker, while E230 and E231 form hydrogen bonds with N238 and H237, respectively, in the random-coil region leading to the C-terminal coiled-coil. Mutational analysis of W77 shows that substitutions to Tyr or Phe reduce the energy required to activate the channel by approximately 3 kcal mol-1 and shift voltage dependence, while Ala and Met mutations eliminate conductance entirely. The motif is proposed to form a physical means of stabilizing the open state and regulating the opening/closing/slow inactivation processes.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- **NavMs (PDB 5HVX)**: Full-length open-state structure reveals the complete interaction
  motif with W77-S3 interacting with R118/Q122 of the S4-S5 linker and E229/E230/E231
  of the C-terminal EEE motif. W77F and W77Y mutants show 17 and 13 mV positive shifts
  in voltage-dependent activation, respectively, with reduced current density.
- **NavAb**: Structural comparison shows the S4-S5 linker elbow mechanism in the
  pre-activated state, where the linker does not yet engage the full interaction motif
  but sets up the conformational change needed for pore opening.
- **Human Nav channels**: The W77 residue is fully conserved across all human sodium
  channel isoforms and domains, suggesting a universal role in gating, though direct
  structural comparison with eukaryotic channels is limited by the lack of complete
  eukaryotic sodium channel structures.
