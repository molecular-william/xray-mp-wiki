# Rocking-Bundle Mechanism

## Overview
The rocking-bundle mechanism is a transport mechanism in which a transporter undergoes rigid-body rotation of two domains around an axis approximately parallel to the membrane plane. This mechanism was first described for the LeuT-fold amino acid transporters, where the scaffold domain and substrate- binding domain rock relative to each other, alternately exposing the binding site to the extracellular and intracellular sides. In some transporters such as the SLC23 family, the rocking-bundle motion is combined with elevator-like movement of the substrate carrier domain.


## Mechanism/Details
The rocking-bundle mechanism involves coordinated conformational changes of transmembrane helices. In LeuT-fold transporters, the scaffold domain (TMs 1, 2, 6, 7) remains relatively static while the substrate-binding domain (TMs 3, 4, 5, 8, 9, 10) rocks around an axis. In SLC23 transporters, the gate domain (TMs 5-7 and 12-14) undergoes local rearrangements including bending of TM5 and TM12, rotation around the core-gate domain interface, and intra-domain shifts. The core domain (TMs 1-4 and 8-11) exhibits both translational and rotational movements relative to the dimeric gate domain.
