# Retinal Chromophore Conformation

## Overview
[Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) chromophore conformation in [Microbial Rhodopsins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/rhodopsin-mechanisms/microbial-rhodopsins/) is determined by the protein binding pocket environment. Wild-type opsins typically bind [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) in the all-trans planar conformation. Rational design of blue-shifted mutants can induce rotation of the beta-ionone ring to a twisted 6-s-cis conformation, which shrinks the pi-conjugated system and produces large blue shifts in absorption maximum (up to 100 nm). The 6-s-cis conformation was first revealed by the crystal structure of the [C1C2GA (C1C2 T198G/G202A)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/c1c2ga/) mutant (T198G/G202A) at 2.5 A resolution.

## Mechanism/Details
The [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) chromophore in [Microbial Rhodopsins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/rhodopsin-mechanisms/microbial-rhodopsins/) can adopt different conformations depending on the protein binding pocket environment. In the wild-type C1C2 chimera, [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) binds in the all-trans planar conformation, with the beta-ionone ring oriented by cavities created by conserved residues (Thr198, Gly202) that recognize the methyl groups at positions 16, 17, and 18 of the chromophore. In the blue-shifted [C1C2GA (C1C2 T198G/G202A)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/c1c2ga/) mutant (T198G/G202A), the reduced steric bulk at position 198 allows the beta-ionone ring to rotate out of plane, adopting a twisted 6-s-cis conformation with C6-C7 bond torsion of -27.7 degrees. This conformation is thermodynamically more stable by -1.1 kcal/mol than the planar 6-s-trans form in the mutant. The twisted 6-s-cis conformation shrinks the pi-conjugated system of the chromophore, producing a large blue shift in absorption maximum. This conformational change is the key mechanism underlying the rational design of blue-shifted microbial rhodopsin optogenetics tools.
