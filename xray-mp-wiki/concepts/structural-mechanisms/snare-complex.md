# SNARE Complex

## Overview
The SNARE (Soluble N-ethylmaleimide-sensitive factor Attachment protein REceptor) complex is a four-helix bundle protein-protein complex that mediates membrane fusion in eukaryotic cells. It is formed by the assembly of SNARE motifs from three or four SNARE proteins: a Qa-SNARE (syntaxin), a Qbc-SNARE (SNAP-25, which contributes two helices), and a R-SNARE (synaptobrevin/VAMP). The neuronal SNARE complex drives synaptic vesicle fusion with the plasma membrane during neurotransmitter release. The complex assembles in a zipper-like fashion from the N- to C-terminus, with the energy released during assembly thought to drive membrane merger.


## Mechanism/Details
SNARE complex assembly proceeds through conserved SNARE motifs, each forming one alpha-helix in the four-helix bundle. The core of the bundle contains stacked layers of interacting, mostly hydrophobic side chains that stabilize the complex. Assembly is initiated amino-terminally and proceeds towards the C-terminus in a zipper-like fashion, pulling the two membranes together. The final phase of assembly extends into the transmembrane regions, with the helical bundle continuing through the linkers and into the membrane. The energy released during zippering is thought to overcome the energy barrier of membrane fusion.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- **Neuronal SNARE complex**: Composed of syntaxin-1A (Qa-SNARE), SNAP-25A (Qbc-SNARE,
  contributing two helices), and synaptobrevin-2 (R-SNARE). The X-ray structure at
  3.4 A resolution revealed continuous helical extension from the SNARE core through
  the linkers into the transmembrane regions.
  [Syntaxin-1A](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/syntaxin-1a/)
  [SNAP-25A](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/snap-25a/)
  [Synaptobrevin-2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/synaptobrevin-2/)
- **Endosomal SNARE complex**: Composed of syntaxin-7 (Qa-SNARE), syntaxin-8 (Qb-SNARE),
  Vti1b (Qc-SNARE), and endobrevin/STXBP4 (R-SNARE). Contains all four transmembrane
  regions and is more stable than the corresponding core complex.
