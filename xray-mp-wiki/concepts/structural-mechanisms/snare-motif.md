# SNARE Motif

## Overview
The SNARE (Soluble N-ethylmaleimide-sensitive factor Attachment protein REceptor) motif is a conserved sequence of approximately 60-70 amino acids that forms an amphipathic alpha helix. SNARE motifs are the core structural elements of SNARE proteins, which mediate membrane fusion in intracellular vesicle trafficking, neurotransmitter release, and other fusion events. Four SNARE motifs (typically from three or four different SNARE proteins) assemble into a parallel four-helix coiled-coil bundle known as the SNARE complex, or SNAREpin. The assembly proceeds in a zipper-like fashion from the N-terminal (membrane-distal) end toward the C-terminal (membrane-proximal) end, progressively bringing the two opposing membranes into close apposition. SNARE motifs are characterized by a heptad repeat (abcdefg) with hydrophobic residues at positions a and d that form the core of the coiled-coil, and an ionic layer at the center of the complex formed by an arginine (R-SNARE) and three glutamines (Q-SNAREs). The SNARE complex is remarkably stable (thermostable to >90 degrees C) and is disassembled by the AAA ATPase NSF (N-ethylmaleimide sensitive factor) in conjunction with alpha-SNAP adaptor proteins.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Neuronal SNARE Complex — Synaptobrevin-2 (VAMP2, R-SNARE), syntaxin-1A (Qa-SNARE), and SNAP-25 (providing Qb and Qc SNARE motifs) form the neuronal SNARE complex that drives synaptic vesicle exocytosis.
- Vesicular SNARE (v-SNARE) — Synaptobrevin-2 (VAMP2) is the prototypical vesicle-associated R-SNARE. Its SNARE motif assembles with target membrane Q-SNAREs to form the trans-SNARE complex.
