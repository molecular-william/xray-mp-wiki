# HOT75BPR (Blue-Light-Absorbing Proteorhodopsin from Pacific Ocean)

## Overview

HOT75BPR is a blue-light-absorbing proteorhodopsin isolated from the Pacific Ocean near Hawaii at 75 m depth (GenBank AAK30179.1). Two mutants were crystallized and solved: D97N (PDB 4kly at 2.72 A) and D97N/Q105L (PDB 4knf at 2.72 A). Both form C5 pentameric arrangements in type I membrane-protein crystals, with alternating protomer direction. Unlike Med12BPR's hexamer, HOT75BPR forms pentamers, demonstrating oligomeric plasticity in proteorhodopsins. The structures reveal an eight-residue N-terminal extension unique to HOT75BPR that interacts with the neighboring protomer's helix C. The cross-protomer His75-Trp34 hydrogen bond is observed in the pentameric arrangement, and functional assays in E. coli cells provide evidence for similar cross-protomer interactions in vivo.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### C5 pentameric oligomeric assembly

HOT75BPR forms C5 pentameric rings, differing from Med12BPR's C6 hexamer and BR's C3 trimer. This demonstrates oligomeric plasticity in proteorhodopsins. Type I membrane-protein crystal with alternating (nonphysiological) protomer direction.

### N-terminal extension interactions

HOT75BPR has an eight-residue N-terminal extension (absent in Med12BPR) that interacts with helix C of the neighboring protomer via hydrogen bonds between Asp22 and main-chain amides of residues on helix C (Thr91, Val92, Phe93).

### In vivo evidence for cross-protomer interaction

Mutation W34D causes dramatic alterations in photochemical function in living E. coli cells. The suppressor effect of H75A on W34D provides evidence for oligomeric interaction of BPR in living cells, confirming that the cross-protomer Trp34-His75 interaction observed in crystals is physiologically relevant.
