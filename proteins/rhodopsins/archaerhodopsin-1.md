# Archaerhodopsin-1 from Halorubrum sp. aus-1

## Overview

Archaerhodopsin-1 (aR-1) is a light-driven proton pump from Halorubrum sp. aus-1, sharing 55-58% sequence identity with [bacteriorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/bacteriorhodopsin/). It functions as a light-driven proton pump with a characteristic acid purple-to-blue transition and contains a unique omega loop near the N-terminus. aR-1 incorporates bacterioruberin as a second chromophore and crystallizes in space group P4₃2₁2.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Comparison with bacteriorhodopsin

aR-1 shares 55-58% sequence identity with bacteriorhodopsin (bR) and the seven-helix transmembrane architecture is conserved. Unlike bR, aR-1 has a unique omega loop near the N-terminus. The proton release channel in the extracellular half is more similar to bR than aR-2, accounting for the similar pKa of the acid purple-to-blue transition.

### Retinal binding pocket conservation

All aromatic residues surrounding the retinal polyene chain are conserved among aR-1, aR-2, and bR, confirming that these residues are required for stereo-specificity of retinal isomerization. Met145 contacts the retinal ionone ring; Leu93 contacts the C13 methyl group.

### Conserved hydrophobic proton uptake pathway

Hydrophobic residues in the proton uptake pathway (Phe27, Leu93, Leu97, Leu99, Leu100, Phe171, Leu174, Phe219, Leu223, Leu224) are conserved across archaeal proton pumps. Their precise arrangement prevents backward proton flow under large pH gradient and membrane potential.

### Micro-cavity near Leu93

A micro-cavity near Leu93 is present in the unexcited state of aR-1, allowing rotation of the Leu93 side-chain during the K-to-L transition in the photocycle, which enables water molecule translocation across the Schiff base.
