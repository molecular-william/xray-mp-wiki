# N2098R — CyanoRhodopsin from Calothrix sp. NIES-2098

## Overview

N2098R is a light-driven outward proton-pumping rhodopsin from the cyanobacterium Calothrix sp. NIES-2098. It is a representative member of the novel [CyanoRhodopsin (CyR)](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/cyanorhodopsin/) clade, which evolved within the cyanobacterial lineage and is distinct from known [Microbial Rhodopsins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/microbial-rhodopsins/) such as [Bacteriorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/bacteriorhodopsin/) and proteorhodopsin. N2098R functions as a light-driven H+ pump and possesses a unique [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) configuration and photocycle optimized for freshwater environments.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Novel CyR clade evolution

N2098R belongs to the [CyanoRhodopsin (CyR)](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/cyanorhodopsin/) clade, a novel lineage of
light-driven proton-pumping rhodopsins unique to cyanobacteria. This
clade evolved within the cyanobacterial lineage and is distinct from
other cyanobacterial rhodopsins such as GR (XLR clade) and ASR (XeR clade).

### Unique photochemical properties

N2098R has an absorption maximum at 550 nm (green light), making it
ideal for utilizing light that chlorophyll cannot directly absorb.
The pKa of the proton acceptor Asp74 (< 2.0) is much lower than that
of BR (Asp85, 2.6) or PR (Asp97, 7.9), indicating it can function
over a wide pH range suitable for freshwater environments. The M-decay
rate is slower than PR but increases under acidic conditions.

### Structural similarity to BR

The crystal structure of N2098R (2.65 A, PDB 6LM0) reveals a
seven-transmembrane helix bundle with a long C-terminal helix covering
the cytoplasmic surface (helices C-F). The overall fold is similar to
BR (RMSD 0.995 A), with a conserved pentagonal water cluster near the
Schiff base (Lys204, Asp74, Asp200) and putative proton release group
(Glu182, Glu192).
