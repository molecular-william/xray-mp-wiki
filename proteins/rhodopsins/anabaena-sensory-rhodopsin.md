# Anabaena Sensory Rhodopsin

## Overview

Anabaena Sensory Rhodopsin (ASR) is a photochromic color sensor
from the cyanobacterium Anabaena (Nostoc) sp. PCC 7120. It is a
member of the microbial rhodopsin family of seven-transmembrane
helix proteins that bind a [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) chromophore via a protonated
Schiff base. The 2.0 A crystal structure revealed that ASR exists
in two photochromic ground states (all-trans and 13-cis) whose
relative amounts depend on the color of illumination, providing a
mechanism for single-pigment color sensing. Unlike archaeal
sensory rhodopsins, ASR interacts with a soluble cytoplasmic
transducer rather than a membrane-embedded one.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Photochromic color sensing mechanism

Anabaena Sensory Rhodopsin exhibits two photochromic ground states determined by the color of ambient light. In the dark-adapted state, the all-trans [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) form predominates (>75%, lambda_max 549 nm). Orange illumination (580 nm) shifts the pigment to a stable >80% 13-cis state (lambda_max 537 nm), while blue light (480 nm) increases the all-trans content. The two ground states thermally interconvert with halftimes of ~100 min (trans) and ~300 min (cis), providing long-lasting color sensitivity analogous to phytochrome photochromicity.

### Structural differences from archaeal rhodopsins

The 2.0 A structure revealed several key differences from archaeal rhodopsins. Pro206 replaces an Asp that is part of the complex counterion in [Bacteriorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/bacteriorhodopsin/) (highly conserved in archaeal rhodopsins). Lys210 exhibits two alternate conformations — in one, its carbonyl oxygen forms an intrahelical hydrogen bond; in the other, it accepts a hydrogen bond from Wat502, which connects helices B and G. A pi-bulge at residues Ser209, Lys210, and Val211 facilitates this conformational flexibility. The cytoplasmic surface is markedly more hydrophilic than that of archaeal rhodopsins, and all three cytoplasmic loops (A-B, C-D, E-F) are well-ordered with conformations distinct from [Bacteriorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/bacteriorhodopsin/). Gln93 in the C-D loop (a four-residue insertion relative to archaeal receptors) creates an enlarged cytoplasmic surface likely involved in transducer interaction.

### Soluble transducer interaction

Unlike archaeal sensory rhodopsins that interact with membrane-embedded transducer proteins (HtrI/HtrII), Anabaena Sensory Rhodopsin interacts with a soluble cytoplasmic transducer. The cytoplasmic surface of ASR is highly ordered with well-defined loop conformations, including Gln93 in the C-D loop which is part of a four-residue insertion that creates an enlarged interaction surface.

### Water-mediated hydrogen bond network

The active site contains an ordered water (Wat502) that bridges the hydroxyl of Ser214 with the backbone carbonyl of Ala40, connecting helices B and G. A second water (Wat402) bridges the protonated Schiff base and its counterion (Asp75), shifted 1.3 A toward the beta-ionone ring compared to sensory rhodopsin II. A chain of ordered waters (Wat502-Wat505) connects the active site to the cytoplasmic surface, providing a hydrophilic path unique among microbial rhodopsins.
