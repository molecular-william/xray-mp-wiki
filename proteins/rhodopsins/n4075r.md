# N4075R — CyanoRhodopsin from cyanobacterium NIES-4075

## Overview

N4075R is a light-driven outward proton-pumping rhodopsin from a cyanobacterial strain NIES-4075, belonging to the novel [CyanoRhodopsin (CyR)](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/rhodopsin-mechanisms/cyanorhodopsin/) (CyR) clade. Its high-resolution crystal structure (1.9 A) is nearly identical to that of [N2098R — CyanoRhodopsin from Calothrix sp. NIES-2098](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/n2098r/), confirming the conserved structural features of the CyR clade including the seven-transmembrane helix bundle and pentagonal water cluster near the Schiff base.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Conserved CyR clade structure

The 1.9 A crystal structure of N4075R is nearly identical to [N2098R — CyanoRhodopsin from Calothrix sp. NIES-2098](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/n2098r/),
confirming the conserved structural architecture of the CyR clade.
Both structures feature the seven-transmembrane helix bundle, long
C-terminal helix, and conserved pentagonal water cluster near the
Schiff base essential for proton transport.
