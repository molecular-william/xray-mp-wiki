# Archaerhodopsin-2

## Overview

Archaerhodopsin-2 (aR2) is a light-driven proton pump found in the claret membrane of Halorubrum sp. aus-2. It shares 56% sequence identity with [Bacteriorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/bacteriorhodopsin/) and forms a trimeric complex with the [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) bacterioruberin, which plays a striking structural role in stabilizing the trimer. The crystal structure reveals how [Bacterioruberin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/bacterioruberin/) binds in the crevices between subunits, trapping specific lipids and creating a suitable environment for the conformational changes required during proton pumping.
