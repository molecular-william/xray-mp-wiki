# GluN1b-GluN2B ATD (Amino-Terminal Domain of NMDA Receptor Subunits)

## Overview

The isolated amino-terminal domains (ATD) of the GluN1b and GluN2B subunits of the N-methyl-D-aspartate receptor (NMDAR). GluN1b is from Xenopus laevis and GluN2B is from rat. NMDARs are ligand-gated ion channels that mediate Ca2+-permeable excitatory synaptic transmission and are involved in synaptic plasticity, learning, and memory. The ATD is the site of action for several GluN2B-selective NAMs including ifenprodil and EU93-108, which bind at the interface between GluN1 and GluN2B subunits. The ATD structure is central to understanding NMDA receptor activation, as conformational changes in the ATD (opening of the bi-lobed GluN2B architecture) are required for ion channel gating.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Apo ATD reveals open conformation of GluN2B ATD bi-lobe

In the absence of allosteric inhibitors, the GluN2B ATD adopts an open-conformation with the R1 and R2 lobes separated by approximately 20 degrees compared to the ifenprodil-bound structure. This opening is accompanied by a 15 degree rotation between GluN1b and GluN2B subunits, bringing their lower lobes (R2) significantly closer together. This conformation represents the active state of the NMDA receptor ATD.

### Subunit arrangement validated by disulfide cross-linking

The subunit arrangement observed in the apo-GluN1b-GluN2B ATD crystal structure was validated in the context of intact GluN1/GluN2B NMDA receptors by engineering cysteine pairs at the subunit interface (GluN1b Phe113/GluN2B Ala107 and GluN1b Gly331/GluN2B Glu75). Spontaneous disulfide bonds formed only when both cysteine mutants were co-expressed, confirming the physiological relevance of the observed arrangement.
