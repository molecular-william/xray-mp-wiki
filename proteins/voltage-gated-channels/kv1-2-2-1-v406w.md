# Kv1.2-2.1 Chimeric Channel V406W (C-type Inactivation Mutant)

## Overview

The Kv1.2-2.1 chimeric channel is a mammalian voltage-gated K+ channel construct used for structural studies, combining the voltage-sensor domain from Kv1.2 and the pore domain from Kv2.1. The V406W mutation (equivalent to Shaker V478W) enhances C-type inactivation, rendering the channel practically nonconducting. The crystal structure of the V406W mutant was determined at 3.3 A resolution (PDB 5WIE), revealing that the mutation in S6 allosterically perturbs the selectivity filter by making the outermost K+ site (IS1) slightly too small to accommodate a K+ ion. The V406W mutation generates two new hydrogen bonds in S6, stabilizing an altered conformation that propagates energetically to the filter without visible structural changes in the intervening region. Two states were observed in the crystal: molecule I with a compromised IS1 (little ion density) and molecule II with a conducting filter stabilized by crystal contacts involving the turret region.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### V406W mutation generates new hydrogen bonds stabilizing altered S6 conformation

The V406W mutation in S6 generates two new hydrogen bonds: Trp406 attaches to the main chain of the adjacent subunit, and the Pro401-Val402-Pro403 triplet acts as a responsive joint enabling S6 to adjust its conformation. The joint is stabilized by a shortened distance between Pro401 carbonyl oxygen and Ile405 amide (3.6 A to 3.2 A), allowing hydrogen bond formation.

### Allosteric perturbation of selectivity filter IS1 site

The V406W mutation allosterically perturbs the extracellular part of the selectivity filter without visible structural changes in intervening regions. The outermost K+ site (IS1) formed by eight carbonyl-oxygen atoms of Tyr373 appears slightly too small to accommodate a K+ ion, showing little electron density. This is consistent with the functional hallmark of C-type inactivation: disruption of the outermost K+ site.

### Crystal contacts stabilize conducting filter in molecule II

Two distinct states are observed in the asymmetric unit. In molecule I, the filter has compromised IS1 density. In molecule II, crystal contacts involving the turret region (Arg350-Asn148, Asp351-Lys104) stabilize a conducting filter similar to wild type, suggesting that C-type inactivation can be modulated by interactions at the extracellular pore entry.
