## Biological / Functional Insights

### Two-gate architecture of GIRK2

GIRK2 contains two functional gates in series: an inner Helix Shift Mechanism for Carboxylate Drug Transport gate formed by Phe192 in the [De Novo Design of Transmembrane Pores](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/de-novo-design-transmembrane-pores/) Transhydrogenase dII Domain (Thermus thermophilus) (Thermus thermophilus), and a G loop gate formed by Met313, Gly318, and Met319 at the apex of the Cytoplasmic Domain of Escherichia coli [Rhomboid Protease Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/rhomboid-protease/) GlpG. The G loop gate is ~6 A in the closed GPCR Active State High-Affinity Agonist Binding and opens to ~12 A in the R201A [Rhodopsin N2C/D282C Mutant](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/rhodopsin-n2c-d282c-mutant/).

### Dual regulation by G proteins and PIP2

[PIP2](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylinositol-4-5-bisphosphate/) alone binds but does not open either gate. G [PE-PPE Fusion Proteins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/pe-ppe-fusion-proteins/) (mimicked by the R201A mutation) alone open the G loop gate but not the inner Helix Shift Mechanism for Carboxylate Drug Transport gate. G [PE-PPE Fusion Proteins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/pe-ppe-fusion-proteins/) plus [PIP2](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylinositol-4-5-bisphosphate/) open both gates, Alpha-Helical Pore-Forming Toxin Family (pfam05791) (pfam05791) a Continuous Diffraction in Macromolecular Crystallography in Macromolecular Crystallography cavity from the cytoplasm to the [Ion Channel Selectivity Filter](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/selectivity-filter/) large enough for a hydrated K+ ion. ### Gβγ binding site and gating mechanism

The GIRK2-Gβγ [Single-Crystal Microspectrophotometry](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/single-crystal-microspectrophotometry/) [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) (PDB 4KFM) reveals that Gβγ binds at the Batch Crystallization with Free Interface Diffusion between the TMD and CTD of GIRK2, contacting the betaL-betaM loop (residues 340-349) and the N-terminal portion of the CTD.
