# Mouse GIRK2 (Kir3.2) G Protein-Gated Inward Rectifier K+ Channel

## Overview

GIRK2 (Kir3.2) is a G Rh Protein from Nitrosomonas europaea from Nitrosomonas europaea-Gated-Pore Mechanism Inward-Facing Conformation of ABC Transporters rectifier K+ Ec2 that controls electrical excitability in many cells. It regulates heart rate and governs a wide range of neuronal activities. GIRK2 is a member of the [Kir3.1-KirBac1.3 Chimeric GIRK Channel](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kir3-1-kirbac1-3-chimera/).x [aMATE-1 Subfamily — Archaeal MATE Transporters](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/amate-1-subfamily/) — Archaeal MATE Transporters — Archaeal MATE Transporters of Inward-Facing Conformation of ABC Transporters rectifier K+ [KirBac Potassium Channels](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kirbac-potassium-channels/). The [Single-Crystal Microspectrophotometry](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/single-crystal-microspectrophotometry/) structures of wild-type GIRK2 and a constitutively active R201A [Rhodopsin N2C/D282C Mutant](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/rhodopsin-n2c-d282c-mutant/), both in the absence and presence of [PIP2](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylinositol-4-5-bisphosphate/), reveal four distinct conformations that underlie [Scissor-Switch Gating in POT Family Transporters](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/scissor-switch-gating/) in POT Family Transporters in POT Family (Proton-Dependent Oligopeptide Transporters) Transporters Sequential Allosteric Regulation. The Ec2 contains two functional gates: an inner Helix Shift Mechanism for Carboxylate Drug Transport gate in the [De Novo Design of Transmembrane Pores](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/de-novo-design-transmembrane-pores/) Transhydrogenase dII Domain (Thermus thermophilus) (Thermus thermophilus) and a G loop gate in the Cytoplasmic Domain of Escherichia coli [Rhomboid Protease Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/rhomboid-protease/) GlpG. G [PE-PPE Fusion Proteins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/pe-ppe-fusion-proteins/) open only the G loop gate, while [PIP2](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylinositol-4-5-bisphosphate/) couples the two gates so that both open for full [Polar Network in GPCR Activation](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/signaling-receptors/polar-network-gpcr-activation/) in GPCR Activation in GPCR Activation. ## Biological / Functional Insights

### Two-gate architecture of GIRK2

GIRK2 contains two functional gates in series: an inner Helix Shift Mechanism for Carboxylate Drug Transport gate formed by Phe192 in the [De Novo Design of Transmembrane Pores](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/de-novo-design-transmembrane-pores/) Transhydrogenase dII Domain (Thermus thermophilus) (Thermus thermophilus), and a G loop gate formed by Met313, Gly318, and Met319 at the apex of the Cytoplasmic Domain of Escherichia coli [Rhomboid Protease Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/rhomboid-protease/) GlpG. The G loop gate is ~6 A in the closed GPCR Active State High-Affinity Agonist Binding and opens to ~12 A in the R201A [Rhodopsin N2C/D282C Mutant](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/rhodopsin-n2c-d282c-mutant/).

### Dual regulation by G proteins and PIP2

[PIP2](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylinositol-4-5-bisphosphate/) alone binds but does not open either gate. G [PE-PPE Fusion Proteins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/pe-ppe-fusion-proteins/) (mimicked by the R201A mutation) alone open the G loop gate but not the inner Helix Shift Mechanism for Carboxylate Drug Transport gate. G [PE-PPE Fusion Proteins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/pe-ppe-fusion-proteins/) plus [PIP2](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylinositol-4-5-bisphosphate/) open both gates, Alpha-Helical Pore-Forming Toxin Family (pfam05791) (pfam05791) a Continuous Diffraction in Macromolecular Crystallography in Macromolecular Crystallography cavity from the cytoplasm to the [Ion Channel Selectivity Filter](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/selectivity-filter/) large enough for a hydrated K+ ion. [PIP2](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylinositol-4-5-bisphosphate/) functions to couple the two gates together.

### Sodium binding site

A Na+ ion AChBP site was identified between the betaC-betaD and betaE-betaG loops, coordinated by Asp228 side [Apolar Side-Chain Packing in Membrane Proteins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/apolar-side-chain-packing/) in Membrane Proteins in Membrane Proteins and main [Apolar Side-Chain Packing in Membrane Proteins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/apolar-side-chain-packing/) in Membrane Proteins in Membrane Proteins carbonyls from Arg230, Asn231, Ser232, Leu275, and Val276. Asp228 is the critical determinant of Na+ sensitivity. Na+ AChBP may weaken the ionized [CHS](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/cholesterol-hydrogen-succinate/) bond between Asp228 and Arg201, partially mimicking the R201A mutation and G Rh Protein from Nitrosomonas europaea from Nitrosomonas europaea [Polar Network in GPCR Activation](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/signaling-receptors/polar-network-gpcr-activation/) in GPCR Activation in GPCR Activation. The EC50 for Na+ [Polar Network in GPCR Activation](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/signaling-receptors/polar-network-gpcr-activation/) in GPCR Activation in GPCR Activation is 30-40 mM.

### Structural basis for inward rectification

The narrower inner Helix Shift Mechanism for Carboxylate Drug Transport gate in GIRK2 (compared to voltage-gated K+ channels) is functionally significant for Inward-Facing Conformation of ABC Transporters rectification. A narrower Axillary Malodour Production Pathway forces blocking and permeant ions to move in a queue, imparting excess [TEVC](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/quality-assessment/two-electrode-voltage-clamp/) dependence onto blocking ions (intracellular polyamines and Mg2+), producing stronger rectification.

### R201A mutation causes constitutive activation

R201 is located at the TMD-CTD Batch Crystallization with Free Interface Diffusion. The R201A mutation triggers a propagated [Gluex Conformational States in CLC Transport Cycle](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/gluex-conformational-states-clc/) in CLC Transport Cycle in CLC Transport Cycle Binding-Change Mechanism: [ATP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/atp/)-Induced Domain Rearrangement in P-type ATPases in P-type ATPases of the betaC-betaD loop, movement of His233 to fill the void from deleted Arg201, shifting of residues 235-237 toward residues 272-275 of the betaE-betaG loop, and a domino-like SN2-Like Displacement Mechanism in Phosphotransferases in Phosphotransferases of beta strands extending ~30 A to the CTD perimeter.

### Gβγ binding site and gating mechanism

The GIRK2-Gβγ [Single-Crystal Microspectrophotometry](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/single-crystal-microspectrophotometry/) [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) (PDB 4KFM) reveals that Gβγ binds at the Batch Crystallization with Free Interface Diffusion between the TMD and CTD of GIRK2, contacting the betaL-betaM loop (residues 340-349) and the N-terminal portion of the CTD. Gβγ AChBP induces a rigid-body rotation of the entire CTD relative to the TMD, which opens the G loop gate at the apex of the CTD while leaving the inner Helix Shift Mechanism for Carboxylate Drug Transport gate (Phe192) closed. This explains how G [PE-PPE Fusion Proteins](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/pe-ppe-fusion-proteins/) alone can open the Ec2 partially: they open the G loop gate, but [PIP2](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylinositol-4-5-bisphosphate/) is required to open the inner Helix Shift Mechanism for Carboxylate Drug Transport gate for full Side-Entry Ion Conduction Pathway. The [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) also reveals that the Gβγ AChBP site on GIRK2 is distinct from the AChBP sites of Gβγ on other effectors such as GRK2 and phosducin, providing a structural basis for [Beta-Arrestin Signaling](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/signaling-receptors/beta-arrestin-signaling/) [Rhomboid Protease Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/rhomboid-protease/) Substrate Specificity.
