# Human K2P2.1 (TREK-1) Potassium Channel I110D Mutant

## Overview

K2P2.1 (TREK-1, KCNK2) is a two-pore domain potassium channel from the K2P channel family that regulates cellular excitability in response to diverse stimuli including mechanical stretch, temperature, pH, and lipids. This study uses an engineered I110D mutant of K2P2.1 (TREK-1) that renders the channel sensitive to ruthenium red (RuR), enabling X-ray crystallographic analysis of polynuclear ruthenium amine inhibition. The structures reveal that RuR binds the "Keystone inhibitor site" formed by an acidic residue pair (Asp110 from each subunit) under the K2P CAP domain archway above the channel pore. This "finger in the dam" mechanism physically blocks ion exit from the selectivity filter and provides an electrostatic barrier to permeant ion movement. The study also established that Ru360, a dinuclear ruthenium amine not previously known to affect K2P channels, inhibits RuR-sensitive K2Ps using the same binding site, and enabled a design strategy for creating K2P RuR "super-responders" with nanomolar sensitivity.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Keystone inhibitor site defined by acidic residue pair

The structures reveal that RuR binds to the Keystone inhibitor site, formed by an acidic residue pair (Asp110 from each subunit under the K2P CAP domain archway). This site is positioned above the selectivity filter and one arm of the extracellular ion pathway (EIP). The RuR molecule plugs one arm of the bifurcated EIP created by the CAP domain, physically blocking ion exit and providing an electrostatic barrier. The binding is primarily electrostatic, with the aspartate side chains directly coordinating the ruthenium centers. Ru360, despite having only two ruthenium atoms, binds the same site with one ruthenium moiety (Ru_A) occupying the S0 ion site and the other (Ru_B) blocking one EIP arm.

### Structure-based design creates RuR super-responders

Based on the structural understanding of the Keystone inhibitor site, a generalizable design strategy was developed to create K2P channels with enhanced RuR sensitivity. By introducing acidic residues at positions that contact the RuR binding site, the study created K2P2.1 N147E mutant with dramatically enhanced sensitivity (IC50 = 73 nM). This "super-responder" design demonstrates the polysite nature of K2P pharmacology and provides a framework for developing K2P-selective inhibitors.

### RuR inhibition is independent of C-type gate activation

Pharmacological activation of the C-type gate by [ML335 (N-aryl-sulfonamide K2P activator)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ml335/) had minimal impact on RuR inhibition (IC50 = 0.173 uM for I110D in the presence of [ML335 (N-aryl-sulfonamide K2P activator)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ml335/)), and RuR block was essentially voltage-independent. These findings are consistent with the structural observation that RuR binds above the selectivity filter, outside the transmembrane electric field, and does not require the C-type gate to be in a particular conformation for binding.
