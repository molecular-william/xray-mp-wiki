# Epithelial Calcium Channel TRPV5

## Overview

TRPV5 is a Ca²⁺-selective transient receptor potential vanilloid channel that, together with [Epithelial Calcium Channel TRPV6](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/trpv6/), plays a vital role in active Ca²⁺ transport across epithelial tissues. TRPV5 and [Epithelial Calcium Channel TRPV6](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/trpv6/) share approximately 75% sequence identity and belong to the vanilloid (TRPV) subfamily of TRP channels. Unlike other TRP channels, TRPV5 and [Epithelial Calcium Channel TRPV6](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/trpv6/) have not been reported to be responsive to temperature, tastants, or odors. They form homo- or heteromeric tetrameric assemblies of four subunits, each containing a central K⁺ channel-like transmembrane domain flanked by intracellular N- and C-terminal domains. TRPV5 and [Epithelial Calcium Channel TRPV6](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/trpv6/) achieve exceptional Ca²⁺ selectivity (P_Ca/P_Na > 100) through direct coordination of Ca²⁺ by a ring of aspartate side chains in the selectivity filter.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Sequence and functional relationship with TRPV6

TRPV5 and [Epithelial Calcium Channel TRPV6](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/trpv6/) share ~75% sequence identity and are both uniquely selective for Ca²⁺ (P_Ca/P_Na > 100). They are both expressed in epithelial tissues and mediate Ca²⁺ uptake across cell membranes. Knockout of [Epithelial Calcium Channel TRPV6](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/trpv6/) in mice leads to impaired Ca²⁺ homeostasis, defective intestinal Ca²⁺ absorption, lower body weight, impaired fertility, and dermatitis. Both channels are involved in tetrameric assembly and regulated by Ca²⁺ sensor calmodulin.

### Structural comparison with TRPV6

The [Epithelial Calcium Channel TRPV6](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/trpv6/) crystal structure reveals shared features with TRPV5 including the ankyrin repeat domain, linker domain with β-hairpin, pre-S1 helix, and K⁺ channel-like transmembrane domain. Both channels use direct coordination of Ca²⁺ by aspartate side chains in the selectivity filter for Ca²⁺ selectivity. The [Epithelial Calcium Channel TRPV6](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/trpv6/) structure provides a structural framework for understanding TRPV5 function, as TRPV5 has not been directly crystallized.
