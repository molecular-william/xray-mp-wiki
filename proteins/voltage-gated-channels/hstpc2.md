# HsTPC2 - Human Two-pore Channel 2

## Overview

HsTPC2 (Human Two-pore Channel 2) is an endo/lysosomal cation channel belonging to the two-pore channel (TPC) family. It is a Na+-selective channel activated by [Phosphatidylinositol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylinositol/) 3,5-bisphosphate (PI(3,5)P2), with high selectivity for Na+ over K+ (PNa/PK ~23.8) and Na+ over Ca2+ (PNa/PCa ~16.8). HsTPC2 shares high sequence similarity with plant AtTPC1 in the selectivity filter region but exhibits drastically different ion selectivity. The selectivity is defined by a Val/Asn pair in filter II (Val651/Asn652), which when introduced into AtTPC1 confers Na+ selectivity. HsTPC2 is not activated by NAADP under the conditions tested.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### HsTPC2 is a Na+-selective channel activated by PI(3,5)P2

Inside-out patch clamp recordings of HsTPC2 (L11A/L12A mutant) on the
plasma membrane of HEK293 cells show that the channel is activated by
cytosolic PI(3,5)P2 (10 microM) but not by NAADP (10 nM-10 microM). Under bi-ionic
conditions, HsTPC2 exhibits a reversal potential of ~81 mV (Na+/K+),
corresponding to PNa/PK of 23.8, and ~-50 mV (Na+/Ca2+), corresponding
to PNa/PCa of ~16.8, confirming its identity as a Na+-selective channel.

### Filter II residues define Na+ selectivity in mammalian TPCs

Mutagenesis of HsTPC2 filter residues shows that the Val651/Asn652 pair
in filter II is essential for Na+ selectivity. Single or double mutations
involving these residues significantly decrease Na+ selectivity. The A272S
mutation in filter I has a more subtle effect. These findings are consistent
with the observation that mouse TPC2 (which has 255TSN257 at filter I)
also exhibits Na+ selectivity.
