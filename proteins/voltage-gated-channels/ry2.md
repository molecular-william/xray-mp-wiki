# Ryanodine Receptor Type 2 (RyR2)

## Overview

Ryanodine receptor type 2 (RyR2) is the calcium release channel isoform primarily expressed in cardiac muscle. It is a large ~2.2 MDa protein that forms mushroom-shaped tetrameric assemblies with a membrane-spanning stalk and a cytosolic cap containing multiple globular domains. RyR2 is structurally and functionally similar to RyR1 but has tissue-specific differences. The SPRY1 domain of RyR2 (mouse, residues 650-844) was crystallized at 1.2 A resolution in this study, revealing a beta-trefoil structure with a finger substructure and a lid region. Disease mutations mapped to RyR2 include [CPVT](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/signaling-receptors/cpvt/) mutations in Repeat12 and the multi-minicore disease mutation D720 in the SPRY1 finger domain.
