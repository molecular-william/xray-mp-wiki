# NTSR1 TM86V-ΔIC3A Mutant

## Overview

NTSR1 TM86V-ΔIC3A is a directed evolution-selected mutant of the neurotensin receptor 1 (NTSR1) that was optimized for crystallization through a directed evolution approach. The construct contains the TM86V mutation and deletion of most of the third intracellular loop (ΔIC3A). Unlike the active-like NTSR1-ELF and NTSR1-LF structures, this mutant adopts an apparent inactive receptor conformation at the intracellular side in the crystal structure, including lacking the outward movement of TM6 that is characteristic of active GPCRs. The structure (PDB 3ZEV) was used as a reference for comparison with the active-like NTSR1 structures in the Ncomms8895 paper. The Na+ ion-binding region in TM86V-ΔIC3A shows different interactions with D113(2.50) compared to active-like NTSR1 structures, and no electron density for water molecules or a Na+ ion has been reported in the structure.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Inactive conformation at intracellular side

The TM86V-ΔIC3A mutant adopts an apparent inactive receptor conformation at the intracellular side, lacking the outward movement of TM6 seen in active GPCRs. TM7 does not unwind as seen in NTSR1-ELF. The F376(8.50) residue is partially inserted into the pocket between TM1, TM2 and TM7, unlike in NTSR1-ELF where it is rotated outward.

### Comparison with active-like NTSR1 structures

The structure is similar to active-like NTSR1 structures in the extracellular half (ligand-binding region) but dissimilar in the intracellular half. The D113(2.50) side chain is in contact with neighbouring residues, but the interactions differ from those in NTSR1-GW5, NTSR1-LF, and NTSR1-ELF. S362(7.46) is mutated to alanine in TM86V-ΔIC3A.
