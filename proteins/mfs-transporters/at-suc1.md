# Arabidopsis thaliana Sucrose Transporter SUC1

## Overview

Arabidopsis thaliana SUC1 is a low-affinity sucrose-proton symporter from the SUC/SUT (sucrose transporter) family. SUC1 functions as a proton-coupled symporter that mediates the active transport of sucrose across the plasma membrane, playing a critical role in phloem loading and sucrose redistribution in plants. The structure was determined in an outward-open conformation at 2.7 Å resolution, revealing a two-step sucrose binding mechanism where the glucosyl moiety of [Sucrose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/sucrose/) binds directly to a key acidic residue in a pH-dependent manner. The structure demonstrates a new mode for proton-driven symport with mechanistic links to cation-driven symporters.
