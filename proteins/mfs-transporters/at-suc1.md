# Arabidopsis thaliana Sucrose Transporter SUC1

## Overview

Arabidopsis thaliana SUC1 is a low-affinity sucrose-proton symporter from the SUC/SUT (sucrose transporter) family. SUC1 functions as a proton-coupled symporter that mediates the active transport of sucrose across the plasma membrane, playing a critical role in phloem loading and sucrose redistribution in plants. The structure was determined in an outward-open conformation at 2.7 Å resolution, revealing a two-step sucrose binding mechanism where the glucosyl moiety of [Sucrose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/sucrose/) binds directly to a key acidic residue in a pH-dependent manner. The structure demonstrates a new mode for proton-driven symport with mechanistic links to cation-driven symporters.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Two-step sucrose binding mechanism

Sucrose binding occurs through a two-step process. G592 is a key residue that interacts with the glucosyl moiety binding directly to a key acidic residue in a stringent pH-dependent manner. Substrate protonation coupling and sucrose are strongly coupled, demonstrating a new two-site binding mode for proton-driven symport with links to cation-driven symport mechanisms.

### Low-affinity sucrose transport

SUC1 is a low-affinity sucrose transporter, operating in highly enriched substrate environments such as the phloem sap. The structure explains how low-affinity transport is achieved in plants, where sucrose concentrations are high, and provides a broad framework for understanding general transport mechanisms in the MFS superfamily.

### Selectivity determinants

The study pinpointed a range of SUC binders that help define substrate selectivity. The glucosyl moiety binds to the key acidic residue, providing the basis for substrate discrimination.
