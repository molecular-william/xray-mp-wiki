# hGLUT1 (Human Glucose Transporter 1)

## Overview

Human [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) transporter 1 (hGLUT1, encoded by [SLC2A1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/glut1/)) is the archetypal member of
the GLUT/SLC2 family of facilitative [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) transporters. It is ubiquitously expressed
and is responsible for basal [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) uptake in most tissues. In antimalarial drug design,
hGLUT1 is the primary human ortholog used as a selectivity counter-screen when developing
[PfHT1 (Plasmodium falciparum Hexose Transporter 1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/pfht1/) inhibitors. The structural comparison between [PfHT1 (Plasmodium falciparum Hexose Transporter 1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/pfht1/) and hGLUT1 revealed that
residues around the glucose-binding site are nearly identical, but an additional pocket
adjacent to the substrate-binding site is highly hydrophobic in [PfHT1 (Plasmodium falciparum Hexose Transporter 1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/pfht1/) and more hydrophilic
in hGLUT1. This difference provides the structural basis for designing PfHT1-selective
inhibitors that do not inhibit human [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) transport. The TH-PF series compounds
selectively inhibit [PfHT1 (Plasmodium falciparum Hexose Transporter 1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/pfht1/) over hGLUT1, achieving selectivity through targeting both the
conserved orthosteric site and the PfHT1-specific allosteric pocket.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Selectivity for PfHT1 over hGLUT1

The TH-PF series compounds were designed to selectively inhibit [PfHT1 (Plasmodium falciparum Hexose Transporter 1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/pfht1/) over hGLUT1.
While the orthosteric glucose-binding site is nearly identical between [PfHT1 (Plasmodium falciparum Hexose Transporter 1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/pfht1/) and hGLUT1,
the allosteric pocket adjacent to it differs significantly: it is highly hydrophobic in
[PfHT1 (Plasmodium falciparum Hexose Transporter 1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/pfht1/) but more hydrophilic in hGLUT1. Residues F85 and V44 in [PfHT1 (Plasmodium falciparum Hexose Transporter 1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/pfht1/) (corresponding to
S85 and T44 in hGLUT1) line the connecting channel and contribute to this difference.
The TH-PF compounds exploit this structural divergence, achieving selectivity ratios
reflected in the CC50/EC50 values (36.1 to 107.2) for mammalian cells.
