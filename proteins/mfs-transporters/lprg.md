# LprG Lipoprotein (Rv1411) from Mycobacterium tuberculosis

## Overview

LprG (Rv1411) is a periplasmic [lipoprotein](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/lipoprotein/) encoded in an operon together with the MFS transporter [Rv1410](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/rv1410/) (P55) in [Mycobacterium tuberculosis](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/mycobacterium-tuberculosis/). It is embedded in the outer leaflet of the inner membrane via its lipid anchor and possesses a hydrophobic pocket capable of accommodating lipids with two or three alkyl chains. LprG accepts [triacylglycerides](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/triacylglyceride/) (TAGs) from Rv1410 via its periplasmic TM11-TM12 helix extensions and transfers them to the mycomembrane, playing a crucial role in sealing the mycobacterial cell envelope.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### LprG hydrophobic pocket accommodates triacylated lipids

Crystal structures of LprG revealed a hydrophobic pocket that can accommodate lipids with two or three alkyl chains. In vitro experiments with purified nonacylated LprG show it can transfer TAGs between lipid vesicles.

### Transient interaction between LprG and Rv1410

Cross-linking studies in live M. smegmatis did not demonstrate a reproducible interaction between LprG and Rv1410, and complex formation could not be shown in vitro. However, shuffled operon experiments show that transporters and lipoproteins from different mycobacterial species can functionally complement each other, suggesting transient rather than specific interactions.
