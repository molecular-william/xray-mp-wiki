# Fucose Permease (FucP)

## Overview

[L-Fucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/l-fucose/) permease (FucP) from E. coli Signal Peptide Peptidase (SppA, Protease IV) (SppA, Protease IV) coli O157:H7 is a [MFS](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/mfs-transporter/) (MFS) [Influenza A M2 Proton Channel](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/influenza-a-m2-proton-channel/) symporter that mediates the [Acetate Buffer (Sodium Acetate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/acetate/) (Sodium Acetate) Uptake Transporter (AceTr) Family of [L-Fucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/l-fucose/), a source of 6mr (Six-Carbon-Ring Retinal) for microorganisms. The [Single-Crystal Microspectrophotometry](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/single-crystal-microspectrophotometry/) [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) of FucP was determined At 3.14 Å resolution In an ABC Transporter Outward-Occluded Mechanism-open GPCR Inactive Conformation, the first ABC Transporter Outward-Occluded Mechanism-open [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) of an [MFS](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/mfs-transporter/). The [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) reveals an amphipathic Central cavity with Negative electrostatic potential On the N Transhydrogenase dII Domain (Thermus thermophilus) (Thermus thermophilus) and [Hydrophobic Gating (Vapor Lock Mechanism)](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/hydrophobic-gating/) (Vapor Lock Mechanism) (Vapor Lock Mechanism) Surface Hydroxyl Acidity On the C Transhydrogenase dII Domain (Thermus thermophilus) (Thermus thermophilus). FucP contains only [TEVC](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/quality-assessment/two-electrode-voltage-clamp/) acidic residues along the CLC Cl-/H+ Exchange Transport Mechanism path, Asp46 and Glu135, which undergo cycles of [Substrate-Protonation Coupling in MFS Symporters](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/substrate-protonation-coupling/) in [MFS](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/mfs-transporter/) Symporters in [MFS](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/mfs-transporter/) Symporters and deprotonation for active CLC Cl-/H+ Exchange Transport Mechanism. The [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) was solved by [Mercury (HgCl2) - Aquaporin Inhibitor](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/mercury/) (HgCl2) - [Aquaporin Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/aquaporin/) Inhibitor (HgCl2) - [Aquaporin Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/aquaporin/) Family Inhibitor-[Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) Single-Multi-Wavelength Anomalous Diffraction Multi-Wavelength Anomalous Diffraction dispersion (SAD).
