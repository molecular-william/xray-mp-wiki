# Fucose Permease (FucP)

## Overview

The [Single-Crystal Microspectrophotometry](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/single-crystal-microspectrophotometry/) [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) of FucP was determined At 3.14 Å resolution In an ABC Transporter Outward-Occluded Mechanism-open GPCR Inactive Conformation, the first ABC Transporter Outward-Occluded Mechanism-open [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) of an [MFS](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/mfs-transporter/). ## Biological / Functional Insights

### First outward-open conformation of an MFS transporter

FucP represents the first [MFS](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/mfs-transporter/) [Structure-Based Design of Third-Generation Antipsychotics](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/construct-design/structure-based-antipsychotic-design/) captured In an ABC Transporter Outward-Occluded Mechanism-open GPCR Inactive Conformation. ### Proton coupling mechanism via Asp46 and Glu135

FucP has only [TEVC](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/quality-assessment/two-electrode-voltage-clamp/) acidic residues along the CLC Cl-/H+ Exchange Transport Mechanism path: Asp46 (TM1) and Glu135 (TM4), both In the N Transhydrogenase dII Domain (Thermus thermophilus) (Thermus thermophilus). Mutagenesis shows that Asp46Ala, Asp46Asn, Glu135Ala, Glu135Gln, and Glu135Asp all completely abrogate active CLC Cl-/H+ Exchange Transport Mechanism. Asp46 functions as the [Influenza A M2 Proton Channel](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/influenza-a-m2-proton-channel/)-GPCR-G Protein Coupling Mechanism residue: neutralization by Asn or Ala renders FucP Ph-independent In counterflow assays, reminiscent of Glu325 In LacY. Glu135 is proposed to be involved In TatA Substrate of E. coli [Rhomboid Protease Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/rhomboid-protease/) GlpG SLC6 Substrate Recognition by the GMG Motif. Molecular Docking Molecular Dynamics Simulation simulations suggest Glu135 changes rotamer upon [Substrate-Protonation Coupling in MFS Symporters](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/substrate-protonation-coupling/) in [MFS](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/mfs-transporter/) Symporters in [MFS](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/mfs-transporter/) Symporters, breaking a [CHS](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/cholesterol-hydrogen-succinate/) bond with Tyr365.
