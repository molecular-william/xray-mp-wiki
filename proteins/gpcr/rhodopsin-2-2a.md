# Bovine Rhodopsin (2.2 A Resolution, PDB 1U19)

## Overview

High-resolution 2.2 A crystal structure of bovine rhodopsin in the dark state (PDB 1U19). This structure completes the polypeptide chain including the cytoplasmic surface region for the first time. The [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal) chromophore shows significant negative pre-twist of the C11-C12 double bond and a 6s-cis beta-ionone ring. Crystallized in [heptylthioglucoside](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/heptylthioglucoside/) micelles with [ammonium sulfate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/ammonium-sulfate/) precipitant.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Complete cytoplasmic surface resolved

The 2.2 A structure completes the cytoplasmic surface region including the third cytoplasmic loop (C3) connecting helices V and VI. C3 folds outside the rhodopsin molecule along the membrane surface, similar to its position in the 2.6 A model. The extended helical structure of helix VI is an outstanding feature of the complete model.

### Retinal chromophore geometry

The C11-C12 double bond shows significant negative pre-twist (-18 deg) in the ground state, suggesting the direction of photoisomerization. The C6-C7 single bond adopts a 6s-cis form with substantial negative twist (-30 to -32 deg). The beta-ionone ring is restricted by hydrophobic residues Phe212, Phe261, and Trp265 on the cytoplasmic side. The C20 methyl group at C13 interacts with Trp265, contributing to the C11-C12 twist.

### Hydrogen bonding network at chromophore site

The hydrogen bonded network around the chromophore mediated by water molecules is confirmed. Glu113 serves as the counterion (OE2 to Schiff base N at 3.28-3.45 A). Wat2b bridges between Glu113 carboxylate and Thr94/Ser186. Wat2a connects Ser186, Cys187 backbone oxygen, and Glu181. The network extends to Tyr268 and Tyr192, supporting the counterion switch hypothesis for meta-I state formation.
