# Sphingosine-1-Phosphate Receptor 1 (S1P1)

## Overview

[Sphingosine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/sphingosine)-1-phosphate receptor 1 ([S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1, also known as S1PR1 or EDG1) is a class A G protein-coupled receptor that binds [Sphingosine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/sphingosine)-1-phosphate ([S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)), a bioactive lysophospholipid signaling molecule. [S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1 primarily couples to Gi proteins and plays critical roles in lymphocyte egress, vascular development, and angiogenesis. It is the target of the FDA-approved drug FTY20 (fingolimod) for multiple sclerosis. The crystal structure of [S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1 revealed a linear binding pocket with membrane-embedded ligand access, in contrast to the related lysophosphatidic acid receptor [LPA1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lpa1) which has a spherical pocket and extracellular ligand access.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Linear binding pocket with membrane-embedded ligand access

The [S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1 binding pocket is linear in nature, contrasting with the spherical pocket of [LPA1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lpa1). The position of TM1 in [S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1 is shifted away from TM7 by approximately 11 A (measured between C-alpha of Thr48(1.35) and Glu294(7.36)), creating a gap that enables access of hydrophobic ligands from the plasma membrane to the binding pocket. This membrane-embedded access pathway is a key distinguishing feature compared to [LPA1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lpa1), where TM1 is positioned 3 A closer to TM7, closing access from the membrane and forcing extracellular ligand entry.

### Key structural differences between S1P1 and LPA1

Three amino acid differences between [S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1 and [LPA1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lpa1) account for the distinct pocket shapes and ligand access mechanisms: position 3.33 is phenylalanine in [S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1 versus aspartate in [LPA1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lpa1); position 5.43 is cysteine in [S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1 versus tryptophan in [LPA1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lpa1); position 6.51 is leucine in [S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1 versus [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine) in [LPA1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lpa1). These differences create a more constrained pocket in [S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1 and open a sub-pocket in [LPA1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lpa1). The short segment between TM1 and the N-terminal capping helix (ECL0) is helical in [S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1 but lacks secondary structure in [LPA1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lpa1), contributing to the different extracellular topologies.

### LPA1 and S1P1 share structural and functional similarity

[LPA1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lpa1) and [S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1 share 41% sequence identity in the transmembrane region and recognize lysophospholipid endogenous ligands. Despite these similarities, structural comparisons revealed substantial divergence in pocket shape and polarity. The relative orientation of [LPA1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lpa1) helices is similar to [S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1, but the primary point of divergence occurs at the tip of TM1. Molecular dynamics simulations confirmed that the distance between TM1 and TM7 is consistently larger and more variable for [S1P](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p)1 than [LPA1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lpa1), both with and without bound ligands.

### Class I and Class II Ligand Binding at S1P1

S1P₁ ligands are classified into two groups based on receptor interactions. Class I ligands are lipid-like compounds that mimic S1P and its sphingophospholipid head-group interactions (e.g., FTY720-P, SEW2871), or non-lipid small molecules with polar groups that interact with the S1P head-group region. Class II agonists (e.g., CYM-5442, CYM-5181, CYM-5178) do not require interactions with the polar head-group-interacting region (R120³·²⁸ and E121³·²⁹) and instead rely on shape complementarity and specific aromatic interactions, particularly with W269⁶·⁴⁸. The acyl chain length relative to the amino-phosphate/phosphonate groups dictates pharmacology — extending the acyl chain by one carbon (from 9 to 10 carbons) converts antagonists to agonists.

### Structural Basis for S1P₁ Selectivity

Selectivity of class II agonists for S1P₁ over S1P₃ and S1P₄ is rationalized by discrete binding pocket substitutions: S1P₃ has F263⁶·⁵⁵ (vs L276⁶·⁵⁵ in S1P₁), introducing steric clashes with class II aromatic ring systems; S1P₄ has L125³·³² (vs M124³·³²), causing constriction at a critical location where the phenyl ring of FTY720-P and indole ring of CYM-5442 bind. No substitutions distinguish S1P₅ from S1P₁, consistent with the lack of selectivity shown by most S1P₁ agonists.
