# Metarhodopsin II

## Overview

Metarhodopsin II is the light-activated conformation of [Bovine Rhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/bovine-rhodopsin/), the visual pigment that initiates phototransduction in rod cells. This is the final active state in the [Rhodopsin Photocycle](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/rhodopsin-mechanisms/rhodopsin-photocycle/), in which the bound chromophore [11-cis-Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/11-cis-retinal/) has isomerized to all-trans-[Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) and the receptor has adopted a conformation competent for G-protein (transducin) activation. The crystal structure of metarhodopsin II (PDB 3PQR) revealed the G-protein-interacting conformation of the receptor, providing a key reference for understanding active-state GPCR conformations. Meta II was generated by diffusion of all-trans-[Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) into crystals of the [OPSIN](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/opsin/) apoprotein in its active Ops* conformation. The structure shows the [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) in its relaxed conformation, with the beta-ionone ring rotated relative to the inactive dark state, and the cytoplasmic end of TM6 displaced by 6-7 A away from TM3, with associated rearrangements of the E(D)RY and NPxxY(x)5,6F motifs.

## Expression and Purification

- **Expression system**: Bovine (native)
- **Construct**: Native [Bovine Rhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/bovine-rhodopsin/) purified from rod outer segment membranes. For crystallization, [OPSIN](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/opsin/) was purified and crystals were soaked with all-trans-[Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) to generate Meta II in crystallo.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### G-protein-interacting conformation of rhodopsin

The metarhodopsin II structure represents the state of rhodopsin after light activation where the receptor is competent to activate transducin (Gt). The R137(3.50) side chain adopts an orientation similar to that found in the beta2AR-Gs complex and active M2 receptor, supporting the conservation of G-protein-coupling determinants across class A GPCRs.

### Global conformational changes upon Meta II activation

The transition from inactive rhodopsin to Meta II involves two major conformational changes also observed in Ops* structures: (1) elongation of TM5 by 1.5-2.5 helix turns at the cytoplasmic side and a 2-3 A motion of TM5 towards TM6, linked to rearrangement of TM3-TM5 hydrogen bonding network; and (2) displacement of the cytoplasmic end of TM6 away from TM3 by 6-7 A. The conserved E(D)RY motif (TM3) and NPxxY(x)5,6F motif (TM7-H8) rearrange: Tyr306(7.53) swivels towards TM6 where it is linked via water molecules to Met257(6.40) and Tyr223(5.58), while the stabilizing interaction between Tyr306(7.53) and Phe313(7.60) in inactive rhodopsin is broken.

### Retinal binding pocket reorganization in the active state

In Meta II, all-trans-[Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) adopts its relaxed conformation and fits closely into the reorganized binding pocket. The beta-ionone ring rotates relative to the dark state, enabled by TM6 rotation and Trp265(6.48) motion that frees space. Two openings into the [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) binding pocket become apparent in Meta II: one between TM1 and TM7, and another between TM5 and TM6, reflecting a loosening of the [OPSIN](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/opsin/) structure that allows [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) entry and egress. A potential water-mediated hydrogen bonding network links the Schiff base to Glu181 and Ser186 via a central water molecule (distances 3.3 A, 2.7 A, and 2.7 A, respectively). The hydrogen bonding network between Glu181, Tyr191, and Tyr268 in rhodopsin is disrupted in Meta II.

### Comparison with light-induced Meta II pathway

The reversibly formed Meta II in crystals is generated by all-trans-[Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) diffusion into [OPSIN](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/opsin/) crystals, distinct from light-induced activation in native membranes. The structural product is likely equivalent to the Meta IIbH+ substate (the final product in the three-step activation scheme: Meta IIa -> Meta IIb -> Meta IIbH+), characterized by TM6 motion, rotated beta-ionone ring, and stabilization by Glu134(3.49) protonation. The [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) in the crystal is in its relaxed conformation matching extensive prior NMR distance restraints, supporting that the same constraints exist in reversibly and light-induced formed Meta II.
