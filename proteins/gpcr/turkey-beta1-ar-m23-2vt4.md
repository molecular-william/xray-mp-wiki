# Turkey Beta1-Adrenergic Receptor Thermostabilized Mutant M23 with Cyanopindolol (2VT4)

## Overview

The turkey beta1-adrenergic receptor (beta1AR) thermostabilized mutant M23 was crystallized in complex with the high-affinity antagonist [Cyanopindolol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/cyanopindolol/) at 2.7 A resolution (PDB: 2VT4). The construct was engineered for crystallization by truncating residues T34-424, adding a His6 tag at the C terminus, deleting segments of cytoplasmic loop 3 (CL3), and introducing six thermostabilizing point mutations (R68S, M90V, Y227A, A282L, F327A, F338M) along with C116L for increased expression and C358A to remove palmitoylation. The structure represents the inactive (R) conformation of a beta-adrenergic receptor and reveals the molecular details of antagonist binding, including the role of extracellular loop 2 (EL2) and a short alpha-helix in cytoplasmic loop 2 (CL2) that interacts with the conserved DRY motif.

## Expression and Purification

- **Expression system**: Baculovirus expression in High 5 insect cells
- **Construct**: Turkey beta1AR truncated T34-424 with His6 tag at C terminus. Six thermostabilizing mutations: R68S, M90V, Y227A, A282L, F327A, F338M. Additional mutations: C116L (increased expression), C358A (removed palmitoylation). CL3 deletions: residues 244-271 and 277-278.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Ligand-binding pocket architecture

The ligand-binding pocket comprises 15 side chains from amino acid residues in four
transmembrane helices and extracellular loop 2 (EL2). EL2 defines the entrance of the
ligand-binding pocket and is stabilized by two disulphide bonds and a sodium ion. The
pocket interacts with [Cyanopindolol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/cyanopindolol/) through both polar interactions (with residues
including Thr 203 and Phe 201 in EL2) and non-polar interactions. Binding of
[Cyanopindolol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/cyanopindolol/) to the beta1AR and binding of [Carazolol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carazolol/) to the beta2AR involve similar
interactions.

### CL2 alpha-helix and DRY motif interaction

A short well-defined alpha-helix in cytoplasmic loop 2 (residues Pro 146-Leu 152),
not observed in either rhodopsin or the beta2-adrenergic receptor, directly interacts
with the highly conserved DRY motif (Asp 138, Arg 139, Tyr 140) at the end of helix 3.
Tyr 149 in CL2 forms a hydrogen bond with Asp 138 of the DRY motif, which is essential
for receptor activation. The Y149A mutation makes beta1AR less thermally stable,
supporting the structural role of this interaction.

### Inactive conformation and the ionic lock

The structure represents the inactive (R) conformation of the receptor, containing
bound antagonist and lacking basal activity. Notably, the salt bridge between Arg
3.50 and Glu 6.30, termed the 'ionic lock', is not present in the beta1AR structure.
This suggests that the ionic lock is not an essential feature of the inactive state,
contrary to earlier proposals.

### Comparison with beta2-adrenergic receptor

Superposition of beta2AR (PDB 2RH1) and beta1AR (chain B) based on residues in H3,
H5, H6 and H7 shows 78 C-alpha atoms superimposed with 0.25 A r.m.s.d., and 269
C-alpha atoms over all transmembrane helices with 0.7 A r.m.s.d. The turkey beta1AR
sequence is 82% identical to human beta1AR and 67% identical to human beta2AR over
the transmembrane region. No evidence for significant backbone changes at the sites
of the six point mutations was found.

### Agonist-induced conformational changes

Comparison of the antagonist-bound structure ([Cyanopindolol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/cyanopindolol/), PDB 2VT4) with
agonist-bound structures ([Isoprenaline (Isoproterenol)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/isoprenaline/), [Carmoterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carmoterol/), [Dobutamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dobutamine/), [Salbutamol (Albuterol)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/salbutamol/)) from
the same beta1AR-M23 construct reveals the molecular basis of agonist-induced
activation. Agonist binding causes a 6.3 A inward movement of the bottom of
transmembrane helix 6 (TM6), an outward movement of TM5, and a rotation of
TM7. The extended ligand binding pocket is occupied differently by the four
agonists: [Carmoterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carmoterol/) and [Dobutamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dobutamine/) make distinct contacts with EL2 residues
(Cys199, Asp200), while [Isoprenaline (Isoproterenol)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/isoprenaline/) and [Salbutamol (Albuterol)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/salbutamol/) do not extend into this
pocket. The H5-H4 interface contacts differ between agonist-bound and
antagonist-bound structures, with the beta2AR polymorphism Thr164Ile making
the interface more similar to the beta1AR.

### Ligand-specific binding pocket interactions

The four agonists ([Isoprenaline (Isoproterenol)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/isoprenaline/), [Carmoterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carmoterol/), [Dobutamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dobutamine/), [Salbutamol (Albuterol)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/salbutamol/)) share a
common binding mode at the orthosteric site, forming hydrogen bonds with
Asp121(3.32), Ser211(EL2), and Ser215(EL2). However, [Carmoterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carmoterol/) and [Dobutamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dobutamine/)
have extended side chains that reach into the extended ligand binding pocket,
forming additional contacts with Cys199 and Asp200 in EL2. The orientation of
the ligand tail varies between the two monomers in the asymmetric unit due to
different crystal contacts at the top of helix 7.

### H5-H4 interface and extracellular leaflet contacts

The H5-H4 interface in the extracellular leaflet of the membrane shows
distinct contacts between agonist-bound and antagonist-bound structures.
In the beta2AR (PDB 3D4S with timolol), water W514 mediates polar interaction
with Thr164 and Ser165 on helix 4. No water equivalent to W514 is observed in
the beta1AR-M23 structures because of the presence of Val172 in beta1AR at
position 4.56, where the equivalent residue in beta2AR is Thr164. The
naturally occurring polymorphism Thr164Ile in beta2AR alters these interactions
to make the interface more similar to beta1AR.
