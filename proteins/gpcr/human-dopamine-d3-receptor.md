# Human Dopamine D3 Receptor (D3R)

## Overview

The human dopamine D3 receptor (D3R) is a class A G protein-coupled receptor
(GPCR) belonging to the D2-like dopamine receptor subfamily. It is a target
for antipsychotic and antidyskinetic drugs. The crystal structure of D3R was
determined in complex with the D2/D3-selective antagonist eticlopride using
a T4 lysozyme (T4L) fusion strategy. The structure reveals the canonical
seven-transmembrane (7TM) bundle of class A GPCRs and contains an intact
ionic lock between [Arg128\u00b3\u00b7\u2075\u2070](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/gpcr-ionic-lock/)
in the conserved D[E]RY motif and Glu324\u2076\u00b7\u00b3\u2070 in helix VI
\u2014 the first observation of this interaction in a non-rhodopsin GPCR
crystal structure. Two molecules in the asymmetric unit show different
conformations of intracellular loop 2 (ICL2), suggesting conformational
dynamics relevant to signaling.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Ionic lock between the D[E]RY motif and Glu324⁶·³⁰

The D3R structure reveals an intact ionic lock \u2014 a salt bridge between Arg128\u00b3\u00b7\u2075\u2070 in the conserved D[E]RY motif of helix III and Glu324\u2076\u00b7\u00b3\u2070 in helix VI. This interaction was previously observed only in rhodopsin crystal structures and was thought to be disrupted in other GPCRs partly due to T4L fusion. Its presence in D3R establishes that the ionic lock can form in the presence of T4L, indicating that different receptors have different intrinsic propensities for this interaction, which may relate to basal signaling activities.

### ICL2 conformational dynamics

Two molecules in the asymmetric unit show different ICL2 conformations. Chain A has a 2.5-turn \u03b1 helix running parallel to the membrane, while chain B shows unstructured ICL2 with the intracellular ends of helices IV and V shifted \u223c2.9 \u00c5 closer together. These distinct states suggest transient \u03b1-helix formation in ICL2, which may modulate signaling properties and contribute to tolerance in D3R signaling.

### Eticlopride binding pocket and D2/D3 selectivity

Eticlopride occupies the orthosteric binding pocket defined by side chains from helices II, III, V, VI, and VII. Its tertiary amine in the ethyl-pyrrolidine ring forms a salt bridge (2.8 \u00c5) to Asp110\u00b3\u00b7\u00b3\u00b2, which is conserved in all aminergic receptors. The substituted aromatic ring fits within a hydrophobic cavity formed by Phe345\u2076\u00b7\u2075\u00b9, Phe346\u2076\u00b7\u2075\u00b2 in helix VI; Val189\u2075\u00b7\u00b3\u2079, Ser192\u2075\u00b7\u2074\u00b2, Ser193\u2075\u00b7\u2074\u00b3 in helix V; Val111\u00b3\u00b7\u00b3\u00b3 in helix III; and Ile183 in ECL2. Of the 18 eticlopride contact residues, 17 are identical in D2R and 5 differ in D4R, explaining eticlopride's D2/D3 selectivity over D4R.

### Second extracellular binding pocket

A second extracellular binding pocket at the junction of ECL2, ECL1, and helices I, II, and VII includes conserved Tyr373\u2077\u00b7\u2074\u00b3 and Glu95\u00b2\u00b7\u2076\u2075. The residue at position 1.39 differs between D3R (Tyr36\u00b9\u00b7\u00b3\u2079) and D2R (Leu41\u00b9\u00b7\u00b3\u2079), and 44% of the extracellular half of helix I is not conserved between the two subtypes. Molecular dynamics simulations suggest reorganization of ECL3 and helices I, II, and VII alters the configuration of this second pocket, providing a structural basis for subtype-selective drug design.
