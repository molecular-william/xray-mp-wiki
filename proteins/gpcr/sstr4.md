# Somatostatin Receptor 4 (SSTR4)

## Overview

Somatostatin receptor 4 (SSTR4) is a class A G protein-coupled receptor belonging to the SRIF2 subfamily of somatostatin receptors. SSTR4 is highly expressed in the central nervous system and mediates potent analgesic and anti-inflammatory actions, making it a promising target for non-opioid pain control, especially for chronic neuropathic, inflammatory and mixed pain. [Cryo-Electron Microscopy](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/cryo-em/) structures of SSTR4-Gi1 bound to endogenous ligand SST-14 (2.9 A) and non-peptide agonist J-2156 (2.8 A) were determined, revealing ligand recognition and subtype selectivity mechanisms.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Ligand recognition by SSTR4

SST-14 binds SSTR4 with the key pharmacophore (F7-W8-K9-T10) similar to [SSTR2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/sstr2/), but with a 30-degree rotation of the SST-14 disulfide bond, twisting it approximately 5 A more toward helices V and VI. Unlike [SSTR2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/sstr2/), SSTR4 has a smaller, more polar hydrophobic pocket due to shorter helix VI and VII residues, which fails to accommodate the hydrophobic phenyl ring of F6 of SST-14. N293(7.35) and N282(6.58) of SSTR4 form two hydrogen bonds locking helices VI and VII in a closer conformation.

### J-2156 binding to SSTR4

J-2156, a sulfonamide compound without the typical W-K pair, adopts a binding pose distinct from L-054,522 in [SSTR2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/sstr2/). Its beta-methyl-naphthyl group extends toward helix V, while a phenyl ring approaches a sub-pocket formed by helices II and III that is absent in [SSTR2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/sstr2/). The protonated amino group forms a salt bridge with D126(3.32), mimicking K9 of SST-14.

### Subtype selectivity between SSTR2 and SSTR4

The hydrophobic cavity formed by helix VI, ECL3 and helix VII, along with non-conserved residues of helix II and ECL2, contribute to ligand selectivity for [SSTR2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/sstr2/). SSTR4 presents a sub-pocket formed by helices II and III, disrupted in [SSTR2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/sstr2/), revealing a possibility to design selective ligands targeting this sub-pocket. Octreotide shows good selectivity for [SSTR2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/sstr2/), while peptide 3 (with alanine at position 7) is SSTR4-selective.
