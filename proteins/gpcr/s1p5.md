# Sphingosine-1-Phosphate Receptor 5 (S1P5)

## Overview

Sphingosine-1-phosphate receptor 5 (S1P5, S1PR5, EDG8) is a class A G protein-coupled receptor that binds the bioactive lysophospholipid sphingosine-1-phosphate ([S1P (Sphingosine-1-Phosphate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1p/)). S1P5 is predominantly expressed in the nervous system (oligodendrocytes) and immune system (NK cells), regulating NK cell egress from lymph nodes and playing roles in autoimmune and neurodegenerative disorders as well as carcinogenesis. The crystal structure of human S1P5 in complex with the selective inverse agonist ONO-5430608 was determined at 2.2 A resolution by [Serial Femtosecond Crystallography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/serial-femtosecond-crystallography/) (SFX) at the PAL-XFEL (PDB 7YXA). The structure reveals a unique ligand-binding mode involving a previously uncharacterized allosteric sub-pocket between TM1 and TM7, formed by non-conserved residues C43(1.39), I93(2.61), L292(7.39), G293(7.40), and M296(7.43). Together with functional assays, mutagenesis, [Molecular Docking](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/molecular-docking/), and MD simulations, the structure provides a template for structure-based drug design of selective S1P5 ligands and reveals structural determinants of inverse agonism in the S1PR family.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Allosteric sub-pocket for subtype selectivity

The bulky naphthyl group of ONO-5430608 does not fit in the narrow orthosteric hydrophobic cleft and instead occupies a previously uncharacterized allosteric sub-pocket between TM1 and TM7 formed by non-conserved residues C43(1.39), I93(2.61), L292(7.39), G293(7.40), and M296(7.43). Mutagenesis showed strong effects of TM7 residues (L292, M296, G293) on ligand potency, demonstrating this pocket's critical role in subtype selectivity.

### Y2.57 conformational flexibility and inverse agonism mechanism

Y89(2.57) in S1P5 exhibits conformational flexibility between upward and downward orientations. The downward conformation is stabilized by the inverse agonist ONO-5430608, opens the allosteric sub-pocket, and locks the dual toggle switch L119(3.36)-W264(6.48) in the inactive state via steric clash with L119. In contrast, the upward conformation is compatible with both active and inactive states. This Y2.57 conformational flexibility provides a structural basis for both receptor subtype selectivity and inverse agonism.

### AlphaFold evaluation for GPCR structure prediction

50 de novo AlphaFold models of S1P5 were generated without using experimental structures as templates. While models captured some conformational diversity of conserved motifs, they did not provide a full description of specific signaling states (active vs inactive) and showed subpar performance in virtual ligand screening (AUC and enrichment) compared to the experimental crystal structure.

### Naturally occurring mutations mapped to structure

Known missense SNVs from gnomAD and COSMIC databases were mapped onto the S1P5 crystal structure. Notable mutations include L318(8.55)Q in helix 8 (3% population frequency, impairs G12 signaling), E112(3.29)G affecting headgroup recognition, and N298(7.45)K/D/S in the sodium-binding site. Somatic cancer mutation S125(3.42)R disrupts a conserved hydrogen-bond network between TMs 2, 3, and 4.
