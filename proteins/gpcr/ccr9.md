# CC Chemokine Receptor 9 (CCR9)

## Overview

CC Chemokine Receptor 9 (CCR9) is a G-protein-coupled receptor (GPCR) activated by CCL25 that plays a key role in leukocyte recruitment to the gut. It is a therapeutic target in inflammatory bowel disease. The crystal structure of CCR9 in complex with the antagonist [Vercirnon (GSK1605786, CCX282-B)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/vercirnon/) (GSK1605786, CCX282-B) was determined at 2.8 A resolution (PDB 5LWE, space group P1), revealing an unprecedented intracellular allosteric binding site for a small-molecule antagonist. [Vercirnon (GSK1605786, CCX282-B)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/vercirnon/) binds approximately 33 A from the orthosteric site, contacting TM1, TM2, TM3, TM6, TM7 and helix 8, exerting antagonism by preventing G-protein coupling and arrestin binding through steric hindrance and conformational restriction.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### First intracellular allosteric binding site for a small-molecule GPCR antagonist

[Vercirnon (GSK1605786, CCX282-B)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/vercirnon/) binds to the intracellular side of CCR9 in a pocket contacting TM1, TM2, TM3, TM6, TM7 and helix 8, approximately 33 Angstroms from the orthosteric site. The sulfone group acts as a helix cap for the N terminus of helix 8 by H-bonding with Glu322, Arg323 and Phe324 backbone. This is the first detailed view of a small molecule bound on the intracellular surface of a GPCR in a pocket within the helical bundle open to the cytoplasm.

### Mechanism of intracellular allosteric antagonism

[Vercirnon (GSK1605786, CCX282-B)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/vercirnon/) prevents G-protein coupling by holding TM6 inwards toward the central helical axis via a polar network from TM6 to ICL1, TM7 and helix 8, restricting the required outward movement of TM6 for G-protein binding. Superposition with the beta2-AR-Gs complex shows G-protein clashes with both [Vercirnon (GSK1605786, CCX282-B)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/vercirnon/) and the constrained TM6/ICL3. Superposition with rhodopsin-arrestin shows [Vercirnon (GSK1605786, CCX282-B)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/vercirnon/) occupies two major arrestin-receptor interfaces, with the TM7-helix 8 junction directly clashing with the arrestin finger loop.
