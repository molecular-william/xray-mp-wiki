# SUCNR1 (Succinate Receptor 1 / GPR91)

## Overview

SUCNR1 ([Succinate (Succinic Acid)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/succinate/) receptor 1), also known as GPR91, is a class A G-protein-coupled receptor that senses the citric acid cycle intermediate [Succinate (Succinic Acid)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/succinate/). It is implicated in various pathological conditions including rheumatoid arthritis, liver fibrosis, obesity, atherothrombosis, and hypertension. Under pathological conditions, [Succinate (Succinic Acid)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/succinate/) is released into the extracellular space and functions as a signaling molecule recognized by SUCNR1 on the plasma membrane. The receptor has been crystallized in both unbound (closed conformation) and succinate-bound (open conformation) states.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Succinate as Danger Signal

[Succinate (Succinic Acid)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/succinate/) accumulates in extracellular space under pathological conditions including ischemia,
hypoxia, toxicity, and hyperglycemia. It functions as a danger signal recognized by SUCNR1 on
the plasma membrane. In arthritis patients, extracellular [Succinate (Succinic Acid)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/succinate/) accumulates in high
concentration in synovial fluid, driving inflammation through IL-1beta production via HIF1alpha
stabilization in macrophages. SUCNR1-deficient mice are protected from arthritis development,
showing reduced neutrophil infiltration, lower proinflammatory cytokines, and decreased
antigen-specific Th17 cells.

### SUCNR1 Antagonist Binding

The X-ray structure of humanized rat SUCNR1 bound to compound 20 (PDB 6Z10) reveals the
binding mode of a potent zwitterionic antagonist (IC50 = 88 nM). The compound forms a salt
bridge interaction with Glu18, a key residue in the orthosteric binding site. The structure
demonstrates that 3-piperidyl substituents can participate in an internal salt bridge with the
carboxylate, shielding the charges and improving membrane permeability.
