# Opsin (Retinal-Free Rhodopsin)

## Overview

Opsin is the apoprotein form of rhodopsin, the G-protein-coupled receptor (GPCR) responsible for vision in vertebrate retinal rod cells. This structure represents the active Ops* conformation (retinal-free) and serves as the best available structural template for homology modeling of olfactory receptors (ORs), which comprise over half of all GPCRs. A molecule of octylglucoside (OG) detergent was found bound in the orthosteric ligand-binding pocket, replacing retinal and stabilizing the active receptor conformation through a hydrogen-bond network reminiscent of OR-odorant interactions.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Opsin as OR homology model

Opsin shares key features expected for olfactory receptors (ORs): deep hydrophobic ligand-binding pocket accessible from lipid bilayer, hydrogen-bond network for ligand recognition, and conformational flexibility between active and inactive states. The hydrogen-bond pattern holding OG in the pocket is reminiscent of the dynamic hydrogen-bond pattern proposed for OR-odorant interactions. Various detergents (DM, OG, NG, HpG) can occupy the binding pocket in a chain-length-dependent manner, providing a model for how hydrophobic odorants enter ORs.
