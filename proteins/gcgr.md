# Human Glucagon Receptor (GCGR)

## Overview

The human glucagon receptor (GCGR, UniProt P47871) is a class B G-protein-coupled receptor
expressed in liver, kidney, intestinal smooth muscle, brain, adipose tissue, heart, and pancreas.
It binds glucagon, a 29-amino-acid peptide from pancreatic alpha-cells, and plays a key role
in glucose homeostasis. GCGR has been considered an important drug target for type 2 diabetes.
The 2.5 A structure of a thermostabilized GCGR construct (StaR) in complex with the antagonist
MK-0893 revealed an unexpected allosteric binding site outside the seven transmembrane helical
bundle, between TM6 and TM7 extending into the lipid bilayer.
