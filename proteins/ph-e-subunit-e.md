# Subunit E of Pyrococcus horikoshii OT3 A-ATP Synthase (PhE)

## Overview

Subunit E from Pyrococcus horikoshii OT3 A-ATP synthase (PhE) is a component of the peripheral stalk that links the A1 catalytic region with the AO ion conducting region of the archaeal A-ATP synthase. The crystal structure reveals an extended S-shaped N-terminal alpha-helix (112.29 A in length) followed by a globular head group. The S-shaped feature facilitates storage of transient elastic energy during rotary motion in the enzyme, providing conformational flexibility in the stator subunits.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### S-shaped elastic connector

The extended S-shaped N-terminal alpha-helix of PhE (112.29 A) is common in elastic connectors and spacers. This feature facilitates the storage of transient elastic energy during rotary motion in the A-ATP synthase enzyme, as the peripheral stalks appear in different conformations (straight vs bent) in the intact enzyme.

### Conformational flexibility of peripheral stalk

The curved PhE structure fits well into the bent peripheral stalk S2 of Pyrococcus furiosus A-ATP synthase (correlation 0.7704), while the straight T. thermophilus E subunit fits the straight peripheral stalk S1 (correlation 0.7827). This supports the model of conformational flexibility in stator subunits that mediates interaction with the nucleotide-binding domain.
