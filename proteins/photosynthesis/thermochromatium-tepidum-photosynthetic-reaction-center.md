# Photosynthetic Reaction Center from Thermochromatium tepidum

## Overview

The photosynthetic reaction center (RC) from the thermophilic purple sulfur bacterium Thermochromatium (formerly Chromatium) tepidum is a membrane protein complex that promotes light-induced charge separation during photosynthesis. Tch. tepidum can grow at the highest temperature of all known purple bacteria (optimum 48-50 C, max 58 C), and its RC is stable up to 70 C in chromatophore and to 48 C in detergent-micelle. The crystal structure was determined at 2.2 Angstrom resolution and is the first structure of a thermostable RC. The RC consists of four subunits: L (light), M (medium), H (heavy), and a tetraheme cytochrome c subunit. The L and M subunits of Tch. tepidum RC contain three additional Arg residues at the membrane surface compared to mesophiles (Blastochloris viridis, Rhodobacter sphaeroides), potentially contributing to thermostability through interactions with phospholipid head groups. The structure includes the special pair of [Bacteriochlorophyll](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/bacteriochlorophyll/) (P870), accessory bacteriochlorophylls, bacteriopheophytins, quinones, and a [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) (spirilloxanthin with one cis-bond at position 15). One lipid and seven detergent molecules were observed on the molecular surface.
