# B800-850 LH2 Light-Harvesting Complex from Rhodopseudomonas acidophila

## Overview

The B800-850 light-harvesting complex II (LH2) from Rhodopseudomonas acidophila strain 10050 is an integral membrane peripheral antenna complex in non-sulphur purple bacteria. The 2.0 A resolution structure at 100 K, refined with TLS analysis, reveals a second [carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) molecule (RG2), the last five C-terminal residues of the alpha-chain, and a carboxyl-modified alpha-Met1 that ligates the B800 [bacteriochlorophyll](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/bacteriochlorophyll/). TLS refinement quantifies non-random thermal motions of B850 pigments that are functionally relevant for modulating inter-pigment energy transfer.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Apoprotein structure and membrane-spanning helix organization

The alpha apoprotein (53 residues, 49 observed) contains a four-residue N-terminal extended section, a three-residue loop coordinating a Bchl a molecule, a short 3(10) helix that penetrates the membrane surface, a seven-turn transmembrane alpha helix (residues 12-35), a three-residue section, and a two-turn amphipathic helix. The C-terminal four residues (KKAA) are disordered. The beta apoprotein (41 residues) has a four-residue N-terminal extended section, a transmembrane alpha helix (residues 13-37) inclined at ~15 degrees to the C9 axis, a five-residue C-terminal loop. The membrane-spanning portion consists of concentric cylinders with alpha apoproteins inner and beta outer.

### Protein-pigment coordination and hydrogen bonding

All Bchl a molecules have two specific protein interactions: Mg coordination through a histidine (B850) or formylated alpha-Met1 oxygen (B800), and a hydrogen bond to the C3-acetyl group on ring A (alpha-Trp45 for alpha-B850, alpha-Tyr44 from adjacent protein for beta-B850, beta-Arg20 for B800). The B850 Bchl a is in a saddle conformation with significant bowing along the long axis, associated with a hydrogen bond from alpha-Tyr44 to the ring A acetyl group causing ~100 nm red-shift potential. The torsion angle of the acetyl group differs between B800 (-30 degrees) and B850 (194/159 degrees for alpha/beta).

### Oligomerization contacts and transmembrane helix interactions

Oligomerization contacts are at periplasmic and cytosolic ends of the complex, predominantly side-chain interactions with only one beta-sheet type contact. In the transmembrane region, protein-protein contacts are limited to hydrophobic interactions between adjacent alpha-helices. Key residue arrangements include: C-terminal end (alpha-Ile34, alpha-Ala33, alpha-Leu35 interacting with alpha-Trp45), central portion (alpha-Thr24, alpha-Ile26, alpha-Ile28 trimer), and N-terminal region (alpha-Gly15, alpha-Ile16, alpha-Pro17 with alpha-Val10 inserting). The saturated phytol chain of alpha-B850 inserts between adjacent helices.

### Carotenoid coordination and mechanical effects

Rhodopin glucoside [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid) makes specific interactions only through its glucoside head group, which is disordered in the structure. The all-trans conjugated double-bond system is extensively immersed among bacteriochlorophylls with only two polar contacts (alpha-His31 and alpha-Gln3). A second external [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid) is poorly defined. The [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid) takes up a helical conformation in LH2 (imparting chirality and CD signal), due entirely to steric interactions in the complex. Bandwidths of Bchl a absorptions depend on the detergent encapsulating the complex.

### TLS refinement reveals functionally relevant thermal motions

TLS refinement at 2.0 A resolution revealed that B850 [bacteriochlorophyll](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/bacteriochlorophyll/) molecules undergo non-random thermal motions that modulate inter-pigment energy interactions. The largest B850 librations are about axes normal to the membrane (~6 deg^2 for alpha-B850, 12 deg^2 for beta-B850). The displacements are designed to optimize modulation of pigment energy interactions, with energy changes of ~370 cm^-1 within dimers and ~280 cm^-1 between dimers. This difference in coupling energy is similar in magnitude to changes arising from pigment displacements described by TLS tensors. This is the first time that LH2 pigment displacements have been quantified experimentally.

### Second carotenoid molecule (RG2) discovered

At 2.0 A resolution, a second rhodopin glucoside molecule (RG2) was discovered at the periplasmic surface. RG2 makes a severe turn (~50 degrees) and covers the outer surface of alpha-B850, making more extensive contacts with the B850 macro-cycle ring than RG1. RG2 may have more efficient Car-B850 energy transfer than RG1. The glucoside moiety of RG2 lies in a pocket created by alpha-Trp40, Ala43-Tyr44 and beta-Leu40-His41 with hydrogen bonds to glucoside hydroxyl groups through a network of eight water molecules.

### Carboxyl-modified alpha-Met1 ligates B800

The B800 [bacteriochlorophyll](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/bacteriochlorophyll/) is ligated to alpha-Met1, which is carboxyl-modified (not formyl or acetyl as previously proposed). The carboxyl oxygen forms a 2.04 A link to Mg2+ and hydrogen bonds to alpha-Asn2, alpha-Gln3, and beta-His12. This modification is proposed to be a determining factor in B800-850 LH2 formation. The presence of the carboxyl modification and the hydrogen bonding network strengthens this assignment over the previously postulated formyl modification.

### C-terminal extension of alpha-chain resolved

At high resolution with TLS refinement, unbroken electron density reveals the last five C-terminal residues of the alpha-chain: Val49-Lys50-Lys51-Ala52-Ala53. Val and Lys side-chains are clearly visible but the last two Ala residues occupy large volumes indicating multiple conformational states. This extended segment passes between the beta-chains of the neighbouring complex related by 2-fold crystallographic symmetry. Hydrogen bonds at the C terminus are made between neighbouring repeating units, whereas at the N terminus contacts are made only within a repeating alpha-beta heterodimer.

### B850 pigment ring organization and coupling

B850 [bacteriochlorophyll](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/bacteriochlorophyll/) molecules are arranged in a ring of 18 pigments composed of nine approximate dimers. These pigments are strongly coupled and at their equilibrium positions the excited state dipole interaction energies, within and between dimers, are approximately 370 cm^-1 and 280 cm^-1, respectively. The B850 Qy transition dipole moments are oriented 81.2 degrees from the membrane normal. The calculated energy changes indicate that molecular displacements contribute significantly to inter-pigment energy interactions and may be important for photosynthetic energy transfer.
