# Rhodobacter sphaeroides Photosynthetic Reaction Center

## Overview

The photosynthetic reaction center (RC) from Rhodobacter sphaeroides is a membrane protein complex that converts light energy into chemical free energy. The RC consists of three protein subunits (L, M, and H) and binds ten cofactors including bacteriochlorophylls, bacteriopheophytins, quinones (Q_A and Q_B), and a non-[HEME](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/) [IRON](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/iron/) atom. Early crystallographic analyses of seven site-directed mutants at 3.0-4.0 A resolution (Chirino et al., 1994) established that residue substitutions and cofactor alterations (heterodimer formation, iron loss) do not cause major structural rearrangements, demonstrating the structural robustness of the RC. High-resolution structures at multiple pH values (6.5, 10.0) and in both dark-adapted and illuminated states reveal the mechanism of [Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) reduction, proton transfer, and quinol release. The landmark 1997 structure at near-atomic resolution showed that Q_B moves ~4.5 A and undergoes a 180-degree propeller twist upon charge separation, explaining the temperature dependence of electron transfer from Q_A^-Q_B to Q_AQ_B^- and identifying two water-mediated proton pathways (P1 and P2) connecting the Q_B pocket to the aqueous surface. Additional structures were obtained by in surfo (detergent-based vapor diffusion), LCP, and [LSP](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/lipidic-sponge-phase/) crystallization. [LSP](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/lipidic-sponge-phase/) crystallization revealed that [Monoolein](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/monoolein/), the major lipid component of the mesophase, can displace native cofactors ([Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) spheroidene and [Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) Q_B) from their binding pockets. Co-crystallization with spheroidene restored native [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) binding. Novel syringe-based and in-tip [LSP](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/lipidic-sponge-phase/) crystallization techniques were developed to increase the number of crystals for serial crystallography. Engineered disulfide bonds were introduced to stabilize the RC structure, with two mutants (G(M19)C+T(L214)C and V(M84)C+G(L278)C) showing significantly increased thermal stability while maintaining photochemical activity. A double mutant I(L177)H + F(M197)H RC was crystallized and its structure determined at 2.6 A resolution (PDB 8C3F), revealing that a new water molecule from the beta-side of BChl B_B is responsible for hexa-coordination of the central Mg2+ atom, and providing support for the correlation between the H-bonding network bulk and the oxidation potential of the primary electron donor.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Crystallographic analysis of site-directed mutants demonstrates structural robustness

Seven site-directed mutants of the RC from Rb. sphaeroides (2.4.1 and WS231 wild-type strains) were analyzed by X-ray diffraction at 3.0-4.0 A resolution. The mutations fall into four categories: (1) cofactor-composition mutants affecting electron transfer (M202HL, L173HL, M214LH); (2) electron-transfer kinetic mutant (M210YF); (3) non-heme iron mutant (M219HC); (4) proton-transfer mutants (L212EQ, L213DN). In all cases, no significant tertiary structure rearrangements were observed despite altered functional behavior, indicating the RC is structurally robust. The L173HL and M202HL mutants showed replacement of a bacteriochlorophyll with a bacteriopheophytin (heterodimer formation) without structural perturbations. The M219HC mutant confirmed the RC maintains proper folding even without the non-heme iron, though with reduced stability.

### Two-position QB shuttle mechanism for electron transfer

[Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) at the QB site can assume two positions. In the dark-adapted state, QB1 is thermodynamically favored, with O1 of QB 7.2 A from His L190 Ndelta, disconnected from the direct electron transfer pathway from Q_A^-. Upon activation, QB moves ~4.5 A to the QB2 position and undergoes a 180-degree propeller twist, forming a hydrogen bond with His L190 that enables electron transfer from Q_A^- to QB. The activation energy represents the barrier between these two states, primarily from breaking the O1 hydrogen bond to Ile L224 backbone amide and the energy of the propeller twist.

### Water-mediated proton transfer pathways P1 and P2

Two distinct water channels connect the QB pocket to the aqueous surface. P1 proceeds ~23 A from QB via Glu L212 through the H subunit to the cytoplasm, approximately normal to the membrane surface. P2 leads from Ser L223 to Asp L213 via the H-M subunit interface, parallel to the membrane surface at the depth of the non-[HEME](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/) [IRON](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/iron/). P2 traverses residues Ser L223, Asp L213, and Glu H173 identified by mutational studies as critical for QB proton uptake.

### Binding affinity hierarchy of quinone species

The binding affinities decrease in the order Q_B^- > Q_B > Q_BH_2, ensuring proper function. Q_B^- forms multiple close (<2.9 A) hydrogen bonds at both carbonyl oxygens, while dark-adapted Q_B has only one hydrogen bond (NH of Ile L224). Ubiquinol has protonated carbonyl oxygens resulting in weaker binding, facilitating release and replacement by [Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/).

### High-resolution structure reveals new QB orientation

At pH 8.0 in the dark-adapted state, the structure was determined at 1.87 A. A new orientation of QB in the distal position was discovered, showing no ring-flip compared to the proximal orientation. This allows QB movement between distal and proximal positions without requiring a 180-degree propeller twist around the isoprenoid tail.

### pH modulates the population of the proximal QB position

The proximal QB occupancy ranges from 55-65% at pH 6.5-9.0 (dark) to 35% at pH 10.0. In the illuminated state, proximal occupancy is 50-70% at pH 8.0-9.0, also dropping at pH 10.0. Electrostatic calculations show the pH-dependence correlates with the protonation state of Glu L212 (dark state) and Asp L213/semiquinone (illuminated state).

### Bound lipid molecules refined in the structure

Three lipid molecules were refined: [Cardiolipin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/cardiolipin/) (CDL), glucosylgalactosyl diacylglycerol (GGD), and [Phosphatidylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylcholine/) (PC). The GGD head-group orientation differs from previous structures, and the PC is rotated by about 70 degrees, placing them parallel to CDL.

### Monoolein displacement of native cofactors in LSP/LCP crystallization

In RC structures obtained by lipid mesophase (LCP/LSP) crystallization, [Monoolein](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/monoolein/) molecules from the crystallization matrix displace the native cofactors [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) spheroidene and secondary [Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) (Q_B) from their binding pockets. The M162 phenylalanine side chain adopts a 'closed' conformation occupying space normally taken by spheroidene. Spectral analysis confirms absence of spheroidene absorption at 490 nm in dissolved in meso crystals. Co-crystallization with spheroidene (added in acetone to 2.5% final concentration) restores native [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) binding. [Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) soaking was ineffective at displacing [Monoolein](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/monoolein/) from the Q_B site, likely due to [Monoolein](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/monoolein/)'s high affinity.

### Thermostabilization of RC by engineered disulfide bonds

Engineered disulfide bonds were introduced into the Cereibacter sphaeroides (formerly Rhodobacter sphaeroides) photosynthetic reaction center to increase thermal stability. Out of five designed cysteine pairs, three formed disulfide bonds (at the protein surface), while two deeper pairs did not. The intra-subunit mutants G(M19)Cys+T(L214)Cys and V(M84)Cys+G(L278)Cys significantly increased thermal stability (>50% and >40% intact RC after 60 min at 48 C vs ~20% for wild-type) while preserving photochemical charge separation activity. Crystal structures at 2.3-2.85 A resolution confirmed disulfide bond formation and showed no significant structural changes beyond the mutation sites.

### Double mutant RC I(L177)H + F(M197)H reveals structural basis for altered pigment-protein interactions and H-bonding network

The crystal structure of the C. sphaeroides RC double mutant I(L177)H + F(M197)H was determined at 2.6 A resolution (PDB 8C3F), improving upon the 2.9 A resolution of the single I(L177)H mutant (PDB 3V3Z). The structure clarifies that only one new water molecule, rather than two, appears on the beta-side of BChl B_B, serving as the 6th ligand of the Mg2+ atom of this BChl. This water donates an H-bond to the imidazole group of His-L177, which also forms a covalent bond with the methyl carbon of pyrrole ring I of BChl P_A (N-C distance 2.06 A). The C2-acetyl group of BChl P_B shows a 29.7 degree out-of-plane rotation (vs 20.7 degrees in the single F(M197)H mutant). The H-bonding network near P_B is similar to F(M197)H RC except that the H-bond between Asp-L155 and water-C is not formed (distance 3.7 A exceeds H-bond distance), reducing the network bulk. The P/P+ midpoint potential is 555 +/- 10 mV, 20 mV lower than the expected sum of individual mutation effects, consistent with the reduced H-bonding network. The absorption Q_Y P band shows an 11 nm blue shift (854 nm, vs 847 nm for I(L177)H and 865 nm for wild-type), less pronounced than the single I(L177)H mutation.
