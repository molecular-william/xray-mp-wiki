# Rps. viridis Photosynthetic Reaction Centre

## Overview

The photosynthetic reaction centre from the purple bacterium Rhodopseudomonas viridis is a four-subunit membrane protein complex (cytochrome, M, L, and H subunits) that executes the primary steps in the conversion of light energy into chemical energy. The refined atomic model at 2.3 A resolution contains 10,288 non-hydrogen atoms including 14 co-factors: four haem groups, four bacteriochlorophylls b, two bacteriopheophytins b, two quinones (menaquinone-9 and ubiquinone-9), a ferrous non-haem [IRON](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/iron/) ion, and a [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/). This structure was the first integral membrane protein complex for which well-ordered three-dimensional crystals suitable for X-ray structure analysis were obtained.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Electron transfer pathway and cofactor arrangement

The closely associated pair of [Bacteriochlorophyll](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/bacteriochlorophyll/) b (the "special pair") near the periplasmic membrane surface initiates a series of electron transfer reactions upon photon absorption. At room temperature, the photoexcited pair reduces one of the bacteriopheophytins b within about 3 ps. From the BPh-b, the electron moves to the primary quinone acceptor QA (menaquinone-9 in Rps. viridis) within about 200 ps, and to the secondary quinone acceptor QB (ubiquinone-9 in Rps. viridis) within 17 to 25 us. Only one of two approximately symmetric branches of co-factors is used for electron transfer. The quantum yield for quinone reduction is very close to unity.

### Subunit interactions and water-mediated contacts

More than half of the 201 ordered water molecules are bound at interfaces between protein subunits and contribute significantly to subunit interactions. 108 waters are on the periplasmic side, 88 on the cytoplasmic side, and 5 are bound in the interior of the L-M complex. The L-M interface is very large. The cytochrome-H interface is small. Six of the putative sulphate ions near the cytoplasmic membrane surface could mimic negatively charged lipid head groups, neutralizing excess positive charges.

### Cofactor symmetry and transmembrane helix arrangement

The polypeptide backbones of subunits L and M, and the attached co-factors, display a high degree of local 2-fold symmetry with the symmetry axis oriented perpendicular to the membrane plane. Both subunits have five membrane-spanning helices. The cytochrome has internal local symmetry with four haem groups arranged along a linear chain leading from the special pair to the cytochrome tip, with alternating high and low redox midpoint potentials.

### Haem environments and redox midpoint potentials

The individual redox midpoint potentials of the four haems in the cytochrome subunit follow the sequence low, high, high, low for haems 1 to 4, respectively, or high, low, high, low if ordered with increasing distance from the special pair. Haem 3, closest to the special pair, has the highest Em (370 mV); haem 4 has Em = 10 mV; haem 2 has Em = 300 mV; and haem 1 has Em = -60 mV. The rationale for this arrangement contrary to the internal local symmetry is unknown.

### Carotenoid and quinone binding

The [Carotenoid](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carotenoid/) (1,2-dihydronurosporene) is located on the M-side of the RC core; its middle part is in van der Waals contact with [Bacteriochlorophyll](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/bacteriochlorophyll/). The QB (unibiquinone) binding site has partial occupancy (0.3); no significant electron density was found for the quinone tail. Ser L223 is involved in proton donation to QB, and Glu L212 is the second proton donor.

### Cis-proline residues

Six peptide units preceding proline residues adopt the cis conformation. The cytochrome subunit contains three (residues C6, C153, C330), subunit H contains two (residues H42, H79), and subunit M contains one cis-proline (residue M48); none is found in subunit L. The cis-proline residues are distributed equally between the periplasmic and the cytoplasmic sides of the membrane.
