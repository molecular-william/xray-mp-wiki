# Photosystem II

## Overview

Photosystem II (PSII) from Thermosynechococcus elongatus/vulcanus is a homodimeric multisubunit protein-cofactor complex embedded in the thylakoid membrane that initiates oxygenic photosynthesis. It captures sunlight and powers the photo-induced oxidation of water to atmospheric oxygen. The 3.0 A structure reveals the arrangement of 20 protein subunits and 77 cofactors per monomer. Structures with artificial electron acceptors (AEAs) bound to the QB site and a novel QD site were determined to elucidate the mechanism of quinone exchange and electron acceptor function. Sr2+-substituted PSII structures provided insight into the role of Ca2+ in the oxygen-evolving complex (OEC). Time-resolved [Serial Femtosecond Crystallography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/serial-femtosecond-crystallography/) ([TR-SFX](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/time-resolved-serial-femtosecond-crystallography/)) studies have untangled the sequence of events during the S2 to S3 transition of the [Kok Cycle](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/enzyme-mechanisms/kok-cycle/), revealing that a water molecule (OX) is inserted as a bridging ligand between Mn1 and Ca upon Mn1 oxidation from Mn(III) to Mn(IV), with the O1 channel acting as a water delivery pathway via a 'water wheel' of five mobile waters (W26-W30).

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Electron transfer chain architecture

In PSII, excitation energy is transferred from the antenna system to the reaction centre, where the primary electron donor P680 is excited, followed by electron transfer along the chain via pheophytin a to [Plastoquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/plastoquinone/) QA and then QB.

### Oxygen-evolving Mn4Ca cluster architecture

The electron density of the Mn4Ca cluster is best approximated by four Mn ions arranged as a hook-like structure. Four Mn ions numbered Mn1 to Mn4, with Mn4 ligated by Asp 170 of D1.

### Radiation-damage-free XFEL structure of PSII at 1.95 A

Using femtosecond X-ray pulses from the SACLA XFEL, the structure of PSII was determined at 1.95 A resolution in the S1 state without radiation damage. Compared with the synchrotron radiation (SR) structure (PDB 3ARC, 1.9 A), the XFEL structure showed Mn-Mn distances shorter by 0.1-0.2 A in the Mn4CaO5 cluster, in agreement with EXAFS and theoretical calculations.

### Artificial electron acceptors bind to the QB site

Three artificial electron acceptors (AEAs) - DBBQ, DCBQ, and PPBQ - were shown to bind to the QB site of PSII in place of native [Plastoquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/plastoquinone/). DBBQ and DCBQ binding was confirmed by anomalous dispersion from Br and Cl atoms.

### Novel QD quinone-binding site discovered

A novel quinone-binding site, designated QD, was discovered approximately 15 A from the QB site and 8 A from the previously reported QC site. This site may function as a channel or storage site for quinones destined for the QB site.

### Sr2+-substitution reveals role of Ca2+ in OEC

Sr2+-substitution of Ca2+ in the Mn4CaO5 cluster (creating Mn4SrO5) caused movement of Sr2+ outward from the cubane structure by 0.3 A relative to Ca2+. The bond distance between Sr and the W3 water ligand was elongated by 0.2-0.3 A, while the W4-Sr distance was unchanged. The W3 water shifted by 0.4-0.6 A and the W2-W3 distance increased from 3.3 to 3.5 A. This suggests W3 is weakly bound and may be a substrate water for O-O bond formation. The D1-Asp170-Sr distance elongated by 0.2 A, while the D1-Glu189-Sr distance shortened by 0.2 A. The PsbY subunit was found in one of the two monomers, confirming its weak binding to PSII.

### OEC architecture at 3.5 A — cubane-like Mn3CaO4 cluster

The 3.5 A structure of PSII from Thermosynechococcus elongatus (PDB 1SSL) revealed the oxygen-evolving center (OEC) as a cubane-like Mn3CaO4 cluster linked to a fourth Mn (Mn4) by a mono-mu-oxo bridge. The metal cluster ligands were identified as D1 Asp342 (Mn1), D1 Glu189 and D1 His332 (Mn2), CP43 Glu354 (Mn3), and D1 Asp170 and D1 Glu333 (Mn4). D1 His337 hydrogen bonds to the cubane O2 oxygen. A non-protein density between Mn4 and Ca2+ was tentatively modeled as bicarbonate, which may act as a tridentate bridge. The CP43 Glu354 ligand, part of a conserved Gly-Gly-Glu-Thr-Met-Arg-Phe-Trp-Asp motif in the large lumenal domain, highlights the role of this chlorophyll-binding protein in water oxidation. PsbO forms an eight-stranded beta barrel that stabilizes D1 loop and C-terminus conformations providing OEC ligands.

### Oxyl/oxo mechanism for O-O bond formation in PSII S3 state

XFEL serial crystallography at 2.15 A resolution captured PSII in S1, S2, and S3 states at cryogenic temperature using a fixed-target method. The S2 state adopts an open cubane form with Mn4 shifted toward Glu333. Upon S2-to-S3 transition, flipping of D1 Glu189 provides space for insertion of an additional oxygen ligand (O6), forming an open cubane Mn4CaO6 cluster. The O5-O6 distance was determined to be 1.9 A by analyzing residual electron densities at different bond lengths, consistent with an oxyl/oxo species and excluding superoxo (1.3 A), peroxo (1.5 A), and hydroxo/oxo (2.4 A) alternatives. Water molecules W665 and W571 become disordered already in the S2 transition, ruling out W665 as the substrate. The O1 channel widens via movement of CP43 Val410, allowing water entry. Glu189 serves as a pivotal residue coupling oxidation of the OEC with water channel opening and substrate delivery.

### Time-resolved sequence of the S2 to S3 transition

Room-temperature [TR-SFX](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/time-resolved-serial-femtosecond-crystallography/) at four time points (50, 150, 250, and 400 us) during the S2 to S3 transition at 2.01-2.27 A resolution revealed the stepwise sequence of events. At 50 us, D1-Tyr161 (YZ) oxidation and Glu189 movement away from Ca were observed. At 150 us, the Mn1-Mn4 distance elongated from ~4.8 to 5.2 A and OX (a bridging oxygen, as oxo or hydroxo) began to appear between Mn1 and Ca. At 250-400 us, OX occupancy reached near-maximum, and the OEC structure closely resembled the stable S3 state. The O1 channel waters (W26-W30) showed significant positional changes during the transition, suggesting a 'water wheel' mechanism for substrate water delivery. The Kb1,3 Mn XES first moment changes indicated Mn oxidation with time constant tau ~350 us, closely correlated with OX insertion kinetics. The data support a mechanism where Ca-bound water W3 is the source of OX, transferred to the Mn1 open coordination site upon Mn1(III) to Mn1(IV) oxidation, coupled with proton transfer to YZ via His190.

### Two chloride-binding sites flanking the Mn4Ca cluster

Anion substitution crystallography with Br- and I- identified two Cl--binding sites in PSII (Kawakami et al., 2009). The two sites are located on opposite sides of the Mn4Ca cluster at equal distances from the nearest Mn atom (Mn4-Br1 6.4 A, Mn2-Br2 7.3 A). Site 1 is close to D1-Glu-333, and site 2 is close to CP43-Glu-354, both of which coordinate the Mn4Ca cluster. Site 1 is located at the entrance of a proposed proton exit channel involving D2-Lys-317, D1-Asp-61, and D1-Asn-181. Br- substitution retains oxygen-evolving activity, while I- substitution (larger ionic radius 2.20 A vs 1.81 A for Cl-) completely inhibits O2 evolution, likely by blocking the proton exit pathway or reducing protein conformational flexibility. The Cl- distances from the metal cluster (>5 A) are consistent with EXAFS data and indicate that Cl- does not directly coordinate Mn or Ca but is bound weakly through hydrogen bonding and aqua-ion interactions.

### S1-to-S2 transition of PSII captured by room-temperature TR-SFX at 10 ms

Room-temperature [TR-SFX](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/time-resolved-serial-femtosecond-crystallography/) at SACLA-XFEL captured the S1-to-S2 transition of PSII at 2.4 A resolution (PDB 7cjj) with a pump-probe delay of 10 ms at 9.8 ul/min flow rate. A boundary excitation method was developed by altering the flow rate and monitoring W665 as a reporter to eliminate light contamination from the preceding flash. Structural changes during the S1-to-S2 transition were observed in three regions: (1) In the O4 channel, water W665 became highly disordered upon transition to S2, disconnecting the 15 A-long water chain from O4, with nearby residues D1-D61 and CP43-E354 shifting to narrow the space. (2) In the O1 channel, water W601 also became disordered, with its mobility propagated to W547, W536, and W571 through the hydrogen-bonded network. (3) In the QB site, QB moved 0.26 A toward the non-heme iron and its B-factor decreased from 123 to 101 A2, indicating tighter binding of the semiquinone QB- to D1-S264, suggesting the first protonation of QB occurs at this site. BCT moved 0.26 A away from the non-heme iron, suggesting its involvement in proton uptake for QB- protonation. These findings support water delivery through the O1 channel (Ca side) rather than the O4 channel for incorporation into the OEC during subsequent S-state transitions.
