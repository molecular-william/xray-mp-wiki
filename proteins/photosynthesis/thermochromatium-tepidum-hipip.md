# High-Potential Iron-Sulfur Protein (HiPIP) from Thermochromatium tepidum

## Overview

The high-potential iron-sulfur protein (HiPIP) from the thermophilic purple sulfur bacterium Thermochromatium tepidum is a soluble electron carrier protein that donates electrons to the photosynthetic reaction center (RC). The crystal structure was determined at 1.5 Angstrom resolution, revealing all 83 amino acid residues and a [4Fe-4S] cluster. The structure is quite similar to HiPIPs from Allochromatium vinosum (RMSD < 1.0 Angstrom C-alpha). The molecular surface proximal to the [4Fe-4S] cluster forms a large hydrophobic patch, while the distal side is hydrophilic and mostly negative. The HiPIP interacts with the cytochrome subunit of the RC through hydrophobic interactions near heme-1 (the most distal [HEME](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/) to the special pair), rather than electrostatic interactions as observed for cytochrome c2 in other species. The redox potential of Tch. tepidum HiPIP is +323 mV.
