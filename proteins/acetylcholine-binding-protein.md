# Acetylcholine-Binding Protein (AChBP)

## Overview

Acetylcholine-binding protein (AChBP) is a soluble protein from the land snail Biomphalaria glabrata that serves as a structural and functional homologue of the extracellular ligand-binding domain of Cys-loop nicotinic acetylcholine receptors (nAChRs). AChBP has been widely used as a surrogate structural model for the extracellular domains of Cys-loop receptors because it retains the characteristic ligand-binding properties and can be crystallized in both ligand-bound and apo states. The AChBP structure has been instrumental in understanding the orthosteric agonist-binding site architecture of Cys-loop receptors.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Carbamylcholine binding site in AChBP as a model for Cys-loop receptors

The AChBP structure with bound carbamylcholine was used as a structural reference for understanding the agonist-binding site in Cys-loop receptors. The superposition of AChBP (grey) with GLIC (green) in the ligand-binding region revealed similarities in the binding pocket architecture. Carbamylcholine bound to AChBP forms ionic interactions with residues in the binding pocket, including contributions from aromatic residues that participate in cation-pi interactions with the positively charged ammonium group of carbamylcholine. This structural comparison helped establish the location of the orthosteric binding site in GLIC and other pLGICs.
