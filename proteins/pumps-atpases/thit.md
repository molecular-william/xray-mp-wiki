# ThiT — thiamin transporter S-component

## Overview

ThiT is the thiamin (vitamin B1)-specific S-component of an energy-coupling factor
(ECF)-type ABC transporter from Lactococcus lactis. It is a membrane protein with six
transmembrane helices that binds thiamin with picomolar affinity. The crystal
structure of ThiT revealed the structural basis of modularity in ECF-type ABC
transporters, showing how S-components interact with the shared EcfT transmembrane
protein and EcfA/EcfA' ATPase subunits via a conserved alanine motif in the first
transmembrane helix. The thiamin binding site involves an intricate network of
hydrogen bonds and aromatic stacking interactions.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Modularity of ECF-type ABC transporters

ThiT is a substrate-specific S-component that interacts with the
energy-coupling module consisting of EcfT (transmembrane protein with two
coupling helices) and the EcfA/EcfA' ATPase subunits. The first transmembrane
helix of ThiT contains a conserved alanine motif that mediates interaction
with EcfT, illustrating how diverse substrate-binding S-components can
associate with a shared energizing module.

The thiamin binding site is formed by residues from multiple transmembrane
helices, including Trp-34, Glu-38, Tyr-74, Gln-80, Glu-84, Tyr-85, Lys-121,
Tyr-122, His-125, Trp-133, Trp-138, Trp-141, Tyr-146, Ser-147, and Asn-151.
The pyrimidine ring of thiamin is stacked by Trp-133, while the thiazole
ring is stacked by Trp-34 and His-125. Glu-84 provides charge compensation
and hydrogen bonding.

### Membrane topology

ThiT contains six transmembrane helices (H1-H6). The N-terminus is
extracellular and the C-terminus is intracellular, as determined by the
positive-inside rule. The L1 loop between H1 and H2 is mechanistically
important for transport function.
