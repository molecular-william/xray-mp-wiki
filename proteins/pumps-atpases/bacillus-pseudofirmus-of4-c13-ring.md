# Bacillus pseudofirmus OF4 ATP Synthase c13 Ring (Wild-Type)

## Overview

The [ATP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/atp/) synthase c-ring from the extreme alkaliphile *Bacillus pseudofirmus* OF4 assembles as a tridecameric (c13) rotor ring, solved by X-ray crystallography at 2.5-Å resolution. The structure reveals a novel type of proton coordination in which a protonated glutamate (Glu54) shares the proton with a bound water molecule (H3O+ or H2O), coordinated by three other amino acids (Leu52, Ala53, Val56) of adjacent outer helices. This represents a third type of ion coordination in [ATP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/atp/) synthases, termed the "E/D-only" type, where no inner helix residue contributes to ion binding. The c13 ring has a distinctive "tulip beer glass" shape with outer diameters of 63 Å (cytoplasmic) and 54 Å (periplasmic).

## Biological / Functional Insights

### New Type of Proton Coordination in ATP Synthase Rotors

The c13 ring reveals a third type of ion coordination in [ATP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/atp/) synthases, termed E/D-only type. Unlike the c11 (Na+-binding, mixed E/D+inner helix) and c15 (H+-binding, E/D+Tyr) rings, the c13 ring uses only a glutamate (Glu54) with a bridging water molecule coordinated by Leu52, Ala53, and Val56 from adjacent outer helices. No inner helix residue contributes to ion binding, and no tyrosine hydrogen bonds with the second glutamate oxygen. This represents a finely tuned adaptation to alkaliphily, where high-affinity proton binding is critical at low proton motive force.

### Hydronium Ion as Possible Coupling Species

The structural data raise the possibility that a hydronium ion (H3O+), rather than a proton alone, may be the coupling ion species, consistent with a hypothesis proposed by Paul Boyer over 20 years ago. At the current resolution (2.5 Å), crystallographic data cannot distinguish between a protonated glutamate associating with a water molecule and a separate hydronium species.

### Alkaliphile-Specific Structural Adaptations

The c13 ring adopts a distinctive "tulip beer glass" shape with outer diameters of 63 Å (cytoplasmic) and 54 Å (periplasmic), contrasting with the hourglass shape of c11 and c15 rings. The AxAxAxA motif (replacing GxGxGxG in neutralophiles) enables tighter packing and the c13 stoichiometry, which is advantageous for [ATP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/atp/) synthesis at the low proton motive force of alkaline environments.
