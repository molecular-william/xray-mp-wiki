# TrkA (ATP-binding Regulatory Subunit from Klebsiella pneumoniae)

## Overview

TrkA is a cytoplasmic ATP-binding regulatory subunit from the bacterium Klebsiella pneumoniae that forms a tetramer and associates with the [TRKH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/trkh/) potassium channel to form the functional TrkH-TrkA potassium transporter complex. TrkA is approximately 50 kD per protomer and forms a tetramer in solution as confirmed by size-exclusion chromatography. TrkA binds ATP, ADP, and NADH and is thought to regulate [TRKH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/trkh/) channel activity through nucleotide-dependent conformational changes.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### TrkA tetramer organization

TrkA forms a tetramer in solution, as demonstrated by size-exclusion chromatography compared with protein standards of known size. The isolated TrkA structure crystallized in P6122 space group with two protomers in the asymmetric unit; the other two subunits of the tetramer are generated by crystallographic symmetry axes. Each protomer contains binding sites for nucleotides.

### NADH binding to TrkA

The TrkH-TrkA complex structure reveals four NADH binding sites in the TrkA tetramer. The NADH binding site features a conserved GXGXXG motif with [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) alpha-carbons shown as key structural elements. Likelihood-weighted Fo-Fc maps calculated without NADH show clear electron density at the binding site contoured at 3 sigma.

### ATPγS binding to isolated TrkA

The isolated TrkA tetramer structure with ATPγS reveals eight ATPγS binding sites distributed across the tetramer. The binding sites were visualized in protomer A and protomer B with likelihood-weighted Fo-Fc maps calculated without ATPγS, contoured at 3 sigma. The protomer is colored by subdomain, showing two bound ATPγS molecules per protomer.

### N2-N2 interface dynamics

Comparison of the N2-N2 interface between the TrkH-TrkA complex structure and the isolated TrkA structures reveals conformational differences. Superposition of N2-N2 interfaces shows distinct conformations, suggesting that TrkA undergoes conformational changes upon association with [TRKH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/trkh/) or upon nucleotide binding.

### TrkH-TrkA interface

The TrkH-TrkA complex interface involves specific salt bridges between [TRKH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/trkh/) and TrkA subunits. Helix D3M2b in each [TRKH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/trkh/) subunit is highlighted at the interface. The approximate location of an unresolved loop between domains D1 and D2 in [TRKH](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/trkh/) is marked, suggesting this region may be flexible or poorly ordered.
