# Human SERCA2b — Sarco/Endoplasmic Reticulum Ca²⁺-ATPase 2b

## Overview

SERCA2b is a ubiquitously expressed isoform of sarco/endoplasmic reticulum Ca²⁺-ATPase that facilitates Ca²⁺ uptake from the cytosol into the ER lumen. It is a P-type ATPase containing a characteristic 11th transmembrane helix (TM11) followed by a luminal tail, which distinguishes it from its splice variant [SERCA2a (Cardiac Sarco/Endoplasmic Reticulum Ca2+-ATPase)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/serca2a/). The crystal structure was determined at 3.45 Å resolution in the E1-2Ca²⁺-[AMPPCP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/amppcp/) state, revealing that TM11 is located adjacent to TM10 and interacts weakly with the L8/9 loop and the N-terminal end of TM10, acting as an auto-inhibitory element.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### TM11 as an auto-inhibitory element regulating SERCA2b activity

The crystal structure revealed that TM11 is located adjacent to TM10 and engages in only limited interactions with other TM regions. The N-terminal end of TM11 interacts with Trp927 (L8/9 loop) via van der Waals contacts with Phe1018, while the C-terminal end (Val1029) is surrounded by Leu967, Leu970, and Lys971 (TM10). Disruption of these interactions (F1018G/V1029G double mutation) increased Vmax ~1.7-fold and decreased Ca²⁺ affinity ~1.5-fold, making SERCA2b activity resemble [SERCA2a (Cardiac Sarco/Endoplasmic Reticulum Ca2+-ATPase)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/serca2a/). TM11 serves as an intramolecular uncompetitive inhibitor.

### Positional comparison with PLB and SLN regulatory proteins

TM11 binds to a completely different site than the small regulatory proteins [PLB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/miscellaneous/phospholamban/) ([PLB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/miscellaneous/phospholamban/)) and [SLN](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/sarcolipin/) ([SLN](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/sarcolipin/)), which bind to [SERCA1a (Sarcoplasmic Reticulum Ca²⁺-ATPase 1a)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/serca1a/) at the TM groove formed by TM2, TM4, TM6, and TM9. TM11 is located adjacent to TM10 on the opposite side, suggesting a distinct regulatory mechanism.

### TM11 restricts TM helix bundle movement

Superposition of SERCA2b and [SERCA2a (Cardiac Sarco/Endoplasmic Reticulum Ca2+-ATPase)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/serca2a/) structures showed that the TM1–TM2, TM3–TM4, and TM5–TM10 subdomains in SERCA2b are shifted by 2.6°–3.4° relative to [SERCA2a (Cardiac Sarco/Endoplasmic Reticulum Ca2+-ATPase)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/serca2a/) due to the presence of TM11. TM11 residues (W1028, V1029) would undergo steric clashes with TM10 (L967, L970, K971) in [SERCA2a (Cardiac Sarco/Endoplasmic Reticulum Ca2+-ATPase)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/serca2a/), restricting movement of the TM helix bundle domain and contributing to lower ATPase activity.
