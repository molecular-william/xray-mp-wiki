# Na+,K+-ATPase (Bovine Kidney)

## Overview

The bovine kidney Na+,K+-ATPase is a P-type ATPase responsible for the
transport of Na+ and K+ across the plasma membrane in animal cells. The
enzyme consists of a catalytic alpha subunit, a supporting beta subunit,
and a regulatory FXYD2 (gamma) subunit. The alpha/beta subunits are
approximately 98/93% identical in sequence to the porcine enzyme and
87/65% identical to the shark enzyme. The first crystal structure of
bovine kidney Na+,K+-ATPase was solved in a high-affinity E2-BeF3- [Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/)
complex with bound magnesium (PDB 4xe5), representing the first structure
of an Na+,K+-ATPase in an E2-BeF3- state analogous to E2P. Crystals
belonged to the orthorhombic space group C2221 with one molecule in the
asymmetric unit, exhibiting anisotropic diffraction to 3.7 A with full
completeness to 4.2 A resolution. The structure revealed unbiased
electron-density features for bound BeF3-, [Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/), and Mg2+ ions, as
well as two [Cholesterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/cholesterol/) molecules.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### First Na+,K+-ATPase structure in E2-BeF3- state

The bovine kidney Na+,K+-ATPase structure is the first Na+,K+-ATPase structure in an E2-BeF3- state, which is analogous to the E2P phosphorylated intermediate state. Beryllium fluoride (BeFx) is a phosphate analogue that mimics phosphorylation at Asp374 within the cytoplasmic P domain, fixing the enzyme in the E2P-like state with inhibited ion transport.

### High-affinity ouabain complex in C2221 crystal form

The structure represents a high-affinity [Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/) complex, supported by: (i) BeF3- forms an E2P analogous state which binds [Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/) with high affinity; (ii) high magnesium concentrations (210 mM [Mgcl2](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/magnesium-chloride/)) facilitate Mg2+-dependent [Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/) binding; (iii) the overall domain arrangement is compatible with magnesium and [Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/) binding. The structure was solved in space group C2221, whereas porcine Na+,K+-ATPase structures were solved in P21 or P212121. The N domain is rotated ~10 degrees compared to the porcine E2P-ouabain structure, attributed to crystal packing differences.

### Bovine kidney as alternative native source for Na+,K+-ATPase crystallization

Bovine kidney represents a novel native source of Na+,K+-ATPase suitable for crystallization. Despite moderate resolution, the diffraction potential is comparable to porcine kidney preparations. The bovine preparation offers an alternative source for future protein crystallization. The alpha/beta subunits are about 98/93% identical to porcine and 87/65% identical to shark.
