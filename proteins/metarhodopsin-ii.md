# Metarhodopsin II

## Overview

Metarhodopsin II is the light-activated conformation of bovine rhodopsin, the visual pigment that initiates phototransduction in rod cells. This is the final active state in the rhodopsin photocycle, in which the bound chromophore 11-cis-retinal has isomerized to all-trans-retinal and the receptor has adopted a conformation competent for G-protein (transducin) activation. The crystal structure of metarhodopsin II (PDB 3PQR) revealed the G-protein-interacting conformation of the receptor, providing a key reference for understanding active-state GPCR conformations. The structure showed that the conserved R137(3.50) residue in the D/ERY motif adopts an orientation similar to that seen in other active GPCR structures, supporting the role of this residue in G-protein coupling.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### G-protein-interacting conformation of rhodopsin

The metarhodopsin II structure represents the state of rhodopsin after light activation where the receptor is competent to activate transducin (Gt). The R137(3.50) side chain adopts an orientation similar to that found in the beta2AR-Gs complex and active M2 receptor, supporting the conservation of G-protein-coupling determinants across class A GPCRs.
