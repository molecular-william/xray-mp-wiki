# SLC35A1 Human CMP-Sialic Acid Transporter

## Overview

SLC35A1 is the human CMP-sialic acid transporter, a member of the SLC35 family of nucleotide sugar transporters. It imports CMP-sialic acid into the Golgi apparatus for sialylation reactions. SLC35A1 is mutated in congenital disorder of glycosylation IIf, resulting in a lack of sialic acid on the surface of cells. Homology modeling based on Vrg4 reveals that SLC35A1 has a larger and more positively charged binding site than Vrg4, required to accommodate the nine-carbon sialic acid sugar group, which contains an N-acetyl extension at the C5 position unlike fucose or mannose.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### NIQM nucleotide-binding motif

The nucleotide-binding pocket of SLC35A1 has changed from the FYNN motif (found in guanine-specific transporters like Vrg4) to the NIQM motif, which contains a glutamine (Gln212) instead of asparagine. The extension of the glutamine side chain over the asparagine may help facilitate the recognition of cytosine, which is smaller than guanine.

### Expanded binding site for sialic acid

The binding site of SLC35A1 is both larger and more positively charged than Vrg4, required to accommodate the nine-carbon sialic acid sugar group. Unlike fucose or mannose, sialic acid contains an N-acetyl extension at the C5 position. The GALNK motif is replaced by a stretch of alanine side chains. Mutation of Lys272 (equivalent to Tyr281 in Vrg4) results in complete inactivation of CST.

### Key residues for ligand recognition

Tyr214 was identified as being important for ligand recognition in previous studies. In the homology model, this residue faces the membrane and probably anchors Glu212 to stabilize its interaction with the cytosine. The replacement of Tyr28 and Tyr281 with lysine side chains (Lys13 and Lys272) distinguishes SLC35A1 from Vrg4.
