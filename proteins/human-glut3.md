# Human Glucose Transporter 3 (GLUT3)

## Overview

Human glucose transporter 3 (GLUT3, SLC2A3) is a major facilitator superfamily (MFS) hexose transporter responsible for cellular uptake of glucose. It plays a pivotal role in metabolic homeostasis and is highly expressed in various cancer types, making it a potential therapeutic target. This paper presents the crystal structure of GLUT3 in complex with the exofacial inhibitor SA47 at 2.3 A resolution, revealing the molecular basis for exofacial inhibition of GLUTs. GLUT3 shares structural similarity with GLUT1 and the E. coli xylose transporter XylE.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Exofacial inhibitor binding site

The SA47-bound GLUT3 structure reveals that SA47 binds at the extracellular side of the transporter, partially overlapping with the glucose binding site. SA47 comprises four moieties: an aryl head, piperazine moiety, pyrazolopyrimidine core, and pyrazoloaryl tail. The pyrazoloaryl tail points toward the extracellular transport path, making it amenable to conjugation with insulin for glucose-controlled insulin delivery systems.

### TM7b conformational shift upon inhibitor binding

Comparison of GLUT3-SA47 and GLUT3-maltose structures reveals a local shift of Phe289 on TM7b, which rotates away from the central transport path due to steric clash with the benzyl group of the pyrazoloaryl tail of SA47. This may explain the increased affinity of SA47 over maltose. Asn286 also shows conformational flexibility, existing in two conformers in the SA47-bound structure.
