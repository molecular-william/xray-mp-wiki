# Signal Peptide Peptidase A from Escherichia coli (SppA_EC)

## Overview

Signal Peptide Peptidase A from Escherichia coli (SppA_EC) is a membrane-bound self-compartmentalized serine protease that cleaves remnant signal peptides after protein secretion. SppA_EC forms a tetrameric complex with four active sites, each formed by the tandem repeat of N- and C-terminal domains within a single protomer. The catalytic mechanism involves Ser409 as the nucleophile and Lys209 as the general base within the same molecule.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Tetrameric structure

SppA_EC forms a tetrameric complex with four separate active sites. Each active site is located at the interface between the N- and C-terminal domains of the same protomer, which are structurally tandem repeats despite only 18% sequence identity.

### S1 pocket difference from SppA_BS

The S1 substrate specificity pocket of SppA_EC is significantly shallower and narrower than that of SppA_BS. Three residue substitutions account for this: SppA_BS Tyr151 to SppA_EC Trp413, SppA_BS Ala120 to SppA_EC Glu383, and SppA_BS Gly226 to SppA_EC Arg487.
