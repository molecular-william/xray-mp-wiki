# Rps. viridis Photosynthetic Reaction Centre

## Overview

The photosynthetic reaction centre from the purple bacterium Rhodopseudomonas viridis is a four-subunit membrane protein complex (cytochrome, M, L, and H) that executes the primary steps in the conversion of light energy into chemical energy. The refined atomic model at 2.3 A resolution contains 10,288 non-hydrogen atoms including 14 co-factors: four haem groups (cytochrome), four bacteriochlorophylls b (BChl-b), two bacteriopheophytins b (BPh-b), two quinones (menaquinone-9 at QA, ubiquinone-9 at QB), a ferrous non-haem iron ion, and a carotenoid. This structure was the first integral membrane protein complex for which well-ordered three-dimensional crystals suitable for X-ray structure analysis were obtained.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Electron transfer pathway

The closely associated pair of BChl-b (the "special pair") near the periplasmic membrane surface initiates a series of electron transfer reactions upon photon absorption. At room temperature, the photoexcited pair reduces one of the BPh-b within about 3 ps. From BPh-b, the electron moves to the primary quinone acceptor QA (menaquinone-9) within about 200 ps, and to the secondary quinone acceptor QB (ubiquinone-9) within 17-25 us. Only one of two approximately symmetric branches of co-factors is used for electron transfer. QB accepts two electrons and two protons, dissociates as quinol, and is re-populated by quinone from the membrane pool.

### Subunit interactions and water-mediated contacts

More than half of the 201 ordered water molecules are bound at interfaces between protein subunits and contribute significantly to subunit interactions. 108 waters are on the periplasmic side, 88 on the cytoplasmic side, and 5 are bound in the interior of the L-M complex. The L-M interface is very large; the cytochrome-H interface is small. Six of the putative sulphate ions near the cytoplasmic membrane surface could mimic negatively charged lipid head groups, neutralizing excess positive charges.

### Cofactor arrangement and symmetry

The polypeptide backbones of subunits L and M, and the attached co-factors, display a high degree of local 2-fold symmetry with the symmetry axis oriented perpendicular to the membrane plane. Both subunits have five membrane-spanning helices. The cytochrome has internal local symmetry with four haem groups arranged along a linear chain leading from the special pair to the cytochrome tip, with alternating high and low redox midpoint potentials.

### Carotenoid and quinone binding

The carotenoid (1,2-dihydronurosporene) is located on the M-side of the RC core; its middle part is in van der Waals contact with BB. The Qn (unibiquinone) site has partial occupancy (0.3); no significant electron density was found for the quinone tail. Ser L223 is involved in proton donation to QB, and Glu L212 is the second proton donor.
