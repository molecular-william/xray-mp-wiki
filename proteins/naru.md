# NarU Nitrate/Nitrite Antiporter from Escherichia coli

## Overview

NarU is a nitrate/nitrite antiporter from the nitrate/nitrite porter (NNP) family in the major facilitator superfamily (MFS). It is one of two NNP family transporters in Escherichia coli, the other being NarK. While NarK is highly expressed during anaerobic growth in the presence of nitrate and plays a central role in nitrate uptake, NarU has important functions during severe nutrient starvation or slow growth. NarK and NarU share 75% amino-acid sequence similarity, suggesting a similar transport mechanism.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### NarU function during nutrient starvation

NarU has important functions during severe nutrient starvation or slow growth, contrasting with NarK which is highly expressed during anaerobic growth in the presence of nitrate. Both are NNP family members with 75% sequence similarity.

### Structural comparison with NarK

NarU shares 75% amino-acid sequence similarity with NarK, suggesting a similar transport mechanism. The previously reported NarK occluded state structure (PDB 4IU8, Mol A) showed fewer inter-domain interactions than the NarK occluded state structure, suggesting it may represent a partially occluded inward-facing state rather than a fully occluded state.
