# BpeF Multidrug Efflux Transporter from Burkholderia pseudomallei

## Overview

BpeF is a Resistance-Nodulation-Division (RND) superfamily multidrug efflux transporter from the Gram-negative pathogen Burkholderia pseudomallei. BpeF forms a tripartite complex with the membrane fusion protein BpeE and the outer membrane channel OprC, conferring resistance to [Chloramphenicol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibiotics/chloramphenicol/), fluoroquinolones, tetracycline, trimethoprim, and sulfamethoxazole. BpeF shows higher amino acid sequence homology (60% identity, 85% similarity) to Klebsiella pneumoniae [OqxB efflux pump from Klebsiella pneumoniae](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/oqxb/) than to [AcrB multidrug efflux pump](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) and [MexB (Pseudomonas aeruginosa multidrug exporter)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mexB/). The crystal structure of BpeF was determined at 3.0 Angstrom resolution (PDB 7WLV). Unlike asymmetric trimers of canonical HAE-RND transporters, BpeF forms a symmetric trimer composed of three "binding"-state monomers (BBB state), similar to the [OqxB efflux pump from Klebsiella pneumoniae](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/oqxb/) structure. This suggests weaker cooperativity among monomers compared to [AcrB multidrug efflux pump](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) and [MexB (Pseudomonas aeruginosa multidrug exporter)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mexB/).
