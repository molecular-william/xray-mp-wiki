# HI1470/1 Putative Metal-Chelate-Type ABC Transporter

## Overview

HI1470/1 is a putative metal-chelate-type ABC transporter from Haemophilus influenzae, encoded by genes HI1470 (ABC ATPase subunit) and HI1471 (membrane-spanning subunit). The transporter exhibits 24% and 33% sequence identity to the BtuD (ABC subunit) and BtuC (membrane-spanning subunit) of the E. coli vitamin B12 importer BtuCD, respectively. The crystal structure of the intact, nucleotide-free transporter was determined at 2.4 Å resolution, revealing an inward-facing conformation with the permeation pathway opening to the cytoplasm, in contrast to the outward-facing conformation of the homologous BtuCD transporter.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Inward-facing conformation of an ABC importer

The HI1470/1 structure reveals an inward-facing conformation, with the permeation pathway through the membrane-spanning subunits opening to the cytoplasm. This contrasts with the outward-facing conformation of BtuCD, despite the similar overall architecture. The switch between inward- and outward-facing conformations involves a packing rearrangement of helices TM3-TM5 relative to the remainder of the TMD. In HI1470/1, a ~9° twist about an axis normal to the molecular two-fold axis repositions TM5 to close periplasmic access while opening the cytoplasmic pathway.

### ABC subunit arrangement in the nucleotide-free state

The HI1470 ABC dimer adopts a semi-open conformation, with the P-loop of one subunit opposing the signature motif (LSGGQ) of the other. Compared to BtuD, the HI1470 dimer has a more open arrangement: the separation between signature motifs on different subunits is greater (24 Å in HI1470 vs 16 Å in BtuD), and the P-loop-to-signature-motif distance across subunits is also larger (16 Å vs 14 Å). The ABC dimer most closely resembles the semi-open or post-ATP hydrolysis state of [MalK (Escherichia coli Maltose Transporter ATPase Subunit)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/malK/) (RMSD 2.7 Å).

### Internal symmetry of the membrane-spanning subunit

The HI1471 membrane-spanning subunit contains 10 transmembrane helices with internal symmetry between the N-terminal half (TM2-TM5) and C-terminal half (TM7-TM10), related by a two-fold axis in the plane of the membrane. This internal symmetry extends to the construction of the permeation pathway. TM2 is positioned through the center of each subunit, in proximity to most other helices. Helices TM3 and TM8 line the permeation pathway but adopt nonhelical, extended conformations through the remainder of the membrane.

### Coupling between NBD positioning and TMD conformational state

Comparison of HI1470/1 and BtuCD reveals a ~10° rotation of individual ABC subunits about an axis near the P-loop, with a ~4.5 Å translational screw component along the NBD-NBD interface. The direction of this translational shift coincides with the ~9° twist motion between TMDs, supporting a coupling mechanism where ABC subunit juxtaposition connects to the permeation pathway state. The outward-facing conformation of BtuCD corresponds to the more closed (ATP-like) state of the NBDs.
