# Archaeoglobus fulgidus ModB2C2 Molybdate ABC Transporter with ModA

## Overview

The molybdate/tungstate ABC transporter from Archaeoglobus fulgidus is a complete ATP-binding cassette (ABC) import system consisting of the transmembrane subunits ModB, the cytoplasmic ATPase subunits ModC, and the periplasmic substrate-binding protein ModA. This study determined the crystal structure of the full ModB2C2A complex at 3.1 A resolution, revealing the molecular basis of molybdate/tungstate transport across the membrane. The complex was trapped in an [ATP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/atp/)-bound, vanadate-inhibited state, capturing the transporter in a pre-translocation conformation. The structure provides insight into the coupling mechanism between the substrate-binding protein, the transmembrane domains, and the nucleotide-binding domains in ABC importers. The ModA binding protein was also solved separately at 1.55 A resolution with bound [Tungstate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tungstate/) (WO4) or molybdate (MoO4), revealing octahedral coordination of the oxyanion in a positively charged binding pocket.
