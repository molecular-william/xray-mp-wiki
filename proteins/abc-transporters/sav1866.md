# Sav1866 (Staphylococcus aureus Multidrug ABC Transporter)

## Overview

Sav1866 is a homodimeric ABC half-transporter from Staphylococcus aureus that extrudes cytotoxic compounds. It is a bacterial homolog of the human multidrug resistance transporter MDR1/P-glycoprotein. The original 3.0 Å crystal structure of Sav1866 (PDB 2HYD, Dawson & Locher 2006) revealed the outward-facing conformation of a bacterial multidrug ABC transporter in the ATP-bound state, with the two nucleotide-binding domains in close contact and the 12 transmembrane helices forming a central cavity shielded from the inner leaflet and cytoplasm. A subsequent 3.4 Å AMP-PNP-bound structure (PDB 2ONJ) confirmed the ATP-bound state.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### First high-resolution structure of a bacterial multidrug ABC exporter

The Sav1866 structure provided the first high-resolution view of an ABC exporter in an outward-facing conformation. The homodimer contains 12 transmembrane helices arranged with TM1-TM2 from one subunit and TM3-TM6 from the other forming each 'wing'. The NBDs are in close contact as in the ATP-bound state, with P-loops and ABC signature motifs sandwiching bound ADP between them.

### Domain-swapped coupling helices at the transmission interface

The intracellular loops (ICL1 and ICL2) contain short 'coupling helices' oriented parallel to the membrane plane that contact primarily the NBD of the opposite subunit (domain swapping). The conserved x-loop motif (TEVGERG) preceding the ABC signature motif in exporters likely transmits conformational changes from ATP binding/hydrolysis to the ICLs by alternating the cross-link between ICLs.

### Outward-facing conformation with central cavity exposed to extracellular space

A large cavity at the interface of the two TMDs is accessible from the outer leaflet and extracellular space but shielded from the cytoplasm and inner leaflet. The cavity is primarily lined with polar and charged residues, suggesting it represents an extrusion pathway with low affinity for hydrophobic drugs in the ATP-bound state, consistent with reduced substrate binding to human MDR1 in the presence of ATP.

### Structural basis for alternating access in ABC exporters

The structure supports a two-state alternating access mechanism: an inward-facing conformation with high-affinity substrate binding from the cell interior, and an outward-facing conformation (captured here) with an extrusion pocket exposed to the external medium. Tight interaction of the NBDs in the ATP-bound state is coupled to the outward-facing TMD conformation.

### Relevance to human ABC transporters and disease

Sav1866 shares significant sequence similarity to human ABC transporters of subfamily B (MDR1, TAP1/TAP2). The structure suggests that the ΔF508 mutation in CFTR (responsible for 70% of cystic fibrosis cases) destabilizes packing at the interface of NBD1 and ICL4, contributing to mutant CFTR misassembly. The analogous residue in Sav1866 (Leu437) is part of a hydrophobic cluster at the ICL2-NBD interface.
