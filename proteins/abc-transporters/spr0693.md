# Spr0693 Membrane Fusion Protein from Streptococcus pneumoniae

## Overview

Spr0693 is the membrane fusion protein (MFP) component of the MacAB-like efflux
pump from Streptococcus pneumoniae R6. It forms a hexameric nanotube channel that
docks onto the [Spr0694-0695 ABC Transporter from Streptococcus pneumoniae](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/spr0694-0695/) ABC transporter. Each subunit comprises three domains:
an alpha-helical hairpin domain, a lipoyl domain, and a beta-barrel domain. Six
helical hairpins pack into a cylindrical assembly forming an alpha-helical barrel
of 31 A diameter and 56 A height, creating a continuous tunnel for substrate
transport from the inner membrane to the peptidoglycan layer.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Hexameric nanotube channel with gating ring

Spr0693 forms a hexameric nanotube with three distinct domain layers. The
six alpha-helical hairpins form a left-handed cylindrical barrel of 31 A
diameter and 56 A height at the top. The lipoyl domains form a double-layered
ring-like structure at the middle. The beta-barrel domains form a stacked
ring at the bottom.

A narrow pore of approximately 10 A internal diameter is formed by flexible
loops (residues Asn211-Val224) from the lipoyl domains that point toward the
center. The innermost residue Gly220 has a relatively high B-factor, suggesting
the gating ring is structurally flexible and may undergo conformational changes
upon substrate access to prevent backflow.

### Comparison with MacA gating ring

Spr0693 shares 21% sequence identity with E. coli MacA. Both have a similar
gating ring formed by lipoyl domain loops, but they differ in the innermost
residue: Gly220 in Spr0693 versus Gln209 in MacA. MacA's six Gln209 residues
form an interprotomer hydrogen-bonding network thought to facilitate one-way
outward-only transfer. In Spr0693, Gly220 lacks this network, suggesting a
different gating mechanism with potentially broader substrate selectivity.

### Spr0693 augments ATPase activity

Addition of full-length Spr0693 significantly augments the ATPase activity
of [Spr0694-0695 ABC Transporter from Streptococcus pneumoniae](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/spr0694-0695/) to approximately two-fold. However, truncated versions lacking
the transmembrane (TM) and membrane-proximal (MP) domains showed no significant
enhancement, indicating that the TM and MP domains of Spr0693 are essential
for productive complex assembly and functional coupling with the ABC transporter.
