# Aq_128 (Aquifex aeolicus MATE Family Multidrug Resistance Transporter) — aMATE-1 Subfamily Prototype

## Overview

Aq_128 is a multidrug and toxic compound extrusion (MATE) family transporter from the hyperthermophilic bacterium [Aquifex aeolicus](/xray-mp-wiki/organisms/aquifex-aeolicus). It functions as an Na⁺-dependent drug efflux pump that confers resistance to fluoroquinolone antibiotics including norfloxacin and moxifloxacin. Aq_128 is the prototype of the newly identified aMATE-1 (archaeal MATE-1) subfamily, which is phylogenetically distinct from the previously known NorM, DinF, and eMATE subfamilies. The crystal structure was determined at 2.0-Å resolution (PDB 6Z70), revealing 12 transmembrane helices arranged into pseudo-symmetric N and C bundles in an outward-open conformation.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### New aMATE-1 subfamily of MATE transporters

Phylogenetic analysis of 386 MATE proteins revealed that MATE transporters cluster into six major clades: eMATE-1, eMATE-2, NorM, DinF, aMATE-1 (archaeal MATE-1), and aMATE-2. Aq_128 is the prototype of the aMATE-1 subfamily, which comprises transporters from ε-Proteobacteria and Archaea. Unlike previously characterized MATE transporters, aMATE-1 members lack conserved acidic residues (e.g., D41 in PfMATE is replaced by S40 in Aq_128) that are essential for transport activity in other subfamilies.

### Na⁺-dependent drug efflux mechanism

In vivo drug efflux assays demonstrated that Aq_128 is an Na⁺-dependent, rather than H⁺-dependent, MATE transporter. An inwardly directed Na⁺ gradient drives extrusion of norfloxacin and moxifloxacin from E. coli cells expressing Aq_128. The Na⁺ binding site is proposed to be located in the N bundle, involving a cation-π interaction with residue Y154.

### Substrate binding in the N bundle

Substrate docking experiments with norfloxacin and moxifloxacin, together with the observation of monoolein bound in the N bundle cavity, indicate that the substrate binding site is located in the N bundle. Key residues involved in substrate binding include W63 (gating residue at the junction between the substrate exit channel and N bundle cavity), Y33, Y66, Y154, E207, E249, and R250. Mutagenesis showed that W63A and Y154A completely abolish moxifloxacin resistance, while E249A and R250A substantially reduce resistance.

### Cation binding site and MD simulations

Molecular dynamics simulations identified transient Na⁺ binding events at residues E36, N174, H177, and E207 in the N bundle. However, mutagenesis (E36A, E207A, E36A/E207A) showed wild-type transport activity, indicating these residues are not essential for transport. The C bundle cavity is positively charged (R250, R400), excluding it as a cation binding site. Together with the functional importance of Y154, the Na⁺ ion is proposed to bind through a cation-π interaction at Y154 in the N bundle, with E36 and E207 facilitating Na⁺ entry.

### Structural comparison with other MATE transporters

Aq_128 adopts the canonical 12-TM MATE fold with pseudo-symmetric N (TM1-6) and C (TM7-12) bundles. Unlike NorM and eMATE transporters, which bind substrate in the C bundle, Aq_128 binds substrate in the N bundle — similar to DinF subfamily transporters but with key differences in active site architecture. TM1 remains straight across pH 5-8, in contrast to the pH-dependent bending proposed for DinF transporters. Aq_128 lacks the conserved acidic residues D41, N180, and D184 (PfMATE numbering) and instead has distinct hydrogen-bonding networks involving E36, Y136, and H177.
