# Thaumatin

## Overview

Thaumatin is a sweet-tasting protein isolated from the West African katemfe
fruit (Thaumatococcus daniellii). It consists of 207 residues including 1
methionine and 16 cysteines forming 8 disulfide bridges. Thaumatin is
commonly used as a model system for testing crystallographic methods, including
native-SAD phasing, due to its well-characterized structure, ease of
crystallization, and the presence of sulfur atoms from its cysteine and
methionine residues. At 5 keV, the calculated Bijvoet difference ratio is
2.8%, making it a suitable test system for low-energy native-SAD phasing.
