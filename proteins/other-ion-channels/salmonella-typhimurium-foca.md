# Salmonella typhimurium FocA Formate Channel (StFocA)

## Overview

FocA (formate channel) from Salmonella typhimurium is a pentameric integral membrane protein belonging to the formate/nitrite transporter (FNT) family (TC 2.A.44). It functions as a pH-dependent formate channel, switching from passive formate export at high external pH to a gated state at low pH. The crystal structure solved at pH 4.0 reveals that pH-dependent gating is mediated by the N-terminal helices of individual protomers, which adopt three distinct conformations — open, intermediate, and closed — to regulate formate flux through the channel.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### pH-dependent gating via N-terminal helix reorientation

At pH 4.0, the N-termini of StFocA monomers adopt three conformations: (1) open state — N terminus disordered, substrate channel freely accessible; (2) intermediate state — N-terminal helix narrows the cytoplasmic entrance but an open pathway remains; (3) closed state — N-terminal helix crosses the cytoplasmic entrance, blocking the channel. Within each pentamer, two channels are closed while three remain open. The transition involves an 80° rotation of the N-terminal helix across the cytoplasmic face.

### Electrophysiological demonstration of pH gating

StFocA reconstituted in planar lipid bilayers (E. coli polar lipid extracts) shows specific formate channel activity at pH 7.0 with reversal potential matching the Nernst potential for formate. Upon acidification to pH 5.1, formate current abruptly ceases in a reversible manner, directly demonstrating pH-dependent gating.

### Comparison with VcFocA and EcFocA

Previous structures of EcFocA (E. coli, N-terminal 21 residues truncated) and VcFocA (Vibrio cholerae, full-length at pH 7.5, 55% sequence identity) showed open channels. The StFocA pH 4.0 structure reveals that the N-terminal helices — disordered at high pH — become ordered at low pH to mediate gating, suggesting a different mechanism from the previously proposed O-loop gating model.
