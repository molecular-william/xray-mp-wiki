# AmP2X — P2X Receptor from Amblyomma maculatum (Gulf Coast Tick)

## Overview

AmP2X is an invertebrate P2X receptor from the Gulf Coast tick Amblyomma maculatum. It is a trimeric ATP-gated cation channel that shares ~40% sequence identity with mammalian P2X4 receptors. The crystal structure was determined at 2.8 Å resolution in the presence of ATP and Zn²⁺ ion, revealing two distinct metal binding sites (M1 and M2) in the extracellular domain that mediate divalent cation modulation of channel gating.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Two distinct metal binding sites in the extracellular domain

The structure revealed two distinct metal binding sites, M1 and M2. The M1 site is located at the trimer interface in the upper body domain, coordinated by Glu105 from one subunit and Glu106 from the neighboring subunit, and is responsible for Zn²⁺ potentiation. The M2 site is coupled with the ATP binding site, coordinated by Asp188 and the γ-phosphate group of ATP, contributing to regulation by Mg²⁺.

### Zn²⁺ potentiation mechanism via M1 site

The M1 site at the trimer interface facilitates Zn²⁺ potentiation by promoting structural changes in the extracellular domain that lead to pore opening. Mutation at the M1 site (E95A in rat P2X4) abolished Zn²⁺ potentiation, while mutation at the M2 site (D170A) did not affect Zn²⁺ potentiation.

### M2 site and Mg²⁺ regulation of ATP binding

The M2 site bridges the receptor and the phosphate groups of ATP, reminiscent of MgATP²⁻ coordination. In human P2X1 receptors, mutation of the equivalent residues (D170A/D171A) caused a 5-fold reduction in ATP affinity at high extracellular Mg²⁺ concentrations, consistent with the M2 site mediating Mg²⁺ sensitivity.
