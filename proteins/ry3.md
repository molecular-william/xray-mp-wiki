# Ryanodine Receptor Type 3 (RyR3)

## Overview

Ryanodine receptor type 3 (RyR3) is the calcium release channel isoform primarily expressed in brain tissue. It is structurally related to RyR1 and RyR2, sharing the same overall architecture of multiple globular domains interspersed by long alpha-helical regions. RyR3 contains three SPRY domains (SPRY1-3) and tandem repeat domains similar to the other isoforms. The SPRY1 and Repeat12 domains are highly conserved among all three RyR isoforms, suggesting conserved structural and functional features. Sequence alignments in the referenced study showed the finger substructure of SPRY1 is highly conserved among RyR1, RyR2, and RyR3.
