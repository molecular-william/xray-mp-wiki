# Interleukin-17 Receptor C (IL-17RC)

## Overview

IL-17RC is a single-pass type I transmembrane glycoprotein and a member of the interleukin-17 receptor (IL-17R) family. It shares only 22% sequence identity with [IL-17RA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17ra/) but has an extracellular domain that is 55% longer, containing four fibronectin type III (FnIII)-like domains (D1-D4). IL-17RC was traditionally believed to operate in concert with [IL-17RA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17ra/) to mediate signaling by [IL-17A](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17a/), [IL-17F](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17f/), and [IL-17A](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17a/)/F cytokines. However, crystallographic analysis revealed that IL-17RC forms a symmetrical 2:1 complex with [IL-17F](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17f/), competing with [IL-17RA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17ra/) for cytokine binding. This finding suggests that IL-17RC can drive IL-17RA-independent signaling pathways and classifies IL-17RC as a "tall receptor" due to its extended stalk region formed by D3 and D4 domains.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### IL-17RC forms a symmetrical 2:1 complex with IL-17F

In contrast to the expected asymmetric 1:1 complex with [IL-17RA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17ra/), IL-17RC binds symmetrically on both sides of the [IL-17F](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17f/) homodimer, forming a 2:1 complex. Each IL-17RC chain buries approximately 1,600 A^2 of surface area on [IL-17F](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17f/), with two-thirds contributed by the D1 domain. The two receptor chains do not interact with each other except for a weak contact between their D3 domains (2 x 70 A^2 combined buried surface). The complex brings the juxtamembrane D4 domains in close proximity, with transmembrane regions approximately 39 A apart.

### IL-17RC and IL-17RA compete for the same binding surface on IL-17F

The footprints of IL-17RC and [IL-17RA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17ra/) on [IL-17F](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17f/) overlap extensively, with both receptors interacting with the same three binding sub-sites (S1-S3) on [IL-17F](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17f/). IL-17RC blocks the [IL-17RA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17ra/) binding site, paradoxically competing with its co-receptor for cytokine binding. The R1 binding motif is presented by the betaA-betaA loop of D1, stabilized by disulfide bridge Cys48-Cys137. R2 is at the tip of the betaC-betaC hairpin of D1, and R3 is contributed by the betaF-betaG loop and betaG strand of D2.

### IL-17RC is a tall receptor with extended stalk domains

IL-17RC has an extracellular domain 55% longer than [IL-17RA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17ra/), with D3 and D4 domains forming a sturdy stalk that positions the cytokine-binding D1-D2 domains approximately 40 A above the cell surface. This "tall receptor" architecture contrasts with the loose connection of [IL-17RA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17ra/) ECD to its transmembrane region. IL-17RC is classified alongside IL-17RE as tall receptors, while [IL-17RA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17ra/), IL-17RB, and IL-17RD have short extracellular domains.

### IL-17RC can form homodimeric signaling complexes

The symmetrical IL-17RC:[IL-17F](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17f/) complex provides a structural basis for IL-17RA-independent signaling. The juxtamembrane stem domains are brought into close proximity by ligand-mediated receptor homodimerization, bringing the intracellular SEFIR domains of the two IL-17RC subunits into close vicinity. This likely represents the signaling complex responsible for IL-17RC-driven il33 gene expression observed in il17ra-deficient mouse lung epithelial cells.

### IL-17RC binds IL-17A and IL-17A/F in 2:1 complexes

Using [SEC-MALS](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/quality-assessment/sec-mals/) and SEC-HPLC, IL-17RC was shown to form 2:1 complexes not only with [IL-17F](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17f/) but also with [IL-17A](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17a/) and the [IL-17A](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17a/)/F heterodimer. In contrast, [IL-17A](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17a/) readily drives formation of the heteromeric IL-17RC:[IL-17RA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17ra/):[IL-17A](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17a/) complex, while [IL-17F](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17f/) has a much lower propensity for assembling the heteromeric complex, requiring approximately 250-fold higher concentrations than [IL-17A](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/receptors-signaling/il-17a/) in [TR-FRET](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/quality-assessment/tr-fret/) assays.
