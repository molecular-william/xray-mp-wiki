# Rhodopseudomonas viridis Reaction Center

## Overview

The photosynthetic reaction center (RC) from the purple bacterium *Rhodopseudomonas viridis* is a multi-subunit membrane protein complex that executes the primary steps of light energy to chemical energy conversion. It consists of four protein subunits (cytochrome, M, L, H) and 14 cofactors. This was the first integral membrane protein complex to be solved by X-ray crystallography (Nobel Prize in Chemistry 1988).

## Structure Determination

- **PDB ID**: 2RCR (refined 2.3 Å model)
- **Resolution**: 2.3 Å (refined from original 3.0 Å)
- **Method**: X-ray crystallography, molecular replacement
- **R-value**: 0.193
- **R-free**: Not reported (pre-R-free era)
- **Non-hydrogen atoms**: 10,288 (10,045 with well-defined density)
- **Average coordinate error**: 0.26 Å (Luzzati plot)
- **Subunits**: Cytochrome (336 residues), M (323), L (273), H (258)
- **Reference**: Deisenhofer, Epp, Sinning & Michel, J. Mol. Biol. (1995) 246, 429–457

## Cofactors

- 4 haem groups (covalently attached to cytochrome subunit)
- 4 BChl-b (bacteriochlorophyll-b)
- 2 BPh-b (bacteriopheophytin-b)
- 2 quinones (menaquinone-9 at QA, ubiquinone-9 at QB)
- 1 ferrous non-haem iron ion
- 1 carotenoid (1,2-di-hydronuporene)

## Expression

- **Source**: Native *Rhodopseudomonas viridis* cells (no heterologous expression)
- **Growth**: Purple bacteria grown anaerobically under illumination

## Solubilization and Purification

- **Solubilization**: OG (n-octyl β-D-glucopyranoside) or similar detergent from thylakoid membranes
- **Purification**: Differential centrifugation followed by detergent solubilization
- **Note**: This is a 1984/1985 paper — purification methods were less standardized than modern protocols. The original isolation used mild detergent extraction from bacterial membranes.

## Crystallization

- **Method**: Vapor diffusion (original 1984 structure)
- **Detergent in crystal**: One molecule of **LDAO** (lauryldimethylamine N-oxide) resolved at high confidence
  - Head group at detergent region edge, hydrocarbon chain points inward
  - Second LDAO molecule tentatively modeled (partial density, 9-carbon chain)
  - Located near periplasmic membrane surface at L/M/H subunit interface
- Most detergent in RC crystals appears disordered

## Solvent in Crystal Structure

- **201 water molecules**: 108 periplasmic, 88 cytoplasmic, 5 in L-M interior
  - Water molecules form hydrogen bonds to accessory BChl and near QB binding site
  - Proton transfer to doubly reduced QB
- **7 sulfate ions**: exclusively on cytoplasmic side near L-M/H interface
- Unidentified density features: possibly heptane-1,2,3-triol, detergent, or water clusters
