# Ryanodine Receptor Type 1 (RyR1)

## Overview

Ryanodine receptor type 1 (RyR1) is the calcium release channel isoform primarily expressed in skeletal muscle. It is a large ~2.2 MDa protein consisting of ~1645 amino acids that forms mushroom-shaped tetrameric assemblies. The stalk crosses the membrane while the cytosolic cap contains multiple globular domains including three SPRY domains (SPRY1-3), tandem repeat domains (Repeat12, Repeat34), and the N-terminal domains A, B, and C. RyR1 is triggered by cytosolic Ca2+, luminal Ca2+, or conformational changes in the skeletal muscle dihydropyridine receptor. It governs several Ca2+-dependent processes including the contraction of skeletal and cardiac muscle. Multiple disease mutations have been mapped to RyR1 domains, including malignant hyperthermia mutations in Repeat12 and central core disease mutations in SPRY1.
