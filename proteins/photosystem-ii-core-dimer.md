# Photosystem II core dimer from Thermosynechococcus elongatus

## Overview

The Photosystem II (PSII) core dimer from the thermophilic cyanobacterium Thermosynechococcus elongatus has been solved at multiple resolutions by X-ray crystallography. The 3.5 A structure (PDB 1S5L) revealed a homodimer with the membrane integral part measuring 190 A x 100 A and 40 A thick. Time-resolved serial femtosecond crystallography (TR-SFX) structures have been determined at 5 A (dark S1 state, PDB 4PBU) and 5.5 A (double-flash putative S3 state, PDB 4Q54), revealing significant conformational changes at the oxygen-evolving complex and electron acceptor side during the Kok cycle of water oxidation.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### D1/D2 reaction center architecture

The D1 and D2 subunits form the reaction center core with each containing five transmembrane alpha-helices arranged in two semicircles interlocked in a handshake motif related by the pseudo-C2 axis. The alpha-helices C and D of each subunit are connected by a long alpha-helix CD on the luminal side. The short alpha-helix DE on the stromal side connects alpha-helices D and E. Subunits D1 and D2 are distinguished by the position of the manganese cluster, which is coordinated by D1, and the QA binding site, which is located at D2. The arrangement of alpha-helices of D1 and D2 resembles that of subunits L and M in the purple bacterial reaction centre.

### Inner antenna organization (CP43 and CP47)

CP43 and CP47 are inner antenna subunits that flank both sides of the D1/D2/cytochrome b-559 complex. CP43 contains 12 chlorophyll a molecules and CP47 contains 14 chlorophyll a molecules, totaling 26 chlorophyll a in the core dimer. Both subunits consist of six transmembrane alpha-helices arranged as a trimer of dimers related by the pseudo-C2 axis. The center-to-center distances between pairs of nearest chlorophylls range from 8.5 to 13.5 A. The chlorophyll a molecules occupy two layers close to the stromal and luminal sides of the membrane.

### Electron transfer chain symmetry

The cofactors of the electron transfer chain form two branches organized symmetrically along the pseudo-C2 axis, assigned to the D1 and D2 proteins by analogy with the purple bacterial reaction centre. Towards the luminal side, two chlorophyll a molecules PD1 and PD2 are observed with a Mg-Mg distance of 10 A and parallel head groups with 5 A interplanar distance. The symmetric arrangement supports the hypothesis that all photosynthetic reaction centres evolved from a common ancestor.

### Extrinsic proteins on the luminal side

Three extrinsic subunits are located on the luminal side of PSII: the manganese-stabilizing 33K protein (PsbO), the 17K protein (PsbV), and the 12K protein (PsbU). Cytochrome c-550 is also located on the luminal side, identified by its haem iron. The PsbO subunit forms a roughly 35 A long cylindrical arrangement of beta-strands tilted 45 degrees against the membrane. The 12K subunit (PsbU) was not unambiguously located in the electron density map due to unassigned secondary structure elements.

### Time-resolved conformational changes in the S-state cycle

TR-SFX structures of PSII in the dark S1 state (PDB 4PBU, 5 A) and double-flash putative S3 state (PDB 4Q54, 5.5 A) reveal significant conformational changes. The Mn4CaO5 cluster elongates in the S3 state with increased distance between the Mn3CaOx cubane and the dangler Mn (Mn4), potentially allowing binding of the second substrate water molecule. At the acceptor side, changes in electron density at the loop regions and the position of the non-haem iron are observed. The CD loop (including D170) may move away from the cluster while the AB loop (with D61) moves closer. Unit cell dimensions increase upon double-flash excitation (a: 133 to 137 A), and these changes reverse upon triple-flash reaching putative S4 state.
