# ASBT_Yf (Apical Sodium-Dependent Bile Acid Transporter from Yersinia frederiksenii)

## Overview

ASBT_Yf (Apical Sodium-Dependent Bile Acid Transporter from Yersinia frederiksenii) is a bacterial homologue of the human apical sodium-dependent bile acid transporter ASBT (SLC10A2) and Na+-taurocholate co-transporting polypeptide NTCP (SLC10A1). ASBT transporters are involved in the enterohepatic recirculation of bile acids and are drug targets for hypercholesterolaemia. Two crystal structures of ASBT_Yf were solved in a lipid environment at 1.95 A (wild-type, inward-open conformation) and 2.5 A (E254A mutant, outward-open conformation) resolution, revealing the alternating-access mechanism of bile acid transport. ASBT_Yf has ten transmembrane segments divided into a panel domain (TM1, 2, 6, 7) and a core domain (TM3-5, 8-10) with internal two-fold pseudosymmetry. The structures show that a large rigid-body rotation of the core domain relative to the panel domain provides alternating access to the central substrate-binding site, with the conserved crossover region (unwound TM4 and TM9) forming a dual-accessibility pathway.
