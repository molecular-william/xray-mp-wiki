# GluN1-GluN2B NMDA Receptor (Xenopus laevis, Full-Length)

## Overview

The full-length N-methyl-D-aspartate (NMDA) receptor composed of GluN1 and GluN2B subunits from Xenopus laevis, solved by X-ray crystallography and cryo-EM. NMDA receptors are ligand-gated ion channels that serve as coincidence detectors in excitatory synaptic transmission, requiring both glycine and glutamate binding plus membrane depolarization to relieve voltage-dependent magnesium block. X-ray structures reveal the closed-blocked channel state with mushroom-like architecture. Cryo-EM structures at resolutions of 5.0-6.8 Angstroms reveal three distinct conformational states (active, non-active1, non-active2) captured in the presence of glycine and L-glutamate without allosteric inhibitors.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### ATD opening drives receptor activation

Upon agonist binding (glycine and L-glutamate) in the absence of ifenprodil, the GluN2B ATD opens by 14-21 degrees, increasing the distance between the two GluN1 ATDs by up to 29 Angstroms compared to the ifenprodil-bound inhibited state. The active conformation shows the ATD opening by approximately 22 degrees and rotation of GluN1b-GluN2B heterodimeric pairs by 12 degrees, strikingly similar to the apo-GluN1b-GluN2B ATD crystal structure.

### LBD rotation causes gating ring dilation

Transition from non-active to active conformation involves 13.5 degree rotation of GluN1b-GluN2B LBD heterodimers. This movement causes vertical displacement of GluN1b Arg684 and lateral separation of GluN2B Glu658 by 7 and 11 Angstroms respectively at the LBD-TMD linkers, dilating the gating ring formed by M3 helices of the transmembrane domain. This concerted ATD-LBD movement likely leads to ion channel gating.

### Three distinct conformational states

3D classification of cryo-EM data reveals three distinct conformations: non-active1, non-active2, and active. Both non-active states have agonists bound to LBD but closed ion channels. Non-active2 has a 7 degree more open GluN2B ATD than non-active1, resulting in 13 Angstrom larger separation between GluN1 ATDs. The active state combines ATD opening with subunit rotation, structurally and functionally consistent with a pre-open or active conformation.
