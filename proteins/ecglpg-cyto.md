# Cytoplasmic Domain of Escherichia coli Rhomboid Protease  (

## Overview

The cytoplasmic domain of Escherichia coli  [GlpG(/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/ecglpg-cyto/) (ecGlpG-cyto) is an N-terminal soluble domain of the membrane-embedded serine protease ecGlpG. The crystal structure at 1.35 A resolution reveals extensive three-dimensional [domain swapping](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/domain-swapping/) between two monomers, forming a dimer. Kinetic analysis shows that removal of the cytoplasmic domain does not alter the catalytic parameters for the full-length enzyme, suggesting the domain does not regulate catalytic activity but may have other regulatory or targeting roles.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Domain-swapped dimer structure

The ecGlpG cytoplasmic domain exists as a [domain-swapped](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/domain-swapping/) dimer in the crystal structure, with extensive exchange of the N-terminal region between two monomers. The hinge region (residues 32-34) mediates the domain swap. The dimeric form is physiologically relevant as it can be isolated from the full-length protein (20% dimeric form).

### Cytoplasmic domain does not regulate catalytic activity

Steady-state kinetic analysis with  FL casein and psTatA substrates shows that removal of the cytoplasmic domain does not significantly alter the catalytic parameters ([Km(/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/), , kcat/Km) of [ecGlpG(/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/ecglpg-cyto/). Mutagenesis of 10 conserved residues on two different surfaces also showed no change in activity.
