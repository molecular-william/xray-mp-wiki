# Sphingosine-1-Phosphate Receptor 1 (S1P1)

## Overview

Sphingosine-1-phosphate receptor 1 (S1P1, also known as S1PR1 or EDG1) is a class A G protein-coupled receptor that binds sphingosine-1-phosphate (S1P), a bioactive lysophospholipid signaling molecule. S1P1 primarily couples to Gi proteins and plays critical roles in lymphocyte egress, vascular development, and angiogenesis. It is the target of the FDA-approved drug FTY20 (fingolimod) for multiple sclerosis. The crystal structure of S1P1 revealed a linear binding pocket with membrane-embedded ligand access, in contrast to the related lysophosphatidic acid receptor LPA1 which has a spherical pocket and extracellular ligand access.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Linear binding pocket with membrane-embedded ligand access

The S1P1 binding pocket is linear in nature, contrasting with the spherical pocket of LPA1. The position of TM1 in S1P1 is shifted away from TM7 by approximately 11 A (measured between C-alpha of Thr48(1.35) and Glu294(7.36)), creating a gap that enables access of hydrophobic ligands from the plasma membrane to the binding pocket. This membrane-embedded access pathway is a key distinguishing feature compared to LPA1, where TM1 is positioned 3 A closer to TM7, closing access from the membrane and forcing extracellular ligand entry.

### Key structural differences between S1P1 and LPA1

Three amino acid differences between S1P1 and LPA1 account for the distinct pocket shapes and ligand access mechanisms: position 3.33 is phenylalanine in S1P1 versus aspartate in LPA1; position 5.43 is cysteine in S1P1 versus tryptophan in LPA1; position 6.51 is leucine in S1P1 versus glycine in LPA1. These differences create a more constrained pocket in S1P1 and open a sub-pocket in LPA1. The short segment between TM1 and the N-terminal capping helix (ECL0) is helical in S1P1 but lacks secondary structure in LPA1, contributing to the different extracellular topologies.

### LPA1 and S1P1 share structural and functional similarity

LPA1 and S1P1 share 41% sequence identity in the transmembrane region and recognize lysophospholipid endogenous ligands. Despite these similarities, structural comparisons revealed substantial divergence in pocket shape and polarity. The relative orientation of LPA1 helices is similar to S1P1, but the primary point of divergence occurs at the tip of TM1. Molecular dynamics simulations confirmed that the distance between TM1 and TM7 is consistently larger and more variable for S1P1 than LPA1, both with and without bound ligands.
