# Ryanodine Receptor (RyR)

## Overview

Ryanodine receptors (RyRs) are large (~2.2 MDa) calcium release channels located in the membranes of the sarcoplasmic and endoplasmic reticulum. They play a major role in excitation-contraction coupling and other Ca2+-dependent signalling events. RyRs consist of multiple globular domains interspersed by long alpha-helical regions, forming mushroom-shaped tetrameric assemblies. The stalk crosses the membrane while the cap is located entirely in the cytosol. There are three isoforms: RyR1 (skeletal muscle), RyR2 (cardiac muscle), and RyR3 (brain). RyRs bind many accessory proteins and small molecules that affect their gating properties allosterically, including FKBP12 and FKBP12.6 which associate with high affinity and promote the closed state.
