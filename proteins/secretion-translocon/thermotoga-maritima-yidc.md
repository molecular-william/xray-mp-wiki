# Thermotoga maritima YidC (TmYidC)

## Overview

YidC is an evolutionarily conserved dual-function membrane protein that adopts insertase and chaperone conformations. The crystal structure of Thermotoga maritima YidC (TmYidC) was solved at 3.4 Å resolution (PDB 6Y86), revealing the N-terminal amphipathic helix (N-AH) for the first time. The structure shows an active insertase conformation with the N-AH acting as a membrane recognition helix lying on the periplasmic side of the membrane at ~15° angle.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### N-terminal amphipathic helix as membrane recognition helix

The N-terminal amphipathic helix (N-AH, residues 1-19) lies on the periplasmic side of the membrane at ~15° angle and acts as a recognition helix for localizing YidC to the lipid bilayer. The N-terminus is embedded in the membrane, and N-AH is essential for species-specific interaction with the Sec translocon.

### Dual insertase/chaperone function

YidC can switch between insertase and chaperone conformations. The C1 region (residues 282-308) shows high flexibility necessary for this switch. The EH1 helix can adopt different orientations: parallel to membrane surface (insertase) or inserted into bilayer (chaperone). MD simulations show the C1 region flexibility underlines its ability to switch between conformations.

### P1 domain orientation differences

The conserved interface structure (CIS) of the P1 domain adopts different positions in insertase vs chaperone conformations. The P1 domain of TmYidC (residues 23-224) consists of fifteen beta-sheets, one 3/10 helix and one CIS, differing from the EcYidC P1 domain (nineteen beta-sheets, two 3/10 helices and one CIS).
