# 5-Lipoxygenase Activating Protein (FLAP)

## Overview

FLAP (5-lipoxygenase activating protein, also known as ALOX5AP) is an integral membrane protein belonging to the MAPEG (Membrane Associated Proteins in Eicosanoid and  metabolism) superfamily. FLAP is localized in the nuclear membrane where it activates 5-lipoxygenase (5-LO) by presenting arachidonic acid (AA) to the enzyme, but is itself devoid of enzymatic activity. FLAP is a trimeric integral membrane protein with four transmembrane helices (α1-α4) per monomer. It shares less than 5% sequence identity with the related MAPEG family member . FLAP is a validated drug target for inflammatory diseases, and several FLAP inhibitors (, MK-886, DG-031/Bay X-1005, ABT-080) have been investigated clinically. High-resolution structures reveal that FLAP ligands bind in the non-polar center of the trimer, between α2 and α4 of one monomer and α1' and α2' of another monomer, with polar ends projecting toward the phosphate-exposed protein surface.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Trimeric architecture and ligand binding mode of FLAP

FLAP is a trimeric integral membrane protein with four transmembrane helices per monomer. Ligands bind in the non-polar center of the trimer, situated between α2 and α4 of one FLAP monomer and α1' and α2' of another FLAP monomer. The quinoline/pyridine substructure conserved among FLAP inhibitors is largely buried below the protein surface, with polar ends projecting to form salt bridges with basic sidechains (K116, H28) at the phosphate-exposed protein surface. The carboxylic acid group of FLAP inhibitors bridges K116 and H28. Unlike , FLAP activity is not dependent on  and does not have a  binding pocket, consistent with its lack of enzymatic activity. Each FLAP monomer also possesses a long acidic C-terminal tail that could serve as a handle for forming a complex with 5-LO.

### Comparison of FLAP and mPGES-1 inhibitor binding

Although FLAP and  are both MAPEG family members, their ligand binding pockets differ markedly. DG-031 binds in a bent conformation deeply buried in the FLAP trimer, but in an elongated conformation along the protein surface in  between α4 and α1'. In , DG-031 displaces  and binds to R126 and S127, whereas in FLAP, the ligand binds in the trimer core. The distinct binding modes create a challenge for designing dual FLAP/ inhibitors. Compounds 4 and 5 from this work showed potent dual inhibition of both targets in biochemical and cellular assays. Compound 4 at 0.24 µM covers IC50 for both targets, resulting in decreased production of both PGE2 and .

### Inhibitor binding in surface grooves (Science 2007)

The original FLAP structure solved at 4.0 Å with  revealed three ~750 Å³
grooves between adjacent monomers on the lipid-exposed surface of the trimer.
 intercalates between monomers, with key van der Waals interactions
involving V20, V21, G24, F25, A27 (α1); Y112, I113 (C2); A63 (α2); and I119,

### Internal pocket and AA transfer mechanism (Science 2007)

A ~3200 Å³ internal pocket at the trimer bottom, open to the lumen, was
identified. The pocket entrance (~6 Å) is formed by lumenal helices and loops,
constricted at mid-membrane by a symmetric P65 ring (minimum radius ~0.5 Å).
Above this sits a Q58/D62 ring. The pocket has a negatively charged entrance
and constriction but hydrophobic interior. AA is proposed to diffuse laterally
from the nuclear membrane into this pocket, coupled to structural changes at
the P65 hinge, then transfer to 5-LO at the cytosolic surface.

### Comparison with other MAPEG members (Science 2007)

FLAP is structurally similar to rat mGST-1 (RMSD 3.1 Å for 107 Cα), but lacks
a  binding site. Unlike other MAPEG members, FLAP is a non-catalytic
scaffold. The proline-induced kink in α2 (P65) is conserved. The C-terminal
region beyond G140 is disordered and unique to FLAP among MAPEG proteins.
