# Salmonella enterica Hydrogenase 1 (SeHyd-1)

## Overview

SeHyd-1 is an O2-tolerant membrane-bound hydrogenase from the prevalent human pathogen Salmonella enterica serovar Typhimurium. It has an amino acid sequence closely related to other [Fe4S3] cluster-containing [NiFe]-hydrogenases. Unlike [Salmonella enterica Hydrogenase 5 (SeHyd-5)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/sehyd-5/), SeHyd-1 is only expressed under anaerobic fermentative conditions and lacks the extra genes required for aerobic maturation.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Anaerobic expression

SeHyd-1, like EcHyd-1, is only expressed under anaerobic fermentative
conditions and lacks the extra genes that permit aerobic maturation.
This expression pattern is consistent with a protective role during
metabolic transitions rather than aerobic energy conservation.
