# JAGN1 (Jagunal Homolog 1) from Homo sapiens

## Overview

JAGN1 (Jagunal Homolog 1) is a human four-transmembrane helix protein involved in vesicle-mediated transport. It is localized to the endoplasmic reticulum membrane and is essential for proper protein trafficking and secretion. JAGN1 mutations are associated with severe congenital neutropenia. The crystal structure of human JAGN1 was determined at near-atomic resolution (2.0-2.5 Å) using the termini-restraining approach with split superfolder GFP (sfGFP). The JAGN1 structure reveals a unique structural fold that may allow specific interaction with proteins involved in vesicle-mediated transport. Prior to this work, JAGN1 was considered a difficult structural target because it only contains small exposed loops suitable for crystal contact formation.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Unique structural fold for vesicle transport

The JAGN1 structure reveals a unique structural fold that may allow specific interaction with proteins involved in vesicle-mediated transport in the endoplasmic reticulum. JAGN1 is essential for proper protein trafficking and secretion.

### Termini-restraining enables structure determination

JAGN1 was successfully crystallized using the termini-restraining approach with split superfolder GFP. Without the sfGFP coupler, JAGN1 only contains small exposed loops that provide insufficient crystal contacts. The restraining raised the melting temperature by 8.5°C and substantially increased the folded fraction during expression.
