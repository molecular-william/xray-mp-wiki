# Salmonella enterica Hydrogenase 5 (SeHyd-5)

## Overview

SeHyd-5 is an O2-tolerant membrane-bound hydrogenase from the prevalent human pathogen Salmonella enterica serovar Typhimurium. Like MBHs from Knallgas bacteria, SeHyd-5 has three extra genes that permit its aerobic maturation and subsequent function under air, coupling H2 oxidation to O2 respiration. SeHyd-5 activity could be essential when Salmonella gets engulfed by macrophages, which undergo a respiratory burst to generate toxic oxidants.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Aerobic maturation genes

SeHyd-5, like MBHs from Knallgas bacteria, has three extra genes that
permit its aerobic maturation and subsequent function under air, coupling
H2 oxidation to O2 respiration. This distinguishes it from [Salmonella enterica Hydrogenase 1 (SeHyd-1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/sehyd-1/),
which is only expressed under anaerobic fermentative conditions and
lacks those extra genes.

### Macrophage defense role

SeHyd-5 activity could be essential when Salmonella gets engulfed by
macrophages, which undergo a respiratory burst in order to generate
toxic oxidants as a defense mechanism. SeHyd-5 provides a mechanism
for the pathogen to oxidize H2 under aerobic conditions generated
during the respiratory burst.
