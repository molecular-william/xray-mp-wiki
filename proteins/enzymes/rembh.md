# Ralstonia eutropha Membrane-Bound Hydrogenase (ReMBH)

## Overview

ReMBH is a membrane-bound, O2-tolerant [NiFe]-hydrogenase from Ralstonia eutropha (formerly Alcaligenes eutropha). It contains an unusual [Fe3S4] cluster in its small subunit and couples H2 oxidation to O2 respiration. The enzyme forms a complex with cytochrome b, and its interaction interface with the hydrogenase shares numerous conserved residues and charge complementarity with EcHyd-1. ReMBH was proposed to form a (BSL)3 stoichiometry, which is at variance with the dimeric structure observed in EcHyd-1.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### O2 tolerance mechanism

ReMBH is an O2-tolerant [Fe4S3]-containing hydrogenase, sharing structural
features with EcHyd-1. The interaction interface between ReMBH and its
cytochrome partner contains numerous conserved residues when compared
with EcHyd-1, suggesting similar coupling mechanisms.

### Stoichiometry debate

The dimeric structure of EcHyd-1 is at variance with the proposed (BSL)3
stoichiometry for ReMBH (Frielingsdorf et al., 2011). The great majority
of residues involved in contacts between the H2ase molecules are conserved
in ReMBH, especially those from the S subunit.
