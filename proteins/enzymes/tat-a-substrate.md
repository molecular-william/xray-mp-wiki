# TatA Substrate of E. coli Rhomboid Protease GlpG

## Overview

TatA is a prokaryotic substrate of the E. coli rhomboid intramembrane protease [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/glpg). It contains the characteristic rhomboid recognition motif (Thr-Ala-Ala-Phe) at its cleavage site, with a small alanine residue at the P1 position that fits into the S1 binding cavity of [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/glpg). The P1 position specificity of [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/glpg) for small residues (Cys, Ser, Ala, Gly) has been demonstrated using TatA and its A8G mutant (alanine-to-[Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine) at P1), which is the smallest possible side chain at this position. TatA is part of the twin-arginine translocation (TAT) pathway in bacteria.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### TatA recognition motif and P1 specificity

TatA contains the characteristic rhomboid substrate recognition motif Thr-Ala-Ala-Phe at its cleavage site. The P1 position alanine fits into the S1 cavity of [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/glpg). The requirement for small residues at P1 (Cys, Ser, Ala, Gly) is consistent with the depth and polarity of the S1 cavity, which is formed by A182, S185, Q189, F197, and A253.

### P1 restriction by A253 mutations in GlpG

Mutations of A253 in [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/glpg) into larger residues (valine, threonine, isoleucine, leucine) progressively restrict P1 specificity toward smaller residues. The A253I mutation retains only 16% of wild-type activity on wild-type TatA (P1 alanine) but 87% activity on the TatA A8G mutant (P1 [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine), smallest possible side chain). This demonstrates that steric occlusion of the S1 cavity by A253 mutations selectively impairs cleavage of substrates with larger P1 residues while preserving cleavage of [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine)-containing substrates.

### Substrate binding model for TatA

A tetrapeptide model of TatA (Thr-Ala-Ala-Phe) docked into the [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/glpg) active site shows the P1 alanine side chain pointing into the S1 cavity, while the P2' phenylalanine fits into the hydrophobic S2' cavity. The P2 threonine and P1' alanine side chains face the solvent, consistent with the observed lack of specificity at these positions. Hydrogen bonding interactions occur between substrate amides and [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/glpg) residues G198 and Q189.
