# Human CD53 Tetraspanin

## Overview

CD53 (also known as TSPAN25 or OX-44) is a human tetraspanin protein belonging
to the TM4SF family. It is a four-pass transmembrane protein expressed on
leukocytes that plays roles in immune cell signaling, adhesion, and migration.
The crystal structure of CD53 was solved by molecular replacement using fused
GFP phases. CD53 forms antiparallel dimers in the asymmetric unit. The
structure reveals the conserved tetraspanin architecture with four
transmembrane helices (TM1-TM4), a small extracellular loop (EC1), a large
extracellular loop (EC2), and intracellular N- and C-termini. Sequence
conservation analysis of 33 human tetraspanins mapped onto the CD53
structure highlights highly conserved regions including the transmembrane
helices, the palmitoylation sites in the juxtamembrane regions, and key
residues in EC2. Unlike [Human CD81 Tetraspanin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/cd81/), CD53 lacks the C and D helices in EC2. [HDX-MS](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/hdx-ms/)
studies identified the EC2-Ig binding interfaces in CD53-CD2 and [Human CD81 Tetraspanin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/cd81/)-CD19
interactions.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### CD53 forms antiparallel dimers in the crystal

Two CD53 molecules form an antiparallel dimer in the asymmetric unit. Superimposition of the two molecules shows their structural similarity. Antiparallel dimerization may reflect how tetraspanins organize in membrane microdomains (tetraspanin-enriched microdomains, TEMs).

### Conserved architecture of human tetraspanins

Sequence conservation analysis of 33 human tetraspanins mapped onto the CD53 structure shows high conservation in the transmembrane regions, particularly TM1, TM2, TM3, and TM4, as well as in the EC2 domain. The N-tail and C-tail show low conservation. Unlike [Human CD81 Tetraspanin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/cd81/), CD53 lacks the C and D helices found in the EC2 domain of [Human CD81 Tetraspanin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/cd81/). The EC1 domain contains a small region of hydrophobic residues involved in EC2 interaction.

### EC2-Ig binding interfaces revealed by HDX-MS

Hydrogen-deuterium exchange mass spectrometry (HDX-MS) identified protected peptides at the EC2-Ig binding interfaces in CD53-CD2 and [Human CD81 Tetraspanin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/cd81/)-CD19 complexes. In CD53, a protected peptide (124-128, LKIQE) and a longer peptide (120-128, KIFDLKIQE) showed reduced deuterium uptake upon CD2 binding. In [Human CD81 Tetraspanin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/cd81/), protected peptides in the EC2 domain (171-180, 171-185, 181-185, 180-185, 182-196, 188-197, 185-197) showed reduced deuterium uptake upon CD19 binding. These experiments were performed in triplicate with error bars showing standard deviation.

### CD81 EC1 and cholesterol-binding mutations affect CD19 maturation

Mutations in [Human CD81 Tetraspanin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/cd81/) EC1 residues (A4, 44E, 45E, 46E, 47E) and [Cholesterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/cholesterol/)-binding pocket residues (219A, 219Q) alter the glycosylation pattern of CD19. The immature m' form of CD19 accumulates and the mature m form shifts to higher molecular weight bands, indicating that [Human CD81 Tetraspanin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/cd81/)-CD19 interaction is modulated by both EC1 contacts and [Cholesterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/cholesterol/) binding. Quantification shows significant differences in m'CD19 levels between wild-type and mutant [Human CD81 Tetraspanin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/cd81/).
