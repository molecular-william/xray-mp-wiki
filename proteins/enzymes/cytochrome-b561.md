# Cytochrome b561 (Cyt b561) from Arabidopsis thaliana

## Overview

Cytochrome b561 (Cyt b561) from Arabidopsis thaliana is a transmembrane
ascorbate-dependent oxidoreductase that plays a key role in ascorbate
recycling and [Iron](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/iron/) absorption. It is a homodimeric enzyme with each
protomer containing six transmembrane helices and two [Heme](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/) groups.
High-resolution crystal structures were determined in substrate-free,
ascorbate-bound, and monodehydroascorbate-bound states, revealing
the structural basis for substrate recognition and electron transfer
in the Cyt b561 family.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Homodimeric architecture

Cyt b561 forms a homodimer with a buried surface area of ~1,410 A2.
Each protomer comprises six transmembrane helices (TMs 1-6) with
both N and C termini located in the cytoplasm. The homodimeric
arrangement contains four [Heme](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/) groups total, with two per protomer.

### Heme coordination

Each [Heme](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/) is sandwiched by TMs 2/3/4/5. The cytoplasmic [Heme](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/) is
coordinated by His84 (TM3) and His157 (TM5). The noncytoplasmic
[Heme](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/) is coordinated by His51 (TM2) and His118 (TM4). The closest
edge-to-edge distance between the two [Heme](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/) groups within a protomer
is ~15.3 A. A water molecule and Phe129 are located between the
two [Heme](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/) centers and may participate in electron transfer.

### Substrate recognition sites

Two positively charged substrate-binding pockets exist, one on
each side of the membrane. On the cytoplasmic side, ascorbate is
recognized by Lys81, Lys77, Arg150, and the [Heme](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/) group via
hydrogen bonds and van der Waals contacts. On the noncytoplasmic
side, monodehydroascorbate is recognized by His106, Tyr115,
Asn186, Phe105, and Phe182 through hydrogen bonds and aromatic
stacking interactions. Lys81 (cytoplasmic) and His106
(noncytoplasmic) play essential roles in substrate recognition
and catalysis.

### Electron transfer mechanism

The proposed electron transfer path involves: (1) electron donation
from cytoplasmic ascorbate to the cytoplasmic [Heme](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/); (2) transfer
through bonding contacts (covalent bonds and H-bonds) to the
noncytoplasmic [Heme](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/heme/); (3) final transfer to monodehydroascorbate
or ferric-chelate on the noncytoplasmic side. The reaction can
support both ascorbate recycling and ferric-chelate reduction,
as exemplified by CGCyt b561 and DCyt b561 homologs respectively.
