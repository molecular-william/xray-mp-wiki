# AF2299 CDP-Alcohol Phosphotransferase from Archaeoglobus fulgidus

## Overview

AF2299 is a CDP-alcohol phosphotransferase (CDP-AP) from the hyperthermophilic archaeon Archaeoglobus fulgidus (UniProt O27985). The 2.0 Å crystal structure is the first reported structure for any CDP-AP family member, revealing a novel fold of six transmembrane helices arranged as two inverted repeats of three helices, forming a homodimer. The active site is located in a polar cavity within the membrane, lined by the absolutely conserved CDP-AP signature motif D₁xxD₂G₁xxAR...G₂xxxD₃xxxD₄. Structures in apo, CMP-bound, CDP-bound, and CDP-glycerol-bound states define functional roles for each of the eight conserved residues and support a sequential, base-catalysed mechanism in which the fourth aspartate (D239/D₄) acts as the catalytic base. The N-terminal cytoplasmic domain has structural similarity to cytidyltransferases, suggesting AF2299 may have evolved from a bifunctional ancestor. Based on sequence similarity to DIPPS (di-myo-inositol-1,3'-phosphate-1'-phosphate synthase) family enzymes and the CDP-glycerol-bound structure, AF2299 likely catalyses condensation of CDP-glycerol with L-myo-inositol-1-phosphate to yield a glyceryl phosphodiester osmolyte.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### First structure of a CDP-alcohol phosphotransferase

The 2.0 Å structure of AF2299 is the first reported X-ray structure for any CDP-alcohol phosphotransferase. The enzyme adopts a novel fold with six transmembrane helices (TM1-TM6) arranged as two inverted repeats of three helices (TM1-3 and TM4-6). The homodimer interface involves TM3 and TM4, burying 1,963 Å². All TM helices except TM5 extend beyond the intracellular border of the membrane.

### CDP-AP signature motif and active site architecture

The conserved CDP-AP signature motif (D₁xxD₂G₁xxAR...G₂xxxD₃xxxD₄) spans the cytosolic ends of TM2 and TM3, lining a polar cavity accessible from the cytosol. Structures in apo, CMP-bound, CDP-bound and CDP-glycerol-bound states reveal functional roles for all eight conserved residues: D₁ (D211) and D₂ (D214) coordinate the primary divalent cation; G₁ (G218) and G₂ (G232) provide backbone flexibility; AR (A221-R222) interact with the CDP pyrophosphate; D₃ (D235) and D₄ (D239) are involved in catalysis with D₄ acting as the catalytic base.

### Base-catalysed mechanism universal for CDP-APs

A sequential, base-catalysed mechanism is proposed for all CDP-APs. D₄ (D239) acts as the catalytic base, abstracting a proton from the terminal hydroxyl of the acceptor alcohol, activating it for nucleophilic attack on the β-phosphorus of the CDP-linked donor. This yields CMP and the phosphodiester product. The mechanism is supported by the observation of alkaline pH optima in many CDP-APs and the clustering of four conserved aspartates which may elevate the pKa of the catalytic aspartate.

### Substrate binding pockets and specificity determinants

Two binding pockets within the polar cavity form putative sites for the donor substituent and acceptor alcohol. Arg240 and Arg304 coordinate a sulphate/bound anion and are conserved in enzymes utilizing inositol/inositol phosphate as acceptor. A secondary pocket lined by residues conserved in archaeal CDP-APs accommodates the donor substituent. The CDP-glycerol structure confirms glycerol binding in this pocket. Based on sequence similarity to DIPPS synthases, AF2299 likely uses CDP-glycerol and L-myo-inositol-1-phosphate as substrates.
