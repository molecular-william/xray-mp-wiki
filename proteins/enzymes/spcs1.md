# SPCS1 (Signal Peptidase Complex Subunit 1) from Gallus gallus

## Overview

SPCS1 (Signal Peptidase Complex Subunit 1) from Gallus gallus (chicken) is a small two-transmembrane helix protein that serves as a subunit of the signal peptidase complex. Signal peptidase is responsible for cleaving signal peptides from secreted and membrane proteins after their translocation across the endoplasmic reticulum membrane. SPCS1 is a major therapeutic target of Zika virus, as the signal peptidase complex is required for processing flaviviral proteins. The crystal structure of SPCS1 was determined at 2.0-2.5 Å resolution using the [Termini Restraining](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/miscellaneous/termini-restraining/) approach with split superfolder GFP (sfGFP). SPCS1 represents, to our knowledge, the first crystal structure of an individual two-transmembrane protein obtained by X-ray crystallography, and its structure suggests a regulatory role for this signal peptidase subunit during the maturation process of Zika and other flaviviruses.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### First 2-TM membrane protein crystal structure

SPCS1 is the first individual two-transmembrane helix protein to have its crystal structure determined by X-ray crystallography. Two-TM proteins constitute approximately 10% of the membrane proteome, making this a significant milestone for structural biology of small membrane proteins.

### Therapeutic target for Zika virus

SPCS1 is a major therapeutic target of Zika virus, as the signal peptidase complex is required for processing flaviviral proteins. The structure of SPCS1 suggests a regulatory role during the maturation process of Zika and other flaviviruses.

### Termini-restraining enables structure determination

The SPCS1 structure was obtained using the [Termini Restraining](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/miscellaneous/termini-restraining/) approach, where the N- and C-termini are fused to the two halves of superfolder GFP. This approach stabilizes the protein and provides a versatile crystallization scaffold. Without the sfGFP coupler, SPCS1 only contains small exposed loops, making crystallization difficult by traditional approaches.
