# Site-2 Protease (mjS2P) from Methanocaldococcus jannaschii

## Overview

The crystal structure of the transmembrane core domain of an S2P family
intramembrane metalloprotease from Methanocaldococcus jannaschii (mjS2P)
reveals the architecture of a site-2 protease. The structure shows six
transmembrane segments with a catalytic zinc atom coordinated by His54,
His58 (TM2) and Asp148 (TM4), located ~14 Å below the lipid membrane
surface. The protease adopts two distinct conformations — open and closed —
revealing a lateral gating mechanism for substrate entry. This is the first
structure of an S2P family intramembrane metalloprotease and provides a
framework for understanding regulated [Intramembrane Proteolysis](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/membrane-mimetics/membrane-mimetics/membrane-mimetics/intramembrane-proteolysis/) (RIP).

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Lateral Gating Mechanism for Substrate Entry

The two molecules in the asymmetric unit reveal open and closed conformations. In the closed state (molecule B), TM1 and TM6 are closely packed, shielding the active site from substrate. Water gains access to zinc through a hydrophilic channel open to the cytosolic side (narrowest point ~2.6 Å). In the open state (molecule A), TM1 pivots outward by ~35° relative to TM2, and TM1 and TM6 separate by 10-12 Å, creating a deep groove that exposes the active site for substrate peptide entry. This lateral gating mechanism is proposed to be the general mechanism for substrate access in S2P family proteases.

### Active Site Architecture

The catalytic zinc is coordinated in a roughly tetrahedral geometry by His54 and His58 from TM2, and Asp148 from TM4. Glu55 is positioned 3.3-3.4 Å from zinc, consistent with its role in activating a zinc-bound water molecule for nucleophilic attack. TM2, TM3, and TM4 form a conserved core domain with nearly identical conformations in both states, while TM1, TM5, and TM6 constitute a flexible gate domain. The AGxxxN/S/G motif in TM3 plays a structural role in stabilizing active site conformation.
