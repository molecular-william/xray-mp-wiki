# Human Microsomal Prostaglandin E2 Synthase 1 (mPGES-1)

## Overview

Human microsomal prostaglandin E2 synthase 1 (mPGES-1) is a 152 amino-acid member of the MAPEG (Membrane-Associated Proteins involved in Eicosanoid and Glutathione metabolism) superfamily. It catalyzes the isomerization of prostaglandin H2 (PGH2) to prostaglandin E2 (PGE2), a key mediator of inflammatory response and pain. mPGES-1 forms an alpha-helical homotrimeric integral membrane protein where each monomer contains a four-helix bundle. Inhibition of mPGES-1 has been proposed as a therapeutic strategy for the treatment of pain, inflammation, and some cancers, potentially avoiding the cardiovascular risks associated with COX-2 inhibitors by targeting the prostaglandin pathway downstream of PGH2 synthesis.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Trimeric structure with central cone-shaped cavity

mPGES-1 forms a homotrimer with each monomer containing a four-helix bundle (TM I-IV). A unique feature is a small cytosolic domain (C-domain) inserted between TM I and TM II. The trimer has a large 16-A-deep cone-shaped cavity extending from the cytosolic side along the threefold symmetry axis.

### Glutathione-binding active site

The active site is a ~15-A-deep cavity at each monomer interface. GSH is coordinated by Arg73, Asn74, Glu77, His113, Tyr117, Arg126, and Ser127. Ser127 activates the GSH thiol for catalysis, distinguishing mPGES-1 from LTC4 synthase.

### Conserved inhibitor binding mode targeting alpha-4 of monomer 1

Four structurally diverse inhibitor scaffolds (biarylimidazole, phenanthrene imidazole, and two biarylindoles) all bind at the same site above GSH, packing against alpha-4 of monomer 1. All inhibitors form critical interactions with residues that are not conserved between human and rat/mouse sequences (H53R, P124R, V128G), explaining species-dependent potency.

### Crystal structures enable SAR understanding

The crystal structures of mPGES-1 bound to four potent inhibitors (compounds 5, 63/MF63, 30, and 3) at 1.41 A resolution provide a framework for understanding structure-activity relationships. Key interactions include hydrogen bonds with H53, S127, and structured waters, salt bridges with R52, and hydrophobic packing against the alpha-4 helix of monomer 1.

### Differential static light scattering as a thermal stability assay for crystallography prioritization

DSLS was used as a biophysical screen to identify mPGES-1/inhibitor complexes most likely to crystallize. Compounds that increased the aggregation temperature by at least 2 degrees Celsius correlated with successful crystallization. This was necessary because significant scaffold-dependent discrepancies were observed between IC50 values measured using membrane extracts versus purified protein.
