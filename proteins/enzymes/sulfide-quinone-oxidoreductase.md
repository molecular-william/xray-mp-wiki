# Sulfide:Quinone Oxidoreductase (SQR) from Aquifex aeolicus

## Overview

Sulfide:quinone oxidoreductase (SQR) from Aquifex aeolicus is a flavoprotein that oxidizes sulfide to zero-valent sulfur, transferring electrons via [FAD](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/flavin-adenine-dinucleotide/) to the quinone pool. It is a homotrimeric, periplasmic integral monotopic membrane protein involved in sulfide detoxification and energy transduction.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Monotopic membrane protein architecture

SQR is a homotrimeric monotopic membrane protein that inserts
~12 Å into the lipid bilayer through an amphipathic helix-turn-helix
tripodal motif. This is distinct from the dimeric organization
of other disulfide oxidoreductase flavoproteins.

### Unusual FAD covalent linkage

[FAD](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/flavin-adenine-dinucleotide/) is linked to the polypeptide via a labile persulfide bridge
between the 8-methyl group of [FAD](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/flavin-adenine-dinucleotide/) and Cys-124, mediated by a
sulfur atom from the substrate, rather than a stable thioether
bond.

### Sulfide oxidation mechanism

Sulfide polymerization occurs on the re side of [FAD](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/flavin-adenine-dinucleotide/), where
invariant Cys-156 and Cys-347 form polysulfur chains. The quinone
binds on the si side of [FAD](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/flavin-adenine-dinucleotide/) in a channel extending to the
membrane. The quinone ring is sandwiched between Phe-385 and
Ile-346 and protonated via Glu-318.

### Substrate and product channeling

A positively charged channel from the periplasm to the active
site guides S2- substrate entry. The product polysulfide chain
(up to 9 S atoms forming an S8 ring) may be released via the
same or an alternative hydrophobic channel.
