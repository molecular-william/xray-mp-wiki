# Diacylglycerol Kinase (DgkA)

## Overview

Diacylglycerol kinase ([Dgka](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/dgka/)) from Escherichia coli is a 121-residue integral membrane enzyme that catalyses the ATP-dependent phosphorylation of diacylglycerol to phosphatidic acid. For half a century, [Dgka](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/dgka/) has served as a model system for investigating membrane protein enzymology, folding, assembly and stability. The enzyme is a homo-trimer with three transmembrane helices and an N-terminal amphiphilic helix per monomer. The active sites are composite, shared between subunits, with lipid substrate binding at the membrane/polar interface and [ATP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/atp/) docking in a cytosolic pocket. The crystal structures rationalize extensive biochemical data and are at variance with a previously published solution NMR model in that domain swapping is not observed.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Trimeric architecture with shared composite active sites

[Dgka](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/dgka/) forms a homo-trimer with three transmembrane helices (H1-H3) and an N-terminal amphiphilic surface helix per monomer. The three active sites are composite, shared between subunits. Each active site is formed by the surface helix of one subunit and the H1-H3 helices of an adjacent subunit. For example, active site AB is formed by surface helix A, H1-B, H2-B and H3-B. This architecture was consistent with previous subunit mixing/complementation experiments but at variance with a published NMR model that showed domain swapping.

### Lipid substrate binding and ATP docking

One putative active site in Delta7 includes density well described by a [MAG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/mag/) molecule, identifying the lipid substrate binding half-site at the membrane polar/apolar interface. [ATP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/atp/) has been docked into the Delta7 model with the adenyl moiety interacting with the cytosolic loop and the triphosphate extending toward the lipid-binding region. The distinct binding sites for [ATP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/atp/) and lipid are consistent with a random-equilibrium phosphoryl transfer mechanism.

### Zinc cofactor coordination

Full activation of [Dgka](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/dgka/) requires free divalent cation. Zinc was found coordinated by the carboxyls of Glu28 and Glu76, both vital to catalytic activity. Superposition onto the docked ATP-Delta7 structure positions zinc 4.9 A from the gamma-phosphate, suggesting a role in substrate placement.

### Disagreement with solution NMR model

The crystal structures differ markedly from a previously published solution NMR model. The NMR model showed domain swapping where H3 from one subunit contacts H1 and H2 from an adjacent subunit, with the active site mapped to H1 and H3 from one subunit and H2 from another. The crystal structure shows no domain swapping, with the active site formed by the surface helix of one subunit and H1-H3 of an adjacent subunit.
