# Human Complement C5a Receptor 1 (C5aR1)

## Overview

The human complement C5a receptor 1 (C5aR1, also known as CD88) is a class A G protein-coupled receptor that mediates the pro-inflammatory effects of the anaphylatoxin C5a. It is expressed on cells of myeloid origin and plays a critical role in the complement cascade, which is essential for host defense against infection and tissue damage. C5aR1 has been implicated in diseases such as sepsis, rheumatoid arthritis, Crohn's disease, ischaemia-reperfusion injuries, and neurodegenerative conditions including Alzheimer's disease. The crystal structure of a thermostabilized C5aR1 construct (StaR) in complex with the small-molecule inverse agonist NDT9513727 revealed an extra-helical binding site between transmembrane helices 3, 4, and 5, providing mechanistic insight into how non-peptide antagonists can negatively modulate GPCR activity from outside the helical bundle.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Extra-helical binding mechanism of NDT9513727

NDT9513727 binds between transmembrane helices 3, 4, and 5, outside the
helical bundle, in an extra-helical pocket within the lipid bilayer. The
extended benzodioxolane packs against TM4, the 2-phenyl group makes packing
interactions between TM3 and TM5 involving Ile124(3.40), Ala128(3.44), and
Pro214(5.50), and the imidazole core hydrogen bonds to Trp213(5.49). This
Trp213 interaction is key for species selectivity, as the corresponding
residue in non-primate species is a leucine. Mutation of Trp213 to leucine
abolishes NDT9513727 binding.

### Inverse agonism through molecular wedge mechanism

NDT9513727 acts as a molecular wedge between TM4, TM3, and TM5, stabilizing
a network of interactions that hold the receptor in the inactive state. It
inhibits the helical movements required to transition to the agonist
conformation. Specifically, it prevents the inward movement of TM5 at the
proline bulge of Pro(5.50) that would disrupt the network between
Pro(5.50), Ile(3.40), Phe(6.44), and Asn(7.45) stabilizing the inactive
state.

### Thermostabilization of C5aR1 via StaR mutagenesis

The C5aR1 StaR construct was generated by 11 thermostabilizing mutations
(S85A, I91A, I142A, N146R, L156A, F172A, R232A, A234E, L311E, S317E,
N321E) in the presence of inverse-agonist radioligand [3H]NDT9513727.
Wild-type C5aR1 has a melting temperature (Tm) of 18 C, while the StaR
construct has a Tm of 44 C. The mutations had no effect on pharmacology
or ligand binding compared to wild-type. The crystallization construct
further removed 29 N-terminal and 17 C-terminal residues.

### Non-crystallographic dimer of C5aR1

The two copies of C5aR1 in the asymmetric unit assemble in parallel
fashion, making a network of interactions between TM4-TM4, TM4-TM5,
ICL2, and the two copies of NDT9513727. This dimer buries a surface
area of 3,565.4 A2 (1,651.5 A2 without contribution from NDT9513727).
The dimer interface most closely resembles that previously postulated
for the Smoothened (SMO) receptor.

### Allosteric site residues and shape complementarity

Mutation of Ala128(3.44) and Thr129(3.45) to phenylalanine severely
affected NDT9513727 binding, demonstrating their role at the hydrophobic
core of the binding pocket. Thr129 to leucine mutation had no effect,
suggesting that bulky phenylalanine substitutions disrupt shape
complementarity while conservative mutations are tolerated. Mutations
of Thr217(5.53) to leucine and Leu125(3.41) to phenylalanine had no
effect on binding.
