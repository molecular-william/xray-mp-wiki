# Escherichia coli Hydrogenase 1 (EcHyd-1)

## Overview

Hydrogenase 1 (EcHyd-1) is a membrane-bound, O2-tolerant [NiFe]-hydrogenase from Escherichia coli that catalyzes the reversible oxidation of molecular hydrogen (H2 ↔ 2H+ + 2e-). Unlike other E. coli hydrogenases (Hyd-2 and Hyd-3), EcHyd-1 is maximally expressed during fermentation under anaerobic conditions. It functions to protect O2-sensitive enzymes during the anaerobic-to-aerobic metabolic transition by reducing intracellular O2 to water. The enzyme forms a complex with its cognate cytochrome b, which serves as a membrane anchor.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### O2 tolerance and physiological role

EcHyd-1 is maximally expressed during fermentation when electron acceptors are
scarce, in the lower part of the host's intestinal tract before excretion and
the anaerobic-to-aerobic metabolic transition. The enzyme functions to keep
intracellular O2 levels low by reducing O2 to water, protecting O2-sensitive
enzymes (such as Hyd-3) during the transition. Under in-vivo fermenting
conditions, O2 entering E. coli is reduced by EcHyd-1 using H2 generated by
EcHyd-3 from formate. Hydrophobic tunnels connect the active site to the
molecular surface, conveying H2 from the cytoplasmic side to the periplasmic
active site.

### Electron transfer pathway to cytochrome b

The crystal structure reveals a viable electron-transfer pathway from the
hydrogenase distal [Fe4S4] cluster to the proximal heme of cytochrome b. The
histidine ligand of the [Fe4S4] cluster and one carboxylate group of the heme
are separated by approximately 5 A. In the [B(SL)2]2 complex, the closest
tunnel entrance virtually touches the membrane surface. The distance between
the two distal [Fe4S4] clusters in the (SL)2 dimers is 12.3 A, well within
the range for rapid electron transfer between H2ase S subunits.

### Structural features of O2 tolerance

Like other O2-tolerant [NiFe]-hydrogenases, EcHyd-1 features a [Fe3S4] cluster
(medial cluster) instead of the [Fe4S4] found in O2-sensitive hydrogenases.
The two redox processes of the proximal cluster have E0 potentials of 30 and
230 mV at pH 6.0, with the E0 of the medial [Fe3S4] cluster being 190 mV.
These positive potentials provide a strong driving force to attract electrons
that can subsequently activate the Ni-Fe site.

### Electronic coupling for fuel cell applications

The EcHyd-1-cytochrome b complex defines a surface on the H2ase with basic
and acidic patches that could be used to design stable and functional
electronic coupling between the enzyme and a fuel-cell anode under air.
The extensive interacting surface between cytochrome b and H2ase (approximately
6,800 A2 buried) explains the stability of the crystallized complex.
