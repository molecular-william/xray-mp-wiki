# Mouse Claudin-3 (mCLDN-3)

## Overview

Mouse claudin-3 (mCLDN-3) is a tetraspanning tight junction membrane protein of the
claudin family. Unlike human CLDN-3, mCLDN-3 is a high-affinity CpE receptor, binding
cCpE with Kd = 9.5 nM. This is due to the NPLVP152 motif containing leucine at position 3,
providing extended hydrophobic surface penetration into the cCpE groove comparable to
hCLDN-4 (NPLVA153, Leu at position 3). mCLDN-3 overexpresses in the mouse small intestine,
making it (along with CLDN-8) a primary CpE receptor in mice. In contrast, human CLDN-3
has valine at position 3 (NPVVP152), resulting in 26-fold lower CpE affinity. CpE is
cytotoxic to mCLDN-3-expressing Sf9 cells, demonstrating functional receptor activity.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### mCLDN-3 as primary mouse CpE receptor

mCLDN-3 binds cCpE with Kd = 9.5 nM (26-fold higher affinity than hCLDN-3 at
252 nM). The leucine at position 3 of the NPLVP152 motif provides extended
hydrophobic side-chain length that deeply penetrates the cCpE binding groove,
similar to hCLDN-4. mCLDN-3 overexpresses in the mouse small intestine,
establishing it (along with CLDN-8) as a primary CpE receptor in mice.
CpE treatment of mCLDN-3-expressing Sf9 cells causes morphological damage
and cell death comparable to hCLDN-4-expressing cells.

### Species-specific claudin expression in gut

In mice, CLDN-3 and CLDN-4 both overexpress in the small intestine, making
both candidate CpE receptors. However, only mCLDN-3 (with Leu at position 3)
binds CpE with high affinity. In humans, CLDN-3 and CLDN-4 expression diverges:
CLDN-3 and -4 overexpress in the large intestine, but only hCLDN-4 (with Leu at
position 3) functions as a high-affinity receptor. This species-specific divergence
in both sequence and expression explains the differing primary CpE receptors
between mice (CLDN-3) and humans (CLDN-4).
