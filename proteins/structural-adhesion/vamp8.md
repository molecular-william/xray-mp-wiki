# VAMP8 (Vesicle-Associated Membrane Protein 8)

## Overview

VAMP8 (also known as endobrevin) is an R-SNARE protein that, together with the Qa-SNARE [STX17](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/syntaxin17/)
and the Qbc-SNARE [SNAP29 (Synaptosomal-Associated Protein 29)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/snap29/), forms the autophagic SNARE core complex required for
autophagosome-lysosome fusion. VAMP8 localizes to lysosomes and provides the R-SNARE motif
(arginine-containing layer 0 residue R37) in the four-helix bundle. The crystal structure of the
STX17-SNAP29-VAMP8 SNARE core complex was determined, revealing a canonical four-helix bundle.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### VAMP8 provides the R-SNARE motif for autophagic SNARE complex

VAMP8's R-SNARE motif assembles with [STX17](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/syntaxin17/) (Qa) and [SNAP29 (Synaptosomal-Associated Protein 29)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/snap29/) (Qb+Qc) to form the
autophagic four-helix bundle. The conserved arginine at position 37 (R37) forms the
hydrophilic 0-layer together with Q196 ([STX17](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/syntaxin17/)), Q84 ([SNAP29 (Synaptosomal-Associated Protein 29)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/snap29/) Qb), and Q230 ([SNAP29 (Synaptosomal-Associated Protein 29)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/snap29/) Qc).
