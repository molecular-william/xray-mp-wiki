# Mouse Claudin-4 (mCLDN-4)

## Overview

Mouse claudin-4 (mCLDN-4) is a tetraspanning tight junction membrane protein whose
cCpE-binding motif contains a methionine at position 3 (unlike human CLDN-4 which
has leucine). BLI measurements show mCLDN-4 binds cCpE with an affinity intermediate
between high-affinity (hCLDN-4, mCLDN-3) and low-affinity (hCLDN-3) receptors.
mCLDN-4 maintains a complex with cCpE for periods approaching hCLDN-4, and CpE is
cytotoxic to mCLDN-4-expressing Sf9 cells. This positions mCLDN-4 as a potential
secondary CpE receptor in mice, alongside the primary receptors CLDN-3 and CLDN-8.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Methionine at position 3 in mCLDN-4

Unlike hCLDN-4 (leucine at position 3 of NPLVA153 motif), mCLDN-4 has a
methionine at this position. This intermediate side-chain length and
hydrophobicity results in CpE binding affinity between high-affinity
(leucine-containing) and low-affinity (valine-containing) claudins.
mCLDN-4 maintains a complex with CpE for periods approaching hCLDN-4,
and CpE induces cytotoxicity in mCLDN-4-expressing cells, supporting
its role as a secondary CpE receptor in mice.
