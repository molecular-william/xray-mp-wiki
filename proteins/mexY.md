# MexY (Pseudomonas aeruginosa multidrug exporter)

## Overview

MexY is the inner-membrane component of the MexXY-OprM tripartite efflux pump in Pseudomonas aeruginosa, responsible for intrinsic resistance to aminoglycosides and other antibiotics. MexY belongs to the resistance-nodulation-cell division (RND) superfamily of secondary active transporters and is the principal multidrug exporter in P. aeruginosa alongside MexB. Unlike its close homologue MexB, MexY is not inhibited by pyridopyrimidine derivatives (ABI-PP) due to a voluminous tryptophan residue (W177) at the edge of the hydrophobic trap in the distal drug-binding pocket, which sterically hinders inhibitor binding. No crystal structure of MexY has been solved; a homology model was constructed using the ABI-PP-bound AcrB/MexB structures as templates.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Homology model of MexY reveals structural basis for inhibitor specificity

A homology model of MexY was constructed using SWISS-MODEL based on the ABI-PP-bound AcrB/MexB structures. The model reveals that the position of F178 in AcrB and MexB (at the edge of the hydrophobic trap) is occupied by W177 in MexY. The voluminous indolyl side chain of W177 protrudes into the narrow hydrophobic trap, sterically hindering ABI-PP binding. This explains why pyridopyrimidine derivatives inhibit MexB but not MexY, despite their high sequence similarity.

### Site-directed mutagenesis validates steric hindrance mechanism

The MexY(W177F) mutant, in which the bulky tryptophan was replaced with phenylalanine, was constructed by site-directed mutagenesis. Fluorescence quenching assays showed that MexY(W177F) became inhibited by ABI-PP, with increased doxorubicin fluorescence quenching in the presence of the inhibitor. This confirms that the bulky side chain at position 177 is the primary reason for ABI-PP insensitivity in wild-type MexY. Mutagenesis did not affect the expression levels of the protein.

### Differential response of AcrB(F178W) and MexB(F178W) mutants to ABI-PP

Despite introducing the same F178W mutation in both AcrB and MexB, the two mutants showed different responses to ABI-PP. AcrB(F178W) lost sensitivity to ABI-PP, while MexB(F178W) remained fully inhibitable. Crystal structures of both mutants revealed that the indolyl side chain of W178 in AcrB protrudes into the narrow hydrophobic trap, whereas in MexB the indolyl side chain does not protrude because the space is partially occupied by a bound DDM molecule. In MexB(F178W), ABI-PP was actually detected in the hydrophobic trap, with the indolyl ring of W178 fitting into a position capable of forming pi-pi interactions with the pyridopyrimidine ring.

### Hydrophobic trap architecture determines inhibitor susceptibility

The hydrophobic trap branches off from the substrate-translocation channel in the distal binding pocket of RND transporters. It is composed of phenylalanine residues (F136, F178, F573, F610, F615, F628 in MexB) that do not directly interact with exported drugs but indirectly affect drug binding. The trap forms a deep and narrow fissure. ABI-PP binds tightly to this trap and sterically hinders the functional rotation mechanism of AcrB/MexB monomers. The inhibitor specificity between MexB and MexY is determined by subtle differences in the amount of space available in the hydrophobic trap, governed by the identity of the residue at position 177/178.
