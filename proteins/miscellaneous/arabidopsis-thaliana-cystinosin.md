# Arabidopsis thaliana cystinosin (AtCystinosin)

## Overview

Arabidopsis thaliana cystinosin is a proton-coupled lysosomal cystine transporter from the PQ-loop family of solute carrier (SLC) transporters. It functions as the primary route for export of cystine from the lysosome/vacuole using the outwardly directed proton electrochemical gradient in a 1:1 stoichiometry. The protein comprises seven transmembrane helices (TMs) with two conserved PQ-motifs located on TM1 and TM5. The plant homologue from A. thaliana was used for structural and biochemical studies, providing mechanistic insights into cystine recognition and proton-coupled transport relevant to human cystinosin (CTNS gene product), mutations in which cause the lysosomal storage disorder cystinosis.
