# Wza (E. coli Capsular Polysaccharide Export Outer Membrane Lipoprotein)

## Overview

Wza is an outer membrane lipoprotein from Escherichia coli that forms an octameric channel essential for the translocation of group 1 capsular polysaccharides (K30 antigen) across the outer membrane. The Wza octamer is 379 residues per monomer and adopts a novel alpha-helical barrel fold comprising four domains: domain 1 (residues 89-169, containing the PES motif), domain 2 (residues 68-84 and 172-252), domain 3 (residues 46-64 and 255-344), and domain 4 (residues 345-379). The C-terminal domain 4 forms an alpha-helical barrel that spans the outer membrane, lined by tryptophan residues (Trp 350) that act as a hydrophobic 'lock' anchoring the protein in the membrane. The octamer encloses a large central cavity that is sealed on the periplasmic side by a ring of tyrosine residues (Tyr 110) that adopt dual conformations — either completely sealing the cavity or creating an 8 A pore. Wza is a member of the outer membrane auxiliary (OMA) protein family and interacts with the inner membrane complex Wzc (a tyrosine autokinase) to form a trans-envelope polysaccharide secretion system.
