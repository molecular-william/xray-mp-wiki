# LbSemiSWEET (Leptospira biflexa SemiSWEET)

## Overview

[LbSemiSWEET from Leptospira biflexa](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/miscellaneous/lbsemisweet/) is a SWEET-family sugar transporter from Leptospira biflexa that functions
as a minimal alternating-access transporter. It is among the smallest characterized
transporters (< 20 kDa), with a simple symmetric dimer of three-helix bundles. [SemiSWEET Transporter Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/semisweet/)
mimics the core of eukaryotic SWEET transporters. Members of the SWEET family play critical
roles in phloem loading, nectar secretion, pollen development, seed filling, and
host-pathogen interactions in plants, and are important regulators of metabolic
homeostasis in animals. Crystal structures of both outward-open (glucose-bound D57A mutant)
and inward-open (Q20A mutant) conformations were determined, providing a complete set
of conformations for a single [SemiSWEET Transporter Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/semisweet/) ortholog. Unguided molecular dynamics simulations
initiated from the outward-open structure spontaneously adopted occluded and inward-open
conformations matching the crystal structures, revealing the atomic-level mechanism of
alternating-access transport.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Complete alternating-access cycle captured by crystallography and MD simulations

Crystal structures of [LbSemiSWEET from Leptospira biflexa](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/miscellaneous/lbsemisweet/) in all three major conformational states
(outward-open, occluded, and inward-open) were obtained for a single ortholog,
providing the most complete structural picture of alternating-access transport
in a uniporter. The outward-open (D57A, PDB 5UHS) and inward-open (Q20A, PDB 5UHQ)
structures were determined in this study, complementing the previously reported occluded
structure (PDB 4QNC). Unguided MD simulations initiated from the outward-open structure
spontaneously reached both the occluded and inward-open conformations, validating
the predictive power of the simulations and confirming the occluded state as an
on-pathway intermediate.

### Extracellular and intracellular gate coordination

The mechanism involves tight allosteric coupling between extracellular and
intracellular gates. The extracellular gate (Tyr51, Ile60, Arg55, Asp57) closes
first, driven by favorable hydrophobic and electrostatic interactions. This closure
destabilizes TM1-TM3 packing, freeing TM1 to kink at Pro19, which opens the
intracellular gate (Phe17, Met37, Tyr38). The occluded state acts as an "air lock"
where both gates are closed, preventing simultaneous opening that would create
a continuous conducting pore. Simulations showed the transporter persisted in the
occluded state for up to several hundred nanoseconds.

### Substrate takes a "free ride" during transport

The substrate [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) appears to take a "free ride" across the membrane, with
the transporter adopting the same conformational states and undergoing the same
transitions regardless of whether substrate is bound. [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) is coordinated by
Trp48, Asn64, and Thr13 in the binding pocket. The [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) ring lies in a vertical
plane off-center from the two-fold axis, partially sandwiched between Trp48
residues from each protomer. [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) becomes less mobile in the occluded and
inward-open states as the binding pocket narrows, allowing it to form hydrogen bonds
with both Asn64 residues simultaneously.

### Mutagenesis validates gate function

Alanine substitution of gate residues Ile60 and Tyr51 (extracellular gate) and
Phe17 (intracellular gate) ablated [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) uptake. The D57A mutation more than
doubled [Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/) affinity (apparent Km ~70 uM vs ~160 uM for WT), and this mutant
crystallized in the outward-open conformation. The D57A mutation disrupts
extracellular interactions to favor the outward-open state. MD simulations of
D57A and I60A mutants confirmed that a bulky hydrophobic residue at position 60
is essential for extracellular gate closure.
