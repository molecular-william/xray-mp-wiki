# VrPPase (Vibrio rotiferans H+-Pumping Pyrophosphatase)

## Overview

VrPPase (H+-pumping inorganic pyrophosphatase from Vibrio rotiferans) is a membrane-bound enzyme that catalyzes the hydrolysis of inorganic pyrophosphate (PPi) and couples the released energy to proton translocation across the membrane. This bacterial M-PPase forms a dimer with 16 transmembrane helices per monomer. The structure reveals distinct inner-ring (gold) and outer-ring (red) helical sub-structures in the dimer. Two conformational states have been captured — substrate-analogue-bound (VrPPase:IDP) and product-bound (VrPPase:P_i) — providing structural insights into the conformational cycle of M-PPases.

## Biological / Functional Insights

### Dimer Architecture with Inner and Outer Ring Helices

The VrPPase dimer features distinct inner-ring (gold) and outer-ring
(red) helical sub-structures in protomers A and B, respectively. The
dimer is viewed from the cytoplasmic side above the hydrolytic center,
with each protomer contributing 16 transmembrane helices arranged in
two concentric rings. This arrangement is conserved across M-PPases
and is essential for the coupled proton translocation mechanism.

### Conformational Changes in TMHs 5, 13, and 14

Superimposition of VrPPase:IDP and VrPPase:P_i structures reveals
significant conformational changes in TMHs 5, 13, and 14. Key
interactions include the E5.71-R13.62-R10.33 and E13.59-R10.33
interaction networks that change between IDP-bound and Pi-bound
states. These helix movements are central to the alternating-access
mechanism of proton translocation.

### Helix 6 and Helix 16 Coordination in Product Release

In the product-bound state (VrPPase:P_i), the D6.43 and D16.39
residues at helices 6 and 16 participate in a bonding network that
differs from the IDP-bound state. The nucleophilic water position
shifts between states, with distances of 2.4 A, 2.5 A, 3.3 A, and
3.4 A observed between key residues and the nucleophilic water in
the two states.

### Substrate Analog Binding and Active Site Coordination

The Mg:IDP complex in VrPPase binds to the hydrolytic center in a
manner similar to TmPPase. The active site shows conserved coordination
geometry with Mg2+ (salmon-colored) and K+ (purple) ions. The IDP
ligand (yellow) coordinates with Mg2+ through water-mediated
interactions, analogous to the PPi binding mode.

### Etidronate Docking and Inhibition

Docking studies of etidronate into the VrPPase active site reveal
steric clashes in both possible orientations. The extra OH group
clashes with D691 (VrPPase D15.61) at 2 A and with the fifth
magnesium ion (Mg5) at 2 A, while the methyl group clashes with
Mg. These clashes would prevent or greatly interfere with closing
of the active-site loops, explaining the inhibitory mechanism of
etidronate against H+-PPases.
