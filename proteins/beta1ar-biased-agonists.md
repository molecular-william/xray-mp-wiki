# β1-Adrenoceptor (β1AR-m23) — Structures with Biased Agonists Bucindolol and Carvedilol

## Overview

The β1-adrenoceptor (β1AR) is a G protein-coupled receptor (GPCR) that is the target of beta blockers used in treating cardiac-related illnesses. This paper reports crystal structures of a thermostabilized avian β1AR mutant (β1AR-m23) bound to two biased agonists — bucindolol (3.2 Å) and carvedilol (2.3 Å) — revealing unique ligand-receptor interactions that promote G protein-independent signaling.

## Structure

- **Protein**: Thermostabilized turkey β1AR (β1AR-m23)
- **Ligands**: Bucindolol (3.2 Å) and Carvedilol (2.3 Å)
- **PDB IDs**: 4AMI (bucindolol), 4AMJ (carvedilol)
- **Space group**: P2₁
- **Cell dimensions**:
  - Bucindolol: a=89.8, b=60.7, c=107.8, β=110.8°
  - Carvedilol: a=90.1, b=62.2, c=100.9, β=109.2°
- **Resolution**: 3.2 Å (bucindolol), 2.3 Å (carvedilol)
- **Detergent molecules**: 5 (bucindolol), 12 (carvedilol) observed in structure

### Biased Agonism

Both bucindolol and carvedilol are classified as **biased agonists**:
- Stimulate G protein-independent signaling (e.g., MAPK pathway, arrestin-mediated signaling)
- Bucindolol: partial agonist of G protein pathway + agonist of MAPK pathway
- Carvedilol: inverse agonist of G protein pathway + agonist of arrestin-mediated signaling
- Interact with additional residues in EL2 and TM7 compared to conventional beta blockers

### Key Structural Features

- **Ser215⁵,⁴⁶**: Forms interhelical H-bond with H3 (partial agonist-like conformation)
- **Ser212⁵,⁴³**: Alternative rotamer conformation (similar to antagonist-bound structures)
- **No binding pocket contraction**: Unlike full/partial agonists
- **H8-H1 interface**: Arg355⁸,⁵⁶ forms H-bonds with Ser68¹,⁵⁹ and Thr69¹,⁶⁰ — stabilizes receptor
- **EL2 interactions**: Bucindolol forms H-bond with Cys199; carvedilol interacts with Phe201 via water bridge

### Arg/Gly389 Polymorphism

- Human β1AR has Arg/Gly389 polymorphism affecting H1-H8 packing
- Arg389 variant: lower barrier to R* state formation
- Structural explanation for pharmacological differences between variants

## Pharmacological Significance

- **Beta blockers**: Bucindolol and carvedilol used in heart failure treatment
- **Biased agonism**: Potential for new generation therapeutics with cardioprotective effects
- **Carvedilol**: Prototype for agents stimulating arrestin-dependent signaling
- **Thermostability**: H1-H8 interface mutations reduce global receptor flexibility
