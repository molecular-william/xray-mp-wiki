# Bis-Tris Propane Chloride

## Overview

Bis-Tris propane chloride (also known as BTP or Tromethamine derivative) is a Good's buffer commonly used in protein crystallization. It has a pKa of ~7.5 at 25°C, making it suitable for near-neutral pH ranges.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 3-[Bis(hydroxymethyl)amino]propane-2,2-diol hydrochloride
- **pKa**: ~7.5 at 25°C
- **pH range**: ~6.5–8.5 (acid form)
- **Class**: Buffer amino acid derivative
- **Use**: Protein crystallization, biochemical assays

## Use in DAP12-TM Crystallization

- **Concentration**: 0.1 M
- **pH**: 6.07
- **System**: 0.1 M BTP pH 6.07, 19.7% PEG 3350, 0.269 M calcium chloride
- **Result**: DAP12-TM tetramer crystallized (2.14 Å, PDB: 4WO1)
- **Cation**: Calcium chloride (0.269 M) provided Ca2+ ions coordinated in the tetramer polar core
