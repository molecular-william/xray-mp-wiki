# Citrate Buffer (Sodium Citrate)

## Overview

Citrate buffer, prepared from citric acid and sodium citrate, is a buffer system effective in the acidic pH range. It has a pKa of 3.13 (pKa1) and is effective in the pH range 3.0-5.6. Citrate buffer is widely used in membrane protein crystallization, particularly for proteins requiring acidic conditions. It is also used as a precipitant in some crystallization conditions and is compatible with many detergent systems.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: citric acid / sodium citrate
- **Class**: organic buffer

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Acidic pH crystallization

Used for crystallizing membrane proteins at acidic pH. Citrate serves both as a buffer and occasionally as a precipitant in crystallization conditions.


### Crystal harvesting

Citrate buffer is sometimes used in cryoprotection and crystal harvesting protocols.


### LCP crystallization buffer for NupG

Sodium citrate at 0.1 M pH 5.0 used as crystallization buffer for NupG (nucleoside proton symporter) in LCP crystallization, yielding P21 crystal form (3.8 A resolution).


### LCP crystallization buffer for Bpe fluoride channel

Sodium citrate at 0.1 M pH 5.0 used as crystallization buffer for Bpe (Bordetella pertussis fluoride channel) in complex with monobody S8, yielding 2.8 A resolution structure (PDB 6BQO) in C 1 2 1 space group.


### Crystallization buffer for glycine receptor α3

Sodium citrate at 25 mM pH 4.0 was used as the buffering agent in the crystallization reservoir for the GlyRα3-strychnine complex. The acidic pH (4.0) was a critical component of the crystallization condition that yielded well-ordered crystals suitable for X-ray diffraction analysis.
