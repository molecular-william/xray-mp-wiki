# Bicine Buffer

## Overview

Bicine (N,N-bis(2-hydroxyethyl)glycine) is a zwitterionic Good's buffer commonly used in membrane protein biochemistry. It has a pKa of 8.35 at 25 deg.C and maintains buffering capacity in the pH range 7.6-9.2. Bicine is particularly useful for crystallizing proteins that require alkaline pH conditions. It is compatible with most detergents and is commonly used in buffers for membrane protein purification and crystallization.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: N,N-bis(2-hydroxyethyl)glycine
- **Class**: zwitterionic Good's buffer

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Alkaline pH crystallization

Used as a buffer component for crystallizing membrane proteins at pH 8.0-9.0. Particularly useful for proteins requiring alkaline conditions.


### Enzyme assays

Used in enzyme activity assays for membrane proteins that function optimally at alkaline pH.


### Beta1AR-M23 crystallization buffer

Bicine at pH 9.0 (100 mM) was used as the crystallization buffer for turkey beta1-adrenergic receptor beta1AR-M23 in complex with salbutamol (sal109) and dobutamine (dob92). Crystallization was performed with 28% PEG 400 or 25% PEG 600 precipitant and 0.5% HEGA-10 detergent. CHS at 1.0 mg/ml was added as a lipid additive for receptor stabilization.
