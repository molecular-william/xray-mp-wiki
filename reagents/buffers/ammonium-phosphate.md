# Ammonium Phosphate (Monobasic)

## Overview

Ammonium phosphate (monobasic) is a commonly used buffer and crystallization precipitant
in protein X-ray crystallography. It provides phosphate ions at a specific pH range and
can serve as both a buffering agent and a precipitating agent for protein crystallization.
In the hSTS structure determination, ammonium phosphate at 0.10-0.20 M concentration
was essential for crystallization and also provided a phosphate ion that binds at
H142 on the enzyme surface.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: ammonium dihydrogen phosphate
- **Chemical formula**: NH4H2PO4
- **Molecular weight**: 115.03 g/mol
- **Class**: phosphate buffer

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### hSTS crystallization precipitant

Ammonium phosphate (monobasic) at 0.10-0.20 M concentration was essential for crystallization of human placental [Steroid Sulfatase (hSTS)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/steroid-sulfatase/). The crystallization reservoir contained 100 mM [Tris-HCl Buffer](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/tris/) (pH 8.5), 0.10-0.20 M ammonium phosphate (monobasic), and 0.3% n-octyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside (BOG). The phosphate ion also binds at H142 on the hSTS surface, forming hydrogen bonds with side chains from H142, W558, K556, S108, and N551, and two water molecules from each monomer.
