# Cacodylate (Sodium Dimethylarsenate)

## Overview

Cacodylate (sodium dimethylarsenate) is a buffering agent used in protein crystallography
and structural biology. In the Porin B (PorB) structure determination from Corynebacterium
glutamicum, cacodylate was observed bound in crystal packing contacts, specifically in
contact type C dimers alongside multiple Zn2+ ion sites. The cacodylate molecule was found
coordinated in the polar interface of crystal form I, where six Zn2+ ion sites and one
cacodylate molecule were present. Cacodylate buffers are commonly used for proteins that
are sensitive to phosphate-based buffers.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: sodium dimethylarsenate
- **Class**: arsenic-containing buffer

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallographic buffer for membrane proteins

Used as a buffer component in crystallization conditions for membrane proteins.
In the PorB structure determination, cacodylate was observed as a bound ligand in
crystal contacts, indicating its role in mediating crystal packing interactions.
