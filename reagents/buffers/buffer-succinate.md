# Succinate Buffer

## Overview

Succinate buffer is a dicarboxylic acid buffering agent with pKa values of 4.2 and 5.6. It is commonly used in electrophysiology experiments requiring low pH, particularly for activating proton-gated channels or studying pH-dependent gating mechanisms.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Butanedioic acid
- **Chemical formula**: C4H6O4
- **Molecular weight**: 118.09 g/mol
- **Class**: buffer

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Low-pH activation buffer for KcsA

Used at 10 mM concentration (pH 3.0) to activate the pH-gated KcsA
potassium channel for electrophysiology recordings. KcsA is activated
by a rapid pH jump from 7.5 to 3.0 using succinate buffer.
