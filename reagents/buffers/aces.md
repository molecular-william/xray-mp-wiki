# ACES Buffer (N-(2-Acetamido)iminodiacetic Acid)

## Overview

ACES (2-[N-(2-Acetamido)iminodiacetic acid]) is a zwitterionic buffering agent commonly used in biochemical and crystallographic applications. It belongs to the ACE buffers family (ACES, ACEP, ACEM, ACBC) and provides buffering capacity in the pH range of 6.2-7.2, making it suitable for crystallization of proteins at near-neutral pH. ACES was used at 100 mM pH 6.5 as the buffer component in the crystallization of the human GABA_A receptor beta-3 subunit.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: N-(2-Acetamido)iminodiacetic acid
- **Chemical formula**: C6H10N2O5
- **Molecular weight**: 220.21 g/mol
- **Class**: zwitterionic acetamide buffer

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization buffer for GABA_A R

ACES buffer at 100 mM pH 6.5 was used as the buffering agent in the crystallization of the human GABA_A receptor beta-3 subunit. The crystallization reservoir contained 11.5% PEG 4000, 100 mM NaCl, 100 mM Li2SO4, 100 mM ACES buffer pH 6.5, and 2% benzamidine. Crystallization was performed by sitting-drop vapor diffusion at 4 C.
