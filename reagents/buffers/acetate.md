# Acetate Buffer (Sodium Acetate)

## Overview

Acetate buffer, typically prepared from [Sodium Acetate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/sodium-acetate/) and acetic acid, is a commonly used buffer system in the acidic pH range. It has a pKa of 4.76 at 25 deg.C and is effective in the pH range 3.6-5.6. Acetate buffer is used in membrane protein crystallization, particularly for proteins that crystallize at lower pH values. It is compatible with many precipitants and is frequently used in crystallization screens targeting acidic pH conditions.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: sodium acetate (CH3COONa) / acetic acid (CH3COOH)
- **Class**: organic buffer

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Acidic pH crystallization

Used for crystallizing membrane proteins that require acidic pH conditions. Commonly used at 50-100 mM concentration for screening at pH 4.0-5.0.


### Protein precipitation

Used in protein precipitation protocols and for adjusting pH in crystallization reservoirs.


### Specific binding sites in GLIC extracellular domain

Acetate serves as a buffer in the crystallization solution of the [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) pentameric ligand-gated ion channel at a concentration of 100 mM in the mother liquor. Ten acetate molecules (two per monomer) occupy two distinct binding sites in the [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) extracellular domain. Acetate 1 binds at the vestibule next to ion binding sites through three hydrogen bonds with the NH of the mainchain of Ile76 (loop A), the guanidinium NH2 atom of Arg85, and the hydroxyl group of Tyr102 (beta6). Acetate 2 binds at the bottom of a pocket below loop C, next to the conserved agonist-binding site of pLGICs, through side chain atoms of Arg77 (loop A), Glu181 (loop C) from one subunit, and Arg105 from the adjacent subunit. In the [GLIC ECD (Extracellular Domain of Gloeobacter violaceus Ion Channel)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic-ecd/) hexameric structure solved at neutral pH, acetate 2 binding pocket is only locally rearranged.


### Acidic pH crystallization buffer

[Sodium Acetate Buffer](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/acetate/) (0.1 M, pH 4.3) was used as the crystallization buffer for the Spirulina platensis c15 ring from proton-dependent ATP synthase. The low pH was necessary to protonate the conserved glutamate (Glu62) at the proton-binding site, enabling the structure to be captured in the proton-locked conformation.
