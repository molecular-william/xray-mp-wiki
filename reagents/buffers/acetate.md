# Acetate Buffer (Sodium Acetate)

## Overview

Acetate buffer, typically prepared from sodium acetate and acetic acid, is a commonly used buffer system in the acidic pH range. It has a pKa of 4.76 at 25 deg.C and is effective in the pH range 3.6-5.6. Acetate buffer is used in membrane protein crystallization, particularly for proteins that crystallize at lower pH values. It is compatible with many precipitants and is frequently used in crystallization screens targeting acidic pH conditions.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: sodium acetate (CH3COONa) / acetic acid (CH3COOH)
- **Class**: organic buffer

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Acidic pH crystallization

Used for crystallizing membrane proteins that require acidic pH conditions. Commonly used at 50-100 mM concentration for screening at pH 4.0-5.0.


### Protein precipitation

Used in protein precipitation protocols and for adjusting pH in crystallization reservoirs.
