# Tris Buffer (Tris-HCl)

## Overview

Tris (tris(hydroxymethyl)aminomethane) is a widely used buffering agent in membrane protein biochemistry. Tris-HCl buffers maintain pH in the range of 7.0-9.0, making it suitable for most purification and crystallization workflows.

## Properties

- **pH range**: 7.0-9.0 (pKa ~8.1 at 25°C)
- **Common concentrations**: 10-100 mM
- **Temperature sensitivity**: pKa decreases by ~0.03 per °C increase

## Proteins Using Tris (from this wiki)

- [nTMATE2-transporter](/proteins/nTMATE2-transporter/) — 20 mM Tris-HCl pH 8.0 in expression buffer; used during cell lysis and initial membrane fraction preparation
- [psi-lhci-supercomplex](/proteins/psi-lhci-supercomplex/) — 50 mM Tris-HCl pH 6.5 during complex isolation (slightly acidic conditions to capture resting state); 50 mM Tris-HCl pH 7.0 in crystallization buffer and cryoprotectant
- [acrB](/proteins/acrB/) — 50 mM Tris-HCl pH 7.0 for membrane fraction preparation; 20 mM Tris-HCl pH 7.5 during [affinity-chromatography](/methods/purification/affinity-chromatography/) purification
- [etb-receptor](/proteins/etb-receptor/) — 20 mM Tris-HCl pH 7.5 throughout workflow: cell lysis buffer, TALON wash/elution, [dialysis](/methods/purification/dialysis/), SEC
