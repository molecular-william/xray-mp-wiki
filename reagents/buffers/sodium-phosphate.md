# Sodium Phosphate Buffer

## Overview

Sodium phosphate buffers are widely used in protein biochemistry and crystallization. They provide stable buffering in the pH range of approximately 5.8–8.0, depending on the ratio of monobasic to dibasic phosphate.

## Properties

- **pH range**: ~5.8–8.0 (pKa2 = 7.2 at 25°C)
- **Common concentrations**: 50–200 mM
- **Temperature sensitivity**: Moderate; pKa decreases with increasing temperature
- **Advantages**: Good buffering capacity near physiological pH, low toxicity, compatible with most proteins
- **Disadvantages**: Can precipitate with divalent cations (Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺, Fe²⁺/³⁺)

## Example: AcrB Crystallization

- **Concentration**: 0.1 M Na-phosphate pH 6.2
- **Role**: Crystallization reservoir buffer for AcrB-Linezolid complex
- **Combined with**: 0.1 M NaCl, 8% PEG 4000
- **Method**: Sitting-drop vapor diffusion
