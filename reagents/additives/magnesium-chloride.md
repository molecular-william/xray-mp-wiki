# Magnesium Chloride

## Overview

Magnesium chloride (MgCl2) is a metal salt commonly used in protein crystallization as an additive to modulate protein solubility and promote crystal nucleation. The magnesium ion can coordinate with phosphate groups and carboxylate side chains, stabilizing specific protein conformations or mediating protein-protein interactions in the crystal lattice.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: magnesium chloride
- **Chemical formula**: MgCl2
- **Molecular weight**: 95.21 g/mol
- **Class**: metal salt

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization additive for VKOR from Synechococcus sp.

Magnesium chloride at 0.1 M was used as a crystallization additive for the Cys212Ala mutant of [VKOR from Synechococcus sp.](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/synechococcus-vkor/) in [HiLiDe Crystallization](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/hilide-crystallization/) conditions. The reservoir contained 0.1 M MgCl2 along with 11% PEG1500, 8% [Glycerol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glycerol/), 5% ethanol, 0.1 M NaCl, and 0.1 M [Sodium Cacodylate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/sodium-cacodylate/) pH 5.5. The Mg2+ ion likely stabilizes the protein conformation during crystallization.
