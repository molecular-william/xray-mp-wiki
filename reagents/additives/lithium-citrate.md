# Lithium Citrate

## Overview

Lithium [Citrate Buffer (Sodium Citrate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/citrate/) is a salt formed from citric acid and lithium, used as a crystallization additive in membrane protein crystallography. In M3 muscarinic receptor crystallization, 113.5 mM lithium [Citrate Buffer (Sodium Citrate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/citrate/) was used in combination with 100 mM Tris (pH 8.1), 110 mM [Ammonium Sulfate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/ammonium-sulfate/), and 45% [PEG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg/) 300 as the crystallization reservoir for the M3-dsT4L fusion construct. This condition produced untwinned crystals in the higher symmetry space group P41212 at 3.6 A resolution.
