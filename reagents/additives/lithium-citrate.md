# Lithium Citrate

## Overview

Lithium citrate is a salt formed from citric acid and lithium, used as a crystallization additive in membrane protein crystallography. In M3 muscarinic receptor crystallization, 113.5 mM lithium citrate was used in combination with 100 mM Tris (pH 8.1), 110 mM ammonium sulfate, and 45% PEG 300 as the crystallization reservoir for the M3-dsT4L fusion construct. This condition produced untwinned crystals in the higher symmetry space group P41212 at 3.6 A resolution.
