# Lithium Sulfate

## Overview

Lithium sulfate (Li2SO4) is an inorganic salt used as a crystallization precipitant and
cryoprotectant in membrane protein crystallography. It is employed in the hanging drop
vapor diffusion crystallization of photosystem I-LHCI supercomplexes, where it promotes
crystal growth alongside PEG 6000 as the precipitating agent. Li2SO4 is also used at
higher concentrations (100 mM) in cryoprotection solutions.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: lithium sulfate
- **Chemical formula**: Li2SO4
- **Molecular weight**: 109.94 g/mol
- **Class**: inorganic salt

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### PSI-LHCI crystallization

50 mM Li2SO4 used in combination with 50 mM Tris-HCl (pH 7.0) and 4.5-7.0% PEG 6000 as the crystallization reservoir for PSI-LHCI Form A. The salt promotes protein precipitation and crystal nucleation in the hanging drop setup.


### Cryoprotection

100 mM Li2SO4 used in the cryoprotection solution for PSI-LHCI crystals. Combined with PEG 400, triethylene glycol, and PEG 6000, it helps stabilize crystals during the freezing process.
