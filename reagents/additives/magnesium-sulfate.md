# Magnesium Sulfate

## Overview

Magnesium sulfate (MgSO4) is a divalent cation salt used in protein crystallization screens. It provides magnesium ions that can coordinate with protein side chains, nucleotides, and phosphate groups.

## Properties

- **Chemical formula**: MgSO4
- **Class**: Inorganic salt, divalent cation source
- **Use**: Protein crystallization, ion coordination

## Use in SecYEG Crystallization

- **Concentration**: 100 mM
- **System**: 30–32% PEG 500 MME, 100 mM MgSO4, 100 mM Na2SO4, 50 mM MOPS
- **Result**: SecYEG resting state crystallized (2.7 Å, PDB: 5AWW)
- **Role**: Likely stabilizes phosphate interactions or protein-lipid interfaces
