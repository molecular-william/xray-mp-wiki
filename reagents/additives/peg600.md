# PEG 600

## Overview

PEG 600 (polyethylene glycol with average molecular weight of approximately 600 Da) is a low-molecular-weight polyethylene glycol used as a crystallization precipitant in sitting drop vapor diffusion crystallization of membrane proteins. PEG 600 promotes protein crystallization by excluded volume effects, concentrating the protein in the crystallization drop and promoting crystal nucleation and growth. In the XylE inward-open conformation study, 20% PEG 600 was used as the primary precipitant with calcium acetate at pH 4.2.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: polyethylene glycol 600
- **Class**: polyethylene glycol crystallization precipitant

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization precipitant for SMO receptor

PEG 600 at 8-12% (w/v) was used as the primary precipitant in sitting drop vapor diffusion crystallization of human SMO receptor complexes (SMO-SANT1, SMO-Anta XV, SMO-SAG1.5). The crystallization buffer contained 8-12% PEG 600, 0.1 M HEPES pH 5.5-5.8, at 20 degrees Celsius. Crystals grew over 5-14 days. The three SMO structures (PDB 4N4W, 4QIM, 4QIN) were all obtained using PEG 600-based crystallization conditions.


### Crystallization precipitant for XylE inward-open conformation

PEG 600 at 20% (w/v) was used as the primary precipitant in sitting drop vapor diffusion crystallization of truncated E. coli XylE (residues 6-480). Combined with 0.2 M calcium acetate at pH 4.2, 20 degrees Celsius. Crystals grew over 3-10 days, yielding the inward-facing open conformation (PDB 4QIQ).
