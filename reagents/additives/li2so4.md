# Lithium Sulfate (Li2SO4)

## Overview

Lithium sulfate (Li2SO4) is a salt used as a crystallization precipitant in protein crystallization screens. It is a kosmotropic salt that promotes protein crystallization through salting-out effects.

## Properties

- **Chemical formula**: Li2SO4
- **Molecular weight**: 109.94 g/mol
- **Solubility**: Highly soluble in water (~348 g/L at 20°C)
- **Classification**: Kosmotropic salt (structure-making)
- **Hofmeister series**: Li⁺ is strongly kosmotropic (promotes protein ordering)

## Use in Crystallization

- **Precipitant**: Common component in crystallization screens (e.g., JCSG+, PEG/Ion)
- **Mechanism**: Salting-out effect — Li2SO4 reduces protein solubility, promoting crystallization
- **Kosmotropic action**: Li⁺ ions strengthen water structure, promoting protein-protein contacts
- **Common concentrations**: 0.5-3.0 M in crystallization drops

### Bovine Rhodopsin (N2C/D282C recombinant mutant)

- **Concentration**: 1.2–1.7 M Li₂SO₄
- **Buffer**: 0.1 M Hepes pH 7.5
- **Additive**: 0.05% LDAO
- **Method**: Sitting drop vapor diffusion
- **Protein**: Recombinant N2C/D282C rhodopsin from COS-1 cells (first recombinant GPCR)
- **Result**: P3₁ crystals at 3.4 Å resolution, microcrystallography (5 µm beam)
