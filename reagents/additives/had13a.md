# HAD13a (Heavy-Atom Additive 13a)

## Overview

HAD13a (heavy-atom additive 13a) is an iodine-labeled detergent composed of
an I3C (magic triangle) head group containing three iodine atoms and a
hydrophobic alkyl chain, synthesized via a single-step amido coupling reaction.
It was designed as a phasing tool for membrane protein structure determination
by serial femtosecond crystallography (SFX) using X-ray free electron lasers.
The alkyl chain provides affinity for the hydrophobic, membrane-facing surface
of transmembrane proteins, enabling derivatization without requiring exposed
cysteine residues. At 7.0 keV (1.771 A), iodine has f' = 8.6e, providing
strong anomalous and isomorphous difference signals for experimental phasing.
