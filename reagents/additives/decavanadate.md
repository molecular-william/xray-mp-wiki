# Decavanadate

## Overview

Decavanadate (V10O28 6-) is a polyvanadate anion consisting of ten vanadium atoms in a specific clustered arrangement. It acts as a phosphate analog and inhibitor of P-type ATPases. In the absence of Ca2+, decavanadate binds in a groove between the cytoplasmic domains N and P of SERCA, acting as a wedge that prevents domain closure. This locks the enzyme in an open conformation and inhibits ATPase activity. Decavanadate is also used to form tubular crystals of SERCA for electron microscopy studies.
