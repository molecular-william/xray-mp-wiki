# Polyethylene Glycol 5000 Monomethyl Ether (PEG 5000 MME)

## Overview

Polyethylene glycol 5000 monomethyl ether (PEG 5000 MME) is a high molecular weight polyethylene glycol derivative used as a cryoprotectant and precipitant in protein crystallization. The monomethyl ether end group provides improved solubility and reduced viscosity compared to standard PEG 5000.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Polyethylene glycol 5000 monomethyl ether
- **Class**: polyethylene glycol cryoprotectant

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Cryoprotectant for PSII XFEL experiments

In TR-SFX experiments on Photosystem II from Thermosynechococcus vulcanus at SACLA XFEL, PEG 5000 MME was used at 9% concentration as part of the cryoprotectant solution (22% glycerol, 9% PEG 1450, 9% PEG 5000 MME). The crystals were transferred to this solution by stepwise replacement of the mother solution over 1.5 hours before loading into a high viscosity micro-extrusion injector.
