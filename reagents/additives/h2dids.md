# H2DIDS (4,4-Diisothiocyanatodihydro-stilbene-2,2-disulfonic acid)

## Overview

H2DIDS (4,4-diisothiocyanatodihydro-stilbene-2,2-disulfonic acid) is a disulfonic stilbene derivative that irreversibly inhibits anion exchangers by covalently binding to the protein and blocking the transport cycle. It locks the human AE1 anion exchanger in an outward-facing open conformation, enabling crystallographic determination of the structure.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 4,4-Diisothiocyanatodihydro-stilbene-2,2-disulfonic acid
- **Class**: Anion exchanger inhibitor

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural determination of AE1 anion exchanger

H2DIDS was used to covalently modify the C-terminal membrane domain of human AE1 (AE1_CTD), locking it in an outward-facing open conformation. The H2DIDS-AE1_CTD complex was then co-crystallized with a monoclonal antibody Fab fragment, enabling the crystal structure determination at 3.5 Å resolution. The covalently bound inhibitor stabilized a single conformational state for crystallographic analysis.
