# PEG 600

## Overview

PEG 600 (polyethylene glycol with molecular weight 600 Da) is a water-soluble polymer used as a precipitating agent in protein crystallization. It works by excluding volume from the protein solution, effectively increasing protein concentration and promoting crystal nucleation and growth.


## Properties


## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Protein crystallization precipitant

PEG 600 was used as the primary precipitant in the crystallization of the Spirulina platensis c15 ring from proton-dependent ATP synthase. The crystallization reservoir contained 10% (v/v) PEG 600, and crystals were cryoprotected in the same buffer supplemented with 35% PEG 600 before flash-freezing in liquid nitrogen.


### Crystallization of HiTehA anion channel

PEG 600 was used as a crystallization precipitant for HiTehA, the TehA anion channel from Haemophilus influenzae. Crystals grew in space group R3 at 4 degrees C from protein in beta-octylglucoside detergent, across a pH range of 5.2 to 10.2. The structure was solved at 1.20 A resolution by SeMet SAD phrasing.
