# Capuramycin

## Overview

Capuramycin is a nucleoside natural product that inhibits bacterial MraY
(phospho-MurNAc-pentapeptide translocase), an essential enzyme in
peptidoglycan biosynthesis. It is one of five classes of natural product
inhibitors with demonstrated antibacterial activity against MraY. In
translocase assays with MraY from Aquifex aeolicus (MraY_AA), capuramycin
shows a median inhibitory concentration (IC50) of approximately 56 uM.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### MraY inhibitor for enzymatic assays

Used as a specific inhibitor of MraY translocase activity in TLC-based assays to validate functional activity of recombinant MraY_AA; shows dose-dependent inhibition with IC50 ~56 uM after 20 min preincubation at 30C


### Lead compound for antibiotic development

As one of only five classes of natural product inhibitors targeting the essential MraY enzyme, capuramycin serves as a lead scaffold for developing novel antibiotics against Gram-positive and Gram-negative bacterial pathogens
