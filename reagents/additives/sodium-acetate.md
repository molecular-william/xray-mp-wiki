# Sodium Acetate

## Overview

Sodium acetate (CH₃COONa) is an acidic buffer and precipitant used in membrane protein crystallization. It provides pH conditions in the range 4.0–5.5, which is useful for crystallizing proteins that prefer acidic environments.

## Properties

- **Chemical formula**: CH₃COONa·3H₂O (trihydrate)
- **Typical concentrations**: 50–200 mM (commonly 100 mM)
- **pH range**: 4.0–5.5 (buffered with acetic acid)
- **Function**: Crystallization precipitant and pH buffer
- **Mechanism**: Acetate ion acts as a weak acid buffer; sodium ions contribute to charge screening

## Proteins Using Sodium Acetate (from this wiki)

- [cd81-tetraspanin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cd81-tetraspanin/) — 0.1 M sodium acetate pH 4.5, 18–22% PEG 400, 0.2 M NaCl as crystallization reservoir
- [lb-semisweet](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lb-semisweet/) — 0.1 M sodium acetate pH 4.5, 18–20% PEG 400, 0.2 M NaCl as crystallization reservoir
- [lpa1-receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/lpa1-receptor/) — 0.1 M sodium acetate pH 4.5, 18–20% PEG 3350 for ONO-3080573 antagonist complex
- [mmpL3](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mmpL3/) — 0.1 M sodium acetate pH 5.4, 0.05 M magnesium acetate as crystallization reservoir (Su et al., PNAS 2019)
