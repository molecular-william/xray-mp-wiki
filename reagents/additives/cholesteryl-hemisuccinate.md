# Cholesteryl Hemisuccinate (CHS)

## Overview

Cholesteryl hemisuccinate (CHS) is a cholesterol derivative commonly used as a stabilizing additive in membrane protein solubilization and purification. It is used in combination with detergents such as DDM to help maintain membrane protein stability, activity, and oligomeric state during purification. CHS integrates into detergent micelles and helps mimic the native lipid bilayer environment.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Stabilization of EcSemiSWEET during purification

CHS was used at 0.4% (w/v) in combination with 2% DDM for solubilization of the E. coli membrane fraction containing EcSemiSWEET. During purification, CHS concentration was reduced to 0.01% in the washing buffer and maintained at this level. The combination of DDM and CHS helped maintain EcSemiSWEET in a soluble, active state throughout purification and tag cleavage.
