# Tris(2-carboxyethyl)phosphine (TCEP)

## Overview

Tris(2-carboxyethyl)phosphine (TCEP) is a water-soluble, strong reducing agent used to prevent disulfide bond formation and maintain cysteine residues in their reduced thiol state during protein purification and crystallization. Unlike dithiothreitol (DTT), TCEP is stable over a wide pH range (1-10), does not interfere with mass spectrometry, and is not volatile. It is widely used in membrane protein biochemistry at concentrations of 0.25-5 mM.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Reducing agent in membrane protein purification

TCEP at 0.25-1 mM was included in all buffers during hSCD1 purification from Sf9 insect cell membranes, including the solubilization buffer (0.25 mM), Ni-NTA elution buffer (0.25 mM), and SEC buffer (1 mM). TCEP prevents oxidation of cysteine residues during the multi-step purification workflow, maintaining protein stability and preventing aggregation.
