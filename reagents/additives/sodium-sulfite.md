# Sodium Sulfite (Na2SO3)

## Overview

Sodium sulfite (Na2SO3) is an inorganic salt that provides sulfite ions (SO3 2-) in solution. In the context of membrane protein research, it has been used as a screening agent for protein thermostabilization, where it was tested for its ability to shift the melting temperature of the HSC hydrosulfide channel.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: sodium sulfite
- **Chemical formula**: Na2SO3
- **Class**: inorganic salt

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Protein thermostabilization screening

Sodium sulfite was used in thermostabilization screening of the HSC hydrosulfide channel from Clostridium difficile. Apparent melting temperature (Tm) was determined by fitting peak heights from size exclusion chromatography to a Boltzmann sigmoidal curve. Na2SO3 increased the Tm of HSC to 53.8 C (control: 53.3 C), providing a modest thermostabilization effect of +0.5 C.
