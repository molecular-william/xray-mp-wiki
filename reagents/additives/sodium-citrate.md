# Sodium Citrate

## Overview

Sodium citrate (sodium citrate dihydrate) is a weak acid buffer and precipitant used in membrane protein crystallization, particularly in [vapor-diffusion](/methods/crystallization/vapor-diffusion/) and LCP methods. It provides acidic pH conditions (pH 4.5–6.0) that favor crystallization of certain membrane proteins.

## Properties

- **Chemical formula**: Na₃C₆H₅O₇·2H₂O
- **Typical concentrations**: 50–200 mM (commonly 100 mM)
- **pH range**: 4.5–6.0 (buffered with citric acid)
- **Function**: Crystallization precipitant and pH buffer
- **Mechanism**: Acts as a weak acid buffer and can promote protein precipitation through charge screening

## Proteins Using Sodium Citrate (from this wiki)

- [nupg-nucleoside-transporter](/proteins/nupg-nucleoside-transporter/) — 0.1 M sodium citrate pH 5.0 in LCP crystallization (WT P2₁ form); also used in WT P1 crystallization
- [adenine-nucleotide-transporter](/proteins/adenine-nucleotide-transporter/) — 5 mM sodium citrate + 50 mM Na arseniate as crystallization additive (alternative to 10–20 mM NiSO₄)
- [p2y12-receptor](/proteins/p2y12-receptor/) — 0.1 M sodium citrate pH 5.6, 18–20% PEG 400 as precipitant
- [lpa1-receptor](/proteins/lpa1-receptor/) — 0.1 M sodium citrate pH 5.6, 18–20% PEG 400 for ONO-9910539 antagonist complex
- [cd81-tetraspanin](/proteins/cd81-tetraspanin/) — 0.1 M sodium citrate pH 5.0 in crystallization screens
