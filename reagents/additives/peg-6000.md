# PEG 6000 (Polyethylene Glycol 6000)

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: Polymer precipitant
- **Molecular weight**: 6,000 Da
- **Function**: Excludes water volume, promoting protein crystallization

## Uses in Membrane Protein Work

PEG 6000 is a common macromolecular precipitant used in [vapor-diffusion](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/vapor-diffusion/) crystallization of both soluble and membrane proteins.

## Proteins Using PEG 6000 (from this wiki)

- [psi-lhci-supercomplex](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/psi-lhci-supercomplex/) — 4.5-7.0% (w/v) in crystallization reservoir (50 mM Tris-HCl pH 7.0, 50 mM Li₂SO₄)
  - Also used as cryoprotectant component (8% in final cryo-solution)

## Related Precipitants

- [peg-400](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg-400/) — low molecular weight PEG, used as cryoprotectant
- [li2so4](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/li2so4/) — salt precipitant used in combination with PEG
