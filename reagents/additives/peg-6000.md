# PEG 6000 (Polyethylene Glycol 6000)

## Overview

[PEG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg/) 6000 (polyethylene glycol with molecular weight 6000 Da) is a high molecular weight polyethylene glycol used as a crystallization precipitant for proteins. [PEG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg/) 6000 promotes protein crystallization through the excluded volume effect, where the large [PEG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg/) molecules are excluded from the protein surface, effectively increasing the protein concentration and promoting protein-protein interactions.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: polyethylene glycol 6000
- **Molecular weight**: 6000 Da
- **Class**: polyethylene glycol (crystallization precipitant)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization precipitant for membrane protein complexes

[PEG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg/) 6000 at 10% (w/v) was used as the primary precipitant in sitting-drop vapor diffusion crystallization of the CusBA heavy-metal efflux complex from Escherichia coli. It was combined with Na-HEPES buffer (0.1 M, pH 7.5), [Ammonium Acetate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/ammonium-acetate/) (0.1 M), and 20% [Glycerol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glycerol/) as cryoprotectant. Co-crystals of the CusA-[CusB Membrane Fusion Protein](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/cusB/) complex grew to full size within 2 months.
