# Hoechst 33342

## Overview

Hoechst 33342 is a fluorescent DNA-binding dye that also serves as a
prototypical substrate for multidrug resistance (MDR) transporters, including
LmrP from Lactococcus lactis. It is a lipophilic bisbenzimide compound with
two positive charges at neutral pH (protonated at the methyl-substituted N
atom in the piperazine ring and the adjacent benzimidazole ring). Hoechst
33342 binds to the outward-open state of LmrP, coordinated by key residues
including Asp235 and Glu327. In the LmrP-Hoechst 33342 complex structure
(PDB 6T1Z, 2.9 A resolution), the ligand sits adjacent to an embedded
phosphatidylglycerol lipid within the binding cavity.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### MDR transport substrate

Hoechst 33342 is a well-established substrate for studying multidrug efflux pump activity. In LmrP, it binds to the outward-open conformation and is transported via proton-motive force.


### Fluorescent probe for transport assays

The fluorescence properties of Hoechst 33342 allow monitoring of transport in real-time using inside-out membrane vesicles, as demonstrated in LmrP functional studies.
