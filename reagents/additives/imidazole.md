# Imidazole

## Overview

Imidazole is a small organic compound commonly used as an eluent in Ni-NTA affinity chromatography for proteins with C-terminal or N-terminal histidine tags (His-tags). Imidazole competes with the histidine residues for binding to nickel ions on the resin, allowing elution of the target protein at increasing concentrations.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: imidazole
- **Class**: nitrogen heterocycle

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### His-tag protein elution

In [MexB Multidrug Exporter from Pseudomonas aeruginosa](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mexB/) purification, imidazole was used at 200 mM to elute MexB with a C-terminal hexahistidine tag from a Ni-NTA affinity column, after washing with 30 mM imidazole.


### His-tag protein elution (TALON resin)

In [NtMATE2 Nicotine Transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/ntmate2/) purification, imidazole at 20 mM was used in the TALON equilibration buffer and at 20-40 mM in the wash buffer. Elution was performed with 200 mM imidazole pH 8.0.
