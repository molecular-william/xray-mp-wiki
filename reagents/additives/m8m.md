# M8M (Bis-(3-Sulfanylpropionyl)disulfide, 8-Carbon Linker MTS Reagent)

## Overview

M8M is a bifunctional maleimide thiosulfonate (MTS) reagent with an 8-carbon linker used for site-directed disulfide cross-linking of engineered cysteine residues. In conformational trapping experiments on GluN1b/GluN2B NMDA receptors, M8M was tested alongside M2M and M4M. Unlike M4M which potently enhanced NMDA receptor currents by 3-4 fold, M8M did not produce significant potentiation, suggesting the 8-carbon linker is too long to effectively bridge the engineered cysteine pairs at the GluN1b-GluN2B ATD interface in the active conformation. The optimal linker length (M4M with 4-carbon linker) corresponds to the estimated distance of approximately 10 Angstroms between gamma-sulfur atoms of the mutated cysteines.
