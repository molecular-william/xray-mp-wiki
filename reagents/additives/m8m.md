# M8M (Bis-(3-Sulfanylpropionyl)disulfide, 8-Carbon Linker MTS Reagent)

## Overview

M8M is a bifunctional maleimide thiosulfonate (MTS) reagent with an 8-carbon linker used for site-directed disulfide cross-linking of engineered cysteine residues. In conformational trapping experiments on GluN1b/GluN2B NMDA receptors, M8M was tested alongside [M2M (Bis-(3-Sulfanylpropionyl)disulfide, 2-Carbon Linker MTS Reagent)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/m2m/) and [M4M (Bis-(3-Sulfanylpropionyl)disulfide, 4-Carbon Linker MTS Reagent)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/m4m/). Unlike [M4M (Bis-(3-Sulfanylpropionyl)disulfide, 4-Carbon Linker MTS Reagent)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/m4m/) which potently enhanced NMDA receptor currents by 3-4 fold, M8M did not produce significant potentiation, suggesting the 8-carbon linker is too long to effectively bridge the engineered cysteine pairs at the GluN1b-GluN2B ATD interface in the active conformation. The optimal linker length ([M4M (Bis-(3-Sulfanylpropionyl)disulfide, 4-Carbon Linker MTS Reagent)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/m4m/) with 4-carbon linker) corresponds to the estimated distance of approximately 10 Angstroms between gamma-sulfur atoms of the mutated cysteines.
