# PEG 5000 MME

## Overview

PEG 5000 MME (Polyethylene Glycol 5000 Monomethyl Ether) is a high-molecular-weight PEG derivative used as a crystallization precipitant. The monomethyl ether form has one end capped with a methyl group, reducing crystallinity and providing more uniform precipitation behavior compared to standard PEG 5000.

## Properties

- **Molecular weight**: ~5,000 Da
- **Form**: Monomethyl ether (one end —OCH3, other end —OH)
- **Class**: Non-ionic polymer precipitant
- **Use**: Protein crystallization, particularly for membrane protein complexes and large macromolecular assemblies

## Use in SARS-CoV-2-CTD/hACE2 Complex Crystallization

- **Concentration**: 10% w/v
- **System**: 0.1 M MES pH 6.5, 10% PEG 5000 MME, 12% 1-propanol
- **Method**: Sitting-drop vapor diffusion at 18°C
- **Result**: Diffraction-quality crystals of SARS-CoV-2-CTD/hACE2 complex (2.5 Å, PDB: 6LZG)
- **Space group**: P41212

## Advantages Over Standard PEG

- Methyl capping reduces PEG crystallization, giving smoother phase behavior
- More reproducible crystallization conditions
- Often preferred for large protein complexes and membrane protein constructs
