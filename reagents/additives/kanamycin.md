# Kanamycin

## Overview

Kanamycin is an aminoglycoside antibiotic used as a selection agent in bacterial expression systems. It is added to culture media to maintain plasmids encoding antibiotic resistance genes (e.g., kanamycin resistance on pET vectors) during protein expression in E. coli.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: kanamycin A
- **Class**: aminoglycoside antibiotic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Bacterial selection

In [MexB Multidrug Exporter from Pseudomonas aeruginosa](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mexB/) expression, kanamycin was added at 50 mg/L to E. coli culture media to maintain the pET28 vector carrying the MexB gene during protein expression.
