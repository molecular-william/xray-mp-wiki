# PEG 400

## Overview

PEG 400 (polyethylene glycol, average MW 400 Da) is a low-molecular-weight PEG used as a crystallization precipitant and cryoprotectant for membrane proteins. Unlike higher MW PEGs (e.g., PEG 4000, PEG 6000), PEG 400 acts as a small-molecule precipitant and can penetrate crystal lattices.

## Properties

- **Average MW**: 400 Da
- **Concentration**: Typically 10-40% (v/v) in crystallization conditions
- **Use**: Precipitant for [vapor-diffusion](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/vapor-diffusion/) and LCP crystallization; cryoprotectant
- **Advantage**: Low viscosity, penetrates crystal lattice for cryoprotection

## Proteins Using PEG 400 (from this wiki)

- [psi-lhci-supercomplex](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/psi-lhci-supercomplex/) — Used as cryoprotectant in [psi-lhci-supercomplex](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/psi-lhci-supercomplex/) workflow:
  - Step 1: Cryoprotectant exchange from PEG 6000 to PEG 400
  - Final cryoprotectant: 17.5% PEG 400, 17.5% TEG, 8% MPD
  - Enables flash-cooling for X-ray data collection

## Proteins Using PEG 400 (from raw papers, not yet in wiki)

- **PEG 400** (sodium/betaine symporter): 35-37% PEG 400 in crystallization
- **PEG 400 CRyst**: 32-35% PEG 400, 0.1 M potassium phosphate pH 7.5
- **PEG 400**: 30% PEG 400, 100 mM MgCl2, 50 mM MES pH 6.5
- **Adenosine A1R** (iso327): PEG 400 in crystallization screens
- **dDATmfc** (dopamine transporter): PEG 400 37%, Na MES pH 6.8
- **GlpG** (rhomboid protease): 30% PEG 400, 0.1 M CHES pH 9.5
