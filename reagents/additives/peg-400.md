# Polyethylene Glycol 400 (PEG 400)

## Overview

Polyethylene glycol 400 (PEG 400) is a water-soluble polymer with an average molecular weight of 400 Da, widely used as a precipitating agent in protein crystallization. PEG 400 acts by excluded volume effects, reducing the available solvent volume and promoting protein protein interactions that lead to crystal formation. It is suitable for crystallizing proteins in the 10-100 kDa molecular weight range and is compatible with most detergents used in membrane protein crystallography. Typical concentrations range from 10-40% (v/v).

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Precipitant in vapor diffusion crystallization of membrane proteins

PEG 400 at 30-35% (v/v) was used as the primary precipitant in the crystallization reservoir solution for hSCD1, combined with 100 mM Tris-HCl pH 8.5 and 220 mM sodium citrate. Crystals were grown by vapor diffusion at 4 C and diffracted to 3.25 A resolution.
