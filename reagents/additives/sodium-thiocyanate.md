# Sodium Thiocyanate (NaSCN)

## Overview

Sodium thiocyanate (NaSCN) is an inorganic salt used as a crystallization additive and precipitant in membrane protein X-ray crystallography. The thiocyanate ion (SCN-) is a chaotropic agent that can modulate protein solubility and crystallization behavior.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Sodium thiocyanate
- **Chemical formula**: NaSCN
- **Molecular weight**: 81.07
- **Class**: Inorganic salt / chaotropic agent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization precipitant for pLGICs

Sodium thiocyanate at 0.4 M concentration is used as a crystallization precipitant for the pentameric ligand-gated ion channel GLIC. It is part of a crystallization solution that also includes sodium acetate buffer, PEG 4000, glycerol, and bromoform. The thiocyanate ion may contribute to protein precipitation through chaotropic effects.
