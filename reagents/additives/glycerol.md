# Glycerol

## Overview

Glycerol (propane-1,2,3-triol) is a small polyol widely used as a cryoprotectant and protein stabilizer in membrane protein biochemistry. It is added to storage and crystallization buffers at concentrations typically ranging from 5% to 30% (v/v) to prevent protein denaturation during freezing and to enhance protein stability during purification and storage. Glycerol is compatible with most detergents and does not interfere with X-ray crystallography.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: propane-1,2,3-triol
- **Class**: polyol cryoprotectant

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Cryoprotection

Glycerol is added to protein samples before flash-freezing in liquid nitrogen or liquid propane to prevent ice crystal formation and protect protein structure during cryo-cooling for X-ray crystallography. Typical concentrations range from 15% to 30% (v/v).


### Protein stabilization

Glycerol is included in purification and storage buffers at 5-20% (v/v) to maintain protein stability and prevent aggregation. It acts as an osmolyte that preferentially excludes from the protein surface, stabilizing the folded state.
