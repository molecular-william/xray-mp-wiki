# Glycerol

## Overview

Glycerol is a viscous polyol used as a protein stabilizer and cryoprotectant in membrane protein workflows. It reduces protein denaturation during purification and freezing.

## Properties

- **Common concentrations**: 1-20% (v/v)
- **Function**: Stabilizes protein structure, reduces surface adsorption, cryoprotection

## Proteins Using Glycerol (from this wiki)

- [nTMATE2-transporter](//xray-mp-wiki/proteins/nTMATE2-transporter/) — used at three different concentrations reflecting optimization across workflow stages:
  - 10% glycerol in expression buffer (300 mM NaCl, 20 mM Tris-HCl pH 8.0)
  - 5% glycerol in [affinity-chromatography](//xray-mp-wiki/methods/purification/affinity-chromatography/) chromatography buffers (300 mM NaCl, 50 mM HEPES-Na pH 7.0)
  - 1% glycerol in SEC buffer (300 mM NaCl, 50 mM HEPES-Na pH 7.0)
  - Concentration decreased progressively to reduce viscosity during size-exclusion chromatography
