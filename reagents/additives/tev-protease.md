# TEV Protease (Tobacco Etch Virus Protease)

## Overview

TEV protease is a highly sequence-specific cysteine protease derived from the Tobacco Etch Virus NIa protein. It recognizes the sequence ENLYFQ-G/S and cleaves between Q and G/S residues. It is widely used in membrane protein crystallography to remove purification and solubility tags (e.g., His6, GST, MBP) after affinity chromatography. TEV protease is active at 4°C, making it compatible with membrane protein stability requirements. It retains ~50% activity at 4°C compared to 30°C optimum. Typical usage is 1:50 to 1:100 (w/w) protease:protein for 12-16 hours at 4°C.
