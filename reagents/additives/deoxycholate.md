# Deoxycholate (DXC)

## Overview

Deoxycholate (DXC), or sodium deoxycholate, is a bile salt with anionic detergent properties. It is an amphipathic molecule used as a ligand that binds to the multidrug recognition pocket of the E. coli transporter [MdfA Multidrug Efflux Transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/mdfA/). DXC is also used in membrane protein purification and crystallization studies. In [MdfA Multidrug Efflux Transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/mdfA/) structural studies, DXC was found to bind in the central cavity of the Mdr recognition pocket and plays a complex role in stabilizing different conformational states.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 3-alpha,12-alpha-dihydroxy-5-beta-cholan-24-oic acid
- **Chemical formula**: C24H40O4
- **Molecular weight**: 408.58 g/mol
- **Class**: bile salt, anionic detergent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### MdfA substrate analog

Deoxycholate binds to the multidrug recognition pocket of [MdfA Multidrug Efflux Transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/mdfA/), a secondary multidrug efflux transporter from E. coli. It was identified as a substrate analog that binds in the central cavity of the Mdr recognition pocket. DXC was used as a ligand for crystallizing [MdfA Multidrug Efflux Transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/mdfA/) mutants (Q131R and Q131R/L339E). In membrane-embedded [MdfA Multidrug Efflux Transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/mdfA/), DXC further stabilizes the inward-closed conformation, opposite to its effect in the crystal structures where it appeared to partially open the cytoplasmic rim.


### Crystallization additive

DXC (1.2 mM) was included in the SEC buffer for [MdfA Multidrug Efflux Transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/mdfA/)(Q131R/L339E) purification prior to crystallization. It serves as a bound ligand that helps stabilize the protein for crystal formation.
