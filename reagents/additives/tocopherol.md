# Tocopherol (Vitamin E)

## Overview

Tocopherol (commonly known as vitamin E) is a lipophilic antioxidant that protects biological membranes and proteins from oxidative damage. In membrane protein crystallization, tocopherol is used as an antioxidant additive to prevent oxidation-sensitive cofactors and protein residues from degradation during purification and crystallization. It is particularly important for photosynthetic protein complexes that contain redox-active cofactors such as chlorophylls, pheophytins, and metal clusters.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: alpha-Tocopherol
- **Chemical formula**: C29H50O2
- **Class**: Lipid-soluble antioxidant

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Antioxidant additive for photosystem II crystallization

Tocopherol at 10 mM was used as an antioxidant additive in the solubilization and crystallization buffers for [Photosystem II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/photosystem-ii/) (PSII) microcrystals from Thermosynechococcus elongatus. It was included alongside PIPES buffer (100 mM, pH 7.0) and 5 mM CaCl2 to protect the redox-sensitive chlorophyll and manganese cluster cofactors from oxidative damage during the extended crystallization process.
