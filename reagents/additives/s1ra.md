# S1RA (4-[2-(5-methyl-1-(naphthalene-2-yl)-1H-pyrazol-3-yl)oxy]ethyl]morpholine)

## Overview

S1RA is a sigma-1 receptor ligand purchased from Topscience Co. Ltd. It was used in crystallization and soaking experiments with the Xenopus laevis sigma-1 receptor (xlS1R). S1RA was soaked into pre-formed xlS1R crystals and also used with the xlS1R C179/C203 double mutant to validate the PATH2 ligand entry hypothesis.


## Properties


## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Sigma-1 receptor crystallization

Soaked into xlS1R crystals to obtain closed conformation structure (PDB 7W2D) and into xlS1R C179/C203 mutant crystals (PDB 7W2H)


### Ligand entry pathway validation

In the xlS1R C179/C203-S1RA structure, an additional S1RA molecule was observed near the ligand entrance between alpha4 and alpha5, possibly representing an entering or exiting pose for the ligand
