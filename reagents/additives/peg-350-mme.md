# Polyethylene Glycol 350 Monomethyl Ether (PEG 350 MME)

## Overview

Polyethylene glycol 350 monomethyl ether (PEG 350 MME) is a low molecular weight polyethylene glycol derivative used as a crystallization precipitant in vapor diffusion crystallization. The monomethyl ether modification reduces the hygroscopic nature of PEG while maintaining its ability to precipitate proteins by excluded volume effects.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: polyethylene glycol 350 monomethyl ether
- **Molecular weight**: ~350 g/mol
- **Class**: polyethylene glycol derivative

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization of TRPV6 ion channel

PEG 350 MME at 20-24% was used as the main crystallization precipitant for
TRPV6_cryst in hanging-drop vapor diffusion. The reservoir solution also
contained 100 mM NaCl and 100 mM Tris-HCl pH 8.0-8.5. Crystals grew as thin
plates over approximately 2 weeks at 20°C. For cryoprotection, the PEG 350 MME
concentration was increased to 33-36%.
