# Glycylglycylglycine (Gly3)

## Overview

Glycylglycylglycine (Gly3, Gly-Gly-Gly) is a tripeptide composed of three glycine residues. In the context of membrane protein crystallography, it was observed as electron density in the crystallization buffer of the neuronal SNARE complex. It may serve as a crystallization additive or buffer component, though its specific role in crystallization is not fully characterized.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: glycylglycylglycine
- **Chemical formula**: C6H12N2O4
- **Molecular weight**: 176.17 g/mol
- **Class**: tripeptide

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization buffer component of neuronal SNARE complex

Considerable electron density observed around the linker regions of the neuronal SNARE complex was attributed to sulphate ions, glycylglycylglycine, and n-nonyl beta-D-glucopyranoside molecules in the crystallization buffer. The positions of glycylglycylglycine could not be fully resolved at the 3.4 A resolution of the structure.
