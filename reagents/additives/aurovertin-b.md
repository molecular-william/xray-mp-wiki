# Aurovertin B

## Overview

Aurovertin B is a polyketide antibiotic from the fungus Calcarisporium arbuscula
that inhibits oxidative phosphorylation by binding to the F1 catalytic sector of
the F1Fo-ATP synthase. It is an uncompetitive inhibitor with respect to nucleotides,
meaning it binds preferentially to the enzyme-substrate complex rather than the
free enzyme. Aurovertins B and D have identical biological properties and are
more potent than aurovertin A. Related compounds include citreoviridin (from
Penicillium citreoviride and Aspergillus terreus) and asteltoxin (from Aspergillus
stellatus). Aurovertins have also been used as fluorescent probes of conformational
changes during catalysis in F1-ATPase, as they fluoresce weakly in aqueous solution
but strongly when bound to the protein.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Fluorescent properties enable use as conformational probe
- Selective inhibition of ATP hydrolysis vs. synthesis
- High affinity (Kd ~1 uM for high-affinity site)

### Disadvantages

- Not commercially available as a research tool
- Complex polyketide structure makes chemical synthesis challenging
- Multiple binding sites with different affinities complicate analysis
