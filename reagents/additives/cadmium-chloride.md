# Cadmium Chloride

## Overview

Cadmium chloride (CdCl2) is an inorganic salt that provides cadmium ions (Cd2+) used as a crystallization additive and functional probe. Cadmium ions can serve as heavy atom derivatives for phasing and also act as structural cross-linking agents by bridging protein residues. Cd2+ is a heavy metal with high atomic number, making it useful for anomalous diffraction experiments. In potassium channel studies, Cd2+ forms specific metal bridges between cysteine residues in the outer vestibule, stabilizing C-type inactivated conformations.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: cadmium chloride
- **Molecular weight**: 183.32 g/mol
- **Class**: inorganic salt (heavy metal salt)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization additive for crystal contacts

Cadmium chloride at 1 mM was used in the MemGold H11 crystallization condition for OsNIP2;1. Cd2+ ions bridge tetramers via their cytoplasmic faces in the crystal lattice, promoting crystal formation. The role of cadmium in lattice formation may be structural rather than specific ligand binding.


### Metal bridge probe for C-type inactivation in K+ channels

Cd2+ at concentrations of 10-1,000 uM enhances the rate of C-type inactivation in the Y82C mutant of KcsA potassium channel via metal-bridge formation between adjacent subunits in the outer vestibule. Cd2+ does not affect wild-type KcsA, confirming specific interaction with engineered cysteines. Metal bridge formation was demonstrated using tandem dimer and tandem tetramer constructs showing subunit-specific coordination.
