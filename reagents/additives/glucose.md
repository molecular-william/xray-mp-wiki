# Glucose

## Overview

Glucose is a monosaccharide (simple sugar) that serves as a control compound in membrane protein studies. In the opsin study, 200 mM glucose was tested for its effect on rhodopsin reconstitution and showed no inhibitory effect, confirming that sugar headgroups alone cannot enter the retinal-binding pocket of GPCRs. This finding was critical in demonstrating that the hydrophobic alkyl chain of detergent molecules is necessary for pocket penetration and ligand binding. In the XylE study, D-glucose was shown to bind to the E. coli sugar transporter XylE with a Kd of about 0.77 mM and to inhibit D-xylose transport by nearly 90%, acting as a competitive inhibitor despite not being a transport substrate.
