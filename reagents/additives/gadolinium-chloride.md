# Gadolinium Chloride (GdCl3)

## Overview

Gadolinium chloride (GdCl3) is a lanthanide salt that dissociates into Gd3+ ions in aqueous solution. Gd3+ is a potent, voltage-independent inhibitor of ATP-gated P2X ion channels. It blocks P2X receptor currents in a concentration-dependent manner, completely abolishing responses to low ATP concentrations (30 uM) while only partially inhibiting responses to high ATP concentrations (1 mM). Gd3+ also accelerates the rate of current deactivation following ATP removal. The inhibition mechanism is direct channel block rather than ATP sequestration, as Gd3+ present only in the control (pre-application) solution is sufficient to inhibit subsequent ATP-evoked currents.
