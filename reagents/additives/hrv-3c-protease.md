# HRV 3C Protease

## Overview

HRV 3C protease (Human Rhinovirus 3C protease) is a cysteine protease that specifically recognizes and cleaves at the sequence LVPRGS. It is widely used in recombinant protein expression to remove affinity tags and fusion partners from purified proteins. In the context of GPCR crystallization, HRV 3C protease has been used to cleave N-terminal extensions from muscarinic receptor constructs to improve diffraction quality. The enzyme requires low concentrations (typically 2% w/w relative to target protein) and moderate incubation times for efficient cleavage.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Human Rhinovirus 3C protease (EC 3.4.22.29)
- **Class**: cysteine protease, tag-cleavage enzyme
