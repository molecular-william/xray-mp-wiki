# Peptide:N-Glycosidase F (PNGase F)

## Overview

Peptide:N-glycosidase F (PNGase F) is a glycosidase enzyme that cleaves all types of N-linked glycans (high-mannose, hybrid, and complex) from glycoproteins at the consensus sequence Asn-X-Ser/Thr. PNGase F completely removes the chitobiose core, leaving no glycan remnants, unlike EndoH which leaves a single N-acetylglucosamine residue. PNGase F is used in membrane protein workflows for complete deglycosylation to achieve maximum protein homogeneity for crystallization and structural studies.


## Properties

- **Class**: glycosidase enzyme

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Complete deglycosylation for crystallization

PNGase F treatment of glycosylated proteins removes all N-linked glycans, producing maximum homogeneity. This is particularly useful for membrane proteins with multiple glycosylation sites that impede crystallization. Complete deglycosylation can be more effective than partial removal with EndoH when high-mannose and complex glycans are both present.


### Glycosylation site mapping

PNGase F treatment followed by mass spectrometry can identify the exact number and positions of N-glycosylation sites by observing the mass shift upon complete deglycosylation. The absence of glycan remnants confirms PNGase F activity.
