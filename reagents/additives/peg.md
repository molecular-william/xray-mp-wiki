# PEG (Polyethylene Glycol)

## Overview

Polyethylene glycol (PEG) is a water-soluble polymer used as a linker in conjugating reporter molecules (fluorophores, PEGylated ligands) to drug scaffolds. In A2AAR structural studies, PEG linkers of varying lengths connect Preladenant derivatives to reporter groups while maintaining receptor binding.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: poly(ethylene glycol)
- **Class**: polymer
- **Typical concentration**: not applicable (used as linker)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Ligand conjugation linker

PEG linkers of different lengths were used to conjugate reporter molecules to Preladenant derivatives. PSB-2113 is a PEGylated Preladenant derivative used for co-crystallization with A2A-PSB1-bRIL. The PEG linker extends out of the binding pocket and was disordered in the crystal structure with no electron density observed.
