# Mercury (HgCl2) - Aquaporin Inhibitor

## Overview

[Mercury(II) Chloride](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/mercury-ii-chloride/) (HgCl2) is a mercurial compound historically used to define and inhibit [Aquaporin Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/aquaporin/) water channels. Mercury selectively binds to cysteine residues near the [Aquaporin Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/aquaporin/) pore, producing steric blockade of water conduction. IC50 values range from 18 uM (L170C mutant) to 345 uM (WT AqpZ). Mercury inhibition is reversible with 5 mM 2-mercaptoethanol. The small ionic radius of Hg2+ (1.10 A) enables deep binding within the protein without causing conformational changes.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: mercury(II) chloride
- **Chemical formula**: HgCl2
- **Molecular weight**: 271.49 g/mol
- **Class**: mercurial compound, heavy metal salt

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Aquaporin inhibition study

HgCl2 was used to determine the mechanism of [Aquaporin Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/transport-mechanisms/aquaporin/) inhibition by mercury. Proteoliposomes were incubated with various concentrations of HgCl2 for 1 hour at room temperature prior to stopped-flow analysis. Inhibition of water conduction was measured by liposome shrinkage. IC50 values: WT AqpZ 345 uM, T183C 84 uM, L170C 18 uM. Inhibition is reversible with 5 mM 2-mercaptoethanol.


### X-ray crystallography heavy atom derivative

Mercury has been historically used as a heavy atom derivative for de novo phasing via isomorphous replacement and anomalous diffraction. However, the thiol-mercury bond is labile under X-ray radiation exposure, causing radiation-induced cleavage and mobility of mercury atoms in electron density maps.
