# Polyethylene Glycol 1450 (PEG 1450)

## Overview

Polyethylene glycol 1450 (PEG 1450) is a medium molecular weight polyethylene glycol used as a precipitant in protein crystallization. Its moderate size makes it suitable for crystallizing large protein complexes such as photosystem II, where it promotes crystal formation through volume exclusion effects.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Polyethylene glycol 1450
- **Class**: polyethylene glycol precipitant

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization precipitant for Photosystem II

In TR-SFX experiments on Photosystem II from Thermosynechococcus vulcanus at SACLA XFEL, PEG 1450 was used at 5-7% in the final crystallization buffer (20 mM MES pH 6.0, 20 mM NaCl, 40 mM MgSO4, 10 mM CaCl2, 5-7% PEG 1450, 0.85% n-heptyl-beta-D-thioglucopyranoside). It was also used at increased concentrations (9% PEG 1450) during post-crystallization cryoprotectant steps.
