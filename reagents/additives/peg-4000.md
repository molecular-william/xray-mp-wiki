# PEG 4000

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: Polyethylene glycol, average molecular weight 4,000 Da
- **Type**: Macromolecular precipitant
- **Concentration range**: Typically 5–20% (w/v) in crystallization conditions

## Use in Crystallization

PEG 4000 is a macromolecular precipitant commonly used in vapor diffusion crystallization of proteins. Unlike low-MW PEGs (PEG 400, PEG 600) which act as small-molecule precipitants and cryoprotectants, PEG 4000 functions through excluded volume effects — it occupies solvent volume and effectively increases protein concentration, promoting crystal nucleation and growth.

## Example: AcrB Crystallization

- **Concentration**: 8% PEG 4000 (w/v)
- **Buffer**: 0.1 M Na-phosphate pH 6.2, 0.1 M NaCl
- **Method**: Sitting-drop vapor diffusion
- **Protein**: AcrB-Linezolid complex (28 mg/mL in 20 mM [sodium-phosphate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/sodium-phosphate/) pH 6.2, 10% [glycerol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glycerol/), 0.2% DDM)
