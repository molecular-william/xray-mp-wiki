# PEG 4000 (Polyethylene Glycol 4000)

## Overview

PEG 4000 (polyethylene glycol with molecular weight 4000 Da) is a high molecular weight polyethylene glycol used as a crystallization precipitant for proteins. PEG 4000 promotes protein crystallization through the excluded volume effect, where the large PEG molecules are excluded from the protein surface, effectively increasing the protein concentration and promoting protein-protein interactions.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: polyethylene glycol 4000
- **Molecular weight**: 4000 Da
- **Class**: polyethylene glycol (crystallization precipitant)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization precipitant for membrane proteins

PEG 4000 at 9-12% (w/v) was used as the primary precipitant in GLIC crystallization. It was combined with ammonium sulfate (200-500 mM) and sodium acetate buffer (50 mM, pH 4.0) in sitting-drop vapor diffusion crystallization at 4 C.
