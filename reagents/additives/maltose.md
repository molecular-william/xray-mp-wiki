# Maltose

## Overview

Maltose is a disaccharide composed of two glucose units linked by an alpha-1,4 glycosidic bond. In the opsin study, 200 mM maltose was tested for its effect on rhodopsin reconstitution and showed no inhibitory effect, making it likely that the hydrophobic alkyl chain of detergent molecules (not just the sugar headgroup) is necessary for entering the retinal-binding pocket. In the bcMalT study, maltose was the physiological substrate of the Bacillus cereus maltose transporter, with an IC50 of 4.5 uM in competitive binding assays. Maltose was also found bound in the active site of bcMalT crystals, originating from the head group of detergents (DDM or DM) used during protein purification.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: alpha-D-glucopyranosyl-(1->4)-D-glucopyranose
- **Molecular weight**: 342.3 g/mol
- **Class**: disaccharide
