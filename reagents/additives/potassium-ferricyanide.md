# Potassium Ferricyanide

## Overview

Potassium ferricyanide (K3[Fe(CN)6]) is an oxidizing agent used as an artificial electron acceptor in time-resolved structural studies of photosystem II. It accepts electrons from the reducing side of PSII, facilitating the turnover of the oxygen-evolving complex during flash illumination experiments.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Potassium ferricyanide
- **Class**: oxidizing agent / electron acceptor

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Artificial electron acceptor for pre-flashing PSII samples

In TR-SFX experiments on Photosystem II from Thermosynechococcus vulcanus at SACLA XFEL, potassium ferricyanide was added to the mother liquid as an electron acceptor for pre-flashed samples (10 mM) and non-pre-flashed samples (2 mM). It accepts electrons from the QB site, enabling controlled S-state transitions upon laser flash illumination.
