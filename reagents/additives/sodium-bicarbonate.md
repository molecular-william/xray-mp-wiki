# Sodium Bicarbonate (NaHCO3)

## Overview

Sodium bicarbonate (NaHCO3) is an inorganic salt that serves as a pH buffer and provides bicarbonate ions (HCO3-) in solution. In the context of membrane protein research, it has been used as a screening agent for protein thermostabilization, where it was tested for its ability to shift the melting temperature of the HSC hydrosulfide channel.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: sodium bicarbonate
- **Chemical formula**: NaHCO3
- **Class**: inorganic salt

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Protein thermostabilization screening

Sodium bicarbonate was used in thermostabilization screening of the HSC hydrosulfide channel from Clostridium difficile. Apparent melting temperature (Tm) was determined by fitting peak heights from size exclusion chromatography to a Boltzmann sigmoidal curve. NaHCO3 increased the Tm of HSC to 57.1 C (control: 53.3 C), providing a significant thermostabilization effect of +3.8 C.
