# Potassium Thiocyanate

## Overview

Potassium thiocyanate (KSCN) is a salt used in protein crystallization screens. It provides both potassium ions and thiocyanate anions, which can influence protein solubility and crystal packing.

## Properties

- **Chemical formula**: KSCN
- **Class**: Inorganic salt
- **Use**: Protein crystallization precipitant, ion source

## Use in DAP12-TM Crystallization

- **Concentration**: 0.149 M
- **System**: 10% cholesterol, 12.4% PEG 3350, 0.149 M KSCN
- **Result**: DAP12-TM trimer crystallized (1.77 Å, PDB: 4WOL)
- **Ion coordination**: Potassium ion coordinated by Asp23 and Thr27 in the trimer polar core (distances 2.7–3.1 Å)
