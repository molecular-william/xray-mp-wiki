# PEG 2000

## Overview

PEG 2000 (polyethylene glycol, average MW 2000 Da) is a medium-molecular-weight PEG used as a crystallization precipitant for membrane proteins. It bridges the gap between low-MW PEGs (PEG 400–600) and high-MW PEGs (PEG 4000–6000).

## Properties

- **Average MW**: 2,000 Da
- **Concentration**: Typically 5–20% (w/v) in crystallization conditions
- **Use**: Precipitant for vapor diffusion crystallization
- **Mechanism**: Excluded volume effect — occupies solvent volume, effectively increasing protein concentration

## Proteins Using PEG 2000 (from this wiki)

- [mmpL3](//xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mmpL3/) — 7.5–17.5% PEG 2000, 10–20% PEGMME 350, 50 mM ADA pH 6.0–7.0 in [vapor-diffusion](//xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/vapor-diffusion/) vapor diffusion crystallization of MmpL3-T4L + ligand (NITD-349 or SPIRO) complexes (Yang et al., JMB 2020)
