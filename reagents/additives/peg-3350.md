# PEG 3350

## Overview

PEG 3350 (Polyethylene Glycol 3350) is a medium-molecular-weight PEG used as a crystallization precipitant. It is one of the most commonly used PEGs in protein crystallization screens.

## Properties

- **Molecular weight**: ~3,350 Da
- **Class**: Non-ionic polymer precipitant
- **Use**: Protein crystallization, including transmembrane peptides in LCP

## Use in DAP12-TM Crystallization

### Knoblich et al. (Cell Reports 2015)

- **Trimer**: 12.4% (w/v) PEG 3350 in 10% cholesterol, 0.149 M potassium thiocyanate
- **Tetramer**: 19.7% (w/v) PEG 3350 in 0.1 M bis-tris propane chloride (pH 6.07), 0.269 M calcium chloride
- **Method**: Lipidic cubic phase (LCP) crystallization in monoolein
- **Result**: DAP12-TM peptide crystallized as trimer (1.77 Å, PDB: 4WOL) and tetramer (2.14 Å, PDB: 4WO1)
