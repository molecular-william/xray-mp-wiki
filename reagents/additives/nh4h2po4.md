# Ammonium Dihydrogen Phosphate (NH4H2PO4)

## Overview

Ammonium dihydrogen phosphate (NH4H2PO4), also known as monobasic ammonium phosphate, is used as a crystallization buffer component and precipitant in protein crystallization screens.

## Properties

- **Chemical formula**: NH4H2PO4
- **Molecular weight**: 115.03 g/mol
- **Solubility**: Highly soluble in water (~226 g/L at 20°C)
- **pH**: Acidic (aqueous solution pH ~4.5)
- **Function**: Provides ammonium ions (NH4⁺) and dihydrogen phosphate (H2PO4⁻)

## Use in Crystallization

- **Buffer component**: Used in crystallization buffers for pH control
- **Precipitant**: Can act as a precipitant in [[vapor-diffusion]] crystallization
- **Ammonium source**: NH4⁺ ions can promote crystal formation through salting-out effects
- **Phosphate source**: H2PO4⁻ can participate in specific protein interactions

## Proteins Using NH4H2PO4 (from this wiki)

- [[nTMATE2-transporter]] — 0.1 M NH4H2PO4 in LCP crystallization reservoir (with 0.1 M HEPES-Na pH 7.5 and 2.5-7.5 mM nicotine); served as primary precipitant for NtMATE2 crystals at 3.5 Å resolution

## Common Concentrations

- **Typical range**: 50-200 mM in crystallization drops
- **Often combined with**: PEGs, salts, or other precipitants
