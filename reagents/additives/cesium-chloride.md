# Cesium Chloride

## Overview

Cesium chloride (CsCl) is an inorganic salt that provides cesium ions (Cs+) used as a structural probe in ion channel crystallography. Cs+ is a heavy monovalent cation with a high atomic number, making it useful for anomalous diffraction studies. In ion channels, Cs+ can compete with K+ for binding sites and is often used to identify the selectivity filter region.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: cesium chloride
- **Molecular weight**: 168.36 g/mol
- **Class**: inorganic salt (heavy alkali metal salt)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Anomalous diffraction probe for pore identification

CsCl was used in GLIC crystallography studies to identify the ion conduction pathway. The strong anomalous signal from Cs+ allowed mapping of the pore region and identification of ion-binding sites within the transmembrane domain.
