# Sodium Cyanoborohydride

## Overview

Sodium cyanoborohydride (NaBH3CN) is a reducing agent used in the reductive methylation of protein lysine side chains for NMR spectroscopy studies of membrane proteins. It selectively reduces imine intermediates formed between primary amines (lysine side chains and N-terminus) and formaldehyde, resulting in the addition of [13C]methyl groups to lysine residues. This isotopic labeling technique enables site-specific monitoring of conformational changes in GPCRs by NMR.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: sodium cyanoborohydride
- **Chemical formula**: NaBH3CN
- **Molecular weight**: 59.34 g/mol
- **Class**: reducing agent for reductive methylation
