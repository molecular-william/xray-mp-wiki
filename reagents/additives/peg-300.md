# PEG 300

## Overview

PEG 300 (polyethylene glycol, average MW 300 Da) is a low-molecular-weight PEG used as a crystallization precipitant for membrane proteins. It functions through the excluded volume effect, reducing protein solubility by occupying solvent volume.

## Properties

- **Average MW**: 300 Da
- **Concentration**: Typically 20–35% (v/v) in crystallization conditions
- **Use**: Precipitant for LCP and [vapor-diffusion](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/vapor-diffusion/) crystallization
- **Advantage**: Very low viscosity, suitable for LCP mesophase mixing

## Proteins Using PEG 300 (from this wiki)

- [nupg-nucleoside-transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/nupg-nucleoside-transporter/) — 28% PEG 300, 0.1 M sodium citrate pH 5.0 in LCP crystallization of WT form (P2₁ space group, 3.8 Å resolution)
