# EGTA (Ethylene Glycol Tetraacetic Acid)

## Overview

EGTA (ethylene glycol tetraacetic acid) is a calcium-selective chelating agent with significantly higher affinity for Ca2+ than for Mg2+. It is used to control free calcium concentrations in biochemical assays and crystallization experiments. EGTA is preferred over EDTA when selective removal of Ca2+ is required while preserving Mg2+ binding, as EGTA has approximately 10,000-fold greater selectivity for Ca2+ over Mg2+.
