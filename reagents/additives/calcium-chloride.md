# Calcium Chloride

## Overview

Calcium chloride (CaCl2) is a divalent cation salt used in protein crystallization screens. It provides calcium ions that can coordinate with protein side chains and water molecules.

## Properties

- **Chemical formula**: CaCl2
- **Class**: Inorganic salt, divalent cation source
- **Use**: Protein crystallization, ion coordination

## Use in DAP12-TM Crystallization

- **Concentration**: 0.269 M
- **System**: 0.1 M bis-tris propane chloride (pH 6.07), 19.7% PEG 3350, 0.269 M CaCl2
- **Result**: DAP12-TM tetramer crystallized (2.14 Å, PDB: 4WO1)
- **Ion coordination**: 2 overlapping Ca2+ sites with pentagonal bipyramidal geometry, coordinated by Asp23 and Thr27 side chains + water molecules
