# Nanobody Nb.AT110i1

## Overview

Nb.AT110i1 is a synthetic, affinity-matured nanobody (single-domain antibody fragment, VHH) that selectively binds to the active-state conformation of the human angiotensin II type 1 receptor (AT1R). It was discovered using a synthetic yeast-displayed nanobody library and was critical for obtaining the first active-state AT1R crystal structure (PDB: 6DO1).

## Discovery and Maturation

1. **Initial clone (Nb.AT110)**: Discovered by magnetic-activated cell sorting (MACS) and fluorescence-activated cell sorting (FACS) of a synthetic yeast-displayed library. Selective for agonist-bound AT1R (AngII-bound vs. antagonist-bound). Increased AngII affinity ~6-fold (reciprocal cooperativity).

2. **Affinity maturation**: Error-prone PCR on Nb.AT110 DNA created a library of ~1.5 × 10⁸ clones. Two rounds of FACS enrichment at progressively lower AT1R concentrations yielded the consensus variant Nb.AT110i1.

3. **Four mutations**: A31V (CDR1), N58D (CDR2), I98V (CDR3), Y113N (CDR3)

## Binding Mode

- Binds to the intracellular transducer-binding pocket of active-state AT1R
- Inserts its unusually long CDR3 loop into the pocket
- Key interactions: D108 (CDR3) forms a polar network with receptor residues; N113 (introduced in maturation) interacts with D108 to rigidify the CDR3 loop
- Forms a parallel dimer with a receptor-bound Nb.AT110i1 molecule through a salt bridge (D58–R45) and β-sheet contacts
- The dimerization partners show poor electron density in the crystal structure

## Production

- Cloned into pET26b with C-terminal 6xHis tag
- Expressed in T7 Express lysY BL21 *E. coli* in Terrific Broth
- Induced with 1 mM IPTG, harvested after overnight at 27°C
- Purified from periplasmic fraction by Ni-NTA chromatography
- Dialyzed against 20 mM HEPES pH 7.5, 150 mM NaCl
