# 2B12 Antibody Fragment

## Overview

2B12 is a conformation-specific monoclonal antibody fragment (Fab) that selectively recognizes the outward-open and outward-occluded states of the LeuT amino acid transporter from Aquifex aeolicus. The 2B12 Fab binds to the cytoplasmic face of LeuT, contacting residues on the N-terminus, TM2, the TM6-TM7 loop, and IL5, burying 827 A^2 of surface area. It was used to stabilize the outward-open conformation of LeuT^K(Y108F) for X-ray crystallography at 3.1 A resolution. The 2B12 Fab does not bind to the inward-open LeuT^K(TSY) mutant, demonstrating its conformation specificity.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to LeuT from Aquifex aeolicus (outward-open/occluded states)

The 2B12 Fab binds on the cytoplasmic face of LeuT, contacting residues on the N-terminus (residues 7-11), TM2 (residues 68, 71, 72), the loop between TM6 and TM7 (residues 268, 270-274), and IL5 (residues 427, 430, 431, 434, 435, 438, 439, 441, 443). The interface buries 827 A^2 of surface area. The Fab does not bind to inward-open states of LeuT.
