# Nb.AT110i1 Synthetic Nanobody

## Overview

Nb.AT110i1 is a synthetic conformation-specific nanobody discovered using a yeast-displayed synthetic nanobody library. It was affinity-matured from the parent clone Nb.AT110 and binds with high specificity to the active-state conformation of the human angiotensin II type 1 receptor (AT1R). The nanobody inserts its unusually long CDR3 loop into the receptor's intracellular transducer-binding pocket, stabilizing the activated conformation and enabling the 2.9 A resolution crystal structure of active-state AT1R bound to the peptide S1I8. Four affinity-maturing mutations (A31V, N58D, I98V, Y113N) were introduced to create Nb.AT110i1 from Nb.AT110.
