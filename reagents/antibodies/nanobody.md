# Nanobody

## Overview

Nanobodies (single-domain antibodies, VHH) are camelid heavy-chain antibody fragments (~15 kDa) that bind to specific conformational states of membrane proteins. They are widely used as crystallization facilitators for GPCRs and other membrane proteins, stabilizing particular states and providing additional crystal contacts.

## Properties

- **Size**: ~15 kDa (130 amino acids), much smaller than conventional antibodies
- **Origin**: Camelid VHH (single-domain) or engineered human single-domain antibodies
- **Advantage**: Can access cryptic epitopes and intracellular loops that are inaccessible to conventional antibodies
- **Use**: Conformational stabilization, crystallization facilitator, transducer mimic

## Mechanism

Nanobodies bind to specific conformational states of their target protein, effectively "locking" the protein in that state. This reduces conformational heterogeneity, which is a major barrier to crystallization. When combined with fusion partners (BRIL, T4 lysozyme), they provide both stability and crystal contacts.
