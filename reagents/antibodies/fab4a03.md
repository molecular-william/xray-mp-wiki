# Fab4A03 Antibody Fragment

## Overview

Fab4A03 is a monoclonal antibody fragment (Fab) that specifically binds the human angiotensin II type 2 receptor (AT2R) at the extracellular vestibule. It functions as a positive allosteric modulator, increasing the binding affinity of both [Angiotensin II](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/angiotensin-ii/) and [S1I8](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/s1i8/) peptide ligands for AT2R. The Fab4A03 fragment was essential for stabilizing the extended binding domain of AT2R, enabling crystallographic structure determination of the receptor-ligand complex at 3.2 A resolution (PDB 5XJM).

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Positive allosteric modulation of AT2R peptide binding

Fab4A03 binds to the extracellular vestibule of AT2R and stabilizes the conformation of the extended binding domain, making it suitable for s-AngII binding. In specific binding assays with [125I]s-AngII, Fab4A03 increased the apparent affinity (Kd decreased from 2.76 nM to 0.56 nM) and Bmax of the receptor. Similar enhancement was observed for [125I]AngII binding. No direct interaction between s-AngII and Fab4A03 was observed (distance > 7 A), confirming its allosteric mechanism of action.


### Crystallization chaperone for AT2R structure determination

The Fab4A03 fragment formed a stable complex with AT2R-BRIL that was monodisperse by SEC and crystallized in lipidic cubic phase (LCP). The Fab4A03-AT2R interface includes interactions with extracellular loop regions, and the Fab occupies a binding site equivalent to the positive allosteric modulator binding site of the M2 muscarinic acetylcholine receptor.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Acts as positive allosteric modulator, increasing ligand affinity
- Enables crystallization of AT2R in complex with peptide ligands
- Binds at a site distinct from the orthosteric pocket, preserving native ligand binding
- Does not directly interact with the peptide ligand, minimizing structural artifacts

### Disadvantages

- Specifically raised against AT2R; not generalizable to other GPCRs
- Requires hybridoma/antibody engineering expertise to generate
