# Fab2838 Antibody Fragment

## Overview

Fab2838 is a mouse monoclonal antibody Fab fragment raised against the human adenosine A2A receptor ([A2AR](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-adenosine-a2a-receptor-a2ar/)) that acts as an allosteric inverse-agonist. Unlike conventional GPCR nanobodies that stabilize the active state (e.g., Nb80 for beta2AR), Fab2838 recognizes the intracellular surface of [A2AR](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-adenosine-a2a-receptor-a2ar/) and locks the receptor in an inactive conformation, preventing agonist binding to the extracellular pocket. Its unusually long CDR-H3 penetrates deeply into the receptor core, forming a modified ionic lock between helices III and VI.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Allosteric inactivation of A2AR

Fab2838 prevents agonist binding to [A2AR](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-adenosine-a2a-receptor-a2ar/) by locking the intracellular G-protein-binding pocket in an inactive conformation. This demonstrates that signal transfer between the ligand-binding pocket and G-protein-binding pocket is bidirectional — locking the intracellular side allosterically inactivates the extracellular agonist-binding site. Kd = 4.4 nM for Fab2838 binding to [A2AR](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-adenosine-a2a-receptor-a2ar/).


### Structural tool for GPCR studies

Fab2838 provides a unique tool for studying GPCR inactivation mechanisms and ligand-binding kinetics by separating ligand binding from equilibrium shifts between different activation states of the receptor. The CDR structure offers a template for designing peptides or small-molecule compounds targeting the intracellular allosteric pocket of GPCRs.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human [A2AR](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-adenosine-a2a-receptor-a2ar/) (intracellular allosteric site)

CDR-H3 forms 6 hydrogen bonds and 8 van der Waals contacts with helices II, III, VI and VII; hydrogen bond network including two water molecules between CDR-H3 and helices III and VI; 14 additional hydrogen bonds from other CDRs (H1, H2, L1, L2, L3) with helices VI, VIII, ICL1, ICL2, and ICL3

- **Key residues**: ['R102(3.50) — forms modified ionic lock with E228(6.30) stabilized by CDR-H3', 'E228(6.30) — ionic lock partner with R102; shifts towards cytoplasmic space', 'T41(2.39) — interacts with CDR-H3 through water-mediated network', 'W246(6.48) — toggle switch for activation; conformation affected by allosteric lock']
