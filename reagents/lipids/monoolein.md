# Monoolein

## Overview

Monoolein (mono-oleoyl-glycerol) is a monoacyl lipid used for preparing the lipid cubic phase for membrane protein crystallization. It was used as the lipid matrix for growing bacteriorhodopsin crystals (Landau & Rosenbusch, 1996). While only monoacyl and unbranched lipid (mono-olein) is used in preparing the lipid cubic phase, native purple membrane lipids were also carried along through the detergent extraction. In LSP-grown crystals of the Blastochloris viridis photosynthetic reaction center (PDB 2WJM, 1.86 Å), monoolein was found to displace the native ubiquinone at the QB binding site, and a 36 Å continuous electron density feature at the H- and M-subunit interface likely arises from an unidentified lipid.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Cubic lipid phase crystallization of bacteriorhodopsin

Monoolein was used to form the lipid cubic phase matrix for crystallization of bacteriorhodopsin at 1.55 Å resolution.


### Cubic lipid phase crystallization of reaction centre

Monoolein (12% w/v) was used as the lipid matrix for cubic phase crystallization of the Rhodobacter sphaeroides photosynthetic reaction centre at 2.35 Å resolution (PDB 1ogv). The lipid forms bilayers of ~34 Å thickness, compatible with the R. sphaeroides membrane lipid composition.


### LSP crystallization of Blastochloris viridis reaction centre

Monoolein was used as both the LSP matrix and as a competing ligand at the QB ubiquinone binding site in the LSP-grown crystal structure of the B. viridis photosynthetic reaction center (PDB 2WJM, 1.86 Å). Monoolein displaced the native ubiquinone at QB, with hydrogen bonds between its hydroxyl groups and surrounding amino acids (Asn-L213, Glu-L212, His-L190, Leu-L193, Phe-L216, Ser-L223) well satisfied. The fatty acid tail was fully visible on the protein surface, indicating that hydrophobic binding interactions in RC_vir are not selective. Time-resolved absorption spectroscopy on related RC_sph LSP-grown crystals showed reduced QB occupancy (20 ± 10%) compared to detergent solution (50 ± 10%), consistent with monoolein competition at the QB pocket.
