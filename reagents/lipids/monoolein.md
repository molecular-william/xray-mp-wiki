# Monoolein

## Overview

Monoolein (1-monoolein) is a monoglyceride consisting of a single oleic acid (cis-9-octadecenoic acid) chain esterified to glycerol. It is the primary lipid component used in lipidic cubic phase (LCP) crystallization of membrane proteins. Monoolein forms a bicontinuous cubic mesophase at physiological temperatures, providing a native-like membrane environment for membrane protein crystallization.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 1-monoolein
- **Chemical formula**: C19H36O4
- **Molecular weight**: 328.49 g/mol
- **Class**: monoglyceride
- **Acyl chain composition**: Oleic acid (C18:1, cis-9)
- **Phase transition temperature**: 20 C (cubic phase stable at room temperature)
- **Class**: monoglyceride

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### LCP crystallization matrix

Monoolein is used as the primary lipid component for LCP crystallization. It is mixed with membrane protein sample at a 1:1 protein-to-lipid ratio using the twin-syringe mixing method. The protein-lipid mixture is deposited over reservoir solution in glass sandwich plates. Monoolein is available from NU-CHEK-PREP and Sigma-Aldrich.
