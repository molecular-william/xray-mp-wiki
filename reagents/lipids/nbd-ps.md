# NBD-Phosphatidylserine (NBD-PS)

## Overview

NBD-phosphatidylserine (NBD-PS) is a fluorescent lipid probe consisting of a phosphatidylserine (PS) lipid backbone with a nitrobenzoxadiazole (NBD) fluorophore attached to the serine head group. The NBD group has an excitation maximum of 470 nm and emission maximum of 530 nm. NBD-PS is used as a fluorescent marker to monitor phospholipid translocation (scrambling) between the inner and outer leaflets of lipid bilayers, similar to NBD-PE. In the nhTMEM16 study, NBD-PS scrambling was used to confirm that nhTMEM16 catalyzes the movement of both PE and PS between membrane leaflets in a Ca2+-dependent manner.


## Properties

- **Class**: fluorescent lipid analog

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Lipid scrambling assay

NBD-PS is used alongside NBD-PE in lipid scrambling assays to test the substrate specificity of lipid scramblases. In the nhTMEM16 study, NBD-PS scrambling was measured to demonstrate that nhTMEM16 catalyzes the Ca2+-dependent movement of anionic phospholipids (PS) across the bilayer, consistent with the proposed role of TMEM16 scramblases in exposing phosphatidylserine on the outer leaflet of the plasma membrane during processes like blood coagulation and apoptosis.
