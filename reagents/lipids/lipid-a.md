# Lipid A

## Overview

Lipid A is the hydrophobic anchor of lipopolysaccharide (LPS) in the outer membrane of Gram-negative bacteria. It consists of a phosphorylated glucosamine disaccharide backbone with multiple acyl chains. Lipid A is synthesized on the cytoplasmic side of the inner membrane via the Raetz pathway and must be flipped across the inner membrane by the ABC transporter MsbA. The hexa-acylated form from E. coli is the most well-characterized. In structural studies, lipid A was co-crystallized with MsbA to capture the transporter in a substrate-bound inward-facing conformation.


## Properties

- **Molecular weight**: ~1800
- **Class**: Glycolipid (LPS anchor)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for MsbA flippase structural studies

Lipid A was co-crystallized with MsbA in the facial amphiphile FA-3 to stabilize an inward-facing conformation. Putative lipid A density was observed both inside the TM cavity (canonical binding site) and on an outer surface cleft at the periplasmic ends of TM1-3, offering insights into the lipid A transport pathway. MD simulations in POPE bilayers showed strong electrostatic interactions between lipid A and POPE head groups.
