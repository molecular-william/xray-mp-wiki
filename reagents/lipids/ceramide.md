# Ceramide

## Overview

Ceramide is a sphingolipid composed of a [Sphingosine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/sphingosine/) base linked to a fatty acid via an amide bond. It serves as a signaling lipid in cellular processes and as the primary substrate for ceramidase enzymes that hydrolyse the amide bond to produce [Sphingosine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/sphingosine/) and a free fatty acid. Ceramide is used as a substrate in functional assays to measure ceramidase activity of membrane proteins.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: N-acylsphingosine
- **Class**: Sphingolipid; diacyl lipid

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for ADIPOR ceramidase activity assays

Ceramide substrates of various chain lengths (C6, C18, C24) were used to measure ceramidase activity of [ADIPOR1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/adipor1/) and [ADIPOR2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/adipor2/). [ADIPOR2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/adipor2/) showed preference for C18 ceramide with a Km of 15.6 uM. Fluorescent NBD-C18 ceramide was used for binding assays by [FSEC](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/quality-assessment/fluorescence-size-exclusion-chromatography/). Ultra-performance liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry (UPLC-MS) detected the conversion of ceramide to [Sphingosine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/sphingosine/) and free fatty acid.
