# Lysophosphatidylcholine (LPC)

## Overview

Lysophosphatidylcholine (LPC) is a monoacyl phospholipid consisting of a glycerol backbone with a phosphocholine head group and a single fatty acyl chain. LPC is generated by the enzymatic removal of one acyl chain from phosphatidylcholine by phospholipase A2. LPC is used as a chemical tool to study mechanosensitive channel gating, as its single-acyl-chain structure allows it to enter transmembrane pockets that normally accommodate diacyl phospholipids, thereby disrupting lipid-protein interactions and triggering channel opening.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 1-acyl-sn-glycero-3-phosphocholine
- **Class**: monoacyl phospholipid, lysolipid

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Mechanosensitive channel gating

LPC 18:1 and LPC 14:0 are used to open the E. coli MscS mechanosensitive channel in planar lipid bilayers and liposomes. LPC exchanges reversibly with lipids in the transmembrane pockets of MscS, displacing normal diacyl phospholipids (PE, PG) and triggering channel opening by reducing the extent of acyl-chain interdigitation in the pockets. LPC addition from the cytoplasmic side is more effective, consistent with the model that pockets are most accessible from the cytosolic leaflet.
