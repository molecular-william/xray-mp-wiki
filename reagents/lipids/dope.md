# 1,2-Dioleoyl-sn-glycero-3-phosphoethanolamine (DOPE)

## Overview

DOPE (1,2-dioleoyl-sn-glycero-3-phosphoethanolamine) is a zwitterionic
diacyl phospholipid with two oleoyl (18:1) chains and a phosphoethanolamine
headgroup. It is one of the most abundant phospholipids in bacterial
membranes and is widely used in membrane protein reconstitution into
nanodiscs, liposomes, and supported lipid bilayers. In the study of LmrP,
DOPE was used as the major lipid component (80%) mixed with DOPG (20%) to
form nanodiscs for native mass spectrometry analysis.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Nanodisc reconstitution for native MS

DOPE (80%) combined with DOPG (20%) was used to form nanodiscs for native mass spectrometry analysis of LmrP, enabling detection of tightly bound PG in the binding cavity.
