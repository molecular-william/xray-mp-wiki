# Phosphatidylcholine

## Overview

Phosphatidylcholine (PC) is a major class of phospholipids found in cell membranes. It consists of a [Glycerol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glycerol/) backbone esterified with two fatty acids and a phosphate group linked to [Choline](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/choline/). PC is amphiphilic and forms lipid bilayers, making it essential for membrane protein crystallization. It is commonly used as an exogenous lipid additive to support membrane protein stability and crystallization. IM-MS analysis of [MSCL](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/mscl/) showed that synthetic PC lipids with varying chain lengths (C14-C22) impart comparable stabilization, and PC was also tested with AqpZ and AmtB.
