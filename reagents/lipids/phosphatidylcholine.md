# Phosphatidylcholine

## Overview

Phosphatidylcholine (PC) is a major class of phospholipids found in cell membranes. It consists of a glycerol backbone esterified with two fatty acids and a phosphate group linked to choline. PC is amphiphilic and forms lipid bilayers, making it essential for membrane protein crystallization. It is commonly used as an exogenous lipid additive to support membrane protein stability and crystallization. IM-MS analysis of MscL showed that synthetic PC lipids with varying chain lengths (C14-C22) impart comparable stabilization, and PC was also tested with AqpZ and AmtB.
