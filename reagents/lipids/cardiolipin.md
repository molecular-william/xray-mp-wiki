# Cardiolipin

## Overview

Cardiolipin is a double-phospholipid found predominantly in the inner mitochondrial membrane. Three cardiolipin molecules bind tightly per bovine ADP/ATP carrier monomer, with two cardiolipins sandwiched between monomers on the matrix side, mediating protein-protein interactions that stabilize the dimeric form. Cardiolipin is not essential for yeast growth but is required for full AAC transport activity — in its absence, AAC activity drops to 20% of wild-type levels.
