# Cardiolipin

## Overview

Cardiolipin is a diphosphatidylglycerol lipid found primarily in the inner mitochondrial membrane. It plays essential roles in mitochondrial structure and function, including stabilizing respiratory chain supercomplexes and membrane-associated transporters.

## Properties

- **Chemical class**: Diphosphatidylglycerol (four fatty acyl chains, two phosphatidyl groups linked by glycerol)
- **Location**: Inner mitochondrial membrane (exclusive)
- **Charge**: Net negative charge (four phosphate oxygen atoms)

## Role in ADP/ATP Carrier

Cardiolipin molecules bind to the ADP/ATP carrier (AAC) at positively charged pockets formed by the N-terminal dipoles of the matrix and even-numbered transmembrane helices. These bound cardiolipin molecules are visible in the 3.3 Å m-state crystal structure.
