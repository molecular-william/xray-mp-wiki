# Cardiolipin

## Overview

Cardiolipin is a double-phospholipid found predominantly in the inner mitochondrial membrane. Three cardiolipin molecules bind tightly per bovine ADP/ATP carrier monomer, with two cardiolipins sandwiched between monomers on the matrix side, mediating protein-protein interactions that stabilize the dimeric form. Cardiolipin is not essential for yeast growth but is required for full AAC transport activity — in its absence, AAC activity drops to 20% of wild-type levels. In the Thermothelomyces thermophila AAC BKA-inhibited m-state structure, cardiolipin molecules bind to positively charged pockets due to N-terminal dipoles of the even-numbered transmembrane and matrix helices. Phosphate groups form hydrogen bonds with amide groups at helix N-terminal ends, interacting with positively charged helix dipoles. A single cardiolipin (cdl802) binds at the interface between domains 2 and 3, acting as an inter-domain bridge. IM-MS analysis revealed that cardiolipin (CDL) significantly stabilizes the aquaporin AqpZ and modulates its water channel function.
