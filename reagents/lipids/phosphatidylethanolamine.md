# Phosphatidylethanolamine

## Overview

Phosphatidylethanolamine (PE) is a glycerophospholipid consisting of a diglyceride backbone with an ethanolamine head group. PE is the most abundant phospholipid in bacterial membranes and is a major component of both the inner and outer membranes of Gram-negative bacteria. PE is one of the primary lipid species bound by MCE (mammalian cell entry) family proteins including MlaD, PqiB, and YebT, and was identified as a ligand in the NESG crystal structure of an MlaC homolog (PDB 2QGU). Mass spectrometry confirmed PE co-purification with MlaD, PqiB, and YebT. PE is also enriched in the transmembrane pockets of the mechanosensitive channel MscS, where it intercalates between transmembrane helices and is proposed to play a role in mechanosensation. IM-MS analysis of AmtB showed that PE confers only moderate stabilization.
