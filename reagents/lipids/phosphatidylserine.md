# Phosphatidylserine

## Overview

L-alpha-phosphatidylserine is a negatively charged phospholipid used as a crystallization additive in membrane protein structural studies. In the crystal structure of EspB from Mycobacterium tuberculosis, phosphatidylserine was included as a crystallization additive to test for lipid-protein interactions. Although no phosphatidylserine electron density was observed in the final structure, structural analysis revealed putative lipid-binding sites on the EspB surface with hydrophobic grooves and adjacent positive charges consistent with phospholipid recognition. Mature EspB has been shown to bind phosphatidylserine and phosphatidic acid, suggesting a role in host-cell signaling subversion. IM-MS analysis of AmtB showed that PS does not significantly stabilize the channel.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: L-alpha-phosphatidylserine
- **Class**: anionic glycerophospholipid

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization additive

Phosphatidylserine (1 mM) was added to the crystallization drop as an additive to test for specific lipid binding to EspB. Crystals were grown in 0.2 M potassium dihydrogen phosphate, 20% w/v PEG3350 with the lipid additive in the mixed drop. Although no lipid density was observed in the crystal structure, this condition produced the best diffraction (1.5 A resolution).


### Lipid binding studies

Purified mature EspB has been shown to recognize phosphatidylserine and phosphatidic acid, suggesting a role in subversion of phospholipid-mediated host-cell signaling pathways. Hydrophobic grooves on the EspB surface with adjacent positive charges may serve as lipid-binding sites.
