# Phosphatidylinositol-4,5-Bisphosphate (PIP2)

## Overview

Phosphatidylinositol-4,5-bisphosphate (PIP2, also known as PtdIns(4,5)P2) is an acidic phospholipid consisting of a diacylglycerol backbone linked to an inositol head group phosphorylated at the 4 and 5 positions. PIP2 is a minor but functionally critical component of intracellular membranes and serves as a lipid cofactor for ion channel regulation. It was identified as a central regulator of TRIC-B channel gating, binding directly to conserved basic residues in the cytoplasmic vestibule of each monomer and stabilizing the closed state of the ion permeation pathway. Multiple acyl chain species (34:0, 34:1, 34:2, 34:3, 36:1, 36:2, 36:3, 36:8) were detected in TRIC-B2 protein samples by mass spectrometry.
