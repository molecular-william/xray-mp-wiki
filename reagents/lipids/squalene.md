# Squalene

## Overview

Squalene is a linear triterpene lipid found in Halobacterium salinarum purple membranes at equimolar amount to bacteriorhodopsin. In the 1.55 Å structure of bacteriorhodopsin (PDB 1C3W), an S-shaped electron density feature in the bilayer core was tentatively modeled as squalene (SQU701). It is located at the distorted region of helix G near the pi-bulge and is thought to affect reprotonation of the retinal Schiff base during the photocycle (Joshi et al., 1998).

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural component of bacteriorhodopsin crystals

Squalene was identified at the pi-bulge region of helix G in the 1.55 Å structure, consistent with its known presence in purple membranes and its effect on the photochemical cycle.
