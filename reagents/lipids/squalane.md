# Squalane

## Overview

Squalane (C30H62) is a fully saturated hydrocarbon (2,6,10,15,19,23-
hexamethyltetracosane) derived from the triterpene squalene. It is a
colorless, odorless oil that is chemically inert and resistant to oxidation.
In membrane protein crystallography, squalane has been used as an additive
to the lipidic cubic phase (LCP) host lipid to improve crystal size and
quality. For bacteriorhodopsin (bR), adding squalane together with a
detergent to the monoolein LCP enabled reproducible growth of large crystals
(300x300x40 to 400x400x50 um) suitable for high-resolution X-ray data
collection.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### LCP crystallization additive

Squalane was added to the monoolein-based LCP host lipid along with a detergent to obtain large, highly reproducible bacteriorhodopsin crystals (up to 400x400x50 um) for high-resolution X-ray crystallography. The additive likely modulates the LCP matrix properties, reducing viscosity or altering phase behavior to promote larger crystal growth.
