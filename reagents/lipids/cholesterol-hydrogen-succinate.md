# CHS (Cholesterol Hydrogen Succinate)

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: Water-soluble cholesterol derivative
- **Function**: Membrane protein stabilizer
- **Common use**: Combined with mild detergents (LMNG, MNG, OG) for GPCR stabilization

## Uses in Membrane Protein Work

CHS is used to stabilize membrane proteins, particularly GPCRs, during purification and crystallization. It mimics the cholesterol-rich lipid environment of native membranes.

## Proteins Using CHS (from this wiki)

- [etb-receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/etb-receptor/) — 0.2% CHS in solubilization buffer; 0.01% CHS in TALON wash; 0.001% CHS in elution and SEC buffers
- [angiotensin-ii-type-1-receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/angiotensin-ii-type-1-receptor/) — 0.2% CHS in solubilization buffer (1% DDM + 0.2% CHS); 0.01% CHS in purification

- [nTMATE2-transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/nTMATE2-transporter/) — 0.003% combined with 0.03% LMNG in purification and SEC buffers
  - Critical for NtMATE2 stability during size-exclusion chromatography
- [angiotensin-ii-type-1-receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/angiotensin-ii-type-1-receptor/) — 0.05% CHS in solubilization buffer, 0.01% in wash buffer, 0.001% in SEC buffer (HNM); also used as 10% of LCP lipid mix (10:1 [monoolein](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/crystallization/monoolein/):cholesterol w:w) for crystallization

## Related Reagents

- [lmng](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/lmng/) — typically used together with CHS
- Cholesterol — native lipid form (less water-soluble)
