# DMPC (1,2-Dimyristoyl-sn-glycero-3-phosphocholine)

## Overview

DMPC (1,2-dimyristoyl-sn-glycero-3-phosphocholine) is a synthetic diacyl phospholipid with two 14-carbon saturated fatty acyl chains. It is widely used as the long-chain lipid component in bicelle assemblies for membrane protein crystallization. DMPC provides a stable, bilayer-forming core that, when combined with short-chain detergents like CHAPSO, creates disk-like bicelle structures mimicking the native lipid bilayer.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 1,2-dimyristoyl-sn-glycero-3-phosphocholine
- **Chemical formula**: C40H80NO8P
- **Molecular weight**: 734.01 g/mol
- **Class**: diacyl phosphatidylcholine
- **Acyl chain composition**: 14:0/14:0 (dimyristoyl)
- **Phase transition temperature**: 23 C
- **Class**: diacyl phosphatidylcholine

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Bicelle bilayer core formation

DMPC forms the flat bilayer core of bicelle assemblies used for membrane protein crystallization. Combined with CHAPSO at a 2.8:1 (w/w) ratio, DMPC creates stable bicelle disks that provide a near-native lipid environment for membrane proteins during crystallization.


### E. coli lipid membrane mimetic

In kinetic assays of rhomboid proteolysis, DMPC-containing liposomes formed from E. coli lipids were used to reconstitute GlpG for real-time Michaelis-Menten kinetic analysis of substrate processing and inhibition.


### Endogenous lipid in voltage-gated calcium channel structures

DMPC was identified as an endogenous lipid molecule bound to the external, lipid-facing surface of the CaVAb bacterial voltage-gated calcium channel pore module. In drug-free CaVAb structures, DMPC occupies the dihydropyridine-binding site at the subunit interface, where it is displaced by dihydropyridine drugs (amlodipine, nimodipine) upon binding.
