# 1-Palmitoyl-2-Oleoyl-sn-Glycero-3-Phosphocholine (POPC)

## Overview

1-Palmitoyl-2-oleoyl-sn-glycero-3-phosphocholine (POPC) is a major phospholipid component of eukaryotic cell membranes. It is a zwitterionic lipid with a saturated palmitoyl chain at the sn-1 position and a monounsaturated oleoyl chain at the sn-2 position. POPC is widely used in reconstitution studies of membrane proteins and in molecular dynamics simulations of membrane systems.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 1-palmitoyl-2-oleoyl-sn-glycero-3-phosphocholine
- **Class**: zwitterionic phospholipid

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Molecular dynamics membrane simulation

In studies of the F-ATP synthase c-ring from Ilyobacter tartaricus, POPC was used as the membrane lipid in molecular dynamics simulations to study the ion-coordination structure of the Na+ binding sites in the c11-ring rotor.


### DAP12 oligomer MD simulations

POPC bilayer used for 200-ns fully atomistic MD simulations of DAP12-TM trimer and tetramer structures. Trimer simulations tested coordinated potassium with 0-3 ionized aspartic acids (150 mM KCl in bulk); tetramer simulations tested coordinated calcium with 4 or 2 ionized aspartic acids (50 mM CaCl2 in bulk). All simulations at 303.15 K, 1 bar NPT ensemble.


### KcsA potassium channel MD simulations

POPC was used as the major lipid component (3POPC:1POPG ratio) for molecular dynamics simulations of wild-type KcsA in open-gate conformation. The channel was embedded in a mixed POPC/POPG bilayer and solvated in 150 mM KCl using CHARMM-GUI. Simulations at 310 K used CHARMM C36 force field with TIP3P water. A 500-ns unbiased trajectory confirmed the stability of the open-gate KcsA structure with Ba2+ bound at the S4 site of the selectivity filter.


### GLIC pentameric ligand-gated ion channel MD simulations

POPC was used as the pure membrane bilayer for molecular dynamics simulations of the GLIC pentameric ligand-gated ion channel at 2.4 A resolution. GLIC was embedded in a POPC bilayer and solvated with 33930 TIP3P water molecules in a hexagonal box (11.3 nm wide, 14.5 nm high) with 175 Na+ and 190 Cl- ions (300 mM salt). Simulations at pH 4.6 used the CHARMM 27 force field for protein and membrane, with CHARMM 36 lipid parameters for S6' mutant simulations. 100-ns simulations of wild-type GLIC and S6' mutants (S6'G, S6'V, S6'T) were performed, along with membrane potential simulations at +50 mV and -50 mV.
