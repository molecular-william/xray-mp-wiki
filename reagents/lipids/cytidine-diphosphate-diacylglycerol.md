# Cytidine Diphosphate Diacylglycerol

## Overview

Cytidine diphosphate-diacylglycerol (CDP-DAG) is a nucleotide lipid intermediate in the bacterial phospholipid biosynthesis pathway. It serves as the activated lipid donor for phosphatidylglycerol phosphate synthase (PgsA), which catalyzes the transfer of phosphatidyl group to glycerol 3-phosphate, producing phosphatidylglycerol phosphate (PGP) and cytidine monophosphate (CMP). CDP-DAG enters the PgsA active site through a lateral portal and its CDP moiety interacts with conserved residues Asn5 and Arg65. At high concentrations, CDP-DAG can form a dead-end complex with PgsA, inhibiting catalysis.
