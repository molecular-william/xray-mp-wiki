# Cytidine Diphosphate Diacylglycerol

## Overview

Cytidine diphosphate-diacylglycerol (CDP-[DAG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/dag/)) is a nucleotide lipid intermediate in the bacterial phospholipid biosynthesis pathway. It serves as the activated lipid donor for [Phosphatidylglycerol Phosphate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylglycerol-phosphate/) synthase (PgsA), which catalyzes the transfer of phosphatidyl group to [Glycerol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glycerol/) 3-phosphate, producing [Phosphatidylglycerol Phosphate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylglycerol-phosphate/) (PGP) and cytidine monophosphate (CMP). CDP-[DAG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/dag/) enters the PgsA active site through a lateral portal and its CDP moiety interacts with conserved residues Asn5 and Arg65. At high concentrations, CDP-[DAG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/dag/) can form a dead-end complex with PgsA, inhibiting catalysis.
