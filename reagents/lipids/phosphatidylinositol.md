# Phosphatidylinositol

## Overview

Phosphatidylinositol (PI) is an acidic phospholipid consisting of a diacylglycerol backbone linked to an inositol head group. PI is a minor but functionally important component of eukaryotic cell membranes and is proposed to play a key role in the mechanosensitivity of the Mycobacterium tuberculosis MscL channel. IM-MS analysis demonstrated that PI binds to MscL with higher avidity than all other tested phospholipids, conferring a large linear increase in stability upon binding multiple PI molecules. This selectivity is consistent with PI's proposed functional role in Mycobacterium mechanosensation.
