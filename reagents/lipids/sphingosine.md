# Sphingosine

## Overview

Sphingosine (D-erythro-sphingosine, d18:1) is a sphingoid base that serves as the product of ceramidase-mediated hydrolysis of ceramide. It is a 18-carbon amino alcohol with a trans double bond at the C4 position. Sphingosine is released from the ceramidase active site toward the cytoplasm during the enzymatic reaction and is detected by LC-MS as evidence of ceramidase activity.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: D-erythro-sphingosine
- **Chemical formula**: C18H37NO2
- **Class**: Sphingoid base; sphingolipid analog

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Product detection in ceramidase activity assays

Sphingosine was detected by LC-MS analysis as the product of ADIPOR2-mediated ceramide hydrolysis. Retention time of 2.27 min (m/z 300.3) was used to quantify sphingosine formation from ceramide substrates of different chain lengths (C6, C18, C24). Sphingosine standards were used for peak area comparison to quantify ceramidase activity. Molecular dynamics simulations showed sphingosine drifting away from the zinc binding site toward the cytoplasm within a few hundred nanoseconds.
