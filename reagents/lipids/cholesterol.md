# Cholesterol

## Overview

Cholesterol is a sterol lipid and essential component of eukaryotic cell membranes. In membrane protein biochemistry, cholesterol serves both as a structural membrane component and as a specific ligand for certain membrane proteins. Cholesterol can act as an endogenous allosteric modulator, binding to specific pockets within transmembrane proteins and modulating their activity. Cholesterol is commonly used as an additive in membrane protein solubilization and crystallization buffers to maintain protein stability.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to CB1 (CNR1)

Cholesterol binds to a specific site in CB1 involving Phe155^2.42 and Phe237^4.46. In the CB1-Gi complex structure, these side chains undergo large conformational changes and occupy the same position that cholesterol had in agonist-bound crystal structures. Phe4.46 is unique to CB1 in class A GPCRs. Cholesterol is not observed in CB2 structures, suggesting CB1-specific allosteric modulation. Pregnenolone, a cholesterol derivative, may also bind to the same region.

- **Pocket volume**: not reported
- **Key residues**: Phe155, Phe237, Phe4.46 (CB1-specific residue)
