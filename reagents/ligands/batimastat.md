# Batimastat

## Overview

Batimastat is a peptide-mimetic inhibitor of site-2 protease (S2P) family members, including E. coli RseP. It was originally developed as a matrix metalloproteinase (MMP) inhibitor but was found to also inhibit the intramembrane metalloprotease RseP. Batimastat adopts an extended conformation in the RseP active site, flanked by the edge strand of the MRE beta-sheet and TM3-N. It forms hydrogen bonds with the main chain of L66 on the edge strand and with the side chain of N394 on TM3, providing a structural model for substrate binding.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural probe for S2P family

Batimastat was co-crystallized with [ECRSEP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/ecrsep/) and [KKRSEP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/kkrsep/) to obtain the first atomic models of group I S2P family members. The inhibitor binding mode informed mutational analyses of substrate recognition and cleavage.


### In vivo activity assay probe

Batimastat sensitivity assays were used to characterize RseP mutants, measuring the relative cleavage ratio under batimastat treatment to assess resistance and substrate accommodation.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [ECRSEP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/ecrsep/) active site

Hydrogen bonds with L66 backbone on MRE edge strand and N394 side chain on TM3; van der Waals with I19, F44, Y69, L390, L435, L438, M439

- **Key residues**: N394 (clamping residue), F44 (GFG motif), Y69 (edge strand)
