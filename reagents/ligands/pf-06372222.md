# PF-06372222

## Overview

PF-06372222 is a negative allosteric modulator (NAM) of the human glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor (GLP-1R). It was co-crystallized with the GLP-1R transmembrane domain (TMD) for the first crystal structure of GLP-1R (PDB: 5VEW, 2.7 A resolution, Song et al., Nature 2017). PF-06372222 binds to an extra-helical pocket between helices V, VI, and VII near the intracellular half of the receptor, locking it in an inactive conformation by restricting movement of the intracellular tip of helix VI. The compound contains a trifluoromethyl-imidazole group that makes extensive hydrophobic interactions with helix V residues I328(5.58b) and V332(5.62b).

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Glucagon-Like Peptide-1 Receptor (GLP-1R)

PF-06372222 binds in a common NAM pocket between helices V, VI, and VII. The hydrophilic subpocket involves residues R348(6.37b), K351(6.40b), and N408(7.61b). The trifluoromethyl group makes extensive hydrophobic contacts with I328(5.58b) and V332(5.62b) on helix V. S352(6.41b) and T355(6.44b) form hydrogen bonds with the ligand. The C347F mutation (part of the crystallization construct) further stabilizes the binding interface.

- **Key residues**: I328, V332, S352, T355, R348, K351, N408
