# Pyridinium-3,5-Biscarboxylic Acid Mononucleotide (P2CMN)

## Overview

Pyridinium-3,5-biscarboxylic acid mononucleotide (P2CMN) is the direct product of the LarB-catalyzed reaction in the nickel-pincer nucleotide (NPN) cofactor biosynthesis pathway. P2CMN is formed by C5 carboxylation of nicotinic acid adenine dinucleotide (NaAD) followed by phosphoanhydride hydrolysis, releasing AMP. P2CMN is subsequently processed by LarE (sulfur insertion) and LarC (nickel insertion) to form the final NPN cofactor.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### LarB Reaction Product

P2CMN is the product of the LarB-catalyzed reaction, produced by carboxylation of
NaAD at C5 and subsequent phosphoanhydride hydrolysis. The production of P2CMN was
monitored by LC-ESI-MS with m/z 378.02. The WT LarB produced P2CMN at a rate 3.8
times faster than the S127A variant, consistent with the S127A variant's reduced
hydrolysis activity.
