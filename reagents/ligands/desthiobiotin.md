# Desthiobiotin

## Overview

Desthiobiotin is a biotin analog lacking the sulfur atom in the tetrahydrothiophene ring. It is the specific elution agent for Strep-tag affinity chromatography, where it competes with the Strep-tag peptide (WSHPQFEK) for binding to Strep-tactin resin, enabling gentle and specific elution of Strep-tagged proteins under native conditions.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Elution of Strep-tagged glycine receptor α3

Desthiobiotin at 5 mM was used as the elution agent from Strep affinity resin (IBA) during purification of the GlyRα3 crystallization construct. The Strep II tag (WSHPQFEK) on the C-terminus of GlyRα3 bound to Strep-tactin and was specifically eluted with 5 mM desthiobiotin in buffer containing 20 mM Tris pH 8.0, 150 mM NaCl, and 1 mM DDM.


### Desthiobiotin binding at TRPV6 intersubunit interface

Desthiobiotin was included as an elution agent during TRPV6_cryst affinity purification with streptavidin-linked resin. A robust cylindrical density was observed at the intersubunit interface formed by the N-terminal helix, ankyrin repeats 2-4 of one subunit, and the linker domain of an adjacent subunit, tentatively attributed to desthiobiotin. This binding site overlaps with the ATP-binding site conserved in TRPV1, TRPV3, and TRPV4 ankyrin repeat domains.
