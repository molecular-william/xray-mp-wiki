# Sertraline

## Overview

Sertraline is a selective serotonin reuptake inhibitor (SSRI) antidepressant used clinically for the treatment of depression, anxiety disorders, and post-traumatic stress disorder. It acts by blocking the serotonin transporter (SERT), increasing extracellular serotonin concentrations in the synaptic cleft. Sertraline binds to the primary binding pocket of biogenic amine transporters with high affinity.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Delta13 LeuBAT (LeuT engineered mutant)

Dichlorophenyl ring participates in hydrophobic interactions with Tyr21 and is sandwiched between Val104, Tyr108 and Phe259. The two chlorine atoms insert into a groove formed by Pro101, Val104, Ala105, Ser356 and Gly359. The amine group forms a salt bridge with Asp24. Extensive hydrophobic and van der Waals interactions contribute to high affinity.
