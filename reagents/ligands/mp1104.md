# MP1104

## Overview

MP1104 is a potent epoxymorphinan opioid agonist with an iodobenzamide substituent. It acts as a balanced full agonist at the kappa opioid receptor (KOP), activating both G protein and beta-arrestin2 pathways with high potency. The compound was used as the co-crystallized ligand in the active-state KOP structure (PDB: 6B73).

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Kappa opioid receptor (KOP)

- Cyclopropylmethyl group forms hydrophobic contacts with W287^6.48, G319^7.42, and Y320^7.43 in the binding pocket
- Iodobenzamide moiety forms a water-mediated hydrogen bond with Y312^7.35
- Methoxy group at position 9 interacts with the binding pocket
- Rigid morphinan scaffold orientation is critical for agonist potency


## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Balanced agonist profile (G protein and beta-arrestin2)
- High potency across opioid receptor subtypes
- Iodobenzamide substituent enables structural probing of binding pocket

### Disadvantages

- Iodobenzamide group limits oral bioavailability
- Potent activity at all three mu/delta/kappa opioid receptors limits subtype selectivity
