# MP1104

## Overview

MP1104 is a potent epoxymorphinan opioid agonist used to capture the active-state conformation of the kappa opioid receptor (KOP). It is a high-affinity agonist that activates both G protein and β-arrestin2 pathways (balanced agonist).

## Properties

- **Chemical class**: Epoxymorphinan (morphinan scaffold with epoxide bridge)
- **Affinity**: High-affinity at KOP (used at 3.1 Å active-state KOP structure)
- **Signaling**: Balanced full agonist — KOP WT: EC₅₀ = 0.003 ± 0.001 nM (cAMP inhibition), EC₅₀ = 0.035 ± 0.010 nM (arrestin recruitment), bias factor ~0.6 toward G protein

## Binding in Active-State KOP (PDB: 6B73)

- Epoxymorphinan nitrogen forms ionic interaction with D138³·³²
- Cyclopropylmethyl group: occupies hydrophobic pocket (W287⁶·⁴⁸, G319⁷·⁴², Y320⁷·⁴³)
- Iodobenzamide moiety: water-mediated H-bond with Y312⁷·³⁵
- Rigid morphinan scaffold determines precise orientation within binding pocket

## Significance

MP1104 was the key ligand used to solve the first active-state KOP crystal structure. Its structural features — particularly the rigid and bulky morphinan scaffold with the cyclopropylmethyl group — make it ideal for capturing the contracted binding pocket of the active-state receptor.
