# HTL6641

## Overview

HTL6641 is a dual orexin receptor antagonist (DORA) with a central benzo- or pyridothiadiazin- 3-one 1,1-dioxide core. It was co-crystallized with OX2R at 2.61 A resolution. In a departure from the horseshoe conformation seen with other DORAs, HTL6641 binds without intramolecular pi-stacking. Instead, the aromatic portion of the central core and the benzyl substituent form an aromatic offset and edge-face pi-stacking clamp around F227^5.42. One sulfonamide oxygen forms a direct hydrogen bond with Q187^4.60, and the pyridine nitrogen forms a hydrogen bond with N324^6.55. The dimethoxypyridyl group occupies the same region as the benzoxazole of [Suvorexant](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/suvorexant/) but sits higher in the orthosteric pocket.
