# N,N'-Dimethylurea (DMU)

## Overview

N,N'-Dimethylurea (DMU) is a urea analogue in which both amide nitrogen atoms are methylated. It is structurally similar to urea but with two additional methyl groups. DMU was used as a crystallization ligand for the urea transporter from Desulfovibrio vulgaris (dvUT) to identify urea binding sites within the selectivity filter. Due to the methyl groups, DMU cannot access the central binding site (Sm) that accommodates native urea.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: N,N'-dimethylurea
- **Chemical formula**: C3H8N2O
- **Molecular weight**: 88.11
- **Class**: urea analogue

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural probe for urea binding sites

DMU was co-crystallized with dvUT at 10 mM to identify urea binding sites. Two strong electron densities were observed at sites So (near the outside) and Si (near the inside) of the selectivity filter. The flat triangular shape of the electron density made the orientation of DMU unambiguous, with both amide nitrogen atoms facing an oxygen ladder. DMU cannot access the central site Sm because the methyl groups clash with threonine side chains.
