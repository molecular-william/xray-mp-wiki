# Carmoterol

## Overview

Carmoterol is a potent beta-adrenergic receptor agonist used in structural studies of beta1- and beta2-adrenergic receptors. It is a synthetic catecholamine derivative with a methylphenoxy tail group that extends into the extended ligand binding pocket of the receptor. Carmoterol acts as a full agonist at beta1AR with high affinity (log KD approximately -7.3) and was used as a crystallization ligand for the turkey beta1AR-M23 structure at 2.6 A resolution.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Turkey Beta1-Adrenergic Receptor (beta1AR)

Carmoterol binds deep within the transmembrane bundle of beta1AR. The catechol headgroup forms hydrogen bonds with Asp121 in transmembrane helix 3 and with Ser211 and Ser215 in extracellular loop 2. The methylphenoxy tail extends into the extended ligand binding pocket, making van der Waals contacts with Trp117, Thr118, Val122, Val125 in helix 3, and Cys199 and Asp200 in EL2. The tail region occupies a space that is not accessible to smaller catecholamines, explaining its higher affinity.

- **Pocket volume**: Extended pocket accessible due to methylphenoxy tail
- **Key residues**: Asp121, Ser211, Ser215, Trp117, Thr118, Val122, Val125, Cys199, Asp200
