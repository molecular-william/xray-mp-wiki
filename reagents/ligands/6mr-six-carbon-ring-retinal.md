# 6mr (Six-Carbon-Ring Retinal)

## Overview

6mr (six-carbon-ring [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/)) is a synthetic [Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/retinal/) chromophore analog featuring a six-carbon ring that locks the C11=C12 double bond in the cis conformation. Unlike native [11-cis-Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/11-cis-retinal/), which undergoes cis-trans isomerization at C11=C12 upon light absorption, 6mr isomerizes at the C13=C14 double bond to form an 11,13-dicis configuration. When bound to [Bovine Rhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/bovine-rhodopsin/) (forming Rh6mr), 6mr enables a photocyclic mechanism of G protein activation. The 11,13-dicis photoproduct thermally reisomerizes to 11-cis over 24-48 hours, allowing repeated activation cycles without chromophore release. All four isomers of 6mr can fit into the [OPSIN](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/opsin/) chromophore binding site, with the 11-cis isomer being energetically preferred and crystallographically observed in Rh6mr.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Photocyclic GPCR activation in bovine rhodopsin

Regeneration of bovine [OPSIN](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/opsin/) with 6mr creates Rh6mr, which undergoes atypical
11-cis to 11,13-dicis isomerization upon light absorption. Rh6mr activates
transducin (Gt) with an activation energy of 11.1 kcal/mol and exhibits
photocyclic behavior with repeated Gt activation after dark recovery periods.


### Optogenetic tool development

The photocyclic behavior of Rh6mr makes it a candidate for optogenetic
applications requiring reversible control over effector ligands, unlike native
vertebrate rhodopsin which undergoes one-way photobleaching and chromophore release.
