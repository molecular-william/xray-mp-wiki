# Carazolol

## Overview

Carazolol is a potent inverse agonist ligand for the beta-adrenergic receptors. It contains a carbazolyl ring system connected via a hydroxy-amine linker to a side chain, making it one of the highest-affinity inverse agonists for the beta2-adrenergic receptor. Carazolol was the key ligand used in the first crystal structure determination of the human beta2-adrenergic receptor (PDB: 2RH1), where it was used to lock the receptor in its inactive conformation for X-ray crystallography.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Beta2-Adrenergic Receptor (beta2 AR)

Carazolol binds deep within the transmembrane bundle of the beta2-adrenergic receptor. The hydroxy-amine motif forms conserved hydrogen bonds with Ser193, Ser194 (TM5) and Ser204 (TM6). The carbazolyl ring system makes extensive hydrophobic contacts with residues from TM3, TM5, TM6, and TM7, including Trp286 (TM7). The inverse agonist activity is attributed to the bulky carbazolyl group that stabilizes the inactive conformation by preventing the outward movement of TM6 characteristic of activation.

- **Key residues**: Asp113, Ser193, Ser194, Ser204, Trp286
