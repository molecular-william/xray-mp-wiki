# Losartan

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: Angiotensin receptor blocker (ARB), non-peptide antagonist
- **Scaffold**: Biphenyl-tetrazole (first clinically used ARB)
- **Active metabolite**: EXP3174 (carboxylic acid derivative, 10–40× more potent)

## Binding to AT1R

Losartan binds to the ligand-binding pocket of the human angiotensin II type 1 receptor (AT1R). Based on docking studies into the 2.9 Å SFX/XFEL structure (PDB: 4YAY):

- **Tetrazole moiety**: Forms a salt bridge with Arg167ᴱᴰᴸ² (ECL2)
- **Naphthyridin-2-one**: Sub-optimal hydrogen bonding with Tyr35¹·³⁹ (distances and angles are less favorable than in ZD7155)
- **Cys180ᴱᴰᴸ²**: Carboxyl group of losartan interacts with Cys180ᴱᴰᴸ² (sub-optimal contacts)
- **Lower binding affinity**: Explains the surmountable (reversible) property of losartan at AT1R

## Active Metabolite: EXP3174

- EXP3174 is the active carboxylic acid metabolite of losartan
- Binds in a similar pose as losartan
- **Key difference**: Carboxyl group engages in a **second salt bridge** with Arg167ᴱᴰᴸ² (similar to ZD7155)
- This explains the higher potency of EXP3174 compared to losartan

## Clinical Properties

- **First ARB approved**: Landmark drug in hypertension treatment
- **Surmountable antagonist**: Effects can be overcome by high AngII [[amicon-filters]]s
- **Oral bioavailability**: Good oral absorption
- **Active metabolite**: EXP3174 contributes significantly to therapeutic effect
