# ML402 (thiophene-carboxamide K2P activator)

## Overview

ML402 (N-[2-(4-chloro-2-methylphenoxy)ethyl]thiophene-2-carboxamide) is a selective small-molecule activator of K2P 2.1 (TREK-1) potassium channels. It functions as a molecular wedge that binds to a cryptic modulator pocket behind the selectivity filter, stabilizing the C-type gate in the leak mode. ML402 is a thiophene-carboxamide compound.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural basis for K2P modulator pocket discovery

ML402 co-crystallization with K2P 2.1cryst confirmed the cryptic binding pocket at the P1-M4 intrasubunit interface behind the selectivity filter. Two ML402 molecules bind per channel dimer. The binding mode is similar to [ML335 (N-aryl-sulfonamide K2P activator)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ml335/), validating the pocket as a druggable site for K2P activation.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to K2P 2.1 (TREK-1) modulator pocket

ML402 binds in the same L-shaped pocket at the P1-M4 intrasubunit interface behind the selectivity filter as [ML335 (N-aryl-sulfonamide K2P activator)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ml335/). The thiophene-carboxamide moiety engages in a cation-pi interaction with Lys271. The 4-chloro-2-methylphenoxyethyl group makes hydrophobic contacts with Phe134. Additional contacts include Trp275. The binding mode positions ML402 as a molecular wedge between P1 and M4 helices, similar to [ML335 (N-aryl-sulfonamide K2P activator)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ml335/).

- **Pocket volume**: 34.4 A^2
- **Key residues**: Lys271, Phe134, Trp275
