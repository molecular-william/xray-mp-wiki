# Nemonapride

## Overview

Nemonapride is an antipsychotic drug that acts as a D2-like [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) receptor
antagonist. It was used as the ligand for determining the high-resolution crystal
structure of the human D4 [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) receptor ([DRD4](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/d4-dopamine-receptor/)) in its inactive state.
Nemonapride binds within the conserved orthosteric binding pocket (OBP) of [DRD4](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/d4-dopamine-receptor/),
forming a salt bridge with D115^3.32. Its conformation is constrained by an
intra-molecular hydrogen bond between its amide nitrogen and the methoxy
substituent of the benzyl ring. Nemonapride's methoxy ring is fixed by
inter-molecular contacts with F411^6.52 and V116^3.33, and a hydrogen bond
with S196^5.42.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### DRD4 structure determination

Nemonapride was used to form a stable [DRD4](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/d4-dopamine-receptor/)/nemonapride complex for crystallization
at the Advanced Photon Source (GM/CA@APS). The complex crystals diffracted to
1.95 Å resolution (PDB 5WIU and 5WIV), representing the highest-resolution
[Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) receptor structures reported.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to D4 [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) receptor ([DRD4](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/d4-dopamine-receptor/))

Salt bridge between tertiary amine and D115^3.32
Hydrogen bond between methoxy group and S196^5.42
Hydrophobic contacts with F411^6.52, V116^3.33
Interactions with non-conserved [EBP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/human-ebp/) residues V87^2.57, L90^2.60, F91^2.61, L111^3.28
