# Thiodigalactoside (TDG)

## Overview

Thiodigalactoside (TDG) is a non-hydrolyzable galactoside analog used as a high-affinity ligand for the lactose permease (LacY) of Escherichia coli. TDG replaces the oxygen atom in the galactosyl-galactosyl linkage with a sulfur atom, rendering it resistant to beta-galactosidase cleavage. It has been used extensively in structural studies of LacY, including the first crystal structure of LacY in complex with a bound sugar analog (PDB 1PV7).

## Binding Mode

### Binding to LacY (Lactose Permease)

TDG binds in the substrate-binding pocket of LacY at the apex of the internal cavity. The galactopyranosyl ring interacts with Trp151 through hydrophobic stacking. Arg144 (helix V) forms bi-dentate hydrogen bonds with the O4 and O3 positions of the galactopyranosyl ring. Glu269 (helix VIII) interacts with the O3 position of the sugar and forms a salt bridge with Arg144. Glu126 (helix IV) also participates in ligand recognition. The sulfur atom in TDG does not significantly alter the binding mode compared to natural galactosides.

- **Key residues**: Arg144, Glu126, Glu269, Trp151, His322
