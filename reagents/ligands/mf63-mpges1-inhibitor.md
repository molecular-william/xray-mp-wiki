# MF63 (Compound 63, Phenanthrene Imidazole mPGES-1 Inhibitor)

## Overview

MF63 (compound 63) is a substituted phenanthrene imidazole inhibitor of microsomal prostaglandin E synthase 1 (mPGES-1). It features a planar chloro-phenanthrene that extends over a flat surface of alpha-4 of monomer 1, and a 2,6-dicyano-phenyl pointing inward between the two protein chains. MF63 shows significant species selectivity due to non-conserved residues between human and rodent mPGES-1 (H53R, P124R, V128G substitutions in mouse/rat).

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Thermal stabilization of mPGES-1

MF63 imparted a 10.4 degree Celsius increase in the aggregation temperature of mPGES-1 as measured by DSLS, the largest shift among the four studied inhibitors. The IC50 was 6 nM using mPGES-1 containing membranes and 1.9 microM using purified protein.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to mPGES-1

Planar chloro-phenanthrene extends over alpha-4 of monomer 1 (P124, S127, V128). 2,6-dicyano-phenyl pointed inward, clamped between two protein chains. First nitrile forms 3.6 A van der Waals contact with C-beta of A123 (monomer 1) and 3.2 A interaction with S127 hydroxyl. Second nitrile packs against L39 side chain (monomer 2) and engages structured water network. Imidazole forms hydrogen bonds with H53 (monomer 2) and structured water (as seen with compound 5). Hydrophobic contacts with R38, L39, F44, D49 side chains (monomer 2).

- **Key residues**: H53 (monomer 2, hydrogen bond with imidazole), P124 (monomer 1, flat hydrophobic surface for phenanthrene), V128 (monomer 1, van der Waals contact with chloro group), S127 (monomer 1)
