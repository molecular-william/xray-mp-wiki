# N-Desmethyltamoxifen

## Overview

N-Desmethyltamoxifen (NM-Tam) is the primary metabolite of tamoxifen formed by N-demethylation, catalyzed primarily by CYP3A4 and CYP3A5 liver enzymes. It accounts for approximately 92% of tamoxifen metabolism but binds to the estrogen receptor with approximately 100-fold lower affinity than tamoxifen itself. N-Desmethyltamoxifen was crystallized in complex with the NavMs sodium channel, revealing binding at both inner and outer sites near the intracellular gate.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to NavMs voltage-gated sodium channel

N-Desmethyltamoxifen binds to both inner and outer sites near the intracellular gate of the NavMs sodium channel. The inner site binds near the ion-conducting pathway and involves hydrogen bond interactions with M222, K226, and E227. The binding site shares geometric features with the estrogen receptor binding pocket, suggesting a common mechanism for off-target sodium channel effects.
