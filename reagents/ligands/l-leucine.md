# L-Leucine

## Overview

L-Leucine is an essential branched-chain amino acid and the canonical substrate for the Na+/Cl--dependent neurotransmitter sodium symporter (NSS) family member LeuT from Aquifex aeolicus. It binds in an occluded site within the LeuT protein core, coordinated by main-chain atoms from unwound transmembrane helices TM1 and TM6. L-Leucine binds as a zwitterion in a completely dehydrated hydrophobic pocket, making it a key model substrate for understanding substrate recognition in NSS transporters.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to LeuT from Aquifex aeolicus

The alpha-amino group is coordinated by main-chain carbonyl oxygens from Ala22 (TM1), Phe253 and Thr254 (TM6), and a side-chain hydroxyl from Ser256 (TM6). The alpha-carboxy group interacts with a sodium ion, amide nitrogens from Leu25 and Gly26 (TM1), and a hydroxyl from Tyr108 (TM3). The aliphatic side chain is cradled in a hydrophobic pocket formed by Val104, Tyr108 (TM3), Phe253, Ser256, Phe259 (TM6), and Ser355, Ile359 (TM8). The binding site is completely dehydrated with leucine bound as a zwitterion.

- **Key residues**: Ala22 (TM1), Leu25/Gly26 (TM1), Tyr108 (TM3), Phe253/Thr254/Ser256 (TM6), Val104/Tyr108 (TM3), Phe259 (TM6), Ser355/Ile359 (TM8)
