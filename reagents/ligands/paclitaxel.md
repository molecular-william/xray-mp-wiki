# Paclitaxel

## Overview

Paclitaxel (Taxol) is a microtubule-stabilizing antimitotic agent widely used in cancer chemotherapy. It is a well-established substrate of the multidrug efflux transporter P-glycoprotein (P-gp). In the C. elegans P-gp study, paclitaxel was used as a cytotoxic substrate to demonstrate that P-gp expression confers cellular resistance to this drug. P-gp actively extrudes paclitaxel from cells, reducing its intracellular concentration and thereby mitigating its cytotoxic effects.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: (2R,3S)-N-benzoyl-3-phenylisoserine N-(2S,3S)-3-(tert-butoxycarbonylamino)-2-hydroxy-3-phenylpropanoate 5-benzoate 2'-ester
- **Class**: taxane diterpenoid antimitotic agent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### P-gp substrate assay

Paclitaxel is used to assess P-glycoprotein drug transport activity in cellular resistance assays. In the C. elegans P-gp study, cells expressing P-gp showed protection from paclitaxel-induced cytotoxicity after 48 hours of exposure, demonstrating functional drug efflux.
