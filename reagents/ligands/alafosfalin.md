# Alafosfalin

## Overview

Alafosfalin is an anti-bacterial peptidomimetic phosphonodipeptide that serves as a substrate for prokaryotic and eukaryotic proton-dependent oligopeptide transporters (POTs), including the human PEPT1 and PEPT2. It consists of an alanine-like amino acid linked via a phosphonate group to a methylphosphonic acid moiety. The compound has expected affinities in the millimolar range for POT transporters and is recognized as a substrate for both prokaryotic and eukaryotic POT transporters. Alafosfalin was used as a crystallization ligand for the first substrate-bound POT structure (PepT_So2) and demonstrated ligand-binding activity through thermal shift assays.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: N-(phosphonomethyl)alanine
- **Class**: peptidomimetic phosphonodipeptide

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization ligand for POT transporters

Alafosfalin at 50 mM was used in the crystallization of PepT_So2, a POT transporter from Shewanella oneidensis. The compound was co-incubated with the purified protein for 30 min at 4 C prior to crystallization, resulting in the first POT structure solved in complex with a substrate. The phosphonate moiety mimics the C-terminal carboxylate of a dipeptide, while the amine group mimics the N-terminus.


### Ligand-binding probe in thermal shift assays

Alafosfalin was used in precipitation-after-unfolding thermal shift assays to demonstrate ligand binding to detergent-solubilized PepT_So2. Incubation with 10 mM alafosfalin stabilized the transporter against heat denaturation, indicating specific ligand binding. The stabilization effect was abolished by mutations of key binding pocket residues (R25A, E402A), confirming the specificity of interaction.
