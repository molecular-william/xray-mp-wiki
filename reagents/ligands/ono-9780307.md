# ONO-9780307

## Overview

ONO-9780307 is a selective antagonist of the lysophosphatidic acid receptor 1 (LPA1). It was selected from a compound library based on SEC stability assay data and co-crystallized with LPA1-bRIL to obtain the first crystal structure of LPA1 in an antagonist-bound state. The compound features a branching aromatic indane ring and a dimethoxy phenyl ring connected by a methylene linker to a carboxylic acid head group. Its bioactive conformation requires torsional strain (9.6 kcal/mol above local minimum) to position the indane ring adjacent to the dimethoxy phenyl ring in the spherical LPA1 binding pocket.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: ONO-9780307
- **Class**: indane-carboxylic acid derivative

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### LPA1 antagonist for crystallography

ONO-9780307 was the first compound selected for LPA1 co-crystallization. It induced receptor stability sufficient for crystal growth in monoolein-cholesterol LCP. The resulting structure (PDB: 4Z34) at 3.0 A resolution revealed the LPA1 binding pocket architecture and enabled structure-based design of improved antagonists.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to LPA1 orthosteric binding pocket

Carboxylic acid forms polar and ionic bonds with His40, Lys39, Tyr34 (N-terminal capping helix/ECL0), Arg124(3.28), and Gln125(3.29). Indane ring adjacent to Gly274(6.51) on TM6. Dimethoxy phenyl ring positioned in spherical pocket.

- **Key residues**: His40, Lys39, Tyr34, Arg124, Gln125, Gly274
