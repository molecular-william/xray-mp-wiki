# Br-Memantine (Bromo-memantine)

## Overview

Br-memantine (3-bromo-5,7-dimethyladamantan-1-amine hydrochloride) is a brominated derivative of memantine, the Alzheimer's disease drug. It was synthesized by bromination of a hydroxyadamantyl amine precursor and used as a heavy-atom derivative for anomalous phasing in X-ray crystallography studies of the prokaryotic pentameric ligand-gated ion channel ELIC. Br-memantine retains the pore-blocking activity of memantine and binds at the extracellular entryway of the ELIC channel pore between the 13' and 16' residues of the M2 helix.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Anomalous phasing probe for ELIC crystallography

Br-memantine was used as a heavy-atom derivative for anomalous diffraction phasing in the X-ray crystal structure determination of ELIC F16'S in complex with a pore blocker. The bromine atom provided a strong anomalous signal for experimental phasing, enabling structure solution at 3.2 Å resolution. Br-memantine was also used independently for molecular replacement validation.


### Pore blocker of pentameric ligand-gated ion channels

Like memantine, Br-memantine binds at the extracellular entryway of the ELIC channel pore between residues 13'A and 16'S of the M2 helix. Crystal structures show five Br-memantine molecules per ELIC pentamer, one in each subunit interface of the pore.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Bromine provides strong anomalous signal for experimental phasing in X-ray crystallography
- Retains similar pharmacological properties to memantine as a pore blocker
- Synthetic route is straightforward from memantine precursor

### Disadvantages

- Custom synthesis required; not commercially available as widely as memantine
