# Telmisartan

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: Angiotensin receptor blocker (ARB), non-peptide antagonist
- **Scaffold**: Biphenyl with carboxylic acid and benz[imidazole](/reagents/additives/imidazole/) groups
- **Unique feature**: Lacks the tetrazole moiety common to most ARBs; instead uses a carboxylic acid

## Binding to AT1R

Telmisartan binds to the ligand-binding pocket of the human angiotensin II type 1 receptor (AT1R). Based on docking studies into the 2.9 Å SFX/XFEL structure (PDB: 4YAY):

- **Carboxylic acid**: Forms salt bridges with both Arg167ᴱᴰᴸ² (ECL2) and Lys199⁵·⁴² — a dual salt bridge arrangement
- **Benzimidazole**: Extensive hydrophobic interactions with the binding pocket
- **Biphenyl core**: Hydrophobic interactions with the pocket wall
- **Unique interaction**: Unlike most ARBs, telmisartan uses a carboxylic acid instead of a tetrazole for the key salt bridge

## Clinical Properties

- **Longest half-life**: ~24 hours among ARBs (allows flexible once-daily dosing)
- **Partial PPAR-γ agonist**: Unique among ARBs; may provide metabolic benefits (insulin sensitivity)
- **Indications**: Hypertension, cardiovascular risk reduction
- **Advantage**: Long half-life allows missed-dose tolerance; PPAR-γ activity may benefit diabetic patients
