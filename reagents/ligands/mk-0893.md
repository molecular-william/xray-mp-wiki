# MK-0893

## Overview

MK-0893 is a potent glucagon receptor (GCGR) antagonist discovered by Merck for the treatment
of type 2 diabetes. It is a small molecule with a pyrazole scaffold featuring a dichlorophenyl
head group and a propionic acid tail. The X-ray structure of GCGR in complex with MK-0893
revealed an unexpected allosteric binding site outside the seven transmembrane helical bundle.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Glucagon receptor (GCGR)

MK-0893 binds to an allosteric site between TM6 and TM7 of GCGR, extending into the lipid bilayer. Key interactions include hydrogen bonds between the ligand amide moiety and receptor residues Arg346, Lys349, Ser350 on TM6, and the ligand carboxyl group interacting with Asn404 on TM7. The dichlorophenyl head group engages in hydrophobic contacts with TM4 residues including Leu294, Phe310, Val313, Leu317, and Val318.

- **Key residues**: Arg346, Lys349, Ser350 (TM6), Asn404 (TM7), Leu294, Phe310, Val313, Leu317, Val318 (TM4)
