# Spiro-Adamantyl Amine

## Overview

Spiro-adamantyl amine is a member of the adamantyl-amine class of [Influenza A M2 Proton Channel](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/influenza-a-m2-proton-channel/) inhibitors. Its extended scaffold was designed to exclude more water from the channel pore and bind with the ammonium group deeper in the channel relative to [Amantadine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/amantadine/). It is an excellent inhibitor of wild-type M2 and also the V27A drug-resistant mutant, and has been shown to rescue mice from lethal influenza infection. Crystal structures of the V27A mutant bound to spiro-adamantyl amine (PDB 6NV1, 6OUG) reveal that the inhibitor adapts to the wider pore caused by the V27A mutation by shifting its adamantyl group upward by approximately 2 A, while the ammonium group remains positioned similarly to amantadine in the WT channel.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Influenza A antiviral therapy

Spiro-adamantyl amine is an adamantyl-amine class inhibitor of the M2 proton channel. Its longer scaffold compared to [Amantadine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/amantadine/) allows it to displace more water from the channel pore, specifically the Ala30 water layer, and engage the deeper Gly34 water layer with its ammonium group. The adamantane group projects one of its methylene groups along its pseudo-two-fold symmetry axis toward the top of the channel, differing from the orientation in [Amantadine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/amantadine/) and [Rimantadine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/rimantadine/) complexes.
In the V27A mutant, the inhibitor shifts binding position to occupy the widened N-terminal pore. Two ordered water layers (Ala30 carbonyl and Gly34 carbonyl) form between the inhibitor ammonium and His37, interrupting the proton-conducting water network while accommodating the wider channel. The dual-position binding mechanism enables inhibition of both WT and V27A channels.
