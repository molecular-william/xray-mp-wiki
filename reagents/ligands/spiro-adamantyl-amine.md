# Spiro-Adamantyl Amine

## Overview

Spiro-adamantyl amine is a member of the adamantyl-amine class of influenza A M2 proton channel inhibitors. Its extended scaffold was designed to exclude more water from the channel pore and bind with the ammonium group deeper in the channel relative to amantadine. It is an excellent inhibitor of wild-type M2 and also the V27A drug-resistant mutant, and has been shown to rescue mice from lethal influenza infection.


## Properties

- **Class**: adamantyl-amine

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Influenza A antiviral therapy

Spiro-adamantyl amine is an adamantyl-amine class inhibitor of the M2 proton channel. Its longer scaffold compared to amantadine allows it to displace more water from the channel pore, specifically the Ala30 water layer, and engage the deeper Gly34 water layer with its ammonium group. The adamantane group projects one of its methylene groups along its pseudo-two-fold symmetry axis toward the top of the channel, differing from the orientation in amantadine and rimantadine complexes.
