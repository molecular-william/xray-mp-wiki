# Desflurane

## Overview

Desflurane (1,2,2,2-tetrafluoroethyl difluoromethyl ether) is a volatile general anesthetic used for maintenance of anesthesia. It is a smaller and more flexible molecule than propofol. X-ray crystallography revealed desflurane binds to the same intra-subunit cavity in the upper transmembrane domain of GLIC as propofol, but binds deeper inside the cavity.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to GLIC (pentameric ligand-gated ion channel)

Van der Waals interactions with residues from M1, M2, M3, M4 helices; binds deeper inside the cavity than propofol; B-factor 121 A^2

- **Key residues**: T255, I201, I202, M205, L206, Y254, I258, I259, F303, N307, Y119
