# Rolofylline

## Overview

Rolofylline is a selective adenosine A1 receptor antagonist with a 3-nor-adamantyl group. It was used in docking studies into the A1AR crystal structure. Rolofylline was previously developed as a diuretic for patients with acute heart failure, though clinical trials were halted due to off-target stroke and on-target seizures.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Docking study into A1AR binding pocket

Rolofylline was docked into the A1AR crystal structure and found to engage the hydrophobic pocket made up by M177(5.35), L253(6.54), T257(6.57), and T270(7.35), similar to other selective A1AR antagonists like DPCPX and PSB36.
