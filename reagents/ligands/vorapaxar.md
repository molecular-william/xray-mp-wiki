# Vorapaxar

## Overview

Vorapaxar is a functionally irreversible small-molecule antagonist of the human protease-activated receptor 1 (PAR1), a thrombin receptor that mediates platelet aggregation and thrombosis. Vorapaxar binds in the orthosteric ligand-binding pocket of PAR1 with high affinity and slow off-rate, resulting in prolonged receptor blockade. The crystal structure of the PAR1-vorapaxar complex (PDB: 4MB0) revealed that vorapaxar forms strong interactions with four conserved aromatic residues (Y183, F271, Y337, Y353) within the transmembrane binding pocket, and that ECL2 forms a structural lid over the ligand. Vorapaxar shows high selectivity for human PAR1 over PAR2, PAR4, and mouse PAR1.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human PAR1

Vorapaxar binds in the closed orthosteric pocket of PAR1 with strong interactions with four aromatic residues: Y183^3.33, F271^5.39, Y337^6.59, and Y353^7.35. The binding pocket is closed with only small openings to the extracellular solvent and lipid bilayer. ECL2 forms a lid over the vorapaxar binding pocket, stabilized by hydrogen bonds between H255 and N242, Y353^7.35, and between D256 and Y95 in the amino terminus. A crystallographic sodium ion is present near D148^2.50.

- **Pocket volume**: closed pocket
