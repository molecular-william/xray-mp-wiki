# AM841

## Overview

AM841 is a synthetic THC-like cannabinoid agonist with high affinity for the CB1 receptor. It was used to determine the cryo-EM structure of the CB1-Gi-scFv16 complex (PDB 6KPG, 3.0 A resolution). AM841 has a Ki of 0.51 nM for CB1 WT. The CB1-AM841 Gi complex was constituted in vitro from purified protein components, unlike the CB2-Gi complex which was co-expressed in vivo. AM841-bound CB1 crystal structures (from Hua et al., 2017) show CB1 in an active-like state with "twin toggle switch" conformational changes.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to CB1 (CNR1)

AM841 binds in the orthosteric pocket of CB1. The binding pose of AM841 in CB1 is superimposable with AM12033 in CB2, reflecting the high structural similarity of agonist-binding pockets between CB1 and CB2. In the CB1-Gi complex, AM841-bound CB1 shows the "twin toggle switch" conformational changes (Phe3.36 and Trp6.48) characteristic of CB1 activation. The agonist-bound CB1 crystal structure is close to the active state, with DRY and NPxxY motifs showing similar rearrangements as in the Gi-coupled state.

- **Pocket volume**: not reported
- **Key residues**: Not specifically reported for AM841; orthosteric pocket residues conserved with CB2
