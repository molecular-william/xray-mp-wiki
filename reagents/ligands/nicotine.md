# Nicotine

## Overview

Nicotine is a naturally occurring alkaloid produced in Nicotiana species as a natural pesticide. It is a secondary specialized metabolic compound in Solanaceae plants. In NtMATE2, nicotine serves as the physiological substrate transported from the cytosol into the vacuolar lumen. The molecule has a positive charge and is recognized by the negatively charged C-lobe cavity of the transporter.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: (S)-3-(1-methyl-2-pyrrolidinyl)pyridine
- **Chemical formula**: C10H14N2
- **Molecular weight**: 162.23 g/mol
- **Class**: alkaloid

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Physiological substrate for NtMATE2

Nicotine is transported from the cytosol into the vacuolar lumen by NtMATE2 in exchange for protons. In crystallization trials, 2.5 mM nicotine was added to purified NtMATE2 and incubated at 4 C for 10 min before LCP reconstitution. An elongated electron density near E285/TM7 and N316/TM8 in Mol-B may represent a partially occupied nicotine molecule in the substrate-binding pocket.


### Co-crystallization ligand for human alpha4beta2 nicotinic receptor

Nicotine was used as a co-crystallization ligand for the human alpha4beta2 nicotinic acetylcholine receptor (2α:3β stoichiometry). Nicotine binds at the two alpha-beta interfaces in the extracellular domain, almost fully buried from solvent. The ligand was positioned based on strong omit electron density (6.8-8.0 sigma) and comparison with the high-resolution structure of the acetylcholine-binding protein in complex with nicotine. Nicotine was also added at 0.1 mM during baculovirus expression to promote the 2α:3β stoichiometry.
