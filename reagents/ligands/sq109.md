# SQ109

## Overview

SQ109 is a synthetic diamine compound that targets MmpL3, a mycobacterial membrane transporter essential for mycolic acid biosynthesis. It is in Phase 2b-3 clinical trials as an anti-tuberculosis agent and has shown excellent activity against all forms of *M. tuberculosis*, including drug-resistant clinical strains.

## Properties

- **Chemical class**: Synthetic diamine
- **Clinical status**: Phase 2b-3 clinical trials for TB
- **Target**: MmpL3 (Mycobacterial membrane protein Large 3)
- **K_d for M. smegmatis MmpL3**: 1.65 µM (microscale thermophoresis)
- **Mutation rate**: Very low in vitro bacterial mutation rate → slow resistance development
- **Spectrum**: Active against *Mtb*, *Helicobacter pylori*, and *Candida albicans*

## Binding Mode in MmpL3

SQ109 binds in an extended conformation at the center of the TMH bundle of MmpL3, occupying the proton-transportat[[ion-channels]]:
- **Geranyl tail**: Hydrophobic subsite (Ile249, Ile319, Ala637, Val638, Ser301, Ile253, Ile297, Gly641, Leu642, Leu686)
- **Adamantine group**: Held by "V"-shaped Phe260-Phe649
- **Amino groups**: Interact with Asp645
- **Pocket volume**: ~282 Å³
- **Effect**: Disrupts both Asp-Tyr pairs (Asp256-Tyr646 and Asp645-Tyr257), blocking proton relay

## Clinical Significance

- Excellent activity against all forms of *Mtb*, including drug-resistant strains
- Synergistic effects in combination with other anti-TB drugs
- Low mutation rate for resistance development
- Direct target confirmed by MST and overexpression reversal assays
