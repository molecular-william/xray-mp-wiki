# SQ109

## Overview

SQ109 (N-[1-(2,6-dioxopiperidin-3-yl)-1-(4-fluorophenyl)ethyl]-N-(2-(piperidin-1-yl)ethyl)propane-1,3-diamine) is a synthetic diamine compound that targets MmpL3, an essential mycobacterial membrane transporter. It is in Phase 2b-3 clinical trials as an anti-tuberculosis agent with excellent activity against all forms of M. tuberculosis, including drug-resistant clinical strains. The in vitro bacterial mutation rate for SQ109 is very low (>10^-11), suggesting slow development of resistance.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to MmpL3 (Mycobacterium smegmatis)

SQ109 binds deeply embedded in the center of the TMH bundle, surrounded by TMH IV, V, VI, X, XI, and XII. The geranyl tail makes favorable interactions with a hydrophobic subsite composed of Ile249, Ile319, Ala637, Val638, Ser301 (layer 1) and Ile253, Ile297, Gly641, Leu642, Leu686 (layer 2). The geranyl tail leans against the groove between TMH V and TMH X. Asp645 re-orients to interact with the amino groups of SQ109. Ser293 hydroxyl rotates to form hydrogen bonds with Asp645 and Tyr257. Phe260 and Phe649 form a V-shaped structure holding the adamantine group.

- **Pocket volume**: 282 A^3
- **Key residues**: Ile249, Ile253, Ser293, Ile297, Leu642, Gly641, Ile319, Ala637, Val638, Leu686, Asp645, Phe260, Phe649, Tyr257
