# Phenindione

## Overview

Phenindione is a vitamin K antagonist belonging to the 1,3-indandione class, used as an oral anticoagulant primarily in France (as fluindione). It was co-crystallized with HsVKOR (PDB 6WV6), revealing that its 1,3-diketone groups form hydrogen bonds with Asn80 and Tyr139, closely resembling the 2-ketone-4-hydroxyl of warfarin in its keto-enol tautomer form. This demonstrates that the indandione and coumarin pharmacophores share the same binding interactions in the VKOR active site.
