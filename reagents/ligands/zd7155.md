# ZD7155

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: Angiotensin receptor blocker (ARB), non-peptide antagonist
- **Scaffold**: Biphenyl-tetrazole with naphthyridin-2-one moiety
- **Relation**: Precursor to the anti-hypertensive drug candesartan
- **Potency**: More potent and longer-lasting than losartan (first clinically used ARB)

## Binding to AT1R

ZD7155 binds to the ligand-binding pocket of the human angiotensin II type 1 receptor (AT1R) with high affinity. Key interactions revealed by the 2.9 Å SFX/XFEL structure (PDB: 4YAY):

- **Arg167ᴱᴰᴸ² (ECL2)**: The positively charged guanidine group forms an extensive interaction network with both the acidic tetrazole and naphthyridin-2-one moieties of ZD7155. Mutation to alanine abolishes both peptide and non-peptide ligand binding. The Arg167Lys mutant retains 2–3 fold binding affinity.
- **Tyr35¹·³⁹**: Forms a hydrogen bond with the naphthyridin-2-one moiety. Tyr35Ala mutation abolishes binding.
- **Trp84²·⁶⁰**: Forms a π–π interaction with the naphthyridin-2-one moiety. Trp84Ala mutation abolishes binding.
- **Hydrophobic contacts**: Ile31¹·³⁵, Phe77²·⁵³, Val108³·³², Leu112³·³⁶, Ile288⁷·³⁹, Tyr292⁷·⁴³

## Unique Features

- **Arg167ᴱᴰᴸ²** is a unique residue in AT1R compared to other structurally similar peptide GPCRs (chemokine, [kappa-opioid-receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/kappa-opioid-receptor/) receptors)
- This residue plays an essential role in determining AT1R ligand-binding affinity and selectivity
- The tetrazole moiety (or other acidic isostere in the ortho position of the biphenyl group) is the most common scaffold among ARBs

## Relation to Other ARBs

ZD7155 shares the biphenyl-tetrazole scaffold with clinically used ARBs:
- **Losartan**: Lower affinity; sub-optimal hydrogen bonding with Tyr35; carboxyl group interacts with Cys180ᴱᴰᴸ²
- **Candesartan**: Insurmountable inverse agonist; tetrazole + carboxylic group of benz[imidazole](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/imidazole/) form dual salt bridges with Arg167ᴱᴰᴸ²; additional salt bridge with Lys199⁵·⁴²
- **Telmisartan**: Lacks tetrazole; carboxylic group forms salt bridges with Arg167ᴱᴰᴸ² and Lys199⁵·⁴²
- **Valsartan**, **Irbesartan**, **Eprosartan**, **Olmesartan**, **Azilsartan**: All dock into the same pocket with distinct interaction patterns

## Use in Structural Biology

- Used to capture the inactive-state conformation of AT1R (PDB: 4YAY, 2.9 Å)
- First AT1R structure solved by serial femtosecond crystallography (SFX) at an X-ray free-electron laser (XFEL)
- Enabled docking studies of all clinically used ARBs into the AT1R structure
