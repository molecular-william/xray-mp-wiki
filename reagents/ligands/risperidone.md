# Risperidone

## Overview

Risperidone is an atypical antipsychotic drug with a benzisoxazole scaffold. It is a widely prescribed medication for schizophrenia and other psychotic disorders. Risperidone targets the D2 [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) receptor ([DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/)) as its primary receptor target, and also binds to [Serotonin (5-Hydroxytryptamine, 5-HT)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/serotonin/) 5-HT2A, alpha-adrenergic, and histamine H1 receptors. Crystal structures of DRD2-risperidone (PDB: 6C38) and 5-HT2A R-risperidone (PDB: 6A93, 3.0 A) have been determined, revealing distinct binding modes at [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) and [Serotonin (5-Hydroxytryptamine, 5-HT)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/serotonin/) receptors.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to D2 [Dopamine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/dopamine/) Receptor ([DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/))

Risperidone binds in the orthosteric pocket of [DRD2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/drd2/) with the benzisoxazole moiety extending into a deep hydrophobic subpocket below the orthosteric site, interacting with Cys118^3.36, Thr119^3.37, Ser197^5.46, Phe198^5.47, Phe382^6.44, Phe390^6.52, and Trp386^6.48. The tetrahydropyridopyrimidinone moiety is enclosed in a hydrophobic pocket above the orthosteric site. Asp114^3.32 forms a salt bridge with risperidone tertiary amine.

- **Pocket volume**: Two distinct pockets: (1) deep hydrophobic subpocket below orthosteric site formed by helices III, V, VI; (2) hydrophobic pocket above orthosteric site for tetrahydropyridopyrimidinone moiety.

- **Key residues**: Asp114^3.32, Cys118^3.36, Thr119^3.37, Ser197^5.46, Phe198^5.47, Phe382^6.44, Phe390^6.52, Trp386^6.48
