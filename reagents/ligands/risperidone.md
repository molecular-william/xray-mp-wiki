# Risperidone

## Overview

Risperidone is an atypical antipsychotic drug with a benzisoxazole scaffold. It is a widely prescribed medication for schizophrenia and other psychotic disorders. Risperidone targets the D2 dopamine receptor (DRD2) as its primary receptor target, and also binds to serotonin 5-HT2A, alpha-adrenergic, and histamine H1 receptors. The crystal structure of DRD2 in complex with risperidone (PDB: 6C38) revealed a unique binding mode distinct from the substituted benzamides eticlopride (DRD3) and nemonapride (DRD4).

## Binding Mode

### Binding to D2 Dopamine Receptor (DRD2)

Risperidone binds in the orthosteric pocket of DRD2 with the benzisoxazole moiety extending into a deep hydrophobic subpocket below the orthosteric site, interacting with Cys118^3.36, Thr119^3.37, Ser197^5.46, Phe198^5.47, Phe382^6.44, Phe390^6.52, and Trp386^6.48. The tetrahydropyridopyrimidinone moiety is enclosed in a hydrophobic pocket above the orthosteric site. Asp114^3.32 forms a salt bridge with risperidone tertiary amine.

- **Pocket volume**: Two distinct pockets: (1) deep hydrophobic subpocket below orthosteric site formed by helices III, V, VI; (2) hydrophobic pocket above orthosteric site for tetrahydropyridopyrimidinone moiety.

- **Key residues**: Asp114^3.32, Cys118^3.36, Thr119^3.37, Ser197^5.46, Phe198^5.47, Phe382^6.44, Phe390^6.52, Trp386^6.48
