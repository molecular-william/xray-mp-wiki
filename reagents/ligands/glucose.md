# Glucose

## Overview

A simple hexose sugar (C6H12O6) that serves as a primary substrate for the SWEET family of sugar transporters. In membrane protein structural biology, glucose appears primarily as a substrate bound to sugar transporters.

## Role in SemiSWEET Crystallography

- **Substrate for LbSemiSWEET** (Latorraca et al., 2017)
- Bound in the outward-open conformation of the D57A mutant (2.8 Å)
- Glucose density clearly visible in the substrate-binding pocket, fitting the transporter
- Coordinates via Trp48 (pi-stacking), Asn64 (H-bond), and Thr13 (H-bond)
- The D57A mutant exhibits ~2x higher glucose affinity (Km ~70 µM) than wild-type (~160 µM)
- Crystal growth depended on the presence of glucose
