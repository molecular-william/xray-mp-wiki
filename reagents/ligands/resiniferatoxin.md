# Resiniferatoxin (RTx)

## Overview

Resiniferatoxin (RTx) is an ultrapotent vanilloid toxin derived from the latex of the cactus-like plant Euphorbia resinifera. RTx is a capsaicin analog that acts as a potent agonist of TRPV1 and, when engineered into [TRPV2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/trpv2/) via quadruple mutation, activates [TRPV2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/trpv2/) channels. RTx binds at the inner leaflet of the membrane near the S4-S5 linker, pushing down on the base of S5 and inducing conformational changes that lead to opening of the selectivity filter gate. Due to its extreme potency (up to 1000-fold more potent than capsaicin), RTx is a valuable tool for studying TRP channel gating and has been extensively used in structural studies of TRPV1.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### TRPV2 selectivity filter gate opening

RTx binding to the quadruple mutant TRPV2_QM (F470S, L505M, L508T, Q528E)
induces a two-fold symmetric opening of the selectivity filter gate, enabling
permeation of large organic cations such as YO-PRO-1 (376 Da). The structure
of miTRPV2_QM in complex with RTx and Ca²⁺ was determined to 3.1 Å (PDB 6BWJ),
revealing that RTx pushes down on the base of S5 via the π-hinge_S4-S5,
forcing subunit rotation and SF gate widening. Each of the four RTx binding
sites in the tetramer is occupied, but differential interactions in adjacent
subunits produce distinct subunit conformations.


### TRPV1 structural studies

RTx has been extensively used in structural studies of TRPV1. The structure
of TRPV1 in complex with both DkTx (double-knot toxin) and RTx (PDB 5IRX)
revealed the mechanism of dual-gate opening: DkTx opens the selectivity filter
gate while RTx opens the common gate at the S6 bundle crossing. RTx binds in
a pocket formed by S3, S4b, the S4-S5 linker, S5, and S6 of TRPV1.
