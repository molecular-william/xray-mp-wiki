# Dopamine

## Overview

Dopamine is the endogenous neurotransmitter substrate of the dopamine transporter (DAT), a eukaryotic member of the neurotransmitter/sodium symporter (NSS) family. It mediates Na+/Cl--coupled reuptake of dopamine from the synaptic cleft, terminating dopaminergic signaling at chemical synapses.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Drosophila melanogaster dopamine transporter (dDAT)

Primary amine interacts with Asp46 (TM1b) at 3 A distance. Catechol group occupies subsite B cavity formed by Ala117, Val120, Asp121, Tyr124, Ser422, and Phe325. Catechol ring forms hydrogen bonds with Asp121 carboxylate. Water molecule observed 3.1 A from amine group, forming hydrogen bond network to sodium ion at site 1.

- **Pocket volume**: 311.9 A^2 total surface area, 279.1 A^2 buried (89.5%)
- **Key residues**: Asp46, Ala117, Val120, Asp121, Tyr124, Ser422, Phe325, Phe319
