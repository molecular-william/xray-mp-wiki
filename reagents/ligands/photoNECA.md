# PhotoNECA (blue) — Photoswitchable [a2a-adenosine-receptor](/proteins/a2a-adenosine-receptor/) Ligand

## Overview

PhotoNECA (blue) is a novel photoresponsive ligand designed for the adenosine A2A receptor ([a2a-adenosine-receptor](/proteins/a2a-adenosine-receptor/)). It contains NECA (5'-N-ethylcarboxamidoadenosine) as the agonist moiety linked to a photoswitchable group that undergoes trans-to-cis isomerization upon 365 nm irradiation.

## Properties

- **Trans-isomer**: EC50 = 27 ± 2.4 nM at A2AAR (agonist)
- **Cis-isomer**: EC50 > 1000 nM at A2AAR (inactive)
- **Photoconversion**: 75% trans-to-cis after 365 nm LED irradiation
- **Stability**: Cis-isomer half-life = 32 hours at 37°C
- **Selectivity**: High [a2a-adenosine-receptor](/proteins/a2a-adenosine-receptor/) selectivity (>1000x vs A1R)

## Binding Mode

- [neca](/reagents/ligands/neca/) moiety binds in orthosteric site (similar to [neca](/reagents/ligands/neca/)-bound [a2a-adenosine-receptor](/proteins/a2a-adenosine-receptor/), PDB 2YDV)
- Photoswitching moiety occupies a hydrophobic pocket (Ser67^2.65, Leu167^ECL2, Leu267^7.32, Tyr271^7.36)
- Cis-isomer cannot maintain stable binding — conformational clash
- Hydrophobic pocket in [a2a-adenosine-receptor](/proteins/a2a-adenosine-receptor/) vs. polar pocket in A1R explains subtype selectivity
