# D-myo-Inositol-3-Phosphate

## Overview

D-myo-inositol-3-phosphate (ino-P) is a soluble substrate of
[Phosphatidylinositol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylinositol/) phosphate synthase PgsA1 in Mycobacterium
tuberculosis. Together with CDP-diacylglycerol (CDP-[DAG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/dag/)), it is
converted into [Phosphatidylinositol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylinositol/) phosphate (PIP) and CMP in a
reaction catalyzed by Class I CDP-alcohol phosphotransferases. Ino-P
is the second alcohol substrate that performs nucleophilic attack on
the β-phosphorus of CDP-[DAG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/dag/) during catalysis. Its binding site was
identified through a combination of crystallography ([Citrate Buffer (Sodium Citrate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/citrate/) and sulfate
binding sites), [Molecular Docking](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/molecular-docking/), and mutagenesis studies. The binding
pocket is a conserved positively charged cleft formed by R94, R152,
R155, R195, K135, and R137 from the second protomer, with R94 being
critical for ino-P phosphate coordination. Ino-P binding is promoted
by prior CDP-[DAG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/dag/) binding through protein conformational changes, and
binding of ino-P induces a [Carboxylate Shift](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/structural-mechanisms/carboxylate-shift/) of D93 that primes the
active site for catalysis.
