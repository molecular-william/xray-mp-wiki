# MPEP (2-Methyl-6-(phenylethynyl)pyridine)

## Overview

MPEP (2-methyl-6-(phenylethynyl)pyridine) is a potent, selective negative allosteric modulator (NAM) of the metabotropic glutamate receptor subtype 5 (mGlu5). It was one of the first identified mGlu5 NAMs and has been extensively used as a reference compound in mGlu5 pharmacology research. The [3H]-[M-MPEP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/m-mpep/) radioligand version ([3H]-2-[(3-methoxyphenyl)ethynyl]-6-methylpyridine) is commonly used for binding assays and fragment screening. MPEP binds to the MPEP site in the transmembrane domain of mGlu5, one of two allosteric sites identified in this receptor.
