# 7-Benzylidenenaltrexone (BNTX)

## Overview

7-Benzylidenenaltrexone (BNTX) is a selective delta-opioid receptor (δ-OR) antagonist containing a cyclopentene functional group. It is one of the classical δ-OR antagonists that transforms into a potent beta-arrestin-biased agonist when the allosteric sodium site of the δ-OR is mutated, revealing the concept of efficacy switches.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 7-[(R)-(1R,5R,6R)-6-(3,4-dichlorophenyl)-3-methyl-2-methylenebicyclo[2.2.1]hept-5-en-7-yl]-N,N-diethyl-6,7-benzomorphan-3-methanamine
- **Class**: benzomorphan opioid antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Delta-opioid receptor selective antagonist

BNTX is a selective δ-OR antagonist used in pharmacological studies. It contains a cyclopentene functional group shared with naltrindole and naltriben, which contributes to its functional switching behavior in sodium site mutants.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- High subtype selectivity for δ-OR
- Cyclopentene structural motif useful for studying efficacy switches

### Disadvantages

- Functional switching in sodium site mutants limits use as pure antagonist
