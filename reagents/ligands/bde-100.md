# BDE-100 (2,2',4,4'-Tetrabromodiphenyl Ether)

## Overview

BDE-100 (2,2',4,4'-tetrabromodiphenyl ether) is a brominated flame retardant
and environmental pollutant belonging to the polybrominated diphenyl ether (PBDE)
class. It serves as a potent molecular probe for studying drug-binding site plasticity
in P-glycoprotein (Pgp) by X-ray crystallography. BDE-100 has a simplified structure
lacking rotatable bonds and contains five bromine atoms that allow sensitive anomalous
difference Fourier electron-density localization by X-ray crystallography. It is
a potent inhibitor of verapamil-stimulated ATPase activity of Pgp.
