# Cmpd-15PA (Compound-15PA)

## Overview

Cmpd-15PA is a negative allosteric modulator of the beta2-adrenergic receptor (beta2 AR), derived from compound-15 (Cmpd-15) by modification with a polyethylene glycol-carboxylic acid derivative (PEG3-COOH). Cmpd-15 was isolated from a DNA-encoded small-molecule library and represents the first allosteric beta-blocker. Cmpd-15PA exhibits negative cooperativity with agonists and positive cooperativity with inverse agonists. The compound is a racemic mixture with respect to the chiral carbon connecting the cyclohexyl and phenyl rings, and the R isomer was determined to fit better into the electron density of the crystal structure.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Beta2-Adrenergic Receptor (beta2 AR)

Cmpd-15PA binds to a pocket formed primarily by the cytoplasmic ends of transmembrane segments 1, 2, 6 and 7, as well as intracellular loop 1 and helix 8. The cyclohexyl and phenyl rings are stabilized in a hydrophobic pocket formed by Val54^1.53 and Ile58^1.57 at the end of TM1, Leu64^ICL1 in ICL1, Ile72^2.43 in TM2, Leu275^6.37 in TM6, Tyr326^7.53 from the conserved NPxxY sequence in TM7, and Phe332^8.50 in H8. Cmpd-15PA stabilizes TM6 in the inactive state through direct polar interactions with Thr274^6.36 and non-polar interactions with Ala271^6.33 and Leu275^6.37. The bromophenyl ring disrupts a salt bridge between Arg63^ICL1 and Asp331^8.49, enabling formation of a new salt bridge between Lys267^6.29 at the end of TM6 and Asp331^8.49 in H8, which traps Cmpd-15PA in its binding pocket.

- **Key residues**: Val54, Ile58, Leu64, Ile72, Gln69, Thr274, Ala271, Leu275, Tyr326, Phe332, Lys267, Asp331
