# Tetraethylammonium (TEA)

## Overview

Tetraethylammonium (TEA) is a quaternary ammonium compound that acts as a potent open channel blocker of cation-selective ion channels, including pentameric ligand-gated ion channels (pLGICs) such as [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/). TEA binds in the transmembrane pore of the channel in its open state, physically occluding ion conduction. It is a widely used pharmacological tool for studying ion channel gating and pore architecture.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: tetraethylammonium
- **Molecular weight**: 130.25 g/mol
- **Class**: quaternary ammonium compound (ion channel blocker)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Open channel blocker for pLGIC functional studies

TEA was used to study open channel block in [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) by two-electrode voltage clamp. The IC50 for TEA block was determined at various membrane potentials, showing voltage-dependent block consistent with pore binding. The fraction of the electric field crossed by TEA (z delta = 0.24) indicated binding approximately halfway across the membrane.
