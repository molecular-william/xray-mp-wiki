# Valinomycin

## Overview

Valinomycin is a macyclic ionophore antibiotic that selectively transports potassium ions (K+) across biological membranes. It is widely used in ion channel functional assays to normalize ion flux measurements by providing a known maximum flux rate, allowing quantification of channel activity.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: valinomycin
- **Class**: ionophore-antibiotic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Flux assay normalization

Valinomycin (1 ug/mL final concentration) was added to NaK channel reconstituted liposomes for 2 min before taking the final reading in the 86Rb flux assay. It serves as a positive control that maximizes Rb+ (and K+) influx through the liposomal membrane, enabling normalization of channel-mediated flux rates.
