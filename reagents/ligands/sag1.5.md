# SAG1.5

## Overview

SAG1.5 (3-chloro-4,7-difluoro-N-[trans-4-(methylamino)cyclohexyl]-N-[[3-(4-pyridinyl)phenyl]methyl]-1-benzothiophene-2-carboxamide) is a potent agonist of the human Smoothened (SMO) receptor. It induces cilia translocation of SMO and GLI transcription factor activation. SAG1.5 binds near the extracellular entrance of the SMO binding pocket and induces a conformational rearrangement of key binding pocket residues (R400, D473, E518), which may serve as an activation trigger.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 3-chloro-4,7-difluoro-N-[trans-4-(methylamino)cyclohexyl]-N-[[3-(4-pyridinyl)phenyl]methyl]-1-benzothiophene-2-carboxamide
- **Class**: benzothiophene-based SMO agonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### SMO agonism in structural studies

SAG1.5 was used to crystallize human SMO (PDB: 4QIN, 2.6 A, C2). The ligand forms a bulky aromatic system that packs tightly into the pocket surrounded by the ECD linker domain and ECLs. The positively charged amino group forms an ionic interaction with D473(6.54f), causing E518(7.38f) to move away and break its interaction with D473. R400(5.43f) moves up to form a hydrogen bond with Q477(ECL3). On the intracellular side, the hydrogen bond between Y262(ICL1) and H361(4.46f) is broken, associated with inward movement of P263(ICL1) contacting W535(7.55f).
