# TC114 (SMO Ligand)

## Overview

TC114 is a designed small-molecule ligand for the human smoothened (SMO) receptor, derived from LY2940680 with a 4-nitro group substitution on the benzoyl moiety. It was specifically engineered to stabilize the multi-domain SMO construct for crystallographic studies by forming a strong electrostatic interaction with K395 on extracellular loop 2.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Stabilization of multi-domain SMO for XFEL crystallography

TC114 was used as the stabilizing ligand for the SMO-FLA fusion construct in the XFEL structure determination (PDB: 5V56, 2.9 Å). The 4-nitro substitution enables a 3.4 Å electrostatic interaction with the protonated epsilon-amine of K395, significantly reducing conformational heterogeneity of the extracellular loop 2 region compared to the parent compound LY2940680.


### Thermal stabilization screening via CPM assay

TC114 showed the highest thermal melting temperature (Tm) among a series of LY2940680 analogues screened using the CPM thermal shift assay, making it the optimal ligand for stabilizing the multi-domain SMO receptor for structural studies.
