# S1I8 Angiotensin II Analog

## Overview

S1I8 (Sarcosine¹, Isoleucine⁸-Angiotensin II) is a synthetic octapeptide analog of angiotensin II (AngII) with partial agonist activity at the AT1R. It differs from native AngII (Asp-Arg-Val-Tyr-Ile-His-Pro-Phe) by two substitutions:

- Position 1: Aspartate → Sarcosine (N-methylglycine, removes the free N-terminus)
- Position 8: Phenylalanine → Isoleucine (reduces hydrophobic bulk at the C-terminus)

## Functional Profile

S1I8 is a **partial agonist** for both Gq and β-arrestin-mediated signaling at the AT1R. The Isoleucine at position 8 is smaller than the Phenylalanine of native AngII, resulting in reduced efficacy. This makes S1I8 ideal for crystallization — it stabilizes an active-like conformation without triggering full receptor activation that could lead to conformational heterogeneity.

## Structural Role

In the AT1R active-state structure (PDB: 6DO1, Wingler et al., 2019), S1I8 binds in an extended conformation spanning from the solvent-exposed extracellular face to the deep orthosteric pocket. The C-terminal Isoleucine (I8) fits into a cavity shaped primarily by TM3, initiating the cascade of conformational changes that propagate to the intracellular G-protein binding site.

The I8 side chain is critical: it is slightly too small to fully engage Y292⁷·⁴³, which is why S1I8 acts as a partial rather than full agonist. Native AngII with F8 would induce additional disruption of the receptor core.

## Related Ligands

- **AngII** (Asp-Arg-Val-Tyr-Ile-His-Pro-Phe) — endogenous full agonist
- **TRV055** — AngII analog with first residue deleted (retains affinity for N-terminal BRIL fusion constructs)
- **ARBs** (losartan, olmesartan) — small-molecule antagonists with different binding mode
