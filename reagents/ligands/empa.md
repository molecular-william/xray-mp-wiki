# EMPA (N-ethyl-2-[(6-methoxy-pyridin-3-yl)-(toluene-2-sulfonyl)-amino]-N-pyridin-3-yl-methyl)

## Overview

EMPA (N-ethyl-2-[(6-methoxy-pyridin-3-yl)-(toluene-2-sulfonyl)-amino]-N-pyridin-3-yl-methyl) is a subtype-selective orexin 2 receptor antagonist (SORA) with high affinity for OX2R (Ki = 1.1 nM) and 900-fold selectivity over OX1R. It is a toluene-sulfonyl-pyridine derivative that acts as an orexin receptor antagonist for the treatment of insomnia. Unlike dual orexin receptor antagonists (DORAs) such as suvorexant, EMPA selectively targets OX2R. EMPA exhibits considerably faster dissociation kinetics from OX2R (k_off = faster than suvorexant's 0.012 min^-1) compared to DORAs. The crystal structure of EMPA bound to OX2R revealed no direct hydrogen bonds or salt bridges between the ligand and receptor; all contacts are van der Waals or pi interactions. Compared to lemborexant, EMPA binds through nonpolar interactions only without the hydrogen bonding to Gln134^3.32 or pi-stacking features.
