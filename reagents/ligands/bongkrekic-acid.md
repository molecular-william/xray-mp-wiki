# Bongkrekic Acid

## Overview

Bongkrekic acid (BKA) is a polyunsaturated methoxy tricarboxylic acid polyketide produced by *Burkholderia gladioli* pathological variant *cocovenenans*. It is a potent inhibitor of the mitochondrial ADP/ATP carrier (AAC) that locks the carrier in the matrix-open state (m-state).

## Properties

- **Chemical class**: Polyunsaturated methoxy tricarboxylic acid polyketide
- **Source**: *Burkholderia gladioli* pv. *cocovenenans*
- **Membrane permeability**: Membrane-permeable (unlike carboxyatractyloside)
- **Toxicity**: Highly toxic — over 2,000 cases of human fatality reported in Indonesia, China, and Mozambique since 1950, due to contamination of corn or coconut products (*tempe bongkrek*)

## Mechanism of Inhibition

BKA binds in the ADP/ATP-binding site at the bottom of the central cavity of the AAC. By occupying this site, it locks the carrier in the matrix-open conformation (m-state), preventing the conformational cycling required for ADP/ATP exchange.

## Use in Structural Biology

BKA was used to solve the first matrix-open state structure of the AAC:
- TtAac (from *Thermothelomyces thermophila*) + BKA + [nanobody](/reagents/antibodies/nanobody/) → 3.3 Å structure
- BKA occupies the substrate-binding site at the center of the membrane, providing a clear view of the m-state conformation
