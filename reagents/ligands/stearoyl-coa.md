# Stearoyl-CoA

## Overview

Stearoyl-coenzyme A (stearoyl-CoA) is a fatty acyl-CoA thioester consisting of an 18-carbon saturated fatty acid (stearic acid) linked to coenzyme A via a thioester bond. It serves as the primary substrate for stearoyl-CoA desaturase-1 (SCD1), which introduces a cis double bond at the delta-9 position to produce oleoyl-CoA. Stearoyl-CoA was found to be bound to hSCD1 during expression in Sf9 insect cells and remained associated throughout purification and crystallization, enabling the first structural view of a membrane di-iron desaturase in complex with its substrate.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for stearoyl-CoA desaturase-1 (SCD1)

Stearoyl-CoA is the primary substrate for SCD1, which catalyzes the delta-9 desaturation to form oleoyl-CoA. In the crystal structure of hSCD1 (PDB 4ZYO), stearoyl-CoA was found bound in the cytoplasmic cap domain with its CoA head group interacting with polar residues on the protein surface and its 18-carbon acyl chain extending into the internal hydrophobic tunnel toward the dimetal catalytic center. The substrate-bound structure revealed the determinants of substrate recognition and regioselectivity in the dehydrogenation reaction.
