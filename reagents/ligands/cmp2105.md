# Cmp2105

## Overview

Cmp2105 is a small molecule intracellular allosteric antagonist of the human
CC chemokine receptor 7 (CCR7). It contains a thiadiazole-dioxide core motif
with two amine-linked substituents. Cmp2105 binds to an intracellular allosteric
pocket in CCR7 located between TM1, TM2, TM3, TM6, and the TM7-H8 turn, with
the sulfonamide group interacting with a conserved patch of residues including
Tyr326, Gly330, Val331, Lys332, and Phe333. It exhibits a strong dose-dependent
stabilizing effect on CCR7 (up to 20.1 C thermal shift) and inhibits CCL19
binding with an IC50 of 35 nM. Cmp2105 stabilizes an inactive CCR7 conformation
that interferes with Gi protein and arrestin binding.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Intracellular allosteric antagonist of CCR7

Cmp2105 was co-crystallized with CCR7 (PDB 6QZH, 2.1 A resolution) and acts as an intracellular allosteric antagonist by binding near the Gi protein binding site in the TM7-H8 turn region.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human CCR7

- Sulfonamide group interacts with TM7-H8 turn residues (Tyr326, Gly330, Val331, Lys332, Phe333)
- Thiadiazole-dioxide core positioned by hydrogen bonds with Asp94(2.40)
- Hydrogen bonds to Thr91(2.37) and Thr93(2.39) in TM2
- Hydrophobic contacts with Val79(1.53), Thr82(1.56), and Phe86(1.60) in TM1
- Interactions with Arg154(3.50) of ERY motif and Tyr326(7.53) of NPxxY motif
