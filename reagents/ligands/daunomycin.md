# Daunomycin

## Overview

Daunomycin is an anthracycline anticancer drug that acts as a substrate for the heterodimeric ABC exporter TM287/288 from Thermotoga maritima. It is also known as adriamycin and is used clinically as a chemotherapy agent. Daunomycin intercalates into DNA and inhibits topoisomerase II. The TM287/288 transporter confers resistance to daunomycin by actively pumping it out of the cell, a mechanism shared with mammalian multidrug resistance ABC transporters such as P-glycoprotein.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for TM287/288 ABC transporter

Daunomycin is transported by TM287/288 from T. maritima. Expression of TM287/288 in L. lactis increased daunomycin resistance by a factor of 1.6 compared to cells expressing an inactive E517Q mutant. Daunomycin transport was measured using daunomycin resistance assays in L. lactis.
