# TRV026

## Overview

TRV026 is a beta-arrestin-biased peptide agonist of the angiotensin II type 1 receptor (AT1R). It is a synthetic octapeptide analog of angiotensin II (AngII) with modifications: N-methyl glycine at position 1 (sarcosine), tyrosine at position 4 (Y4), and a C-terminal amide group with the terminal residue 8 deleted. Unlike AngII which fully activates both Gq and beta-arrestin pathways, TRV026 is strongly biased toward beta-arrestin signaling with attenuated Gq activation. Crystal structures of AT1R bound to TRV026 (PDB 6OS2) were solved at 2.7-2.9 A resolution, revealing distinct active conformations compared to AngII-bound AT1R.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [Angiotensin II Type 1 Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/at1r/)

TRV026 binds to AT1R with the N terminus at the extracellular face and C terminus at the base of the ligand-binding pocket. The Y4 substitution (compared to AngII V5) and the lack of residue 8 produce distinct conformational changes, notably failing to induce the N111^3.35 flipping that is essential for Gq signaling.
