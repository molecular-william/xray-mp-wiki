# Imipramine

## Overview

Imipramine is a tricyclic antidepressant (TCA) of the dibenzazepine class. It was among the three TCAs co-crystallized with LeuT from Aquifex aeolicus to reveal the molecular basis of noncompetitive inhibition by tricyclic antidepressants. Imipramine is structurally related to clomipramine but lacks a chlorine atom, resulting in approximately eightfold lower inhibitory potency (IC50 ~2,090 uM for LeuT leucine transport).

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### LeuT TCA binding site characterization

Imipramine was co-crystallized with LeuT (leucine- and sodium-bound state). Unlike clomipramine, imipramine lacks the chlorine atom and cannot form a polar interaction with Gln34, which partly explains its lower potency. The EL4 residues are shifted upward by up to 1.4 A to accommodate the TCA. Imipramine also exhibited a second, weak binding site on the lipid-exposed face of the V formed between TM4 and TM5, near the intracellular surface.
