# Succinate (Succinic Acid)

## Overview

Succinate (succinic acid) is a small dicarboxylic acid and a crucial intermediate of the citric acid cycle (TCA cycle). Under physiological conditions, it is deprotonated to its anionic form (succinate). It serves as the endogenous ligand for the G-protein-coupled receptor SUCNR1 (GPR91). Succinate is normally located in mitochondria but can be released into the extracellular space under pathological conditions, where it functions as a danger signal and inflammatory mediator.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to SUCNR1 (GPR91)

Succinate binds to the orthosteric site of SUCNR1 in its cis-conformation. The two carboxylate groups form ionic interactions with key basic residues in the binding pocket, particularly Arg123 and Arg126, which are conserved in class A GPCRs. Succinate analogs that act as SUCNR1 agonists were shown to bind in the cis-conformation.
