# Sarafotoxin S6b

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: Endothelin-like peptide toxin
- **Source**: Venom of Atractaspididae family snakes (mole vipers)
- **Length**: 21 amino acids
- **Architecture**: Bicyclic structure with two disulfide bonds (C1–C15 and C3–C11)
- **Regions**: N-terminal region (residues 1–7), α-helical region (residues 8–17), C-terminal region (residues 18–21)

## Structure Comparison with Endothelins

Sarafotoxins share high structural and functional homology with endothelins (ET-1, ET-2, ET-3):
- 4 cysteines completely conserved (bicyclic architecture)
- 8 residues differ between S6b and ET-1/ET-3
- S6b superimposes well with ET-1 and ET-3 (RMSD for Cα = 0.39 and 0.40 Å)
- C-terminal region has conformational flexibility in solution, adopts fixed conformation upon receptor binding

## Subtypes

Four major isopeptides: S6a, S6b, S6c, S6d

| Sarafotoxin | Residue 9 | ETB affinity | ETA affinity |
|-------------|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| S6b | Lysine (K) | High | High |
| S6c | Glutamate (E) | High | 100–10,000× lower |
| [E9]S6b | Glutamate (E) | High (ETB-selective) | Low |

## Mechanism of Action

S6b stimulates both ETA and ETB endothelin receptors, causing:
1. **Vasoconstriction** (via ETA and smooth muscle ETB)
2. **Left ventricular dysfunction**
3. **Bronchoconstriction**
4. **Increased airway resistance**

## Binding to ETB Receptor

- K9 forms a salt bridge with D246ᴱᴰᴸ² in ECL2 of ETB, anchoring the α-helical region
- Extensive hydrophobic interactions with ECL2 residues (L252ᴱᴰᴸ², I254ᴱᴰᴸ² in ETB)
- The 9th residue determines subtype selectivity: K9 (S6b) forms favorable salt bridge with D246ᴱᴰᴸ² in ETB, while E9 (S6c) is repelled by the same residue
- In ETA, hydrophobic residues L252ᴱᴰᴸ² and I254ᴱᴰᴸ² are replaced with H236ᴱᴰᴸ² and T238ᴱᴰᴸ², reducing hydrophobic interactions

## Use in Structural Biology

- Used to capture active-state conformation of ETB receptor (PDB 6LRY, 3.0 Å)
- S6b-bound ETB shows 4 Å outward displacement of TM5 intracellular end, suggesting a more active conformation for G-protein coupling
- Molecular dynamics simulations based on S6b-bound structure revealed structural basis for ETB-selectivity of S6c
