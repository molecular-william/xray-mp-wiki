# Z-Endoxifen

## Overview

Z-Endoxifen is the Z-isomer of endoxifen (4-hydroxy-N-desmethyltamoxifen), the primary active metabolite of tamoxifen. It was tested for sodium channel inhibition alongside E-endoxifen, showing an IC50 of 2.3 uM against NavMs. Both endoxifen isomers were tested in the study to determine stereoselectivity of sodium channel binding.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to NavMs voltage-gated sodium channel

Z-Endoxifen binds to both inner and outer sites near the intracellular gate of the NavMs sodium channel, similar to the parent endoxifen compound. The inner site involves hydrogen bond interactions with M222, K226, and E227.
