# AM12033

## Overview

AM12033 is a synthetic cannabinoid agonist with high affinity for CB2 (Ki = 0.37 nM). It features a tricyclic tetrahydrocannabinol system with a long alkyl side chain. Used as the activating ligand for CB2-Gi complex cryo-EM and CB2 crystal structure determination.

## Chemical Structure

- Tricyclic tetrahydrocannabinol core with phenolic hydroxyl at C1
- Long alkyl chain extending from the core (7-methyl-1-octanenitrile side chain)
- Full IUPAC name: 7-[(6aR,10aR)-3-(7-bromo-2-methylheptan-2-yl)-1-hydroxy-6,6-dimethyl-6a,7,8,10,10a-hexahydro-9H-benzo[c]chromen-9-yl]-7-methyloctanenitrile (via synthetic intermediate 13)
- Synthesized in-house (Hua et al., 2020)

## Binding to CB2

- **Conformation**: L-shape in orthosteric binding pocket
- **Pi-pi interactions**: Phe183(ECL2), Phe281(7.35), Phe94(2.64)
- **H-bond**: Phenolic hydroxyl at C1 with Ser285(7.39)
- **Alkyl chain**: Hydrophobic interactions with TM3, TM5, TM6 residues
- **Binding pose**: Nearly identical in cryo-EM (2.9 Å) and crystal (3.2 Å) structures

## Use in Structural Biology

- **CB2-Gi cryo-EM**: Activating ligand in CB2-AM12033-Gi-scFv16 complex (PDB: 6KPF, 2.9 Å)
- **CB2 crystal structure**: Agonist-bound CB2 structure (PDB: 6KPC, 3.2 Å)
- **Positive control**: Non-selective agonist used in MD simulations to validate binding assays
