# AM12033

## Overview

AM12033 is a synthetic cannabinoid agonist with a tricyclic tetrahydrocannabinol core structure. It is a potent agonist at both CB1 and CB2 cannabinoid receptors, with Ki values of 0.51 nM for CB1 and 0.37 nM for CB2, making it nearly equipotent at both receptors. AM12033 was used to determine the crystal structure of CB2 (PDB 6KPC, 3.2 A) and the cryo-EM structure of the CB2-Gi complex (PDB 6KPF, 2.9 A). The compound adopts an L-shape conformation in the orthosteric binding pocket of CB2.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to CB2 (CNR2)

AM12033 adopts an L-shape conformation in the orthosteric binding pocket. The tricyclic tetrahydrocannabinol system forms pi-pi interactions with Phe183^ECL2, Phe281^7.35, and Phe94^2.64. The phenolic hydroxyl at C1 forms a hydrogen bond with Ser285^7.39. The alkyl chain extends into a long channel forming hydrophobic interactions with residues from TM3, TM5, and TM6. The agonist-bound CB2 crystal structure (6KPC) and cryo-EM structure (6KPF) show almost identical binding poses.

- **Pocket volume**: not reported
- **Key residues**: Phe183, Phe281, Phe94, Ser285, TM3, TM5, TM6 residues
