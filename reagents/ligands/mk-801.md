# MK-801 (Dizocilpine)

## Overview

MK-801 (dizocilpine) is a non-competitive, open-channel blocker of N-methyl-[D-Aspartic Acid (D-Aspartate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/d-aspartate/) (NMDA) receptors. It binds within the ion channel pore and can only access its binding site when the channel is open, making it a use-dependent blocker. MK-801 is widely used as a pharmacological tool to study NMDA receptor function and has been investigated for neurological and psychiatric applications. In the full-length GluN1-GluN2B NMDA receptor crystal structure, MK-801 was observed trapped in the central vestibule during crystallization.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: (+)-5-methyl-10,11-dihydro-5H-dibenzo[a,d]cyclohepten-5,10-imine maleate
- **Class**: Non-competitive NMDA receptor antagonist, open-channel blocker
