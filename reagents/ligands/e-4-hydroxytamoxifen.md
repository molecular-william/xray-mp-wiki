# E-4-Hydroxytamoxifen

## Overview

E-4-Hydroxytamoxifen is the E-isomer of 4-hydroxytamoxifen, a primary metabolite of tamoxifen. The E-isomer is the predominant circulating form of 4-hydroxytamoxifen in vivo. E-4-Hydroxytamoxifen was tested for sodium channel inhibition, showing an IC50 of 2.3 uM against NavMs, which is approximately 10-fold less potent than the Z-isomer.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to NavMs voltage-gated sodium channel

E-4-Hydroxytamoxifen binds to both inner and outer sites near the intracellular gate of the NavMs sodium channel, in a similar manner to the Z-isomer and other tamoxifen metabolites. The inner site involves hydrogen bond interactions with M222, K226, and E227.
