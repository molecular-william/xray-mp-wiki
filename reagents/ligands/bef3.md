# BeF3 (Beryllium Fluoride)

## Overview

Beryllium fluoride (BeF3-) is a phosphate analog used in structural biology to trap phosphorylated intermediate states of ATP-dependent enzymes. It mimics the geometry and charge distribution of the terminal phosphate group in ATP or the aspartyl-phosphate intermediate of P-type ATPases. When combined with Mg2+, BeF3- binds to the phosphorylation site and forms a tetrahedral complex that structurally resembles the transition state of phosphate transfer.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Forms a stable tetrahedral complex with Mg2+ that closely mimics the aspartyl-phosphate intermediate
- Enables trapping of phosphorylated states for high-resolution structural studies
- Useful for studying the conformational changes associated with phosphorylation in ATPases

### Disadvantages

- Beryllium compounds are toxic and require careful handling
- BeF3- complexes are labile and must be prepared immediately before use
- Only traps specific phosphorylated states, not the complete reaction cycle
