# Digoxin

## Overview

Digoxin is a cardiotonic steroid (cardiac glycoside) of the cardenolide class, characterized by a five-membered unsaturated lactone ring and a trisaccharide moiety (tridigitoxose) attached at C3 of the steroid core. It is a potent and specific inhibitor of the Na+,K+-ATPase, used clinically for the treatment of heart failure and atrial fibrillation. The crystal structure of the pig kidney Na+,K+-ATPase E2P form in complex with digoxin was determined at 3.9 A resolution, revealing that the steroid core binds in the same cavity as ouabain, with the trisaccharide extending into the extracellular vestibule. The E2P-digoxin complex contains a single Mg2+ ion at cation-binding site II, similar to the ouabain complex. Digoxin lacks the extensive hydrogen-bonding network of ouabain; only the conserved OH14beta group forms a hydrogen bond with Thr797 (alphaM6), while OH12beta may hydrogen bond with Asn122 (alphaM2). The tridigitoxose moiety interacts with Gln111, Thr114, Glu116, Gln117 (loop alphaM1-2), Glu312 (alphaM4), Arg880 (loop alphaM7-8), and Gln84 (beta-ectodomain).

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Na+,K+-ATPase inhibition

Digoxin inhibits Na+,K+-ATPase by binding to the E2P phosphoenzyme state. The binding occurs in a two-step process: an initial low-affinity complex forms via hydrophobic interactions with the steroid alpha-surface, followed by a conformational transition to a tight complex. Unlike ouabain, the aglycone form of digoxin (digitoxigenin) shows incomplete inhibition that is strongly antagonized by K+. Glycosylation shifts the equilibrium toward the tight complex, resulting in 100% inhibition.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Na+,K+-ATPase alpha subunit (E2P state)

The cis-trans-cis steroid core binds in the cavity formed by alphaM1-M6. The OH14beta group hydrogen bonds with Thr797 (alphaM6) and is further stabilized by Asp121 (alphaM2). OH12beta may form hydrogen bonds with Asn122 (alphaM2). The tridigitoxose moiety (trisaccharide) extends into a hydrophilic cavity in the extracellular vestibule, where the first proximal digitoxose unit interacts with Gln111, Thr114, Glu116, Gln117 (loop alphaM1-2), Glu312 (alphaM4), Arg880 (loop alphaM7-8), and Gln84 (beta-ectodomain). The five-membered lactone ring is located in a hydrophobic funnel formed by Leu125 (alphaM2), Ala323 (alphaM4), and Ile800 (alphaM6), reaching toward cation-binding site II.

- **Key residues**: Thr797, Asp121 (alphaM6/M2 interactions); Gln111, Thr114, Glu116, Gln117 (alphaM1-2 loop); Glu312 (alphaM4); Arg880 (alphaM7-8 loop); Gln84 (beta-subunit)
