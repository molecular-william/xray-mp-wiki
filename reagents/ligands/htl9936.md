# HTL9936

## Overview

HTL9936 is a selective M1 muscarinic acetylcholine receptor partial agonist designed through structure-based drug design (SBDD) for the treatment of memory loss in Alzheimer's disease. It was progressed through preclinical efficacy models and Phase 1 clinical trials. HTL9936 shows high M1 selectivity (EC50 32 nM at M1, > 20 μM at M2 and M3) and partial agonism that limits peripheral adverse effects.

## Chemical Structure

- **Core scaffold**: Azepine ring with homopiperidine moiety
- **Key functional groups**: Carbamate and secondary amide
- **Chirality**: (S)-enantiomer is the active form
- **Synthesis**: Multi-step synthesis from tert-butyl 4-oxoazepane-1-carboxylate via reductive amination, ozonolysis, and amide coupling

## Binding to M1 Receptor

- **PDB structures**: M1-StaR-T4L:HTL9936 complex at 2.4 Å (PDB: 6ZG9)
- **Primary interaction**: Protonated nitrogen of homopiperidine ring forms charge-charge interaction with D105(3.32)
- **Tyrosine cage disruption**: Piperidine nitrogen contacts Y404(7.39), causing rotation of Y381(6.51)
- **Water-mediated networks**:
  - Carbamate moiety: Y106(3.33), T196(5.46), Y381(6.51), N382(6.52) in major pocket
  - Amide group: C178(4.50), Y404(7.39) in minor pocket/extracellular vestibule
- **Binding site volume**: Closer to antagonist tiotropium than full agonist iperoxo — consistent with partial agonism

## Pharmacology

- **M1**: EC50 32 nM (pERK1/2), 79 nM (DMR), 631 nM (IP1)
- **M2**: > 20 μM (no detectable agonism)
- **M3**: > 20 μM (no detectable agonism)
- **M4**: EC50 398 nM (partial agonist)
- **M5**: No detectable agonism
- **Selectivity panel**: < 25% agonism at 10 μM against 62 other GPCRs
- **Bias**: Unbiased agonist across Gq, G11, G15, IP pathway, pERK, and arrestin recruitment

## Clinical Development

- **Phase 1 SAD/MAD**: NCT02291783 — well-tolerated up to 175 mg oral dose
- **CNS penetration**: CSF/plasma ratio 0.11–0.39 (21% AUC ratio)
- **fMRI study**: NCT02546310 — significant hippocampal activation at 10.1 mg i.v. (low dose)
- **Adverse effects**: Mild cholinergic effects (salivation, sweating, BP changes) only at 175 mg cohort (Cmax 242 ng/mL)
- **Therapeutic window**: ~5× margin between effective plasma exposure and adverse effect threshold

## Preclinical Efficacy

- **Mouse fear conditioning**: Reversed scopolamine-induced memory deficits
- **Rat passive avoidance**: Reversed amnesia in dose-dependent manner (10 and 30 mg/kg p.o.)
- **Novel object recognition**: Improved working memory at 10 mg/kg
- **Prion disease model**: Improved hippocampal-dependent contextual learning
- **Aged beagle dogs**: Improved visual-spatial working memory at 0.3 and 1 mg/kg
- **Non-human primates**: Increased delta power on qEEG (CNS target engagement)
