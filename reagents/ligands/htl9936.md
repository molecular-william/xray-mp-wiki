# HTL9936

## Overview

HTL9936 is a selective, partial orthosteric agonist of the human M1 muscarinic acetylcholine receptor designed through structure-based drug design. It was developed as a potential therapeutic for cognitive decline in Alzheimer disease, with the goal of activating central M1 receptors involved in learning and memory while avoiding peripheral cholinergic side effects. HTL9936 demonstrates high selectivity over M2, M3, and M5 muscarinic receptor subtypes, with only partial agonist activity at M4. The crystal structure of M1-StaR-T4L in complex with HTL9936 (PDB 6ZG4) at 2.17 A resolution revealed the molecular basis for its selectivity and partial agonism.
