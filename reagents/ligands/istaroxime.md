# Istaroxime

## Overview

Istaroxime (IST) is a next-generation cardiotonic steroid (CTS) developed for the treatment of acute heart failure. Unlike classical cardiotonic steroids such as ouabain and digoxin, istaroxime has a fairly flat steroid ring (different from the chair configuration typical of conventional CTSs) and an aminoalkyloxime group attached at the C3 position replacing the lactone ring at C17. Cryo-EM structures reveal that istaroxime binds to the Na+,K+-ATPase in an upside-down orientation compared to classical CTSs, with its aminoalkyloxime group extending deep into the transmembrane cation binding cavity at site II.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Na+,K+-ATPase inhibition

Istaroxime inhibits the Na+,K+-ATPase by binding to the E2P state in a unique upside-down orientation relative to classical CTSs. The aminoalkyloxime group at C3 extends into the cation binding cavity, with the primary amine reaching the cluster of oxygen atoms that coordinate Na+ and K+ at site II. Hydrogen bonds form with Glu327/334 carboxyl and Val325/332 carbonyl oxygen. The carbonyl group on C17 forms a hydrogen bond with Gln111/118 on M1. These interactions explain the fourfold higher affinity of IST to NKA in E2P^ATP than in E2P^Pi·Mg2+.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Na+,K+-ATPase alpha subunit

Istaroxime binds to the Na+,K+-ATPase in the E2P state with an upside-down orientation relative to classical CTSs. The aminoalkyloxime group at C3 extends deep into the cation binding cavity, with the primary amine forming hydrogen bonds with Glu327/334 and Val325/332 at site II. The carbonyl group on C17 hydrogen bonds with Gln111/118 on M1. The steroid ring forms a stacking interaction with Phe783/790. The flat steroid ring of IST (different from the chair configuration of ouabain) allows deeper penetration into the binding site. Two methyl groups at the β-position to the amine fill space around Ile800/807 and Leu801/808, while α-position methyl groups would clash with Val325/332 and Glu327/334.

- **Key residues**: Gln111/118 (M1), Val325/332 (M4), Glu327/334 (M4), Phe783/790 (M5), Ile800/807 (M6), Leu801/808 (M6), Asp804/811 (M6), Asp808/815 (M6)
