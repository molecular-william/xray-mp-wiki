# AMPPCP (Adenosine 5'-[beta,gamma-methylene]triphosphate)

## Overview

AMPPCP (adenosine 5'-[beta,gamma-methylene]triphosphate) is a non-hydrolyzable ATP analogue in which the bridging oxygen between the beta and gamma phosphates is replaced by a methylene group (CH2). It is widely used in structural and biochemical studies of ATP-binding proteins and ATPases to trap the enzyme in an ATP-bound conformation without allowing catalytic turnover. Unlike AMP-PNP (which uses an imido group NH), AMPPCP uses a methylene bridge, conferring distinct conformational properties that are particularly useful for capturing specific enzyme states.
