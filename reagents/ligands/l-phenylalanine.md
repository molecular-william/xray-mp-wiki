# L-Phenylalanine

## Overview

L-Phenylalanine is a large hydrophobic amino acid that binds to the AvGluR1 ligand- binding domain (LBD) with relatively low affinity compared to other ligands. Unlike alanine, serine, and methionine, phenylalanine does not utilize a chloride ion as a surrogate ligand. Instead, its bulky aromatic side chain occludes the anion binding site, preventing chloride binding. The phenylalanine complex shows distinct side-chain conformations compared to other ligand complexes.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to AvGluR1 LBD

The alpha-amino and alpha-carboxyl groups make identical interactions to the glutamate complex. The bulky aromatic side chain occupies space in the binding cavity and occludes the anion binding site, preventing chloride ion binding. Side-chain conformations and cavity geometry differ from all other ligand complexes, representing the most structurally distinct complex among the six ligand structures solved.

- **Pocket volume**: Different from other complexes due to phenylalanine side chain
- **Key residues**: Arg522 (domain 1), Asp720 (domain 2), no chloride ion bound
