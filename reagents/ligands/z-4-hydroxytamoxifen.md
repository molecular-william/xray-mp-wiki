# Z-4-Hydroxytamoxifen

## Overview

Z-4-Hydroxytamoxifen is the Z-isomer of 4-hydroxytamoxifen, a primary metabolite of tamoxifen. It is approximately 30- to 100-fold more potent in vivo as an anti-estrogen than tamoxifen itself. Z-4-Hydroxytamoxifen was tested for sodium channel inhibition alongside E-4-hydroxytamoxifen, showing an IC50 of 239 nM against NavMs, making it the most potent isomer tested in the study.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to NavMs voltage-gated sodium channel

Z-4-Hydroxytamoxifen binds to both inner and outer sites near the intracellular gate of the NavMs sodium channel. The inner site involves hydrogen bond interactions with M222, K226, and E227. The binding pocket shares geometric features with the estrogen receptor tamoxifen binding pocket, including hydrogen bonds with an aspartic acid at one end and a glutamate at the other.
