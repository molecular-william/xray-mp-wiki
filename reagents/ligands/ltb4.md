# Leukotriene B4 (LTB4)

## Overview

Leukotriene B4 (LTB4) is a potent inflammatory lipid mediator derived from arachidonic acid via the 5-lipoxygenase pathway. It serves as the endogenous high-affinity agonist for the leukotriene B4 receptor 1 ([Leukotriene B4 Receptor 1 (BLT1) — Guinea Pig and Human](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/blt1/)), a class A G protein-coupled receptor. [3H]LTB4 has been widely used as a radioligand for binding assays to characterize [Leukotriene B4 Receptor 1 (BLT1) — Guinea Pig and Human](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/blt1/) pharmacology and for determining ligand affinities in membrane preparations.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: leukotriene B4
- **Class**: eicosanoid, inflammatory mediator

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Endogenous agonist of BLT1

LTB4 is the high-affinity endogenous agonist for the [Leukotriene B4 Receptor 1 (BLT1) — Guinea Pig and Human](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/blt1/) receptor. Radiolabeled [3H]LTB4 has been used in saturation and competition binding assays to characterize [Leukotriene B4 Receptor 1 (BLT1) — Guinea Pig and Human](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/blt1/) pharmacology. The binding of [3H]LTB4 to BLT1-expressing membranes was used to demonstrate that sodium ions act as negative allosteric modulators and that [BIIL260](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/biil260/) competes with the sodium ion for binding to [Leukotriene B4 Receptor 1 (BLT1) — Guinea Pig and Human](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/blt1/).
