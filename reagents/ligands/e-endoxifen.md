# E-Endoxifen

## Overview

E-Endoxifen is the E-isomer of endoxifen (4-hydroxy-N-desmethyltamoxifen), the primary active metabolite of tamoxifen. It was tested for sodium channel inhibition, showing an IC50 of 4.2 uM against NavMs, which is approximately 2-fold less potent than the Z-isomer.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to NavMs voltage-gated sodium channel

E-Endoxifen binds to both inner and outer sites near the intracellular gate of the NavMs sodium channel, similar to the parent endoxifen compound and the Z-isomer. The inner site involves hydrogen bond interactions with M222, K226, and E227.
