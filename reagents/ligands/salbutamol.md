# Salbutamol (Albuterol)

## Overview

Salbutamol (also known as albuterol in the United States) is a synthetic catecholamine derivative and a selective beta2-adrenergic receptor agonist widely used as a bronchodilator in the treatment of asthma and COPD. It contains a hydroxymethyl group on the aromatic ring in place of one of the catechol hydroxyl groups. Salbutamol acts as a partial agonist at beta1AR with lower affinity (log KD approximately -4.0 to -6.1 depending on receptor construct) compared to [Isoprenaline (Isoproterenol)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/isoprenaline/) and [Carmoterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/carmoterol/), and was used as a crystallization ligand for the turkey beta1-adrenergic receptor (beta1AR-M23) structure at 3.05 A resolution. The methylhydroxyl substituent in the salbutamol headgroup adopts different conformations in different crystallographic monomers.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Turkey Beta1-Adrenergic Receptor (beta1AR)

Salbutamol binds deep within the transmembrane bundle of beta1AR. The hydroxymethyl group forms a hydrogen bond with Asp121 in transmembrane helix 3 and with Ser211 and Ser215 in extracellular loop 2. The methylhydroxyl substituent on the aromatic ring interacts with Asn310 in monomer A but not in monomer B, even though the binding pockets appear very similar. This conformational difference between monomers A and B at the top of helix 7 may affect the extended binding pocket.

- **Key residues**: Asp121, Ser211, Ser215, Asn310, Trp117, Val122, Val125
