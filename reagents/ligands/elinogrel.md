# Elinogrel

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: P2Y12 receptor antagonist, reversible, inverse agonist
- **Relation**: Similar to ticagrelor and selatogrel in mechanism
- **Status**: Investigational antiplatelet drug

## Mechanism

Elinogrel acts as an inverse agonist of the P2Y12 receptor, stabilizing the inactive form of the Gαi/o-Gβγ complex. Like selatogrel and ticagrelor, it prevents platelet activation by blocking ADP binding to the P2Y12 receptor.

- **Inverse agonism**: Completely abolishes constitutive P2Y12 receptor activity
- **Reversible**: Unlike clopidogrel (irreversible covalent), elinogrel binds reversibly
- **Potency**: Similar maximal efficacy to ticagrelor but different pharmacokinetic profile

## Clinical Properties

- **Reversible antagonist**: Effects wear off as drug is cleared (unlike clopidogrel's long half-life)
- **Target**: P2Y12 receptor (purinergic GPCR)
- **Indication**: Prevention of stroke and myocardial infarction
- **Advantage over clopidogrel**: No prodrug activation required; more predictable effect
