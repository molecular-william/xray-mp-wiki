# MK-0812

## Overview

MK-0812 is a tetrahydropyranyl cyclopentyl tetrahydropyridopyridine derivative discovered by Merck as a dual CCR2/CCR5 antagonist. It acts as a competitive orthosteric antagonist at the CC chemokine receptor 2 (CCR2), preventing CCL2 binding. MK-0812 showed in vivo activity by preventing macrophage infiltration in mouse models but failed to meet clinical efficacy endpoints in phase II trials for rheumatoid arthritis and multiple sclerosis. The compound provides the highest thermal stabilization among tested CCR2 antagonists (Tm shift from 55.6 C to 68.1 C).

## Binding Mode

### Binding to CC Chemokine Receptor 2A ([CCR2A](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/ccr2a/))

MK-0812 binds in the orthosteric pocket between TM1 and TM7. Key interactions include: (1) A charge-reinforced hydrogen bond (2.5 A) between the secondary amine of MK-0812 and the carboxylate oxygen of E291(7.39) - this is a distinguishing feature versus [BMS-681](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/bms-681/) which has a tertiary amine that cannot form this interaction; (2) A water-mediated hydrogen bond between the tetrahydropyran ring oxygen and the hydroxyl group of T179(4.64); (3) The bridging cyclopentane group lies on top of W98(2.60); (4) The bicyclic ring systems overlap in a sub-pocket between TM1 and TM7; (5) The trifluoromethyl group protrudes into the lipid bilayer. MK-0812 acts as a competitive inhibitor of CCL2 binding.

- **Pocket volume**: not reported
- **Key residues**: E291(7.39), W98(2.60), Y120(3.32), Y49(1.39), T179(4.64)
