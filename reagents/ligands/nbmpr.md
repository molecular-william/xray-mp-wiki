# NBMPR (S-(4-Nitrobenzyl)-6-thioinosine)

## Overview

NBMPR (S-(4-Nitrobenzyl)-6-thioinosine) is a potent nucleoside-derived adenosine reuptake inhibitor that binds hENT1 with high affinity. It consists of a thioinosine (adenosine analog) moiety connected to a p-nitrobenzyl ring. NBMPR is a standard pharmacological tool for studying hENT1 function and expression, exhibiting isoform-specific inhibition with ~2500-fold lower potency toward hENT2.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to hENT1 (orthosteric site + opportunistic site 2)

The thioinosine moiety occupies the orthosteric site: ribose 2'-OH and 3'-OH interact with Arg345 and Asp341; Gln158 coordinates N-1 and N-3 of the purine; hydrophobic residues Leu26, Met89, Leu92, Leu442 contact the purine. The p-nitrobenzyl ring occupies opportunistic site 2, a deep hydrophobic pocket lined by TM1, TM3, TM4. Gly154 at the entrance of this pocket is the key determinant of NBMPR isoform specificity.
