# LUF5833 (Compound 8)

## Overview

LUF5833 (compound 8) is a nonriboside partial agonist of the human adenosine A2A receptor (A2AR). It belongs to the dicyanopyridine class and lacks the ribose moiety typically critical for adenosine receptor agonist activity. LUF5833 was discovered as a partial agonist predominantly for the A1AR and was further developed for the A2AR subtype. It binds in the orthosteric pocket with unique interactions involving the cyano groups, exocyclic amine, and phenyl substituent.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 2-((1H-imidazol-2-yl)methylthio)-4-amino-6-phenyl-5-pyridinedicarbonitrile
- **Class**: dicyanopyridine partial agonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### A2AR crystallography as partial agonist ligand

LUF5833 was crystallized with the A2A-StaR2-bRIL construct at 1.9 A resolution (PDB 7ARO) via in meso soaking of pre-formed theophylline-bound crystals. The structure revealed a partial agonist binding mode intermediate between antagonist and full agonist states.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to A2AAR orthosteric binding pocket

Pyridine core between I274(7.39) and F168(5.29); phenyl group contacts W246 and pi-pi stacks with F168(5.29); one cyano group H-bonds to N253(6.55); exocyclic amine interacts with N253(6.55) and E169(ECL2); imidazole contacts L267(7.32), M270(7.35), Y271(7.36), I274(7.39)

- **Key residues**: N253, E169, F168, W246
