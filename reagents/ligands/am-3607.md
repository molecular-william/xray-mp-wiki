# AM-3607

## Overview

AM-3607 is a synthetic cannabinoid derivative and a novel class of analgesic potentiator that binds to the transmembrane domain of glycine receptors. It acts as a positive allosteric modulator of GlyRalpha3, potentiating glycine-activated chloride currents. AM-3607 was developed as a potential therapeutic for neuropathic pain. Crystal structures of GlyRalpha3 in complex with AM-3607 and glycine revealed how this compound binds in the transmembrane domain and induces channel gating.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Glycine Receptor Alpha 3 (GlyRalpha3)

AM-3607 binds in the transmembrane domain of GlyRalpha3 alongside glycine at the orthosteric site. The binding of AM-3607 causes the M2 helices to tilt outward by approximately 12 degrees away from the pore axis. It increases the separation between M3(+) and M1(-) of GlyRalpha3 from 6.9 A (in the strychnine-bound closed state) to 9.4 A. The spacing between Ala288 (+) and Ile225 (-) defines this separation. AM-3607 binding works in concert with glycine and ivermectin to stabilize the desensitized state of the channel.
