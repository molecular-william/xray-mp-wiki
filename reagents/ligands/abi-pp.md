# ABI-PP (tert-butyl thiazolyl aminocarboxyl pyridopyrimidine)

## Overview

ABI-PP is a pyridopyrimidine derivative that acts as a specific inhibitor of the RND-family multidrug efflux pumps AcrB (Escherichia coli) and MexB (Pseudomonas aeruginosa). It does not inhibit MexY, another RND efflux pump in P. aeruginosa. ABI-PP binds tightly to a hydrophobic trap that branches off from the substrate-translocation channel in the distal drug-binding pocket of AcrB and MexB. By occupying this trap, ABI-PP sterically hinders the functional rotation mechanism of the transporters, thereby potentiating the activity of all antibiotics exported by AcrB/MexB. Unlike the PA-beta-N class of inhibitors, ABI-PP does not cause membrane dysfunction and has almost no antibiotic activity on its own.


## Properties

- **Class**: pyridopyrimidine derivative

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### RND efflux pump inhibition

ABI-PP binds to the hydrophobic trap in the distal drug-binding pocket of AcrB and MexB, sterically hindering the functional rotation mechanism. The TAP moiety (tert-butyl thiazolyl aminocarboxyl pyridopyrimidine) inserts into the narrow hydrophobic trap surrounded by phenylalanine residues (F136, F178, F573, F610, F615, F628 in MexB). The hydrophilic tetrazole ring interacts with D274, R620, and K151. ABI-PP is not exported by AcrB/MexB and thus potentiates the activities of all antibiotics exported by these pumps, including both distal-binding and proximal-binding drugs.
