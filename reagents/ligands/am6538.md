# AM6538

## Overview

AM6538 is a tight-binding, wash-resistant antagonist of the human cannabinoid
receptor CB1 (CNR1). It was synthesized by strategic modification of rimonabant
to introduce an acetylenic chain with a terminal nitrate group, enhancing its
ability to stabilize the ligand-receptor complex and promote CB1 crystal formation.
AM6538 exhibits high affinity for CB1 (Ki = 3.4 ± 1.0 nM) and was used as the
crystallization ligand for the first high-resolution CB1 crystal structure
(PDB 5TGZ, 2.8 A). Affinity mass spectrometry confirmed that AM6538 binds as an
intact molecule with no evidence of covalent modification of cysteine residues.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 4-(4-(1-(2,4-dichlorophenyl)-4-methyl-3-(piperidin-1-ylcarbamoyl)-1H-pyrazol-5-yl)phenyl)but-3-yn-1-yl nitrate
- **Chemical formula**: C26H25Cl2N5O4
- **Molecular weight**: 542.42 g/mol
- **Class**: cannabinoid receptor antagonist, pyrazole carboxamide

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization ligand for CB1 structure determination

AM6538 was used as the stabilizing antagonist for crystallization of the human CB1 receptor (PDB 5TGZ, 2.8 A). Its high affinity and wash-resistant binding enabled formation of stable CB1-AM6538 complexes suitable for LCP crystallization. 29 crystals were merged to yield the 2.8 A dataset.


### Probe for CB1 ligand binding and functional characterization

AM6538 acts as a competitive antagonist of CP55,940 and THC-mediated CB1 inhibition of adenylyl cyclase and beta-arrestin2 recruitment. In pretreat-and-wash experiments, AM6538 continued to compete for radioligand binding even after extensive membrane washing, demonstrating its slow dissociation kinetics.
