# Irbesartan

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: Angiotensin receptor blocker (ARB), non-peptide antagonist
- **Scaffold**: Biphenyl-tetrazole with spiro-linker
- **Relation**: Clinically used anti-hypertensive drug

## Binding to AT1R

Irbesartan binds to the ligand-binding pocket of the human angiotensin II type 1 receptor (AT1R). Based on docking studies into the 2.9 Å SFX/XFEL structure (PDB: 4YAY):

- **Tetrazole moiety**: Forms a salt bridge with Arg167ᴱᴰᴸ² (ECL2)
- **Spir-linker**: Positions the biphenyl core optimally in the hydrophobic pocket
- **Hydrophobic contacts**: Biphenyl core interacts with Ile31¹·³⁵, Phe77²·⁵³, Val108³·³², Leu112³·³⁶, Ile288⁷·³⁹, Tyr292⁷·⁴³

## Clinical Properties

- **Oral bioavailability**: ~60-80% oral bioavailability
- **Half-life**: 11-15 hours (allows once-daily dosing)
- **Indications**: Hypertension, diabetic nephropathy in type 2 diabetes
- **Advantage**: Does not interact with CYP450 enzymes (fewer drug-drug interactions)
