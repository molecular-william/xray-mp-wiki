# MSX-2

## Overview

MSX-2 is a xanthine-based radioligand used for binding assays at adenosine receptors. It serves as a reference ligand for competitive binding experiments with A2AAR antagonists. MSX-2 binds to A2AAR with high affinity and is used to measure antagonist affinity via displacement assays.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 8-(4-[4-[2-carbomethoxyphenyl]-1-piperazinyl]butyl)-1,3-dipropylxanthine
- **Chemical formula**: C25H35N5O3
- **Molecular weight**: 457.57 g/mol
- **Class**: xanthine
- **Kd/Ki**: 0.8 nM at A2AAR
- **Clinical status**: Research radioligand

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Radioligand binding assay

[3H]MSX-2 is used as the radioligand in competitive binding experiments to measure antagonist affinity at A2AAR. Preladenant and other antagonists were tested for displacement of [3H]MSX-2 binding to wild-type and mutant A2AAR constructs. MSX-2 binding was unaffected by C-terminal truncation or bRIL fusion.
