# Serotonin (5-Hydroxytryptamine, 5-HT)

## Overview

Serotonin (5-hydroxytryptamine, 5-HT) is a biogenic amine neurotransmitter that modulates central nervous system activity as well as processes throughout the body ranging from cardiovascular function to digestion, body temperature, endocrinology and reproduction. In the brain, serotonin is synthesized from tryptophan in the raphe nuclei and distributed via long projections reaching nearly every major brain region. The serotonin transporter (SERT) terminates serotonergic signalling by reuptaking serotonin from the synaptic cleft into presynaptic neurons through Na+/Cl--coupled transport. SERT is the primary molecular target for selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs), the most widely prescribed class of antidepressants.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Serotonin Transporter (SERT)

Serotonin binds to the central (primary) binding site (S1) of SERT, located between transmembrane helices 1, 3, 6, 8 and 10. The primary amine group of serotonin interacts with the conserved Asp98 carboxylate in subsite A, while the indole ring occupies subsite B formed by hydrophobic residues including Tyr176, Ile172, and Phe341. Serotonin transport is strictly dependent on Na+ and Cl- ions coordinated by the transporter. SSRIs such as (S)-citalopram and paroxetine compete with serotonin for binding to the central site, blocking reuptake.
