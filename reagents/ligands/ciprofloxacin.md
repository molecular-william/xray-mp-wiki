# Ciprofloxacin - Fluoroquinolone Antibiotic

## Overview

Ciprofloxacin (Cip) is a broad-spectrum fluoroquinolone antibiotic that inhibits bacterial DNA gyrase and topoisomerase IV. It is used clinically to treat a wide range of bacterial infections. In membrane protein research, ciprofloxacin serves as a model substrate for studying multidrug efflux pump mechanisms, as it is actively transported by RND efflux pumps such as AcrB.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to AcrB Multidrug Efflux Transporter

Ciprofloxacin binds between two protein subunits of AcrB. Residues within 6 A include Phe458, Phe459, and Lys29', Leu25', Ala385' from the neighboring subunit. The cyclopropyl group interacts with Phe458 and Phe459 (3.3 A to Phe459). The epsilon-amino group of Lys29' is ~5 A from the electron-poor ring nitrogen of the quinolone moiety, suggesting dipolar contributions. The carbonyl oxygen of Ala385 is ~5 A from one carboxyl oxygen of Cip, possibly interacting via an intervening water molecule. Binding is mostly hydrophobic with some polar contributions from Lys29'.
