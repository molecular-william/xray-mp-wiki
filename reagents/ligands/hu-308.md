# HU-308

## Overview

HU-308 is a highly CB2-selective synthetic cannabinoid agonist. It shows approximately 5,000-fold higher binding affinity for CB2 compared to CB1 (Ki = 22.7 nM for CB2; almost no measurable Ki for CB1). HU-308 was used as a model ligand to probe the structural basis of agonist selectivity in CB1 and CB2. Molecular dynamics (MD) and metadynamics (metaMD) simulations revealed that HU-308 exhibits distinct binding behavior in CB1 versus CB2, with unstable binding in CB1 and stable binding in CB2, correlating with its high CB2 selectivity.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to CB2 (CNR2)

HU-308 was studied by molecular docking and MD simulations in both CB1 and CB2. In CB2, HU-308 shows stable binding throughout 2 microsecond MD simulations with low RMSD values. In CB1, HU-308 exhibits unstable binding with significantly larger RMSD values, indicating weak interaction with the CB1 orthosteric pocket. MetaMD simulations showed HU-308 entry paths into both CB1 and CB2, with three typical positions: P1 (bulk environment in extracellular region), P2 (initial ligand-binding position when N-terminal opened), and P3 (fully ligand-binding position in the orthosteric site). The distinct binding behavior correlates with CB2 selectivity.
