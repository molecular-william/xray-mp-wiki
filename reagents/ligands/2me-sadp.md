# 2-Methylthio-ADP (2MeSADP)

## Overview

2-Methylthio-adenosine 5'-diphosphate (2MeSADP) is a stable analog of adenosine diphosphate (ADP) that acts as a potent agonist at P2Y12 receptors. It is commonly used as a radiolabeled ligand (tritiated 2MeSADP) for binding assays to characterize P2Y12 receptor pharmacology. 2MeSADP has a thiomethyl group at the 2-position of the adenine ring, conferring resistance to enzymatic degradation and enabling high-affinity binding to P2Y12R.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 2-methylthio-adenosine 5'-diphosphate
- **Class**: P2Y12 receptor agonist; ADP analog

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### P2Y12 receptor binding assays

Tritiated 2MeSADP was used as the radiolabeled ligand for saturation and competition binding assays on P2Y12R cell membranes. Saturation binding yielded Kd values of 4.25 nM (WT), 33.6 nM (D294N), and 51.5 nM (WT + BRIL + D294N). Competition assays with AZD1283 yielded Ki = 43.9 nM (WT + BRIL + D294N). Mutagenesis of residues K80, K280, and E281 abolished specific tritiated 2MeSADP binding, indicating their role in agonist recognition.
