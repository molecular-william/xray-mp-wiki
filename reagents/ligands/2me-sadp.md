# 2-Methylthio-ADP (2MeSADP)

## Overview

2-Methylthio-adenosine 5'-diphosphate (2MeSADP) is a stable analog of adenosine diphosphate (ADP) that acts as a potent agonist at P2Y12 receptors. It is commonly used as a radiolabeled ligand (tritiated 2MeSADP) for binding assays to characterize P2Y12 receptor pharmacology. The co-crystal structure of P2Y12R with 2MeSADP at 2.5 A resolution revealed a closed extracellular lid conformation and provided the first structural insights into agonist-bound GPCR from the delta-group of class A receptors.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 2-methylthio-adenosine 5'-diphosphate
- **Class**: P2Y12 receptor agonist; ADP analog

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### P2Y12 receptor binding assays

Tritiated 2MeSADP was used as the radiolabeled ligand for saturation and competition binding assays on P2Y12R cell membranes. Saturation binding yielded Kd values of 4.25 nM (WT), 33.6 nM (D294N), and 51.5 nM (WT + [BRIL](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/protein-tags/bril/) + D294N). Competition assays with [AZD1283](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/azd1283/) yielded Ki = 43.9 nM (WT + [BRIL](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/protein-tags/bril/) + D294N). Mutagenesis of residues K80, K280, and E281 abolished specific tritiated 2MeSADP binding, indicating their role in agonist recognition.
