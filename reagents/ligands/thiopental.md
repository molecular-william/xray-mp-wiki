# Thiopental

## Overview

Thiopental is a clinically used barbiturate anesthetic. It contains a sulfur atom (thiol group replacing a carbonyl oxygen) that provides an anomalous scattering signal for X-ray crystallography. Thiopental was chemically synthesized from pentobarbital sodium salt using Lawesson reagent. Co-crystallization with the locally-closed [GLIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) mutant (PDB 5L4E, 3.5 A resolution) revealed that thiopental binds in the central ion channel pore in an inverted orientation compared to [Bromobarbital](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/bromobarbital/) and [Selenocyanobarbital](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/selenocyanobarbital/), due to steric hindrance from its branched isoamyl tail.
