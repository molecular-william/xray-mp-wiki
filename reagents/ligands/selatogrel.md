# Selatogrel (ACT-246475)

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: P2Y12 receptor antagonist, reversible, inverse agonist
- **Scaffold**: Non-[nupg-nucleoside-transporter](/proteins/nupg-nucleoside-transporter/), small molecule
- **Affinity**: Kd = 1.5 nM (binding studies); IC50 = 14 nM (platelet aggregation)
- **Relation**: Highly selective P2Y12 antagonist developed by Idorsia Pharmaceuticals

## Mechanism

Selatogrel acts as a potent inverse agonist of the P2Y12 receptor:

- **Inverse agonism**: Completely abolishes constitutive P2Y12 receptor activity
- **Potency**: ~100-fold more potent than ticagrelor
- **Binding site**: Binds to pocket 1 (spanning helices III–VII) of P2Y12R
- **Structural basis**: 2.8 Å co-crystal structure confirms selatogrel stabilizes the inactive, basal state of P2Y12R
- **Steric overlap**: Binding mode overlaps with ADP and 2MeSADP binding site

## Clinical Properties

- **Reversible antagonist**: Unlike clopidogrel (irreversible), selatogrel effects are reversible
- **Rapid onset**: Fast platelet inhibition
- **Blood loss profile**: Lower blood loss than clopidogrel and ticagrelor at equivalent antithrombotic efficacy
- **No off-target effects**: Does not interfere with vascular tone regulation (unlike ticagrelor)
- **Indication**: Rapid platelet inhibition in acute coronary syndrome
