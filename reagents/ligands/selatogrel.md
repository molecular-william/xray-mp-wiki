# Selatogrel

## Overview

Selatogrel (ACT-246475) is a potent, reversible, and highly selective inverse agonist of the human P2Y12 receptor. It binds to the orthosteric pocket 1 of P2Y12 with a Kd of 1.5 nM and inhibits ADP-induced platelet aggregation with an IC50 of 14 nM. Selatogrel is approximately 100-fold more potent than ticagrelor and acts as an inverse agonist, stabilizing the inactive conformation of the P2Y12 receptor and blunting constitutive Gi/o signaling. It has been shown to have an improved blood-loss profile compared to clopidogrel and ticagrelor at equivalent antithrombotic efficacy.


## Properties

- **Class**: Reversible P2Y12 receptor inverse agonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Co-crystallized ligand for P2Y12 receptor structure

Selatogrel was used as a crystallization chaperone at 2 mM concentration during IMAC purification of the P2Y12-BRIL fusion protein. The presence of selatogrel stabilized the receptor in its inactive conformation, enabling determination of the 2.78 A resolution crystal structure (PDB 7pp1). Selatogrel binds to pocket 1 of the P2Y12 receptor, with steric overlap with the ADP binding site, consistent with competitive antagonism.


### Inverse agonist for functional characterization of P2Y12 signaling

Selatogrel was used in BRET2 assays to demonstrate inverse agonist efficacy at the P2Y12 receptor. It increased the BRET signal in cells expressing P2Y12 and G alpha-RLuc8/G beta gamma-GFP2 probes, indicating reassembly of the dissociated G protein heterotrimer. Selatogrel completely abolished constitutive cAMP inhibition in CAMYEL-expressing HEK293T cells and increased basal cAMP levels in human platelets, confirming inverse agonism on endogenous P2Y12 receptors.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to P2Y12 receptor orthosteric binding pocket (pocket 1)

Binds spanning helix III to VII. Key residues: His 187(5.38), Phe 252(6.51), Arg 256(6.55), Lys 280(7.35). Pushes Tyr 259(6.58) farther out of pocket than AZD1283. Conformational change of Tyr 259(6.58) specific to selatogrel binding.
