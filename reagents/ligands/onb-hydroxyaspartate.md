# ONB-hydroxyaspartate (o-nitrobenzyl-hydroxyaspartate)

## Overview

ONB-hydroxyaspartate is a photocaged compound consisting of beta-hydroxyaspartate protected by an o-nitrobenzyl (ONB) photoremovable protecting group (PPG). In the nonirradiated state, it acts as a competitive inhibitor of glutamate transporters, binding in a mode similar to TFB-[TBOA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tboa/). Exposure to UV light (312 nm) cleaves the ONB group and releases beta-hydroxyaspartate, which is a known substrate for mammalian glutamate transporters and their prokaryotic homologues. The compound was prepared via a nine-step chemoenzymatic synthesis route using an engineered variant of methylaspartate ammonia lyase (MAL-L384A) as a biocatalyst. The UV-vis spectrum features a band at lambda = 270 nm characteristic of the ONB system. Irradiation at 312 nm leads to uncaging with a quantum yield of 28% in water, corresponding to a high uncaging cross section (599 M-1 cm-1).

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [GltTk (Glutamate Transporter Homologue from Thermococcus kodakarensis)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/glt-tk/)

In the caged (nonirradiated) state, ONB-hydroxyaspartate binds as a competitive inhibitor. The TBOA-equivalent part (hydroxyaspartate moiety) is clearly observed in the electron density omit map, indicating binding in the substrate binding site. The compound inhibits uptake of radiolabeled aspartate into proteoliposomes reconstituted with purified [GLTTK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/glttk/). After UV uncaging, the released beta-hydroxyaspartate acts as a transport substrate.
