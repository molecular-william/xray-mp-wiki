# BIIL260

## Overview

BIIL260 is a selective antagonist for the leukotriene B4 receptor BLT1, bearing a benzamidine moiety that enables it to bind both the orthosteric ligand-binding site and the conserved sodium ion-binding site of class A GPCRs. The benzamidine group occupies the sodium ion position and mimics the entire sodium ion-centered water cluster, locking BLT1 in the inactive conformation. This dual-site binding mechanism makes BIIL260 an inverse agonist and negative allosteric modulator for BLT1. The first crystal structure of BLT1 was determined in complex with BIIL260 (PDB 5X33).


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: BIIL260
- **Class**: benzamidine-containing BLT1 antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### BLT1 inverse agonist and crystallization ligand

BIIL260 binds to both the orthosteric binding site and the sodium ion-binding site of BLT1. Three of its four benzene rings occupy the orthosteric pocket, while the benzamidine moiety extends to interact with D66 (2.50), V69 (2.53), S106 (3.39), W236 (6.48), and S276 (7.45) in the conserved sodium ion-binding site. This dual binding stabilizes BLT1 in the inactive state and prevents the transmembrane helices from adopting active-state conformations. BIIL260 was used as the crystallization ligand for the first BLT1 structure (PDB 5X33, 3.7 A).
