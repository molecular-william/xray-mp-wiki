# p-OMe-azo-TBOA (4-methoxyazobenzene-TBOA)

## Overview

p-OMe-azo-[TBOA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tboa/) is a photoswitchable azobenzene derivative of [TBOA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tboa/) (DL-threo-beta-benzyloxyaspartic acid), a competitive inhibitor of glutamate transporters. The compound incorporates an azobenzene photoswitch between the [TBOA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/tboa/) scaffold and a p-methoxybenzyl group, enabling reversible interconversion between thermally stable trans and metastable cis isomers upon light irradiation. The trans isomer is more elongated and binds with higher affinity (KD 1.89 +/- 1.26 uM) to glutamate transporters than the bent cis isomer (KD 3.19 +/- 0.49 uM). The compound was used to solve crystal structures of [GLTTK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/glttk/) in both isomeric states, providing structural insight into light-controlled inhibition of membrane transporters.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [GltTk (Glutamate Transporter Homologue from Thermococcus kodakarensis)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/glt-tk/)

Both isomers bind with the aspartate moiety in the substrate binding site and the azobenzene photoswitch extending outward. In the trans configuration, the azobenzene nitrogen forms a hydrogen bond with the main-chain amide group of Gly360 in the HP2 loop (distance 2.8-3.1 A). Isomerization to the cis configuration shifts the p-OMe-benzyl group toward the HP1 loop and increases the distance between Gly360 and the azo-nitrogen (3.0-4.2 A), weakening or breaking this hydrogen bond. The cis form also causes a shift of the p-OMe-benzyl group toward the HP1 loop.

- **Pocket volume**: Substrate binding site with HP2 loop occlusion by photoswitchable group
- **Key residues**: Gly360 (HP2 loop, hydrogen bond with azo-nitrogen in trans state)
