# IRL2500

## Overview

IRL2500 is a peptide-mimetic of the C-terminal tripeptide of endothelin-1 (ET-1)
that functions as a potent and selective ETB receptor inverse agonist. It was
developed based on the partial region of ET-1, not on the bosentan scaffold,
making it structurally distinct from most ETR antagonists. IRL2500 has an IC50
value of 1.2 nM for the ETB receptor and shows higher affinity than bosentan.
The compound has been shown to have neuroprotective effects, attenuating
cold injury-mediated brain edema and disruption of the blood-brain barrier
in animal models.


## Properties


## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### ETB-selective inverse agonist

IRL2500 functions as an inverse agonist for the ETB receptor, stabilizing the
inactive conformation. This was demonstrated using the constitutively active
ETB mutant L192[3.43]Q, where IRL2500 reduced basal signaling.


### Neuroprotective agent

Intracerebroventricular administration of IRL2500 attenuated cold injury-mediated
brain edema and disruption of the blood-brain barrier in animal models,
indicating a neuroprotective effect.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Endothelin ETB Receptor (ETB)

The tryptophan group of IRL2500 interacts with positively charged residues in the ETB binding pocket. The dimethylbenzoyl group superimposes with W21 of ET-1 and forms hydrophobic interactions. The biphenyl group penetrates into the transmembrane core proximal to D147[2.50], stabilizing the inactive conformation. Key binding residues include N158[2.61], K182[3.33], R343[6.55], D147[2.50], and W336[6.48].
