# Caffeine

## Overview

Caffeine (1,3,7-trimethylxanthine) is a non-selective, weak antagonist of adenosine receptors. It is the most widely consumed psychoactive substance and serves as a prototypical xanthine antagonist in structural studies of adenosine receptors. Crystal structure of caffeine bound to the A2A receptor at 2.1 A resolution revealed a dual binding mode with two orientations at approximately 50:50 ratio.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 1,3,7-trimethylxanthine
- **Chemical formula**: C8H10N4O2
- **Molecular weight**: 194.19
- **Class**: xanthine antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### A2AR crystallography

Caffeine was crystallized with the A2AR-StaR2-b562RIL construct at 2.1 A resolution, revealing a dual binding mode. The structure was solved at pH 5.0 and superimposes well with the previous A2AR-caffeine structure at pH 8.2.
