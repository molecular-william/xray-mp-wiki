# Vismodegib (GDC-0449)

## Overview

Vismodegib (GDC-0449) is an FDA-approved small-molecule antagonist of the smoothened (SMO) receptor, indicated for the treatment of advanced basal cell carcinoma. It binds to the transmembrane domain (TMD) of SMO in a pocket resembling the canonical ligand-binding site of class A GPCRs. Multiple crystal structures of SMO with vismodegib have been determined, providing key insights into SMO pharmacology.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### FDA-approved SMO antagonist for basal cell carcinoma treatment

Vismodegib is one of two FDA-approved drugs targeting the SMO-mediated hedgehog signalling pathway (along with sonidegib/LDE-225). It acts as an SMO antagonist by binding to the transmembrane domain, inhibiting hedgehog pathway activation in cancer cells. Long-term administration can lead to resistance through SMO mutations.


### Structural studies of SMO transmembrane domain

Vismodegib-bound SMO structures (PDB: 5L7I) have been used as references for comparative analysis of SMO conformations across different ligand-bound states. The vismodegib-bound structure shows the CRD tilted by approximately 3 degrees towards the straight-up conformation perpendicular to the membrane plane, and the glycan on N493 partially occupies the cholesterol binding site.
