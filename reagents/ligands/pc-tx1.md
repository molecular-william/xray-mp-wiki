# Pilidium Toxin 1 (PcTx1)

## Overview

Pilidium toxin 1 (PcTx1) is a 37-amino-acid peptide toxin from the venom of the tarantula *Psalmopoeus cambridgei*. It is a potent and selective activator of acid-sensing ion channels (ASICs), particularly ASIC1a and ASIC1b. PcTx1 activates these channels at neutral pH and stabilizes the desensitized state, producing a sustained current that does not fully desensitize. It is widely used as a pharmacological tool to study ASIC function and as a probe for ASIC structural biology. PcTx1 has analgesic effects in rodent models of acute pain and provides neuroprotection in a mouse model of ischemic stroke.


## Properties


## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Electrophysiological activation of ASIC1a at neutral pH

PcTx1 activates ASIC1a channels at neutral pH (pH 7.4) with an EC50 of 188.6 +/- 17.8 nM and a Hill slope of 1.75. The toxin-induced current is sustained and does not fully desensitize, unlike the rapidly desensitizing proton-gated current. This unique property makes PcTx1 valuable for studying the open state of ASIC channels.


### Structural probe for ASIC crystallography

PcTx1 was used as a crystallization chaperone to solve the structure of the ASIC1a delta13 construct in complex with the toxin. Structures were solved at both high pH (3.35 A, space group R3) and low pH (2.80 A, space group C2), revealing the toxin bound at subunit interfaces and the conformational changes accompanying pH-dependent gating.


### Gating modifier toxin binding mechanism

The first three-dimensional structure of a gating modifier toxin bound to an ion channel was determined for the PcTx1-ASIC1 complex at 3.0 A resolution. The structure revealed that three PcTx1 molecules bind at the proton-sensitive acidic pockets of ASIC1, with a hydrophobic patch and a basic cluster locking two separate regulatory regions in their relative, desensitized-like arrangement. This provides a general concept for gating modifier toxin binding.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to ASIC1a

PcTx1 binds at the subunit interfaces of the ASIC1a trimer, contacting residues across the wrist, palm, and thumb domains. The toxin's arginine residues form key interactions at the subunit interface, and the aromatic residues engage in an 'aromatic embrace' with the channel. Binding occurs at the acidic pocket region between adjacent subunits, stabilizing the closed/desensitized conformation of the channel. The hydrophobic patch of PcTx1 wraps around helix 5 of ASIC1, shielding the basic cluster from bulk solvent and enhancing electrostatic interactions between arginine guanidinium groups and ASIC1 carboxylates. Phe351 and Phe174 of ASIC1 are critical for PcTx1 selectivity, accounting for 155 A^2 of the 860 A^2 total binding surface.
