# Endoxifen

## Overview

Endoxifen (4-hydroxy-N-desmethyltamoxifen) is the primary active metabolite of tamoxifen formed by sequential 4-hydroxylation and N-demethylation. It is the predominant estrogen receptor (ER) binder in vivo and shows comparable potency to tamoxifen and 4-hydroxytamoxifen against voltage-gated sodium channels. Endoxifen was crystallized in complex with the NavMs sodium channel, revealing binding at both inner and outer sites near the intracellular gate.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to NavMs voltage-gated sodium channel

Endoxifen binds to both inner and outer sites near the intracellular gate of the NavMs sodium channel, overlapping with the estrogen receptor tamoxifen binding pocket. The inner site involves hydrogen bonds with M222 (intracellular gate residue), K226, and E227. The binding pocket shares geometric features with the estrogen receptor, including hydrogen bonds with an aspartic acid at one end and a glutamate at the other.
