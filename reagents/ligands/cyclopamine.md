# Cyclopamine

## Overview

Cyclopamine is a naturally occurring teratogen and the first selective small-molecule ligand of the human Smoothened (SMO) receptor discovered. It acts as an antagonist of the hedgehog signalling pathway by binding to the SMO 7-transmembrane domain. While cyclopamine inhibits GLI transcription factor activation, it induces cilia translocation of SMO, unlike SANT1 which does not. Mutations within the SMO ligand binding site that disrupt cyclopamine binding fail to impair normal basal and hedgehog-induced activity, suggesting an alternative modulation site.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: jervine alkaloid (not specified in crystallography paper)
- **Class**: jervine alkaloid SMO antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Historical SMO antagonist reference

Cyclopamine was the first selective SMO ligand discovered and is referenced throughout structural studies of the SMO receptor. It was used to demonstrate that the SMO ligand binding pocket can accommodate bulky reporter moieties via long-chain flexible linkers, as demonstrated with KAAD-cyclopamine derivatives. The binding site for cyclopamine was characterized in structural comparisons with other SMO ligands.
