# Nisoxetine

## Overview

Nisoxetine is a selective norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor (NRI) and antidepressant that binds with high affinity to human norepinephrine transporter (hNET). It is used as a pharmacological tool to study norepinephrine transport and as a treatment for panic disorder and ADHD.

## Properties


## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural studies of biogenic amine transporters

Co-crystallized with dDAT to reveal the molecular basis of NET-specific inhibitor binding. Stabilizes the outward-open conformation by occupying the central substrate-binding site.


### Radioligand binding assays

Used in scintillation proximity assays (SPA) to measure inhibitor binding affinity to biogenic amine transporters.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- High selectivity for NET over DAT and SERT
- Useful model for structure-based studies of NET-specific inhibitors
- Well-characterized pharmacological profile

### Disadvantages

- Tricyclic structure contributes to off-target effects
- Lower affinity for mammalian DAT compared to NET
