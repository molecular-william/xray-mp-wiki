# Spermine

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: Polyamine (tetravalent cation)
- **Structure**: H₂N-(CH₂)₃-NH-(CH₂)₄-NH-(CH₂)₃-NH₂
- **Charge**: +4 at physiological pH
- **Molecular weight**: 202.35 Da

## Role in Kir Channel Rectification

Spermine is the primary physiological blocker responsible for inward rectification in Kir potassium channels. Polyamines (putrescine, spermidine, spermine) diffuse from the cytoplasm into the conduction pathway, occluding K⁺ efflux in a voltage-dependent manner. This "valve-like" action allows K⁺ influx but prevents efflux, stabilizing the resting membrane potential.

## Binding Sites in KirBac

Two spermine-binding sites were identified in KirBac3.1 structures:

1. **Intracellular pocket**: Located at latched subunit interfaces, buried within the cytoplasmic assembly. The pocket is preformed and accommodates the extended spermine molecule. Binding involves extensive van der Waals contacts and coordination by counter ions (chlorides). Key residues include His177 (equivalent to Kir6.2 His216, implicated in pH-titratable rectification) and Asp197.

2. **Deep pore block**: An extended spermine molecule can protrude from the intracellular pocket into the transmembrane pore, forming a cation-π interaction between the aromatic ring of Tyr132 at the bundle crossing and the secondary amine of spermine. This fully occludes the narrow pore entrance.

## Conformational Dependence

Spermine binding is conformationally dependent:
- **Latched interfaces**: Accommodate spermine within a closed pocket of sufficient length
- **Unlatched interfaces**: Morphology incompatible with spermine binding — Pro206, Ile172, and Leu242 reposition, reducing pocket length

Unlatching causes intracellular vestibule dilation (~4.5 Å), simultaneously disrupting binding sites and facilitating polyamine release into the conduction path. This mechanism ensures that rectification is coupled to the activation state of the channel.

## Related Polyamines

- **Putrescine** (divalent): Weaker rectification
- **Spermidine** (trivalent): Intermediate rectification strength
- **Mg²⁺ and Ca²⁺**: Divalent cations also block Kir channels but with different kinetics and mechanisms
