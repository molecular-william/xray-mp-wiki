# ACPC (1-Aminocyclopropane-1-carboxylic Acid)

## Overview

ACPC (1-aminocyclopropane-1-carboxylic acid) is a cyclic amino acid that acts as a partial agonist at N-methyl-[D-Aspartic Acid (D-Aspartate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/d-aspartate/) (NMDA) receptors. It binds to the [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) co-agonist site on the GluN1 subunit ligand-binding domain ([LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/)). ACPC was used in crystallization of the full-length GluN1-GluN2B NMDA receptor to help stabilize the receptor in an activated but closed-blocked channel conformation.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 1-aminocyclopropane-1-carboxylic acid
- **Class**: Partial agonist at NMDA receptors, [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) site agonist
