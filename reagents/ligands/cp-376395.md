# CP-376395

## Overview

CP-376395 is a potent inverse agonist ligand for the human corticotropin-releasing factor 1 receptor (CRF1R). It was the key ligand used in the first crystal structure determination of CRF1R, where it locked the receptor in its inactive conformation for X-ray crystallography. CP-376395 has nanomolar affinity for CRF1R and acts as an inverse agonist, reducing basal cAMP signaling in HEK293T cells expressing CRF1R.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Corticotropin-Releasing Factor 1 Receptor (CRF1R)

CP-376395 binds within the transmembrane bundle of CRF1R. Key residues involved in ligand binding include F203 (3.44), M206 (3.47), V279 (5.46), L280 (5.47), N283 (5.50), F284 (5.51), L287 (5.54), I290 (5.57), T316 (6.42), L319 (6.45), L320 (6.46), L323 (6.49), G324 (6.50), Y327 (6.53), and Q355 (7.49). Alanine mutagenesis of these residues significantly reduced ligand binding.
