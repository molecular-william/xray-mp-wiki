# TBOA (DL-threo-beta-benzyloxyaspartic acid)

## Overview

TBOA (DL-threo-beta-benzyloxyaspartic acid) is a competitive, nontransported inhibitor of glutamate transporters. It binds to the substrate binding site of transporters in the SLC1A family (excitatory amino acid transporters, EAATs), blocking transport by preventing closure of the extracellular HP2 gate. The aspartate moiety of TBOA occupies the substrate binding site while the benzyloxy substituent extends outward, sterically occluding the HP2 loop. TBOA is widely used as a pharmacological tool to study glutamate transporter function and has been the scaffold for numerous derivative compounds including photoswitchable and photocaged variants.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Glutamate transporters (SLC1A family, EAATs)

The aspartate moiety of TBOA resides in the substrate binding site of the transporter, engaging the same residues that bind the physiological substrate glutamate. The benzyloxy substituent extends outward from the binding pocket, occupying the space where the HP2 loop would need to move for gate closure. This steric occlusion prevents the conformational changes required for transport. TBOA has been compared structurally to its derivatives including p-OMe-azo-TBOA (azobenzene derivative) and TFB-TBOA (trifluoromethylbenzoyl derivative).
