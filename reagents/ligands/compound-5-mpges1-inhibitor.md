# Compound 5 (Biarylimidazole mPGES-1 Inhibitor)

## Overview

Compound 5 is a nanomolar biarylimidazole inhibitor of microsomal prostaglandin E synthase 1 (mPGES-1). It features a brominated core imidazole used for SAD phasing, a bis-ortho-chlorofluorophenyl group inserted into a groove above GSH, and a pyridine-triple bond-trifluoromethylbenzene tail that emphasizes interactions with alpha-4 of monomer 1. The bromine atom provided the anomalous signal for the first mPGES-1 structure determination by SAD.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### SAD phasing for mPGES-1 structure determination

Compound 5's bromine atom provided the anomalous signal for SAD phasing, enabling the first crystal structure determination of mPGES-1 before any coordinates were publicly available. Data collected at LRL-CAT at the Advanced Photon Source. The structure was phased using SHELX with density modification in ARP/wARP.


### Thermal stabilization for crystallization

Compound 5 imparted a 7.8 degree Celsius increase in the aggregation temperature of mPGES-1 as measured by DSLS, correlating with successful crystallization. The IC50 was 7.3 nM using membrane-based assay but >62 microM using purified protein.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to mPGES-1

Bis-ortho-chlorofluorophenyl inserted into groove above GSH at top of pocket. Core imidazole forms hydrogen bonds with H53 and S127 side chains and structured water molecule W75. Pyridine nitrogen within hydrogen bonding distance of T131 side chain oxygen (3.0 A). Hydrophobic and van der Waals contacts with alpha-4 of monomer 1 (P124, S127, V128, T131, L132, L135). Interactions with monomer 2: R38, L39, F44, D49, H53, R52. Bromine extends towards solvent interacting with R52 side chain.

- **Key residues**: H53 (monomer 2, hydrogen bond with imidazole), S127 (monomer 1, hydrogen bond with imidazole), T131 (monomer 1, hydrogen bond with pyridine nitrogen), W75 (structured water bridging imidazole and GSH)
