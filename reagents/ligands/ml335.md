# ML335 (N-aryl-sulfonamide K2P activator)

## Overview

ML335 (N-[(2,4-dichlorophenyl)methyl]-4-(methanesulfonamido)benzamide) is a selective small-molecule activator of K2P 2.1 (TREK-1) potassium channels. It functions as a molecular wedge that binds to a cryptic modulator pocket behind the selectivity filter, stabilizing the C-type gate in the leak mode. ML335 is an N-aryl-sulfonamide compound.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural basis for K2P modulator pocket discovery

ML335 co-crystallization with K2P 2.1cryst revealed a cryptic binding pocket at the P1-M4 intrasubunit interface behind the selectivity filter (PDB 5VKN). This pocket was previously unknown and represents the first VGIC superfamily pore domain small-molecule activator site. Two ML335 molecules bind per channel dimer.


### K2P C-type gate stabilization at varying potassium concentrations

In the 2020 study (doi/10.1126/sciadv.abc9174), ML335 was co-crystallized with K2P 2.1cryst at seven potassium concentrations (0-200 mM [K+]). ML335-bound structures showed canonical selectivity filter conformations with ions at all four sites (S1-S4) at all potassium concentrations, demonstrating that ML335 stabilizes the ion-filled conductive state of the selectivity filter C-type gate by rigidifying the P1-M4 interface and increasing the strength of the Glu234 hydrogen-bonding network.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to K2P 2.1 (TREK-1)

- **Pocket volume**: 56.3 A^2
- **Key residues**: Phe134, Lys271, Trp275

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Selective K2P 2.1 activator
- Stabilizes the conductive state of the C-type gate
- Useful tool for studying K2P channel gating mechanisms

### Disadvantages

- Limited to K2P 2.1; not active on all K2P subtypes
- Research tool only; not clinically developed
