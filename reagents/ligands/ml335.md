# ML335 (N-aryl-sulfonamide K2P activator)

## Overview

ML335 (N-[(2,4-dichlorophenyl)methyl]-4-(methanesulfonamido)benzamide) is a selective small-molecule activator of K2P 2.1 (TREK-1) potassium channels. It functions as a molecular wedge that binds to a cryptic modulator pocket behind the selectivity filter, stabilizing the C-type gate in the leak mode. ML335 is an N-aryl-sulfonamide compound.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural basis for K2P modulator pocket discovery

ML335 co-crystallization with K2P 2.1cryst revealed a cryptic binding pocket at the P1-M4 intrasubunit interface behind the selectivity filter. This pocket was previously unknown and represents the first VGIC superfamily pore domain small-molecule activator site. Two ML335 molecules bind per channel dimer.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to K2P 2.1 (TREK-1) modulator pocket

ML335 binds in an L-shaped pocket at the P1-M4 intrasubunit interface behind the selectivity filter. The 2,4-dichlorophenyl ring engages in pi-pi stacking with Phe134. The benzamide sulfonamide group forms a cation-pi interaction with Lys271. Additional interactions include contacts with Trp275. The binding mode positions ML335 as a molecular wedge between P1 and M4 helices.

- **Pocket volume**: 56.3 A^2
- **Key residues**: Phe134, Lys271, Trp275
