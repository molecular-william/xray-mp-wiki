# Formoterol

## Overview

Formoterol is a long-acting selective beta2-adrenergic receptor agonist used in the treatment of asthma and COPD. It contains a catecholamine-like structure with a hydroxy-amine linker connecting an aromatic ring to a side chain. Formoterol acts as a full agonist at the beta2-adrenergic receptor, stabilizing the active conformation of the receptor and inducing conformational changes in both the transmembrane core and the extracellular surface (ECS), including displacement of the Lys 305-Asp 192 salt bridge that links extracellular loops 2 and 3.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Beta2-Adrenergic Receptor (beta2 AR)

Formoterol binds within the transmembrane bundle of the beta2-adrenergic receptor as a full agonist. The hydroxy-amine motif forms conserved hydrogen bonds with residues in TM5 and TM6. Binding of formoterol induces an inward movement at the extracellular end of TM6 and an outward motion at the intracellular end, consistent with GPCR activation. This motion necessitates a lateral displacement of TM7 that reorients the Lys 305 salt bridge. Formoterol stabilizes a distinct ECS conformation different from that stabilized by inverse agonists or neutral antagonists.

- **Key residues**: Asp113, Ser193, Ser194, Ser204
