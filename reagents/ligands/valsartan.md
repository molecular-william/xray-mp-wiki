# Valsartan

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: Angiotensin receptor blocker (ARB), non-peptide antagonist
- **Scaffold**: Biphenyl with [[imidazole]] and carboxylic acid groups
- **Relation**: Clinically used anti-hypertensive drug (one of the most widely prescribed ARBs)

## Binding to AT1R

Valsartan binds to the ligand-binding pocket of the human angiotensin II type 1 receptor (AT1R). Based on docking studies into the 2.9 Å SFX/XFEL structure (PDB: 4YAY):

- **Imidazole nitrogen**: Forms a salt bridge with Arg167ᴱᴰᴸ² (ECL2)
- **Carboxylic acid**: Forms a salt bridge with Lys199⁵·⁴²
- **Biphenyl core**: Hydrophobic interactions with the binding pocket
- **Lacks tetrazole**: Unlike losartan and candesartan, valsartan uses an imidazole for the key salt bridge

## Clinical Properties

- **Oral bioavailability**: Good oral absorption, once-daily dosing
- **Indications**: Hypertension, heart failure, post-MI treatment
- **Mechanism**: Selective AT1R antagonist; does not affect angiotensin II levels (unlike ACE inhibitors)
- **Side effects**: Generally well-tolerated; hyperkalemia possible

## Comparison with Other ARBs

| ARB | Key Interaction with Arg167ᴱᴰᴸ² | Lys199 Interaction | Dissociation |
