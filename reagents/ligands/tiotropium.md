# Tiotropium

## Overview

Tiotropium is a long-acting anticholinergic medication used in the maintenance treatment of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). It is a quaternary ammonium compound derived from atropine, consisting of a tropane ring linked to a thiopheneacetic acid moiety. Tiotropium acts as a non-selective muscarinic receptor antagonist with particularly high affinity for M1, M2, and M3 subtypes. Its quaternary ammonium structure limits central nervous system penetration, reducing systemic side effects. Crystal structures of tiotropium bound to the M1, M3, and M4 muscarinic receptors reveal its binding mode in the orthosteric pocket at resolutions up to 2.6 A. The binding pose is nearly identical across all three structures.


## Properties
