# RTI-55

## Overview

RTI-55 (2-beta-carbomethoxy-3-beta-(4-iodophenyl)tropane) is a tropane alkaloid derivative that acts as a high-affinity competitive inhibitor of the dopamine transporter (DAT). It features a 4-iodophenyl group that enables anomalous diffraction studies to confirm binding.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Drosophila melanogaster dopamine transporter (dDAT)

Tropane-based competitive inhibitor with 4-iodophenyl group. Highest affinity among tropane inhibitors tested for dDAT_mfc (371 nM). Binds to central S1 site overlapping cocaine and nortriptyline binding sites. Carboxylate of Asp121 does not form favorable interactions with iodophenyl group, explaining reduced affinity of dDAT compared to human DAT. Anomalous difference density for iodide used to confirm binding.

- **Pocket volume**: 466.2 A^2 total surface area, 416.7 A^2 buried (89.4%)
- **Key residues**: Asp46, Phe319, Phe325, Phe43, Ala44, Ala48, Ser421, Asp121
