# Aprepitant

## Overview

Aprepitant is an FDA-approved small-molecule antagonist of the human neurokinin 1 receptor (NK1R), used clinically for the treatment of chemotherapy-induced nausea and vomiting (CINV). It was developed from the CP-99,994 scaffold through medicinal chemistry optimization, featuring a morpholine core with three substituent arms. The additional triazolinone arm 3 substituent enables aprepitant to engage an extended binding pocket (EBP) at the interface of helices IV, V and ECL2, inducing a distinct receptor conformation. It was co-crystallized with NK1R at 2.40 A resolution (PDB 6HLO).

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Clinical antiemetic for CINV

Aprepitant (Emend) is approved for chemotherapy-induced nausea and vomiting, typically used in combination with a 5-HT3 antagonist and dexamethasone. It acts as an insurmountable antagonist at NK1R, inducing a "histidine lock" via an interhelical hydrogen bond network between H197(5.39), Y272(6.59), T201(5.43) and H265(6.52) that cross-links helices V and VI.


### NK1R antagonist structure determination

Aprepitant was co-crystallized with the human NK1R-PGS fusion construct at 2.40 A resolution. The structure revealed that aprepitant's arm 1 (3,5-di-trifluoromethylphenyl) straddles W261(6.48), arm 2 (fluorophenyl) targets a lipophilic subpocket, and arm 3 (triazolinone) creates an EBP with W184(ECL2) pushed outward. The morpholine core sits between F268(6.55) and Q165(4.60).
