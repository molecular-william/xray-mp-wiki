# Desipramine

## Overview

Desipramine is a tricyclic antidepressant (TCA) of the dibenzazepine class. It acts primarily as a norepinephrine reuptake inhibitor (NRI) by blocking the human norepinephrine transporter (NET). Desipramine was co-crystallized with LeuT from Aquifex aeolicus to reveal the molecular mechanism by which tricyclic antidepressants block neurotransmitter reuptake.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### LeuT TCA binding site characterization

Desipramine was co-crystallized with LeuT (leucine- and sodium-bound state) as part of a panel of TCAs. In the LeuT-desipramine complex, unlike clomipramine, desipramine lacks a chlorine atom and thus cannot interact with Gln34, which partly explains its lower inhibitory potency compared to clomipramine. The EL4 residues (Ala317-Ala319) shift upward by as much as 1.4 A to accommodate desipramine binding.
