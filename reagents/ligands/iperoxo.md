# Iperoxo

## Overview

Iperoxo is a high-affinity muscarinic [Acetylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/acetylcholine/) receptor agonist. It is a potent full agonist at all muscarinic receptor subtypes with exceptional potency at the M2 receptor (EC50 = 235 pM). In the active-state M2 receptor structure, iperoxo adopts a bent conformation and is completely buried within the orthosteric binding pocket, fully occluded from solvent. The agonist forms key hydrogen bonds with conserved residues Asp 103 (3.32) as a counter-ion and Asn 404 (6.52), and its smaller size relative to antagonists like [QNB](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/3-quinuclidinyl-benzilate/) results in more limited hydrophobic contacts, necessitating inward motion of TM6 for binding.
