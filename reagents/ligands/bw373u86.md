# BW373U86

## Overview

BW373U86 is a selective delta-opioid receptor (δ-OR) agonist that displays minimal sodium sensitivity in binding assays. It serves as a pharmacological tool for studying δ-OR signaling independent of allosteric sodium modulation.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: (2R,6S)-1-(1-allyl-2-pyrrolidinyl)-2-hydroxy-4-(2,6-dichlorophenyl)piperidine
- **Class**: piperidine opioid agonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Sodium-insensitive delta-opioid receptor agonist

BW373U86 is a selective δ-OR agonist whose binding to the receptor is minimally affected by sodium ions, unlike peptide agonists such as DADLE. This property makes it useful for studying δ-OR signaling independent of allosteric sodium modulation. It displays low-to-moderate reduction in G-protein and beta-arrestin signaling at sodium-anchored-residue mutants compared to wild-type.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Minimal sodium sensitivity enables study of sodium-independent signaling
- Selective for delta-opioid receptor
- Useful for comparing sodium-dependent vs independent signaling pathways

### Disadvantages

- Low-to-moderate reduction in efficacy at sodium site mutants
