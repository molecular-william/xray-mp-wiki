# Amiloride

## Overview

Amiloride is a pharmacological ion channel blocker that targets acid-sensing ion channels (ASICs) and epithelial sodium channels (ENaC). In the ASIC1a-MitTx complex, amiloride binds within the acidic pocket of the extracellular domain and within the fenestrations of the extracellular vestibule, providing structural insight into the mechanism of channel blockade. Amiloride is a well-known blocker of sodium-selective ion channels and has been used as a pharmacological probe for ASIC function.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to ASIC1a

Two amiloride molecules bind in the acidic pocket of the extracellular domain. One amiloride molecule binds in the fenestration of the extracellular vestibule. Amiloride binds within the acidic pocket and fenestrations, blocking ion permeation.
