# Dobutamine

## Overview

Dobutamine is a synthetic catecholamine derivative and a beta1-adrenergic receptor agonist used clinically for the short-term treatment of heart failure. It contains a chiral center and is administered as a racemic mixture of (R,S)-dobutamine. In structural studies, the (R)-isomer was found to be the active form binding to beta-adrenergic receptors. Dobutamine acts as a partial agonist at beta1AR with moderate affinity (log KD approximately -5.8 to -6.8 depending on receptor construct) and was used as a crystallization ligand for the turkey beta1-adrenergic receptor (beta1AR-M23) structure at 2.5 A resolution. The n-butyl side chain of dobutamine extends into the extended ligand binding pocket.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Turkey Beta1-Adrenergic Receptor (beta1AR)

Dobutamine binds deep within the transmembrane bundle of beta1AR. The catechol headgroup forms hydrogen bonds with Asp121 in transmembrane helix 3 and with Ser211 and Ser215 in extracellular loop 2. The n-butyl side chain extends into the extended ligand binding pocket, making van der Waals contacts with Leu101, Val102, Trp117, and Thr118 in helices 2 and 3. The bulky n-butyl group creates distinct interactions not seen with smaller catecholamines, contributing to its partial agonist profile.

- **Key residues**: Asp121, Ser211, Ser215, Leu101, Val102, Trp117, Thr118
