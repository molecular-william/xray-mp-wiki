# 11-cis-Retinal

## Overview

11-cis-retinal is the light-sensitive chromophore covalently bound to the apoprotein opsin
to form rhodopsin, the photoreceptor GPCR in vertebrate retinal rod cells. It is linked to
Lys296 on helix 7 via a protonated Schiff base. Absorption of a photon at ~500 nm
photoisomerises the retinal to all-trans form within picoseconds, initiating the visual
transduction cascade. In the dark state, 11-cis-retinal acts as an inverse agonist, keeping
the basal activity of rhodopsin extremely low. The chromophore is bound in a tight pocket
formed by seven transmembrane helices, with extensive van der Waals contacts and hydrogen
bonding networks stabilizing its conformation.
