# Compound 30 (MK-886 Derivative, Biarylindole mPGES-1 Inhibitor)

## Overview

Compound 30 is a biarylindole inhibitor of microsomal prostaglandin E synthase 1 (mPGES-1) derived from the MK-886 scaffold. It binds in an elongated orientation with its long axis parallel to the transmembrane helices forming the pocket, interacting with mPGES-1 more extensively than the other three inhibitors studied. The carboxylate at the top of the binding site forms a pair of salt bridges with R52, and a bridging water connects the carboxylate to H53.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Thermal stabilization of mPGES-1

Compound 30 imparted a 5.4 degree Celsius increase in the aggregation temperature of mPGES-1 as measured by DSLS.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to mPGES-1

Carboxylate forms pair of salt bridges with R52 (monomer 2). Bridging water between carboxylate and H53 imidazole. Aromatic CH from indole and fluorophenyl H-bond with T131 hydroxyl lone pairs (monomer 2). Chlorophenyl attached to indole nitrogen extends between helical turns at top of alpha-4 (monomer 1) and alpha-1 (monomer 2). Chlorine placed on top of glutathione sulfur. Fluorinated biaryl extends downward from indole with fluorophenyl in two conformations. Hydrophobic contacts with R38, L39, F44 (monomer 2) and GSH. gem-di-methyl helps maintain low-energy bound conformation without contacting protein.

- **Key residues**: R52 (monomer 2, salt bridge with carboxylate), H53 (monomer 2, bridging water), T131 (monomer 2, H-bonds with indole and fluorophenyl CH), Y130, T131 (monomer 1, groove for fluorophenyl)
