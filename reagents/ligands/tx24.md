# Tx24 (Engineered M2-Selective Muscarinic Toxin)

## Overview

Tx24 is an engineered variant of [MT7](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/mt7/) with redirected selectivity from [M1 Muscarinic Acetylcholine Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/m1-muscarinic-acetylcholine-receptor/) to [M2 Muscarinic Acetylcholine Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/m2-muscarinic-acetylcholine-receptor/). It was created by in vitro evolution using yeast surface display, starting from the MT7 three-finger toxin (3FT) scaffold. Tx24 contains five consensus mutations (W10R, E15G, R37M, P40L, G41V) that confer high-affinity binding to M2AChR. Tx24 acts as a probe-dependent positive allosteric modulator (PAM) for orthosteric antagonists (particularly atropine and NMS bound to M2AChR) and a weak negative allosteric modulator (NAM) for agonist responses. It has a >700-fold decrease in the off-rate of [3H]NMS from M2AChR and shows minimal cross-reactivity with other muscarinic receptor subtypes.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [M2 Muscarinic Acetylcholine Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/m2-muscarinic-acetylcholine-receptor/)

Binds allosterically to the extracellular vestibule of M2AChR
- Confers conformational selectivity toward the inactive (antagonist-bound) state
- Probe-selective PAM: enhances binding of small antagonists (atropine, NMS) more than tiotropium
- Weak NAM for agonist (ACh) potency
- Stabilizes a distinct conformation at the TM5-TM6 interface
