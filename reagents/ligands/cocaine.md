# Cocaine

## Overview

Cocaine is a tropane alkaloid that acts as a competitive inhibitor of the dopamine transporter (DAT) and other neurotransmitter/sodium symporters (NSS). It blocks dopamine reuptake by binding to the central S1 site, locking the transporter in a transport-inactive conformation.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Drosophila melanogaster dopamine transporter (dDAT)

Tertiary amino group forms salt bridge with Asp46 (TM1b). TM6a-6b linker contracts, allowing Phe325 to form edge-to-face aromatic interactions with benzyl ring. Tropane ring bordered by Phe43, Ala44, Asp46, Ala48, Phe319, and Ser421. Inhibition achieved by combining free amine with bulky tropane and aromatic moieties to limit conformational movements of the transporter.

- **Pocket volume**: 484.7 A^2 total surface area, 447 A^2 buried (92.2%)
- **Key residues**: Asp46, Phe325, Phe319, Phe43, Ala44, Ala48, Ser421
