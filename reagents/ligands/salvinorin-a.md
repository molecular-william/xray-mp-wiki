# Salvinorin A

## Overview

Salvinorin A (SalA) is a selective κ-opioid receptor (KOP) agonist derived from the plant *Salvia divinorum*. It is the principal psychoactive compound in S. divinorum and produces potent hallucinogenic effects. Unlike most opioid agonists, it is a neoclass diterpenoid (not an alkaloid), making it chemically distinct from morphinans and other classical opioids.

## Properties

- **Chemical class**: Neoclass diterpenoid (boronocyclitol derivative)
- **Selectivity**: Highly selective for KOP
- **Efficacy**: Full agonist at KOP

## Binding in Active-State KOP (PDB: 6B73)

Docking into the active-state KOP structure reveals:
- **1-position carbonyl**: H-bond with Y312⁷·³⁵
- **2-position acetyl group**: hydrophobic interactions with Y312⁷·³⁵ ring; potential H-bond with Y313⁷·³⁶ hydroxyl
- **4-position methyl ester**: enclosed pocket, stabilized by H-bond with C210ᴱᶜˡ² backbone NH
- **Furan ring**: edge-face aromatic interactions with Y139³·³³
- **Note**: Lack of phenol moiety means no interaction with H291⁶·⁵² or Y139³·³³ (unlike classical opioids)

## Significance

Salvinorin A was used to immunize llamas for generation of KOP-specific nanobodies (Nb6/Nb7). Its unique chemical structure (non-alkaloid diterpenoid) provides a distinct binding mode compared to classical morphinan opioids, useful for understanding KOP pharmacology.
