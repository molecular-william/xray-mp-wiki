# Duloxetine

## Overview

Duloxetine is a serotonin-noradrenaline reuptake inhibitor (SNRI) antidepressant used clinically for the treatment of major depressive disorder, generalized anxiety disorder, and neuropathic pain. It acts by blocking both the serotonin transporter (SERT) and norepinephrine transporter (NET). Duloxetine binds to the primary binding pocket of biogenic amine transporters with the naphthalene ring and thiophene ring adopting a characteristic orientation.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Delta13 LeuBAT (LeuT engineered mutant)

Naphthalene ring is sandwiched by hydrophobic groups Tyr21, Val104, Phe259 and Tyr108. The thiophene ring protrudes into the extracellular vestibule and interacts with Phe253 by edge-to-face interactions. The amine group interacts with the carboxyl group of Asp24 and also with the main-chain carbonyl of Tyr21.
