# Naltrindole

## Overview

Naltrindole is a selective delta-opioid receptor (δ-OR) antagonist containing a cyclopentene functional group. It is one of the most widely used subtype-selective ligands for the δ-OR, with high affinity (Ki ~0.9 nM) and excellent selectivity over mu- and kappa-opioid receptors. Naltrindole serves as a reference antagonist in opioid receptor pharmacology and was used as the crystallization ligand for the first high-resolution structure of the human δ-OR.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 2',3'-dihydro-5-methyl-3-[(1R,5R,6R)-6-(1-pyrrolidinyl)methylene]-3,6-methano-2-benzomorphan hydrochloride
- **Class**: benzomorphan opioid antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Delta-opioid receptor selective antagonist

Naltrindole is a selective δ-OR antagonist with Ki values of 0.6-1.2 nM at human δ-OR. It was used as the binding ligand in the crystal structure determination of the BRIL-δOR(ΔN/ΔC) construct at 1.8 A resolution. Naltrindole binds to the orthosteric site with water-mediated interactions and forms a salt bridge with Asp128 (3.32) of the receptor.


### Beta-arrestin-biased agonist conversion

In sodium site mutants (Asp95Ala, Asn310Ala, Asn314Ala), naltrindole transforms from a classical δ-OR antagonist into a potent β-arrestin-biased agonist. This transformation demonstrates the concept of efficacy switches at the δ-OR, where mutating allosteric sodium-coordinating residues fundamentally alters the functional profile of ligands.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Delta-Opioid Receptor (δ-OR, OPRD1)

Naltrindole binds in the orthosteric pocket of the δ-OR with water-mediated hydrogen bonds to receptor side chains. The cyclopentene ring forms hydrophobic interactions with transmembrane helices. Asp128 (3.32) deep in the orthosteric site establishes a salt bridge with the nitrogen group of naltrindole. Trp284 (6.58) positions for π-π interaction with the ligand. The ligand is coordinated by multiple water molecules in the binding pocket.

- **Key residues**: Asp128 (3.32), Trp284 (6.58), Arg291 (ECL3)

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- High subtype selectivity for δ-OR over μ- and κ-opioid receptors
- Well-characterized binding affinity and pharmacology
- Useful for structural studies of δ-OR

### Disadvantages

- Transforms into β-arrestin-biased agonist in sodium site mutants
- Cyclopentene functional group contributes to mutant-dependent functional switching
