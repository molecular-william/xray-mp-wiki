# 2'3'-O-(4,5-Dinitrobenzoyl)adenosine 5'-triphosphate (TNP-ATP)

## Overview

TNP-ATP is a fluorescent analog of adenosine triphosphate (ATP) in which the adenine ring is substituted at the 2' and 3' positions with a 4,5-dinitrobenzoyl group. This modification confers strong fluorescent properties, making TNP-ATP a valuable tool for studying ATP-binding proteins. The dinitrobenzoyl group is bulky and sterically hinders the molecule from inducing full conformational changes that ATP binding normally triggers. TNP-ATP acts as a competitive inhibitor of ATP binding and can be used to probe nucleotide-binding sites in ATP-dependent enzymes and ion channels.
