# Ramatroban

## Overview

Ramatroban (BAY U 3405) is a nonprostanoid antagonist of the thromboxane A2 receptor (TP). It was developed as a TP antagonist against thrombosis and coronary artery disease and was also found to inhibit TXA2- and PGD2-induced bronchoconstriction. Ramatroban is currently marketed in Japan for the treatment of allergic rhinitis. The crystal structure of the human TP-ramatroban complex was determined at 2.5 A resolution (PDB 6IIU), revealing that the carboxylic acid group of ramatroban forms salt bridges with H89(2.65) and R295(7.40), and a hydrogen bond with S181. The benzenesulfonamide moiety occupies a subpocket in helices II, III, VI and VII, making hydrogen bonds with T81(2.57) and Q301(7.46).

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Human Thromboxane A2 Receptor (TP)

Ramatroban has three key functional groups: (1) a carboxylic acid that forms salt bridges with H89(2.65) and R295(7.40) and a hydrogen bond with S181; (2) a benzenesulfonamide moiety that fits into a subpocket shaped by helices II, III, VI and VII, with the sulfonamide nitrogen donating a hydrogen bond to T81(2.57) and the sulfonamide oxygens forming hydrogen bonds with Q301(7.46) and the main chain of L78(2.54); (3) a tetrahydrocarbazole ring as the spacer that makes extensive hydrophobic contacts with residues in helices I, III and VII.

- **Key residues**: L78(2.54), T81(2.57), H89(2.65), S181, R295(7.40), Q301(7.46)
