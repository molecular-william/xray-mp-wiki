# AZD1283

## Overview

AZD1283 is a non-nucleotide reversible antagonist of the [Human P2Y12 Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/p2y12-receptor/), developed by AstraZeneca for the treatment of arterial thrombosis. It efficiently inhibits platelet activation in vivo with only limited increase in bleeding time. The compound binds to the orthosteric ligand-binding pocket of P2Y12R within the transmembrane domain (pocket 1), forming the basis for the first high-resolution crystal structure of P2Y12R in complex with a ligand.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: ethyl 6-(4-((benzylsulphonyl)carbamoyl)piperidin-1-yl)-5-cyano-2-methylnicotinate
- **Class**: Non-nucleotide P2Y12 receptor antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### P2Y12 receptor structural studies

AZD1283 was used as the co-crystallized ligand in the first high-resolution crystal structure of human P2Y12R-[BRIL](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/protein-tags/bril/) at 2.6 A resolution (PDB 4NTJ). The structure revealed a distinct straight conformation of helix V in P2Y12R, and the ligand binds deep in the 7TM domain within pocket 1 formed by helices III-VII. AZD1283 binding also revealed that the conserved disulfide bridge between helix III and ECL2 is not observed and appears dynamic.
