# LSD (Lysergic Acid Diethylamide)

## Overview

LSD (lysergic acid diethylamide) is a potent ergoline-class hallucinogen and one of the most
powerful known psychoactive compounds. It acts as a full agonist at [Serotonin (5-Hydroxytryptamine, 5-HT)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/serotonin/) receptors,
particularly 5-HT2A and 5-HT2B, with extremely slow dissociation kinetics that likely
underlie its prolonged psychoactive effects lasting 6-15 hours. The crystal structure of
LSD bound to the 5-HT2B receptor reveals a shallow binding mode with an unexpected
conformation of the diethylamide substituent, providing structural insights into its
functional selectivity and stereospecificity.
