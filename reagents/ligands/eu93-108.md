# EU93-108

## Overview

EU93-108 is a novel piperazine-containing GluN2B-selective negative allosteric modulator (NAM) of N-methyl-[D-Aspartic Acid (D-Aspartate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/d-aspartate/) receptors (NMDARs). It exhibits high potency (IC50 = 0.557 muM at GluN1/GluN2B) and substantial selectivity for GluN2B over GluN2A, GluN2C, and GluN2D subtypes (less than 6% inhibition at 10 muM for non-2B subunits). EU93-108 demonstrates brain penetrance (brain-to-plasma ratio of 0.6), intrinsic analgesic properties in rodent pain models, and the ability to enhance morphine-induced analgesia when co-administered. The structural basis for its binding was determined by X-ray crystallography of the GluN1b-GluN2B ATD-EU93-108 complex (PDB: 8G18, 2.85 A), revealing binding at the GluN1-GluN2B ATD interface in the same pocket as [Ifenprodil](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ifenprodil/) and EU93-31.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to GluN2B subunit of NMDA receptor

Sulfonamide group forms polar interaction with GluN1b residues; piperazine and difluorophenyl groups occupy the same pocket as [Ifenprodil](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ifenprodil/)
