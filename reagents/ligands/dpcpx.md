# DPCPX (8-Cyclopentyl-1,3-dipropylxanthine)

## Overview

DPCPX (8-cyclopentyl-1,3-dipropylxanthine) is a selective adenosine A1 receptor antagonist. It was used extensively in radioligand binding assays and competition experiments in the determination of the A1AR crystal structure. DPCPX binds deep in the A1AR orthosteric site, and its short half-life is attributed to the broken H264-E172 salt bridge in the A1AR structure.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 8-cyclopentyl-1,3-dipropylxanthine
- **Chemical formula**: C15H20N4O2
- **Molecular weight**: 288.35 g/mol
- **Class**: xanthine antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Radioligand binding assay for A1AR

[3H]DPCPX was used as the radioligand in saturation and competition binding experiments on Sf9 insect cell membranes expressing A1AR constructs. DPCPX competition binding was performed to determine antagonist affinities at wild-type and mutant A1AR receptors.


### Docking studies for A1AR binding

DPCPX was docked into the A1AR crystal structure, revealing that it engages the hydrophobic pocket made up by M177(5.35), L253(6.54), T257(6.57), and T270(7.35). Molecular dynamics simulation of the DPCPX-A1AR complex over 104 ns showed RMSD of 1.12 A.
