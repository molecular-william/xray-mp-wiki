# KW-6356

## Overview

KW-6356 (N-[4-(furan-2-yl)-5-(oxane-4-carbonyl)-1,3-thiazol-2-yl]-6-methylpyridine-3-carboxamide) is a novel non-xanthine adenosine A2A receptor antagonist/inverse agonist developed by Kyowa Kirin. It exhibits potent and selective A2A receptor binding (pKi = 9.93 for human receptor) with very slow dissociation kinetics (koff = 0.016 +/- 0.006 min-1). KW-6356 shows insurmountable antagonism and inverse agonism, distinguishing it from the surmountable antagonist istradefylline. Its efficacy as monotherapy in Parkinson's disease patients has been reported.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural basis of inverse agonism

The KW-6356-bound A2A receptor structure was determined at 2.3 A resolution using the A2AR-BRIL(N154Q) construct. The furyl moiety of KW-6356 occupies the same position as the furan of ZM241385, making contacts with His250(6.52) and Trp246(6.48), residues critical for receptor activation. Steric conflict between the furyl moiety and His250(6.52) in the active-state conformation prevents KW-6356 from binding to the active state, explaining its inverse agonist properties. In contrast, istradefylline binds equally well to both active and inactive states, consistent with its neutral antagonist profile.


### Insurmountable antagonism mechanism

KW-6356 exhibits insurmountable antagonism, reducing both the potency and maximal response of CGS21680. This is attributed to the bulky oxanyl and furyl moieties occupying the deep orthosteric pocket, combined with the p-MNA group stabilizing the extracellular loop conformation. These interactions contribute to the slow dissociation kinetics (koff = 0.016 min-1) and sustained receptor occupancy, which may result in better clinical efficacy.


### Selectivity profiling

KW-6356 showed weak affinity (mostly <25% inhibition at 10 uM) for a panel of 50+ receptors, transporters, and ion channels, and did not inhibit MAO-A, MAO-B, or COMT enzymes. This high selectivity is supported by the multiple specific interactions observed in the cocrystal structure, including interactions with A2A-specific residues.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to A2AR orthosteric pocket

The N-thiazol-2-ylnicotinamide scaffold forms conserved hydrogen bonds with Asn253(6.55). The furyl moiety extends deep into the orthosteric pocket, making hydrophobic contacts with Trp246(6.48) and His250(6.52), which is key for inverse agonism. The oxane group interacts with Ala63(2.61), Ile66(2.64), and Ile274(7.39), with the O1 oxygen on the oxane interacting with Ser277(7.42) and His278(7.43) via water-mediated hydrogen bonds. The p-methylnicotinamide (p-MNA) group extends to the pocket lid, forming a hydrogen bond with Thr256(6.58) and CH-pi interactions with His264(ECL3) and Met270(7.35). Glu169(ECL2) is located near the pyridine ring, possibly forming an electrostatic interaction.

- **Key residues**: Asn253, Trp246, His250, Thr256, His264, Glu169, Ser277, His278, Ile274
