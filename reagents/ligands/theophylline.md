# Theophylline

## Overview

Theophylline (1,3-dimethylxanthine) is a non-selective, weak antagonist of adenosine receptors. It is a methylxanthine derivative related to caffeine and is used clinically as a bronchodilator for asthma and COPD. Crystal structure of theophylline bound to the A2A receptor at 2.0 A resolution revealed details of xanthine binding in the orthosteric pocket.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 1,3-dimethylxanthine
- **Chemical formula**: C7H8N4O2
- **Molecular weight**: 180.16
- **Class**: xanthine antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### A2AR crystallography

Theophylline was crystallized with the A2AR-StaR2-b562RIL construct at 2.0 A resolution. The structure served as a starting point for PSB36 soaking experiments to obtain the A2AR-PSB36 structure at 2.8 A.
