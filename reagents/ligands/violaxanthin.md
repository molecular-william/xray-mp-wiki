# Violaxanthin

## Overview

Violaxanthin is a xanthophyll carotenoid that serves as a key component of the xanthophyll cycle in plants. It binds to light-harvesting complex II (LHC-II) at the monomer interface with relatively low binding affinity compared to other carotenoids. Through the activity of violaxanthin de-epoxidase, it is converted to zeaxanthin under high-light conditions, playing a central role in non-photochemical quenching (NPQ) by dissipating excess excitation energy as heat.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: (3R,3'R,5R,6R,6'R)-3,6,3'-trihydroxy-beta,c-carotene-5,6-epoxide
- **Chemical formula**: C40H56O4
- **Molecular weight**: 600.87 g/mol
- **Class**: xanthophyll carotenoid

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Non-photochemical quenching via xanthophyll cycle

Violaxanthin binds to LHC-II at the monomer interface with its OH group at the C1 end making a hydrogen bond to the phytol carbonyl of chlorophyll 10. Under high-light conditions, violaxanthin de-epoxidase converts it to zeaxanthin, which has a more extended pi system and acts as an energy sink, dissipating excess excitation energy as heat through non-photochemical quenching.


### Carotenoid binding site in LHC-II

Violaxanthin occupies the fourth carotenoid binding site in LHC-II at the monomer interface. It has the lowest binding affinity among the LHC-II carotenoids and is present in substoichiometric amounts (~0.5 per monomer) in the isolated complex. The binding pocket is entirely hydrophobic except for polar contacts to the phytol carbonyl of chlorophyll 10 and the glycerol OH of the phosphatidylglycerol lipid.
