# Rimonabant

## Overview

Rimonabant is a CB1 cannabinoid receptor antagonist that was repurposed as an MmpL3 inhibitor. It was approved by the European Medical Agency for treating obesity (as an adjunct to diet and exercise) but was later withdrawn from the market due to psychiatric side effects. Its discovery as an MmpL3 inhibitor represents an example of drug repurposing.

## Properties

- **Chemical class**: Pyrazole derivative (1-(2,4-dichlorophenyl)-5-(4-chlorophenyl)-4-methyl-1H-pyrazole-3-carboxamide)
- **Original indication**: CB1 antagonist for obesity treatment (withdrawn)
- **Target**: MmpL3 (mycobacterial)
- **K_d for M. smegmatis MmpL3**: 29 µM
- **MIC against Mtb**: 54 µM

## Binding Mode in MmpL3

Distinct from SQ109/AU1235/ICA38 — three "arms":
- **Arm 1** (2,4-dichlorophenyl): Hydrophobic pocket (aliphatic side chains)
- **Arm 2** (4-chlorophenyl): Neighboring hydrophobic subsite (Val245, Ile319, Val638, Leu708)
- **Arm 3** (pi-peridinyl-1-carbamoyl): Thrusts into heart of Asp-Tyr pairs, breaking their H-bonds
- **Pyrazole core**: Inserts at TMH bundle center, interacts with Gly641, Leu642, Ile253
- **Pocket volume**: ~305 Å³
- TMH IV₂₄₄₋₂₅₄ undergoes marked flexing

## Significance

- First CB1 antagonist shown to directly target MmpL3
- Approved drug → potential for faster clinical translation
- Different binding mode from known MmpL3 inhibitors → new scaffold for drug design
