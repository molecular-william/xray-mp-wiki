# Doxorubicin - Anthracycline Anticancer Drug

## Overview

Doxorubicin is a widely used anthracycline anticancer antibiotic that functions as a substrate for multidrug efflux pumps including the MATE family transporter NorM-VC from Vibrio cholerae. It is a fluorescent compound commonly used in transport assays to measure efflux pump activity. Doxorubicin binds to NorM-VC with a dissociation constant (Kd) of 1.00 +/- 0.08 uM as determined by fluorescence polarization.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to NorM-VC

Doxorubicin binds to the internal cavity of NorM-VC with Kd = 1.00 +/- 0.08 uM. Excitation wavelength: 485 nm, Emission wavelength: 595 nm. The binding was measured by fluorescence polarization using native NorM-VC protein.
