# Benzyl-Hydantoin

## Overview

Benzyl-hydantoin (L-5-benzyl-hydantoin) is a substrate analog of the NCS1 family transporter Mhp1 from Microbacterium liquefaciens. It serves as a model compound for studying nucleobase transport, as it mimics the structure of purine and pyrimidine bases. Benzyl-hydantoin binding to Mhp1 is sodium-dependent, with affinity increasing over 10-fold in the presence of 15 mM sodium.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for Mhp1 NCS1 Transporter

Benzyl-hydantoin is the primary substrate used to characterize the Mhp1 transporter from Microbacterium liquefaciens. The structure of Mhp1 in complex with benzyl-hydantoin (4 Å resolution) revealed the outward-facing occluded conformation, showing how the outward-facing cavity closes upon substrate binding.
