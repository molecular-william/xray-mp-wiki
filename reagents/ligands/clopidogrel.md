# Clopidogrel (Plavix)

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: P2Y12 receptor antagonist, irreversible covalent binder
- **Relation**: Prodrug requiring hepatic activation to active metabolite
- **Trade name**: Plavix (one of the most widely prescribed antiplatelet drugs worldwide)

## Mechanism

Clopidogrel is a prodrug that must be metabolized by CYP450 enzymes (primarily CYP2C19) to form the active thiol metabolite. This active metabolite forms an **irreversible covalent bond** with the P2Y12 receptor at Cys97³·²⁵, permanently disabling the receptor on that platelet.

- **Covalent binding**: Active metabolite covalently modifies Cys97³·²⁵ (helix III)
- **Irreversible**: Effect lasts for the lifetime of the platelet (7-10 days)
- **P2Y12 selectivity**: Highly selective for P2Y12 over other receptors
- **C97 role**: The dynamic/labile disulphide bridge in P2Y12R (C97-C270) leaves C97 available for covalent modification

## Clinical Properties

- **Prodrug**: Requires CYP2C19 activation (genetic polymorphism affects efficacy)
- **Duration**: Long-lasting effect (platelet lifespan)
- **Indications**: Acute coronary syndrome, post-MI, stroke prevention
- **Limitations**: 
  - Variable response due to CYP2C19 polymorphism
  - Very long half-life of action (cannot be rapidly reversed)
  - Bleeding risk

## Structural Basis

The P2Y12R crystal structure (2.6 Å, P2Y12R-BRIL, Wu et al., 2014) reveals:
- **Cys97³·²⁵ location**: In pocket 2 of the ligand-binding site
- **Labile disulphide**: The C97-C270 disulphide bond is dynamic/unresolved in the structure
- **Pocket 2**: Adjacent to pocket 1 (AZD1283 binding site); C97 belongs to pocket 2
- **C97 mutations**: C97A retains protein yield and stability but abolishes covalent drug binding
