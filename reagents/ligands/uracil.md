# Uracil

## Overview

Uracil is a pyrimidine base and the natural substrate of the uracil:proton symporter UraA from Escherichia coli. It is a planar heterocyclic molecule that is one of the four nucleobases in RNA. UraA mediates the coupled transport of uracil with protons across the bacterial inner membrane, utilizing the proton motive force to accumulate uracil against its concentration gradient.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: uracil
- **Chemical formula**: C4H4N2O2
- **Molecular weight**: 112.09 g/mol
- **Class**: pyrimidine nucleobase

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for UraA transporter

Uracil is the natural substrate of UraA, a uracil:proton symporter from Escherichia coli. The substrate binds in the core domain of UraA in an occluded conformation, coordinated primarily by the core domain residues Glu241, Glu290, and backbone amide groups of Phe73 and Gly289. Multiple water-mediated hydrogen bonds contribute to substrate coordination.
