# beta-FNA (beta-Funaltrexamine)

## Overview

beta-Funaltrexamine (beta-FNA) is an irreversible narcotic antagonist that covalently binds to the mu-opioid receptor. It is a fumaramate methyl ester derivative of naltrexone that forms a covalent bond with Lys233^5.39 on the receptor. beta-FNA shares a common morphinan scaffold with [BU72](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/bu72/) and other morphinan ligands. It was co-crystallized with the inactive muOR structure and its pose serves as a reference for docking agonists like [beta-FOA (beta-Fuoxymorphamine)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/beta-foa/) into the inactive-state receptor.
