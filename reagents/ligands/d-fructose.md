# D-Fructose

## Overview

D-Fructose is a monosaccharide sugar that serves as the primary substrate of the GLUT5 fructose transporter. It is the sweetest naturally occurring carbohydrate and is a key energy source in the human diet. GLUT5 is the only member of the GLUT/SLC2 transporter family specific to fructose, with a Kd of ~6-9 mM as measured by tryptophan fluorescence quenching. D-Fructose binding can be inhibited by HgCl2, a known GLUT inhibitor.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for GLUT5 fructose transporter

D-Fructose is the specific substrate of GLUT5 (SLC2A5), a facilitated-diffusion
monosaccharide transporter in the MFS superfamily. Crystal structures of rat GLUT5
(outward-open) and bovine GLUT5 (inward-open) reveal the fructose-binding site,
with key residues including Gln166, Gln287, Gln288, Asn324, Trp419, Tyr31,
His386, Ala395, His418, and Ser391. The Q166E mutation switches substrate
preference from fructose to glucose.
