# TRV023

## Overview

TRV023 is a beta-arrestin-biased peptide agonist of the angiotensin II type 1 receptor (AT1R). It is a synthetic octapeptide analog of angiotensin II (AngII) with N-methyl glycine at position 1 (sarcosine) and an F8A substitution (phenylalanine to alanine at position 8). This C-terminal modification profoundly attenuates Gq-mediated signaling while preserving robust beta-arrestin coupling. TRV023 was co-crystallized with AT1R (PDB 6OS1) at 2.7-2.9 A resolution, providing structural insight into how the C-terminal residue substitution leads to biased signaling.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to [Angiotensin II Type 1 Receptor](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/at1r/)

The F8A substitution at the C terminus of TRV023 prevents the deep insertion and conformational heterogeneity observed with AngII F8. This results in a distinct active conformation where TM6 undergoes outward rotation characteristic of GPCR activation, but the polar network rearrangement (N111^3.35 flipping) essential for Gq signaling does not occur.
