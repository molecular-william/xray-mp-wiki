# 3,4-Dichlorophenethylamine (DCP)

## Overview

3,4-Dichlorophenethylamine (DCP) is a halogenated phenethylamine derivative that serves as a stable analogue of dopamine. It was developed for crystallographic studies of the dopamine transporter because it is approximately isosteric to dopamine but resistant to oxidation.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Drosophila melanogaster dopamine transporter (dDAT)

Primary amine forms hydrogen bond with Asp46 at 3.2 A distance. Chlorine atoms at 3,4 positions on aromatic ring provide high binding affinity and stability against oxidation. Isosteric with dopamine, occupies central binding pocket. Induces partial occlusion of the binding pocket through inward rotations of TM1b (5.6 degrees) and TM6a (7 degrees). Phe319 rotates inward to cover the ligand. Associated with dehydration of binding pocket, similar to LeuT occluded state.

- **Pocket volume**: 337.3 A^2 total surface area, 321.5 A^2 buried (95.3%)
- **Key residues**: Asp46, Tyr124, Phe319, Ala117, Val120, Asp121, Phe325
