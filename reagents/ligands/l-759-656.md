# L-759,656

## Overview

L-759,656 is a CB2-selective synthetic cannabinoid agonist. It shows approximately 406-fold higher binding affinity for CB2 compared to CB1 (Ki = 11.8 nM for CB2; Ki = 4.8 uM for CB1). L-759,656 was used as a model ligand alongside HU-308 to probe the structural basis of agonist selectivity in CB1 and CB2. Molecular dynamics (MD) and metadynamics (metaMD) simulations revealed that L-759,656 exhibits stable binding in CB2 but unstable binding in CB1, correlating with its CB2 selectivity.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to CB2 (CNR2)

L-759,656 was studied by molecular docking and MD simulations in both CB1 and CB2. In CB2, L-759,656 shows stable binding throughout 2 microsecond MD simulations. In CB1, L-759,656 exhibits unstable binding with significantly larger RMSD values. The key residues involved in L-759,656 binding also show larger RMSD fluctuations in CB1 compared to CB2, indicating weaker and less stable interactions. MetaMD simulations explored the ligand entry paths into CB1 and CB2, revealing distinct binding behaviors consistent with selectivity.
