# Amino-Methoxy-Isocoumarin (JLK-6)

## Overview

Amino-methoxy-isocoumarin (7-amino-4-chloro-3-methoxy isocoumarin, also known as JLK-6) is a stable mechanism-based inhibitor of serine proteases, including the rhomboid intramembrane protease GlpG. It is a derivative of isocoumarin with increased stability (half-life ~13 h in phosphate buffer) compared to diisocoumarin (DCI), making it suitable for crystallographic studies. It inhibits GlpG with an IC50 of approximately 6 uM and forms a doubly covalent-bonded alkylated acyl enzyme complex with the catalytic serine (S201) and histidine (H254) of GlpG.
