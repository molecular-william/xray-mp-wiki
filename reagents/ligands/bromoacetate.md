# Bromoacetate

## Overview

Bromoacetate (BrCH2COO-) is a short carboxylic acid that serves as an anomalously detectable mimic of the glutamate carboxylate side chain. In the E148A mutant of [CLC-ec1 Cl-/H+ Antiporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/clc-ec1/), bromoacetate was used to probe anion binding in the ungated transporter. It can occupy either the external (Sext) or central (Scen) Cl- binding site, demonstrating that a mobile carboxylate group from solution can substitute for the missing Gluex side chain. The bromine atom enables anomalous diffraction for unambiguous identification of binding sites.
