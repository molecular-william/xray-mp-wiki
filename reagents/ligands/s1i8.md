# S1I8 Peptide

## Overview

S1I8 (Sarcosine1, Isoleucine8-Angiotensin II) is a partial agonist peptide analog of angiotensin II with the sequence N-methyl-Gly-Arg-Val-Tyr-Ile-His-Pro-Ile. The S1I8 peptide was used to crystallize the active-state human angiotensin II type 1 receptor (AT1R) in complex with the synthetic nanobody Nb.AT110i1 at 2.9 A resolution (PDB 6DO1). Compared to full agonist AngII (which ends in Phe8), the Ile8 substitution converts the peptide into a partial agonist for both Gq and beta-arrestin-mediated activities. The C-terminal residue 8 is critical for determining signaling bias: Ile8 produces partial Gq agonism, Phe8 (AngII) produces full Gq agonism, and smaller residues or deletion at position 8 produce beta-arrestin-biased signaling.
