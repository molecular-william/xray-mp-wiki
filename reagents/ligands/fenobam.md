# Fenobam

## Overview

Fenobam is a negative allosteric modulator (NAM) of the metabotropic glutamate receptor 5 (mGlu5) featuring a urea linkage between a 3-methyl-4H-imidazol-5-one ring and a chlorobenzene ring. Originally developed as an anxiolytic compound decades ago, fenobam was progressed into clinical trials for fragile X syndrome (FXS). The urea chemotype is structurally distinct from the acetylene-based NAMs such as M-MPEP and mavoglurant. The X-ray crystal structure of mGlu5 in complex with fenobam (PDB 6FFH, 2.65 A) revealed a binding mode that is the inverse of a previously published induced-fit docking model, with most polar interactions being water-mediated.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 1-(4-chlorophenyl)-3-(3-methyl-4H-imidazol-5-one-4-yl)urea

- **Class**: urea-containing NAM
