# AMP-PNP (Adenylyl Imidodiphosphate)

## Overview

AMP-PNP (adenylyl imidodiphosphate) is a non-hydrolysable ATP analogue commonly used in biochemical assays to study ATP-binding proteins. It mimics ATP in binding interactions but cannot be hydrolyzed, making it useful for trapping nucleotide-binding states. In this study, AMP-PNP was used in pull-down assays to demonstrate that ATP-binding alone (without hydrolysis) triggers dissociation of BhuT from BhuUV.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Pull-down assay for nucleotide-induced BhuT dissociation

AMP-PNP was used in His-tag pull-down assays to test whether the non-hydrolysable ATP analogue could cause dissociation of GFP-BhuT from immobilized His-BhuUV. Both ATP and AMP-PNP caused dissociation of GFP-BhuT from BhuUV, while ADP (the hydrolysis product) did not. This demonstrated that ATP-binding alone, not hydrolysis, is sufficient to reduce BhuUV affinity for BhuT.
