# MRS2500

## Overview

MRS2500 [(1'R,2'S,4'S,5'S)-4-(2-iodo-6-methylaminopurin-9-yl)-1-[(phosphato)methyl]- 2-(phosphato)bicycle[3.1.0]-hexane] is a potent, selective, and stable antagonist of the human P2Y1 receptor. It is a nucleotide analogue containing a bicyclo[3.1.0] hexane ring system locked in a northern conformation, with a 2-iodo-6-methyladenine base and 3',5'-bisphosphate groups. MRS2500 completely blocks ADP-induced platelet aggregation and effectively reduces arterial thrombosis with only moderate prolongation of bleeding time, making it an attractive candidate for antithrombotic therapy.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: (1'R,2'S,4'S,5'S)-4-(2-iodo-6-methylaminopurin-9-yl)-1-[(phosphato)methyl]- 2-(phosphato)bicycle[3.1.0]-hexane

- **Class**: Nucleotide P2Y1 receptor antagonist

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### P2Y1 receptor structural studies

MRS2500 was used as the co-crystallized ligand in the crystal structure of human P2Y1R at 2.7 A resolution (PDB 4M48). The structure revealed the orthosteric nucleotide-binding site within the transmembrane bundle, with the adenine ring forming hydrogen bonds with N283(6.58) and the 2-iodo group fitting into a sub-pocket shaped by the N terminus.


### Platelet aggregation inhibition assays

MRS2500 completely blocks ADP-induced platelet aggregation in vitro and effectively reduces arterial thrombosis in vivo in mouse models. It was used in competition binding assays with tritiated 2MeSADP to characterize P2Y1R pharmacology and mutagenesis effects.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to P2Y1 receptor

The adenine ring forms hydrogen bonds with N283(6.58) side chain; the 2-iodo group interacts with the main chain carbonyl of C42; the N6-methyl group forms hydrophobic interactions with A286(6.61) and N299(7.28); a salt bridge forms with R195. Key residues K46, R195, Y303(7.32), and Y306(7.35) coordinate the phosphate groups.
