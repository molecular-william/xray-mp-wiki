# Isocoumarin

## Overview

Isocoumarin is a non-peptidic inhibitor of rhomboid protease [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/glpg/) that forms a covalent bond with the catalytic histidine (His-254) rather than the catalytic serine. The crystal structure of the GlpG-isocoumarin complex (PDB 2XOW, 2.09 A) reveals that isocoumarin induces conformational changes strikingly similar to those caused by CAPF and [DFP](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/diisopropyl-fluorophosphate/) inhibitors. The movements of TM helices S2, S1, L1, S3, and S5 are nearly identical across all three inhibitor complexes. The isocoumarin structure was determined at higher resolution (2.09 A) than the CAPF complex, and the similarities between the two structures suggest that the observed conformational changes reflect intrinsic properties of the membrane protease rather than specific interactions with individual inhibitors. In the isocoumarin complex, a detergent molecule (NG) was found occupying the position where the Cbz group of CAPF binds, suggesting a conserved hydrophobic binding site near Met-149 and Phe-153. Isocoumarins are the only inhibitor class reported to have significant activity against rhomboids, although the activity is relatively weak compared to classical serine protease inhibitors.
