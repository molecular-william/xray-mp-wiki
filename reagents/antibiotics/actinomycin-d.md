# Actinomycin D

## Overview

Actinomycin D is a cytotoxic polypeptide antibiotic that intercalates into DNA and inhibits RNA synthesis. It is a well-known substrate of the multidrug efflux transporter P-glycoprotein (P-gp), and its cellular toxicity is used as a functional assay for P-gp drug transport activity. In the C. elegans P-gp study, actinomycin D was used to demonstrate that P-gp expression confers cellular resistance to this cytotoxic drug.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: D-valine-L-proline-N-methyl-L-tryptophan-N-methyl-L-valylactamide
- **Class**: polypeptide antibiotic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### P-gp substrate assay

Actinomycin D is used as a standard substrate to assess P-glycoprotein drug transport activity. In the C. elegans P-gp study, cells expressing P-gp showed protection from actinomycin D-induced cytotoxicity, demonstrating functional drug efflux activity.
