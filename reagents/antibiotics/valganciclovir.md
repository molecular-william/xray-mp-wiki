# Valganciclovir

## Overview

Valganciclovir is an antiviral prodrug consisting of a guanosine analog (ganciclovir) conjugated to L-valine. It is used for treating cytomegalovirus (CMV) infections. The valine conjugation improves water solubility and intestinal uptake by enabling recognition as a substrate for the human peptide transporter PepT1 (SLC15A1). Valganciclovir is synthesized as a mix of two diastereomers differing at the C2 position of the valine side chain, and both diastereomers are transported by human PepT1. In the E. coli peptide transporter DtpA, valganciclovir binds with the guanine base mimicking the N-terminal residue of a peptide and the valine residue occupying the P2 peptide side chain binding pocket.
