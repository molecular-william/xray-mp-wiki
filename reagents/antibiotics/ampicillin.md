# Ampicillin

## Overview

Ampicillin is a beta-lactam antibiotic that inhibits bacterial cell wall synthesis by binding to penicillin-binding proteins (PBPs) and preventing peptidoglycan cross-linking. It is a broad-spectrum antibiotic effective against Gram-positive and some Gram-negative bacteria. In Escherichia coli, ampicillin resistance is mediated by efflux pumps such as AcrB and by beta-lactamase enzymes. The crystal structure of the AcrB multidrug efflux pump in complex with ampicillin revealed binding within the central cavity of the trimer.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to AcrB (Acriflavine resistance protein B)

Six ampicillin molecules were observed bound within the central cavity of the AcrB trimer. Ampicillin binds to the same location as other substrates including nafcillin, ethidium, and linezolid, near the A385/F386 loops on the wall of the upper portion of the central cavity. The binding site accommodates a broad range of substrate structures, consistent with the multidrug specificity of AcrB.

- **Key residues**: A385, F386
