# Thrombin Protease

## Overview

Thrombin is a serine protease that specifically cleaves at the sequence Leu-Val-Pro-Arg|Gly-Ser. It is widely used in protein biochemistry to cleave affinity tags (such as His-tags, GST-tags, or Flag tags) from recombinant proteins after purification. The high specificity of thrombin minimizes unwanted proteolysis of the target protein.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: thrombin (serine protease)
- **Class**: serine protease

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Tag cleavage in SMO receptor purification

Thrombin protease was used to cleave the N-terminal affinity tags from the BRIL-deltaCRD-SMO-deltaC construct during purification. The construct contained an HA signal, Flag tag, and 10xHis tag at the N-terminus, connected by a thrombin protease cleavage site. After Ni-NTA affinity purification, thrombin cleavage removed the N-terminal tags, releasing the untagged chimera protein for subsequent SEC and crystallization.
