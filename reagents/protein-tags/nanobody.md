# Nanobody

## Overview

A nanobody is a single-domain antibody fragment derived from camelid heavy-chain-only antibodies. Nanobody-80 (Nb80) was generated by immunizing a llama with purified agonist-bound beta2 adrenergic receptor (beta2 AR) reconstituted into phospholipid vesicles. Nb80 was selected because it exhibits G protein-like behaviour: it binds preferentially to agonist-occupied beta2 AR, increases agonist affinity by 95-fold, and stabilizes an active-state conformation. Nb80 binds to the cytoplasmic end of beta2 AR via its CDR3 loop, which projects into the core of the receptor and occupies a position analogous to the C-terminal peptide of Galpha s. The Nb80-stabilized active state structure (PDB: 3NY7, 3.5 A) represents the first active-state crystal structure of a diffusible-ligand GPCR. In another study, a nanobody was selected that specifically targets the matrix-open state (m-state) of the Thermothelomyces thermophila ADP/ATP carrier locked by bongkrekic acid, stabilizing the otherwise unstable m-state in detergent solution and enabling crystallization.
