# GDP-Fucose

## Overview

GDP-fucose (guanosine 5'-diphosphate-[L-Fucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/l-fucose/)) is an activated sugar donor used in fucosylation reactions. It is the substrate for the human GDP-fucose transporter [SLC35C1 Human GDP-Fucose Transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/slc35c1/), which imports it into the Golgi apparatus. In competition assays with [Vrg4 GDP-Mannose Transporter (Saccharomyces cerevisiae)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/vrg4/), GDP-fucose was recognized but showed lower apparent affinity compared to [GDP-Mannose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/substrates/gdp-mannose/), reflecting the different sugar specificity of the mannose transporter. Fucose transport is fundamental for pathogenic fungi and parasites where it contributes to surface glycocalyx formation.
