# GDP-Mannose

## Overview

GDP-mannose (guanosine 5'-diphosphate-alpha-D-mannose) is an activated sugar donor used in glycosylation reactions. It is the primary substrate for the GDP-mannose transporter [Vrg4 GDP-Mannose Transporter (Saccharomyces cerevisiae)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/vrg4/), which imports it into the lumen of the Golgi apparatus where glycosyltransferases use it as a mannose donor for N- and O-linked glycosylation. [Vrg4 GDP-Mannose Transporter (Saccharomyces cerevisiae)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/vrg4/) displays higher apparent affinity for GDP-mannose compared to [GDP-Fucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/substrates/gdp-fucose/) or GDP-[Glucose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/glucose/), supporting specific recognition of the mannose moiety.
