# L-Aspartate

## Overview

L-Aspartate is an acidic amino acid and a canonical agonist for ionotropic glutamate receptors (iGluRs). It binds to the ligand-binding domain (LBD) in a similar mode to glutamate, with the alpha-carboxyl and alpha-amino groups making equivalent contacts. The shorter side chain (beta-carboxyl instead of gamma-carboxyl) means it does not engage the domain 2 arginine residues (Arg676, Arg702) that coordinate the glutamate gamma-carboxyl group.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to AvGluR1 LBD

The alpha-carboxyl group forms a bidentate salt bridge with Arg522 in domain 1. The alpha-amino group is bound by Asp720, Asp515 main chain carbonyl, and Thr517 hydroxyl in domain 1. The beta-carboxyl group interacts with Arg676, which adopts two conformations. In the aspartate complex, the Trp4 residue (W4) moves into the position occupied by one of the gamma-carboxyl group oxygen atoms in the glutamate complex. The LBD structures of glutamate and aspartate complexes are nearly identical (rmsd 0.29 A for 242 C-alpha atoms).

- **Pocket volume**: Comparable to glutamate complex
- **Key residues**: Arg522 (domain 1), Asp720 (domain 2), Arg676 (domain 2, two conformations), Thr517 (domain 1)
