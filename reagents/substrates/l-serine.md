# L-Serine

## Overview

L-Serine is a polar neutral amino acid that binds to the AvGluR1 ligand-binding domain (LBD) with micromolar affinity. Like alanine, it utilizes a chloride ion as a surrogate for the gamma-carboxyl group of glutamate, but with a distinct binding geometry where the serine hydroxyl group forms hydrogen bonds with the binding pocket.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to AvGluR1 LBD

The alpha-amino and alpha-carboxyl groups make identical interactions to the glutamate complex. The chloride ion is displaced by 2.3 A from its position in the alanine complex and is coordinated by both Arg702 and Arg676 side chains and the main-chain amide of Asp720. The serine hydroxyl group forms hydrogen bonds with W3 and the side chains of Asp720 and Arg676. Water structure differs from the alanine complex due to movement of W3, which now forms hydrogen bonds linking W2 and W5. Removal of chloride lowers serine affinity 19-fold.

- **Pocket volume**: 302 +/- 1.9 A3, identical to glutamate complex
- **Key residues**: Arg522 (domain 1), Asp720 (domain 2), Arg676 and Arg702 (domain 2, chloride coordination), Cl- ion (surrogate gamma-carboxyl)
