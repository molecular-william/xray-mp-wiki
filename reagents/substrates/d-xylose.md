# D-Xylose

## Overview

D-Xylose is an aldopentose (five-carbon sugar) that serves as the natural substrate for the Escherichia coli sugar transporter XylE. It binds to XylE in the outward-facing conformation through eight hydrogen bonds with polar residues in the substrate-binding site. D-Xylose is transported by XylE but D-glucose, despite binding similarly, is not a transport substrate and acts as a competitive inhibitor.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: D-xylose
- **Chemical formula**: C5H10O5
- **Molecular weight**: 150.13 g/mol
- **Class**: aldopentose

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Substrate for sugar transporter XylE

D-Xylose is the natural substrate for XylE, an E. coli homologue of human glucose transporters GLUT1-4. It was used at 20 mM for crystallization of the XylE-D-xylose complex structure at 2.8 A resolution. D-Xylose binds in the centre of the transmembrane domain, coordinated by eight hydrogen bonds with polar residues Gln 168, Gln 288, Gln 289, Asn 294, Trp 392, Gln 415, plus water-mediated bonds with Tyr 298 and Gln 415.
