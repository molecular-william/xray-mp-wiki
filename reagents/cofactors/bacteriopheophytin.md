# Bacteriopheophytin

## Overview

Bacteriopheophytin (BPhe) is a chlorin pigment found in photosynthetic reaction centres,
formed by the removal of the central magnesium ion from bacteriochlorophyll (BChl). In
bacterial reaction centres, BPhe serves as the primary electron acceptor in the productive
electron transfer chain, accepting an electron from the photoexcited special pair of BChl
molecules. BPhe is found at the HA and HB sites in the reaction centre core, with only the
A-branch BPhe participating in productive electron transfer.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Rhodobacter sphaeroides Reaction Centre

BPhe molecules are tightly bound at the HA and HB cofactor sites in the reaction centre core. In wild-type RC, the photoexcited special pair transfers an electron to BA (accessory BChl), then to HA (BPhe) within approximately 3 ps. From HA, the electron proceeds to QA (primary quinone acceptor) within approximately 200 ps. The HB-site BPhe is structurally essential but does not participate in productive electron transfer.

- **Key residues**: Histidine ligands coordinate the peripheral positions of the chlorin macrocycle
