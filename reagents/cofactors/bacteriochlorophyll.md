# Bacteriochlorophyll

## Overview

Bacteriochlorophyll (BChl) is a magnesium-coordinated chlorin pigment found in all
photosynthetic bacteria. It serves as the primary light-harvesting and electron-transfer
cofactor in photosynthetic reaction centres. BChl molecules absorb light in the near-infrared
region and are essential components of the photosynthetic apparatus in purple bacteria,
green sulfur bacteria, and anoxygenic phototrophs. Zinc-substituted BChl (Zn-BChl) has been
used as a spectroscopic probe to study coordination state effects on electron transfer in
reaction centres.

## Binding Mode

### Binding to Rhodobacter sphaeroides Reaction Centre

Bacteriochlorophyll molecules are tightly bound within the reaction centre protein core at multiple cofactor sites: the special pair (P), the accessory bacteriochlorophyll (BA), the primary bacteriopheophytin (HA), and the secondary bacteriopheophytin (HB). In the A-branch, electron transfer proceeds through BA and HA (both BChl or Zn-BChl). The B-branch contains symmetrically arranged BA and HA cofactors that are not used for productive electron transfer but are structurally essential.

- **Key residues**: Histidine ligands coordinate central metal ion at multiple cofactor sites
