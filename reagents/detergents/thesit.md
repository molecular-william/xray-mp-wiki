# Thesit (Polyoxyethylene 9-dodecyl ether / C12E9)

## Overview

Thesit (polyoxyethylene 9-dodecyl ether), also known as C12E9 or dodecyl nonaoxyethylene ether, is a nonionic surfactant consisting of a dodecyl (C12) alkyl chain attached to nine units of ethylene oxide (E9). It is used for the solubilization of membrane proteins from lipid bilayers and for maintaining membrane proteins in detergent-solubilized form during purification. Thesit has been successfully employed for the extraction, purification, and crystallization of the nitrate reductase A (NarGHI) complex from E. coli, and for the solubilization and crystallization of the mouse 5-HT3A serotonin receptor.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Polyoxyethylene 9-dodecyl ether
- **Molecular weight**: 583 Da
- **Class**: detergent-nonionic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Detergent extraction and purification of respiratory enzymes

Thesit at 0.7 mM was used for detergent extraction of nitrate reductase A (NarGHI) from E. coli membranes. The enzyme was purified in 0.7 mM Thesit through two anion exchange chromatography steps (DEAE-Sepharose) and gel filtration (Superdex 200). Crystals of NarGHI were grown in the presence of 0.7 mM Thesit, yielding diffraction to 1.9 Å resolution.


### Crystallization of Cys-loop receptors

Thesit (C12E9) at 1% was used for the solubilization of the mouse 5-HT3A serotonin receptor from T-REX-293 cell membranes, and at 0.02% in the purification buffer for streptactin affinity chromatography and size-exclusion chromatography. The purified receptor was subsequently crystallized in complex with nanobody VHH15, yielding diffraction to 3.5 Å resolution.


### Membrane protein crystallization medium

Thesit was used as the detergent for crystallization of the NarGHI heterotrimer. Native and selenomethionine-substituted crystals were obtained by sitting-drop vapor diffusion with 0.7 mM Thesit in the protein sample. For heavy-atom derivatization, crystals were soaked in cryoprotectant containing 0.7 mM Thesit.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Effective for both initial membrane solubilization and maintaining protein stability through multi-step purification (ion exchange and size exclusion)
- Compatible with high-resolution crystallography; allowed diffraction to 1.9 Å for the NarGHI heterotrimer
- Relatively low CMC (0.1 mM) allows use at low concentrations (0.7 mM), reducing detergent interference in crystallization
- Mild nonionic character preserves protein complex integrity during membrane extraction, as demonstrated by the intact NarGHI heterotrimer

### Disadvantages

- Less widely characterized for membrane protein crystallography compared to alkyl glucosides or maltosides
- May not be suitable for all membrane protein classes; successful application has been documented primarily for bacterial respiratory complexes and Cys-loop receptors
- Polyoxyethylene head group can produce heterogeneous micelle sizes, potentially complicating crystallization optimization
