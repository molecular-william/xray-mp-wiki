# LDAO (Lauryldimethylamine N-Oxide)

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: Zwitterionic detergent
- **Alkyl chain**: 12 carbons (lauryl)
- **Headgroup**: Dimethylamine N-oxide

## Uses in Membrane Protein Work

LDAO is a zwitterionic detergent historically used in early membrane protein crystallization. It is more disruptive than non-ionic detergents but can be useful for certain applications.

### Bovine Rhodopsin (P3₁ crystal form)

- **Concentration**: 0.05% added to C8E4-solubilised rhodopsin
- **Role**: Critical additive for obtaining isotropically diffracting crystals
- **Binding site**: Wrapped around the Pro267-Tyr268 kink in helix 6, just to the extracellular side
- **Conformation**: Kinked at C5 atom; amine oxide arm points along H6 toward extracellular face; hydrocarbon chain wraps around the kink
- **Structural function**: Stabilizes the structure around the potential hinge in H6; the kink in LDAO mirrors the kink in the protein
- **Key finding**: The addition of 0.05% LDAO to C8E4-solubilised rhodopsin was the key to obtaining the P3₁ crystal form at 2.65 Å resolution

## Proteins Using LDAO (from this wiki)

- [rps-viridis-reaction-center](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rps-viridis-reaction-center/) — one LDAO molecule resolved in the 2.3 Å crystal structure
  - Head group at detergent region edge
  - Hydrocarbon chain points to interior of detergent region
  - Second LDAO tentatively modeled at subunit L/M/H interface
- [bovine-rhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/bovine-rhodopsin/) — 0.05% LDAO added to C8E4-solubilised rhodopsin; critical for obtaining isotropically diffracting P3₁ crystals at 2.65 Å
  - Single ordered LDAO molecule bound at extracellular side of H6 kink (Pro267-Tyr268)
  - LDAO kinked at C5, mirroring the protein kink
- [bovine-rhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/bovine-rhodopsin/) (recombinant N2C/D282C) — 0.05% LDAO in sitting drop crystallization (1.2–1.7 M Li₂SO₄, 0.1 M Hepes pH 7.5); used with COS-1 expressed protein
