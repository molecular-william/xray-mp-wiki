# Lauryldimethylamine N-oxide (LDAO)

## Overview

Lauryldimethylamine N-oxide (LDAO) is a zwitterionic detergent with a 12-carbon hydrocarbon chain commonly used for solubilizing and crystallizing membrane proteins. In the [Rps. viridis Photosynthetic Reaction Centre](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rps-viridis-reaction-centre/) crystal structure, LDAO was found to be the only detergent molecule well-ordered in the crystal lattice. LDAO is also widely used for functional assays and proteoliposome reconstitution studies.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural detergent in membrane protein crystals

LDAO was identified in the electron density of the [Rps. viridis Photosynthetic Reaction Centre](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rps-viridis-reaction-centre/) crystal structure at 2.3 A resolution. Two LDAO molecules were found: one well-ordered molecule with its head group at the detergent region edge and hydrocarbon chain pointing inward, and a second partially ordered molecule with a nine-carbon chain at the L-M-H subunit interface. The head group of the ordered LDAO is positioned at the edge of the detergent region determined by neutron diffraction experiments.


### Crystallization detergent

Used at 0.023% (w/v) in combination with 0.2% beta-NG for GadC crystallization. The low concentration of LDAO was critical for obtaining rod-shaped crystals in space group P2_12_12_1 that diffracted to 2.95 A resolution.
