# Lauryldimethylamine N-oxide (LDAO)

## Overview

Lauryldimethylamine N-oxide (LDAO) is a zwitterionic detergent with a 12-carbon hydrocarbon chain commonly used for solubilizing and crystallizing membrane proteins. In the Rps. viridis reaction centre crystal structure, LDAO was found to be the only detergent molecule well-ordered in the crystal lattice.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Structural detergent in membrane protein crystals

LDAO was identified in the electron density of the Rps. viridis reaction centre crystal structure at 2.3 A resolution. Two LDAO molecules were found: one well-ordered molecule with its head group at the detergent region edge and hydrocarbon chain pointing inward, and a second partially ordered molecule with a nine-carbon chain at the L-M-H subunit interface. The head group of the ordered LDAO is positioned at the edge of the detergent region determined by neutron diffraction experiments.
