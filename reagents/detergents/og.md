# n-Octyl beta-D-glucopyranoside (OG)

## Overview

n-Octyl beta-D-glucopyranoside (OG) is a mild nonionic detergent with an 8-carbon alkyl chain and a single glucose head group. It is commonly used for solubilizing membrane proteins and GPCRs. OG can enter deep hydrophobic binding pockets of membrane proteins and has been shown to bind in the orthosteric ligand-binding pocket of [Opsin (Retinal-Free Rhodopsin)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/opsin/), stabilizing the active conformation.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: n-octyl beta-D-glucopyranoside
- **Chemical formula**: C14H28O6
- **Molecular weight**: 296.37 g/mol
- **Class**: nonionic alkyl glucoside
- **CMC**: 20-28 mM
- **HLB**: ~13
- **Head group**: glucose (single)
- **Tail length**: 8 carbons

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### GPCR solubilization and crystallization

OG is used to solubilize [Opsin (Retinal-Free Rhodopsin)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/opsin/) from retinal disc membranes at pH 5.6, promoting formation of the active Ops* conformation. A single OG molecule was found bound in the retinal-binding pocket of Ops* at 2.65 A resolution, replacing retinal and forming a defined hydrogen-bond network with multiple hydroxy groups. This binding stabilizes the active G-protein-interacting state of the receptor.


### Ligand-binding pocket probe

OG and related alkyl glucosides (NG, HpG) can occupy the retinal-binding pocket of [Opsin (Retinal-Free Rhodopsin)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/opsin/) in a chain-length-dependent manner, with maximal inhibition of reconstitution observed above the critical micellar concentration. This property makes OG useful for probing hydrophobic binding pockets of GPCRs and other membrane proteins.

### Crystallization medium for HiTehA anion channel

beta-Octylglucoside was used as the detergent medium for crystallizing HiTehA, the bacterial homologue of plant SLAC1 anion channel. Crystals grew in space group R3 at 4 degrees C from protein solubilized in beta-octylglucoside, using PEG600 or PEG400 as precipitant, across a pH range of 5.2 to 10.2. The structure was solved at 1.20 A resolution and includes four detergent molecules in the asymmetric unit.
