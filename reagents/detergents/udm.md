# n-Undecyl-beta-D-maltoside (UDM)

## Overview

n-Undecyl-beta-D-maltoside ([UDM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/udm/)) is a non-ionic maltoside detergent with an undecyl (C11) hydrocarbon tail and a [Maltose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/maltose/) headgroup. It is slightly shorter than the more common n-dodecyl-beta-D-maltoside ([DDM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/ddm/), C12). [UDM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/udm/) was used together with [DDM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/ddm/) in the crystallization of the E. hirae V-ATPase K ring (NtpK10 ring). The [Maltose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/maltose/) ring was resolved for [UDM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/udm/) molecules at crystal contact sites, while the undecyl moieties were visible as elongated features but the [Maltose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/maltose/) moieties at other sites were disordered.
