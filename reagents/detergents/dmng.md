# Decyl Maltose Neopentyl Glycol (DMng)

## Overview

Decyl [Maltose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/maltose/) neopentyl glycol (DMng) is a non-ionic detergent derived from the [Maltose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/maltose/) neopentyl glycol (MNG) family of amphipols. It features a decyl (C10) hydrophobic tail and a neopentyl glycol linker connecting to a [Maltose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/maltose/) head group. DMng provides mild membrane solubilization while maintaining protein stability, making it suitable for crystallization of membrane proteins. It behaves similarly to other MNG derivatives such as [LMNG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/lmng/) and MAng.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: decyl-beta-D-maltopyranoside-2-hydroxy-2,2-bis(hydroxymethyl)propanoate
- **Class**: non-ionic detergent ([Maltose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/maltose/) neopentyl glycol family)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein solubilization and crystallization

OsNIP2;1 from rice behaves well in DMng detergent, allowing preparation of well-diffracting crystals. The truncated construct (residues 38-264) was purified in DMng and crystallized using the MemGold H11 condition.
