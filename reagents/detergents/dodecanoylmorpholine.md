# Dodecanoylmorpholine (DM)

## Overview

Dodecanoylmorpholine (DM) is a nonionic surfactant commonly used as a mild detergent for solubilizing and purifying membrane proteins. It is particularly valued for preserving the native oligomeric state of membrane proteins during purification and is widely used in ion channel studies.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: dodecanoylmorpholine
- **Class**: detergent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein solubilization and purification

DM was used to solubilize and purify the NaK nonselective cation channel from Bacillus cereus. All channel proteins used in flux assays were purified in DM detergent. The protein was reconstituted into lipid vesicles composed of POPE and POPG using 10 mM DM to solubilize the lipid, with detergent removed by dialysis.
