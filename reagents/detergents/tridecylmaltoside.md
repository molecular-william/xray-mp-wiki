# Tridecyl Maltoside (TDM)

## Overview

Tridecyl maltoside (TDM) is a mild nonionic detergent with a 13-carbon alkyl chain and a [Maltose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/maltose/) head group. It is an alkyl maltoside detergent used for solubilizing membrane proteins. TDM has a longer hydrophobic tail than dodecyl maltoside ([DDM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/ddm/)), giving it slightly different solubilization properties and critical micelle concentration.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: tridecyl-beta-D-maltopyranoside
- **Class**: nonionic alkyl maltoside

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Rhodopsin reconstitution compatibility test

In [OPSIN](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/opsin/) crystallization studies, TDM was tested for its ability to compete with [11-cis-Retinal](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/11-cis-retinal/) for binding in the retinal-binding pocket. TDM showed almost no inhibitory effect on rhodopsin reconstitution, suggesting it does not enter the retinal-binding pocket due to its longer alkyl chain. This property makes TDM suitable for solubilization without interfering with ligand binding in olfactory receptor studies.
