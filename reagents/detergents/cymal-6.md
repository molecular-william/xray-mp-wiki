# Cymal-6 (6-Cyclohexyl-1-hexyl-beta-D-maltoside)

## Overview

Cymal-6 (6-cyclohexyl-1-hexyl-beta-D-maltoside) is a nonionic detergent in the Cymal family characterized by a cyclohexyl-modified hexyl tail and a maltoside head group. It is milder than conventional alkyl maltosides and is particularly useful for membrane protein crystallization, often used in combination with other detergents such as CHAPSO to optimize crystal formation. Cymal-6 maintains the enzymatic activity of membrane proteins such as the lipid A phosphoethanolamine transferase NmEptA and the ABC transporter AlgM1M2SS, making it suitable for both functional studies and structural biology.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 6-Cyclohexyl-1-hexyl-beta-D-maltoside
- **Molecular weight**: 508.6 g/mol
- **Class**: detergent-nonionic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### ABC transporter crystallization

Cymal-6 was used at 0.06% as the primary detergent for ATPase activity assays of the ABC transporter AlgM1M2SS from Sphingomonas sp. A1, and at 0.045% combined with 0.25% CHAPSO for crystallization of the SeMet-AlgM1M2SS/AlgQ2 complex. The structure was solved at 3.2 Å resolution in an inward-facing conformation.


### Conformational flexibility studies of membrane enzymes

Cymal-6 was used alongside DDM and Fos-choline-12 to study the conformational states of the full-length lipid A phosphoethanolamine transferase NmEptA from Neisseria meningitidis. The enzyme in Cymal-6 retained PEA hydrolysis activity (confirmed by NBD-PEA TLC assay), showed higher resistance to proteolytic cleavage, and exhibited a fluorescence emission maximum (336 nm) consistent with a more compact, active conformation.


### GPCR crystallization

Cymal-6 was used as an additive at 0.56 mM for the crystallization of the mouse serotonin 5-HT3 receptor in complex with nanobody VHH15. Crystals were grown by vapor diffusion in hanging drops with PEG 10000 as precipitant at 12 °C, yielding diffraction to 3.5 Å resolution.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Milder than conventional alkyl maltosides such as DDM, maintaining enzymatic activity of membrane proteins during purification and functional assays
- Effective for crystallization of membrane protein complexes, particularly in combination with zwitterionic detergents like CHAPSO
- Lower CMC (~0.028 mM) than short-chain detergents, allowing use at lower concentrations for crystallization
- Supports productive conformational states in multi-domain membrane enzymes as shown by limited proteolysis and fluorescence studies

### Disadvantages

- Higher cost compared to conventional detergents like DDM
- May not solubilize all membrane protein complexes; some proteins require harsher detergents for initial extraction
- Forms relatively small micelles that may not accommodate very large multi-protein complexes
