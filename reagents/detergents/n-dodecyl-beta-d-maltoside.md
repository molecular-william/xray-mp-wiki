# n-Dodecyl-beta-D-Maltoside (DDM)

## Overview

n-Dodecyl-beta-D-maltoside (DDM) is a mild nonionic detergent widely used in membrane protein purification and crystallization. It was used to solubilize and crystallize TmYidC, enabling the first structure with the N-terminal amphipathic helix resolved. The shorter-chain decyl maltoside (DM) was noted as harsher and possibly disrupting these flexible regions.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: n-Dodecyl-beta-D-maltoside
- **Molecular weight**: 510.6 g/mol
- **Class**: detergent-nonionic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### TmYidC crystallization

DDM was used as the detergent for TmYidC purification and crystallization. The structure revealed that DDM preserved the N-AH and C1 regions, unlike DM which had been used in previous TmYidC structures where these regions were not visible.


### TmYidC crystallization and structure determination

DDM was used as the detergent for crystallizing Thermotoga maritima YidC (TmYidC), enabling the first structure with the N-terminal amphipathic helix (N-AH) resolved. The milder nature of DDM compared to DM (decyl maltoside) was essential for preserving the N-AH and C1 regions, which were disordered in previous DM-solubilized structures.


### hGPR40 receptor extraction

DDM was used at 1% (w/v) for membrane solubilization of the hGPR40 receptor (free fatty acid receptor 1) expressed in Sf9 insect cells. After initial extraction, the detergent was exchanged to LMNG during IMAC purification for subsequent LCP crystallization.


### OX2 orexin receptor extraction

DDM was used at 1% (w/v) for solubilization of the human OX2 orexin receptor from Sf9 insect cell membranes, together with 0.2% sodium cholate and 0.2% CHS as co-detergents, enabling purification and subsequent LCP crystallization of the receptor bound to suvorexant.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Milder than shorter-chain maltoside detergents like DM, preserving flexible protein regions during crystallization
- Well-established for GPCR and transporter extraction from insect cell membranes at 1% (w/v)
- Long C12 chain provides excellent hydrophobic matching with membrane protein transmembrane domains

### Disadvantages

- Low CMC (~0.17 mM) makes DDM difficult to remove by dialysis, requiring exchange to other detergents for crystallization
- Forms relatively large micelles that may not be suitable for all crystallization methods
