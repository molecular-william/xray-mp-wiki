# Foscholine-12

## Overview

Foscholine-12 is a nonionic detergent from the Fc (foscholine) series developed by Anatrace for membrane protein solubilization and purification. It is a phospholipid- based detergent that combines a phosphocholine head group with fatty acid tails, providing mild solubilization properties that help maintain membrane proteins in native-like conformations during purification and crystallization.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Foscholine-12
- **Class**: phosphocholine nonionic detergent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### FlaK preflagellin peptidase solubilization

1% (w/v) foscholine-12 used for solubilization of cytoplasmic membranes containing His-tagged [FLAK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/flak/) from M. maripaludis expressed in E. coli Rosetta 2 (DE3) cells. The membrane suspension was solubilized by adding foscholine-12 powder directly, and the detergent was maintained at 0.1% throughout subsequent purification steps including [TALON](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/talon/) [Affinity Chromatography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/purification/affinity-chromatography/) and size-exclusion chromatography on [Superdex 200](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/superdex-200/).


### Probing conformational states of NmEptA

Fos-choline-12 was used alongside [DDM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/ddm/) and [Cymal-6](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/cymal-6/) in conformational studies of NmEptA, a [Lipid A](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/lipid-a/) phosphoethanolamine transferase. Unlike [DDM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/ddm/) and [Cymal-6](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/cymal-6/), NmEptA purified in FC-12 failed to show catalytic activity in NBD-PEA TLC assays. [Limited Proteolysis](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/purification/limited-proteolysis/) and intrinsic tryptophan fluorescence revealed that FC-12-purified enzyme adopts a distinct conformational state with higher solvent exposure of tryptophan residues, suggesting FC-12 stabilizes a non-productive conformation.
