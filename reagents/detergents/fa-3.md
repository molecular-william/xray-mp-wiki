# FA-3 (Facade-EM Facial Amphiphile)

## Overview

FA-3 (Facade-EM, 3alpha-hydroxy-7alpha,12alpha-di-((O-beta-D-maltosyl)-2-hydroxyethoxy)-cholane) is a facial amphiphile designed for membrane protein stabilization and crystallization. Unlike conventional detergents that form micelles, facial amphiphiles present a hydrophobic face and a hydrophilic face, providing a more membrane-like environment. FA-3 was essential for obtaining well-diffracting crystals of MsbA at 2.8 A resolution and maintained MsbA ATPase activity comparable to lipid bilayer conditions (~200 nmol/min/mg). It provided superior thermal stability (Tm ~57 C) compared to conventional detergents like UDM or LMNG.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 3alpha-hydroxy-7alpha,12alpha-di-((O-beta-D-maltosyl)-2-hydroxyethoxy)-cholane
- **Class**: Facial amphiphile (bile acid-derived)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein crystallization

FA-3 was used at 0.04 wt% (0.36 mM) for MsbA crystallization. The detergent was exchanged from UDM to FA-3 using centrifugal filters. Co-crystallization with lipid A and FA-3 enabled the 2.8 A structure of MsbA in an inward-facing conformation. FA-3 lacks the dissolving power of phospholipids for lipid A, which may help retain lipid A at binding sites in the crystal.
