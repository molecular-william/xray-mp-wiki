# Octyltetraoxyethylene (C8E4)

## Overview

Octyltetraoxyethylene (C8E4, n-octyltetraoxyethylene) is a nonionic detergent with an 8-carbon alkyl chain and a tetraethylene glycol head group. It is used for solubilizing membrane proteins and is particularly notable in rhodopsin structural biology, where it serves as the primary solubilization and crystallization detergent. C8E4 can bind in the orthosteric ligand-binding pocket of GPCRs and has been observed to fill crystal packing crevices between stacked helix bundles.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Rhodopsin crystallization

C8E4 is the primary detergent used for solubilizing and crystallizing bovine rhodopsin. In the P31 crystal form, C8E4 molecules are intercalated in tapered crevices between rhodopsin helix bundles, filling space together with palmitoylate chains from Cys322 and Cys323. The choice of C8E4 and its interactions precipitated the P31 crystal form.


### Thermal stability studies

C8E4 is used for thermal stability measurements of rhodopsin mutants. The N2C/D282C mutant shows increased thermal stability in C8E4 compared to native rhodopsin. Detergent exchange from DDM to C8E4 is performed via Sephadex G50 column before stability measurements.
