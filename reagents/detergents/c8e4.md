# Octyltetraoxyethylene (C8E4)

## Overview

Octyltetraoxyethylene (C8E4, n-octyltetraoxyethylene) is a nonionic detergent with an 8-carbon alkyl chain and a tetraethylene glycol head group. It is used for solubilizing membrane proteins and is particularly notable in [Bovine Rhodopsin (Dark State)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/bovine-rhodopsin/) structural biology, where it serves as the primary solubilization and crystallization detergent. C8E4 can bind in the orthosteric ligand-binding pocket of GPCRs and has been observed to fill crystal packing crevices between stacked helix bundles.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Octyltetraoxyethylene (C8E4)
- **Molecular weight**: 306.4 g/mol
- **Class**: detergent-nonionic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Rhodopsin crystallization

C8E4 is the primary detergent used for solubilizing and crystallizing [Bovine Rhodopsin (Dark State)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/bovine-rhodopsin/). In the P31 crystal form, C8E4 molecules are intercalated in tapered crevices between [Bovine Rhodopsin (Dark State)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/bovine-rhodopsin/) helix bundles, filling space together with palmitoylate chains from Cys322 and Cys323. The choice of C8E4 and its interactions precipitated the P31 crystal form.


### Thermal stability studies

C8E4 is used for thermal stability measurements of [Rhodopsin N2C/D282C Mutant](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/rhodopsin-n2c-d282c-mutant/). The [Rhodopsin N2C/D282C Mutant](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/rhodopsin-n2c-d282c-mutant/) shows increased thermal stability in C8E4 compared to native [Bovine Rhodopsin (Dark State)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/bovine-rhodopsin/). Detergent exchange from DDM to C8E4 is performed via Sephadex G50 column before stability measurements.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Mild nonionic detergent that preserves GPCR structure, particularly useful for rhodopsin
- High CMC (~8.5 mM) makes it easily removable by dialysis (dialyzable)
- Short alkyl chain (C8) reduces denaturing tendency compared to longer-chain detergents
- Forms small micelles that can pack into crystal lattice crevices, aiding crystal formation

### Disadvantages

- High CMC means relatively high concentrations needed for solubilization
- Short chain length may not stabilize all membrane protein targets as effectively as longer detergents like DDM
- May require detergent exchange from a milder, longer-chain detergent (e.g., DDM) before use
