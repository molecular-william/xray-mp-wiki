# n-Nonyl-beta-D-maltopyranoside (NM)

## Overview

n-Nonyl-beta-D-maltopyranoside (NM) is a nonionic maltoside detergent with a 9-carbon alkyl chain and a disaccharide maltose headgroup. It is widely used for membrane protein purification due to its mild solubilization properties and ability to maintain protein stability during chromatographic steps. NM is commonly used as a replacement for the harsher solubilization detergents (e.g., beta-OG) during affinity and size-exclusion chromatography. It is used for solubilization of membrane protein complexes, including the wtDsbB-DsbA-Q8 complex from Escherichia coli. NM is structurally related to other maltoside detergents such as DDM (n-dodecyl-beta-D-maltopyranoside) and hexylmaltoside, with a shorter alkyl chain that confers different solubilization properties.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein SEC purification

NM is used at 0.56% (w/v) in size-exclusion chromatography buffers for PTS transporter purification. It was used in the elution buffer and SEC step following Ni-NTA purification of pmUlaA, where it replaced DDM used during initial solubilization.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Milder than shorter-chain alkyl maltosides
- Suitable for SEC of membrane proteins after initial DDM extraction
- Forms relatively small micelles

### Disadvantages

- Less commonly used than DDM or LMNG, less characterized
- Nonyl (C9) chain may not be optimal for all membrane proteins
