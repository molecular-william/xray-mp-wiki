# n-Nonyl-beta-D-maltopyranoside (NM)

## Overview

n-Nonyl-beta-D-maltopyranoside (NM) is a nonionic maltoside detergent with a 9-carbon alkyl chain and a disaccharide maltose headgroup. It is widely used for membrane protein purification due to its mild solubilization properties and ability to maintain protein stability during chromatographic steps. NM is commonly used as a replacement for the harsher solubilization detergents (e.g., beta-OG) during affinity and size-exclusion chromatography. It is used for solubilization of membrane protein complexes, including the wtDsbB-DsbA-Q8 complex from Escherichia coli. NM is structurally related to other maltoside detergents such as DDM (n-dodecyl-beta-D-maltopyranoside) and hexylmaltoside, with a shorter alkyl chain that confers different solubilization properties.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: n-Nonyl-beta-D-maltopyranoside
- **Molecular weight**: ~468.5 g/mol

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein SEC purification

NM is used at 0.56% (w/v) in size-exclusion chromatography buffers for PTS transporter purification. It was used in the elution buffer and SEC step following Ni-NTA purification of pmUlaA, where it replaced DDM used during initial solubilization.


### Purification of Glu-GABA antiporter GadC

> NM at 0.4% (w/v) is used in wash and elution buffers during Ni-NTA affinity purification of E. coli GadC, following initial membrane solubilization with beta-OG. NM maintains GadC stability during subsequent gel filtration chromatography, enabling structural studies of the inward-open conformation.


### Purification of Na+/H+ antiporter NapA

> NM at 0.5-0.6% (w/v) is used for gel filtration and final buffer exchange during NapA purification from Thermus thermophilus. NM was selected after detergent screening showed NapA stability in a wide range of detergents including NM, allowing crystallization of the outward-facing active state at pH 7.8.


### Solubilization of DsbB-DsbA charge-transfer complex

> NM (nonylmaltoside) is used to solubilize the kinetically stable wtDsbB-DsbA(C33Ala)-Q8 charge-transfer complex from E. coli membranes. The complex remains stable in NM during gel filtration purification and subsequent crystallization, enabling structural characterization of the disulfide bond formation intermediate.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Milder than shorter-chain alkyl maltosides
- Suitable for SEC of membrane proteins after initial DDM extraction
- Forms relatively small micelles
- Shorter alkyl chain (C9) provides different solubilization properties compared to longer maltosides like DDM (C12) or UDM (C11)
- Useful as a replacement for harsher detergents (e.g., beta-OG) during affinity and size-exclusion chromatography steps
- Effective for both purification and crystallization of diverse membrane protein targets including transporters, channels, and enzyme complexes
- Compatible with structural biology techniques including X-ray crystallography at moderate to high resolutions

### Disadvantages

- Less commonly used than DDM or LMNG, less characterized
- Nonyl (C9) chain may not be optimal for all membrane proteins
- Higher CMC (~6 mM) compared to longer-chain maltosides, requiring higher detergent concentrations in buffers
- May not provide sufficient stabilization for larger or more fragile membrane protein complexes
- Limited structural characterization data compared to DDM or LMNG
