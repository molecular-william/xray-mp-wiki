# Digitonin

## Overview

Digitonin is a non-ionic steroidal glycoside detergent extracted from the seeds of *Digitalis purpurea* (foxglove). It is a relatively mild detergent used for solubilizing membrane proteins, particularly for cryo-EM sample preparation where its gentle properties help maintain protein stability and monodispersity.

## Use in NaVAb Resting-State Structure

Digitonin (High Purity, MilliporeSigma) was used for the cryo-EM sample preparation of the MBP-NaVAb/KAV/G94C/Q150C resting-state complex (Wisedchaisri et al., 2019, Cell):

- **Function**: Solubilized the channel complex for cryo-EM grid preparation
- **Resolution achieved**: 4.0 Å overall; 3.0–3.9 Å in pore module
- **Advantage**: Digitonin's mild nature preserves the native conformation of the channel complex, critical for capturing the functionally relevant resting state

## Properties

- **Class**: Non-ionic steroidal glycoside
- **Source**: Digitalis purpurea (foxglove) seeds
- **CMC**: Very low (~0.015%)
- **Molecular weight**: ~1,256 Da (mixture of glycosides)
- **Advantages**: Mild solubilization, preserves protein-protein interactions, suitable for cryo-EM
- **Disadvantages**: Heterogeneous mixture, batch-to-batch variability, limited solubility
