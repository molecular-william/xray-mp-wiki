# Cymal-4

## Overview

Cymal-4 (4-(1-cyclohexylbutoxy)-3-cyclohexyl-1-propanol) is a nonionic detergent in the Cymal family characterized by a cyclohexyl head group and a butyloxy tail. It is used for solubilizing membrane proteins and is particularly effective for crystallization of membrane protein complexes. Cymal-4 molecules bind nonspecifically to hydrophobic surfaces of membrane protein complexes, mimicking the outer membrane leaflet.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein crystallization

Cymal-4 was used as the primary detergent for crystallization of the Spirulina platensis c15 ring from proton-dependent ATP synthase. The protein sample was prepared at 1.8 mg/mL in 2.5% Cymal-4 solution in water, and crystals grew by vapor diffusion against a reservoir containing [PEG 600](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/peg-600/), [Lithium Sulfate](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/lithium-sulfate/), and sodium acetate buffer at pH 4.3. Cymal-4 molecules bind to the hydrophobic surfaces of the c15 ring, with 15 molecules per ring mimicking the outer membrane leaflet and forming a belt around the outer perimeter.
