# Maltose Neopentyl Glycol 3-C8 (MNG-3-C8)

## Overview

MNG-3-C8 is a [Maltose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/maltose/) neopentyl glycol (MNG) detergent variant featuring an octyl (C8) hydrophobic tail. It belongs to the MNG family of amphipathic detergents that combine a [Maltose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/maltose/) head group with a neopentyl glycol linker and a hydrophobic alkyl chain. MNG-3-C8 was identified through solution NMR screening as the detergent that uniquely stabilizes the Inward_closed conformational state of the [Influenza A M2 Proton Channel](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/influenza-a-m2-proton-channel/), enabling the first crystallization of the S31N mutant in this state. Its longer hydrophobic tail compared to shorter-chain MNG variants contributes to its unique ability to stabilize specific conformational states of membrane proteins.


## Properties

- **Class**: non-ionic detergent ([Maltose](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/maltose/) neopentyl glycol family)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Stabilization of M2 proton channel conformational states

MNG-3-C8 was identified as the detergent that selectively stabilizes the Inward_closed conformation of the [Influenza A M2 Proton Channel](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/influenza-a-m2-proton-channel/) in solution NMR experiments. This property enabled the crystallization of the S31N mutant in the Inward_closed state for the first time using LCP crystallization with MNG-3-C8 as an additive. In contrast, shorter-chain MNG variants and other detergents favored the Inward_open conformation.
