# LAPAO

## Overview

LAPAO (3-laurylamido-N,N'-dimethylpropylaminoxyde) is a zwitterionic detergent used for extracting the [Bovine ADP/ATP Carrier](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/bovine-adp-atp-carrier/) from heart muscle mitochondria. Used at 2% (w/v) during extraction, it preserves the protein-inhibitor complex with carboxyatractyloside and maintains endogenous cardiolipin binding critical for structural integrity.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 3-Lauramidopropyl-N,N-dimethylamine oxide
- **Molecular weight**: ~320 g/mol
- **Class**: detergent-zwitterionic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Extraction of mitochondrial ADP/ATP carrier

> LAPAO at 2% (w/v) is used for extracting the bovine ADP/ATP carrier (AAC) from heart muscle mitochondria. It preserves the carboxyatractyloside (CATR)-inhibitor complex and maintains endogenous cardiolipin binding, which is critical for structural integrity and dimerization of the carrier.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Mild zwitterionic detergent that preserves native protein-lipid interactions, including cardiolipin binding critical for mitochondrial carrier structure
- Effective for extracting conformationally sensitive protein-inhibitor complexes (e.g., AAC-CATR) without disrupting binding
- Widely used for mitochondrial carrier family proteins, with demonstrated compatibility with crystallization

### Disadvantages

- Higher cost compared to commonly used detergents like DDM or OG
- Not as broadly applicable as zwitterionic detergents like LDAO or CHAPS — primarily established for mitochondrial carrier family proteins
- Higher CMC (~1.1 mM) than low-CMC detergents, requiring higher concentration for solubilization
