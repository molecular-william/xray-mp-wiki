# Decylphosphocholine (DPC)

## Overview

Decylphosphocholine ([DPC](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/dodecylphosphocholine/)) is a zwitterionic detergent commonly used in NMR studies of membrane proteins. It features a decyl (C10) hydrophobic tail linked to a phosphocholine head group. [DPC](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/dodecylphosphocholine/) was one of several detergents screened by NMR to characterize the conformational equilibrium of the [Influenza A M2 Proton Channel](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/influenza-a-m2-proton-channel/). In [DPC](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/dodecylphosphocholine/), the inhibitor WJ10 was shown to almost fully shift the Inward_open conformation to the Inward_closed state.


## Properties

- **Molecular weight**: 335.5 g/mol
- **Class**: zwitterionic detergent (phosphocholine)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### NMR studies of M2 conformational states

[DPC](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/dodecylphosphocholine/) was used in solution NMR experiments to screen detergents for their effects on the conformational equilibrium of the [Influenza A M2 Proton Channel](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/influenza-a-m2-proton-channel/). In [DPC](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/dodecylphosphocholine/), the small molecule inhibitor WJ10 was shown to almost fully shift the protein from the Inward_open to the Inward_closed conformation, demonstrating the ability of certain inhibitors to lock the channel in a specific state.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Enables clear NMR discrimination between Inward_open and Inward_closed conformations of the M2 proton channel — the Trp41 indole proton peak shifts from ~10.2 ppm to ~11.0 ppm between states
- Supports functional inhibitor binding studies — the small molecule WJ10 almost fully shifts the M2 conformational equilibrium to the Inward_closed state in DPC
- Zwitterionic headgroup mimics natural phospholipids, providing a membrane-mimetic environment suitable for solution NMR of transmembrane peptides and proteins

### Disadvantages

- Short C10 chain may not stabilize larger membrane protein complexes as effectively as longer-chain phosphocholine detergents
- Zwitterionic nature can interfere with some biochemical assays and may not be suitable for crystallization of all membrane protein targets
