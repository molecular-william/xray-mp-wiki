# Octaethylene Glycol Monododecyl Ether (C12E8)

## Overview

Octaethylene glycol monododecyl ether (C12E8) is a nonionic detergent with a 12-carbon alkyl chain and a polyethylene glycol head group consisting of eight ethylene oxide units. C12E8 is milder than short-chain ethoxylated detergents and is commonly used for solubilizing and stabilizing membrane proteins during purification and crystallization. It is particularly effective for size-exclusion chromatography (SEC) due to its low micelle size and compatibility with SEC resins.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### SEC buffer detergent for membrane protein purification

C12E8 at 0.02% (w/v) was used in the size-exclusion chromatography buffer for hSCD1 purification. The buffer contained 25 mM Tris pH 7.2, 200 mM NaCl, 1 mM TCEP, and 0.02% C12E8. C12E8 was used in the final SEC step rather than DDM to reduce the detergent micelle size, improving SEC resolution and yielding a more monodisperse sample for crystallization.
