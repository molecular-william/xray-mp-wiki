# Anzergent 3,12

## Overview

Anzergent 3,12 is a mild zwitterionic detergent used for solubilizing membrane proteins. It was used at 1% concentration for extracting full-length [KirBac Potassium Channels](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kirbac-potassium-channels/) from E. coli cell membranes, enabling crystallization of multiple [KirBac Potassium Channels](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kirbac-potassium-channels/) channel structures at resolutions between 2.6 and 4.2 A.

## Properties

- **Chemical name**: 3-(Dodecyldimethylammonio)propanesulfonate
- **Molecular weight**: ~335 g/mol
- **Class**: detergent-zwitterionic

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein solubilization

> Anzergent 3-12 (C12-APS) is a zwitterionic sulfobetaine detergent used for solubilizing membrane proteins from biological membranes. It is effective for extracting full-length KirBac potassium channels from E. coli cell membranes at 1% concentration, enabling crystallization at resolutions between 2.6 and 4.2 Å.

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Mild zwitterionic detergent with low denaturing tendency, preserving native protein conformation during extraction and purification
- Zwitterionic head group combines properties of both ionic and nonionic detergents, providing effective solubilization without excessive charge interactions
- Sulfobetaine group is stable over a wide pH range, making it compatible with diverse buffer conditions

### Disadvantages

- More expensive than commonly used detergents like DDM or OG
- Higher CMC (~1-3 mM) compared to low-CMC detergents, requiring higher concentrations for solubilization
- Not as extensively characterized for membrane protein crystallization as traditional detergents
