# Dodecanoyl Sucrose

## Overview

Dodecanoyl sucrose is a sucrose-based nonionic detergent used for solubilizing and crystallizing membrane proteins. It is a glycolipid detergent derived from sucrose esterified with lauric acid (dodecanoic acid). It was used for crystallization of the inhibitor-bound AcrB trimer in this study, yielding C2 crystals solved at 3.05 A resolution.


## Properties

- **Class**: sucrose-based glycolipid detergent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### AcrB crystallization

Dodecanoyl sucrose was used for crystallization of the ABI-PP-bound AcrB trimer. Crystals grew in C2 space group and were solved at 3.05 A resolution. This enabled the first inhibitor-bound structure of AcrB, revealing the pyridopyrimidine derivative ABI-PP bound in the hydrophobic trap of the distal drug-binding pocket.
