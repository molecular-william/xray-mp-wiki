# n-Dodecyl-beta-D-maltopyranoside (beta-DDM)

## Overview

n-Dodecyl-beta-D-maltopyranoside (beta-DDM) is a mild nonionic detergent with a 12-carbon
alkyl chain and a maltose head group, chemically identical to DDM. It is widely used for
solubilizing membrane protein complexes from thylakoid membranes while maintaining protein
activity and complex integrity. beta-DDM is particularly effective for photosynthetic
supercomplexes including PSI-LHCI, where it preserves the intact supercomplex structure
during purification and crystallization.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: n-dodecyl-beta-D-maltopyranoside
- **Chemical formula**: C26H48O12
- **Molecular weight**: 532.65 g/mol
- **Class**: nonionic alkyl maltoside

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Photosynthetic supercomplex solubilization

beta-DDM is used for solubilizing PSI-LHCI supercomplexes from Chlamydomonas reinhardtii thylakoid membranes. The mild detergent preserves the intact PSI-8LHCI supercomplex structure, allowing isolation of complexes with 4-10 LHCI antenna complexes bound.


### Cryoprotection in X-ray crystallography

beta-DDM (0.05% w/v) is included in the cryoprotection solution for PSI-LHCI crystals to maintain protein stability during freezing. The detergent prevents crystal damage during the cryoprotectant exchange procedure.
