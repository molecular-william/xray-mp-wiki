# OGNG (Octyl Glucose Neopentyl Glycol)

## Overview

OGNG (octyl glucose neopentyl glycol) is a non-ionic neopentyl glycol detergent with an 8-carbon alkyl chain. It is the octyl-chain analogue of LMNG (lauryl maltose neopentyl glycol, 12C chain). Like other neopentyl glycol detergents, it has a bulky dual-headgroup structure that makes it extremely mild.

## Chemical Properties

- **Class**: Non-ionic detergent (neopentyl glycol family)
- **Structure**: Dual-headgroup detergent with two sugar moieties and two hydrocarbon chains
- **Alkyl chain**: 8 carbons (octyl)
- **Head group**: Glucose + neopentyl glycol spacer
- **Relation**: Octyl-chain analogue of LMNG

## Properties

- **Mildness**: Extremely mild — bulkier head group prevents penetration of protein binding pockets
- **CMC**: Very low (neopentyl glycol detergents have lower CMC than standard alkyl glucosides)
- **Effect on reconstitution**: Minimal inhibitory effect on rhodopsin reconstitution, suggesting it cannot enter the retinal-binding pocket

## Proteins Using OGNG (from this wiki)

- [opsin-gpcr](//xray-mp-wiki/proteins/opsin-gpcr/) — Tested in detergent reconstitution inhibition assays alongside LMNG; showed minimal effect on rhodopsin reconstitution, consistent with its bulky structure preventing entry into the retinal-binding pocket
