# HpG (Heptylglucoside)

## Overview

Heptylglucoside (HpG, n-heptyl β-D-glucopyranoside) is a short-chain alkyl glucoside detergent with a C7 hydrocarbon chain. It is the shortest-chain detergent tested in the [[opsin-gpcr]] detergent screen, used to probe chain-length-dependent effects on membrane protein structure.

## Properties

- **Chain length**: C7 (heptyl)
- **Type**: Non-ionic alkyl glucoside
- **CMC**: Higher than [[og]] and [[ng]] due to shortest chain
- **Use**: Detergent screening, solubilization

## Proteins Using HpG (from this wiki)

- [[opsin-gpcr]] — HpG tested alongside [[og]] and [[ng]] in detergent screening
  - Inhibition of ligand-binding pocket occupation: HpG < [[ng]] < OG (chain-length dependent)
  - Shortest chain tested, showed minimal effect on pocket occupation
  - Demonstrated that hydrophobic interaction strength correlates with chain length

## Related Detergents

- [[og]] — Octylglucoside (C8), used to solubilize [[opsin-gpcr]]
- [[ng]] — Nonylglucoside (C9), intermediate chain length
- [[ddm]] — Dodecyl-β-D-maltopyranoside (C12), much longer chain
- [[lmng]] — Lauryl Maltose Neopentyl Glycol (C12), ultra-mild
