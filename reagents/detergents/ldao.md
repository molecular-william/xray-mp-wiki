# n-Dodecyl-N,N-dimethylamine-N-oxide (LDAO)

## Overview

Lauryldimethylamine N-oxide (LDAO) is a zwitterionic detergent with a 12-carbon hydrocarbon chain commonly used for solubilizing and crystallizing membrane proteins. In the [Rps. viridis Photosynthetic Reaction Centre](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/rps-viridis-reaction-centre/) crystal structure, LDAO was found to be the only detergent molecule well-ordered in the crystal lattice. LDAO is also widely used for functional assays and proteoliposome reconstitution studies.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: N,N-dimethyldodecylamine N-oxide
- **Chemical formula**: C14H31NO
- **Molecular weight**: 229.4 g/mol
- **Class**: detergent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Final dialysis buffer for KcsA potassium channel

[KCSA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kcsa/) from Streptomyces lividans was exchanged into 5 mM [LDAO](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/ldao/) by dialysis
following cobalt affinity and gel filtration purification. Crystals were grown
from this LDAO-containing protein solution.


### Membrane protein solubilization

Used in combination with [Nonyl-beta-D-glucopyranoside](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/nonylglucoside/) for purification of
E. coli ecPgpB phosphatidylglycerophosphate phosphatase B for X-ray crystallography.
