# NG (Nonylglucoside)

## Overview

Nonylglucoside (NG, n-nonyl β-D-glucopyranoside) is a short-chain alkyl glucoside detergent used in membrane protein solubilization and crystallization. Like OG (octylglucoside), it is a mild non-ionic detergent with a C9 hydrocarbon chain.

## Properties

- **Chain length**: C9 (nonyl)
- **Type**: Non-ionic alkyl glucoside
- **CMC**: Higher than [og](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/og/) due to shorter chain
- **Use**: Solubilization, crystallization, detergent screening

## Proteins Using NG (from this wiki)

- [opsin-gpcr](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/opsin-gpcr/) — NG tested alongside [og](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/og/) and [hpg](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/hpg/) in detergent screening experiments
  - Inhibition of ligand-binding pocket occupation depended on hydrocarbon chain length and concentration
  - Maximal inhibition observed at longer chain lengths (NG < [og](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/og/) < longer detergents)
  - Used to probe the specificity of detergent occupancy in the [opsin-gpcr](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/opsin-gpcr/) retinal-binding pocket
