# Heptylglucoside (HpG)

## Overview

Heptylglucoside (HpG, n-heptyl beta-D-glucopyranoside) is a nonionic detergent with a 7-carbon alkyl chain and a glucose headgroup. In the opsin study, HpG was tested for its ability to occupy the retinal-binding pocket of the active Ops* conformation. HpG showed concentration-dependent inhibition of rhodopsin reconstitution (critical micellar concentration ~70 mM), indicating it can enter the binding pocket and form hydrogen bonds with multiple hydroxy groups, providing a model for how hydrophobic odorants enter olfactory receptors.
