# n-Undecyl-β-D-maltoside (UDM)

## Overview

n-Undecyl-β-D-maltoside (UDM) is a nonionic detergent with an 11-carbon alkyl chain and a maltoside head group, closely related to n-dodecyl-β-D-maltoside (DDM) but with one fewer carbon in the alkyl chain. UDM was used as the crystallization detergent for the human connexin 26 (Cx26) gap junction channel structure determined at 3.5 Å resolution (Maeda et al., 2009). After purification in DDM, Cx26 was exchanged into UDM (0.01%) for size-exclusion chromatography and crystallization, likely because the shorter chain of UDM produces smaller micelles that may improve crystal packing.

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Crystallization detergent for Cx26 gap junction channel

UDM at 0.01% was used as the detergent for size-exclusion chromatography and crystallization of human connexin 26. The protein was purified initially in DDM, then exchanged into UDM for the final purification step and crystallization setup (10 mM HEPES pH 7.5, 200 mM NaCl, 2 mM DTT, 0.01% UDM).

## Advantages and Disadvantages

### Advantages

- Shorter C11 alkyl chain produces smaller micelles compared to DDM (C12), which can improve crystal packing of membrane proteins
- Closely related to DDM but with different micelle properties that may be beneficial for crystallization of specific targets

### Disadvantages

- Higher CMC (~0.59 mM) compared to DDM (~0.17 mM), requiring more detergent to maintain micellar concentration
- May be less stabilizing than DDM for some membrane proteins due to shorter hydrophobic tail
