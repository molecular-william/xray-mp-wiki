# Heptylthioglucoside (HpG)

## Overview

Heptylthioglucoside (HpG) is a mild nonionic detergent with a 7-carbon alkyl chain and a thioglucose head group. Used as the sole detergent for solubilizing and crystallizing bovine rhodopsin at 2.2 A resolution, replacing the previous mixed micelle system of nonylglucoside and heptanetriol. HpG crystallization conditions substantially reduced the ammonium sulfate concentration needed and significantly reduced merohedral twinning.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: heptyl beta-D-thioglucopyranoside
- **Chemical formula**: C13H26O6S
- **Molecular weight**: 306.41 g/mol
- **Class**: nonionic alkyl thioglucoside
- **Head group**: thioglucose (single)
- **Tail length**: 7 carbons (heptyl)

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Rhodopsin solubilization and crystallization

HpG replaced the mixed micelle solvent (nonylglucoside + heptanetriol) for rhodopsin purification and crystallization. This change allowed ammonium sulfate concentration to be substantially decreased, making crystal manipulation easier. The probability of finding crystals with twin fraction < 0.2 increased from 5% to 50%. Two HpG molecules were observed in the 2.2 A structure, indicating approximate range of the transmembrane domain.
