# Lauryl Maltose Neopentyl Glycol (LMNG)

## Overview

LMNG (lauryl maltose neopentyl glycol, also known as MNG or MNG-3) is a nonionic amphipathic detergent derived from maltose and a neopentyl glycol headgroup with a C12 lauryl (dodecyl) alkyl chain. It is widely used for solubilization and purification of membrane proteins. LMNG provides mild detergent conditions that help maintain membrane protein stability and monodispersity, often outperforming conventional maltoside detergents like DDM. It is particularly effective in combination with cholesterol hemisuccinate (CHS) for stabilizing GPCR-class receptors during purification and crystallization. LMNG is particularly valuable for crystallizing difficult membrane proteins, including GPCRs in active conformations.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: Dodecyl beta-D-maltopyranoside neopentyl glycol
- **Chemical formula**: C26H48O11
- **Molecular weight**: 528.6 g/mol
- **Class**: nonionic detergent
