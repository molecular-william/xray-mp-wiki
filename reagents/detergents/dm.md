# DM (Decylmaltoside)

## Chemical Properties

- **Full name**: n-decyl-β-D-maltoside (DM)
- **Class**: Nonionic maltoside detergent
- **Micelle formation**: Critical micelle concentration (CMC) ~0.17 mM
- **Chain length**: 10-carbon alkyl chain (shorter than DDM's 12-carbon chain)
- **Head group**: Maltose (disaccharide)

## Properties

- **Mildness**: Shorter alkyl chain makes DM milder than DDM
- **Solubilization efficiency**: Less efficient than DDM for membrane protein solubilization
- **Steric compatibility**: Smaller head group and shorter chain fit better in sterically constrained pockets (e.g., retinal-binding pockets in rhodopsin)
- **Detergent strength**: Weaker than DDM, may not fully solubilize all membrane proteins

## Use Cases

- **Rhodopsin crystallization**: DM fits best sterically in the retinal pocket, preserving native conformation
- **Mild solubilization**: Preferred when preserving protein-ligand interactions is critical
- **Crystallography**: Sometimes used in crystallization screens alongside DDM

## Proteins Using DM (from this wiki)

- [opsin-gpcr](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/opsin-gpcr/) — DM tested in reconstitution inhibition assays; showed strong concentration-dependent inhibition, fits best sterically in retinal-binding pocket
  - **Verification**: Raw paper (10.1002/anie.201302374) confirms: "Decylmaltoside (DM) showed strong concentration-dependent inhibition of reconstitution... DM has a similar size as retinal and seems to fit best into the retinal-binding pocket"
