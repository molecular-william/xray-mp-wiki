# Decylmaltoside (DM)

## Overview

Decylmaltoside (DM, n-decyl beta-D-maltopyranoside) is a nonionic detergent with a 10-carbon alkyl chain and a maltose headgroup. In the opsin study, DM showed the strongest concentration-dependent inhibition of rhodopsin reconstitution among all tested detergents, suggesting it fits best into the retinal-binding pocket due to its similar size to retinal. This indicates DM can enter the orthosteric binding pocket and serve as a structural probe for ligand recognition in GPCRs. DM was also used in the bcMalT study for EIIC domain solubilization and crystallization. It is commonly used for solubilizing and purifying membrane proteins while preserving their native structure and function. Its moderate detergent strength makes it suitable for exchanging proteins from harsher detergents into cleaner buffers for downstream applications such as crystallization.


## Properties

- **Chemical name**: n-decyl beta-D-maltopyranoside
- **Chemical formula**: C22H42O11
- **Class**: nonionic detergent

## Use in Membrane Protein Work

### Membrane protein buffer exchange

Decyl maltoside is used for buffer exchange during membrane protein purification. Its moderate detergent strength makes it suitable for exchanging proteins from harsher detergents (e.g., digitonin/cholate mixtures) into cleaner buffers for downstream applications such as crystallization.


### Membrane protein purification

DM is used throughout membrane protein purification workflows for maintaining solubility of transmembrane proteins. Its mild nonionic nature helps preserve protein-protein interactions and complex integrity during purification.


### Solubilization of BhuUV and BhuUV-T from membranes

DM at 2% (w/v) was used to solubilize the BhuUV and BhuUV-T complexes from the membrane fraction of E. coli cells. The membrane fraction was solubilized in 50 mM Tris-HCl pH 7.5, 150 mM NaCl with 2% DM for 1 h at 4 C. The final purification buffer contained 0.02% DM.
