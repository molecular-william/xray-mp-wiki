# GPCR Inactive Conformation

## Overview
The inactive conformation of G-protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs), also known as the R state, is characterized by a specific arrangement of transmembrane helices that prevents G-protein coupling. Key structural features include the absence of the 'ionic lock' salt bridge in some GPCRs, the position of the conserved DRY motif, and the conformation of cytoplasmic loops. Antagonist and inverse agonist ligands bind preferentially to the inactive state, stabilizing it. The transition from the inactive to the active (R*) state involves a 5-6 A outward movement of helix 6 and tightening of the ligand-binding pocket.
