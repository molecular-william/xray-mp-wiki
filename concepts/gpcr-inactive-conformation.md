# GPCR Inactive Conformation

## Overview
The inactive conformation of G-protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs), also known as the R state, is characterized by a specific arrangement of transmembrane helices that prevents G-protein coupling. Key structural features include the absence of the 'ionic lock' salt bridge in some GPCRs, the position of the conserved DRY motif, and the conformation of cytoplasmic loops. Antagonist and inverse agonist ligands bind preferentially to the inactive state, stabilizing it. The transition from the inactive to the active (R*) state involves a 5-6 A outward movement of helix 6 and tightening of the ligand-binding pocket.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- CC Chemokine Receptor 2 with T4 Lysozyme Fusion ([CCR2-T4L](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/ccr2-t4l/)) — Double-antagonist-bound CCR2 (with [BMS-681](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/bms-681/) and CCR2-RA-[R]) represents the most inactive GPCR conformation solved to date. Conserved microswitch residues show inactive-state signatures: R138^3.50 (DRY motif) interacts with D137^3.49 and T77^2.39; Y305^7.53 (NPxxY motif) is twisted outward toward TM2; helix VII is repositioned in inactive conformation; intracellular ends of TM3 and TM6 are close together.
- Turkey Beta1-Adrenergic Receptor Thermostabilized Mutant M23 with [Cyanopindolol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/cyanopindolol/) (2VT4) — The 2.7 A crystal structure of beta1AR-M23 in complex with [Cyanopindolol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/cyanopindolol/) represents the inactive R conformation. The structure lacks the ionic lock (Arg3.50-Glu6.30 salt bridge), demonstrating that it is not essential for the inactive state. A short alpha-helix in CL2 interacts with the DRY motif via Tyr149-Asp138 hydrogen bonding.
- Human Adenosine A2A Receptor ([A2AR](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-adenosine-a2a-receptor-a2ar/)) — [Fab2838 Antibody Fragment](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibodies/fab2838/) complex — The structure of [A2AR](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-adenosine-a2a-receptor-a2ar/) in complex with [Fab2838 Antibody Fragment](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibodies/fab2838/) (PDB 3PKK, 3.0 A) reveals an allosteric inverse-agonist antibody that locks the receptor in an inactive conformation. [Fab2838 Antibody Fragment](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibodies/fab2838/) CDR-H3 penetrates deeply into the intracellular pocket formed by helices II, III, VI and VII, stabilizing a modified ionic lock between R102(3.50) and E228(6.30). Unlike Nb80 and G-protein C-terminal peptides that bind similar pockets in active GPCRs, [Fab2838 Antibody Fragment](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibodies/fab2838/) binds ~6 A closer to helices II and VII, forcing helices III and VI together and preventing agonist-induced conformational changes in the extracellular binding pocket.
- Human Beta2-Adrenergic Receptor with T4 Lysozyme Fusion (beta2AR-[T4L](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/protein-tags/t4-lysozyme/)) — Multiple beta2AR structures in complex with inverse agonists and antagonists ([ICI 118,551](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ici-118-551/), [Alprenolol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/alprenolol/), compound 2) represent the inactive conformation. The conserved hydroxy-amine motif of beta-blockers forms hydrogen bonds with Ser193 and Ser197 in helix 5.
- Rhodopsin — Dark-state rhodopsin represents the inactive conformation of a class A GPCR. The ionic lock residues are within hydrogen bonding distance but show large B-factor differences, suggesting the lock may not be a stable feature.
- [Human NPY Y2 Receptor (Y2R)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/human-y2-receptor-npy2r/) — [JNJ-31020028](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/jnj-31020028/) complex — The 2.8 A crystal structure of human Y2R bound to the selective antagonist JNJ-31020028 (PDB 7DDZ) represents the inactive antagonist-bound conformation of the NPY receptor family. The interactions between the antagonist and residues W281(6.48) and H285(6.52) at the bottom of the binding pocket constrain helix VI rearrangement, stabilizing the receptor in an inactive state.
