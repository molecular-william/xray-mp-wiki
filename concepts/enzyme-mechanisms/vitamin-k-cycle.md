# Vitamin K Cycle

## Overview
The [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) cycle is a biochemical pathway that recycles [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) between its reduced (hydroquinone) and oxidized (epoxide) forms. The cycle involves two key enzymes: gamma-glutamyl carboxylase (GGCX), which uses [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) hydroquinone as a cofactor to carboxylate glutamate residues on vitamin K-dependent proteins (producing [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) epoxide as a byproduct), and [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) epoxide reductase (VKOR), which reduces [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) epoxide back to [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/), completing the cycle. The cycle is essential for blood coagulation and other physiological processes. [Warfarin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/warfarin/) and related anticoagulants disrupt the cycle by inhibiting VKOR, thereby depleting the supply of active [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) hydroquinone.

## Mechanism/Details
The [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) cycle operates as follows: (1) [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) hydroquinone serves as an essential cofactor for GGCX (gamma-glutamyl carboxylase), which catalyzes the carboxylation of glutamate residues on precursor proteins to form gamma-carboxylated glutamate (Gla) residues. This reaction requires CO2 and O2 and produces [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) epoxide as a byproduct. (2) VKOR ([Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) epoxide reductase) reduces the [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) epoxide back to [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/), completing the cycle. The reduction is thought to proceed through a [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) quinol intermediate. (3) [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) is then available for another round of carboxylation by GGCX. The cycle is tightly regulated and can be disrupted by [Warfarin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/warfarin/) and related anticoagulants, which bind to VKOR and prevent the reduction of [Vitamin K](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/vitamin-k/) epoxide. The Synechococcus sp. VKOR structure revealed the active site where [Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) binds and where the CXXC motif cysteines participate in the redox reaction. Mammalian VKOR is postulated to interact with PDI (protein disulfide isomerase) as a redox partner, which transfers electrons to VKOR for the reduction reaction.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Human Vitamin K Epoxide Reductase (HsVKOR)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/hsvkor/) — Human VKOR catalyzes both epoxide and quinone reduction steps of the vitamin K cycle
- [TrVKORL](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/trvkorl/) — Pufferfish VKOR-like protein used to capture all four states of the catalytic cycle through 11 crystal structures
- [VKOR from Synechococcus sp.](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/synechococcus-vkor/) — Bacterial VKOR homolog providing earlier structural framework for understanding the catalytic mechanism
