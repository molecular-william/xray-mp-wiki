# Pyridylpiperazine-based Allosteric Efflux Pump Inhibitors

## Overview
Pyridylpiperazine-based compounds represent a novel class of allosteric efflux pump inhibitors (EPIs) that target RND-type multidrug efflux pumps in Gram-negative bacteria. Unlike previously characterized EPIs that bind in the periplasmic porter domain (e.g., D13-9001, MBX3132), pyridylpiperazines bind to a unique pocket in the transmembrane domain of the [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) L protomer, near the proton relay residues D407, D408, and K940. By forming a salt bridge with D408 and a halogen bond with K940, these inhibitors lock [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) in the L state and prevent the functional catalytic cycle of the pump.


## Mechanism/Details
The pyridylpiperazine inhibitors access the binding pocket from the cytoplasm via a channel exclusively present in the [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) L protomer. Once bound, the protonated piperazine moiety forms a critical salt bridge with D408, while a halogen atom forms a halogen bond with the main-chain carbonyl oxygen of K940. This interaction disrupts the H-bond network of the proton relay (D407, D408, K940, R971, S481, T978), interfering with H+-translocation and preventing the L-to-T protomer transition. Two possible mechanisms are proposed: (1) steric prevention of the L-to-T transition, and (2) interference with the proton motive force utilization.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) multidrug efflux transporter — BDM88855 (compound 9) binds to the transmembrane domain of the [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) L protomer between TM4, TM5, and TM10, with EC90 = 3.4 uM for antibiotic boosting activity. Co-crystal structure solved at 2.6 A (PDB 7OUK).
- [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) multidrug efflux transporter — BDM88832 (compound 8) co-crystallized with AcrB-R971A variant at 2.8 A (PDB 7OUL), confirms iodine-substituted pyridylpiperazine binding at the same TM pocket.
