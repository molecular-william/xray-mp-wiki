# Phosphoenzyme E2P State

## Overview
The E2P state is a phosphorylated intermediate of P-type ATPases in which the enzyme carries a covalently bound aspartyl phosphoanhydride at a conserved aspartate residue in the P domain. In the Na+,K+-ATPase, the E2P state has high affinity for cardiotonic steroids (CTS) such as ouabain, making it a key conformational state for understanding CTS-mediated inhibition and signaling. The E2P state is structurally related to the E2:BeF3- state of SERCA1a at the cytoplasmic domains. Phosphorylation can be induced by ATP (type I phosphoenzyme) or by inorganic phosphate (type II phosphoenzyme).
