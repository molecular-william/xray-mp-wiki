# AAA Protease Mechanism

## Overview
[AAA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/aaa/)+ proteases (ATPases associated with various cellular activities) are a family of
energy-dependent proteolytic machines that couple ATP hydrolysis to protein unfolding and
degradation. They form hexameric ring-like structures with a central pore through which
substrates are translocated. FtsH is a founding member of this family and is unique in
containing both [AAA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/aaa/) and protease domains in the same polypeptide chain. The [AAA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/aaa/) ring
typically exhibits C2 symmetry in the ADP-bound state. Substrates are recognized by
aromatic pore residues (the pore motif Phi-VG), unfolded through ATP-driven conformational
changes, and translocated through the narrow central pore to the internal degradation
compartment where peptide bond hydrolysis occurs at zinc metalloprotease active sites.
The active-site switch beta-strand serves as a docking edge for substrate peptides,
and a conserved [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) in the AAA-protease linker is essential for interdomain
communication and function. The 2.6 A apo-FtsH structure from *Thermotoga maritima* revealed the first high-resolution view of nucleotide-dependent pore residue movements of >45 A, demonstrating C6-symmetric AAA ring opening in the apo-state versus C2-symmetric arrangement in the ADP state, and establishing the active-site switch beta-strand mechanism for substrate fixation.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- FtsH from Aquifex aeolicus — The 2.96 A structure of Delta-AaFtsH (PDB 4ww0) shows ADP-bound C2-symmetric [AAA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/aaa/) ring with closed pore. The active-site switch beta-strand is disordered. T450P mutation in this segment severely impairs proteolytic activity while retaining ATPase activity. The conserved linker [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) G399 is essential for AAA-protease interdomain communication.
- FtsH from Thermotoga maritima — The 2.6 A apo-FtsH structure (K207A Walker A mutant, nucleotide-free) reveals a C6-symmetric AAA ring with open pore configuration featuring Phe-234 pore residues lining an open circular entrance. The active-site switch (residues 450-460) forms a substrate-binding beta-strand in the apo-state. Comparison with the ADP-bound structure (PDB 2ce7) shows >45 A inward movement of aromatic pore residues, generating a model of substrate translocation. The G404L mutation in the linker between AAA and protease domains causes monomeric, inactive protein, demonstrating the essential role of the conserved glycine for hexamerization and interdomain communication.
