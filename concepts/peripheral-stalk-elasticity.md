# Peripheral Stalk Elasticity in A-ATP Synthases

## Overview
The peripheral stalk elasticity model proposes that the peripheral stalks of A-ATP synthases and V-ATPases provide transient elastic energy during the rotary catalytic cycle. Subunit E tethers the peripheral stalk to the A1 headpiece sector and can switch between extended (straight) and curved (bent) conformations, storing and releasing elastic energy during ATP synthesis or hydrolysis.
