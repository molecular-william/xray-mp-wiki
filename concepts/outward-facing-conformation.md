# Outward-Facing Conformation of ABC Transporters

## Overview
The outward-facing conformation is a structural state of ABC transporters in which the transmembrane domain opens toward the periplasm while the cytoplasmic gate is closed. This conformation represents the substrate-receptive state in the transport cycle.

## Mechanism/Details
The outward-facing conformation is a structural state of ABC transporters in which the transmembrane domain opens toward the periplasm (or extracellular space) while the cytoplasmic gate is closed. This conformation represents the substrate-receptive state in the transport cycle, prior to substrate binding and nucleotide-driven conformational changes. The crystal structure of HmuUV from Yersinia pestis (3.0 A, P2_1) revealed an outward-facing conformation in the nucleotide-free state, with a haem translocation pathway cavity accessible from the periplasm but closed to the cytoplasm. This finding was significant because type I ABC importers (e.g., MalFGK) and ABC exporters (e.g., Sav1866) adopt outward-facing conformations only in the ATP-bound state, while inward-facing in the absence of nucleotide. The fact that both HmuUV and the related vitamin B12 transporter BtuCD adopt outward-facing conformations in their apo states suggests that type II ABC importers have a coupling mechanism distinct from other ABC transporters. The outward-facing HmuUV structure has a periplasmic entrance to the translocation pathway formed mainly by TM5 and H5a, with conserved residues Gly164 and Arg176 at key positions in the gating helices.
