# POT Family Substrate Specificity by Electrostatic Interactions

## Overview
Peptide transporters of the proton-dependent oligopeptide transporter (POT)
family (also known as the PTR family) mediate the cellular uptake of di- and
tripeptides. The molecular basis for recognition of charged amino acid side
chains in dipeptides was established by the X-ray crystal structure of [YEPEPT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/yepept/)
from Yersinia enterocolitica. A conserved lysine residue (Lys314 in [YEPEPT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/yepept/)) in
the substrate-binding pocket forms electrostatic interactions with negatively
charged N-terminal amino acid side chains (Asp-Ala, Glu-Ala), defining
substrate specificity. In the apo state, the positive charge of this lysine is
stabilized by a hydrogen bond and pi-cation interaction with a neighboring
phenylalanine (Phe311). Charge reversal mutagenesis (K314E) switches
specificity to positively charged dipeptides (Lys-Ala), while the K314A
mutation abolishes charged dipeptide recognition. This electrostatic gating
mechanism is conserved in human PEPT1, where the Q300K mutation introduces
affinity for Asp-Ala.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [YEPEPT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/yepept/) (Yersinia enterocolitica) — Wild-type [YEPEPT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/yepept/) has Lys314. It shows significant specificity for Asp-Ala (Ki = 82 uM) and Glu-Ala (Ki = 2.4 mM) over Lys-Ala (Ki > 10 mM). The K314E mutant reverses specificity to favor Lys-Ala (Ki = 2.0 mM vs Asp-Ala Ki = 8.9 mM).
- Human PEPT1 (SLC15A1) — Wild-type human PEPT1 has Gln300 at the corresponding position and does not show pronounced affinity for charged dipeptides. The Q300K mutant gains specificity for Asp-Ala.
