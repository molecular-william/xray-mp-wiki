# P-type ATPase Family

## Overview
P-type ATPases are a major family of ion-transporting ATPases that form a covalent aspartyl-phosphate intermediate (hence "P-type") during their reaction cycle. They transport a wide variety of ions across membranes against concentration gradients, using ATP hydrolysis as energy source. P-type ATPases are found in all domains of life and include Ca²⁺-ATPases (SERCA), Na⁺/K⁺-ATPases, H⁺-ATPases (gastric H⁺/K⁺-ATPase), and heavy metal transporters. The cytoplasmic catalytic domain shares a Rossman fold similar to L-2-haloacid dehalogenase (HAD).
