# Self-processing of Membrane Proteins

## Overview
Self-processing refers to the phenomenon where a membrane protein cleaves its own C-terminal or N-terminal regions without the aid of an external protease. This intramolecular or inter-protomer processing can serve regulatory functions, acting as an autoinhibitory mechanism that is relieved upon proper oligomeric assembly. The C-terminus of a self-processing protein may function as a type II intramolecular chaperone or as a competitive inhibitor that regulates proteolytic activity until the enzyme reaches its functional state.
