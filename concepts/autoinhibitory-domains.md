## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [SERCA1a (Sarcoplasmic Reticulum Ca2+-ATPase)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/serca1a/) (Sarcoplasmic Reticulum Ca2+-ATPase 1a) — Small membrane proteins [SLN](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/sarcolipin/) and [PLB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/miscellaneous/phospholamban/) bind to SERCA and inhibit its Ca2+ transport activity by stabilizing the E1 intermediate state without bound Ca2+
- Plasma membrane Ca2+-ATPases — Autoinhibitory domains activated by Ca2+/calmodulin binding; the autoinhibitory domain forms a straight helix that maps to a conserved groove similar to the SLN-binding site on SERCA
- Heavy-metal pumps (P-type ATPases) — P-type ATPases including heavy-metal pumps are controlled by autoregulatory domains that modulate transport activity
