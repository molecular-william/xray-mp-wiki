# ABC Transporter Outward-Occluded Mechanism

## Overview
The outward-occluded conformation of ABC exporters represents an intermediate
state between the outward-open and inward-open conformations in the alternating
access transport cycle. Unlike the outward-open state, where the transmembrane
cavity is accessible from the periplasm, or the inward-open state, where the
cavity opens to the cytoplasm, the outward-occluded state seals both sides of
the membrane. This conformation was first observed in the [MCJD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mcjd/) antibacterial
peptide ABC exporter from E. coli, captured in complex with the ATP analog
[AMP-PNP (Adenylyl Imidodiphosphate)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/amp-pnp/). In [MCJD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mcjd/), the outward-occluded state is stabilized by rotation of TMs 1
and 2 toward the equivalent helices of the opposite monomer, forming a closed
periplasmic gate. The NBDs form a firmly closed dimer when nucleotides are bound,
but disengage in the apo state, as revealed by McjD-ADP-VO4 and McjD-apo
structures. The outward-occluded conformation may represent a universal feature
of the transport cycle of ABC exporters where the substrate is bound prior to
ATP hydrolysis.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [MCJD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mcjd/) ABC exporter — [MCJD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mcjd/) was the first ABC exporter crystallized in an outward-occluded conformation. The AMP-PNP-bound structure shows periplasmic gate closure formed by rotation of TM1/TM2, while NBDs form a closed dimer. The occluded state is confirmed by cysteine cross-linking (L53C) in membranes.
- [Sav1866 Multidrug ABC Transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/sav1866/) ABC exporter — [Sav1866 Multidrug ABC Transporter](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/sav1866/) was previously crystallized in an outward-open state where the periplasmic side is open, in contrast to the [MCJD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/mcjd/) outward-occluded state. The different conformations may correspond to different steps in the transport cycle.
