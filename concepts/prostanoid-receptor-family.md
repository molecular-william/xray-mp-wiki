# Prostanoid Receptor Family

## Overview
Prostanoid receptors are a family of class A G protein-coupled receptors that mediate the physiological actions of prostanoids, including prostaglandins ([PGD2](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/prostaglandin-d2/), PGE2, PGF2alpha, PGI2) and thromboxane A2 (TXA2). There are eight prostanoid receptor subtypes: EP1, EP2, EP3, EP4 (PGE2 receptors), DP1 and [Human CRTH2 (PGD2 Receptor)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/crth2/)/DP2 ([PGD2](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/prostaglandin-d2/) receptors), FP (PGF2alpha receptor), IP (PGI2 receptor), and TP (TXA2 receptor). The EP3 receptor was the first prostanoid receptor to have its crystal structure determined, with two independent structures solved nearly simultaneously: EP3 bound to misoprostol-FA (Audet et al., 2.5 A) and EP3 bound to PGE2 (Morimoto et al., 2.9 A). These structures revealed a completely enclosed ligand-binding pocket, a unique ECL2 lid conformation, and a conserved Arg333(7.40) that forms a critical salt bridge with the ligand carboxylate group common to all prostanoids. More recently, the crystal structure of [Human CRTH2 (PGD2 Receptor)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/crth2/) bound to [15R-methyl-PGD2 (15mPGD2)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/15r-methyl-pgd2/) (PDB 7M8W) was solved by [Serial Femtosecond Crystallography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/serial-femtosecond-crystallography/), revealing a "polar group in" lipid binding mode distinct from the "polar group out" mode of PGE2 in EP3. Structural comparison across prostanoid receptors suggests these two lipid-binding modes may apply to other lipid GPCRs.
