# Intramembrane Proteolysis

## Overview
Intramembrane proteolysis is the cleavage of peptide bonds within the hydrophobic interior of the lipid bilayer by intramembrane proteases. These enzymes cleave transmembrane proteins to release cytoplasmic, luminal, or extracellular domains that move to new locations to carry out biological functions. The first atomic-resolution structure of any intramembrane protease was the E. coli rhomboid GlpG at 2.1 A resolution (Wang et al., 2006, Nature), which revealed how peptide bond scission occurs within the membrane. Rhomboid proteases are a widespread family of intramembrane serine proteases characterized by a core of six transmembrane helices. Time-resolved crystallographic snapshots of GlpG (Cho et al., 2019) subsequently captured the complete catalytic cycle from gate opening to peptide release at atomic resolution, revealing the stepwise mechanism of L5 loop dynamics, tetrahedral intermediate formation, acyl intermediate stabilization, and catalytic resolution.

## Mechanism/Details
Intramembrane proteases include serine, aspartyl, and metallo-proteases, all with multiple TM helices and active-site residues sequestered within distinct TM domains. Rhomboid proteases (such as GlpG from E. coli) contain a catalytic dyad (Ser201/His254) in a water-filled cavity embedded at a depth below the membrane surface. The active site is accessible through a V-shaped lateral opening between helices S1 and S3 that faces the lipid bilayer. Substrate TM proteins diffuse laterally through the bilayer and enter through a gated lateral opening. The L1 loop acts as a membrane-embedded gate, blocking the active site in the resting state. Upon substrate binding, the gate opens, allowing the substrate transmembrane segment to bend and enter the active site. A flexible loop (L5) also caps the active site and must move during catalysis. Water molecules enter the membrane-embedded active site through multiple routes. This lateral gating mechanism differs from the proposed mechanisms of other intramembrane proteases like S2P (metalloprotease) and presenilin/gamma-secretase (aspartyl protease).

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- GlpG (E. coli rhomboid protease) — Prototypical intramembrane serine protease. First atomic-resolution structure of any intramembrane protease (2.1 Å, 2006). Six TM helices with catalytic Ser201/His254 dyad. Structures in lipid (2XTV) and detergent (2XTU) environments reveal conserved architecture, L1 lateral gate, and a three-state gating model.
- Drosophila Rhomboid-1 — First rhomboid discovered, part of EGF receptor signaling pathway. Cleaves Spitz (a TGF-alpha-like precursor) to activate EGF receptor signaling.
- Human presenilin (gamma-secretase) — Aspartyl intramembrane protease; cleavage of APP produces amyloid-beta peptides implicated in Alzheimer's disease.
- Human PARL — Human rhomboid homologue of yeast Pcp1. Regulates cytochrome c release during apoptosis.
