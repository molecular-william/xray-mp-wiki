# Bacterial Pump Oligomerization Classification by Structural Determinants

## Overview
A classification system for bacterial light-driven pumps based on the
correlation between conserved structural determinants (extended helices,
3-omega motif, and flipped B-C loop) and oligomerization state. Three
groups are defined: (1) Archaeal and trimeric eubacterial rhodopsins
lacking both extended helices and the 3-omega motif, which form trimers;
(2) Proteorhodopsins with extended helices but lacking the 3-omega motif
and beta-sheet B-C loop, which form pentamers or hexamers; and (3)
XR-type rhodopsins with extended helices, 3-omega motif, and flipped
B-C loop, which form pentamers.


## Mechanism/Details
The classification emerged from structural comparison of Gloeobacter
rhodopsin (GR) with other microbial rhodopsins. Three key structural
determinants correlate with oligomerization propensity:

**Group 1 (Trimers):** Archaeal rhodopsins (BR, HR, SR, AR) and
trimeric eubacterial rhodopsins (ASR, XeR, MrHR) have relatively short
TM helices A, B and G, lack the 3-omega motif, and have the B-C loop
oriented towards the center of the TM helical bundle. These form
trimers exclusively, mediated mainly by helix B and D interactions.

**Group 2 (Pentamers/Hexamers):** Proteorhodopsins (BPR, GPR, ESR)
have extended helices A, B and G but lack the beta-sheet structure of
the B-C loop and the 3-omega motif. They form pentamers or hexamers
with cross-protomer interactions involving helices A, B and C.

**Group 3 (Pentamers):** XR-type rhodopsins (GR, XR, TR, FR, KR2,
CIR) have extended helices A, B and G, a 3-omega motif tethering
the B-C loop outwards, and a flipped B-C loop with antiparallel
beta-sheet structure. They form pentamers with additional interactions
between the flipped B-C loops of neighboring protomers.

The extension of interface helices (branch 1 in the phylogenetic tree)
was acquired at the Archaea-Bacteria divergence. The 3-omega motif
and flipped B-C loop (branch 2) were acquired later in XR-type
rhodopsins, enabling tighter pentameric organization through
inter-protomer B-C loop contacts. The functional divergence of XR-type
proteins into proton pumps (GR, XR, TR), sodium pumps (KR2), and
chloride pumps (CIR) occurred after these structural acquisitions
(branch 3).


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- GR (Gloeobacter rhodopsin) — Group 3 XR-type proton pump; pentameric at pH 8.0, monomeric at pH 3.0, as shown by DEER spectroscopy
- Bacteriorhodopsin — Group 1 archaeal proton pump; trimeric, lacking extended helices and 3-omega motif
- Blue-absorbing proteorhodopsin (BPR) — Group 2 proteorhodopsin; pentameric (HOT75) or hexameric (Med12) depending on species
- KR2 (Krokinobacter eikastus rhodopsin) — Group 3 XR-type sodium pump; pentameric crystal structure used as template for GR pentamer model
