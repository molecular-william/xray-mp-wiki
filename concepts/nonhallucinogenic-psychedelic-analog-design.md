# Nonhallucinogenic Psychedelic Analog Design

## Overview
Structure-based design of nonhallucinogenic psychedelic analogs represents a therapeutic strategy that targets the serotonin 2A receptor (5-HT2AR) to achieve antidepressant-like effects without hallucinogenic side effects. By targeting the extended binding pocket (EBP) and leveraging β-arrestin-biased agonism, compounds such as IHCH-7079 and IHCH-7086 were designed to activate β-arrestin2 recruitment without Gq-mediated signaling, producing antidepressant-like activity in mouse models without the head-twitch response (HTR) that correlates with hallucinogenic potential.


## Mechanism/Details
The design strategy exploits the discovery that serotonin and psilocin adopt a second binding mode at the extended binding pocket (EBP) of 5-HT2AR, in addition to their canonical binding at the orthosteric binding pocket (OBP). Ligand engagement with TM7, specifically at Y370^7.43 in the EBP, leads to selective β-arrestin signaling activation. Rigid substructures from known antipsychotics (lumateperone, spiperone, pimozide, benperidol) were repurposed to target the EBP without engaging the hydrophobic deep binding pocket responsible for antagonist activity. This yielded IHCH-7113 (weak β-arrestin bias, Ki=758.58 nM), which was further optimized to IHCH-7079 and IHCH-7086 (pure β-arrestin2 partial agonists with no Gq activity). These compounds showed no hallucinogenic-like HTR in mice up to 10 mg/kg, blocked LSD-induced HTR, and demonstrated antidepressant-like effects in both acute restraint stress and corticosterone-induced depression models.
