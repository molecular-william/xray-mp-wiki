# GXGD Proteases

## Overview


## Mechanism/Details
The GXGD proteases are a family of polytopic membrane proteases that share a conserved GXGD sequence motif containing a catalytic aspartyl residue. These proteases cleave membrane-spanning substrates using a pair of essential aspartyl residues positioned at the ends of transmembrane helices. Representative members include preflagellin peptidase (PFP), type 4 prepilin peptidase (TFPP), presenilin, and signal peptide peptidase (SPP). The family spans both prokaryotic and eukaryotic organisms, with important medical implications: mutations in presenilin are associated with Alzheimer's disease, and TFPP contributes to bacterial pathogenesis.
