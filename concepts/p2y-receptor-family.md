# P2Y Receptor Family

## Overview
The P2Y receptors are a family of purinergic G-protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) activated by extracellular nucleotides. There are eight distinct functional P2Y receptors expressed in humans, subdivided into P2Y1-like receptors and P2Y12-like receptors. Their ligands are generally charged molecules with relatively low bioavailability and stability in vivo. P2Y12 receptor regulates platelet activation and thrombus formation, making it a major drug target for antithrombotic therapy. Multiple crystal structures of P2Y receptors have been determined, revealing distinct structural features including a unique straight helix V conformation in P2Y12R.


## Mechanism/Details
P2Y receptors are class A GPCRs that respond to extracellular nucleotide ligands. Upon ligand binding, they activate Gi/o protein signaling pathways that regulate diverse cellular processes including platelet activation (P2Y12), smooth muscle contraction (P2Y1), and sensory transduction (P2Y1, P2Y2, P2Y4, P2Y6). The P2Y12 receptor exhibits high levels of basal activity, which can be modulated by inverse agonists. The receptor family displays distinct structural features, including unique helix V conformations in P2Y12-like receptors (P2Y12, P2Y13, P2Y14) that lack the conserved proline-mediated bend found in most class A GPCRs.
