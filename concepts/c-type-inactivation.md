# C-type Inactivation

## Overview
C-type inactivation is a gating process in potassium channels whereby prolonged depolarization causes the channel to enter a nonconductive state. It is associated with conformational changes at the selectivity filter and the outer vestibule of K+ channels. Unlike N-type inactivation, which involves an intracellular inactivating particle, C-type inactivation occurs in the absence of cytoplasmic domains and is controlled by structural rearrangements along the permeation pathway. It plays a crucial role in controlling firing patterns in excitable cells and determining the duration of the cardiac action potential.
