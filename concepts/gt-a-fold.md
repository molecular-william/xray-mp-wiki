# GT-A Fold (Glycosyltransferase A Fold)

## Overview

The GT-A fold is a structural domain found in glycosyltransferase family A enzymes, which catalyze the transfer of sugar moieties from activated donor molecules (such as UDP-glucose) to acceptor substrates. This fold is characteristic of processive beta-glycosyltransferases, including cellulose synthases (CESAs), chitin synthases, and hyaluronan synthases. The GT-A fold consists of a mixed, seven-stranded beta-sheet surrounded by seven alpha-helices.

## Mechanism/Details

The GT-A fold contains a conserved signature motif D,D,D,Q(Q/R)XRW that includes three variably spaced aspartic acids and a pentapeptide. The first two aspartic acids (e.g., Asp 179 and Asp 246 in BcsA) coordinate the nucleotide diphosphate of the sugar donor (UDP), while the third aspartic acid (e.g., Asp 343 in the invariant TED motif) acts as the catalytic base. The Q(Q/R)XRW sequence, together with a conserved FFCGS pentapeptide, forms a binding site for the terminal disaccharide of the glucan acceptor chain.

In cellulose synthases, the GT-A fold catalyzes the transfer of glucose from UDP-Glc to the non-reducing end of a growing glucan chain, inverting the configuration at the anomeric carbon from alpha to beta to form beta-1,4-glycosidic linkages. The structure of BcsA's GT domain superimposes with the inverting glycosyltransferase SpA from Bacillus subtilis with 2.15 A RMSD between C-alpha atoms.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work

- **BcsA from Rhodobacter sphaeroides** — The GT-A fold of the BcsA cellulose synthase was solved in complex with BcsB at 4.5 A resolution, revealing a cellulose synthase-cellulose translocation intermediate with 18 glucose molecules visible in the electron density. The structure demonstrates how the GT-A fold is integrated with a transmembrane channel for coupled synthesis and translocation.
