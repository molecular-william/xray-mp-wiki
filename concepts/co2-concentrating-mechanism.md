# CO2-Concentrating Mechanism (CCM)

## Overview
The CO2-concentrating mechanism (CCM) is an adaptive system evolved by cyanobacteria and some algae to enhance photosynthetic carbon fixation. It actively transports dissolved inorganic carbon (Ci) — both HCO3- and CO2 — into the cell, elevating the intracellular CO2 concentration around RuBisCO encapsulated in carboxysomes. This improves the efficiency of carbon fixation and reduces photorespiration. The cyanobacterial CCM comprises five distinct Ci uptake systems: three bicarbonate transporters ([SBTA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/sbta/), BicA, and BCT1/CmpABCD) and two CO2 uptake systems (NDH-I3 and NDH-I4). These systems are regulated at transcriptional and post-translational levels to adapt to changing environmental CO2 availability.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [SBTA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/sbta/) (High-Affinity Bicarbonate Transporter) — [SBTA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/sbta/) is a high-affinity, Na+-dependent HCO3- transporter that uses an [Elevator Mechanism](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/elevator-mechanism/) to move bicarbonate across the membrane. It is allosterically inhibited by its regulatory partner [SBTB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/sbtb/) in response to cellular adenyl nucleotide levels (AMP vs cAMP).
- BicA (Medium-Affinity Bicarbonate Transporter) — BicA is a medium-affinity Na+-dependent HCO3- transporter of the SulP family. It works alongside [SBTA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/sbta/) and BCT1 to provide bicarbonate for the CCM.
- BCT1/CmpABCD (ATP-Powered Bicarbonate Transporter) — BCT1 is an ABC-type HCO3- transporter complex powered by ATP hydrolysis. Its expression is induced under Ci limitation.
- NDH-I3 and NDH-I4 (CO2 Uptake Systems) — These NADPH dehydrogenase complexes are responsible for converting CO2 to HCO3- at the cytoplasmic side, effectively serving as CO2 uptake systems. NDH-I3 is induced under low Ci conditions.
