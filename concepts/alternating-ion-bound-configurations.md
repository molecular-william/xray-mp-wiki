# Alternating Ion-Bound Configurations in K+ Channel Selectivity Filters

## Overview
The alternating ion-bound configurations model proposes that a K+ channel's selectivity filter (SF) exists in two isoenergetic arrangements: the 1,3-ion-bound configuration (K+-water-K+-water) and the 2,4-ion-bound configuration (water-K+-water-K+). The interconversion between these configurations is driven by electrostatic repulsion between K+ ions separated by intervening water molecules. The canonical model, based on four K+ ions observed in crystal structures of K+ channels at sites S1-S4, proposes that these alternating configurations underlie the rapid, single-file movement of ions through the selectivity filter.


## Mechanism/Details
In the canonical model, the selectivity filter contains four ion-binding sites (S1-S4, counting from the extracellular side). Two K+ ions and two water molecules alternate within the filter, switching between the 1,3 configuration (ions at S1 and S3, waters at S2 and S4) and the 2,4 configuration (ions at S2 and S4, waters at S1 and S3). Electrostatic repulsion between adjacent K+ ions (separated by ~7.5 A with intervening water) drives the alternating occupancy. In contrast, the direct knock-on model proposes that K+ ions can establish direct contact within the filter, triggering stronger electrostatic repulsion for higher transport rates. The [KCSA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kcsa/) G77A (PDB 6NFU) and G77C (PDB 6NFV) mutant structures selectively stabilize the 2,4-ion-bound configuration, providing decisive structural evidence for the canonical model.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [KCSA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kcsa/) Potassium Channel — [KCSA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kcsa/) G77A (PDB 6NFU, 2.09 A) and G77C (PDB 6NFV, 2.13 A) mutants stabilize the 2,4-ion-bound configuration with K+ at S2 and S4, and water at S1 and S3. The G77A mutant shows wild-type-like K+ selectivity and binding affinity (Kd=0.29 mM) but reduced single-channel conductance (~1.95 pS vs ~63 pS WT).

- [KCSA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kcsa/) Potassium Channel — [KCSA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kcsa/) T75C mutant favors the 1,3-ion-bound configuration (ions at S1 and S3, waters at S2 and S4), demonstrating the energetic coupling between the two alternating configurations.
