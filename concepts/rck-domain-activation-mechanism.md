# RCK Domain Activation Mechanism

## Overview
The RCK (Regulator of K⁺ conductance) domain activation mechanism describes how ligand binding to cytoplasmic RCK domains triggers conformational changes that control the gating of potassium channels and transporters. RCK domains form octameric gating rings that undergo ligand-dependent structural transitions from inactive to activated states, coupling cytoplasmic ligand binding to transmembrane pore opening.


## Mechanism/Details
The RCK domain activation mechanism follows a sequential, multi-step pathway: (1) In the inactive state, the gating ring adopts a closed conformation with the pore occluded. (2) Binding of a cytoplasmic ligand (Ca²⁺, Ba²⁺, or other activators) to the C1 activation site on individual RCK domains induces a local conformational change, transitioning the ring to a partially activated or singly-liganded intermediate state. (3) Additional ligand binding to successive RCK domains drives the ring toward a fully activated conformation, where the gating ring expansion is transmitted through linker regions to the transmembrane pore domain, opening the ion conduction pathway. Ba²⁺ binding studies demonstrate that the C1 site (formed by residues D184 and E210 in MthK) is the primary activation site, with Ba²⁺ selectively binding at C1 while being excluded from other Ca²⁺ sites. The D184N mutation abolishes Ba²⁺ activation, confirming this site's essential role. The Ba²⁺-bound gating ring adopts an intermediate-activated conformation distinct from the fully activated Ca²⁺-bound form, supporting the sequential activation model.
