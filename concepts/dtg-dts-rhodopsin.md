# DTG/DTS Rhodopsin

## Overview
DTG/DTS rhodopsins are a family of light-driven outward proton-pumping microbial rhodopsins that lack a cytoplasmic proton donor residue (homologous to BR Asp96). Instead, they are defined by a DTG (Asp-Thr-Gly) or DTS (Asp-Thr-Ser) three-residue motif. Proton uptake occurs directly from the cytoplasmic solvent to the retinal Schiff base through large hydrophilic cytoplasmic cavities. This family is found in soil and plant-associated bacteria and exhibits pH-dependent pumping activity, making them advantageous for energy production while avoiding intracellular alkalization.

## Mechanism/Details
DTG/DTS rhodopsins are a family of light-driven outward proton-pumping microbial rhodopsins characterized by the absence of a cytoplasmic proton donor residue homologous to BR Asp96. They are defined by a three-residue motif at the position corresponding to BR Asp96: either DTG (Asp-Thr-Gly) in DTG rhodopsins or DTS (Asp-Thr-Ser) in DTS rhodopsins.

Key distinguishing features:
- No cytoplasmic proton donor: Proton directly binds from cytoplasmic solvent to the retinal Schiff base (RSB)
- Conserved histidine at BR Thr46 position (TM2): Proposed to participate in proton uptake, though mutational effects are complex
- Large cytoplasmic cavities: Structural rearrangement creates hydrophilic cavities that connect cytoplasmic surface to RSB region
- Glycine at position homologous to BR Asp96: Enables direct proton uptake but results in pH-dependent activity
- pH-dependent pumping: Activity strongly depends on intracellular pH, unlike DTD/DTE-type pumps which maintain constant activity

Biological significance: DTG/DTS rhodopsins are found mainly in soil and plant-associated bacteria (e.g., Pseudomonas putida, Pantoea vagans, Sphingomonas paucimobilis). Their pH-dependent activity is advantageous in alkaline environments where they can regulate proton pumping to avoid intracellular alkalization. This makes them promising candidates for next-generation optogenetic tools that avoid side effects from strong cell alkalization.
