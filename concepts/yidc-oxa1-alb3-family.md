# YidC/Oxa1/Alb3 Family

## Overview
The YidC/Oxa1/Alb3 family is a conserved family of membrane protein chaperones
and insertases found in bacteria (YidC), mitochondria (Oxa1), and chloroplasts
(Alb3). Family members are characterized by a core of five transmembrane helices
that form a hydrophilic groove, which provides a binding surface for nascent
membrane proteins during their folding and insertion into the lipid bilayer.
These proteins are particularly important for the biogenesis of respiratory chain
complexes and function both independently and in cooperation with the Sec
translocon.


## Mechanism/Details
The five core TM helices (TM2-TM6) are tightly packed in the periplasmic leaflet
but loosely interact in the cytoplasmic leaflet, creating a hydrophilic groove that
is open toward both the cytoplasmic and membrane sides. The groove contains
conserved hydrophilic residues including a critical arginine (Arg366 in EcYidC,
Arg72 in BhYidC) that generates a positively-charged surface. Substrate-contacting
sites cluster in the groove opening and adjacent TM3/TM5 helix surfaces.

In Gram-negative bacteria, YidC includes additional N-terminal elements: the TM1
helix and a periplasmic P1 domain with a β-supersandwich fold. The P1 domain is
connected to the TM region by an amphipathic PH1 helix that lies parallel to the
membrane plane. The P1 domain surface (residues 215-265) provides the interaction
interface with SecF, facilitating formation of the holo-translocon complex
(SecYEG-SecDFYajC-YidC). The YidC structure from B. halodurans (BhYidC, PDB 3WO6,
2.4 Å) and the full-length E. coli YidC (EcYidC, PDB 3WVF, 3.2 Å) revealed that
the hydrophilic groove is the conserved structural and functional feature across
the family.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- E. coli YidC (EcYidC) — The full-length EcYidC structure (PDB 3WVF, 3.2 Å) revealed the orientation of
the P1 domain and the conserved hydrophilic groove, providing structural insight
into the holo-translocon organization and substrate recognition.
- Bacillus halodurans YidC (BhYidC) — The BhYidC structure (PDB 3WO6, 2.4 Å) first revealed the conserved hydrophilic
groove formed by five TM helices, establishing the structural framework for the
YidC/Oxa1/Alb3 family.
