# KCTD-Mediated Modulation of GABA_B Receptor Signaling

## Overview
KCTD (potassium channel tetramerization domain-containing) proteins, specifically KCTD8, KCTD12, KCTD12b, and [KCTD16 (Potassium Channel Tetramerization Domain-Containing Protein 16)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/kctd16/), function as auxiliary subunits of metabotropic GABA_B receptors. They bind directly to the C-terminal tail of the GABA_B2 subunit via their N-terminal T1 oligomerization domain and modify the kinetics of GABA_B receptor signaling, including the rate of GIRK (G protein-activated inwardly rectifying K+) channel activation and receptor desensitization. The crystal structure of the [KCTD16 (Potassium Channel Tetramerization Domain-Containing Protein 16)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/kctd16/) T1 domain bound to a GABA_B2-derived peptide (residues 895-909) revealed that [KCTD16 (Potassium Channel Tetramerization Domain-Containing Protein 16)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/kctd16/) forms an open pentamer that binds a single GABA_B2 peptide within its interior.


## Mechanism/Details
KCTD proteins use their T1 domain (broad-complex, tramtrack, and bric a brac fold) to form oligomeric structures - [KCTD16 (Potassium Channel Tetramerization Domain-Containing Protein 16)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/kctd16/) assembles as an open pentamer. A single GABA_B2 C-terminal peptide binds to the interior of this pentamer, contacting up to four of the five KCTD subunits. The interaction is mediated by conserved residues including F80 (forming a hydrophobic belt), Q34, and E102. KCTD8 and [KCTD16 (Potassium Channel Tetramerization Domain-Containing Protein 16)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/kctd16/) are unique in having two C-terminal homology domains (H1 and H2), where the H2 domain inhibits receptor desensitization. KCTD12 and KCTD12b have a single H1 domain and accelerate both GIRK activation and receptor desensitization. All four KCTDs constitutively bind G proteins through G-beta-gamma subunits.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [KCTD16 (Potassium Channel Tetramerization Domain-Containing Protein 16)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/kctd16/) — [KCTD16 (Potassium Channel Tetramerization Domain-Containing Protein 16)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/gpcr/kctd16/) T1 pentamer binds GABA_B2(895-909) via conserved interface involving F80, Q34, and E102. The D76R mutation disrupts pentamer formation and abolishes functional modulation of GIRK activation.

- Human GABA_B Receptor — The GABA_B2 C-terminal tail (residues 895-909) is sufficient for KCTD binding. Mutations in this region (I898A, Y903A, L904A) disrupt both biochemical association and GIRK channel modulation.
