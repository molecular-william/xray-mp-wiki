# Sodium Channel Gating and Ion Selectivity

## Overview
Voltage-gated sodium channels (NaV) belong to the voltage-gated ion channel (VGIC) superfamily and share structural principles with voltage-gated calcium channels (CaV). Bacterial NaV channels (BacNav) are modular, with pore-only constructs that can fold, assemble, and form functional selective channels independently of voltage-sensing domains. The NavAe1p structure reveals key gating elements: (1) S6 activation gate at the intracellular pore constriction (Met241 equivalent), (2) a cytoplasmic tail with neck and coiled-coil domains that stabilize the closed state, and (3) an outer ion binding site at the selectivity filter. BacNav selectivity filters possess multiple ion binding sites (inner and outer), supporting multi-ion pore models shared with eukaryotic NaV and CaV channels.
