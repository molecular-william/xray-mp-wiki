# Sodium Channel Gating and Selectivity

## Overview
Voltage-gated sodium channels use a gating mechanism involving voltage-sensing domains and a selectivity filter. The channel opens in response to membrane depolarization, allowing sodium influx that propagates action potentials. The selectivity filter ensures sodium over other ions. The resting-state structure of NavAb reveals the complete voltage-dependent gating mechanism including the sliding helix mechanism of voltage sensing. Prokaryotic sodium channels also exhibit CTD-mediated inactivation gating distinct from eukaryotic inactivation mechanisms.
