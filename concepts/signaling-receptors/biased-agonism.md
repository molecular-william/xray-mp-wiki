# Biased Agonism in G Protein-Coupled Receptors

## Overview
Biased agonism refers to the ability of different ligands to preferentially activate specific signaling pathways through the same receptor. This phenomenon has important implications for drug development, as it allows for selective activation of desired pathways while avoiding undesirable side effects. Crystal structures of the AT1R bound to balanced (AngII) and beta-arrestin-biased (TRV023, TRV026) ligands revealed a bipartite activation mechanism where N295^7.46 rearrangement is sufficient for beta-arrestin coupling while N111^3.35 flipping is required for Gq signaling.
