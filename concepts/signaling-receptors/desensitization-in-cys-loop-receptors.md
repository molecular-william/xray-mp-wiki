# Desensitization in Cys-Loop Receptors

## Overview
Desensitization is a fundamental regulatory mechanism in Cys-loop receptors where prolonged agonist exposure leads to a non-conducting state despite continued ligand binding. In the alpha4beta2 nicotinic [Acetylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/acetylcholine/) receptor, the agonist [Nicotine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/nicotine/) locks the receptor in a desensitized, non-conducting conformation. The receptor pore conformation in this state most closely resembles the desensitized GABA_A receptor, with the gate located at the cytosolic end of the pore. Desensitization involves extensive conformational rearrangements at the extracellular domain (ECD) to transmembrane domain (TMD) interface, with the ECD and TMD behaving largely as rigid bodies during state transitions.
