# GPCR G Protein-Arrestin Common Interface

## Overview
G-protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) transmit extracellular signals to activate intracellular heterotrimeric G proteins and arrestins. Despite their divergent functions — G proteins initiate signaling cascades while arrestins mediate signal shut-off and alternative signaling — both use a common receptor-binding interface. The Gα C-terminus (GαCT) and the finger loop of all four arrestins (ArrFL1-4) share a conserved consensus motif (E/D)x(I/L)xxxGL that binds to the same cytoplasmic crevice of active GPCRs. Upon receptor binding, both GαCT and ArrFL adopt a similar reverse turn structure (αL-type C-cap), although significant structural differences are seen at the rim of the binding crevice. This remarkable homology reflects a convergent or divergent evolution of a shared recognition mechanism for the active GPCR conformation.

## Mechanism/Details
The (E/D)x(I/L)xxxGL consensus motif is shared among GαCT from the Gi/Gt family and the finger loop region of all four arrestins. The conserved sequence features: an acidic residue (Glu/Asp) at position 1, a hydrophobic residue (Ile/Leu) at position 3, and a Gly-Leu pair (positions 7-8 in GαCT numbering). The Gly provides backbone flexibility for forming the reverse turn (C-cap) structure upon receptor binding. The Leu C-terminal residue forms critical hydrophobic contacts with the receptor cytoplasmic crevice. In the active GPCR (R*), the cytoplasmic ends of TM5 and TM6 move apart upon activation, opening a crevice that accommodates the peptide. Both GαCT and ArrFL bind with Arg135 of the conserved E(D)RY motif and Lys311 of the NPxxY motif as key interaction partners. However, GαCT engages in more extensive hydrophobic interactions along the TM5/TM6 inner face while ArrFL exhibits a larger interaction surface at the rim.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Ops* (Bovine rhodopsin apoprotein) — Crystal structure of Ops* in complex with GαCT peptide (PDB 3DQB, 3.2 Å) and ArrFL-1 peptide (PDB 4PXF, 2.75 Å) reveals both proteins binding the same cytoplasmic crevice via the shared (E/D)x(I/L)xxxGL motif.
- Transducin (Gt) — GαCT (residues 340-350) forms an α-helix terminated by a reverse turn (C-cap) upon interaction with R*. The structure communicates receptor activation to the nucleotide-binding site for GDP/GTP exchange.
- Rod photoreceptor arrestin — ArrFL-1 (residues 71-77, DVMGLLL) forms a reverse turn-like structure upon R* binding. The finger loop elongates through progression from basal to pre-activated to R*-bound arrestin.
