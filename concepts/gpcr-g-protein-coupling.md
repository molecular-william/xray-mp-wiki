# GPCR-G Protein Coupling Mechanism

## Overview
GPCR-G protein coupling is the fundamental mechanism by which G protein-coupled receptors transmit extracellular signals into the cell. Upon agonist binding, the GPCR undergoes conformational changes that create a binding surface for the heterotrimeric G protein, leading to GDP release from the Galpha subunit, GTP binding, and subsequent dissociation of Galpha-GTP from the Gbeta gamma dimer. Both Galpha-GTP and Gbeta gamma can then regulate downstream effector proteins. The structural basis of this coupling involves specific interactions between the intracellular loops and C-terminus of the GPCR with the amino- and carboxy-terminal helices of Galpha.
