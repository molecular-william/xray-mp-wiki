# BCCT Family (Betaine/Carnitine/Choline Transporter Family)

## Overview
The BCCT (Betaine/Carnitine/Choline Transporter) family comprises secondary active transporters that couple the uptake of compatible solutes ([Glycine Betaine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/glycine-betaine/), carnitine, [Choline](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/choline/), ectoine) to sodium or proton gradients across cell membranes. These transporters are found predominantly in bacteria and some archaea, where they play essential roles in osmoregulation by accumulating osmoprotectants from the environment. The family is named after its best-characterized members: [BETP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/betp/) ([Glycine Betaine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/glycine-betaine/) transporter), [CAIT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/cait/) (carnitine/γ-butyrobetaine antiporter), and BetT ([Choline](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/choline/) transporter).

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [BETP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/betp/) — [BETP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/betp/) from Corynebacterium glutamicum is the founding member of the BCCT family. It is a Na+-coupled [Glycine Betaine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/glycine-betaine/) symporter that forms homotrimers and is regulated by intracellular K+ concentration. The crystal structure (PDB 3JEO) revealed a distinctive 10-transmembrane helix architecture with a 4-helix bundle regulatory domain.
- not specified — OpuD from Bacillus subtilis is a Na+/glycine betaine transporter of the BCCT family involved in osmoprotection.
- not specified — ButA from Tetragenococcus halophila is a Na+/glycine betaine transporter of the BCCT family.
- not specified — BetT from Escherichia coli is a H+/choline transporter of the BCCT family, demonstrating proton-coupled substrate transport within the same family.
- not specified — EctP from Corynebacterium glutamicum is a Na+/ectoine transporter of the BCCT family, transporting a different compatible solute.
- not specified — [CAIT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/cait/) from Escherichia coli is a carnitine/gamma-butyrobetaine antiporter of the BCCT family, functioning in reverse (antiport) compared to most BCCT members which are symporters.
