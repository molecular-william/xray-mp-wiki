# PE-PPE Fusion Proteins

## Overview
PE-PPE fusion proteins are chimeric proteins that combine the N-terminal PE (Pro-Glu) and PPE (Pro-Pro-Glu) domains into a single polypeptide chain. In Mycobacterium tuberculosis, PE and PPE proteins are typically encoded as separate genes that form heterodimers. [EspB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/espB/) is the archetypal PE-PPE fusion protein, where the PE and PPE domains are organized as a single chain with a linker region connecting them. The fusion protein adopts a paperclip-like all-helical fold that structurally resembles the PE25-[PPE41](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/ppe41/) heterodimer despite limited sequence identity. Unlike canonical PE-PPE heterodimers, PE-PPE fusion proteins like [EspB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/espB/) lack the chaperone-binding domain and do not require EspG chaperones for folding or stability.


## Mechanism/Details
The PE-PPE fusion architecture represents an evolutionary innovation in mycobacterial virulence. [EspB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/espB/) contains 7 alpha helices arranged in a paperclip-like structure. The first two helices correspond to [PE25](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/pe25/) helices and adopt an antiparallel hairpin. The third helix corresponds to [PPE41](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/ppe41/) alpha1. A partially unstructured linker region (residues 90-132) connects the PE and PPE domains. The fusion lacks the hh motif (residues D121-T129) on the PPE domain that mediates EspG chaperone binding, meaning [EspB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/espB/) has evolved to fold independently without chaperone assistance. The PE and PPE sequence motifs are also not conserved in [EspB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/espB/) (QQ at 11-12 and DLK at 132-134 replace PE and PPE respectively).
