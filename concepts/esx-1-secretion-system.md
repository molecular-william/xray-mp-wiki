# ESX-1 Secretion System

## Overview
The ESX-1 secretion system is a type VII protein secretion system in Mycobacterium tuberculosis that is essential for virulence. ESX-1 exports multiple virulence factors across the bacterial membrane to the extracellular environment, including PE-PPE heterodimers (PE25-PPE41), WxG100 family proteins (EsxA/ESAT-6 and EsxB/CFP-10), and Esp proteins (EspA, EspB, EspC, EspE, EspJ, EspK). The system is required for bacterial replication in macrophages, phagosomal escape into the cytosol, host-cell death, and subsequent bacterial dissemination. Deletion of ESX-1 leads to attenuation of M. tuberculosis. The ESX-1 locus encodes multiple components including the secreted substrates and the secretion machinery itself.


## Mechanism/Details
The ESX-1 secretion system is an important virulence determinant in M. tuberculosis. It exports proteins that lack canonical Sec or Tat secretion signals. The system mediates the secretion of folded proteins, including PE-PPE heterodimers and EspB fusion proteins. EspG chaperones are involved in targeting PE-PPE heterodimers to the ESX-1 machinery. EspB interacts with EspK, which in turn interacts directly with the large membrane-associated ATPase EccC1a-EccC1b. The system requires MycP1 protease for processing of EspB during secretion. All ESX clusters except ESX-4 encode pe and ppe genes, suggesting a conserved role for PE-PPE proteins in ESX secretion.
