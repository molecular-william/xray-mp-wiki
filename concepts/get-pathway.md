# GET Pathway for Tail-Anchored Protein Insertion

## Overview
The guided entry of tail-anchored proteins (GET) pathway is a conserved eukaryotic pathway for the post-translational targeting and insertion of tail-anchored (TA) membrane proteins into the endoplasmic reticulum (ER) membrane. TA proteins, which comprise approximately 5% of eukaryotic membrane proteins, are anchored in the lipid bilayer by a single C-terminal transmembrane domain. The pathway involves cytosolic targeting by the Get3 chaperone and membrane insertion by the Get1/Get2 insertase complex.

## Mechanism/Details
In the GET pathway: (1) The homodimeric ATPase Get3 binds the TA protein substrate in its closed ADP-Pi form, shielding the hydrophobic TMD. (2) The Get3/TA complex is captured at the ER membrane by the Get1/Get2 insertase. (3) The Get2 N-terminal cytoplasmic domain binds Get3, followed by Get1-CD coiled-coil inserting at the Get3 dimer interface. (4) Get2 helix alpha3' interacts with the Get3 TABD, triggering Get3 dimer opening and nucleotide dissociation. (5) The TA protein is released to the ER membrane through the Get1/Get2 hydrophilic groove, with the hydrophilic groove and membrane thinning reducing the energetic barrier for insertion. (6) The GET insertase can adopt two conformations (state 1 and state 2), with state 2 allowing lateral release of the TA protein to the membrane.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Get1/Get2 GET Insertase Complex — Membrane insertase of the GET pathway, forms a 2:2 heterotetramer with a hydrophilic groove for TA protein insertion
- Get3 (TRC40) — Cytosolic ATPase chaperone that delivers TA proteins to the ER membrane
