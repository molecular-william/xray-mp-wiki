# LCP Microseeding for Serial Crystallography

## Overview
LCP microseeding for serial crystallography is a crystallization strategy that combines lipidic cubic phase (LCP) crystallization with microseeding to produce well-diffracting microcrystals of membrane proteins for [Serial Femtosecond Crystallography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/serial-femtosecond-crystallography/) (SFX) at XFELs. Unconventionally, the seed stock is produced from vapour-diffusion microcrystals rather than LCP-grown crystals. High seed stock volumes (5-10% v/v) are used to provide sufficient nucleation sites. This approach was successfully applied to crystallize the Blastochloris viridis photosynthetic reaction centre (RCvir) in LCP, yielding microcrystals that diffracted to 2.25-2.4 A at XFEL sources and enabling high-occupancy incorporation of [Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) cofactors.

## Mechanism/Details
The LCP microseeding protocol for RCvir: (1) Vapour-diffusion microcrystals grown with detergent-solubilized protein ([LDAO](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/ldao/)); (2) microcrystals crushed with Teflon beads to form seed stock; (3) seed stock mixed into LCP at 5.4% v/v (high seeding density); (4) LCP/protein/seed mixture set up in gas-tight syringes or glass wells. The technique overcomes difficulties in crushing LCP crystals for seeding while providing the benefits of LCP (ability to add hydrophobic cofactors, low sample consumption, viscous injection). The resulting microcrystals were delivered using a viscous injector at SACLA and SwissFEL, yielding diffraction data with very high multiplicity (705-1189x) and 100% completeness.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Blastochloris viridis Photosynthetic Reaction Centre — LCP microcrystals grown with vapour-diffusion microseed stock, diffracting to 2.25 A at SwissFEL (PDB 7q7q) and 2.4 A at SACLA (PDB 7q7p). [Ubiquinone](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/cofactors/ubiquinone/) 1/2 doped into [Monoolein](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/monoolein/) for high-occupancy QB pocket.
