# High-Viscosity Sample Injection for Serial Femtosecond Crystallography

## Overview
High-viscosity sample injection is a sample delivery method for [Serial Femtosecond Crystallography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/serial-femtosecond-crystallography/) (SFX) that uses a viscous carrier medium such as lipidic cubic phase (LCP), grease, or agarose to embed and deliver microcrystals at low flow rates (0.1-0.5 uL/min). The High-Viscosity Cartridge (HVC) injector developed at SACLA features a cartridge-type sample reservoir, a coaxial gas flow for stream stabilization, a suction device to prevent sample curling, and a temperature-regulated cooling jacket. This approach significantly reduces sample consumption compared to liquid-jet methods and operates at atmospheric pressure, avoiding the need for expensive lipids like 7.9 [MAG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/mag/) or 9.7 [MAG](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/mag/) to prevent LCP phase transitions. The HVC injector has been applied to diverse proteins including GPCRs, ion channels, and soluble proteins for both static structural analysis and time-resolved pump-probe studies.
