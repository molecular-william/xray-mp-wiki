# MAPEG Superfamily

## Overview
The MAPEG (Membrane-Associated Proteins involved in Eicosanoid and Glutathione metabolism) superfamily consists of integral membrane enzymes involved in the biosynthesis of eicosanoids and glutathione metabolism. Members include microsomal prostaglandin E2 synthase-1 (mPGES-1), microsomal glutathione transferase-1 (MGST1), leukotriene C4 synthase (LTC4S), 5-lipoxygenase activating protein (FLAP), MGST2, and MGST3. MAPEG members share a conserved four-helix bundle fold but exhibit diverse catalytic functions.


## Mechanism/Details
MAPEG family members are typically homotrimeric integral membrane
proteins with each monomer containing a four-helix transmembrane
bundle. The structures share a conserved fold despite low sequence
homology (15-40% identity). Key structural features include:

- **Four-helix bundle topology:** Each monomer consists of four
  transmembrane helices (TM I-IV) that form the core fold. Helices II
  and IV often contain proline-induced kinks (e.g., Pro81 and Pro136
  in mPGES-1) that contribute to cavity formation.

- **Active site location:** The active site is located between TM
  helices at monomer-monomer interfaces, placing catalysis within
  or adjacent to the membrane bilayer. In mPGES-1, a 15-A-deep cavity
  at each interface binds glutathione and the PGH2 substrate.

- **C-domain insert:** mPGES-1 and MGST1 contain a unique cytosolic
  domain (C-domain) inserted between TM I and TM II that contributes
  to the active site and is absent in LTC4S and FLAP.

- **Central cavity:** Many MAPEG members have a central cavity along
  the trimer threefold axis that varies in size and function. In
  mPGES-1 this is a cone-shaped cavity extending 16 A from the
  cytosol into the membrane that may facilitate substrate access.

- **Catalytic diversity:** Despite shared fold, MAPEG members catalyze
  different reactions. mPGES-1 isomerizes PGH2 to PGE2 using GSH as
  a cofactor. LTC4S conjugates GSH to LTA4. MGST1 has glutathione
  transferase and peroxidase activities. FLAP is a non-catalytic
  scaffold for 5-lipoxygenase.

- **Glutathione coordination:** GSH binding varies between members.
  In LTC4S, Arg104 coordinates the GSH thiol. In mPGES-1, Ser127
  activates the thiol instead, while the corresponding Arg126 is
  sequestered in a bidentate complex with Asp49.
