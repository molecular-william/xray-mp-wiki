# Site-2 Protease Family Mechanism

## Overview
Site-2 proteases (S2Ps) are a conserved family of zinc metalloproteases that perform regulated intramembrane proteolysis of transmembrane substrates. They are classified into groups based on domain architecture: group I (e.g., E. coli RseP, human S2P) have extracytoplasmic PDZ domains; group III lack PDZ domains and instead have CBS domains. S2Ps typically function in sequential cleavage pathways where site-1 protease first removes the extracytoplasmic domain, after which S2P cleaves the TM segment. Structural studies of EcRseP and KkRseP revealed a conserved catalytic core with zinc-binding HEXXH motif, an MRE beta-sheet for substrate strand addition, and a gating mechanism involving PDZ domains, PCT-H2, and TM4 to regulate substrate entry. A conserved asparagine residue (N394 in EcRseP) clamps the unwound substrate at the active center.
