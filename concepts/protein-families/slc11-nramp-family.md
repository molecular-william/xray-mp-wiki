# SLC11 (NRAMP) Family

## Overview
The SLC11 (Solute Carrier 11) family, also known as the NRAMP (Natural Resistance-Associated Macrophage Protein) family, consists of secondary active transporters that catalyze the proton-coupled transport of divalent transition-metal ions across cellular membranes. Members are found in all kingdoms of life with a high degree of sequence conservation. In vertebrates, the family includes NRAMP1 (SLC11A1) in macrophage phagosomes for host defense, and DMT1 (SLC11A2) for intestinal [IRON](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/iron/) absorption and systemic metal homeostasis. Bacterial SLC11 transporters primarily catalyze Mn2+ import.


## Mechanism/Details
The SLC11 family shares a common LeuT-fold architecture predicted to contain 11 or 12 transmembrane helices with the N terminus located in the cytoplasm. A conserved DPGN motif in the unwound region of alpha-helix 1 serves as a family signature. The ion-binding site is located at the center of the transporter and is composed of conserved residues that coordinate Mn2+, Fe2+, Cd2+, Co2+, Ni2+, and Pb2+ while excluding alkaline earth metal ions (Ca2+, Mg2+, Sr2+, Ba2+). The binding site features predominantly hard oxygen ligands along with a soft thioether sulfur ligand, conferring selectivity for transition metals over alkaline earth metals.
The first outward-facing conformation of a SLC11 family member was determined for EcoDMT from Eremococcus colecola at 3.3 A resolution (ncomms14033). Comparison with the inward-facing ScaDMT structure defined the conformational changes underlying transition-metal ion transport by the alternating-access mechanism. A conserved histidine on alpha-helix 6b (His236 in EcoDMT, His267 in human DMT1) was identified as the primary proton acceptor, providing the first molecular insight into proton coupling in the SLC11 family. Two potential proton exit pathways were proposed: release via the main metal ion exit path together with Mn2+, or via a narrow side tunnel lined by conserved acidic (Glu119, Asp126, Glu129) and basic residues (Arg368, Arg369, Arg373) that are unique to SLC11 family members.
