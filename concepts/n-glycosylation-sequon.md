# N-Glycosylation Sequon

## Overview
The N-glycosylation sequon (Asn-X-Ser/Thr, where X is not Pro) is the minimal consensus sequence recognized by oligosaccharyltransferase (OST) enzymes for Asn-linked (N-linked) glycosylation. In eukaryotes and archaea, OST transfers a preassembled oligosaccharide from a lipid-linked oligosaccharide donor to the carboxamide group of Asn within the sequon. Eubacteria use an extended sequon (Asp/Glu-X1-Asn-X2-Ser/Thr). The sequon adopts an extended conformation when bound to OST, with the Asn side chain activated by the carboxylate dyad and the +2 Ser/Thr residue recognized by a dedicated binding pocket.

## Mechanism/Details
The canonical N-glycosylation sequon is Asn-X-Ser/Thr (X != Pro). The Asn residue is the site of glycosylation, while the +2 Ser/Thr residue is recognized by a conserved Ser/Thr-binding pocket in OST. The X position side chain makes few contacts with OST and shows no strong structural constraint, although free-peptide assays reveal preferences (Glu/Gln favored, Arg/Lys/Trp disfavored). The +2 hydroxy group is not directly involved in catalysis but contributes to proper positioning. In the cross-linked OST-peptide complex, the sequon consensus requirements are greatly relaxed: Gln can substitute for Asn, Ala can substitute for Ser/Thr at +2, and X-position preferences disappear (except Pro rejection).
