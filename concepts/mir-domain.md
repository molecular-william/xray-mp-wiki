# MIR Domain (Mannosyltransferase, IP3R, RyR)

## Overview
The MIR domain (Mannosyltransferase, IP3R, and RyR) is a structural domain found in diverse membrane proteins including inositol 1,4,5-trisphosphate receptors (IP3Rs), ryanodine receptors (RyRs), and protein O-mannosyltransferases (PMTs). The MIR domain adopts a canonical beta-trefoil fold comprising 12 beta-strands forming a six-stranded beta-barrel topped by a triangular beta-hairpin cap (hairpin triplet). The fold consists of three tandem MIR-motif repeats (MIRm1, MIRm2, MIRm3), each contributing specific structural elements. In PMTs, the MIR domain is located in the ER lumen and functions as a carbohydrate-binding module (CBM) related to CBM family 13/42. The domain contains conserved sugar-binding sites (typically sites alpha and beta) on the lateral faces of the triangular cap, formed by conserved histidine-containing LH(S/T)H motifs. The MIR domain was originally identified in IP3R and RyR calcium channels where it also likely functions in carbohydrate recognition, though its role there is less well understood.

In PMT2 subfamily members (yeast Pmt2, Pmt3, human POMT2), the MIR domain additionally contains a unique positively charged FKR motif at site delta in MIRm3. Missense mutations in the MIR domains of human POMT1 and POMT2 cause alpha-dystroglycanopathies, a class of congenital muscular dystrophies. The MIR beta-trefoil is structurally similar to the lectin domain of GalNAc-transferases (GalNAc-Ts), suggesting convergent evolution of beta-trefoil domains for processive O-glycosylation.


## Mechanism/Details
The MIR domain comprises three trefoil MIR-motif units (MIRm1-3). Each MIR-motif contains a conserved tryptophan and leucine residue that form the hydrophobic core of the beta-trefoil. The triangular cap region presents the primary ligand-binding surfaces.

**Ligand-binding sites:**
- **Site alpha**: Located at the MIRm1/MIRm3 interface. Contains two conserved histidines (H362, H364 in Pmt2) that coordinate sugar moieties. This is the primary binding site for O-mannosylated peptide products in PMTs, with the aromatic side chain of a conserved tyrosine (Y380 in Pmt2) providing stacking interactions.
- **Site beta**: Located at the MIRm2/MIRm1 interface. Contains another conserved histidine pair (H428, H430 in Pmt2). Preferential binding site for free mannose and alpha-mannose-1-phosphate. Works together with site alpha for processive O-mannosylation.
- **Site delta**: PMT2-subfamily-specific site in MIRm3 containing a FKR motif (F516, K517, R518 in Pmt2). Forms a positively charged trident that may mediate MIR-TMD interactions through a complementary site delta-prime on the Pmt1 transmembrane domain.
- **Sites alpha and beta a**: Additional conserved surface patches adjacent to the primary sites.

**Protein O-mannosylation function:**
In PMT dimers (Pmt1-Pmt2 heterodimers), the MIR domain acts as a processivity factor. After the active center mannosylates a serine/threonine residue, the O-Man peptide product binds to site alpha, which either keeps the product away from the active center or increases local concentration of nearby unmodified residues. This is essential for efficient glycosylation of S/T-rich multi-acceptor substrates but dispensable for O-mannosylation of unfolded protein targets (UPOM). The MIR domain is structurally and functionally analogous to the lectin domain of GalNAc-T2.
