# Helix Pi-Bulge in Membrane Proteins

## Overview
A pi-bulge is a local disruption of the alpha-helical hydrogen-bonding pattern where the peptide plane tilts away from the helix axis, creating n->n+5 (pi-helical) hydrogen bonds instead of the standard n->n+4 (alpha-helical) pattern. In bacteriorhodopsin, a pi-bulge at Ala215 in helix G causes a non-proline kink that displaces the C-terminal portion of the helix by nearly one helix diameter.

## Mechanism/Details
The pi-bulge at Ala215 causes the carbonyl groups of residues 213 and 214 to form hydrogen bonds with amide groups of residues 218 and 219 respectively (n->n+5 pattern). The free carbonyl of Ala215 accepts a hydrogen bond from water W501, which connects to Trp182. A second water (W502) bridges Lys216 to Thr46 of helix B. This arrangement couples helix G conformation to retinal isomerization state and Asp96 protonation state.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Bacteriorhodopsin — Pi-bulge at Ala215 in helix G creates kink functionally linked to retinal binding and proton transfer
