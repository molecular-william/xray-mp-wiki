# Ca2+ Bowl

## Overview
The Ca2+ bowl is a stretch of well-conserved aspartate residues within the second RCK domain of BK (Slo1) channels that functions as a high-affinity Ca2+ sensing motif. It forms an EF-hand-like helix-loop-helix structure that becomes ordered upon Ca2+ binding and is strategically positioned at the inter-subunit assembly interface of the gating ring.

## Mechanism/Details
The Ca2+ bowl is located after alphaE in RCK2, followed by a short helix (alphaEF). Together alphaE, the acidic-residue-rich loop (the Ca2+ bowl), and alphaEF form a helix-loop-helix motif resembling EF-hand Ca2+-binding proteins such as calmodulin. In the Ca2+-free (apo) state, the Ca2+ bowl is disordered. Ca2+ binding induces ordering of the loop. Due to its proximity to the inter-subunit assembly interface, Ca2+ binding at the Ca2+ bowl likely triggers conformational changes that alter the gating ring dimensions, contributing to channel opening.
