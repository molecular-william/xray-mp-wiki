# Cl1 Proton Channel of Photosystem II

## Overview
The Cl1 channel is a proton release pathway in Photosystem II (PSII) that connects the oxygen-evolving complex (OEC) to the lumenal side of the thylakoid membrane. It starts from the Mn4 side of the OEC near chloride ion Cl1 and passes through D2-K317 and D1-D61 residues that form a bottleneck. The channel is distinguished by a gating mechanism involving residue D1-E65, which undergoes a conformational change during the S2 to S3 transition to allow proton release while preventing back-reaction.


## Mechanism/Details
The Cl1 channel serves as the proton exit pathway during water oxidation in PSII. The channel begins near Cl1 of the OEC and passes through a bottleneck formed by D1-E65, D1-P66, D1-V67, and D2-E312. Through D1-D61, the Cl1 channel connects to W19, which is also the first water in the O4 channel.

The key gating mechanism centers on D1-E65, which serves as a proton gate. In the closed state, the H-bonding network connecting the OEC to the bulk is disrupted by D1-N335 and D1-R334. During the S2 to S3 transition, specifically at 150 microseconds after the second flash, D1-E65 rotates by approximately 50 degrees, forming an open state for effective proton transfer. This rearrangement creates hydrogen bonds to W119 and connects the OEC via D1-E65 and waters to the bulk.

The open state is stabilized by a newly formed hydrogen bond between E65 and R334, preventing the released proton from returning. A proton can be transferred from D61 to E65 via at least two possible water chains involving W40/42 or the alternative water position of W150. The newly arrived proton is repelled by R334 and attracted by E312, causing D1-E65 to rotate back to its original position by 250 microseconds. At 400 microseconds, W150 is no longer present, marking the last indication of open states.

Water molecules in the Cl1 channel have lower mobility (B-factor average 27 A^2) compared to the O1 channel, and Fo-Fc peaks appear only near the bulk water on the lumen side. This lower mobility, combined with the reversible motion of D1-E65, makes the Cl1 channel more suitable as a proton pathway rather than a water intake channel.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Photosystem II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/photosystem-ii/) — The 1.89 A room-temperature XFEL structure (PDB 7RF1) of PSII revealed the D1-E65 proton gate mechanism. During the S2 to S3 transition, D1-E65 rotates ~50 degrees (at 150 microseconds after second flash) to open the proton gate, then rotates back at 250 microseconds. This gating action enables proton release while preventing back-reaction, with W150 serving as a transient water chain component.
