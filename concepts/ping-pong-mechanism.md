# Ping-Pong Catalytic Mechanism

## Overview


## Mechanism/Details
The ping-pong mechanism is a two-step catalytic mechanism in which the enzyme first reacts with one substrate to form a covalent intermediate, releases the first product, and then reacts with a second substrate to complete the reaction. In the case of Lnt, the first step involves acylation of the catalytic cysteine (C387) by the lipid donor (e.g. phosphatidylglycerol), forming a thioester intermediate. The leaving group (sn-2-lyso-glycerolphosphate) is released to the lipid bilayer. In the second step, the apolipoprotein substrate enters the active site, and its N-terminal amine attacks the thioester intermediate, resulting in N-acylation of the apolipoprotein and release of the product.
