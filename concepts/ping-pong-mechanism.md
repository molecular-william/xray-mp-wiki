# Ping-Pong Catalytic Mechanism

## Overview


## Mechanism/Details
The ping-pong mechanism is a two-step catalytic mechanism in which the enzyme first reacts with one substrate to form a covalent intermediate, releases the first product, and then reacts with a second substrate to complete the reaction. In the case of [LNT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/lnt/), the first step involves acylation of the catalytic cysteine (C387) by the lipid donor (e.g. [Phosphatidylglycerol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylglycerol/)), forming a thioester intermediate. The leaving group (sn-2-lyso-glycerolphosphate) is released to the lipid bilayer. In the second step, the apolipoprotein substrate enters the active site, and its N-terminal amine attacks the thioester intermediate, resulting in N-acylation of the apolipoprotein and release of the product.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- E. coli [LNT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/lnt/) — [LNT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/lnt/) uses a two-step ping-pong mechanism to transfer acyl groups from phospholipid donors to apolipoprotein acceptors
- [E. coli Lnt](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/e-coli-lnt/) — Nine cryo-EM and MX structures (Smithers et al., Sci. Adv. 2023) capture all six states of the Lnt ping-pong reaction: Apo, M1 (PE bound), P1 (acyl-enzyme + lyso-PE), M2 mimic (TITC-modified), and P2 (TA-BLP product). Three distinct acyl chain binding sites identified. Arm 3 flexibility regulates active site access.
