# Rhomboid Protease Family

## Overview
The rhomboid family comprises intramembrane serine proteases found across all kingdoms of life. These enzymes cleave transmembrane substrates within the lipid bilayer plane. The rhomboid fold consists of seven transmembrane helices with an internal water-filled cavity containing a Ser-His catalytic dyad. A distinctive L1 loop lies laterally adjacent to the TM bundle, partially embedded in the membrane. A conserved WR motif at the tip of L1 is critical for optimal activity. The L5 cap regulates substrate access to the active site. Sequence identity among functional rhomboid proteases is extremely low, with only eight residues being invariant (six at the active site plus two glycines for helix packing).
