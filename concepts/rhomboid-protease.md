# Rhomboid Protease Family

## Overview
Rhomboid proteases are intramembrane serine proteases that cleave transmembrane domains of substrate proteins within the lipid bilayer. They play key roles in signal transduction, protein processing, and mitochondrial dynamics. The catalytic mechanism involves a serine-histidine dyad in the transmembrane region. Structural studies of the E. coli rhomboid [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/glpg/) in complex with peptidyl-chloromethylketone inhibitors revealed the detailed architecture of the S1-S4 substrate-binding subsites and provided mechanistic insights into sequence-specific [Intramembrane Proteolysis](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/intramembrane-proteolysis/).
