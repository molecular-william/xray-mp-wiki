# Rhomboid Protease Substrate Specificity

## Overview
Rhomboid intramembrane proteases exhibit sequence-specific cleavage of transmembrane substrates, with a strong preference for small residues (Ala, Ser) at the P1 position. Crystal structures of E. coli [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/glpg/) in complex with peptidyl-chloromethylketone inhibitors defining the S1-S4 subsites revealed the structural basis for this specificity. The P1 side chain projects into a water-retention cavity that accommodates only small residues; larger side chains would disrupt the conserved water network. The S4 subsite is a hydrophobic surface patch formed by the L1 loop (residues 145-155), explaining the preference for hydrophobic residues at P4. The S2 subsite accommodates polar residues (e.g., Thr) through interactions with Ser248. This specificity mechanism is conserved across rhomboid family members from bacteria to humans.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/glpg/) rhomboid intramembrane protease — [Ac-IATA-cmk (N-Acetyl-Ile-Ala-Thr-Ala-Chloromethylketone)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ac-iata-cmk/) (a tetrapeptide CMK with Ile-Ala-Thr-Ala sequence) binds to [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/glpg/) with the P1-Ala projecting into the water-retention cavity, defining optimal substrate geometry at S1-S4 subsites.
- [GLPG](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/glpg/) rhomboid intramembrane protease — Positional scanning mutagenesis of TatA substrate reveals that hydrophobic residues (Ile, Leu, Val, Phe) are preferred at P4 (I5), small residues at P3 (A6), polar residues at P2 (T7), and small residues at P1 (A8). P1 mutations are least tolerated.
