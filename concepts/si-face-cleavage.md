# si-Face Cleavage in Rhomboid Proteases

## Overview
Rhomboid intramembrane proteases cleave transmembrane substrates on the si-face of the scissile peptide bond, a stereochemistry distinct from the re-face cleavage characteristic of most soluble serine proteases (e.g., trypsin). The rhomboid catalytic machinery employs a Ser-His dyad (Ser116-His65 in hiGlpG) with an oxyanion hole formed by the main-chain NH of Ser116 and the protonated N-epsilon of His65. This si-face mechanism is shared with other serine peptidases featuring a Ser-Lys catalytic dyad, including bacterial signal peptidase (SPase), Lon protease, LexA peptidase, and VP4 protease.
