# CLC Windmill Antiport Mechanism

## Overview
The windmill mechanism is a proposed model for CLCF-type F-/H+
antiport, based on crystal structures of the Eca CLCF transporter
from Enterococcus casseliflavus in two conformations. In this model,
F- and H+ move through separate ion-specific pathways that switch
sidedness during the transport cycle, with the gating glutamate
(Glu_g) executing a clockwise rotary trajectory through Up, Middle,
and Down rotamer positions. This contrasts with conventional
alternating-access or ping-pong mechanisms where coupled substrates
bind in a mutually exclusive fashion—here, F- and H+ occupy their
transport sites simultaneously at certain steps in the cycle. The
mechanism has been proposed specifically for CLCF antiporters with
1:1 F-/H+ stoichiometry.
