# POT Family (Proton-Dependent Oligopeptide Transporters)

## Overview
The POT (proton-dependent oligopeptide transporter) family is a subgroup of the Major Facilitator Superfamily. Members transport di- and tripeptides using a proton gradient. They typically have 12 transmembrane helices and use a scissor-switch gating mechanism. The binding pockets are rich in aromatic residues enabling multispecific recognition. Adversarial ligands such as HEPES can bind in the POT substrate cavity, mimicking peptide binding. The first substrate-bound POT structure was solved for PepT_So2 with alafosfalin, revealing conserved N- and C-terminus coordination by Arg25, Glu402, Asn151, Asn329, and Tyr29.
