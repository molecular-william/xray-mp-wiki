# Steered Molecular Dynamics (SMD)

## Overview
Steered Molecular Dynamics (SMD) is a computational simulation technique where an external force is applied to a molecule or specific atoms to drive conformational changes or molecular interactions over a defined trajectory. In SMD, a harmonic potential (spring) is used to pull a specific atom or group of atoms along a defined reaction coordinate at a constant pulling speed. The technique allows visualization of molecular translocation pathways and estimation of free energy profiles through the Jarzynski equality, which relates non-reversible work to free energy changes. SMD is commonly used to study ligand unbinding pathways, protein conformational changes, and mechanical properties of biomolecules.
