# Leu25 Gatekeeper Mechanism in NSS Transporters

## Overview
The Leu25 gatekeeper is a conserved mechanism in the NSS (neurotransmitter:sodium symporter) family where the invariant Leu25 residue of the unwound TM1 region serves a dual function: first, it rotates into the empty substrate-binding pocket (S1) to stabilize the Na+- and substrate-free return state; second, it must rotate out of the S1 site before Na+ ions can bind to the Na1 and Na2 sites from the extracellular side. This dual role ensures vectorial control in the return transition against a Na+ gradient, preventing Na+ leak during inward-to-outward return transitions.
