# Acetate Uptake Transporter (AceTr) Family

## Overview
The acetate uptake transporter (AceTr) family (TC 2.A.96) comprises bacterial and
fungal membrane proteins that facilitate the translocation of acetate and other
short-chain monocarboxylates. The prototypical member, SatP (yaaH) from *E. coli*,
has been structurally characterized as a hexameric channel with a UreI-like fold.
Each protomer contains six transmembrane helices surrounding a central pore, with a
highly conserved "FLY" hydrophobic constriction motif (F17, Y72, L131 in [ECSATP](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/ecsatp/))
forming the narrowest point. The AceTr family shares no significant sequence similarity
with other known transporters, indicating a novel fold. Members are conserved from
bacteria to fungi; the yeast homolog Ady2 has been functionally characterized as an
acetate permease essential for growth on acetate as a carbon source.
