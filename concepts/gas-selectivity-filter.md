# GAS Selectivity Filter

## Overview
The GAS (Gly-Ala-Ser) selectivity filter is the ion selectivity determinant in acid-sensing ion channels (ASICs) and epithelial sodium channels (ENaC), members of the degenerin/epithelial sodium channel (Deg/ENaC) superfamily. It adopts an extended, belt-like conformation that positions the carbonyl oxygen atoms of conserved glycine residues directly into the ion conduction pathway, creating a size-based barrier that discriminates ions by the size of their hydrated species.


## Mechanism/Details
The GAS (Gly-Ala-Ser) motif in the second transmembrane segment (TM2) of ASICs and ENaC channels forms the ion selectivity filter. In the open state, the GAS motif adopts an extended, belt-like conformation aligned approximately parallel to the membrane plane. The carbonyl oxygen atoms of Gly 443 from three subunits form a triangular ring with a radius of approximately 3.6 A, precisely matching the radius of a hydrated Na+ ion (approximately 3.8 A). The radius of a hydrated K+ ion (approximately 4.2 A) is too large to be accommodated, achieving selectivity through a barrier mechanism based on hydrated ion size. The GAS belt crosslinks subunits and reinforces the architecture of the ion channel pore.
