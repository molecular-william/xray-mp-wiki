# Sodium-Motive Force (SMF)

## Overview
Sodium-motive force is the electrochemical gradient of sodium ions across a membrane that drives ATP synthesis or secondary transport. It is analogous to the proton-motive force but uses sodium instead of protons. Many archaea and bacteria use sodium gradients for energy transduction.
