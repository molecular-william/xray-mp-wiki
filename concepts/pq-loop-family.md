# PQ-Loop Family

## Overview
The PQ-loop family is a group of membrane transporters defined by a conserved Pro-Gln motif on the first transmembrane helix. Members include bacterial SemiSWEETs, eukaryotic SWEETs, and lysosomal amino acid exporters, all sharing a 3TM repeat architecture and using the PQ-loop as a hinge for conformational transport cycles.


## Mechanism/Details
The PQ-loop family comprises membrane transporters characterized by a highly conserved Pro-Gln (PQ) motif on the first transmembrane helix of each 3TM repeat. This family includes bacterial SemiSWEETs (sugar transporters), eukaryotic SWEETs (sugar transporters), and lysosomal amino acid exporters whose dysfunction causes lysosomal storage diseases such as cystinosis. All members share the '3TM repeats' architecture with the PQ-loop motif on TM1. The conserved proline residue is present in both SemiSWEETs and SWEETs, while the glutamine is conserved only in SemiSWEETs. The PQ-loop motif serves as a flexible molecular hinge that enables the binder clip-like conformational changes essential for the transport mechanism. The proline residue is critical for transport function in vivo, as demonstrated by mutagenesis studies.
