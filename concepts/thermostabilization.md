# Protein Thermostabilization

## Overview
Protein thermostabilization is a construct design strategy in which targeted point mutations are introduced to increase the thermal stability of a protein without significantly altering its native structure or function. This approach is particularly valuable for membrane proteins, which are inherently flexible and prone to denaturation during purification and crystallization. Thermostabilized constructs exhibit improved solubility, reduced aggregation, and enhanced crystal formation probability.


## Mechanism/Details
Thermostabilizing mutations typically work by:
- Introducing new hydrogen bonds or salt bridges between secondary structure elements
- Filling hydrophobic cavities that allow water penetration and destabilization
- Restricting conformational flexibility in flexible loops or termini
- Optimizing packing interactions within the hydrophobic core
- Removing surface-exposed polar residues that destabilize the membrane environment
