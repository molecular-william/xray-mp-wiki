# Membrane Lipid Asymmetry

## Overview
Membrane lipid asymmetry refers to the non-random distribution of lipids between the inner and outer leaflets of biological membranes. In the plasma membrane, phosphatidylcholine and sphingomyelin are enriched in the outer leaflet, while phosphatidylethanolamine, phosphatidylserine, and phosphoinositides are confined to the inner leaflet. This asymmetry is maintained by flippases, floppases, and scramblases, and is critical for signaling, membrane curvature, and protein function.
