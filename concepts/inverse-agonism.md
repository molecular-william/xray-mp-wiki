# Inverse Agonism in GPCR Signaling

## Overview
Inverse agonism refers to the ability of certain ligands to reduce the basal (constitutive) activity of G protein-coupled receptors below the level observed in the absence of any ligand. Unlike neutral antagonists, which simply block agonist binding without affecting basal signaling, inverse agonists actively stabilize the receptor in its inactive conformation. This property has therapeutic relevance for receptors that exhibit significant constitutive activity, such as the P2Y12 receptor, which constitutively activates Gi/o proteins in platelets.
