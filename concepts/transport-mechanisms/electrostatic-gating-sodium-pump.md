# Electrostatic Gating in Light-Driven Sodium Pumps

## Overview

The electrostatic gating mechanism describes how light-driven sodium pumps such as Krokinobacter eikastus rhodopsin 2 (KR2) achieve unidirectional sodium transport across the membrane. After retinal isomerization, deprotonation of the retinal Schiff base (SB) opens an electrostatic gate in microseconds, creating conditions for transient sodium binding between N112 and D251 at ~1 ms. A second sodium-binding site near E11, N106, and E160 on the extracellular side at ~20 ms enables ion release. Reprotonation of the SB blocks backflow, ensuring directionality.

## Mechanism/Details

In KR2, the photocycle begins with retinal isomerization from all-trans to 13-cis (completed within femtoseconds). In the early nanoseconds, the retinal C20 methyl group pushes sideways against V117 in helix C (rather than against W182 in helix F as in bacteriorhodopsin). This propagates structural changes into the helical bundle. At the microsecond stage, deprotonation of the retinal Schiff base (proton transfer to the D116 counterion) opens an electrostatic gate. The loss of positive charge at the SB creates an electrostatic environment that favours sodium ion entry. At ~1 ms, a sodium ion binds transiently between N112 and D251 — confirmed by QM/MM calculations showing the sodium produces a 55-66 nm red shift matching the O intermediate absorption maximum (592 nm). The sodium coordination distances (2.5 Å to N112, 2.7 Å to D251) support this assignment. Sodium binding favours reprotonation of the SB through alteration of the hydrogen-bonding network involving D116, N112, and the SB, which blocks backflow. At ~20 ms, the sodium is released from the first site and appears at a second site between E11, N106, and E160 near the extracellular exit. A rotamer change of R109 and Q78, along with a shift of helix D, opens the exit pathway. R109 displacement favours sodium binding by moving a positive charge away.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work

- Krokinobacter eikastus Rhodopsin 2 (KR2) — TR-SFX structures from 800 fs to 20 ms (PDB 6TK7, 6TK6, 6TK5, 6TK4, 6TK3, 6TK2, 6TK1). Sodium binds N112/D251 at 1 ms (O intermediate), exits via E11/N106/E160 at 20 ms.
