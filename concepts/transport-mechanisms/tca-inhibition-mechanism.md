# Tricyclic Antidepressant (TCA) Inhibition Mechanism in NSS Transporters

## Overview
Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs) such as clomipramine, desipramine, imipramine, and nortriptyline inhibit neurotransmitter reuptake by binding to a conserved site in the extracellular-facing vestibule of neurotransmitter:sodium symporter (NSS) family transporters. The mechanism was elucidated by cocrystal structures of the LeuT-TCA complexes from Aquifex aeolicus, which showed that TCAs bind non-competitively to a pocket about 11 A above the substrate binding site, stabilizing the occluded state and slowing substrate release.

## Mechanism/Details
The TCA-binding site in NSS transporters is located in the extracellular-facing vestibule, about 11 A above the substrate- and sodium-binding sites. The TCAs are cradled by the C-terminal half of transmembrane helix 1 (TM1), the N-terminal regions of TM6 and TM10, the midpoint of TM3, and the sharp turn of extracellular loop 4 (EL4). The binding site has a pronounced negative electrostatic potential that enhances binding of positively charged inhibitors.
Upon TCA binding, two key conformational changes occur: 1. The guanidinium group of Arg30 rotates to form a direct salt bridge with Asp404, displacing intervening water molecules and stabilizing the extracellular gate in a closed conformation. 2. Ala319 in EL4 flips or shifts upward to accommodate the bound inhibitor.
The TCAs are noncompetitive inhibitors: they do not compete with substrate for the same binding site. Instead, the bound TCA molecule stabilizes the occluded state of the transporter, trapping the substrate and impeding its release to either the intracellular milieu or extracellular solution. In LeuT, clomipramine reduces the off-rate of [3H]leucine by as much as 700-fold.
Clomipramine is the most potent TCA inhibitor of LeuT (IC50 ~250 uM), followed by imipramine (IC50 ~2,090 uM). The higher potency of clomipramine is partly explained by an additional polar interaction between its chlorine atom and Gln34 (3.1 A), a feature absent in imipramine and desipramine.
The TCA-binding pocket is conserved from bacterial to human NSS proteins. Sequence variations at the tip of the EL4 hairpin and the extracellular end of TM10 determine the different affinities of TCAs for different NSS family members.
