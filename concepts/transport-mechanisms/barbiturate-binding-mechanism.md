# Barbiturate Binding Mechanism in Pentameric Ligand-Gated Ion Channels

## Overview
Barbiturates are sedative-hypnotic drugs that modulate pentameric ligand-gated ion channels (pLGICs) by binding within the central ion channel pore. They inhibit cationic pLGICs (e.g., nicotinic acetylcholine receptors, 5-HT3 receptors, GLIC) by stabilizing the closed state of the channel, while they activate anionic pLGICs (e.g., GABAA and glycine receptors) by binding at subunit interfaces. The first atomic-resolution structures of barbiturates bound to a pLGIC were obtained using the prokaryotic GLIC channel, revealing a binding site between the 2' and 9' residues of the pore-lining M2 helix.


## Mechanism/Details
The barbiturate binding site in the GLIC ion channel pore is located between residues at levels 2' (threonine) and 9' (isoleucine) of the M2 helix. The primary interactions are hydrogen bonds between the barbiturate ring carbonyl groups and the 6' serine residues lining the pore. Van der Waals interactions between the barbiturate C5 aliphatic tails and the 9' isoleucines provide additional stabilization. The orientation of the barbiturate within the pore depends on the structure of the C5 tail: unbranched tails allow the pyrimidine ring to orient sideways (as seen with bromobarbital and selenocyanobarbital), while branched tails (e.g., thiopental with an extra methyl group near the ring) cause an inverted orientation to avoid steric clashes. Docking calculations across closed, open, and desensitized states of GLIC show that barbiturates preferentially stabilize the closed state, explaining their inhibitory mechanism on cationic pLGICs. The same pore site also binds other general anesthetics including xenon, bromoform, and propofol (predicted).


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [GLIC (Gloeobacter violaceus Ion Channel)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/glic/) — The locally-closed (LC) mutant K33C-L246C of GLIC was co-crystallized with bromobarbital (3.3 Å, PDB 5L4H), selenocyanobarbital (2.99 Å, PDB 5L47), and thiopental (3.5 Å, PDB 5L4E), all revealing barbiturate binding in the central ion channel pore between residues 2' and 9'.
