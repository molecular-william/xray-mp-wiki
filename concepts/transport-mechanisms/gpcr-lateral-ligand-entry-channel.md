# GPCR Lateral Ligand Entry Channel

## Overview
Some G-protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs), particularly those that bind lipophilic ligands, use a membrane-buried lateral channel between transmembrane helices IV and V to provide ligand access directly from the lipid bilayer, bypassing the extracellular aqueous environment. This lateral ligand entry channel was first structurally characterized in the human MT1 and MT2 melatonin receptors, where the orthosteric binding pocket is sealed from extracellular solvent by extracellular loop 2 (ECL2). The lateral channel connects the binding site to the lipid bilayer, allowing lipophilic ligands like melatonin to enter directly from the membrane. In MT2, an additional narrow opening towards the extracellular solvent (the ECL opening) provides a secondary access route. The relative importance of these access routes differs between receptor subtypes and contributes to differential ligand residence times and subtype selectivity.


## Mechanism/Details
The lateral ligand entry channel is formed by an opening between transmembrane helices IV and V that connects the orthosteric binding pocket to the surrounding lipid bilayer. In melatonin receptors, the channel entrance lies approximately 6.5-10.9 A below the membrane boundary. The channel diameter and the conformation of key residues (particularly Y^5.38) determine the rate of ligand access and residence time. A wider channel (as in MT1) allows faster ligand entry/exit, while a narrower channel (as in MT2) restricts access and prolongs residence time. In MT2, an additional opening through the extracellular loops (ECL opening) bounded by residues T191^ECL2, Q194^ECL2, and Y294^7.39 provides a secondary path. The distinct conformations of residue Y^7.39 between MT1 (closed, seals the opening) and MT2 (open) determine whether the ECL route is functionally important. Mutations that widen or constrict either access route systematically alter ligand residence time, confirming the functional relevance of both pathways.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- human-mt1-melatonin-receptor — MT1 has a lateral channel between TM4 and TM5 with Y187^5.38 in an 'open' conformation, creating a wider channel. The ECL opening is sealed by Y281^7.39. Kinetic studies confirm the lateral channel as the primary access route, with A171M mutation increasing residence time.
- human-mt2-melatonin-receptor — MT2 has a more constricted lateral channel (Y200^5.38 in a 'closed' conformation) and an additional ECL opening (Y294^7.39 in an 'open' conformation). The narrower channel results in longer ligand residence time (up to 20 h with A171M mutation). The ECL opening provides a potential route for more polar ligands, offering opportunities for subtype-selective drug design.
