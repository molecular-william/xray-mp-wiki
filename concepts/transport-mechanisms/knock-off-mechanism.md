# Knock-off Mechanism of Ion Permeation

## Overview
The knock-off mechanism is a model of ion permeation through selectivity filters in which a bound ion at the highest affinity site must be displaced ("knocked off") by an incoming ion from the extracellular side before translocation can proceed. This mechanism requires high local ion concentration at the channel entrance and produces characteristic ion selectivity and permeation kinetics based on the relative affinities of ions for different binding sites within the pore.


## Mechanism/Details


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Epithelial Calcium Channel TRPV6](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/trpv6/) — [Epithelial Calcium Channel TRPV6](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/trpv6/) uses a Ca²⁺ knock-off permeation mechanism. Site 1 at the selectivity filter (D541 plane) is constitutively occupied by Ca²⁺ due to electrostatic repulsion between D541 side chains. A new Ca²⁺ ion recruited from the extracellular vestibule must knock off the bound Ca²⁺ from Site 1. After knock-off, the displaced Ca²⁺ moves to Site 2 (coordinated by T538 through its hydration shell), then to Site 3 in the hydrophobic cavity. The rate-limiting step is knock-off from Site 1, requiring high local Ca²⁺ concentration. Ba²⁺ shows different permeation kinetics with higher affinity for Site 2, making knock-off from Site 2 to Site 3 the rate-limiting step. Gd³⁺ blocks divalent permeation through strong binding at Site 1.
- CaVAb Bacterial Voltage-Gated Calcium Channel — The engineered Ca²⁺-selective channel CaVAb was proposed to use a knock-off mechanism of Ca²⁺ permeation. Ca²⁺ binds in the middle of the selectivity filter, and permeation proceeds through sequential knock-off between binding sites. The [Epithelial Calcium Channel TRPV6](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/trpv6/) knock-off mechanism shares similarities with CaVAb in that the main binding site at the selectivity filter is constitutively occupied.
