# RCK Domain (Regulator of Conductance of K+)

## Overview
An RCK (Regulator of Conductance of K+) domain is a conserved structural module found in ligand-gated K+ channels and bacterial K+ uptake/efflux machinery (KTN domain). RCK domains assemble into gating rings that regulate channel opening in response to intracellular signals such as Ca2+, pH, or nucleotide binding.

## Mechanism/Details
RCK domains adopt a conserved Rossmann-like fold with alternating alpha-helices and beta-strands. They function by assembling into tetrameric or octameric gating rings where RCK modules from adjacent subunits interact in a head-to-tail fashion via conserved alphaD and alphaE helices. Ligand binding sites are typically located at interdomain interfaces or in conserved pockets. In BK channels, each subunit contains two tandem RCK domains (RCK1 and RCK2) that are structurally similar despite low sequence homology. RCK1 shares high sequence similarity with prokaryotic counterparts, while RCK2 is more divergent. Hydrophobic residues at the assembly interface (Ile441, Met442, Ile445 on alphaD of RCK1; Ile821, Leu822, Leu825 on alphaD of RCK2) mediate the head-to-tail interaction.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [BK channel (human Slo1)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/human-bk-channel-slo1/) — Two tandem RCK domains per subunit (RCK1 residues ~329-616, RCK2 residues ~684-1059). RCK1 contains Asp367 and Glu374/Glu399 Ca2+/Mg2+ sites; RCK2 contains Ca2+ bowl.
- MthK channel — Prokaryotic Ca2+-gated K+ channel; homodimeric RCK assembly forms the gating ring. First RCK domain structure determined.
- E. coli K+ channel (Kch) — Prokaryotic RCK domain first identified; established the RCK family.
