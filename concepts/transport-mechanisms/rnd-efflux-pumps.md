# RND Efflux Pumps

## Overview
Resistance nodulation cell division (RND) transporters are a family of multidrug efflux pumps found in gram-negative bacteria. They utilize the proton electrochemical gradient to energize efflux of chemically diverse antibiotics and other compounds out of the bacterial cell. RND systems assemble into tripartite complexes spanning both the inner and outer membranes, consisting of an RND inner membrane transporter, a periplasmic membrane fusion protein (MFP), and an outer membrane factor (OMF) channel. The two most extensively characterized RND systems are the Escherichia coli AcrA-AcrB-ToIC complex and the Pseudomonas aeruginosa MexA-MexB-OprM complex.
