# Switch-Loop Gating in RND Multidrug Transporters

## Overview
The switch-loop (also called the G-loop or substrate-gating loop) is a conformationally flexible loop in the periplasmic domain of RND-type multidrug efflux pumps such as [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/). It separates the access pocket (in the L monomer lateral periplasmic cleft from the deep binding pocket (in the T monomer). The loop is defined by a conserved glycine-rich sequence motif (G616-P620 in E. coli [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/)) that undergoes conformational changes during the L-to-T transition, allowing substrate to move from the initial binding site to the deep binding pocket.


## Mechanism/Details
In the L (loose) conformation, the lateral periplasmic cleft forms an access pocket where drugs initially bind. During the L-to-T transition, the switch-loop repositions, opening a pathway from the access pocket to the deep binding pocket of the T (tight) monomer. The loop contains two critical [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) residues (G619, G621 in [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/)) that provide the backbone flexibility needed for this conformational change. Mutation of these glycines to proline (as in the G616N variant) restricts loop mobility and impairs transport of large substrates such as macrolide antibiotics while retaining activity toward smaller substrates.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) multidrug efflux transporter — The [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/)/doxorubicin co-crystal structure (PDB 4DX5, 2.25 A) shows three [Doxorubicin - Anthracycline Anticancer Drug](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/doxorubicin/) molecules - one in the deep binding pocket of the T monomer and two stacked in the access pocket of the L monomer, separated by the switch-loop (G616-P620).
- [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) multidrug efflux transporter — The G616N [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) variant (PDB 4DX7, 2.0 A) shows an altered switch-loop conformation and is deficient in macrolide transport, confirming the functional importance of the loop.
