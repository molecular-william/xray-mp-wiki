# Dual Drug-Binding Sites in MFS Multidrug Transporters

## Overview
X-ray structures of the MdfA variant I239T/G354E revealed that this multidrug transporter possesses two discrete, non-overlapping substrate-binding sites within the central cavity formed between the N and C domains. The two sites (LDAO1 in the N-domain interacting with D34, and LDAO2 in the C-domain interacting with G354E) are separated by ~20 Å and can independently bind and export drugs. This dual-site architecture enables I239T/G354E to transport two monocationic drugs per transport cycle (2:2 drug/H+ stoichiometry) or one short dicationic drug (1:2 stoichiometry), with each site associated with its own protonation residue. The existence of two discrete drug-binding sites helps explain the extraordinarily broad substrate specificity of MdfA by accommodating one or two drugs of various sizes and shapes.

## Mechanism/Details
In the MdfA variant I239T/G354E, each substrate-binding site is coupled to a distinct protonation site: LDAO1 is proximal to D34 and LDAO2 is proximal to G354E. Upon binding of zwitterionic LDAO, two protons are released (one from D34 via LDAO1, one from G354E via LDAO2). The two sites are functionally non-equivalent — the LDAO1/D34 site is essential for transport activity, while the LDAO2/G354E site is functionally subordinate. The dual-site architecture can accommodate: (a) two monocationic drugs that bind to LDAO1 and LDAO2 separately, or (b) one short dicationic compound (e.g., methyl viologen) that binds at the LDAO1/D34 site while utilizing long-range electrostatic interactions with G354E for charge neutralization. Long-range charge-charge interactions, enhanced >10-fold by the low-dielectric membrane environment, facilitate recognition of zwitterionic and cationic substrates at these sites without strict geometric requirements.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- mdfa-i239t-g354e — The LDAO-bound crystal structure of I239T/G354E (PDB 6OOM, 2.2 Å) directly reveals two bound LDAO molecules (LDAO1 and LDAO2) occupying distinct, non-overlapping sites. Fluorescence measurements confirmed 2.0 ± 0.1 H+ released per I239T/G354E molecule upon LDAO binding.
- mdfa — Wild-type MdfA (single protonation site D34) has only one functional drug-binding site, with drug/H+ stoichiometry of 1:1. The I239T mutation and G354E addition create the second binding/protonation site.
