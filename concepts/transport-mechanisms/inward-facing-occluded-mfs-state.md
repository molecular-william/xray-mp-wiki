# Inward Facing Occluded State in MFS Transporters

## Overview
The inward facing occluded state is a conformational intermediate in the transport cycle of Major Facilitator Superfamily (MFS) transporters. In this state, the transporter remains open to the cytoplasm but one or more pore-lining helices bend toward the central substrate-binding site, forming a constriction gate that prevents substrate from leaving or entering. This state represents a transition between the fully inward open and fully occluded conformations during the alternating access mechanism.


## Mechanism/Details
Two distinct occluded conformations have been identified for MFS transporters: outward facing occluded and inward facing occluded. The inward facing occluded state is formed by bending of one or more pore-lining helices on the cytoplasmic side — specifically TM4, TM5, TM10, and/or TM11 — toward the binding site. In PepTSt from Streptococcus thermophilus, the P3_121 crystal form (PDB 5MMT) captures the transporter in an inward facing state where lobe TM10-TM11 is markedly bent toward the central pore, suggesting either a fully occluded state or an intermediate between inward open and occluded. A slight domain rotation is also observed in dipeptide-bound C222_1 structures, likely contributing to the occlusion mechanism.
The A-motif (consensus G-X-X-X-(D/E)-(R/K)-X-G-[X]-(R/K)-(R/K) in loop TM2-TM3 or TM8-TM9) plays a key regulatory role. In the inward facing occluded state, the salt bridge between the second basic residue of the A-motif and a conserved acidic residue in TM4 (or TM10) is broken, allowing these helices to bend toward the pore. In PepTSt, the relevant salt bridge residues are missing, suggesting that regulated bending of TM4 may be replaced by alternative mechanisms.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [PepTSt (S. thermophilus)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/mfs-transporters/peptst-transporter/) — The P3_121 crystal form (PDB 5MMT) shows TM10-TM11 lobe bending toward the central pore, representing an inward facing occluded or intermediate state. Similar bending is observed in C222_1 structures with bound dipeptides.
- XylE (E. coli) — An inward facing occluded MFS structure where only lobe TM10-TM11 is bent, providing a precedent for PepTSt observations.
- EmrD (E. coli) — Inward facing occluded structures featuring bending of both TM4-TM5 and TM10-TM11 lobes.
