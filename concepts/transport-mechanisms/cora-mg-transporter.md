# CorA Mg2+ Transporter

## Overview
CorA is a Mg2+ transporter that forms a pentameric structure. It uses a common pore-loop mechanism for ion selectivity and transport. The transporter is regulated by intracellular Mg2+ levels through an allosteric mechanism.


## Mechanism/Details
CorA is a Mg2+ transporter that forms a pentameric structure. It uses a common pore-loop mechanism for ion selectivity, with a GMN signature motif forming the selectivity filter. The transporter is regulated by intracellular Mg2+ levels through an allosteric mechanism involving ligand binding at interfacial sites between subunits. Gating is accomplished by rigid-body motions between symmetric (closed) and asymmetric (open) assemblies of the cytosolic domain subunits. The cytoplasmic pore contains Mg2+ binding sites including a N249/Q252 polar ring site and a Q242 site. Cobalt hexamine can compete with Mg2+ at the pore site, providing structural evidence for hexa-hydrated Mg2+ permeation. The full-length protein shows 10-fold higher Mg2+ affinity than the isolated cytoplasmic domain, reflecting stabilization by the membrane domain.
