# Twin-Arginine Translocation (TAT) Pathway

## Overview


## Mechanism/Details


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Aquifex aeolicus TatC — Crystal structure of TatC from A. aeolicus revealed a glove-like structure with a lipid-exposed pocket for signal sequence binding. The twin-arginine signal sequence is recognized by TatC, leading to conformational changes that facilitate higher-order TatABC complex assemblies and pore formation by TatA.

- Escherichia coli TatC — Mutagenesis and crosslinking studies of E. coli TatC mapped signal sequence binding residues to the N terminus of TM1 and Cyt1 region. The twin-arginine consensus motif (RRxFLK) is recognized by TatC, distinguishing TAT substrates from SEC pathway substrates.

- Aquifex aeolicus TatC (Se-MAD structure) — High-resolution (3.5 A) crystal structure of A. aeolicus TatC revealed a cupped-hand architecture with seven transmembrane helices. The structure identified two functionally important surface sites: surface site (1) on the cytoplasmic face for signal peptide binding via Glu 9 and Glu 96, and surface site (2) for TatB/TatA interaction. TM5 and TM6 were found to be too short to span the membrane, potentially assisting in forming the protein translocation pathway through bilayer distortion.
