# Scissor-Switch Gating in POT Family Transporters

## Overview
The scissor-switch gating mechanism is a specialized form of the alternating-access mechanism proposed for POT (proton-dependent oligopeptide transporter) family members. It describes how the periplasmic gate (H1, H2, H7, H8) and cytoplasmic gate (H4, H5, H10, H11) open and close in a concerted double scissor motion.

## Mechanism/Details
In the scissor-switch model, the transporter is described as two pairs of scissors, with each pair representing either the N- or C-terminal half of the protein. Each blade in a pair embodies one of the four inverted-topology repeat units. The gating motions involve: (1) the periplasmic gate closes upon peptide and proton binding, characterized by movement of helices H7 and H8 toward H1 and H2; (2) the cytoplasmic gate then opens as salt bridges (D136-K439, K84-D79) break and helices H10 and H11 swing away from H4 and H5. The C-terminal scissors move more than the N-terminal scissors. Scaffold helices (H3, H6, H9, H12) are less mobile and provide a platform for gating helices.
