# Sodium Solute Symporter (SSS) Family

## Overview
The sodium solute symporter (SSS) family (SLC5 in humans) is a large family of secondary active transporters that use Na+ electrochemical gradients to drive the uptake of extracellular substrates. Members include the sodium/galactose transporter (vSGLT), human SGLT1/SGLT2 (glucose transporters), the sodium/iodide symporter (NIS), and bacterial sialic acid transporters like SiaT from Proteus mirabilis. SSS family members adopt the LeuT-fold with 14 transmembrane helices arranged in two inverted five-helix repeats (Tm1-Tm5 and Tm6-Tm10). The first structural representative was vSGLT from Vibrio parahaemolyticus, revealing the central substrate and Na+ binding sites. The high-resolution (1.95 Å) structure of SiaT in an outward-open conformation provided the first view of a bacterial sialic acid SSS transporter and revealed a novel Na3 sodium binding site that explains the variable Na+:substrate stoichiometry within the family.

## Mechanism/Details
SSS family transporters operate through an alternating access mechanism. The LeuT-fold consists of two inverted repeats that form a central substrate-binding site accessible from alternating sides of the membrane. Substrate and Na+ ions bind to the outward-open conformation, triggering conformational changes that occlude the substrate and then open the cytoplasmic gate to release the cargo into the cell. The number of Na+ binding sites varies across the family: vSGLT and hSGLT2 have one Na+ site (Na2), while SiaT and hSGLT1 have two (Na2 and Na3). The conserved Na2 site is located between Tm1 and Tm8 near the unwound region of Tm1. The Na3 site, discovered in SiaT, is positioned close to Na2 but does not contact the substrate directly; instead it allosterically stabilizes substrate binding. The Na3 site requires conserved residues including Asp182, Ser345, and Ser346, and is present in ~19.6% of SSS sequences.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- SiaT sialic acid transporter from Proteus mirabilis — First high-resolution (1.95 Å) substrate-bound outward-open structure of a bacterial sialic acid SSS transporter. Revealed the Na3 sodium binding site. PDB: 5NV9.
- vSGLT (sodium galactose transporter from Vibrio parahaemolyticus) — First structural representative of the SSS family. 14 TM helices with LeuT-fold. Single Na+ binding site (Na2 only). Inward-open conformation. PDB: 3DH4.
- hSGLT1 (human sodium/glucose cotransporter 1) — Human SSS family member with 2 Na+:1 substrate stoichiometry. Contains both Na2 and Na3 sites. Key transporter for intestinal glucose absorption. Mutations cause glucose-galactose malabsorption.
- hSGLT2 (human sodium/glucose cotransporter 2) — Human SSS family member with 1 Na+:1 substrate stoichiometry. Lacks Na3 site. Key target for SGLT2 inhibitor diabetes drugs (dapagliflozin, empagliflozin).
