# Channel 4 (CH4) - PN2/PC1 Drug Transport Pathway in AcrB

## Overview
Channel 4 (CH4) is a proposed drug transport pathway in the AcrB multidrug efflux pump that connects the TM1/TM2 groove (membrane-localized drug entry site) to the deep binding pocket in the periplasmic porter domain. CH4 proceeds through the PN2/PC1 subdomain interface and is distinct from previously described channels CH1, CH2, and CH3. Key residues involved in CH4-mediated transport include Y327 and S630 at the intersection of multiple channels, with intermediate binding sites S1' (at the TM-periplasm interface) and S4 (a cleft between PC1 and PN2 subdomains). The pathway is specific for carboxylated substrates such as fusidic acid and carboxylated beta-lactams and does not involve the access pocket.
