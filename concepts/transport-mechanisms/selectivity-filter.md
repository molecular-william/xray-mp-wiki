# Ion Channel Selectivity Filter

## Overview
The selectivity filter is a narrow region of the ion channel pore that determines which ions can pass through. It is formed by conserved amino acid sequences that create specific coordination geometries for target ions. The architecture and chemistry of the selectivity filter differ between sodium, potassium, and calcium channels, reflecting their distinct physiological roles.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [NAVAB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navab/) Bacterial Voltage-Gated Sodium Channel — The [NAVAB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navab/) selectivity filter has a TLESW sequence. Key residues include Glu177 (high field-strength site), Leu176, and Thr175. Potential Na+ ion coordination sites are at Site_HFS (E177), Site_CEN (L176*), and Site_IN (T175*). Putative water molecules coordinate Na+ ions in square pyramidal geometry off the Leu176 and Thr175 backbone carbonyls.
- [KCSA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kcsa/) Potassium Channel — The [KCSA](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/kcsa/) selectivity filter has the canonical TVGYG sequence. Four K+ binding sites (site 1-4) are occupied by K+ ions in either site 1,3 or site 2,4 configuration with intervening water molecules. The filter coordinates K+ ions through backbone carbonyl oxygens in an ideal geometry.
- [NaChBac Bacterial Voltage-Gated Sodium Channel](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/nachbac/) Bacterial Sodium Channel — [NaChBac Bacterial Voltage-Gated Sodium Channel](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/nachbac/) shares the conserved TLESW selectivity filter sequence with [NAVAB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/navab/). Cation selectivity filter studies show the filter determines Na+ over K+ selectivity. Ser178 side-chain does not appear to play an essential role in Na+ selectivity.
- CaVAb Bacterial Voltage-Gated Calcium Channel — CaVAb selectivity filter has sequence FQVMTLDDWSDG. Key substitutions include E177 and D178 (negatively charged residues creating a calcium-coordinating environment) and D181. Three Ca2+ binding sites identified at the selectivity filter through anomalous difference Fourier maps. Systematic mutagenesis of filter residues (E177D, D181N, D181M, S180D) reveals position-specific contributions to ion selectivity between Ca2+, Ba2+, and Na+.
