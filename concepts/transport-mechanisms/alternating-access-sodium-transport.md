# Alternating Access Model for Light-Driven Sodium Transport

## Overview

The alternating access model for light-driven sodium transport describes how Krokinobacter eikastus rhodopsin 2 (KR2) achieves active, unidirectional sodium translocation across the membrane. In this model, the substrate (sodium) binds while the protein rearranges to allow release without backflow. Time-resolved serial femtosecond crystallography (TR-SFX) structures from femtoseconds to milliseconds have provided high-resolution snapshots of each step, illustrating how minimal structural changes generate ion selectivity and prevent back leakage.

## Mechanism/Details

KR2 does not bind substrate close to the retinal Schiff base in the resting state. After light activation, retinal isomerization (all-trans to 13-cis within femtoseconds) initiates a cascade of structural changes. The alternating access model proceeds through four distinct stages: (1) Early photoproducts (800 fs-2 ps): retinal isomerization and local rearrangements in the binding pocket, including shift of water w406 and counterion D116. (2) Nanosecond backbone rearrangements (1-16 ns): the C20 methyl group of retinal pushes sideways against V117 in helix C, transmitting forces into the transmembrane helical bundle. (3) Microsecond electrostatic gating (1-150 µs): deprotonation of the retinal SB opens an electrostatic gate; Y218 and S254 approach the retinal; helix C shifts ~1 Å. (4) Millisecond sodium translocation (1-20 ms): at 1 ms, sodium binds between N112 and D251 (intracellular access opened); at 20 ms, the ion is released via a second binding site between E11, N106, and E160 (extracellular exit opened). The reprotonation of the SB blocks backflow, sealing the intracellular gate before the extracellular gate opens, ensuring the ion cannot flow backwards. This strict temporal ordering of gate openings is the hallmark of the alternating access mechanism.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work

- Krokinobacter eikastus Rhodopsin 2 (KR2) — Complete structural trajectory from dark state to 20 ms intermediate (PDBs 6TK7/6TK6 → 6TK5 → 6TK4 → 6TK3 → 6TK2 → 6TK1). Demonstrates alternating access with intracellular sodium entry at 1 ms and extracellular release at 20 ms.
