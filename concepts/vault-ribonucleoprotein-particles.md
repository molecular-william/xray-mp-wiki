# Vault Ribonucleoprotein Particles

## Overview
Vaults are large ribonucleoprotein (RNP) particles found in a wide variety of eukaryotes. They present a strikingly conserved barrel-shaped structure with an invaginated waist and two protruding caps when imaged by electron microscopy. Two-dimensional (2D) crystals of naturally produced vaults in murine cells show hexagonal packing with lattice parameters defined by the vault barrel diameter. Three-dimensional (3D) crystals of vaults from monkey cells reach approximately 0.15 x 0.15 x 0.02 mm³ in size, with flat triangular morphology and R32 space group symmetry, diffracting weakly to approximately 10 Å resolution. The 3D crystals appear to form by layering vaults that retain the hexagonal organization seen in 2D crystals. The 2D and 3D crystal symmetries imply neighbor interactions following 6-fold and 3-fold dihedral symmetry, respectively.
