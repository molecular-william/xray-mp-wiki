# TMBIM Protein Family

## Overview
The TMBIM (transmembrane Bax inhibitor motif) protein family comprises highly conserved membrane proteins across all domains of life that regulate Ca2+ homeostasis and cell death. TMBIM proteins function as Ca2+ leak channels that prevent overloading of intracellular Ca2+ stores, thereby promoting cell survival and resistance to cell death stimuli. The family is characterized by a conserved aspartyl dyad (Asp-X-Asp) that serves as a pH sensor regulating pore opening. In human TMBIM6 (BI-1), mutation of the equivalent Asp213 to arginine abolishes Ca2+ flux and the protective role against apoptosis. The bacterial homolog [BSYETJ](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/bsyetj/) provides structural evidence for a seven-transmembrane helix architecture with lateral TM2 movement controlling pore access. The pH-dependent gating mechanism involves protonation of the conserved aspartyl dyad, disengagement of a conformational latch (Arg60), and rearrangement of the pore-lining helix.

## Mechanism/Details
The TMBIM (transmembrane Bax inhibitor motif) protein family is a group of evolutionarily conserved membrane proteins that regulate Ca2+ homeostasis, cell death, and disease progression. TMBIM proteins are found in prokaryotes, eukaryotes, viruses, and archaea. The family includes BI-1 (TMBIM1), TMBIM2, TMBIM3/GRINA, TMBIM4/GAAP, TMBIM5/Life Expectancy Protein 6, and TMBIM6/BI-1. Human TMBIM6 (BI-1) functions as a Ca2+ leak channel from the endoplasmic reticulum, with basal channel activity promoted by low pH. TMBIM4 (GAAP) lowers ER and Golgi Ca2+ stores and enhances store-operated Ca2+ entry. The bacterial homolog [BSYETJ](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/bsyetj/) from Bacillus subtilis provides structural insights into the family, revealing a seven-transmembrane helix fold with a conserved Asp171-Asp195 aspartyl dyad that serves as a pH sensor. Protonation of Asp171 triggers conformational changes leading to pore opening and Ca2+ flux.
