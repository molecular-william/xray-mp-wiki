## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- /xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/k2p-2-1-trek-1/ — The K2P 2.1 (TREK-1) modulator pocket is located at the P1 pore helix and M4 transmembrane helix intrasubunit interface, behind the selectivity filter. Small-molecule activators [ML335 (N-aryl-sulfonamide K2P activator)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ml335/) and [ML402 (thiophene-carboxamide K2P activator)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ml402/) bind to this pocket as molecular wedges, restricting interdomain interface movement and stabilizing the C-type gate in the leak mode. Lys271 forms a key cation-pi interaction with both activators.
- /xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/traak/ — The K2P 4.1 ([TRAAK](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/voltage-gated-channels/traak/)) G124I gain-of-function mutation reshapes the K2P modulator pocket through structural consequences similar to [ML335 (N-aryl-sulfonamide K2P activator)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ml335/)/ML402 binding, causing an outward M4 movement and repositioning of Phe134 equivalent residue. This confirms the modulator pocket as a functional site for K2P channel activation.
