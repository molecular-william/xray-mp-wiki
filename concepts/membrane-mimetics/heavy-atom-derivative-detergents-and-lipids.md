# Heavy Atom Derivative Detergents and Lipids for Membrane Protein Crystallography

## Overview
Heavy atom derivative detergents and lipids are artificially synthesized amphiphilic compounds that contain heavy atoms (halogens such as Br, I, or selenium) incorporated into the detergent or lipid structure. These compounds serve two purposes in membrane protein structural biology: (1) they enable de novo phasing of X-ray crystallographic data via isomorphous replacement (SIR, MIR) or anomalous diffraction (SAD, SIRAS) methods, and (2) they allow identification and visualization of lipid/detergent binding sites on membrane proteins through the strong anomalous scattering signal of the heavy atom. The amphiphilic nature ensures that these compounds interact with membrane proteins in a manner similar to native lipids and detergents, making them minimally perturbing while providing a powerful phasing handle. Recent applications include use with [Serial Femtosecond Crystallography](/xray-mp-wiki/methods/structure-determination/serial-femtosecond-crystallography/) (SFX) at X-ray free-electron laser facilities for damage-free room-temperature structure determination.

## Mechanism/Details
The development of heavy atom derivative detergents and lipids involves several strategies:
(1) Halogen-labeled compounds: Bromine (Br) and iodine (I) atoms are incorporated into detergent/lipid structures. Br has good anomalous scattering at typical X-ray wavelengths (f'' ~ 2-4 e), while I exhibits excellent anomalous scattering at longer wavelengths (f'' = 8.6 e at 1.771 A). The iodine-based [HAD13a (Heavy-Atom Additive 13a)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/had13a/) (5-amino-2,4,6-triiodoisophthalic acid with a caprylic acid tail, CMC 4.6 mM) has been successfully used for de novo phasing of [Bacteriorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/bacteriorhodopsin/) and A2A adenosine receptor via SAD, SIR, and SIRAS methods in SFX. HAD16 contains one I and one Br per molecule and mimics a [Phosphatidylcholine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/phosphatidylcholine/) structure for lipid-protein interaction studies. Brominated phospholipids (mono-bromo DPPC, di-bromo phospholipids) are used to determine lipid chain positions in bilayers and reveal lipid binding sites on membrane protein surfaces.
(2) Selenium-labeled compounds: Selenium serves as a bioisostere for sulfur and methylene groups due to similar van der Waals radii (Se: 190 pm; CH2: 200 pm). Dodecyl-beta-D-selenomaltoside (SeDDM) is a heavy atom analog of [DDM](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/detergents/ddm/) used for MAD phasing, successfully applied to leukotriene C4 synthase and prokaryotic pentameric ligand-gated ion channel structures. Seleno-fatty acids and seleno-glycolipids provide alternative routes for introducing selenium into the hydrophobic region of membrane protein crystals.
(3) Application in SFX: The combination of heavy atom detergents with SFX is particularly powerful because SFX allows data collection at longer X-ray wavelengths (optimized for anomalous signal) without radiation damage. HAD13a-derivatized [Bacteriorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/bacteriorhodopsin/) microcrystals required only 7,000- 23,000 indexed images for successful phasing, demonstrating efficient membrane protein structure determination by experimental phasing at XFEL facilities.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Bacteriorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/bacteriorhodopsin/) — [HAD13a (Heavy-Atom Additive 13a)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/had13a/) iododetergent used for de novo SAD/SIR/SIRAS phasing of [Bacteriorhodopsin](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/rhodopsins/bacteriorhodopsin/) by SFX at SACLA. 23,000 indexed images at 2.1 A (SAD) or 7,000 at 3.3 A (SIRAS). PDB: 5B34 ([HAD13a (Heavy-Atom Additive 13a)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/had13a/) derivative).
- A2A Adenosine Receptor (A2A GPCR) — [HAD13a (Heavy-Atom Additive 13a)](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/had13a/) used for heavy atom labeling of A2A GPCR microcrystals obtained by LCP, demonstrating applicability to GPCRs.
- Leukotriene C4 synthase — SeDDM detergent used for MAD phasing of leukotriene C4 synthase structure (PDB 3B29).
- Prokaryotic pentameric ligand-gated ion channel ([ELIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/elic/)) — SeDDM used for phasing [ELIC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/cys-loop-receptors/elic/) structure (PDB 4IL4); detergent bundles clearly observed inside the pore.
