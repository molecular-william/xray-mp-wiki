# Periplasmic Drug-Binding Site in RND Efflux Pumps

## Overview
The periplasmic drug-binding site of [RND Efflux Pumps](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/rnd-efflux-pumps/) such as [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) is a binding pocket located within the deep external depression formed by the C-terminal periplasmic loop, distinct from the central cavity in the transmembrane domain. This site was first observed crystallographically in the N109A mutant of E. coli [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/), where five structurally diverse ligands ([Ciprofloxacin - Fluoroquinolone Antibiotic](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ciprofloxacin/), rhodamine 6G, [Ethidium - Fluorescent Intercalating Dye](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ethidium/), [Nafcillin](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/antibiotics/nafcillin/), and the peptidomimetic inhibitor MC) were found to bind to this external pocket in addition to the central cavity. The periplasmic binding site involves residues Phe664, Phe666, Glu673, Arg717, Asp566, and Ser715. Mutagenesis of these residues (particularly F664A, F666A, E673A) significantly decreased drug resistance, confirming functional relevance. This site explains genetic evidence implicating periplasmic domains in substrate recognition and specificity of RND transporters.


## Mechanism/Details
Ligand binding in the periplasmic pocket involves both hydrophobic contacts (primarily via Phe664 and Phe666) and electrostatic/dipolar interactions with backbone carbonyls and side-chain hydroxyls. Unlike typical drug-binding sites, few acidic residues directly neutralize positive charges on ligands. The binding is loose and expansive, resembling the binding mode seen in multidrug-binding regulators like QacR. Binding of the inhibitor MC induces an outward movement of residues 650-659 in the C-alpha-3 helix, involving a ~10-degree rotation that displaces Gln657 by 3.6 A, likely contributing to transport inhibition. The periplasmic binding site functions in concert with the central cavity binding site, suggesting a multi-step substrate recognition and export pathway.
