# X-Ray Radiation Damage in Protein Crystallography

## Overview
X-ray radiation damage is a fundamental limitation in macromolecular crystallography. Synchrotron radiation induces specific chemical and structural changes in protein crystals, including decarboxylation of acidic residues, disulfide bond breakage, loss of metal centers, retinal isomerization, and water molecule displacement. These changes can mimic functional conformational changes, leading to misinterpretation of structural data. The general maximum tolerable dose is 3 x 10^7 Gy (30 MGy), but specific damage occurs at much lower doses. For retinal proteins, retinal isomerization can begin at doses as low as 4 x 10^14 photons/mm^2 (approximately 1 MGy). Damage must be carefully distinguished from genuine functional structural rearrangements.
