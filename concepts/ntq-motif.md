# NTQ Motif in Chloride Pumping Rhodopsins

## Overview
The NTQ motif (Asn-Thr-Gln) is a conserved residue pattern in the ion conduction pathway of light-driven chloride-pumping rhodopsins (ClRs), distinguishing them from other microbial light-driven ion pumps.

## Mechanism/Details
The NTQ motif (Asn-Thr-Gln) is a conserved residue pattern in the ion conduction pathway of light-driven chloride-pumping rhodopsins (ClRs). It replaces the DTD motif found in bacteriorhodopsin (proton pump) and the TSA motif in halorhodopsin (chloride pump). The NTQ motif is central to chloride ion binding and transport in CIR.
