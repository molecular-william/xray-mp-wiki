# BRIL Fusion Technology

## Overview

BRIL (apocytochrome b562RIL) fusion technology is a widely used strategy for stabilizing G protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs) for structural studies. The thermostabilized mutant of apocytochrome b562 (M7W, H102I, R106L) is fused to GPCRs to enhance their thermal stability and crystallizability.

## Development

- **First described**: Chun et al. (2012), Structure 20:967–976
- **Concept**: "Fusion partner toolchest" — BRIL can be fused to multiple GPCRs at different sites
- **Impact**: Revolutionized GPCR structural biology; enabled structures of many previously intractable targets

## Fusion Sites

### N-terminal Fusion
- BRIL fused to the N-terminus of the GPCR
- Used for: AT1R (Zhang et al., Cell 2015, PDB: 4YAY)
- Advantage: Does not interfere with intracellular signaling interfaces

### Intracellular Loop 3 (ICL3) Fusion
- BRIL inserted into ICL3 of the GPCR
- Used for: β2AR, A2AR, 5-HT1B, 5-HT1AR, A1R, P2Y12R
- Advantage: ICL3 is often flexible and disordered; BRIL provides order
- Can be combined with nanobodies for additional stabilization
