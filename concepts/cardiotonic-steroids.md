# Cardiotonic Steroids

## Overview
Cardiotonic steroids (CTS), also known as cardiac glycosides, are a class of inhibitors that bind to the Na+,K+-ATPase and inhibit its functional activity. They are named for their positive effect on cardiac performance. Digoxin, extracted from the foxglove plant, has been used for treating heart failure since 1785. CTS bind with a stoichiometry of one molecule per one alpha subunit of the Na+,K+-ATPase. In addition to their pump inhibitory action, CTS initiate intracellular signaling when the pump serves as a receptor for endogenous CTS found in human blood at very low concentrations. The conformational rearrangements induced by CTS binding are a prerequisite for the initiation of signal transduction pathways.
