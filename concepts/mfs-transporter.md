# Major Facilitator Superfamily (MFS)

## Overview

The major facilitator superfamily (MFS) is the largest family of secondary transporters, containing thousands of integral membrane transporters across all domains of life. MFS transporters move a wide variety of substrates (sugars, drugs, ions, amino acids) across cell membranes using the proton-motive force or other ion gradients.

## Structural Architecture

- **12 transmembrane helices** divided into two six-helix bundles:
  - **N-domain**: Helices 1–6 (N-terminal half)
  - **C-domain**: Helices 7–12 (C-terminal half)
- Two domains connected by a cytosolic loop
- Substrate binding site located between the two domains
- Both domains undergo rigid-body rearrangement during transport

## Transport Mechanism: Alternating Access

MFS transporters operate via the **rocker-switch mechanism**:

1. **Inward-open**: Binding site accessible from cytoplasm
2. **Occluded**: Both gates closed, substrate trapped
3. **Outward-open**: Binding site accessible from extracellular space/periplasm

The transition between states involves rigid-body rotation of the N and C domains relative to each other, like a rocking motion.

## Transport Patterns

| Pattern | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| Uniport | Single substrate movement | Glucose uniporters (GLUTs) |
| Symport | Co-transport with H⁺ or Na⁺ | Lactose permease (LacY), [glucose](/reagents/ligands/glucose/)-H⁺ symporters |
| Antiport | Exchange of substrate for H⁺ | Drug-proton antiporters (DHA), Na⁺/H⁺ antiporters |

## MFS Subfamilies

- **DHA (Drug-H⁺ Antiporters)**: Multi-drug resistance transporters
- **MFS sugar transporters**: GLUTs, LacY
- **Aquaglyceroporins**: Glycerol and water transport
- **Nucleotide sugar transporters**
- **Ion-coupled transporters**

## Structural Biology

Multiple MFS structures have been solved in different conformations, providing snapshots of the transport cycle:
- **GlpT** ([glycerol](/reagents/additives/glycerol/)-3-phosphate transporter): Inward-facing
- **LacY** (lactose permease): Multiple conformations
- **EmrD** (multidrug efflux): Occluded
- **SotB** (drug-proton antiporter): 4 conformations (occluded, inward-facing, inward-open)
