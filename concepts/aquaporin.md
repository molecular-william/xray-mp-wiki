# Aquaporin Family

## Overview
Aquaporins are water channel proteins that facilitate water transport across
cell membranes. They form tetramers with each monomer containing a selective
filter for water molecules. The NPA (Asn-Pro-Ala) motifs and aromatic/arginine
(ar/R) selectivity filter are key structural features. The aquaporin
superfamily includes water-specific channels (aquaporins) and
aquaglyceroporins that also transport glycerol and other small solutes.
A universal mechanism of proton exclusion based on bipolar water orientation
established by the NPA motifs and helix dipoles applies to the entire AQP
family. The 2.5 A structure of E. coli AqpZ demonstrated that selectivity
results from both steric pore restriction and specific amino acid
substitutions regulating hydrophobic vs. hydrophilic substrate preference.
A 3.2 A tetrameric AqpZ (tAqpZ) structure revealed two distinct Arg-189
conformations among the four monomer channels, corresponding to open and
closed states at the narrowest channel constriction, providing structural
evidence for water permeation gating by Arg-189 side chain dynamics at
the selectivity filter.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [GlpF (Glycerol Facilitator)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/glpf/) — E. coli glycerol facilitator; member of the aquaglyceroporin subfamily; archetype for the bipolar water orientation proton exclusion mechanism
- [Aquaporin Z (AqpZ)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/aquaporin-z/) — E. coli water-specific aquaporin; conserved NPA motif mechanism; 2.5 A structure revealed selectivity filter architecture; 3.2 A tetrameric structure revealed Arg-189 open/closed gating mechanism
- [Human Aquaporin 2](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/human-aquaporin-2/) — Human water channel; kidney collecting duct; regulated by vasopressin
- [Human Aquaporin 4](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/human-aquaporin-4/) — Human water channel; brain and central nervous system
- [cpAQP1aa (Climbing Perch Aquaporin)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/cpaqp1aa/) — First structurally characterized fish aquaporin; 1.9 A structure reveals unique extracellular fold and novel loop C constriction region; proposed extracellular gating via phosphorylation of Tyr107 and Thr38
