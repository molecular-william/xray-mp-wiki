# Aquaporin Family

## Overview
Aquaporins (AQPs) are integral membrane channel proteins that mediate bidirectional water and selected small amphipathic molecule transport across cellular membranes. The AQP fold consists of six transmembrane helices (M1-M8, with M1-M2 and M5-M6 as half-helices) forming a right-handed bundle with the pore running through the center. AQPs occur as homotetramers with four monomer channels. Two subfamilies exist: water-selective channels and aquaglyceroporins (promiscuous for water and small amphipathic molecules). The AQP fold is highly conserved, with two NPA (Asn-Pro-Ala) motifs at the ends of M3 and M7 defining the selectivity filter.
