# Helix Shift Mechanism for Carboxylate Drug Transport

## Overview
The helix shift mechanism describes how carboxylate drugs are actively transported from the inner membrane leaflet to the periplasmic drug-binding pockets by the [AcrB multidrug efflux pump](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrB/). The mechanism is driven by protonation events at the catalytic residues D407 and D408 in the transmembrane domain, which trigger conformational changes during the T-to-O and O-to-L transitions of the [ACRB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/acrb/) protomer cycle.
