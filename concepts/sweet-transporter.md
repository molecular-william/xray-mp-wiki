# SWEET Transporter Family

## Overview
The SWEET family of sugar transporters is a widespread class of uniporters found across all domains of life. SWEET proteins are among the smallest characterized membrane transporters and operate via an alternating-access mechanism to transport sugars across cell membranes.


## Mechanism/Details
The SWEET (Sugars Will Eventually be Exported Transporter) family comprises a widespread class of sugar transporters found in bacteria, plants, and animals. SWEET transporters are among the smallest characterized transporters, typically less than 20 kDa, and operate as dimers of three-helix bundles. They function as uniporters, transporting a single sugar substrate without coupling to ion gradients. In plants, SWEET transporters play critical roles in phloem loading, nectar secretion, pollen development, seed filling, and host-pathogen interactions. In animals, they regulate metabolic homeostasis. The SWEET family was first identified through bioinformatics and has since been shown to transport a variety of sugars including glucose, galactose, and sucrose. Structural studies have revealed that SWEET transporters undergo conformational transitions between outward-open, occluded, and inward-open states following the alternating-access mechanism.
