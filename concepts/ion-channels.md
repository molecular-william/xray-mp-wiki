# Ion Channels

## Overview

Ion channels are transmembrane protein complexes that form aqueous pores through cell membranes, allowing selective passage of ions (K⁺, Na⁺, Ca²⁺, Cl⁻) down their electrochemical gradients. They are fundamental to electrical signaling in excitable cells (neurons, muscle) and regulate numerous cellular processes.

## Major Classes

### Voltage-Gated Channels

Channels that open/close in response to membrane potential changes:
- **NaV** (voltage-gated sodium): Action potential initiation and propagation
- **CaV** (voltage-gated calcium): Neurotransmitter release, muscle contraction
- **Kv** (voltage-gated potassium): Action potential repolarization

### Ligand-Gated Channels

Channels that open in response to ligand binding:
- **Kir** (inwardly rectifying potassium): Resting membrane potential maintenance
- **P2X** (ATP-gated): Purinergic signaling
- **nAChR** (nicotinic acetylcholine receptor): Neuromuscular transmission

### Mechanosensitive Channels

Channels that respond to membrane tension:
- **MscL/MscS** (bacterial): Osmotic pressure relief

## Gating Mechanisms

Ion channels employ various gating strategies:
1. **Activation gate**: Inner helix bundle crossing controls access to the pore
2. **Selectivity filter**: Conserved amino acid motif (e.g., TVGYG in K⁺ channels) selects for specific ions
3. **Inactivation gate**: Blocks conduction after opening (e.g., ball-and-chain, C-type inactivation)

## Structural Features

- **Tetrameric assembly**: Most K⁺ channels are homotetramers (four identical subunits)
- **Two transmembrane helices per subunit**: Inner and outer helices supporting a pore helix
- **Selectivity filter**: Extracellular loop containing the signature ion selectivity sequence
- **Cytoplasmic domains**: Extended domains for regulation (e.g., Kir channels have large cytoplasmic domains with immunoglobulin-like β-sandwich folds)

## Prokaryotic Models

Bacterial channels serve as structural models for eukaryotic counterparts:
- **KirBac** — Model for eukaryotic inwardly rectifying K⁺ channels
- **NaVAb** — Ancestral voltage-gated sodium channel model
- **KcsA** — First K⁺ channel structure; model for K⁺ selectivity and gating
