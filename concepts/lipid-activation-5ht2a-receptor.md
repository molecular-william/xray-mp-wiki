# Lipid Activation of 5-HT2A Receptor

## Overview
The human serotonin 2A receptor (5-HT2AR) is modulated by lipids including monoolein, oleamide, oleoylethanolamide (OEA), and 2-oleoylglycerol (2OG) through a specific side-extended pocket (SEP) formed by a conserved glycine residue at position G238^5.42. Crystal structures revealed that monoolein binds to the SEP, acting as a G protein-mediated calcium flux partial agonist at 5-HT2AR. This lipid sensitivity is unique among 5-HT2 receptors - 5-HT2BR and 5-HT2CR lack the conserved glycine, and lipids do not induce robust G protein signaling at these related receptors.


## Mechanism/Details
The SEP is formed by a cavity between TM5 and TM6 that is only present in 5-HT2AR due to the small side chain of G238^5.42. Other aminergic receptors have an alanine, serine, cysteine, or threonine at this position whose side chain blocks the cavity. Introducing a G238^5.42S substitution in 5-HT2AR to mimic other 5-HT receptors abolished the agonist activity of lipids but not of serotonin. 5-HT2BR and 5-HT2CR have the conserved glycine, but the side chain of F^5.38 disrupts the SEP. Monoolein acts as a positive allosteric modulator of 5-HT2AR-mediated β-arrestin2 recruitment in the presence of serotonin, but requires serotonin's second binding mode at the extended binding pocket (EBP) for this effect. In the absence of serotonin, monoolein shows only modest G protein partial agonism without detectable β-arrestin activity.
