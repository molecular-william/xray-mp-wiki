# FNT Family (Formate/Nitrite Transporter Family)

## Overview
The FNT (formate/nitrite transporter) family comprises pentameric membrane channel proteins that transport small anions including formate, nitrite, and hydrosulfide (HS-) across biological membranes. Members of this family share a common architecture of six transmembrane helices per protomer, arranged as five helical hairpin pairs. The channel features a substrate translocation pore within each monomer (not at the pentamer axis) with a selectivity filter composed of a cytoplasmic slit and central constriction ring. The FNT family is phylogenetically linked to various metabolic enzymes: genes encoding FNT proteins are often genetically linked to pyruvate formate lyase (pflBA) in bacteria or formate dehydrogenase (fdhAB) in archaea, to nitrite reductase (nirBD), or to sulfite reductase (asrABC). These genetic linkages suggest that FNT channels function as partners in sulfur and carbon metabolism pathways. Well-characterized FNT family members include FocA (formate channel A) from Escherichia coli and Vibrio cholerae, TehA from Haemophilus influenzae, NirC (nitrite channel) from various bacteria, and HSC (hydrosulfide channel) from Clostridium difficile.
