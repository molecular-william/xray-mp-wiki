# Manganese-Calcium Cluster (Mn4Ca Cluster)

## Overview
The manganese-calcium cluster (Mn4Ca cluster) is the catalytic center of the oxygen-evolving complex (OEC) in photosystem II, where water is oxidized to molecular oxygen. The cluster consists of four manganese ions and one calcium ion arranged in a ~hook~-shaped structure with a Mn3CaO4 pyramid connected asymmetrically to a fourth Mn ion. The cluster cycles through five S-states (S0 to S4) during the Kok cycle of water oxidation, accumulating four oxidizing equivalents before releasing O2. The Mn4Ca cluster is coordinated by carboxylate, amino acid, and oxo ligands from D1 and CP43 subunits of PSII.


## Mechanism/Details
The Mn4Ca cluster architecture was revealed at 3.0 A resolution in the Thermosynechococcus elongatus PSII structure (PDB 1K4C). The four Mn ions are numbered Mn1 to Mn4, starting from the highest electron density at the bend of the ~hook~. The cluster contains a Mn3CaO4 cubane-like unit connected to Mn4 via a Mn-O-Mn bridge. Five carboxylates are in positions that could act as bidentate ligands bridging different cations. The combination of bidentate ligands and mono- or di-mu-oxo bridges enhances the stability of the cation arrangement and facilitates rearrangement of mu-oxo bridges during the S-state cycle. Key coordinating residues include: Glu 189 (D1, ligates Mn1 and Ca2+), Asp 170 (D1, ligates Mn4), Glu 333 (D1, ligates Mn4), Asp 342 (D1, ligates Mn2), Glu 354 (D1, ligates Mn3), and Glu 333 (D1). Ca2+ is coordinated by Glu 189 of D1. The cluster coordination differs from the previously postulated cubane-like model, with none of the coordinating residues modeled as bridging two metal ions. Mn4 is the most prone to radiation damage and represents the first Mn ion bound during cluster assembly. The oxidation state distribution in the S1 state is probably Mn(III)2Mn(IV)2, with Mn1 and Mn3 attaining either +3 or +4, and Mn4 probably remaining in a lower oxidation state throughout the S-state cycle.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Photosystem II from Thermosynechococcus elongatus (3.0 A, PDB 1K4C):
  The Mn4Ca cluster in the S1 state shows a hook-shaped arrangement with
  four Mn ions and one Ca2+. Mn-Mn distances refined with EXAFR restraints.
  Glu 189 and Ala 344 (D1) identified as Ca2+ ligands. Mn4 coordinated by
  Asp 170 and Glu 333 (D1).
- Photosystem II from spinach (2.9 A, PDB 1W5C): S1 state structure
  revealing Mn4Ca cluster coordination by D1 and CP43 residues.
- X-ray spectroscopy of PSII single crystals: Progressive X-ray damage
  to the Mn4Ca cluster reveals Mn4 as the most radiation-sensitive ion.
