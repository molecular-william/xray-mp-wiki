# Enzyme Rhodopsins

## Overview
Enzyme rhodopsins are a recently discovered class of microbial
rhodopsins that combine a light-sensitive N-terminal rhodopsin domain
with a C-terminal enzyme domain, enabling light-dependent enzymatic
activity. They are found in eukaryotes such as fungi, green algae, and
choanoflagellates. Unlike conventional microbial rhodopsins (ion pumps,
channels, or sensors), enzyme rhodopsins directly couple light
detection to enzymatic output, making them valuable optogenetic tools.
Known types include rhodopsin phosphodiesterase (Rh-PDE), rhodopsin
guanylyl cyclase (Rh-GC), and histidine kinase rhodopsins (HKRs).
Enzyme rhodopsins feature an 8-TM topology, with an N-terminal extra
transmembrane helix (TM0) not found in canonical 7-TM microbial
rhodopsins.
