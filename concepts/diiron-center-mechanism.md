# Diiron Center Mechanism in Membrane Desaturases

## Overview
The diiron center in membrane-bound desaturases, exemplified by [Stearoyl-CoA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/stearoyl-coa/) desaturase 1 (SCD1), features a unique coordination geometry where nine highly conserved histidine residues ligate two metal ions separated by approximately 6.4 angstroms. This distance is significantly longer than the 3-4 angstrom separation found in soluble diiron enzymes, and unlike soluble counterparts, there is no carboxylate bridge between the metal ions. This distinct architecture suggests a novel mechanism for oxygen activation that differs from the classical cis-mu-1,2-peroxo and diferryl intermediates observed in soluble diiron enzymes.


## Mechanism/Details


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Mouse [Stearoyl-CoA](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/stearoyl-coa/) Desaturase 1 (mSCD1) — The diiron center in SCD1 is coordinated by nine conserved histidine residues from three motifs: two HXXHH motifs and one HX4H motif. Metal ion M1 is coordinated by four histidines (His116, His121, His153, His156) and a water molecule. Metal ion M2 is coordinated by five histidines (His265, His294, His297, His298, and one from the HX4H motif). The two metals are separated by 6.4 angstroms, longer than any soluble diiron enzyme structure. No carboxylate bridge is present.

- Castor Bean Stearyl-Acyl Carrier Protein Desaturase — In contrast to SCD1, the soluble plant stearyl-ACP desaturase features a dimetal center bridged by a glutamate residue with bidentate coordination, constraining the inter-[IRON](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/additives/iron/) distance to 3-4 angstroms. This allows formation of classical diiron reaction intermediates such as cis-mu-1,2-peroxo and diferryl species.
