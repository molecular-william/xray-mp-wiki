# Diiron Center Mechanism in Membrane Desaturases

## Overview
The diiron center in membrane-bound desaturases, exemplified by stearoyl-CoA desaturase 1 (SCD1), features a unique coordination geometry where nine highly conserved histidine residues ligate two metal ions separated by approximately 6.4 angstroms. This distance is significantly longer than the 3-4 angstrom separation found in soluble diiron enzymes, and unlike soluble counterparts, there is no carboxylate bridge between the metal ions. This distinct architecture suggests a novel mechanism for oxygen activation that differs from the classical cis-mu-1,2-peroxo and diferryl intermediates observed in soluble diiron enzymes.


## Mechanism/Details


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- **Mouse Stearoyl-CoA Desaturase 1 (SCD1)**: The diiron center is coordinated by nine highly conserved histidine residues from three motifs (two HXXHH and one HX4H). Metal ion M1 is coordinated by His116, His121, His153, His156, and a water molecule. Metal ion M2 is coordinated by His265, His294, His297, His298, and one from the HX4H motif. The 6.4 angstrom separation between metals exceeds the range for classical diiron intermediates.
- **Castor Bean Stearyl-ACP Desaturase**: In contrast, the soluble plant enzyme features a glutamate-bridged dimetal center with 3-4 angstrom separation, enabling classical cis-mu-1,2-peroxo and diferryl intermediates.
