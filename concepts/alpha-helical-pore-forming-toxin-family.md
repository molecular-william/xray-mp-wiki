# Alpha-Helical Pore-Forming Toxin Family (pfam05791)

## Overview
The alpha-helical pore-forming toxin family (pfam05791) comprises a group of
structurally related bacterial toxins that form pores in target cell membranes
using an elongated bundle of long alpha helices. Members include hemolysin E
(HlyE/ClyA/SheA) from Escherichia coli, the HblB and NheA/NheC components of
the Bacillus cereus hemolytic and non-hemolytic toxins, and [Cry6Aa (Bacillus thuringiensis Pore-Forming Toxin)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/toxins/cry6aa/) from
Bacillus thuringiensis. These toxins share a characteristic elongated fold
consisting of a bundle of long (~90 A) amphipathic alpha helices (typically
5 major helices) with a head domain folded across one end. The head domain
contains a beta tongue region that carries a putative transmembrane sequence
that inserts into the target membrane to form oligomeric pores. [Cry6Aa (Bacillus thuringiensis Pore-Forming Toxin)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/toxins/cry6aa/) is the
first B. thuringiensis Cry toxin identified with this fold, establishing a new
structural paradigm for pesticidal crystal proteins. Unlike the canonical
three-domain Cry toxins, the alpha-helical pore-forming family is defined
purely by structural homology at very low sequence identity (10-12%).

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Cry6Aa (Bacillus thuringiensis Pore-Forming Toxin)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/toxins/cry6aa/) (Bacillus thuringiensis) — [Cry6Aa (Bacillus thuringiensis Pore-Forming Toxin)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/toxins/cry6aa/) (PDB 5KUC, 5KUD) is an alpha-helical pore-forming toxin active against coleopteran insects and nematodes. It shows unique features including internal repeat sequences, wing-like intra-helical loops, and two disulfide bonds.
- HlyE/ClyA (Escherichia coli) — HlyE (hemolysin E, also known as [ClyA Cytotoxin from Escherichia coli](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/toxins/clyA/) or SheA) is a prototypical member that forms pores from 8 or 13 monomers. Its structure revealed the alpha-helical bundle architecture with a head domain containing the transmembrane tongue region.
- HblB (Bacillus cereus) — HblB is a component of the B. cereus hemolytic toxin (HBL). Its structure (PDB 2NRJ) was used as the search model for solving the [Cry6Aa (Bacillus thuringiensis Pore-Forming Toxin)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/toxins/cry6aa/) structure by molecular replacement (Z-score 24.3, RMSD 2.8 A over 300 C-alpha atoms).
- NheA (Bacillus cereus) — NheA is a component of the B. cereus non-hemolytic toxin (NHE). It shares the same fold with a Z-score of 21% and RMSD 3.5 A to [Cry6Aa (Bacillus thuringiensis Pore-Forming Toxin)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/toxins/cry6aa/).
