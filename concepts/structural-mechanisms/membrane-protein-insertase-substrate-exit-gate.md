# YidC Substrate Exit Gate and Helical Bundle Rearrangement

## Overview
The [YidC/Oxa1/Alb3 Family](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/protein-families/yidc-oxa1-alb3-family/) of membrane protein insertases facilitates the insertion, folding, and assembly of alpha-helical membrane proteins. The crystal structure of full-length YidC from Thermotoga maritima ([TMYIDC](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/secretion-translocon/tmyidc/), PDB 5Y83, 3.8 A) in a detergent-solubilized monomeric state, together with the periplasmic domain (TmPD, PDB 5Y82, 2.5 A), provided key insights into the mechanism of YidC-mediated membrane protein insertion. In vivo photo-crosslinking experiments demonstrated that YidC substrates exit to the lipid bilayer laterally through the TM3-TM5 interface (one of two alpha-helical bundle interfaces). Engineered intramolecular disulfide bonds coupled with complementation assays showed that significant rearrangement of the two alpha-helical bundles at the top of the hydrophilic groove is critical for YidC function, whereas the TM2-TM6 interface does not rearrange significantly during substrate insertion.
