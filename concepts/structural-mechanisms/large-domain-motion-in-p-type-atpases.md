## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [SERCA1a (Sarcoplasmic Reticulum Ca2+-ATPase)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/serca1a/) (Sarcoplasmic Reticulum Ca²⁺-ATPase 1a) — Domain A undergoes ~90° rotation; domain N moves toward domain P during phosphorylation; distance between ATP-binding pocket and Ca²⁺-binding sites is ~80 Å
- [SERCA1a E340A Mutant](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/pumps-atpases/serca1a-e340a/) — E340A structure shows ~10 Å downward rotation of headpiece, altered A-domain connectivity, and stabilization of occluded state (doi/10.1073##pnas.2014896117)
- Na+,K+-ATPase (Pig Kidney) — A domain rotation and transmembrane helix rearrangement enable ion occlusion and high-affinity [Ouabain](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/ouabain/) binding
- Na+,K+-ATPase (Pig Kidney) — A domain undergoes ~110 degree rotation in the E1P state; N domain rotates ~80 degrees toward P domain; P domain rotates ~35 degrees relative to alphaM7-10. These large domain motions couple ATP hydrolysis to Na+ occlusion and K+ countertransport (DOI: 10.1126/science.1243352)
- V1-ATPase from Thermus thermophilus — Related ATPase family; cytoplasmic domain movements couple ion transport to ATP hydrolysis
