# GXXXG Motif (Helix Packing Motif)

## Overview
The GXXXG motif is a helix-helix packing motif commonly found in transmembrane proteins. It consists of two [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) residues separated by three amino acids (G-X-X-X-G) on adjacent alpha-helices, enabling close packing of helices due to the small side chains of [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/). In the beetle ICMT structure, the GXXXG motif between helices M1 and M3 mediates helix packing and is hypothesized to prevent dimerization in ICMT enzymes that contain M1 and M2, in contrast to yeast ICMT which lacks these helices and may dimerize through an equivalent GXXXG packing interaction.

## Mechanism/Details
In the beetle ICMT structure from Tribolium castaneum, the GXXXG motif between helices M1 and M3 mediates close helix-helix packing. Because beetle ICMT contains M1 and M2 helices, this GXXXG motif prevents dimerization of the enzyme. In contrast, yeast ICMT lacks M1 and M2 helices, and the GXXXG motif in its first transmembrane helix (equivalent to M3 of beetle ICMT) may cause dimerization through packing of these helices. The presence or absence of M1 and M2 helices thus determines the oligomerization state of ICMT enzymes through the GXXXG packing motif.
