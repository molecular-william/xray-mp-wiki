# SLC6 Substrate Recognition by the GMG Motif

## Overview
The (G/A/C)PheG motif (GMG in [MhsT Multi-Hydrophobic Amino Acid Transporter from Bacillus halodurans](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/mhsT/), GFG in [LEUT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/leut/)) in the unwound region of
transmembrane helix 6 (TM6) of SLC6/NSS transporters mediates substrate
recognition and determines transport specificity. Crystal structures of [MhsT Multi-Hydrophobic Amino Acid Transporter from Bacillus halodurans](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/mhsT/)
in complex with six different hydrophobic amino acids (Tyr, Phe, 4F-Phe, Ile,
Leu, Val) reveal a bimodal binding site: the GMG loop shifts inward by ~2 A
to accommodate small aliphatic substrates (reducing binding pocket volume from
~230 A^3 to ~144 A^3), while remaining in an outward position for bulkier
aromatic substrates. The central residue (Met236 in [MhsT Multi-Hydrophobic Amino Acid Transporter from Bacillus halodurans](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/mhsT/), Phe259 in [LEUT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/leut/))
undergoes rotamer changes that fine-tune the chemical environment. The M236F
mutation (mimicking human SLC6A18/SLC6A19 AFG motif) abolishes aromatic
amino acid transport while maintaining aliphatic transport. A conserved
glutamate (Glu66 in [MhsT Multi-Hydrophobic Amino Acid Transporter from Bacillus halodurans](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/mhsT/)) on TM2 acts as a fulcrum, maintaining interaction
with the unwound TM6 through water-mediated hydrogen bonds across all
conformational states.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [MhsT Multi-Hydrophobic Amino Acid Transporter from Bacillus halodurans](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/mhsT/) (Bacillus halodurans) — [MhsT Multi-Hydrophobic Amino Acid Transporter from Bacillus halodurans](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/mhsT/) has a GMG motif (Gly235-Met236-Gly237) in TM6. It transports both aliphatic and aromatic hydrophobic amino acids. The GMG loop shifts by ~2 A to accommodate different-sized substrates. M236F abolishes aromatic transport.
- [LEUT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/leut/) (Aquifex aeolicus) — [LEUT](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/enzymes/leut/) has a GFG motif (Phe259 as the central residue). It preferentially transports smaller aliphatic amino acids and shows maximal catalytic efficiency for L-Ala. The bulkier Phe central residue limits the binding site for large aromatic substrates.
- Human SLC6A18/SLC6A19 (BOAT3/BOAT1) — These neutral amino acid transporters have an AFG motif in TM6. Like the M236F mutant of [MhsT Multi-Hydrophobic Amino Acid Transporter from Bacillus halodurans](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/slc-transporters/mhsT/), they preferentially transport aliphatic amino acids (Leu, Ile, Val, Ala, Met) and show reduced transport of aromatic amino acids (Phe, Trp, Tyr).
