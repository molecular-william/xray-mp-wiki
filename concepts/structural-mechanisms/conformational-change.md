# Conformational Change

## Overview
Conformational changes are structural rearrangements within a protein that enable its function. In membrane proteins, these include helix movements, domain rotations, and loop reorganizations that facilitate transport, signaling, and catalysis. Key examples include the alternating access mechanism in transporters, the helix-packing changes in GPCR activation, and the iris-like gating in ion channels. X-ray crystallography captures discrete conformational states; comparison of multiple structures reveals the conformational landscape.
