# Chloride-Binding Sites in Photosystem II

## Overview
The chloride ion (Cl-) is an essential cofactor for oxygen evolution in photosystem II (PSII), required for the S2->S3 and S3->S0 transitions of the [Kok Cycle](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/enzyme-mechanisms/kok-cycle/). Anion substitution crystallography with Br- and I- identified two Cl--binding sites flanking the [Mn4Ca cluster](/xray-mp-wiki/concepts/miscellaneous/manganese-calcium-cluster/) in each PSII monomer (Kawakami et al., 2009). Site 1 (Cl1) is close to D1-Glu-333 (which coordinates Mn4) and is located at the entrance of the proton exit channel. Site 2 (Cl2) is close to CP43-Glu-354 (which coordinates Mn3). Br- substitution at both sites retains oxygen-evolving activity, while I- substitution (ionic radius 2.20 A vs 1.81 A for Cl-) completely inhibits O2 evolution. The Cl- ions are bound weakly (>5 A from the nearest Mn) through hydrogen bonding and aqua-ion interactions, explaining the easy release and exchange of Cl- from PSII.

## Mechanism/Details
Both Cl- binding sites are located on opposite sides of the Mn4Ca cluster at similar distances from the nearest Mn atom and Ca atom. The two Br- ions (Br1, Br2) in Br--substituted PSII were identified at 3.7 A resolution using difference Fourier and anomalous Fourier maps. Site 1 (Br1) is near D1-Glu-333, with Mn4-Br1 = 6.4 A and Ca-Br1 = 9.4 A. Site 2 (Br2) is near CP43-Glu-354, with Mn2-Br2 = 7.3 A and Ca-Br2 = 11.1 A. The Br1-Br2 distance is 14.6 A. For I--substituted PSII at 4.0 A, the corresponding distances are Mn4-I1 = 7.4 A, Ca-I1 = 10.1 A, Mn2-I2 = 6.5 A, Ca-I2 = 9.6 A, and I1-I2 = 13.9 A. The Br1 site is close to D1-Asn-338 (main chain N, 4.1 A) and D1-Glu-333 (main chain N, 6.0 A), while Br2 is close to CP43-Glu-354 (main chain N, 4.9 A) and D1-Asn-338 (main chain N, 4.1 A). The 2 Cl- anions are required to maintain the coordination structure of the Mn4Ca cluster as well as the proposed proton channel. Cl- is not present within 5 A of the Mn4Ca cluster, consistent with EXAFS studies, and no covalent bond exists for Cl- coordination, explaining the easy release and substitution of Cl-. The functional role of Cl- likely involves maintaining the structural integrity of the OEC through hydrogen bonding networks rather than direct metal coordination. The larger radius of I- (2.20 A) may physically block the proton exit channel or reduce protein conformational flexibility required for S-state transitions.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Photosystem II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/photosystem-ii/) — Br--substituted PSII from T. vulcanus at 3.7 A resolution. Difference Fourier maps (Br-PSII minus Cl-PSII) showed two electron density peaks coinciding with anomalous Fourier peaks, confirming two Br- binding sites (Br1 near D1-Glu-333, Br2 near CP43-Glu-354). Br- substitution preserved ~90% oxygen-evolving activity.
- [Photosystem II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosynthesis/photosystem-ii/) — I--substituted PSII from T. vulcanus at 4.0 A resolution. Two I- binding sites (I1, I2) at the same locations as Br1/Br2, plus three additional I- sites at CP47-Cys-112, PsbTc-Cys-12, and D2-Tyr-160 (the latter three non-functional). I- substitution completely inhibited oxygen evolution at 15-20 mM I-.
