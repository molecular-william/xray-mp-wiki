# NPC1-C Luminal Domain

## Overview
NPC1-C is the C-terminal luminal domain (also called domain C or CTD-like domain) of human Niemann-Pick C1 ([NPC1](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/structural-adhesion/human-niemann-pick-c1/)), a multi-pass membrane protein that regulates [Cholesterol](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/lipids/cholesterol/) egress from late endosomes and lysosomes. NPC1-C spans residues 371-621 and is one of three large luminal loops (domains A, C, and I) that protrude into the endosomal lumen. This domain serves as the intracellular receptor for filoviruses including Ebola virus (EBOV) and Marburg virus, directly binding the viral glycoprotein (GP). The structure of NPC1-C reveals an independently folding module with flexible protruding loops that interact with different binding partners including EBOV GP and NPC2. N-glycosylation at seven predicted sites (Asn452, Asn459, Asn478, Asn524, Asn557, Asn572, Asn598) does not affect EBOV GP binding. The nonglycosylated NPC1-C crystallizes in space group P2_1 (a=53.145, b=65.207, c=68.534 A, beta=91.686 deg) with two antiparallel copies per asymmetric unit, diffracting to 2.3 A resolution (PDB 8eus).
