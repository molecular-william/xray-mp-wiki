# Peptide Selectivity in Cholecystokinin Receptors

## Overview
Peptide selectivity in cholecystokinin receptors refers to the molecular basis by which CCKₐR preferentially binds sulfated cholecystokinin (CCK) peptides while CCKᴅR recognizes both CCK and gastrin with similar affinities. The structural basis for this differential selectivity was revealed by comparing the CCKₐR-NN9056 (agonist) and CCKᴅR-gastrin-17-G protein complex structures, identifying key differences in ECL2 that govern peptide recognition.

## Mechanism/Details
The C-terminal pentapeptide-amide region of CCK and gastrin peptides (the portion responsible for receptor activation) is nearly identical between the two peptide families. However, differences in ECL2 between CCKₐR and CCKᴅR dictate which peptides can bind. In CCKₐR, residue R197 in ECL2 forms a critical salt bridge with the sulfated tyrosine of CCK peptides (sul-Y7), providing a 1,000-fold affinity enhancement for sulfated over non-sulfated CCK. This arginine is locked by either the salt bridge with the peptide or by interaction with E344 in ECL3, creating spatial hindrance that prevents gastrin (which has a different backbone conformation) from binding CCKₐR with high affinity. In CCKᴅR, the equivalent position is a valine (V198), removing this spatial hindrance and allowing the receptor to accommodate both CCK and gastrin. Additionally, W209 in CCKᴅR ECL2 (L200 in CCKₐR) pushes H207 toward gastrin to enable a hydrogen bond with the peptide backbone, further stabilizing the gastrin-CCKᴅR interaction. The sulfated tyrosine of NN9056 anchors into a binding cavity formed by helix II, ECL1, and ECL2 of CCKₐR via its interaction with R197. In CCKᴅR, gastrin Y6 occupies a different position rotated by about 90°, forming hydrogen bonds with ECL2 (including R208 in CCKᴅR, which is L199 in CCKₐR). Gastrin-17 residue W14 would clash with R197 in CCKₐR, further reducing gastrin binding to CCKₐR.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- Human Cholecystokinin A Receptor (CCKₐR) — R197 in ECL2 forms salt bridge with sulfated tyrosine of CCK, providing 1,000-fold selectivity for sulfated CCK over non-sulfated forms. R197A mutation greatly diminishes CCK-8 binding.
- Human Cholecystokinin B Receptor (CCKᴅR) — V198 (equivalent to R197 in CCKₐR) removes spatial hindrance, allowing gastrin binding. W209 pushes H207 toward gastrin for backbone hydrogen bond. W14 of gastrin-17 clashes with CCKₐR but not CCKᴅR.
