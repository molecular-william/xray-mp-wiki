# Large Domain Motion in P-type ATPases

## Overview
Large domain motions are a fundamental feature of P-type ATPase function. The cytoplasmic headpiece consists of three domains (A, N, and P) that undergo substantial conformational changes during the transport cycle. Domain A acts as an actuator or anchor, domain N binds nucleotides, and domain P contains the phosphorylation site. The distance between the ATP-binding pocket and the Ca²⁺-binding sites is approximately 80 Å, requiring large-scale domain movements to couple ATP hydrolysis to ion transport.
