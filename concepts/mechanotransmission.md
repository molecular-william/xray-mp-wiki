# Mechanotransmission

## Overview
Mechanotransmission is a transmembrane conformational coupling mechanism used by type VII ABC transporters, first described in the [MACB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/macb/) ABC transporter. Instead of transporting substrates across the inner membrane via a central pore (the canonical alternating-access mechanism), mechanotransmissive ABC transporters couple cytoplasmic ATP hydrolysis to extracytoplasmic conformational changes that perform mechanical work in the periplasm.


## Mechanism/Details
In [MACB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/macb/), the prototypical mechanotransmissive ABC transporter, the mechanism operates as follows:

1. The transmembrane domain consists of four helices (TM1-TM4) with a rigid dimerization interface dominated by hydrophobic interactions. The TM1 and TM2 helices from both monomers extend into the periplasm to form a four-helix bundle (the stalk) that elevates the periplasmic domain above the membrane.

2. The periplasmic domain is divided into two subdomains: the Porter subdomain and the Sabre subdomain. ATP binding drives reversible dimerization of the cytoplasmic nucleotide-binding domains (NBDs).

3. NBD dimerization is communicated to the transmembrane domain via the major coupling helix (between TM2 and TM3) and the minor coupling helix (C-terminus), which interact with grooves on the NBD surface.

4. This causes concerted movements of the second transmembrane segment (TM2) and the major coupling helix, resulting in opening and closing of the [MACB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/macb/) periplasmic domains — without the need for a transmembrane pore.

5. In the tripartite MacAB-TolC pump, this conformational cycle drives a molecular bellows mechanism: substrates enter an interior cavity between parted periplasmic domains, ATP binding closes the domains (reducing cavity volume), forcing substrates through the MacA gate ring and into TolC for export to the extracellular space. ATP hydrolysis resets the system.

The mechanotransmission architecture is conserved in related systems such as LolCDE (lipoprotein extraction from the outer leaflet of the inner membrane) and FtsEX (regulation of periplasmic peptidoglycan hydrolases during cell division). These systems use the same four-transmembrane-helix architecture to transmit conformational change across the membrane and perform diverse biological functions in the bacterial envelope.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [MACB](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/abc-transporters/macb/) (Aggregatibacter actinomycetemcomitans) — Prototypical mechanotransmissive ABC transporter. ATP-driven opening/closing of periplasmic domains powers the MacAB-TolC tripartite efflux pump.
- LolCDE (Escherichia coli) — Lipoprotein trafficking complex. Uses mechanotransmission to extract lipoproteins from the outer leaflet of the inner membrane and present them to the LolA chaperone.
- FtsEX — Cell division signaling complex. Uses mechanotransmission to regulate recruitment and activation of periplasmic peptidoglycan hydrolases.
