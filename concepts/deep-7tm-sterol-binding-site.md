# Deep 7TM Sterol-Binding Site of Smoothened

## Overview

The deep 7TM sterol-binding site is a cholesterol-binding pocket located deep within the seven-transmembrane (7TM) helical bundle of the Hedgehog pathway transducer Smoothened (SMO). Identified in the crystal structure of active mouse SMO (PDB 6O3C, 2.8 Å), this site binds a cholesterol molecule that is critical for SMO activation. The 7TM sterol site is the primary mediator of sterol action in SMO signalling, stabilizing the outward displacement of TM6 that characterizes the active GPCR conformation.

## Mechanism/Details

In the active SMO structure, cholesterol is bound deep within the 7TM bundle, analogous to agonist binding in other GPCRs. The cholesterol hydroxyl group forms a hydrogen bond with Y398. The bound sterol stabilizes an outward displacement of TM6 (8 Å) and inward movement of TM5 (6 Å), which are hallmark features of GPCR activation. Molecular dynamics simulations (6 independent simulations of ~0.8 µs each) showed that cholesterol bound stably within the 7TM site and maintained the active conformation even after removal of SAG21k and NbSmo8. Antagonists like SANT-1 extensively overlap with the 7TM cholesterol site — SANT-1 contacts H470 (hSMO) in TM6, stabilizing the inward, inactive conformation. Mutations at the 7TM site (V333F, V408F, I412F, T470Q) completely ablate GLI activation by SAG21k, ShhNp, and direct cholesterol activation. The Y398F mutation, which ablates the hydrogen bond between cholesterol and SMO, also decreases GLI activation. Membrane sterols may access the 7TM site via a hydrophobic tunnel between TM5 and TM6 that opens to the inner leaflet, avoiding the energetically prohibitive step of membrane desolvation.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work

- Mouse Smoothened (SMO) — Deep 7TM sterol site identified in active SMO-SAG21k-NbSmo8 structure (PDB 6O3C, 2.8 Å). Cholesterol bound deep in 7TM bundle, stabilized by hydrogen bond to Y398. Mutations V333F, V408F, I412F, T470Q abolish activation.
