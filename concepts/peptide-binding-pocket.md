# PepT_So2 Substrate-Binding Pockets (P1, P2, P3)

## Overview
The substrate-binding site of PepT_So2 (a proton-dependent oligopeptide transporter from Shewanella oneidensis) is divided into three distinct pockets (P1, P2, and P3) that accommodate peptide side chains at different positions. These pockets collectively enable the broad substrate specificity of PepT transporters for di- and tripeptides. The peptide backbone is clamped in a conserved polar pocket at the N-terminus and extends to a positively charged patch at the C-terminus. The three side-chain pockets allow differential recognition of various amino acid side chains, which is important for the transport of diverse peptides and drug prodrugs.


## Mechanism/Details
P1 Pocket: A small pocket formed by Ser154, Tyr29, Met158, and Pro330. It accommodates only small- to medium-sized residues (alanine, valine strongly favored). Aromatic or large charged side chains are not suitable for P1. [Glycine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/buffers/glycine/) residues were found to be highly unfavorable in position 1.
P2 Pocket: A hydrophobic pocket dominated by residues Phe287, Phe288, Tyr291, and the polar residue Ser406. It is suitable for hydrophobic side chains.
P3 Pocket: An aromatic pocket formed by Arg25, Tyr28, Trp54, Ile61, Tyr62, Pro65, Asn292, Phe430, and Asn437. It is designed for aromatic side chains, as demonstrated by the binding of the brominated tyrosine in AY(Br) and AY(Br)A peptides. Conformational changes of aromatic clusters allow modulation of the binding site.
The C-terminal carboxylate of dipeptides primarily interacts with Arg25, while tripeptides extend further to interact with Lys121, indicating differential roles for di- versus tripeptide recognition.
