# Channelrhodopsin Photocycle

## Overview
The channelrhodopsin photocycle is a sequence of light-induced conformational and protonation states that channelrhodopsins pass through upon illumination. Light absorption triggers all-trans to 13-cis isomerization of the retinal chromophore, initiating a cascade of structural changes that lead to ion pore opening and cation conductance. Key intermediates include P1(520) with isomerized retinal, P2(390) with deprotonated Schiff base, and P3(520) with reprotonated Schiff base. The DC-pair (Asp-Cys) in the retinal binding pocket is critical for photocycle transitions and ion conductance.
