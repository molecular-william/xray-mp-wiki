# Dual-Topology Channels

## Overview
Dual-topology channels are membrane protein complexes in which the subunits that form the functional pore are arranged in antiparallel orientation across the membrane. This means that identical subunits span the membrane in opposite directions, such that the intracellular and extracellular ion entryways are structurally identical despite the asymmetric membrane environment. This arrangement contrasts with conventional homodimeric channels where subunits have parallel topology. Dual-topology is a defining feature of the Fluc family of fluoride-specific ion channels.


## Mechanism/Details
In dual-topology Fluc channels, the homodimer consists of two identical subunits arranged antiparallel to each other across the lipid bilayer. Each subunit contributes residues to both of the channel's two fluoride permeation pathways. The antiparallel arrangement results in structurally symmetric ion entryways at both membrane interfaces, even though the intracellular and extracellular environments are chemically distinct. This symmetry has important implications for crystallization: monobody chaperones bind identically to both ends of the channel, and the channel's conductive pore structure remains symmetric regardless of which end a chaperone binds to.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Fluoride Channel from B. pertussis (Bpe)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/bpe-fluoride-channel/) — Bpe forms a homodimer with antiparallel transmembrane topology. Each subunit contributes to both fluoride permeation pathways. The Bpe-S8 structure (PDB 6BQO) shows that despite asymmetric monobody binding (one end only), both channel subunits remain structurally identical and symmetric.
- [Fluoride Channel from B. pertussis (Bpe)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/bpe-fluoride-channel/) — The Bpe-L2 structure (PDB 5NKQ) shows doubly-bound symmetry with monobodies at both ends. Comparison with Bpe-S8 (singly bound) demonstrates that dual-topology symmetry is preserved regardless of chaperone occupancy.
- [Fluoride Channel from E. coli (Ec2)](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/ec2-fluoride-channel/) — Ec2 is an E. coli Fluc homologue with 33% sequence identity to Bpe. The Ec2-S9 structure (PDB 5A43) confirms identical fold and dual-topology architecture. The inferred Na+ density appears in equivalent locations, demonstrating that the dual-topology arrangement with central cation coordination is conserved across the Fluc family.
