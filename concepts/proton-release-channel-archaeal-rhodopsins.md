# Proton-Release Channel in Archaeal Rhodopsins

## Overview
The proton-release channel is a conserved structural feature in
light-driven proton-pumping archaeal rhodopsins (bacteriorhodopsin,
archaerhodopsin-2, cruxrhodopsin-3, deltarhodopsin-3). It contains
a pair of glutamate residues that form a short-distance (<2.5 A)
interaction maintained by a low-barrier hydrogen bond, functioning
as a proton-release complex during the photocycle.


## Mechanism/Details
**Low-barrier hydrogen bond between paired glutamates:** In archaeal rhodopsins crystallized by the membrane-fusion method, two glutamates in the proton-release channel (Glu194/Glu204 in bR; Glu199/Glu209 in aR2) are separated by ~2.3 A. This unusually short distance is characteristic of a low-barrier hydrogen bond (LBHB), where the proton is shared almost equally between the two carboxyl groups. The LBHB stabilizes the neutral purple form of the protein.

**pH-dependent conformational changes:** At neutral pH, the Glu194/Glu204 pair adopts a closed conformation with Glu194 OE2 directed toward the backbone amide NH of the same residue. Upon alkalization (pH >9.5 in bR P622 crystals), Glu194 reorients toward Tyr83, accompanied by a blue shift of the visible absorption band (alkaline pink form, lambda_max ~480 nm).

**Sensitivity to lipid environment:** The conformation of the proton-release channel is sensitive to the crystallization procedure and lipid composition. In crystal forms where native lipids are partially removed (e.g., bR C2 crystal form from epitaxial growth), the neutral purple form is destabilized and the Glu194/Glu204 pair is broken at lower pH. This suggests the lipid-protein interaction plays a crucial role in maintaining the functional conformation.

**Role in the photocycle:** The Glu194/Glu204 pair functions as a proton-release complex that breaks during the proton-pumping cycle. This conformational change facilitates proton release to the extracellular side. The rising rate of the M state is influenced by the integrity of this paired structure.
