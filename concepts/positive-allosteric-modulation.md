# Positive Allosteric Modulation

## Overview
Positive allosteric modulators (PAMs) are small molecules that bind to an allosteric site on a receptor and enhance its response to the endogenous agonist without directly activating the receptor. Unlike orthosteric agonists, PAMs do not trigger receptor activation on their own; instead, they increase the potency and/or efficacy of the natural ligand. PAMs of AMPA receptors (AMPARs) are of particular therapeutic interest because decreased AMPA receptor signaling is implicated in Alzheimer's disease, Parkinson's disease, attention deficit hyperactivity disorder, depression, and schizophrenia. PAMs also stimulate the release of brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), suggesting therapeutic promise as neuroprotectants.

## Mechanism/Details
AMPA receptor PAMs of the benzothiadiazine dioxide (BTD) class bind at the allosteric site located at the ligand-binding domain (LBD) dimer interface. The binding pocket is formed by residues from both subunits of the dimer. Key interactions include hydrogen bonding between the sulfonamide nitrogen of the modulator and the backbone oxygen of Pro515, and halogen bonding between a chlorine atom of the modulator and the side chain hydroxyl of Ser518 from the adjacent subunit. The thiophene ring of the modulator sits within 4 A of backbone atoms of Ser750, Lys751, and Gly752 of one subunit and Pro515 of both subunits. PAM binding does not affect the lobe D1-D2 domain closure angle, which is consistent with the mechanism of positive allosteric modulation. The shape complementarity between the modulator and the binding pocket is critical for potency, with optimal fit achieved by cyclopropyl substitution at the 4-position of the thiadiazine ring.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- **Thienothiadiazine dioxide compound 32 (BPAM395)**: Potent AMPAR PAM with EC2x = 0.24 microM on native AMPARs in rat cortical primary cell cultures. Crystallized with GluA2-LBD (L504Y, N775S) at 1.55 A resolution. Forms hydrogen bond with Pro515 backbone oxygen via sulfonamide nitrogen; chlorine atom bonds to Ser518 hydroxyl from adjacent subunit. Shows good shape complementarity with cyclopropyl group fitting the pocket at 3.3 A closest distance. - **Benzothiadiazine dioxide compound 10 (BPAM344)**: Reference BTD-type AMPAR PAM. Similar binding mode to compound 32, with a fluorine atom forming a hydrogen bond with Ser518. - **BTD-type PAMs**: General class of AMPA receptor positive allosteric modulators. Orally active, some showing cognitive enhancement in vivo at low doses (1 mg/kg).
