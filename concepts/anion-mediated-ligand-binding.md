# Anion-Mediated Ligand Binding in iGluRs

## Overview
Anion-mediated ligand binding is a mechanism in which chloride ions serve as surrogate ligand atoms in protein binding pockets, substituting for functional groups of the primary ligand. This mechanism was discovered in the [LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/) ligand-binding domain ([LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/)) of the ionotropic glutamate receptor from the freshwater bdelloid rotifer Adineta vaga. In this system, chloride ions occupy positions equivalent to the gamma-carboxyl group oxygen atoms of glutamate, enabling binding of neutral amino acids (alanine, serine, methionine) that lack this carboxyl group.


## Mechanism/Details
The [LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/) ligand-binding pocket has an overall positive charge and a pyramidal cavity of approximately 302 A3. For acidic amino acids (glutamate, aspartate), the alpha-carboxyl group binds via bidentate salt bridge to Arg522 in domain 1, the alpha-amino group is coordinated by Asp720, Asp515, and Thr517, and the gamma- carboxyl group (for glutamate) is coordinated by Arg676 and Arg702 in domain 2.
For neutral amino acids lacking the gamma-carboxyl group, a chloride ion is recruited into the binding pocket and occupies the position equivalent to the gamma-carboxyl oxygen. The chloride ion is coordinated by arginine side chains and main-chain amides of the protein, plus water molecules. The specific chloride coordination geometry varies with the bound neutral amino acid: in the alanine complex, chloride is coordinated by Arg676, Asp720 main-chain amide, and waters W3 and W7; in the serine complex, chloride is displaced by 2.3 A and coordinated by both Arg702 and Arg676 side chains plus Asp720 main-chain amide.
Functional validation shows that removal of chloride ions lowers binding affinity for alanine (53-fold) and serine (19-fold) but not for glutamate or aspartate. The methionine affinity decreases only 18-fold in chloride-free conditions, suggesting the thiomethyl group can partially substitute for the chloride ion. Phenylalanine, the largest neutral amino acid, occludes the anion binding site and does not utilize chloride, binding with even lower affinity.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/) [LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/): Crystal structures of [L-Alanine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/substrates/l-alanine/) (4IO5), [L-Serine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/substrates/l-serine/) (4IO4), and
  [L-Methionine](/xray-mp-wiki/reagents/ligands/l-methionine/) (4IO6) complexes reveal chloride ions in the binding pocket acting as
  surrogate gamma-carboxyl groups. Removal of Cl- reduces alanine affinity 53-fold
  and serine affinity 19-fold.
- [LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/) [LBD](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/other-ion-channels/avglur1/) dimer: A single Cl- ion also binds at the dimer interface, buried in
  a small cavity, likely stabilizing the dimer assembly by acting as a countercharge
  to arginine side chains.
