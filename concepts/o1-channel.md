# O1 Water Channel of Photosystem II

## Overview
The O1 channel is a water intake pathway in Photosystem II (PSII) that connects the lumenal side of the thylakoid membrane to the oxygen-evolving complex (OEC). It serves as the primary conduit for substrate water molecules to reach the Mn4CaO5 cluster during the water oxidation catalytic cycle. The channel has two branches (A and B) and features a bottleneck formed by residues including PsbV-G132, PsbV-G133, PsbV-K134, and D2-R348 at the distal end, approximately 15 A from the OEC.


## Mechanism/Details
The O1 channel functions as a water delivery conduit from the lumen to the OEC. Structural analysis of a 1.89 A room-temperature X-ray structure revealed that water molecules in the O1 channel have higher B-factor values (average 38 A^2) compared to those in the Cl1 (27 A^2) and O4 (31 A^2) channels, indicating greater mobility. The Fo-Fc difference density peaks (+3 sigma or higher) are distributed throughout the entire O1 channel, from bulk waters on the lumen side to waters close to O1 of the OEC, suggesting partial occupancy of highly mobile waters.

During the S2 to S3 transition, specific waters in close proximity to the OEC (W26, W27, W28, and W39) undergo the most significant positional changes. W27 shows a significant decrease in electron density and occupancy at 150 microseconds after the second flash, while W39 electron density increases to reach maximum at the same time point. This coincides with the starting of the Ox (oxo/hydroxo) density build-up at the open coordination site of Mn1. A new water near D1-E329, located approximately 15 A from the OEC, appears at 400 microseconds after the second flash, after completion of Ox insertion at the Mn1 site.

The channel's two branches (A and B) are separated by the bottleneck region. In crystallization conditions containing glycerol or DMSO, these small cryoprotectant molecules can occupy positions in both branches of the O1 channel, potentially altering the water network and slightly reducing S-state turnover efficiency during the S2 to S3 and S3 to S0 transitions.

## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
- [Photosystem II](/xray-mp-wiki/proteins/photosystem-ii/) — The 1.89 A room-temperature XFEL structure (PDB 7RF1) of PSII from Synechococcus elongatus provided the first high-resolution visualization of the O1 channel water pathway during the S2 to S3 transition. Waters W26, W27, W28, and W39 in the O1 channel show the most significant movement during this transition, with W39 becoming the newly inserted substrate water (Ox) at the open coordination site of Mn1.
