# K_LT Intermediate

## Overview
The K_LT intermediate is a low-temperature photointermediate in the bacteriorhodopsin photocycle. It forms when bacteriorhodopsin crystals are photoexcited at cryogenic temperatures (110 K) and represents the structural state immediately following retinal photoisomerization but before significant protein conformational rearrangements occur. The K_LT intermediate is characterized by a 13-cis,15-anti configuration of the retinal chromophore and distinctive structural changes at the cytoplasmic side of the protein.


## Mechanism/Details
Upon photoexcitation at 110 K, the retinal chromophore of bacteriorhodopsin photoisomerizes from the all-trans,15-anti configuration to the 13-cis,15-anti configuration. This isomerization forces the C20 atom of retinal towards the cytoplasmic side of the membrane, bringing it into closer contact with the indole ring of Trp 182. This movement has been proposed as the mechanism by which retinal isomerization induces large conformational changes at the cytoplasmic end of helix F.
Concurrently, changes occur in the position of Lys 216 and its peptide backbone in helix G. These rearrangements partially disrupt a hydrogen-bonding network and weaken helix G, allowing it to flex at later stages in the photocycle.
A key feature of the K_LT intermediate is the displacement of water molecule W402, which in the ground state forms hydrogen bonds to the O delta 1 atom of Asp 85 and the nitrogen of the Schiff base (N epsilon of Lys 216). The displacement of W402 is accompanied by the mutual approach of the Asp 85 and Asp 212 carboxylates, creating an electrostatic environment that facilitates proton transfer from the Schiff base to Asp 85 in subsequent photocycle steps.
The K_LT population was estimated to be approximately 35% in the illuminated crystal at 110 K, as determined by both X-ray crystallography (simulated annealing omit maps and selected-atom refinement) and off-line microspectrophotometry measurements.


## Examples in Membrane Protein Work
Bacteriorhodopsin: The K_LT intermediate is observed when bacteriorhodopsin crystals are illuminated at 110 K with a 532 nm laser, representing the first structural snapshot after retinal isomerization in the photocycle. The intermediate was captured at approximately 35% population in the crystal.
